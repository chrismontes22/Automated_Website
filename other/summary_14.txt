GEEKOM GeekBook X14 Pro Review: 2.2 pound laptop from a mini PC maker
============================================================

**GEEKOM’s first laptop, the X14 Pro, offers a premium display and solid build quality but suffers from inconsistent performance and misleading marketing.**

**Key points**

*   **Misleading Specs:** Despite initial marketing claims that the laptop would feature the latest Intel "Arrow Lake" processors, the units currently shipping use the older "Meteor Lake" (Series 1) chips.
*   **Impressive Display:** The 14-inch OLED screen is the laptop's standout feature, offering high resolution, vibrant colors, and a 120Hz refresh rate.
*   **Performance Issues:** The laptop experiences thermal throttling and inconsistent benchmark results, often performing significantly worse than mini PCs using the same processor.
*   **Design Quirks:** While the magnesium alloy body is light and well-built, the laptop lacks an SD card slot, has a limited BIOS, and uses a non-standard keyboard layout that lacks a dedicated "Print Screen" key.
*   **Upgradability:** The RAM and Wi-Fi card are soldered to the board and cannot be upgraded, though the SSD storage is user-accessible.

**Why it matters**
While the GEEKOM X14 Pro is an attractive, affordable entry into the laptop market for general users, its inconsistent performance and outdated processor make it a risky choice for power users or those expecting the latest hardware technology.
