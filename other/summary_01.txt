Tech Leaders Support California Bill to Stop 'Dominant Platforms' From Blocking Competition
============================================================

**California bill aims to stop Big Tech from rigging the market against competitors**

**Key points**
* The proposed "BASED Act" would ban massive tech companies (worth over $1 trillion) from favoring their own products over those of competitors on their platforms.
* The bill targets "self-preferencing," such as manipulating search results to boost a company's own services or using private data from third-party sellers to create competing products.
* It mandates that platforms allow users to easily move their personal data to other services, preventing companies from "locking in" customers.
* Supporters, including startup founders and privacy advocates, argue the bill will foster innovation and give smaller companies a fair chance to compete.
* The legislation is designed to prevent dominant platforms from acting as both the "stadium owner" and a player in the game.

**Why it matters**
This bill seeks to curb the power of tech giants that currently control the digital marketplace, aiming to lower prices and increase choices for consumers. By leveling the playing field, it hopes to ensure that the next generation of startups can succeed based on merit rather than being stifled by industry gatekeepers.
