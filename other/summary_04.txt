**16-year-old student creates affordable AI tool to help farmers monitor crop health**

**Key points**

*   Rudrojas Kunvar developed "Evion," an AI-powered platform that analyzes images from standard, low-cost drones to create detailed crop health maps.
*   The tool identifies specific areas of a field that need water or fertilizer, helping farmers reduce waste and save money compared to expensive, specialized agricultural drone services.
*   Kunvar turned down a $300,000 offer to drop out of high school and run the startup full-time, choosing instead to prioritize keeping the technology accessible to small and midsize farms.
*   The software is already being used by farmers across North America, Southeast Asia, and India to replace guesswork with data-driven insights.

**Why it matters**
By making high-tech agricultural analysis affordable, this tool helps smaller farmers compete with large-scale operations while promoting more sustainable farming practices through precise resource management.
