Russians are posing as Signal support to launch phishing attacks
============================================================

**Russian intelligence agents are using fake customer support messages to hijack Signal accounts, while other global cyber threats continue to target government, military, and personal data.**

**Key points**

*   **Signal Phishing:** Russian-affiliated hackers are impersonating Signal support staff to trick high-profile users (such as government officials and journalists) into clicking malicious links, allowing them to take over accounts and steal private messages.
*   **Iranian Propaganda Sites Seized:** The U.S. government shut down several websites linked to an Iranian hacking group that was spreading propaganda, inciting violence, and leaking stolen data from medical and religious organizations.
*   **Massive Data Breach:** A marketing firm for banks, Marquis, notified 670,000 people that their Social Security numbers and financial data were stolen by a ransomware gang.
*   **New Malware Tactics:** The ransomware group "LeakNet" is using a "ClickFix" scam, which tricks users into running hidden malicious commands by pretending to be a standard "prove you are not a robot" security check.
*   **Fitness App Security Risks:** A French sailor accidentally revealed the location of an aircraft carrier by tracking a run on the fitness app Strava, highlighting ongoing security vulnerabilities caused by wearable technology.

**Why it matters**
These incidents demonstrate that even secure platforms and sophisticated organizations are vulnerable to human error and social engineering. Staying vigilant against unexpected "support" messages and being cautious about what data you share on public apps is essential for protecting your digital identity.
