U.S. CISA adds Apple, Laravel Livewire and Craft CMS flaws to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog
============================================================

**CISA adds critical Apple, Craft CMS, and Laravel vulnerabilities to its list of actively exploited security threats**

**Key points**

*   **Apple devices:** Three vulnerabilities in Apple software are being used by an exploit kit called "DarkSword" to deliver malware to users.
*   **Craft CMS breaches:** Hackers are chaining two vulnerabilities in Craft CMS and the Yii framework to break into servers and steal data.
*   **State-sponsored threats:** A vulnerability linked to the Iranian-backed hacking group "MuddyWater"—known for targeting government, energy, and telecommunications sectors—has been added to the list.
*   **Mandatory fixes:** U.S. federal agencies are now legally required to patch these specific vulnerabilities by April 3, 2026.
*   **Patches available:** All mentioned vulnerabilities have been addressed by their respective developers; users should ensure their software is updated to the latest versions.

**Why it matters**
By adding these flaws to its "Known Exploited Vulnerabilities" catalog, CISA is signaling that these security gaps are actively being used by criminals and state-sponsored hackers to compromise systems. Organizations and individuals should prioritize updating their software to prevent unauthorized access and data theft.
