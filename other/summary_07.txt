From Festivals to Streamers, Political Documentaries Are Getting Shut Out
============================================================

**Documentary filmmakers are struggling to find U.S. buyers for politically sensitive or independent projects, even when those films are successful internationally.**

**Key points**

*   **Market Disappearance:** Producers report that the U.S. market for documentaries has effectively stalled, with major streamers and distributors increasingly reluctant to acquire films that challenge powerful political or corporate interests.
*   **The "Tesla" Case Study:** A documentary investigating Tesla’s Autopilot technology has secured distribution in 20 countries but remains unsold in the U.S., with producers citing fears of political retaliation as a primary barrier.
*   **Festival Hurdles:** While major festivals like Sundance and SXSW still screen some political content, filmmakers note a decline in the programming of hard-hitting domestic political documentaries.
*   **The "IP" Problem:** Beyond politics, independent documentaries that lack "big-name" celebrity subjects or established intellectual property are finding it nearly impossible to secure U.S. distribution, despite strong critical acclaim.
*   **Audience Disconnect:** Industry experts warn that distributors are ignoring a clear public appetite for socially and politically engaged content, potentially alienating younger viewers who are eager for these stories.

**Why it matters**
The reluctance of major platforms to distribute challenging or independent documentaries threatens to narrow the scope of public discourse in the U.S. By avoiding controversial topics, streamers may be effectively silencing vital investigative journalism and limiting the diversity of stories available to American audiences.
