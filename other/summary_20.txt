Irish firms will have greater freedom to work with military under plan to cut ‘red tape’
============================================================

**Government to remove red tape for Irish businesses working in the defence sector**

**Key points**
* The government plans to scrap a 1987 law that requires full Cabinet approval before state agencies can support companies working on military-related projects.
* The change aims to help small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) access support from agencies like Enterprise Ireland and the IDA.
* Officials state the current law is outdated and fails to account for modern technology, such as AI, cybersecurity, and space systems, which often have both civilian and military uses.
* Removing these restrictions will allow Irish firms to compete for significant European funding, including a share of the upcoming €409 billion European Competitiveness Fund.
* The government emphasized that this move is purely economic and does not change Ireland’s official defence or neutrality policy.

**Why it matters**
This reform allows Irish tech companies to participate in the growing European defence market, ensuring they aren't locked out of major funding opportunities due to bureaucratic hurdles.
