Microsoft outlines agentic AI security strategy with new Defender, Entra and Purview capabilities
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**

Microsoft has unveiled a comprehensive security strategy designed to govern and protect the growing use of autonomous AI agents within enterprise environments.

**Key points**

*   **New Governance Framework:** Microsoft is introducing "Agent 365," a centralized control plane that allows IT and security teams to monitor, manage, and govern AI agents across their entire organization.
*   **Enhanced Visibility:** New tools, such as the "Security Dashboard for AI" and "Shadow AI Detection," help companies identify where AI is being used and spot potential risks from unmanaged or unauthorized AI applications.
*   **Data and Identity Protection:** Updates to Microsoft Purview and Entra provide stricter controls to prevent sensitive data from being leaked into AI prompts and strengthen identity security through automated backups and advanced authentication.
*   **AI-Powered Defense:** Microsoft is integrating its own AI security agents into its existing platforms (Defender, Entra, and Purview) to help security teams automatically detect, prioritize, and respond to threats faster.
*   **Shift in Strategy:** Microsoft is moving away from treating AI as a standard application, instead positioning it as a new, core security layer that requires its own dedicated infrastructure and oversight.

**Why it matters**

As businesses increasingly rely on autonomous AI agents to perform complex tasks, these systems create new security vulnerabilities that traditional software protections cannot address. Microsoft’s strategy aims to provide a unified "safety net" to ensure that as AI takes on more responsibility, it does not become a gateway for data breaches or cyberattacks.
