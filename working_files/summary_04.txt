Should you wait for the Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 8?
============================================================

Consumers weighing a purchase of the upcoming Samsung Galaxy Z Flip 8 must balance potential performance upgrades against expected price hikes driven by current industry-wide component shortages.

### Key Points
- The Galaxy Z Flip 8 is expected to feature the Exynos 2600 chipset, offering up to 39% faster CPU performance than the previous generation.
- Samsung is rumored to pair the new processor with 12GB of RAM and storage options of 256GB or 512GB.
- The device will launch with Android 17 and is slated to receive seven years of guaranteed operating system updates.
- Industry trends suggest a potential price increase of $100 to $200, following similar hikes seen in the Motorola Razr 2026 and Galaxy S26 lineups.
- A potential Galaxy Unpacked event is rumored for July 22, 2026, in London, with retail availability expected shortly thereafter.

### Why it Matters
The decision to wait for the Galaxy Z Flip 8 involves navigating a volatile smartphone market where rising component costs are increasingly passed on to consumers. While the new model promises significant hardware and software longevity, buyers must determine if these advancements justify a potential premium over current-generation devices.
