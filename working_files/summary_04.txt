Robot Dogs, Teslas, and Rescue Helicopters: The UN AI Summit Was a Lot
============================================================

The United Nations’ 10th annual AI for Good summit in Geneva gathered global leaders to debate how artificial intelligence can address humanitarian challenges while mitigating systemic risks.

### Key Points
- The International Telecommunication Union organized the event to explore AI applications for global issues like hunger, disease, and climate change.
- Critics, including representatives from Access Now, warned against an overreliance on big tech companies and the lack of transparency in proprietary AI systems.
- Pro-Palestine activists disrupted a keynote by Amazon CTO Werner Vogels to protest the company’s alleged role in the conflict in Gaza.
- Experts highlighted a growing digital divide, noting that limited access to compute power and English-centric models may exclude developing nations from the AI economy.
- A new 44-member commission, cochaired by Rwandan President Paul Kagame and Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff, was formed to foster international cooperation on AI governance.

### Why it Matters
The summit highlights the tension between the idealistic goal of using AI for global development and the reality of corporate dominance and geopolitical competition. As technology advances faster than regulatory consensus, the event underscores the urgent need to translate human rights principles into enforceable technical standards.
