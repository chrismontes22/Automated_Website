A Script for Mark Zuckerberg
============================================================

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg outlines a strategic pivot toward AI-driven advertising, justifying massive capital expenditures as essential for future growth and long-term dominance in the digital entertainment market.

### Key Points
- Meta plans to monetize AI infrastructure by renting out excess compute capacity on the spot market to establish a disciplined hurdle rate for internal investments.
- Zuckerberg identifies past strategic errors, including an over-obsession with building platforms and the heavy, underperforming investment in Reality Labs.
- The company is shifting its focus toward AI-enhanced advertising, aiming to use machine learning to predict content and ad preferences for users.
- Meta intends to leverage its massive inventory expansion to drive revenue, comparing the current AI opportunity to previous growth cycles seen with Stories and Reels.
- The strategy explicitly avoids competing with productivity-focused AI firms like OpenAI, focusing instead on human connection, entertainment, and commerce.

### Why it Matters
This strategic shift signals a major transition for Meta as it moves away from platform-building experiments to prioritize its core, high-margin advertising business. By tying compute investments to market-validated returns, the company aims to restore investor confidence while securing its competitive position against existential threats in the digital landscape.
