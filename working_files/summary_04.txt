The Samsung Galaxy S26 is a solid phone that desperately needs a camera upgrade
============================================================

The Samsung Galaxy S26 features a larger 6.3-inch display and the powerful Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 processor, but faces criticism for its $899 starting price and stagnant hardware.

### Key Points
- The device features a 6.3-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X display and a 4,300mAh battery.
- Performance is driven by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset paired with 12GB of RAM.
- The camera system remains unchanged from the Galaxy S22 series, lacking significant hardware upgrades.
- The phone lacks modern connectivity standards like mmWave 5G, UWB, and Bluetooth 6.
- Charging speeds are limited to 25W wired and 15W wireless, with no integrated Qi2 magnetic support.
- The starting price of $899.99 represents a $100 increase over the previous generation's launch price.

### Why it Matters
The Galaxy S26 marks a shift away from its historical identity as an affordable, compact flagship by increasing both its physical footprint and its retail price. This positioning makes it a difficult recommendation for consumers, as the device lacks the premium connectivity and camera improvements found in higher-tier models or competing smartphones.
