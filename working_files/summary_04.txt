Europe Is Fed Up and Wants Its Own AI
============================================================

European leaders and tech executives are aggressively pursuing AI sovereignty to reduce reliance on American models, driven by concerns over US export controls and shifting geopolitical trade policies.

### Key Points
- French President Emmanuel Macron is spearheading efforts to secure over 100 billion euros in infrastructure investment, including a major data center project backed by Softbank.
- Canadian firm Cohere is forming strategic partnerships with European companies like Germany’s Aleph Alpha to build a decentralized, sovereign-first AI supply chain.
- AI pioneer Yann LeCun is leading Project Tapestry, an initiative aimed at developing an open-source foundation model that allows nations to integrate their own cultural and political values.
- Recent US export restrictions on Anthropic’s Claude Fable model served as a catalyst, convincing European firms that relying on American technology poses a significant business risk.
- Industry experts note that restrictive US immigration and trade policies are encouraging top-tier foreign researchers to consider returning to Europe to build independent AI ecosystems.

### Why it Matters
The push for AI sovereignty represents a fundamental shift in the global technology landscape as nations move to protect their digital infrastructure from foreign political interference. If successful, this movement could break the current American hegemony in artificial intelligence and create a more fragmented, yet competitive, international market for frontier models.
