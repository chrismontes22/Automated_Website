10 Monday AM Reads
============================================================

Major U.S. stock indices recently achieved their strongest monthly performance since 2020, defying traditional "sell in May" market trends despite ongoing concerns regarding inflation and rising household living costs.

### Key Points
- The S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite gained 10% and 15% respectively in April, marking their best monthly performance in four years.
- Approximately 1 in 6 American households now holds a net worth exceeding $1 million, though many report a disconnect between paper wealth and actual financial security.
- Homeowners association (HOA) fees have surged 26% since 2019, often outpacing wage growth and creating significant affordability challenges for property owners.
- BlackBerry has returned to profitability by embedding its software into 275 million vehicles, maintaining a significant presence in the automotive industry.
- The "Enhanced Games," a competition allowing performance-enhancing drugs, is gaining attention through backing from high-profile figures including Peter Thiel and Donald Trump Jr.

### Why it Matters
These trends highlight a complex economic landscape where strong equity market returns contrast with the rising cost of living and stagnant consumer sentiment. Understanding these shifts is essential for investors and households navigating the intersection of asset-price inflation, corporate innovation, and changing housing affordability.
