The Clapper was a bad smart home gadget — and a viral sensation
============================================================

The latest episode of The Verge’s Version History podcast explores the cultural legacy and technical shortcomings of The Clapper, the iconic sound-activated home automation device from the 1980s.

### Key Points
- The Clapper, originally marketed as The Great American Turn-On, became a viral sensation despite frequent malfunctions and potential hardware damage.
- Podcast hosts David Pierce and Victoria Song analyze the device's history with University of South Carolina professor Allison Marsh.
- This episode marks the sixth and final installment of the fourth season of the Version History series.
- Previous episodes in the season covered the development of the Roomba, Nest thermostat, Keurig coffee maker, and Philips Hue smart lights.

### Why it Matters
The Clapper serves as a foundational case study in how effective advertising can drive mass-market adoption for flawed consumer technology. Its enduring popularity highlights the early consumer appetite for home automation long before the modern era of smart devices.
