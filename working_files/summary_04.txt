The CEO using AI to double revenue with 1,000 fewer hires: ‘Nobody’s going to replace the last mile’
============================================================

Pennsylvania-based home remodeling company West Shore Home is leveraging artificial intelligence to streamline operations, achieving over $1 billion in revenue while significantly reducing its total workforce requirements.

### Key Points
- Founder B.J. Werzyn built West Shore Home into a billion-dollar business by digitizing traditional remodeling processes like permitting, inventory, and scheduling.
- The company currently employs over 3,200 staff members, including 1,200 installers and 650 design consultants, across multiple regional markets.
- West Shore Home has completed more than 334,000 installations since its founding in 2006.
- Private equity firm Leonard Green & Partners acquired a 20% stake in the company in 2020, with Werzyn retaining 80% ownership.
- The business reported $933 million in gross revenue for 2025 and is currently operating at a $1.15 billion annual revenue pace.

### Why it Matters
This transition demonstrates how AI can modernize labor-intensive, traditional industries by replacing manual administrative tasks with automated systems. It highlights a growing trend where physical service companies achieve significant scale and efficiency by integrating software-driven operational models.
