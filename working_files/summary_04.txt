SpaceX IPO: Inside the S-1 filing, potential valuation, and what Elon Musk's empire means for the market
============================================================

SpaceX has officially filed its S-1 registration statement, signaling a historic initial public offering that highlights the company's massive AI investments and long-term ambitions for interplanetary colonization.

### Key Points
- SpaceX reported $18.7 billion in 2025 revenue alongside a $4.9 billion loss driven by heavy investment in AI infrastructure.
- The company holds approximately 19,000 bitcoins valued at $1.5 billion and maintains a $1.25 billion monthly contract with Anthropic for data center access.
- Elon Musk will retain over 85% of voting power while serving as CEO, CTO, and board chairman following the public listing.
- The filing details $660 million in transactions between SpaceX and other Musk-owned entities, including Tesla, X, and The Boring Company.
- SpaceX plans to trade on the Nasdaq under the ticker "SPCX" and is expected to qualify for rapid inclusion in the Nasdaq 100 index.

### Why it Matters
This IPO represents a significant shift in the aerospace and technology sectors by positioning SpaceX as a diversified AI and data infrastructure provider rather than just a launch company. The offering's massive scale and unique governance structure will likely influence market trends and set a new benchmark for future high-profile tech listings.
