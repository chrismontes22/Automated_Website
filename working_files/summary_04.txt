All the Ways Europe Is Ditching American Technology
============================================================

European governments and organizations are increasingly abandoning American technology providers in favor of local and open-source alternatives to secure digital sovereignty amid shifting geopolitical tensions and policy concerns.

### Key Points
- The European Commission and European Parliament have launched initiatives to reduce reliance on US firms, including switching default search engines to the French service Qwant.
- French government agencies are deploying "LaSuite," an open-source office software, while cities in the Netherlands, France, and Germany are migrating away from Microsoft and Google products.
- The Dutch government is transitioning its code repositories from Microsoft-owned GitHub to independent platforms.
- Finland and Belgium have moved critical infrastructure and domain management away from Amazon Web Services (AWS) and other US-based cloud providers.
- Motivations for these shifts include concerns over the US CLOUD Act, data privacy, and the influence of the Trump administration on international technology policy.

### Why it Matters
This trend represents a significant challenge to the dominance of US Big Tech firms within the European digital infrastructure market. By prioritizing digital sovereignty, European entities aim to mitigate risks associated with foreign data control and geopolitical instability, potentially reshaping the global technology landscape.
