Tech execs are hoping you forget all that 'AI is going to kill jobs' stuff
============================================================

Leading artificial intelligence executives, including Sam Altman and Dario Amodei, are shifting their public messaging from apocalyptic job-loss warnings to optimistic narratives as their companies pursue major public offerings.

### Key Points
- OpenAI CEO Sam Altman and Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei have recently walked back previous claims that AI would rapidly eliminate large swaths of white-collar jobs.
- Anthropic filed a confidential S-1 registration statement for an initial public offering, signaling a transition toward courting retail investors and public markets.
- Public sentiment toward AI has soured, with recent polling showing negative favorability ratings and growing anxiety among Gen Z workers regarding employment stability.
- Despite industry rhetoric, official data shows the U.S. unemployment rate has only risen slightly from 3.9% to 4.3% since April 2024, contradicting claims of mass displacement.
- Experts suggest the rhetorical pivot is a strategic response to changing audiences, as companies move from impressing tech enthusiasts to satisfying skeptical investors and regulators.

### Why it Matters
The sudden shift in tone highlights how AI leadership is attempting to rehabilitate the industry's image to secure long-term financial viability and public trust. By moving away from "doomerism," these companies hope to mitigate regulatory backlash and consumer hostility that could threaten their path to successful public market debuts.
