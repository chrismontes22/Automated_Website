U.S. says it seized about $1 billion in Iranian crypto as pressure campaign expands
============================================================

The United States government has seized approximately $1 billion in cryptocurrency linked to Iran as part of Operation Economic Fury, an initiative designed to disrupt Tehran's global funding channels.

### Key Points
- Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent confirmed the seizure of digital assets to restrict Iran's access to banking networks and revenue.
- Operation Economic Fury targets shadow banking networks, weapons supply chains, and illicit oil sales facilitated by Iran-backed militias.
- U.S. officials report that the pressure campaign has contributed to Iran's economic instability, including inflation rates exceeding 200%.
- The Treasury is also actively targeting overseas real estate and other assets allegedly diverted from the Iranian public by government officials.

### Why it Matters
This enforcement action highlights the increasing use of digital asset tracking to dismantle state-sponsored financial networks and enforce international sanctions. By targeting crypto infrastructure, the U.S. aims to limit the regime's ability to bypass traditional banking systems and fund military operations.
