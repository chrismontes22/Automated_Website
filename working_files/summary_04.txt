Big Tech’s desperate last push at AI regulation
============================================================

Big Tech lobbyists are pushing for federal AI preemption legislation, but the effort faces significant hurdles as the White House attempts to link the policy with children's safety laws.

### Key Points
- Tech companies are seeking a comprehensive federal law to override fragmented state-level AI regulations before the upcoming congressional election cycle.
- The White House is attempting to bundle AI preemption with the Kids Online Safety Act (KOSA) to secure broader legislative support.
- Negotiations have stalled due to disagreements between the House and Senate versions of KOSA and a lack of coordination with key lawmakers.
- Trump-allied lawyer Mike Davis is demanding that any final bill must address his "Four Cs" framework: children, conservatives, creators, and communities.
- Legislative time is limited, with the congressional calendar crowded by other priorities like defense spending, immigration, and the upcoming general election.

### Why it Matters
Linking AI preemption to child safety creates a complex trade-off for tech companies that must decide if federal regulatory consistency is worth accepting stricter "duty of care" mandates. The failure to align House and Senate leadership suggests that a comprehensive AI framework remains unlikely before the current legislative session concludes.
