Some of the nation’s rich are letting AI teach their kids
============================================================

Wealthy American families are increasingly enrolling their children in private schools like Alpha School and Forge Prep that utilize unproven AI-driven curricula and interactive technology for student instruction.

### Key Points
- Alpha School and Forge Prep charge families tens of thousands of dollars annually for AI-integrated educational programs.
- Venture capitalist Shaun Johnson is paying $75,000 for his son to attend an Alpha Kindergarten program in San Francisco.
- Alpha School cofounder MacKenzie Price stated that the curriculum intentionally excludes discussions regarding sensitive social and political issues.
- These private institutions currently provide no public performance metrics or empirical evidence to demonstrate improved educational outcomes for students.

### Why it Matters
This trend highlights a growing shift among affluent parents toward experimental, technology-first education models that bypass traditional pedagogical standards. The lack of transparency regarding student progress and curriculum content raises significant questions about the long-term efficacy and societal impact of AI-led schooling.
