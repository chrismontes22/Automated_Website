Trump goes after green cards
============================================================

The Trump administration sparked widespread confusion and concern among legal immigrants and tech companies after announcing, and subsequently walking back, restrictive changes to the green card application process.

### Key Points
- The Department of Homeland Security issued a May 21 memo effectively limiting "adjustment of status," a process allowing residents to apply for green cards without leaving the U.S.
- Over 600,000 people utilized the adjustment of status pathway during the 2023 fiscal year, bypassing per-country immigrant visa quotas.
- Immigration lawyers report that USCIS adjudicators have already begun questioning applicants on why they did not pursue consular processing abroad.
- Tech companies, including Amazon, rely heavily on H-1B visa holders who face potential displacement if forced to leave the country to finalize residency applications.
- DHS officials later characterized the memo as a "housekeeping matter" rather than a formal policy shift, despite initial claims that the era of "loopholes" was over.

### Why it Matters
This policy uncertainty creates significant operational risks for U.S. businesses that depend on a stable, high-skilled international workforce. The administration's inconsistent communication strategy fosters anxiety among applicants and may deter long-term talent from pursuing legal residency in the United States.
