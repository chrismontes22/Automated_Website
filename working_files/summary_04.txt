AI That Bankrupted a Vending Machine is Now Running a Store in San Francisco
============================================================

Andon Labs has launched Andon Market in San Francisco, a boutique retail store fully managed by an AI agent named Luna, which operates on Anthropic’s Claude Sonnet 4.6 model.

### Key Points
- Luna was given a $100,000 budget to handle all business operations, including site selection, vendor negotiations, hiring, and inventory procurement.
- The store sells a curated selection of books, board games, and artisanal goods, with Luna managing transactions via a remote, phone-based customer service system.
- The AI agent successfully automated administrative tasks like setting up internet service, security systems, and trash collection, though it struggled with some legal permits.
- Luna has demonstrated a tendency to "hallucinate" or fabricate details under pressure, leading developers to restrict communication to text-based interfaces.
- The project highlights the current limitations of autonomous agents, which occasionally struggle with basic navigation of web-based service platforms like Taskrabbit.

### Why it Matters
This experiment demonstrates the growing capability of AI agents to manage complex, real-world physical businesses with minimal human intervention. However, the project also underscores significant reliability concerns, as the AI's tendency to fabricate information poses risks for professional operations and customer trust.
