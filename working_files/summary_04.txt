U.S. Grants UAE Expanded Access to Advanced AI Technology After Support in Iran Conflict
============================================================

The United States has granted the United Arab Emirates expedited access to sensitive military and AI technologies, citing the nation's strategic security contributions and regional defense cooperation.

### Key Points
- The UAE now receives the same streamlined export licensing for sensitive U.S. technology as South Korea, India, and several European allies.
- Emirati AI firm G42 can now purchase advanced Nvidia chips without standard federal review processes.
- The policy change follows the UAE's participation in Operation Epic Fury and its role in intercepting Iranian missiles during recent regional conflicts.
- U.S. tech giants Microsoft and OpenAI are planning to construct major data centers within the UAE as part of this expanded partnership.
- Defense firms Anduril Industries and EDGE Group launched a joint venture in November 2025 to develop AI-powered autonomous drones in Abu Dhabi.
- Lawmakers have raised concerns regarding potential conflicts of interest due to the UAE's significant financial investments in Trump-linked business entities.

### Why it Matters
This policy shift marks a significant consolidation of the U.S.-led "Pax Silica" initiative, which aims to secure global AI supply chains and limit Chinese influence. While the administration frames the move as a national security necessity, the integration of sensitive technology with private financial interests has sparked intense debate regarding transparency and geopolitical ethics.
