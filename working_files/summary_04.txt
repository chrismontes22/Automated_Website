The FCC is planning to retroactively ban disguised DJI gadgets
============================================================

The Federal Communications Commission is initiating a retroactive ban on several companies suspected of disguising DJI technology to bypass existing U.S. import restrictions on Chinese-manufactured drones and cameras.

### Key Points
- The FCC is targeting eight companies, including Xtra Technology and the entities behind Skyrover drones, for allegedly circumventing national security bans.
- Proposed actions include prohibiting the import, distribution, and sale of existing products, such as the Xtra version of the DJI Osmo Pocket 3.
- The FCC has temporarily deferred the grantee codes for these companies, effectively halting their ability to authorize new equipment for the U.S. market.
- The agency is also severing ties with SGS-CSTC Shenzhen, a Chinese testing lab accused of failing to meet U.S. ownership and control standards.
- A 30-day public comment period is currently open, during which the affected companies may provide evidence to contest the proposed bans.

### Why it Matters
This move marks the first time the FCC has utilized its authority to retroactively revoke equipment approvals based on national security concerns. The decision could force major retailers to pull popular electronics from shelves and sets a significant precedent for how the U.S. government regulates foreign-made technology already present in the domestic supply chain.
