I designed cybersecurity content for a bank. I still almost got roped into two scams when job hunting.
============================================================

New York City graphic designer Julius von Brunk shares his experience navigating sophisticated recruitment scams on LinkedIn while searching for employment in a highly competitive and challenging job market.

### Key Points
- Julius von Brunk, a graphic designer with 16 years of experience, encountered two distinct job scams while searching for new employment.
- The first scam involved an impersonator requesting a $100 fee for a "professional résumé review" to secure a fake referral link.
- The second scam featured a fraudulent "MetaCareers" LinkedIn page that directed users to a phishing site designed to steal Facebook login credentials.
- Von Brunk identified the phishing site by checking domain registry records, which revealed the site was purchased only one day prior.
- LinkedIn removed both fraudulent listings after the designer flagged the accounts and reported the suspicious activity to the platform.

### Why it Matters
These incidents highlight the increasing prevalence of sophisticated recruitment fraud targeting job seekers who are often vulnerable due to a difficult economic climate. As scammers leverage AI and impersonation to steal personal data or money, job seekers must remain vigilant by verifying recruiter credentials and avoiding external payment requests.
