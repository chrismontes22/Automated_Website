Elon Musk and the plot to hijack America’s broadband
============================================================

The Trump administration’s restructuring of the $42.45 billion BEAD broadband program has prioritized satellite providers like Starlink, causing significant delays and undermining efforts to expand high-speed fiber infrastructure.

### Key Points
- The Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment (BEAD) program, established in 2021, aimed to provide affordable, future-proof fiber internet to underserved U.S. communities by 2030.
- Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick’s "technology neutral" policy shift redirected hundreds of millions in subsidies toward Elon Musk’s SpaceX and Jeff Bezos’ Amazon.
- Revised federal guidelines forced states to retool plans, resulting in the cancellation of fiber projects in areas like Lake Providence, Louisiana.
- As of June 2026, fewer than a few hundred homes have been connected via BEAD, and 33 states have yet to receive confirmation of their grant awards.
- Several internet service providers in states like Nebraska have abandoned the program entirely due to bureaucratic confusion and shifting federal requirements.

### Why it Matters
The redirection of funds toward satellite services over fiber infrastructure threatens to leave rural and low-income communities with less reliable, more expensive internet options while failing to generate local economic growth. This shift effectively transforms a bipartisan infrastructure initiative into a vehicle for corporate subsidies, potentially stalling the nation's progress in closing the digital divide for years.
