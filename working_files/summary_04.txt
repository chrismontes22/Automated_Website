AI won’t save advertising, says Digitas’ Amy Lanzi
============================================================

Digitas North America CEO Amy Lanzi argues that the advertising industry must move beyond AI hype to focus on building sustainable marketing systems that drive measurable business growth.

### Key Points
- Amy Lanzi, CEO of Digitas North America, warns that overpromising on AI capabilities creates long-term risks for the advertising industry.
- The agency is restructuring its leadership to include roles like Chief Intelligence Officer and Chief Systems Officer to better integrate data and technology.
- Digitas acquired the creator-focused firm Influential in 2024 to better analyze and scale the creator economy as a formal marketing channel.
- Lanzi asserts that the traditional Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) role is becoming obsolete, shifting toward a Chief Growth Officer (CGO) model focused on commercial value.
- The agency emphasizes that while AI can improve workflows, human-led storytelling and brand fundamentals remain essential for authentic consumer connection.

### Why it Matters
The shift from traditional campaign-based marketing to data-driven "growth engines" reflects a broader industry transition toward systems-based advertising. As tech platforms increasingly automate creative output, agencies are prioritizing data ownership and creator integration to maintain brand identity and long-term business results.
