The startup looking to solve health care’s fax machine problem
============================================================

Health tech startup Insight Health has secured $11 million in Series A funding to deploy AI assistants that automate administrative tasks and reduce reliance on traditional medical faxing.

### Key Points
- Insight Health raised $11 million in a Series A round led by Standard Capital, with participation from Pear VC, Kindred Ventures, and ElevenLabs.
- The company’s AI assistant, Lumi, automates patient intake, referral processing, and scheduling to reduce administrative burdens on clinical staff.
- Pilot programs indicate that AI-led patient encounters can save 15 to 20 minutes per visit, helping clinics address significant appointment backlogs.
- Insight was cofounded in 2023 by Jaimal Soni, Dr. Eric Stecker, Dr. Pankaj Gore, and Saran Siva to address systemic inefficiencies in the U.S. healthcare sector.

### Why it Matters
Administrative costs in the U.S. healthcare industry reach an estimated $1 trillion annually, contributing significantly to physician burnout and operational inefficiencies. By automating routine paperwork, AI platforms aim to reduce medical errors and allow clinicians to dedicate more time to direct patient care.
