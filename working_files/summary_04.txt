After resurrecting an iconic PC brand, Commodore is getting into flip phones
============================================================

Retro gaming enthusiast Christian Simpson has revived the Commodore brand, launching a nostalgia-focused flip phone called the Callback 8020 designed to limit digital distractions and social media usage.

### Key Points
- The Callback 8020 is a flip phone featuring a 3.25-inch screen, MediaTek Helio G81 processor, 4GB of RAM, and 64GB of storage.
- The device runs on Jolla’s Sailfish operating system and blocks social media and web browsers by default to reduce screen time.
- Users can request specific Android apps through an allow-listing system, which uses AI and human review to maintain the phone's minimalist focus.
- Hardware features include a 48-megapixel camera, an FM radio antenna, a headphone jack, and an audiophile-grade DAC.
- Pricing starts at $499 for standard colors, with a translucent blue model at $549.99 and a gold "Founders Edition" at $640.
- Commodore plans to begin shipping the devices by the end of 2026, following the successful sale of 30,000 retro-styled Commodore 64 units.

### Why it Matters
The Callback 8020 taps into the growing "dumb phone" market by offering a hardware-based solution for users seeking to disconnect from constant digital notifications. By blending Y2K-era aesthetics with modern privacy-focused software, Commodore is positioning itself as a lifestyle brand for consumers looking to reclaim their attention from mainstream smartphone ecosystems.
