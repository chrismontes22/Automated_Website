Apple reveals price hikes for MacBooks and iPads
============================================================

Apple has increased the starting prices of its Mac, iPad, and home device lineups by hundreds of dollars, citing ongoing global shortages in memory and storage components.

### Key Points
- The M3 Ultra Mac Studio saw the largest increase, rising $1,300 to a new starting price of $5,299.
- MacBook Pro models experienced price hikes ranging from $300 to $500 depending on the specific configuration.
- iPad and iPad Air models increased by $100 to $150, while the Vision Pro rose to $3,699.
- Surging demand for RAM and SSDs from AI data centers has constrained supply for major manufacturers like Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron.
- Apple CEO Tim Cook stated that the company could no longer shield consumers from the unsustainable costs of these hardware components.

### Why it Matters
These widespread price hikes reflect a broader industry trend as tech companies struggle to secure essential memory components amid intense competition from the artificial intelligence sector. Consumers should expect these elevated costs to persist as supply chain experts predict that the global hardware shortage will continue for several years.
