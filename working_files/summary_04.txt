Federal Judge Allows Search of ChatGPT Records in Crypto Fraud Case
============================================================

A federal judge has ruled that prosecutors can access Richard Kim’s ChatGPT records, establishing that AI chat logs are subject to search warrants in ongoing criminal fraud cases.

### Key Points
- US District Judge Lorna Schofield ordered OpenAI to provide account records, including prompts and responses, for former Zero Edge CEO Richard Kim.
- Prosecutors allege Kim misappropriated $3.8 million in investor funds and used ChatGPT to research trial strategies and gambling activities.
- The defense unsuccessfully argued that the chatbot data should be protected by attorney-client privilege and work-product doctrines.
- The ruling confirms that AI platforms are considered third-party data collectors rather than legal counsel, making their logs discoverable in court.
- This decision follows a similar precedent in *United States v. Heppner*, where a judge ruled that exchanges with Anthropic’s Claude were not legally privileged.

### Why it Matters
This ruling clarifies that conversations with AI chatbots are not inherently private and can be treated as discoverable evidence similar to emails or text messages. It serves as a significant warning that using AI for legal research does not grant the same confidentiality protections afforded to communications with a licensed attorney.
