Bitcoin pressured by Fed uncertainty, oil, and AI slowdown
============================================================

Bitcoin is trading near $77,000 as investors weigh persistent inflation concerns driven by high oil prices against potential shifts in AI demand and upcoming Federal Reserve policy decisions.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin prices have declined approximately 3% as markets await critical U.S. economic data, including GDP, PCE inflation, and the Employment Cost Index.
- Brent crude oil prices exceeding $100 per barrel are complicating the inflation outlook and reducing expectations for near-term Federal Reserve interest rate cuts.
- Bitcoin is currently trading below the $80,700 technical resistance level, which serves as a key indicator for marginal buyer conviction.
- OpenAI missing revenue targets has raised concerns about the sustainability of AI demand, potentially impacting bitcoin miners who have pivoted toward AI data center hosting.
- A potential slowdown in AI infrastructure expansion could eventually reduce bitcoin selling pressure from miners, though broader market volatility remains a significant risk.

### Why it Matters
The current market environment reflects a complex intersection of macroeconomic uncertainty and shifting corporate strategies in the technology sector. Investors are closely monitoring these variables to determine if bitcoin can overcome technical resistance or if inflationary pressures will continue to suppress risk assets.
