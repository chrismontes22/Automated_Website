Are prices really dropping in the US, as Trump claims?
============================================================

President Donald Trump is touting economic growth and lower prices following a US-Iran peace deal, though analysts note that inflation and labor market challenges persist for many Americans.

### Key Points
- The Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a record high of 51,999.67 on Tuesday before fluctuating following a Federal Reserve interest rate decision.
- Average petrol prices dropped to $3.99 per gallon, though experts warn that supply chain bottlenecks will keep costs elevated through late 2027.
- US consumer inflation remains at 4.2 percent, with Kroger announcing price cuts across its 3,000 stores to combat shifting consumer spending habits.
- The US economy added 172,000 jobs in May, while layoffs rose 16 percent to 97,000, with artificial intelligence cited as a primary factor in job losses.
- Crude oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz are resuming, with Brent crude futures falling to $76.51 per barrel following the ceasefire agreement.

### Why it Matters
While stock market gains and stabilizing oil prices suggest a positive shift, the disconnect between financial indices and the daily cost of living remains a significant concern for households. Persistent inflation and a cooling labor market indicate that the broader economic recovery will likely be a slow process for the average consumer.
