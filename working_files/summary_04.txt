Trump made more than $1bn from crypto in first year back in office
============================================================

President Donald Trump reported over $1.4 billion in cryptocurrency-related earnings last year, according to a mandatory financial disclosure that highlights his significant business interests in the digital asset sector.

### Key Points
- Trump earned $635 million in royalties from Celebration Coins and over $500 million from World Liberty Financial, a firm founded by his sons and associates.
- Total income for the year reached at least $2.2 billion, with crypto earnings significantly outpacing revenue from his traditional real estate and golf club holdings.
- The White House maintains that Trump’s assets are managed by a trust and denies any conflict of interest regarding his presidential duties.
- Under the current administration, the Securities and Exchange Commission has shifted toward a more crypto-friendly regulatory approach under chair Paul Atkins.
- The president signed the GENIUS Act into law, aiming to establish the United States as a global leader in digital assets.

### Why it Matters
The scale of these earnings raises significant questions regarding the intersection of presidential policy and personal financial gain in the emerging cryptocurrency market. This approach marks a departure from the practices of previous presidents who typically utilized blind trusts to avoid potential conflicts of interest.
