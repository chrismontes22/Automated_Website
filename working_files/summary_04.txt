Galaxy Ring 2 rumors say no to 2026, point us toward 'an early' 2027 reveal
============================================================

Samsung may delay the release of the Galaxy Ring 2 until early 2027 as the company focuses on improving battery life, sensor accuracy, and overall device comfort.

### Key Points
- Reports suggest Samsung will skip a 2026 launch, targeting an early 2027 debut at the Galaxy Unpacked event.
- Development efforts are focused on enhancing sleep tracking accuracy, temperature sensors, and cardiovascular health insights.
- Engineers are exploring "dream battery" solid-state technology to increase energy density from 200 to 360Wh/L.
- The company is redesigning internal component placement to create a thinner, more comfortable, and practical wearable form factor.
- Long-term research into non-invasive blood glucose monitoring remains a potential, though unconfirmed, feature for the new hardware.

### Why it Matters
The extended development timeline reflects Samsung's strategy to prioritize hardware refinement and advanced health features over a rapid release cycle. This delay highlights the technical challenges of miniaturizing complex sensors and battery technology while maintaining the durability required for a competitive smart ring market.
