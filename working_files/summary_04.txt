San Francisco homebuyers have officially lost their minds
============================================================

San Francisco has reclaimed its status as the nation's most expensive housing market as an AI-fueled wealth boom triggers intense bidding wars and record-breaking home price appreciation.

### Key Points
- San Francisco home prices have surged 15% over the past year, the highest growth rate among major U.S. cities.
- Inventory remains critically low, with only about 250 single-family homes currently available for sale in the city.
- Luxury properties are frequently selling for millions of dollars above their asking prices, with some homes trading for double their list price.
- The city recently recorded its first month of positive net domestic in-migration since 2018, reversing pandemic-era population losses.
- Real estate agents report that buyers are increasingly using equity from companies like OpenAI and Anthropic to fund aggressive, all-cash offers.

### Why it Matters
The rapid escalation of San Francisco home values highlights a growing K-shaped economic divide where high-earning tech workers drive luxury prices while entry-level markets remain stagnant. This "boom loop" serves as a localized case study for how concentrated wealth from the AI sector can rapidly reshape urban real estate dynamics and affordability.
