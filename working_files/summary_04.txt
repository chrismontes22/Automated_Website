Google Security Engineer Arrested in Million-Dollar Polymarket Trading Scheme
============================================================

Google security engineer Michele Spagnuolo was arrested in New York on fraud and money laundering charges for allegedly using confidential internal data to place profitable trades on Polymarket.

### Key Points
- Michele Spagnuolo, a Google employee based in Zurich, faces charges of commodities fraud, wire fraud, and money laundering.
- Prosecutors allege Spagnuolo used internal Google data to win $1.2 million on a Polymarket bet regarding the company's most-searched person of 2025.
- The Commodity Futures Trading Commission has filed a parallel civil complaint against Spagnuolo for insider trading.
- Polymarket stated it referred the activity to authorities, noting that blockchain transparency helped identify the suspect's account, "AlphaRaccoon."
- This case marks the first time a tech worker has been arrested for alleged insider trading on a prediction market platform.

### Why it Matters
This arrest highlights the growing regulatory scrutiny surrounding prediction markets as lawmakers and federal agencies increase efforts to combat market manipulation. It underscores the significant legal risks for employees who leverage proprietary corporate data for personal financial gain in emerging digital betting sectors.
