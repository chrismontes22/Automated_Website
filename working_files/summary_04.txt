Will Sensex, Nifty extend gains on Monday? Q1 earnings, global tech selloff among 6 factors to steer D-St this week
============================================================

Indian stock markets showed resilience with Sensex and Nifty 50 posting weekly gains despite global tech selloffs, rising crude oil prices, and escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.

### Key Points
- The Sensex rose 582 points to close the week, while the Nifty 50 settled at 24,334.
- Brent crude prices surged 16% for the week, reaching $88.10 per barrel due to the US-Iran conflict.
- Foreign institutional investors (FIIs) were net sellers, withdrawing Rs 8,743.35 crore from Indian equities.
- The Indian rupee weakened to 96.28 against the US dollar, marking its sharpest weekly decline since May.
- Global tech stocks faced significant pressure, with the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index entering bear market territory.
- Upcoming market focus includes Q1 earnings reports from companies like Infosys, UltraTech Cement, and Bajaj Auto.

### Why it Matters
The Indian market's ability to remain resilient amid global volatility highlights a shift toward large-cap stocks and strong domestic institutional support. Investors are closely monitoring energy costs and geopolitical stability to determine if this momentum can be sustained against broader international economic headwinds.
