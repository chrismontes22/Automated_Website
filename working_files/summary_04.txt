Smartphones broke dating. AI might finish the job.
============================================================

Global fertility rates have fallen below the critical replacement level of 2.1, with experts suggesting that smartphone adoption and AI-driven social isolation are accelerating this demographic decline.

### Key Points
- Global fertility dropped below the 2.1 replacement rate in 2023, with over two-thirds of nations now experiencing below-replacement birth levels.
- Research links the post-2010 "relationship recession" and declining in-person socialization to the mass adoption of smartphones and digital entertainment.
- A 2025 study from OpenAI and MIT found that intensive use of AI chatbots is associated with increased social isolation among users.
- Surveys indicate that 19% of American adults have used AI to simulate romantic partners, with some users reporting dissatisfaction with their real-life relationships.
- Experts suggest that future advancements in AI, virtual reality, and haptics could further displace human intimacy and traditional family formation.

### Why it Matters
The decline in fertility and romantic partnership poses a significant threat to global economic stability by creating aging populations and shrinking workforces. If technology continues to prioritize digital convenience over human connection, society may face long-term structural challenges that are difficult to reverse.
