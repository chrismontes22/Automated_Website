10 Gadgets That Don’t Fit the Usual Playbook
============================================================

This week’s tech roundup highlights emerging AI wearable form factors, innovative hardware from unexpected brands, and new consumer devices ranging from modular smartphones to retro-inspired handheld gaming consoles.

### Key Points
- Razer unveiled Project Motoko, an AI-integrated headphone system that avoids face-mounted hardware by using ear-cup cameras.
- Samsung research indicates the Galaxy Watch6 can predict vasovagal syncope with 84.6% accuracy using heart rate variability data.
- The Zondision ZIMO1, a 27-inch glasses-free 3D monitor, launched on Kickstarter with a starting price of $999.
- Apple’s rumored "iPhone Ultra Fold" appears to feature an iPad-like aspect ratio, according to a leaked physical dummy unit.
- The Kodak Charmera, a $35 keychain camera, received both iF and Red Dot design awards despite its budget-tier specifications.
- Organic Transit is reviving the ELF solar electric trike, featuring a new bidirectional charging system for emergency home power backup.

### Why it Matters
These developments signal a shift toward practical, specialized hardware that prioritizes specific use cases over generic, all-in-one device designs. As AI and high-end optics become more accessible, companies are successfully challenging established market leaders with lower price points and unconventional form factors.
