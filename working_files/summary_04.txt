The FBI Wants ‘Near Real-Time’ Access to US License Plate Readers
============================================================

The FBI is seeking nationwide, near real-time access to automated license plate reader data, even as US lawmakers introduce bipartisan legislation to restrict the use of such surveillance technology.

### Key Points
- The FBI Directorate of Intelligence is planning to spend millions to access searchable databases containing vehicle locations, timestamps, and license plate information.
- Google accidentally published a proof-of-concept for a high-severity Chromium vulnerability that allows websites to monitor browsing activity and launch DDoS attacks.
- The Federal Trade Commission warned 12 "nudifying" websites to implement removal processes for nonconsensual AI-generated images under the Take It Down Act.
- Two men were arrested by the Department of Justice for distributing thousands of AI-generated sexual images of real women on pornography platforms.
- Former federal prosecutor Carmen Mercedes Lineberger was charged with stealing a sealed report regarding Donald Trump’s handling of classified documents.

### Why it Matters
These developments highlight a growing tension between federal surveillance ambitions and legislative efforts to protect individual privacy and digital security. As government agencies expand their data-gathering capabilities, the public faces increasing risks from both state-sponsored tracking and unpatched software vulnerabilities.
