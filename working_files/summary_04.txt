Massachusetts was just ranked the best economy in the US. Innovation is a big reason why.
============================================================

Massachusetts has been ranked the state with the strongest economy in the United States by a recent WalletHub study, outperforming all other states due to its high innovation potential.

### Key Points
- Massachusetts secured the top spot in the WalletHub economic study, followed by Washington state and Utah.
- The state ranks first nationally for innovation potential, driven by high concentrations of STEM jobs and venture-capital funding.
- High-tech industries account for over 25% of the state's GDP, with major companies like Moderna, Google, and Amazon maintaining significant operations in Cambridge.
- Massachusetts leads in educational attainment, supported by $22 billion in public school spending and the presence of elite institutions like MIT and Harvard.
- Despite economic success, the state faces significant challenges, ranking 44th in affordability and struggling with persistent cost-of-living and inequality issues.

### Why it Matters
The state's economic model demonstrates how heavy investment in education and research can foster a dominant high-tech sector that punches above its weight class. However, the disparity between this innovation-driven growth and the state's low affordability ranking highlights the ongoing struggle to balance elite economic performance with equitable living standards for all residents.
