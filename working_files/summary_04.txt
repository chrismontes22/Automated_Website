Jensen Huang made his $172 billion fortune on AI chips. His family’s $75 million gift to Vanderbilt argues art decides what technology is for
============================================================

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang and his wife, Lori, have donated $75 million to Vanderbilt University to establish a new college focused on the intersection of technology, art, and design.

### Key Points
- The gift will create the Jen-Hsun and Lori Huang College of Art, Architecture and Design at Vanderbilt’s new San Francisco campus.
- The donation has already catalyzed an additional $25 million in funding, bringing the total investment for the project to over $100 million.
- The new institution will occupy the former site of the California College of the Arts, which is scheduled to close in 2027.
- The curriculum aims to integrate technical breakthroughs with studio practice to foster business and design fluency among students.
- This contribution marks the largest university donation in Huang's philanthropic history, surpassing his previous $50 million gift to Oregon State University.

### Why it Matters
This investment highlights a growing trend among technology leaders to bridge the gap between rapid AI development and human-centric design. By situating the school in San Francisco, the initiative aims to ensure that future industry leaders possess the creative and ethical frameworks necessary to guide the societal impact of emerging technologies.
