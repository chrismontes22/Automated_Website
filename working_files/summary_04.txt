Portable L-Shaped Triple Screen Monitor turns your laptop setup into a 2×2 command center
============================================================

NexFold has introduced the Fold 7, an innovative portable triple-display monitor system that utilizes an inverted L-shaped design to create a compact, four-screen workstation for mobile professionals.

### Key Points
- The Fold 7 features three 16-inch IPS panels with a 16:10 aspect ratio, designed to sit around a laptop to create a 2x2 quad-display setup.
- Users can choose between FHD (1920 x 1200) and QHD (2560 x 1600) resolution models, with peak brightness reaching up to 500 nits.
- The device weighs 3.1 kg (6.83 lbs) and collapses into a form factor similar to a 16-inch laptop for portability.
- Connectivity options include single-cable USB-C with 65W power delivery or a combination of USB-A and mini HDMI for older hardware.
- The system is compatible with Windows, macOS, Linux, and ChromeOS, supporting laptops ranging from 13 to 18.5 inches.
- Early-bird pricing starts at $649 for the FHD model, with shipping scheduled to begin in September 2026.

### Why it Matters
This device addresses the ergonomic and spatial limitations of traditional horizontal multi-monitor setups by concentrating screen real estate into a tighter, more efficient visual field. It offers power users and developers a high-density, portable workspace that significantly reduces neck strain while maintaining the flexibility required for remote work.
