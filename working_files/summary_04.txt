Flock is falling out of favor with police departments
============================================================

The Los Angeles Police Department has allowed its contract with surveillance firm Flock Safety to expire, citing significant concerns regarding civil liberties, data privacy, and federal information sharing.

### Key Points
- The LAPD ended its agreement for 138 pole-mounted license plate cameras following an internal audit regarding data ownership and security.
- Reports indicate Flock Safety allegedly allowed federal agencies to access local license plate data without the knowledge or consent of municipal law enforcement.
- Multiple jurisdictions, including Mountain View, Santa Cruz, and Dayton, have recently terminated or challenged their contracts with the surveillance provider.
- Critics and internal reviews have linked the company's automated license plate readers to wrongful arrests, police dog attacks, and incorrect vehicle flagging.
- Amazon’s Ring canceled a planned partnership with Flock earlier in 2026 following public backlash regarding the company's data practices.

### Why it Matters
The loss of a major contract with the LAPD signals a growing trend of municipal skepticism toward private surveillance networks and their data-sharing policies. This shift could force other law enforcement agencies to reevaluate their reliance on third-party technology providers to avoid legal liability and public scrutiny.
