The future of AI regulation is courting the strangest, most anxious bedfellows
============================================================

Washington insiders and tech lobbyists are navigating a volatile regulatory landscape as President Trump’s unpredictable AI policy shifts and upcoming midterm elections create significant uncertainty for the industry.

### Key Points
- President Trump signed an executive order on June 2 requiring a 30-day government review for unreleased advanced AI models after multiple reversals.
- The Washington AI Network recently hosted a gala featuring tech industry leaders, government officials, and Vatican diplomat Archbishop Gabriele Caccia.
- Pope Leo XIV’s encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas*, advocates for human-centric AI development but currently lacks legal authority in the United States.
- Journalist Molly White launched Tech Influence Watch to track political spending by AI and cryptocurrency super PACs ahead of the midterm elections.
- Tech companies face heightened scrutiny as voters increasingly link corporate political influence to the rapid, often unwanted integration of AI into daily life.

### Why it Matters
The tech industry’s aggressive lobbying and political spending are colliding with a chaotic regulatory environment where personal loyalty to the administration often outweighs traditional policy strategy. This instability forces companies to prepare for unpredictable legislative shifts while managing public backlash against the rapid deployment of AI technologies.
