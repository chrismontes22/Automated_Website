5 Things I Am Thinking About
============================================================

Strong corporate profits and historical data regarding market highs suggest a constructive outlook for U.S. equities despite ongoing concerns about consumer sentiment and a K-shaped economic recovery.

### Key Points
- Corporate profits remain a primary driver of equity growth, supported by strong performance across sectors like Communication Services, Health Care, and Financials.
- Historical data indicates that investing at all-time market highs frequently yields better long-term returns than attempting to time market drawdowns.
- Low consumer sentiment scores, such as those from the University of Michigan, often serve as a contrarian indicator rather than a signal of impending market decline.
- The current economic landscape reflects a K-shaped structure where the top 10% of earners drive a significant portion of total economic activity.
- Geopolitical tensions involving Iran and potential impacts on energy prices remain a risk factor, though regional conflicts historically have limited effects on broader stock indices.

### Why it Matters
Understanding these market dynamics helps investors distinguish between personal economic sentiment and the broader data-driven trends that actually influence equity performance. Recognizing that record highs and profit growth often coexist allows for more informed decision-making in an environment that remains fundamentally constructive despite persistent social and economic inequalities.
