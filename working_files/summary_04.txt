Nvidia's culture of frugality extends all the way to the cafeteria
============================================================

Nvidia maintains a culture of frugality by charging employees for cafeteria meals, distinguishing the chip giant from Silicon Valley peers that historically offered lavish workplace perks and free food.

### Key Points
- Software engineer Gergely Orosz highlighted that Nvidia does not provide free meals, contrasting with the generous perks offered by companies like Google.
- Former employees confirmed that while some coffee is complimentary, most cafeteria food is only partially subsidized rather than provided at no cost.
- Nvidia’s corporate culture emphasizes a separation between work and pleasure, intentionally avoiding amenities like gyms, massage services, or ping-pong tables.
- The company’s frugal approach extends to executive travel, with vice presidents required to fly economy class and operate without dedicated executive assistants.
- Employees report that the lack of free perks is not a significant issue, as the company prioritizes high-impact work over office-based lifestyle benefits.

### Why it Matters
Nvidia’s policy reflects a broader shift in the technology sector toward cost discipline and efficiency as companies move away from the lavish perk wars of the past. This approach suggests that industry leaders are increasingly prioritizing operational focus over the office-centric amenities once used to retain talent.
