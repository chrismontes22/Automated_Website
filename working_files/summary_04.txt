LinkedIn China Spying Threat Prompts Warning From US, Allies
============================================================

The Five Eyes intelligence alliance has issued a formal warning regarding Chinese military operatives using LinkedIn to recruit individuals with access to sensitive government, military, and economic information.

### Key Points
- Intelligence agencies from the U.S., U.K., Canada, Australia, and New Zealand identified aggressive recruitment tactics targeting security-cleared personnel and academics.
- Chinese operatives frequently pose as representatives from think tanks, consultancies, or human resources firms to solicit classified or proprietary data.
- Targets are often commissioned to write reports for payments ranging from hundreds to thousands of dollars, sometimes processed via cryptocurrency.
- Compromised individuals face severe consequences, including criminal prosecution, termination of employment, and the permanent revocation of security clearances.
- The Chinese embassy in the U.K. rejected the allegations, labeling the joint warning as malicious slander and fabricated misinformation.

### Why it Matters
This warning highlights a significant shift in espionage tactics toward professional networking platforms to bypass traditional security measures. It underscores the growing risk for government and defense personnel to maintain strict digital hygiene to prevent the unauthorized disclosure of sensitive national security information.
