AI backlash is coming for elections
============================================================

Rising public anxiety over artificial intelligence and data center development is fueling political tension, though the issue has yet to become a primary driver for most American voters.

### Key Points
- Over 60 percent of Americans support government regulation of AI to ensure economic stability and public safety.
- Opposition to data center projects has stalled approximately $64 billion in development across the United States.
- Super PACs like Leading the Future and Public First Action have raised millions to influence political candidates on AI policy.
- The Alliance for Secure AI reports over 110,000 US job losses attributed to AI, with significant cuts occurring at companies like Oracle.
- Political resistance to AI is bipartisan, with Data Center Watch noting that 55 percent of project opponents are Republicans and 45 percent are Democrats.

### Why it Matters
While AI is not yet a top-tier issue for the average voter, the rapid influx of lobbying money and localized protests suggest it is becoming a significant political flashpoint. As concerns over job displacement and infrastructure growth intensify, candidates may increasingly use AI regulation as a platform to capture public sentiment and differentiate themselves in future elections.
