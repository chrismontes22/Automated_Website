Apple @ Work: How zero-touch enrollment killed the market for stolen corporate devices
============================================================

Apple’s Automated Device Enrollment and Apple Business platform have significantly reduced corporate hardware theft by linking device serial numbers to company servers, rendering stolen Macs and iPads effectively unusable.

### Key Points
- Apple Business Manager permanently maps device serial numbers to corporate accounts at the point of purchase.
- Zero-touch enrollment forces stolen devices to connect to Apple activation servers, triggering mandatory corporate login screens.
- Managed Activation Lock prevents unauthorized users from wiping or reselling stolen hardware on the secondary market.
- IT administrators can track the IP address of a stolen device once it connects to the internet.
- Devices not enrolled through official enterprise channels remain vulnerable to theft and unauthorized resale.

### Why it Matters
These security features protect corporate data and eliminate the financial incentive for stealing enterprise-grade hardware. By rendering stolen devices effectively bricked, Apple has shifted the burden of security from manual firmware passwords to a seamless, server-level management system.
