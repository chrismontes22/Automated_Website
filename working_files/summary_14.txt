Android apps track you by default — this free app tells you how bad it really is
============================================================

The open-source Android application Exodus Privacy allows users to identify hidden third-party trackers and data-collection software embedded within their installed mobile apps to improve personal digital security.

### Key Points
- Exodus Privacy is a free, non-profit tool that scans Android devices to detect embedded tracking libraries and SDKs.
- The app functions locally on the device, ensuring user data is not transmitted to external servers during the scanning process.
- It identifies various types of trackers, including advertising networks like Google AdMob and behavioral profiling tools.
- Users can utilize the findings to identify and delete unused applications that may be collecting unnecessary personal data.
- The platform provides transparency into software behavior, distinguishing between essential app permissions and third-party tracking code.

### Why it Matters
This tool empowers users to make informed decisions about their digital footprint by exposing the often-hidden data collection practices of common mobile applications. By identifying excessive tracking, individuals can proactively remove unnecessary software and better manage their privacy in an increasingly data-driven mobile ecosystem.
