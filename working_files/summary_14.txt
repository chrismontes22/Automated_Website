US Weighs Removing Steering Wheel Requirement for Driverless Cars
============================================================

National Highway Traffic Safety Administration administrator Jonathan Morrison signaled support for removing mandatory steering wheels and brake pedals from future autonomous vehicles designed exclusively for driverless operation.

### Key Points
- NHTSA administrator Jonathan Morrison stated that manual controls are unnecessary for vehicles engineered to operate without human intervention.
- Autonomous vehicle manufacturers, including Tesla and Uber, have lobbied for updated design requirements since 2019 to accommodate driverless technology.
- The agency is currently balancing the promotion of autonomous innovation with ongoing investigations into vehicle stalls and interference with emergency services.
- Industry experts emphasize that removing manual controls requires new safety standards to ensure passenger egress and predictable behavior for first responders.
- Morrison acknowledged government-wide concerns regarding the prevalence of Chinese-manufactured lidar sensor technology in global autonomous vehicle fleets.

### Why it Matters
Updating federal safety standards to remove manual controls marks a significant shift in how regulators approach the transition from human-operated to fully autonomous transportation. This regulatory evolution is essential for the commercial viability of driverless fleets, though it necessitates rigorous new safety protocols to replace traditional human-in-the-loop safeguards.
