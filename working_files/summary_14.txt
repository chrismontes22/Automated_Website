Mark Cuban says AI risks making US healthcare worse by fueling an arms race with insurers
============================================================

Mark Cuban warns that artificial intelligence could exacerbate healthcare costs and administrative friction as insurers and intermediaries deploy rival AI agents to deny claims and protect corporate profits.

### Key Points
- Mark Cuban argues that healthcare conglomerates use AI to manipulate contracts and increase administrative burdens on physicians.
- Hospitals are increasingly hiring revenue cycle management firms to deploy counter-AI agents to combat insurer denials.
- Cuban estimates that doctors currently spend at least 25% of their time navigating obstacles created by healthcare middlemen.
- The entrepreneur suggests that AI should be used to facilitate direct contracting between employers and providers to bypass traditional insurers.
- Cuban previously founded the Cost Plus Drug Company to reduce prescription costs by eliminating pharmacy benefit managers.

### Why it Matters
This conflict highlights a growing technological arms race that threatens to offset the potential clinical benefits of AI in medicine. If insurers and providers continue to weaponize automation against each other, the resulting administrative complexity may further inflate healthcare spending rather than reducing it.
