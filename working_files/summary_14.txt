Travel scams are everywhere. Here’s how to avoid them
============================================================

Travelers face increasing cybersecurity risks as scammers leverage fake websites, phishing emails, and fraudulent rental listings to steal sensitive financial information and personal data from unsuspecting vacation planners.

### Key Points
- Booking.com-related scams increased by 56% following a data breach disclosed in April, according to Scam Guard data.
- Attackers frequently use SEO poisoning and fake websites to mimic legitimate travel brands and distribute malware like Remote Access Trojans.
- Scammers often impersonate platforms like Airbnb and TripAdvisor to lure victims into making payments outside of secure, official booking channels.
- Common red flags include requests for cryptocurrency or gift card payments, urgent demands for card verification, and suspicious PDF attachments.
- Experts recommend using credit cards for better fraud protection and avoiding sponsored search results when booking flights or accommodations.

### Why it Matters
The travel and hospitality sector remains a primary target for cybercriminals due to the high volume of sensitive personal data and large financial transactions processed daily. By understanding these common tactics, consumers can better protect their identities and finances while navigating the increasingly complex landscape of online travel bookings.
