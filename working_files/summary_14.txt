Review: Boox's feature-packed E Ink tablet is unlike any e-reader I've used
============================================================

The Boox Go 10.3 Gen II Lumi is a versatile E Ink tablet that combines e-reading, digital note-taking, and Android functionality into an ultra-slim, high-performance mobile device.

### Key Points
- The device features a 300-PPI E Ink display, an octa-core processor, and a 3,700-mAh battery for extended usage.
- It measures 4.8 mm thick, weighs 364 grams, and includes the pressure-sensitive InkSense Plus stylus for writing and sketching.
- Running on Android 15 with Google Play Store access, the tablet supports over 20 file formats and various third-party productivity apps.
- Pricing starts at $379.99 for the base model, with the premium Lumi version retailing for $429.99.
- The tablet is designed for reading and annotation, though its E Ink technology is not optimized for video playback or fast-scrolling applications.

### Why it Matters
This device represents a growing market segment that bridges the gap between dedicated e-readers and full-featured tablets by offering a distraction-free, paper-like experience. Its ability to run Android apps provides users with a flexible alternative to closed ecosystems, appealing to students and professionals who prioritize long battery life and tactile note-taking.
