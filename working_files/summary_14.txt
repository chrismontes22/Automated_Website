Adaptive Battery was making decisions I didn't approve — so I took back control
============================================================

Android’s Adaptive Battery feature aims to extend device longevity by predicting app usage, but users may prefer disabling it to ensure consistent performance for critical, infrequently used applications.

### Key Points
- Adaptive Battery, introduced in Android 9 Pie, uses App Standby Buckets to restrict background activity for apps deemed less important by the system.
- The feature can inadvertently delay critical notifications or background tasks for essential tools like banking, smart-home, and messaging apps.
- Users can disable Adaptive Battery through the main Battery settings menu on most Android devices, including Pixel and Samsung Galaxy phones.
- Manual power management allows users to set individual app permissions to Unrestricted, Optimized, or Restricted to maintain control over background behavior.
- Disabling system-wide automation requires users to manually audit app settings to prevent excessive battery drain while ensuring reliable performance.

### Why it Matters
Automated power management features often prioritize general battery efficiency over the specific, nuanced needs of individual users. By taking manual control, users can eliminate unpredictable app behavior and ensure that essential services remain functional regardless of how frequently they are accessed.
