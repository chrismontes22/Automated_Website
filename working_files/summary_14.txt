A relationship banker and a risk manager: Meet the two candidates vying to succeed JPMorgan's Jamie Dimon
============================================================

JPMorgan Chase has appointed Troy Rohrbaugh and Doug Petno as co-presidents, effectively narrowing the succession race to replace long-time CEO Jamie Dimon to these two senior executives.

### Key Points
- Doug Petno will lead the corporate and investment bank, while Troy Rohrbaugh takes over the consumer and community banking unit.
- Current consumer banking head Marianne Lake is retiring, removing a key contender from the potential CEO succession field.
- Both Rohrbaugh and Petno received one-time $30 million retention bonuses following their promotions to co-president.
- Petno has been with JPMorgan for over 35 years, while Rohrbaugh joined the firm in 2005 with a background in trading and risk management.
- Jamie Dimon, 70, has not announced a specific retirement date, leading analysts to suggest the succession timeline remains flexible.

### Why it Matters
This leadership shift provides the clearest signal yet regarding the future of the world's largest bank by market capitalization. The transition process is critical for investors and stakeholders who are closely monitoring how the firm will eventually move beyond the long-standing tenure of Jamie Dimon.
