Live markets: Bitcoin remains under pressure as Korea's SK Hynix joins Micron in $1 trillion club
============================================================

Memory chip manufacturers Micron Technology, SK Hynix, and Samsung Electronics have all surpassed $1 trillion in market valuation as surging demand for artificial intelligence hardware drives significant investor interest.

### Key Points
- Micron Technology shares rose 21% yesterday, pushing the company’s market capitalization past the $1 trillion threshold following a positive price target adjustment from UBS.
- South Korean chipmaker SK Hynix saw its stock climb 9.3%, marking a 1,000% increase over the past year and reaching a $1 trillion valuation.
- Samsung Electronics also recently achieved a $1 trillion market cap, solidifying the dominance of major memory producers in the current tech market.
- Analysts project that persistent chip shortages will provide these companies with significant pricing power through 2028.
- Bitcoin prices fell 1.5% to $75,800 as capital continues to shift away from cryptocurrency markets toward high-growth artificial intelligence stocks.

### Why it Matters
The rapid valuation growth of major memory chip producers highlights a massive reallocation of capital toward hardware infrastructure essential for artificial intelligence development. This trend suggests that investors are prioritizing tangible industrial growth over speculative assets like cryptocurrency, which currently faces significant negative market sentiment.
