Climate Denier Group Pushes States to Embrace Coal Power for Data Centers
============================================================

The Heartland Institute is actively lobbying state lawmakers through the American Legislative Exchange Council to revive coal power, citing the massive electricity demands of artificial intelligence and data centers.

### Key Points
- The Heartland Institute is pressuring big tech companies to abandon green energy commitments in favor of coal to power AI-driven data centers.
- ALEC has adopted model policies designed to prevent the closure of coal and natural gas plants and to streamline energy facility approvals.
- Legislative efforts in states including Arkansas, Missouri, Indiana, Louisiana, and Ohio have successfully introduced or passed bills favoring fossil fuel grid reliability.
- The International Energy Agency reported a 10% rise in U.S. coal consumption in 2025, reversing years of decline and contributing to record global carbon emissions.
- The Trump administration has intervened to keep aging coal plants operational, despite warnings from utilities regarding the high costs and inefficiency of these assets.

### Why it Matters
This push to revive coal threatens to undermine long-term climate goals and corporate sustainability pledges by prioritizing short-term energy capacity for the AI boom. By influencing state-level legislation, these groups are creating a regulatory environment that could lock the U.S. into using outdated, high-emission energy infrastructure for decades.
