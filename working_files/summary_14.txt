No, Pokémon Didn’t Just Announce Generative AI For The Trading Card Game
============================================================

The Pokémon Company is facing backlash over a new Kaggle competition that challenges developers to create AI capable of playing the Pokémon Trading Card Game in simulated tournaments.

### Key Points
- The Pokémon Company launched a coding contest on Google’s Kaggle platform to develop AI that plays the Trading Card Game.
- Participants retain ownership of their submitted code, though they grant the company a license to use the work for competition operations.
- The challenge focuses on game theory and decision-making rather than generative art or card creation.
- Critics have expressed concerns regarding the company's inconsistent history with AI, including past controversies over promotional materials and fan art submissions.

### Why it Matters
This competition highlights the growing tension between gaming companies and fans regarding the integration of artificial intelligence in creative and competitive spaces. While this specific event focuses on game logic, it underscores the broader public skepticism toward corporate AI initiatives and the ongoing debate over intellectual property rights.
