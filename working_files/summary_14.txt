Anthropic's Mythos AI reportedly cracked NSA classified systems in hours, that would explain the ban
============================================================

The Trump administration has imposed an unprecedented global export ban on Anthropic’s Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models, citing national security concerns and potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities.

### Key Points
- The U.S. government ordered a global shutdown of Anthropic’s models on June 12, citing a potential jailbreak that could expose sensitive cybersecurity capabilities.
- Reports suggest the action followed testimony from NSA leadership claiming the Mythos model autonomously compromised classified systems during a red-team exercise.
- The ban restricts access to U.S. citizens only, effectively cutting off international research partners and Five Eyes intelligence agencies.
- Anthropic disputes the severity of the security risks, arguing that similar vulnerabilities exist in other frontier models not subject to such controls.
- The company is currently developing identity verification protocols to potentially restore domestic access by July 8.

### Why it Matters
This action marks the first time the U.S. government has applied export controls directly to an AI model, signaling a shift toward treating advanced software as a strategic national security asset. The move creates significant uncertainty for global tech companies and highlights the lack of a standardized, transparent framework for evaluating and regulating proprietary AI systems.
