This ransomware negotiator was paid to fight hackers, he was secretly working with them instead
============================================================

Angelo Martino, a former ransomware negotiator for DigitalMint, has been sentenced to 70 months in prison for conspiring with BlackCat hackers to inflate ransom payments and extort victims.

### Key Points
- Angelo Martino leaked confidential victim insurance limits and negotiation strategies to BlackCat operators to maximize ransom payouts.
- Between April and September 2023, five DigitalMint clients paid over $75 million in ransoms influenced by Martino’s illicit disclosures.
- Martino obtained direct affiliate access to the BlackCat ransomware platform in May 2023, enabling him to launch independent attacks on new targets.
- The FBI seized assets including homes, a boat, and vehicles purchased by Martino with cryptocurrency proceeds from the extortion scheme.
- DigitalMint stated it was unaware of the misconduct, noting that Martino used unauthorized, external communication channels to bypass internal security safeguards.

### Why it Matters
This case exposes a critical vulnerability in the cybersecurity industry where trusted negotiators operate within infrastructure controlled by the very criminals they are hired to combat. It highlights the severe risks of insider threats and the potential for intermediaries to weaponize sensitive victim data for personal financial gain.
