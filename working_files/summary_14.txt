Anthropic Files for IPO as AI Race Hits Public Markets
============================================================

AI startup Anthropic has officially filed for an initial public offering, signaling a major move to test investor demand following a recent $965 billion post-money valuation.

### Key Points
- Anthropic filed for an IPO on Monday, allowing the company to go public pending a formal review by the Securities and Exchange Commission.
- The company recently closed a $65 billion Series H funding round, achieving a $965 billion post-money valuation.
- Founded in 2021 by former OpenAI executives, the firm focuses on safety-oriented large language models and its flagship AI assistant, Claude.
- Anthropic is expanding its enterprise presence with tools like Claude Design, which automates the creation of marketing materials and sales presentations.

### Why it Matters
This IPO filing highlights the rapid financial escalation of top-tier AI firms as they compete for dominance in the enterprise software market. The company's public market debut will serve as a critical benchmark for investor confidence in the long-term profitability of generative AI technologies.
