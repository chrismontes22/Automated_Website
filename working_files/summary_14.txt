The RAM crisis is getting worse — I take a look at when prices might fall, and whether you'll ever see the savings when they do
============================================================

Global memory and storage prices are surging as major manufacturers prioritize high-bandwidth memory for AI datacenters, leading to significant cost increases for consumer PCs, laptops, and gaming consoles.

### Key Points
- Samsung, SK hynix, and Micron control 90% of the DRAM market and have shifted production capacity toward high-profit AI hardware.
- Gartner reports that DRAM and SSD prices could surge by 130% by the end of 2026, potentially raising overall PC prices by 17%.
- Micron has secured long-term contracts for most of its DRAM supply through 2030, signaling that the current supply crisis will likely persist for years.
- Analysts predict a 10.4% to 11.3% decline in global PC shipments for 2026 as rising costs price out entry-level consumers.
- New fabrication plants, such as Micron’s facility in Idaho, are not expected to provide significant consumer supply relief until at least late 2027.

### Why it Matters
The shift in manufacturing priorities suggests that the era of affordable consumer electronics may be permanently altered as companies prioritize high-margin enterprise AI clients. Even if component costs eventually stabilize, historical trends indicate that manufacturers may maintain higher price baselines, potentially eliminating the budget-friendly PC market entirely.
