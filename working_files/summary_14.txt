AMD's new memory tiering tech may soon turn storage into RAM, but your PC won't see it
============================================================

AMD has acquired California-based startup MEXT to integrate AI-driven memory tiering technology, aiming to reduce data center reliance on expensive DRAM by utilizing more affordable NAND flash storage.

### Key Points
- AMD acquired MEXT on June 15 to address the rising costs and supply constraints of DRAM in data center environments.
- MEXT’s Predictive Memory Engine uses AI to analyze memory access patterns and pre-fetch data from NAND flash into DRAM.
- Memory costs currently account for approximately 30% of hyperscaler data center expenditures, a fourfold increase since 2023.
- The technology is optimized for structured, repetitive enterprise workloads like AI inference and virtualization rather than unpredictable consumer tasks.
- This acquisition reflects a broader industry trend of using software algorithms to compensate for physical hardware limitations and supply shortages.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition signals a strategic shift toward software-defined hardware to mitigate the high costs of scaling AI infrastructure. By effectively extending memory capacity through predictive analytics, AMD aims to provide cloud providers with a more sustainable alternative to traditional, expensive DRAM expansion.
