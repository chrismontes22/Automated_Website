WhatsApp is building its own iCloud alternative for chat backups
============================================================

WhatsApp is developing a proprietary cloud backup service for iPhone users that offers an alternative to iCloud, featuring mandatory end-to-end encryption and up to 1TB of potential storage.

### Key Points
- WhatsApp is testing a native backup service that allows users to bypass iCloud for storing chat history and media.
- The service provides 2GB of free storage, with reports suggesting future paid tiers could reach up to 1TB.
- Unlike iCloud backups, which make encryption optional, WhatsApp’s system enforces end-to-end encryption by default.
- Users can secure their backups using a passkey, a traditional password, or a 64-digit encryption key.
- Similar backup infrastructure is currently under development for Android users to replace Google Drive integration.

### Why it Matters
This development signals a shift toward platform-agnostic data management for Meta, reducing reliance on Apple and Google’s ecosystem storage limits. By mandating end-to-end encryption, the company is also positioning its own servers as a more secure alternative for privacy-conscious users.
