‘Can AI Do My Job?’ Is the Wrong Question
============================================================

While experts debate whether artificial intelligence will render many professions obsolete, historical data and economic frameworks suggest that job security depends on task integration, demand elasticity, and expertise.

### Key Points
- Radiology serves as a prime example of a "strong bundle" job, where AI automation has increased demand and salaries rather than replacing human specialists.
- The "Jevons paradox" explains how increased efficiency can lower costs and trigger a surge in demand, leading to higher employment in fields like software engineering.
- Economists distinguish between "clean" tasks, which are easily automated, and "messy" tasks that require human judgment, interpersonal skills, and complex context.
- Technology impacts wages differently based on whether it enhances a worker's expertise or commodifies their skills, as seen in the divergence between accounting and inventory clerks.
- Current labor market trends show rising demand for recruiters and software engineers despite the widespread adoption of AI tools in those sectors.

### Why it Matters
Understanding these economic dynamics helps professionals assess their vulnerability to automation by evaluating how their specific roles balance technical tasks with human-centric expertise. This framework suggests that the future of work will be defined less by total replacement and more by how technology reshapes the value of human labor across different industries.
