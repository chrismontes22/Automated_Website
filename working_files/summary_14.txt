Micro AGI sends free cleaning staff to NYC apartments for AI training
============================================================

Munich-based startup MicroAGI is offering free apartment cleaning services in New York City to capture first-person video footage used to train artificial intelligence models for household robotics.

### Key Points
- MicroAGI launched its Shift app on May 28, providing free professional cleaning services in exchange for recording tasks via head-mounted cameras.
- The company anonymizes the collected video data to train AI systems on real-world physical chores and navigation in cluttered environments.
- MicroAGI operates a global network of over 10,000 contributors across 15 countries who are paid approximately $20 per hour to record daily tasks.
- The startup reported over $5 million in revenue during the first quarter of 2026 and offers research fellowships providing up to $2 million in compute resources.
- Operations are currently active in major cities including New York, London, Zurich, and Istanbul.

### Why it Matters
This model addresses the critical shortage of real-world physical data required to advance robotics beyond simulated environments. However, the practice raises significant privacy concerns regarding the recording of private residences and may face strict regulatory scrutiny under data protection laws like the GDPR.
