The AI Big Crunch Is Starting
============================================================

The artificial intelligence industry is entering a correction phase known as the "Big Crunch," where organizations are shifting from speculative hype toward realistic, practical applications of the technology.

### Key Points
- Companies like Ford are rehiring veteran engineers after discovering that AI cannot replace decades of specialized human judgment in critical quality processes.
- Research from Forrester and Robert Half indicates that over half of employers regret AI-driven layoffs, with many firms forced to rehire staff to correct operational errors.
- Princeton researchers Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor argue that much of the current market offerings constitute "AI snake oil" that fails to perform as advertised.
- The current market transition marks a shift from the "AI Big Bang" of rapid, unchecked expansion to a period of evaluating actual utility versus inflated expectations.
- Experts emphasize that AI is a tool for automation rather than a comprehensive business strategy, warning that technology often accelerates poorly defined problems.

### Why it Matters
This transition signals a maturing market where businesses must prioritize proven value over the fear of missing out on technological trends. Organizations that successfully navigate this correction will likely be those that integrate AI as a supportive tool rather than a replacement for human expertise and sound judgment.
