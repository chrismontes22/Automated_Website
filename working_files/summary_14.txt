Meta sued for ‘misleading’ Texans over WhatsApp privacy
============================================================

Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has filed a lawsuit against Meta, alleging the company misled millions of WhatsApp users by falsely claiming their private messages are fully end-to-end encrypted.

### Key Points
- Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton accuses Meta of misrepresenting the privacy and security protections of WhatsApp to consumers.
- The lawsuit claims Meta employees and third parties can access supposedly private communications, contradicting the company's public marketing materials.
- Meta spokesperson Andy Stone denied the allegations, stating that the company cannot access encrypted user communications and will contest the lawsuit.
- The legal action follows previous whistleblower reports and ongoing investigations by U.S. federal authorities into Meta’s data handling practices.
- Telegram founder Pavel Durov publicly criticized WhatsApp’s security, citing alleged vulnerabilities and past comments from WhatsApp co-founder Brian Acton.

### Why it Matters
This lawsuit challenges the core privacy marketing of one of the world's most popular messaging platforms, potentially impacting user trust and regulatory scrutiny. If proven, these allegations could force significant changes to Meta’s data policies and result in substantial legal penalties for the company.
