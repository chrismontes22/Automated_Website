Steven Soderbergh Says He Felt “Obligated” To Use AI On John Lennon Documentary
============================================================

Director Steven Soderbergh is incorporating artificial intelligence into his upcoming film projects, including a documentary about John Lennon, despite ongoing industry controversy regarding the technology's ethics and legality.

### Key Points
- Steven Soderbergh plans to use AI in future productions, specifically for a documentary featuring the final interview of John Lennon.
- The director previously utilized non-traditional technology by filming the features Unsane and High Flying Bird entirely on an iPhone.
- Industry criticism of AI in film centers on concerns regarding copyright infringement, ethical standards, and the quality of generated content.
- Recent AI-generated projects, such as Darren Aronofsky’s collaboration with Google DeepMind, have faced scrutiny for historical inaccuracies and visual inconsistencies.
- Soderbergh acknowledges that AI may ultimately prove to be a temporary trend rather than a permanent fixture in professional filmmaking.

### Why it Matters
The adoption of AI by a prominent filmmaker like Soderbergh highlights the ongoing tension between creative experimentation and the broader ethical concerns surrounding generative technology in Hollywood. As major studios and directors navigate these tools, the industry continues to grapple with the long-term implications for copyright, labor, and artistic integrity.
