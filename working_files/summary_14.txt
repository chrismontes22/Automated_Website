Forget Elon’s Data Centers In Space. This Startup Wants To Float Them At Sea
============================================================

SpaceX and startup Panthalassa are developing competing concepts to move energy-intensive AI data centers off land, utilizing orbital satellites and floating ocean buoys to bypass terrestrial infrastructure constraints.

### Key Points
- SpaceX aims to launch orbital data centers by 2028, though the company acknowledges significant technical hurdles and unproven technologies.
- Portland-based startup Panthalassa plans to deploy floating, wave-powered data center buoys in the Southern Ocean by 2027.
- Panthalassa’s Ocean-2 prototype generates up to one megawatt of electricity using wave energy and utilizes cold seawater for natural cooling.
- Panthalassa recently raised $140 million in Series B funding from investors including Peter Thiel, John Doerr, and Marc Benioff.
- Proponents argue that ocean-based computing offers a 100-fold cost advantage over space-based alternatives while avoiding the noise and pollution of land-based facilities.

### Why it Matters
The race to relocate data centers reflects the urgent need to solve the massive power and cooling demands of modern artificial intelligence. By moving infrastructure to the ocean or space, companies hope to bypass local zoning conflicts and high utility costs while accessing abundant, emission-free energy sources.
