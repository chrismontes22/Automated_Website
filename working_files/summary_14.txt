The Free And Open Web Is Under Attack At The IETF
============================================================

The Internet Engineering Task Force is currently debating new technical standards that could allow website operators to restrict automated web scraping and prioritize paid access over open information.

### Key Points
- The IETF is evaluating proposals from its AI Preferences and Web Bot Auth working groups regarding automated data collection.
- Proposed standards could enable websites to use cryptographic authentication to block specific bots or require licensing fees for data access.
- Critics argue these changes threaten the ability of researchers, journalists, and non-profits like the Internet Archive to crawl and preserve public web content.
- The Electronic Frontier Foundation is actively opposing these standards, citing concerns that they undermine the fundamental openness of the internet.
- While some proposals aim to prevent infrastructure strain from aggressive bots, others could grant publishers broad veto power over how their content is accessed.

### Why it Matters
These proposed changes could fundamentally alter the internet by shifting it from a public, accessible resource to a gated ecosystem controlled by licensing agreements. If adopted, these standards may stifle innovation and limit the ability of independent organizations to conduct research, archive history, or provide public-interest tools.
