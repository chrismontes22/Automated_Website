FBI Claims Google Employee Won Over $1M On Polymarket Bets Using Company Data
============================================================

Google software engineer Michele Spagnuolo faces federal charges for allegedly using confidential company data to secure over $1.2 million in illicit profits through bets on the Polymarket platform.

### Key Points
- Michele Spagnuolo, a 36-year-old software engineer, allegedly wagered $2.75 million on Google’s "Year in Search" data using the pseudonym "AlphaRaccoon."
- The FBI tracked Spagnuolo’s identity by tracing cryptocurrency transactions from his Polymarket account to a payment processor linked to his Italian identification.
- Spagnuolo faces charges of wire fraud, money laundering, and commodities fraud, which carry a potential sentence of up to 50 years in prison.
- Blockchain sleuths first identified the suspicious trading activity after noting the account achieved a 22-for-23 success rate on specific search-related predictions.
- The Commodity Futures Trading Commission has filed a civil complaint against the engineer alongside the federal criminal charges.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing regulatory and ethical challenges facing the prediction market industry as it struggles to police anonymous, blockchain-based wagering. The incident raises significant questions about whether these platforms can maintain market integrity while operating with limited oversight and high incentives for insider exploitation.
