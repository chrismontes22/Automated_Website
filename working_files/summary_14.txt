I can tell when my ISP is throttling my connection — here's the test
============================================================

Internet service providers often use deep packet inspection to identify and throttle data-heavy traffic, but users can verify these artificial slowdowns by comparing baseline speeds against encrypted VPN connections.

### Key Points
- Internet providers use deep packet inspection and machine learning to identify and throttle specific high-bandwidth activities like 4K streaming or large game downloads.
- Throttling occurs when providers intentionally restrict data speeds for specific applications to manage network congestion during peak usage hours.
- Users can detect throttling by comparing standard speed tests from services like Ookla or Fast.com against speeds recorded while using a VPN.
- A VPN hides traffic patterns through encryption, preventing providers from identifying the data type and triggering automated throttling rules.
- If VPN-encrypted speeds are significantly faster than standard connection speeds, it serves as a primary indicator that the provider is intentionally restricting traffic.

### Why it Matters
Understanding how to identify traffic throttling empowers consumers to hold service providers accountable for the speeds they promise in their subscription plans. By using encryption tools to bypass artificial restrictions, users can ensure they receive the full performance they pay for during high-demand activities.
