Iran and US have been fighting a silent war without bombs or bullets for over a decade
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
Iran-linked hacking groups continue to target critical infrastructure and government officials, marking a shift from simple service disruption to sophisticated, long-term strategic cyber campaigns against Western interests.

**Key points**
- Operation Ababil utilized coordinated DDoS attacks to disrupt major United States financial institutions and online banking services.
- Operation Cleaver expanded Iranian cyber efforts to probe vulnerabilities within energy, aviation, and transportation networks.
- A pro-Iranian hacking group recently claimed to infiltrate the personal account of FBI Director Kash Patel, leaking dated documents.
- Former CIA Director John Brennan has publicly characterized Iran’s evolving cyber capabilities as highly sophisticated and capable of widespread disruption.
- Tehran’s strategy focuses on asymmetric, low-cost, and deniable operations rather than achieving traditional military dominance.

**Why it matters**
These persistent cyber operations demonstrate how nations use low-cost digital tactics to exert geopolitical pressure and psychological influence without triggering conventional military conflict. As these capabilities mature, they pose a continuous risk to the stability of critical infrastructure and the security of high-level government officials.
