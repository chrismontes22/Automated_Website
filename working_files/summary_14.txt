The Secret History of Polymarket – Part 1
============================================================

Polymarket founder Shayne Coplan and industry peers are facing scrutiny over the platform's origins, alleged ties to intelligence-linked networks, and the potential for market manipulation in political betting.

### Key Points
- Polymarket and competitor Kalshi have emerged as dominant prediction markets, often operating with minimal regulatory oversight despite concerns regarding insider trading.
- Shayne Coplan’s rise is linked to a network of Silicon Valley figures, including Peter Thiel and Sean Parker, who have historically backed ventures with intelligence-community ties.
- The platform’s development mirrors the "napsterization" model, where companies intentionally operate in regulatory gray areas to gain market share before seeking legitimacy.
- Critics argue Polymarket resurrects concepts from the defunct Pentagon program Policy Analysis Market (PAM), which was originally designed to predict geopolitical instability and terror attacks.
- High-profile media partnerships and the involvement of political figures, such as Donald Trump Jr., have raised significant questions about conflicts of interest and the integrity of market data.

### Why it Matters
The rapid normalization of prediction markets as tools for political and financial forecasting grants private entities unprecedented influence over public perception and governance. This shift toward "futarchy" risks replacing traditional democratic processes with market-based systems that may be susceptible to manipulation by well-connected insiders.
