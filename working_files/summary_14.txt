5 Ways Data Centers Endanger Their Local Communities and the Country as a Whole
============================================================

The rapid expansion of data centers to support artificial intelligence is straining local power grids, water supplies, and air quality, raising significant environmental and economic concerns for nearby communities.

### Key Points
- The U.S. Department of Energy projects data centers could consume 12% of total U.S. electricity by 2028, up from 4.4% in 2023.
- Virginia hosts over 600 data centers, which accounted for 26% of the state's total electricity consumption in 2023.
- Large data centers can require 10 million gallons of water daily for cooling, threatening local aquifers and municipal water supplies.
- Fossil-fuel-powered data centers contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollutants linked to respiratory, cardiovascular, and neurological health risks.
- A 2024 Virginia legislative report estimates that grid strain from data centers could increase residential electricity bills by up to $37 per month by 2040.

### Why it Matters
The unchecked growth of data infrastructure creates a direct conflict between the digital economy and the physical well-being of local communities. Without improved planning, renewable energy integration, and stricter grid cost-sharing, the environmental and financial burdens of AI development will disproportionately impact residential consumers and public health.
