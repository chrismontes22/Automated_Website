Here's How to Recycle Your Old Laptop, PC and Printer
============================================================

Major retailers like Best Buy and Staples offer convenient, often free, electronics recycling programs to help consumers responsibly dispose of old computers and printers while protecting personal data.

### Key Points
- Best Buy accepts up to three household items and five laptops per day for free, though some state-specific monitor fees may apply.
- Staples provides free in-store recycling for desktop computers, laptops, and printers regardless of where the original items were purchased.
- Office Depot offers a trade-in program for store credit and provides free recycling for devices that hold no remaining market value.
- Apple requires the purchase of a new device to qualify for their free in-store recycling program for computers and monitors.
- Consumers should perform a factory reset or use data-scrubbing tools to permanently remove personal information before recycling hardware.
- Search tools like Earth911 and the Consumer Technology Association’s Greener Gadgets locator help users find local, specialized e-waste recycling centers.

### Why it Matters
Properly recycling electronics prevents hazardous materials from entering landfills and allows for the recovery of valuable components. By utilizing retail drop-off programs and performing thorough data wipes, consumers can clear household clutter while ensuring their sensitive digital information remains secure.
