Germany’s spy agency comes in from the cold
============================================================

Germany is planning the most significant reforms in the BND’s 70-year history to modernize the intelligence agency and strengthen national defense against increasing threats from Russia.

### Key Points
- The German government has increased the BND’s annual budget by approximately 25 percent to €1.51 billion.
- New legislation expected by autumn aims to expand powers regarding signals intelligence, AI integration, and "hack back" cyber capabilities.
- Proposed reforms seek to extend data retention periods, which are currently limited to a maximum of six months.
- The BND currently operates under strict oversight from four separate entities, including a council of judges and the Bundestag.
- The agency employs 6,500 staff and operates from a massive headquarters in Berlin that is larger than the CIA’s base in Langley.

### Why it Matters
These reforms represent a fundamental shift in Germany’s security posture as the nation seeks to reduce its reliance on US intelligence and counter Russian hybrid warfare. By attempting to balance constitutional privacy protections with the need for proactive espionage, the government is testing whether its bureaucratic culture can adapt to a more volatile global landscape.
