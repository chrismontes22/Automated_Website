Android is almost dead
============================================================

Google is reportedly using a system process called Android Developer Verifier to restrict third-party software installations on billions of devices running Android 8 or higher across the globe.

### Key Points
- The "Android Developer Verifier" (ADV) process operates with root privileges and cannot be disabled or removed by users.
- Google is allegedly distributing this software through its own Play Protect service to bypass standard malware detection.
- The mechanism is designed to block applications sourced from outside Google-approved channels, such as the open-source repository F-Droid.
- An estimated 4 billion Android handsets and tablets are reportedly affected by this background service.

### Why it Matters
This development signals a significant shift toward a closed ecosystem that limits user control over software installation and threatens the viability of independent app repositories. If these restrictions become permanent, it could effectively end the era of open-source computing on mobile devices by centralizing all application authority under Google.
