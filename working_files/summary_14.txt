Trump Just Gave Dell Stock a Big Boost. What to Know.
============================================================

Dell Technologies shares surged on Monday following a public endorsement from President Donald Trump, who encouraged citizens to purchase company products during a White House event on July 6.

### Key Points
- President Trump publicly recommended buying Dell computers after founder Michael Dell donated $6.25 billion to a new tax-advantaged investment program for American children.
- Dell stock closed above its 20-day moving average, reflecting strong bullish momentum and a significant technical breakout for the company.
- Financial disclosures reveal President Trump holds $5.1 million in Dell stock, which he acquired during the first quarter of 2026.
- The company projects AI server revenue to reach $60 billion by 2027, supported by its role as a primary hardware provider for corporate infrastructure.
- Wall Street analysts maintain a "Moderate Buy" consensus on the stock with a mean price target of $487, suggesting an 18% potential upside.

### Why it Matters
The presidential endorsement provides a significant psychological and structural tailwind for Dell as it scales its AI-driven enterprise business. This alignment between federal policy and corporate growth reinforces the company's position as a key player in the expanding American technology economy.
