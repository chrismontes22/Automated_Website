Cable Lobbyists Show How Trump FCC’s Extortive ‘Foreign Router Ban’ Isn’t Workable
============================================================

The NCTA has petitioned the FCC for a broad exemption from the Trump administration’s foreign router ban, citing significant supply chain constraints and the impracticality of immediate domestic manufacturing.

### Key Points
- The NCTA, representing the cable industry, filed a petition requesting an expedited waiver from the FCC’s restrictions on foreign-made networking hardware.
- Industry groups argue that current supply chain shortages for critical components like memory modules make immediate onshoring of router production impossible.
- Manufacturers such as Amazon’s Eero and Netgear have already received conditional exemptions, though the specific terms of these agreements remain undisclosed.
- The FCC policy, championed by Brendan Carr, aims to force hardware manufacturing back to the United States under the guise of national security.
- Cable providers warn that failing to grant these waivers could lead to widespread broadband service disruptions for consumers and businesses across the country.

### Why it Matters
The ongoing conflict highlights the tension between aggressive protectionist trade policies and the complex, globalized reality of modern telecommunications supply chains. If these regulations remain unworkable, they risk increasing costs for providers and potentially destabilizing internet access for millions of American users.
