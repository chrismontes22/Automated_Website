Samsung Galaxy Book6 Enterprise Edition review: Beautifully engineered business laptop but at this price, I expected better ports than USB 3.2 Gen 2
============================================================

The Samsung Galaxy Book6 Enterprise Edition offers reliable performance and impressive battery life for professionals, though its lack of modern connectivity features and high price point limit its appeal.

### Key Points
- The laptop features Intel’s new Core Ultra Series 3 "Panther Lake" processors with a 49 TOPS NPU for local AI tasks.
- It includes a 16-inch WUXGA IPS display, a physical RJ-45 LAN port, and a redesigned symmetrical aluminum chassis.
- Pricing starts at £1,405 in the UK and $1,280 in the US, with configurations offering up to 32GB of soldered RAM.
- The device lacks USB4 and Thunderbolt 4 support, relying instead on USB 3.2 Gen 2 ports.
- Enterprise-specific features include Samsung Knox, BIOS-level customization, and support for Windows Autopilot.

### Why it Matters
This laptop serves as a functional, productivity-focused machine for users already integrated into the Samsung Galaxy ecosystem. However, the omission of premium display technology and high-speed connectivity at this price point makes it a difficult recommendation compared to the more capable Pro model or previous-generation alternatives.
