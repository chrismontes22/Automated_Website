Data Centers Start to Drive Residential Electricity Bills Down
============================================================

Major electric utilities in states like Georgia and Indiana are lowering residential electricity rates as increased revenue from data center investments funds grid modernization and new power plant construction.

### Key Points
- The Georgia Public Service Commission approved a plan to save typical residential customers $50 annually, totaling $285 million in savings.
- Entergy announced $5 billion in total savings for 2.3 million customers across Arkansas, Louisiana, and Mississippi over the next 20 years.
- Indiana Michigan Power plans to file for a base rate reduction this summer, citing load growth from large data center customers.
- Data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration shows Virginia and Texas, which host 25% of U.S. data centers, maintain residential rates below the national average.
- A May 2026 study by Energy + Environmental Economics concluded that blaming data centers for rising electricity costs lacks mathematical and regulatory support.

### Why it Matters
These utility agreements demonstrate that large-scale industrial energy demand can subsidize infrastructure upgrades that ultimately lower costs for residential ratepayers. By leveraging data center investments to expand capacity, regulators are effectively mitigating the impact of rising electricity prices on household budgets.
