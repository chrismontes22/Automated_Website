Adaptable film camera shoots photos in any frame size you want
============================================================

Exposing Engineering has launched a Kickstarter campaign for the VZ-6617, a modular medium format camera that allows photographers to switch between multiple frame sizes mid-roll using 3D-printed masks.

### Key Points
- The VZ-6617 features a Variable Zone Film Gate, enabling users to change frame sizes from 6x6 to 6x17 without removing film.
- The camera utilizes a magnetically sealed lens cone system, allowing for lens swaps while film is loaded.
- Constructed using 3D-printing technology, the camera body weighs 925 grams and is designed for easy user repair with standard tools.
- A US$549 Kickstarter pledge includes the camera body and a lens cone compatible with specific 90mm lenses.
- The modular design includes multiple cold shoes, bubble levels, and a removable back for accessories like ground glass attachments.
- Exposing Engineering expects to begin shipping the units to backers in August.

### Why it Matters
This camera lowers the barrier to entry for medium format photography by providing a flexible, cost-effective alternative to carrying multiple expensive camera bodies. Its modular, repairable design represents a shift toward sustainable hardware that empowers users to maintain and customize their equipment independently.
