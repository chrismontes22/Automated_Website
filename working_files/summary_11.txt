1666: Amsterdam Developer Gets Caught, Fesses Up To Using AI Assets
============================================================

Panache Digital Games has apologized for including generative AI assets in the prologue of its upcoming action-adventure title 1666: Amsterdam following backlash from the gaming community.

### Key Points
- Players identified AI-generated portraits and marketing assets in the 1666: Amsterdam prologue released on Steam.
- Panache Digital Games confirmed the presence of AI assets and pledged to replace them with human-created art in an upcoming update.
- The studio employs a team of over a dozen artists and stated that future Early Access and full game builds will contain no AI-generated content.
- The controversy emerged shortly after the game's reveal at the Summer Game Fest.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the growing tension between game developers and players regarding the use of generative AI in creative assets. It demonstrates that public scrutiny remains a powerful tool for consumers to enforce industry standards and protect the role of human artists in game development.
