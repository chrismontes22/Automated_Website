iPhone 18 Pro Leaks Reveal Apple’s Move to an Ultra-Efficient 2Nm Chip
============================================================

A massive data breach at Apple supplier Tata Electronics has exposed over 630GB of confidential schematics and technical specifications regarding the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro flagship smartphone.

### Key Points
- Hackers infiltrated Tata Electronics systems, leaking proprietary documents including A20 Pro chip architecture and device design schematics.
- The A20 Pro chip features a 96-bit memory bus and relocated RAM to improve thermal management and multitasking performance.
- New hardware includes a 24MP front-facing camera, under-display Face ID 2.0, and a larger vapor chamber cooling system.
- The device introduces "Ganymede" C2 modem technology, enabling 5G connectivity via satellite for improved remote communication.
- Aesthetic updates include a seamless back glass design and a new cherry red color option.

### Why it Matters
This breach highlights critical vulnerabilities within Apple’s global supply chain and the significant risks associated with protecting intellectual property at third-party manufacturing partners. The incident will likely force Apple to implement stricter cybersecurity protocols and encryption standards to prevent future leaks of sensitive product development data.
