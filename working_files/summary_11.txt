AI's biggest challenge is not compute - it's data storage
============================================================

As artificial intelligence scales, industry experts argue that organizations must shift their focus from compute-centric infrastructure to sustainable, multi-tiered data storage strategies to ensure long-term economic viability.

### Key Points
- Western Digital (WD) identifies data storage, rather than just compute power, as the primary bottleneck for large-scale AI deployment.
- Hard disk drives (HDDs) remain the most cost-effective solution for storing the vast majority of enterprise data, costing up to 20 times less than flash storage.
- Modern AI infrastructure requires a tiered approach, utilizing high-performance flash for real-time inference and high-capacity HDDs for long-term data retention.
- Approximately 90 percent of Western Digital's current revenue is derived from AI and cloud-related storage technologies.
- Effective data lifecycle management is essential for maintaining ROI, as inefficient storage at the exabyte scale can significantly inflate total cost of ownership.

### Why it Matters
Prioritizing storage architecture allows enterprises to treat data as a long-term strategic asset rather than an escalating operational expense. By balancing performance needs with cost-efficient capacity, organizations can avoid resource-draining bottlenecks and ensure their AI models continue to improve through the sustained use of historical data.
