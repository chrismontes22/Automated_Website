Xiaomi might have a 'Privacy Display' on the way that's all software
============================================================

Xiaomi is reportedly developing a software-based Privacy Display feature for its upcoming HyperOS 4 update, aiming to compete with the hardware-integrated privacy technology recently introduced by Samsung.

### Key Points
- Tipster Yogesh Brar claims Xiaomi is developing a privacy-focused display feature for its mobile devices.
- The feature is expected to debut in HyperOS 4, which is based on the Android 17 operating system.
- Unlike Samsung’s hardware-based solution, Xiaomi is reportedly pursuing a software-driven approach to limit screen visibility.
- The update is rumored to arrive later this year, potentially following a release schedule similar to previous HyperOS rollouts.
- Honor is also speculated to be exploring similar privacy display technologies to compete with the Galaxy S26 series.

### Why it Matters
This development signals a growing industry trend toward prioritizing on-screen privacy as a standard feature for flagship smartphones. If successful, a software-based implementation could allow manufacturers to offer privacy benefits without the hardware costs or potential brightness trade-offs associated with Samsung's current technology.
