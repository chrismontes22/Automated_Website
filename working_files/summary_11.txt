Crypto for Advisors: The crypto due diligence questions you forgot to ask
============================================================

Financial advisors must update their crypto due diligence processes to address evolving stablecoin regulations, AI-driven transaction risks, and the shifting legal landscape surrounding the implementation of the GENIUS Act.

### Key Points
- Advisors should evaluate client cash management by comparing traditional sweeps against tokenized money market funds from firms like BlackRock, Fidelity, and J.P. Morgan.
- The GENIUS Act, signed July 18, 2025, will transition stablecoin oversight from state money transmitter licenses to a new federal regulatory framework by early 2027.
- Due diligence must now include verifying AI-driven crypto infrastructure, specifically focusing on quantum readiness, data privacy, and the accountability of agentic transaction workflows.
- Regulatory assumptions in client portfolios require transparent disclosure, as enforcement postures from the SEC and other agencies remain subject to political and legislative changes.
- Advisors are urged to prioritize security and verifiable AI claims to avoid potential SEC enforcement actions related to misleading technology marketing.

### Why it Matters
Updating due diligence frameworks is essential for advisors to maintain fiduciary standards while navigating the integration of digital assets and automated trading tools. Failure to adapt to these technological and regulatory shifts could expose firms to significant liability, data privacy breaches, and client trust issues.
