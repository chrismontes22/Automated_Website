Who Needs Bitcoin & Cryptos (-50%) Amid the Semiconductor & Miracle IPO Mania?
============================================================

The cryptocurrency market has experienced a significant downturn, losing over $2 trillion in value, as investors increasingly shift their capital toward high-growth semiconductor stocks and upcoming mega-IPOs.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin has plunged over 50% from its October 2025 peak, while Ethereum and XRP have declined by 64% and 68% respectively from their recent highs.
- The total crypto market cap tracked by CoinMarketCap has dropped by $2.08 trillion since October.
- Semiconductor stocks and triple-leveraged ETFs, such as the Direxion Daily Semiconductor Bull 3X Shares (SOXL), have seen parabolic growth, with some assets rising over 1,400% in 12 months.
- SpaceX is preparing for a mega-IPO with a valuation of $1.77 trillion, representing 93 times its trailing 12-month revenue of $18.7 billion.
- Micron Technology’s market capitalization reached $1 trillion after an 850% share price increase over the past year.

### Why it Matters
The shift from speculative digital assets to AI-related semiconductor stocks moves market risk from niche crypto holdings into widely held S&P 500 index funds. Because these stocks represent a much larger portion of global institutional portfolios, a significant market correction could create far broader economic consequences than the recent crypto collapse.
