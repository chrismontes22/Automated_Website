Hyperliquid bigger than NASDAQ, says ICE CEO Jeffrey Sprecher
============================================================

Intercontinental Exchange CEO Jeffrey Sprecher praised the decentralized platform Hyperliquid for its significant trading volume, highlighting the growing influence of crypto-native derivatives markets over traditional financial exchange incumbents.

### Key Points
- ICE CEO Jeffrey Sprecher stated that Hyperliquid’s trading activity in perpetual futures now exceeds that of the Nasdaq exchange.
- Hyperliquid currently controls over 70 percent of the decentralized perpetual futures market, clearing billions in daily notional turnover.
- The platform attracts traditional traders by offering 24/7 access to oil derivatives, including during weekends when regulated markets are closed.
- Sprecher noted that Hyperliquid operates as an unregulated offshore entity, creating a competitive disparity with firms bound by Dodd-Frank and EMIR regulations.
- ICE leadership has held multiple meetings with Hyperliquid’s core team, signaling a shift in how traditional Wall Street firms view crypto-native platforms.

### Why it Matters
The comparison highlights a growing regulatory and competitive tension between traditional exchanges and decentralized platforms that operate outside established financial frameworks. Policymakers now face pressure to either create new regulatory categories for perpetual futures or force offshore venues to comply with existing global derivatives laws.
