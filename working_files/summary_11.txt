SpaceX's Public Debut Marks The End Of Big Tech's 'Magnificent 7' Era
============================================================

SpaceX’s successful Nasdaq debut has pushed its market capitalization to $2.85 trillion, prompting analysts to reconsider the "Magnificent 7" market acronym as the rocket company outperforms major tech stocks.

### Key Points
- SpaceX began trading under the ticker SPCX on June 12, raising a record $85.7 billion in the largest public offering in history.
- The company’s valuation has surged 35% since its debut, vaulting it past Tesla, Meta, and Amazon in total market capitalization.
- Market researchers are proposing new acronyms like "MANGOS" and "Fab 10" to include SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic alongside established tech giants.
- OpenAI and Anthropic have filed confidential paperwork to go public later this year, with both companies seeking valuations exceeding $1 trillion.
- Elon Musk’s 38% stake in SpaceX has increased his net worth by over $100 billion per trading day, making him the world's first trillionaire.

### Why it Matters
The rapid rise of SpaceX and the anticipated public offerings of AI leaders signal a fundamental shift in market leadership away from traditional consumer-internet companies. Investors are now prioritizing the "AI stack" over legacy tech, forcing a reevaluation of how the most influential and valuable companies are categorized.
