What new Google Messages features are rolling out [June 2026]
============================================================

Google is actively updating its Messages for Android app with new interface designs, enhanced RCS features, and a transition away from Samsung Messages in the United States.

### Key Points
- Google is phasing out Samsung Messages in the U.S., requiring users to switch to Google Messages for RCS functionality on Android 14 and newer devices.
- Apple introduced end-to-end encrypted RCS support for iPhone users in iOS 26.5, enabling secure cross-platform messaging with Google Messages users.
- New interface updates include a floating long-press menu for images, a redesigned read receipt system, and a "Tap to draft" option for Smart Replies.
- Google is transitioning Messages for web from QR code pairing to a mandatory Google Account sign-in process.
- A new trash folder feature now retains deleted chats for 30 days before permanent removal.

### Why it Matters
These updates reflect Google's ongoing effort to standardize the messaging experience across Android and iOS through the universal adoption of RCS. By streamlining features and security protocols, Google aims to improve cross-platform communication while consolidating its ecosystem under a unified Google Account framework.
