Don't believe the hype: Aluminium OS won't replace Windows anytime soon
============================================================

**Google is developing a new Android-based desktop operating system, codenamed Aluminium, which aims to integrate Gemini AI directly into the user experience to compete in the personal computing market.**

**Key points**
- The project was identified through a Google job listing for a Senior Product Manager seeking to build an AI-centric, Android-based desktop platform.
- Android Ecosystem President Sameer Samat confirmed excitement regarding the project's development during MWC 2026.
- Windows currently dominates the global desktop market with approximately 72% share, while Linux and ChromeOS hold significantly smaller portions.
- Previous attempts at Android desktop integration, such as Samsung’s DeX and Google’s own Pixel Slate, have struggled to achieve widespread developer and consumer adoption.
- Reports suggest ChromeOS may remain active until 2033, creating potential compatibility hurdles for the transition to the new Aluminium platform.

**Why it matters: **
The development of Aluminium OS signals Google's ambition to unify its mobile and desktop ecosystems through deep AI integration. However, the project faces significant challenges in overcoming the entrenched market dominance of Windows and the historical difficulty of convincing developers to optimize applications for Android-based desktop environments.
