I always add these 6 quick settings tiles to my stock Android phone when setting it up for the first time
============================================================

**Android 16 introduces a highly customizable quick settings menu on devices like the Pixel 10, allowing users to optimize their daily workflow by adding essential, frequently used shortcut tiles.**

**Key points**
- Android 16 allows users to add, remove, reorganize, and resize tiles within the quick settings menu.
- Essential tiles missing from default stock settings include Location, Hotspot, and a dedicated QR code scanner.
- Users can add system-level shortcuts like the TV remote and Song Search to control smart home devices and identify music.
- The Battery Saver tile provides a convenient toggle to manage power consumption without navigating through deep system menus.
- Tiles are organized into categories such as privacy, connectivity, and utilities, accessible via the pencil edit icon in the quick settings panel.

**Why it matters**
Customizing quick settings significantly improves user efficiency by reducing the time spent navigating through complex system menus for routine tasks. By tailoring these shortcuts, Android 16 users can better manage battery life, privacy, and smart home connectivity directly from their notification shade.
