LG UltraFine 6K Review: A Premium 6K Display Designed With Mac Users in Mind
============================================================

LG’s new 32-inch UltraFine 6K monitor offers Mac users a high-resolution, large-format alternative to Apple’s current display lineup, featuring Thunderbolt 5 connectivity and a premium, Mac-compatible industrial design.

### Key Points
- The 32U990A features a 32-inch 6K Nano IPS Black panel with a resolution of 6,144 x 3,456 pixels.
- Connectivity includes Thunderbolt 5, DisplayPort 2.1, and HDMI 2.1, with 96 watts of power delivery for MacBook charging.
- The display supports height, tilt, swivel, and pivot adjustments, offering more ergonomic flexibility than standard Apple monitor stands.
- It is currently priced at $1,299.99, reflecting a $700 discount from its standard $1,999.99 retail price.
- Performance limitations include a 60Hz refresh rate and a requirement for the LG Switch app to enable macOS keyboard shortcuts for brightness and volume.

### Why it Matters
This monitor fills a significant gap in the Apple ecosystem for users who require a 32-inch screen with Retina-level pixel density. It provides a competitive, high-performance option for creative professionals who need more workspace than Apple’s 27-inch Studio Display currently offers.
