The AI boom is creating surging demand for this nontraditional tech job
============================================================

The rapid expansion of AI infrastructure is driving a surge in demand for physical security professionals to protect the growing number of data centers across the United States.

### Key Points
- Job postings mentioning "physical security" and "data centers" have nearly quadrupled since early 2020, according to Indeed.
- By the end of 2024, there were 1,240 data centers either built or approved for construction across the U.S.
- Security roles now require technical expertise to manage advanced tools like drones, robotics, and remote video monitoring systems.
- Compensation varies significantly, with senior management roles at companies like CoreWeave offering salaries up to $191,000.
- Facilities face diverse threats, including industrial espionage, drone incursions, and public backlash regarding resource consumption and land use.

### Why it Matters
Data centers are increasingly viewed as critical economic infrastructure, necessitating a shift toward more sophisticated and human-led security operations. As these facilities grow in scale and public visibility, the demand for specialized security talent will remain a long-term requirement for the tech industry.
