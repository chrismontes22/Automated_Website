SpaceX IPO Could Make Musk a Trillionaire at Your Expense in "Massive Wealth Transfer": Eric Gardner
============================================================

SpaceX is preparing for a historic $1.8 trillion initial public offering, sparking concerns from analysts that rule changes regarding index fund inclusion may unfairly impact everyday retirement savers.

### Key Points
- SpaceX is targeting a $1.8 trillion valuation, which could potentially make Elon Musk the world’s first trillionaire.
- The company’s valuation relies heavily on unproven AI data center projects rather than its established rocket and satellite internet business.
- Nasdaq reportedly bypassed standard "seasoning" waiting periods to fast-track SpaceX for inclusion in major index funds.
- Financial analysts warn that the IPO’s high revenue multiple—reportedly 94 times—could lead to significant losses for retail investors.
- The IPO follows the merger of SpaceX with Musk’s other ventures, including the struggling social media platform X and the AI firm xAI.

### Why it Matters
This IPO represents a significant shift in how major companies access public markets by leveraging mandatory index fund purchasing to bypass traditional investor scrutiny. If the valuation proves unsustainable, millions of Americans with retirement savings tied to index funds could face substantial financial losses to subsidize the exit of early private investors.
