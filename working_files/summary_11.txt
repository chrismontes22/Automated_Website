The Latest Foldable iPhone Rumors: What's Changed and What We Know Now
============================================================

Apple has reportedly entered a new testing phase for its first foldable smartphone, which is expected to feature a 7.8-inch display and a price exceeding $2,000.

### Key Points
- The device may be branded as the "iPhone Ultra" to distinguish it from competitors like the Samsung Galaxy Fold.
- Design specifications include a 5.5-inch external display and a 7.8-inch internal screen with a 4:3 aspect ratio.
- The chassis is expected to be 4.5mm thin, necessitating the use of Touch ID instead of the traditional Face ID system.
- Analysts and reports suggest a potential release window alongside the iPhone 18 Pro, though production complexities may cause supply shortages.
- Pricing is projected to start at or above the $2,000 threshold, positioning the device as a premium high-end offering.

### Why it Matters
The entry of a foldable device into Apple's lineup represents a significant shift in the company's hardware strategy and its approach to the premium smartphone market. If successful, this product could set a new standard for foldable design and force competitors to adjust their pricing and feature sets to remain relevant.
