New Bit2Watt Attack Could Let Cloud Tenants Disrupt Power Grids Without an Exploit
============================================================

Zhejiang University researchers have identified a potential security vulnerability called Bit2Watt, where cloud-based GPU workloads can be manipulated to destabilize power grids through controlled, high-frequency electrical load oscillations.

### Key Points
- Bit2Watt uses synthetic CUDA kernels or modified LLM training scripts to create rapid, rhythmic power consumption spikes in GPUs.
- Simulations show that 1,000 synchronized GPUs could cause significant harmonic distortion and grid instability, potentially triggering cascading load shedding.
- The technique requires no system exploits or elevated privileges, as it utilizes standard, legitimate computational workloads to modulate power draw.
- Researchers successfully used the power-modulation technique as a covert channel to exfiltrate data via electromagnetic emissions.
- Current data center monitoring tools, such as PDU counters and standard telemetry, lack the sampling frequency required to detect these high-speed oscillations.

### Why it Matters
This research highlights a critical architectural gap where the tight coupling between massive, volatile data center loads and modern power grids creates a new, unpatchable security risk. Because compute and utility infrastructures are managed by separate entities, there is currently no unified oversight to prevent these physical-layer disturbances from impacting grid reliability.
