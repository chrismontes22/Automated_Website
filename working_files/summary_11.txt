Google Drive's 5GB limit forced me to rethink how I use cloud storage
============================================================

Google is reportedly planning to restrict new unverified accounts to 5GB of free storage, requiring a verified phone number to access the standard 15GB of cloud capacity.

### Key Points
- Google is tightening storage policies, potentially reducing free space for unverified accounts from 15GB to 5GB.
- The 15GB free tier is shared across Google Drive, Gmail, and Google Photos, often leading to rapid capacity depletion.
- Users are increasingly adopting multi-layered storage strategies, such as using cloud services for active work and local hardware for archives.
- A Network Attached Storage (NAS) device offers a scalable, self-hosted alternative for managing large media collections and long-term backups.
- Experts recommend separating "live" files from permanent archives to avoid recurring cloud subscription fees and data fragmentation.

### Why it Matters
These policy changes signal a shift in how major tech companies monetize cloud infrastructure, forcing users to reconsider their reliance on free, centralized storage. By moving away from "digital junk drawer" habits, individuals can regain control over their data while reducing long-term dependency on subscription-based cloud services.
