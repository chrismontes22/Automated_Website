I found a hidden Pixel feature that explains what's wrong with Bluetooth (and Android Auto)
============================================================

Google has introduced a new Bluetooth diagnostics tool within the Pixel Troubleshooting app to help users identify and resolve persistent connectivity issues on their Pixel smartphones.

### Key Points
- The new Bluetooth diagnostics tool is available via an update to the Pixel Troubleshooting app, version 1.0.885948717.
- Users can access the feature by navigating to Settings, selecting Connected devices, and tapping Bluetooth diagnostics.
- The tool performs automated tests to detect connection failures and provides specific troubleshooting steps for accessories, wearables, and Android Auto.
- Similar diagnostic features are available on other devices, including Samsung Galaxy phones, which offer phone diagnostics through the Support menu.
- Pixel devices also include built-in tools for troubleshooting battery, touch, and overheating issues via the My Pixel app.

### Why it Matters
These diagnostic tools empower users to resolve common hardware and software connectivity problems independently without needing immediate professional support. By standardizing self-repair features, manufacturers can reduce user frustration and improve the overall reliability of the Android ecosystem.
