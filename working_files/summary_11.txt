Binance.US CEO says exchange is rebuilding, eyes return to 20% U.S. market share
============================================================

Binance.US CEO Stephen Gregory announced plans to regain a 20% market share by leveraging low trading fees and new regulated products following a two-year period of regulatory challenges.

### Key Points
- CEO Stephen Gregory aims to restore Binance.US to its previous 20% U.S. market share after a period of regulatory-driven stagnation.
- The exchange currently offers 0% maker fees and 2-basis-point taker fees to attract retail customers and improve platform liquidity.
- Binance.US is pursuing additional licenses to expand its offerings beyond spot trading into derivatives, perpetual futures, and prediction markets.
- The company maintains a lean operational structure and is actively soliciting feedback from top users to rebuild its competitive standing.
- Centralized exchange spot trading volumes increased 15.3% to $1.11 trillion in June, signaling a broader recovery in the digital asset market.

### Why it Matters
The exchange's aggressive push to regain market share highlights the intensifying competition among U.S. platforms like Coinbase and Kraken. If successful, this expansion could increase liquidity and lower costs for American crypto investors while signaling a potential shift in the domestic regulatory landscape.
