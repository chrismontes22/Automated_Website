See the first X-rays of humans taken in space
============================================================

The Fram2 mission crew successfully performed the first-ever diagnostic x-rays in orbit, demonstrating that portable, wireless imaging technology can effectively function within a microgravity environment for future space exploration.

### Key Points
- The SpaceX Falcon 9 mission launched on March 31, 2025, carrying four crew members into the first human-crewed polar orbit.
- Astronauts used a portable, wireless x-ray system to image the hand, forearm, abdomen, pelvis, and chest after only four hours of training.
- Radiologists confirmed that the quality of space-based x-rays matched preflight and postflight images taken on Earth.
- The crew successfully tested the technology's versatility by x-raying a smartwatch to demonstrate its potential for inspecting mission-critical equipment.
- Lead author Sheyna Gifford of the Mayo Clinic noted that x-rays provide a faster, more accessible diagnostic tool than traditional ultrasound machines.

### Why it Matters
This successful demonstration provides a critical medical and maintenance capability for long-duration missions to the moon and Mars. By enabling rapid diagnostics and non-invasive equipment inspections, this technology significantly enhances the safety and self-sufficiency of future deep-space crews.
