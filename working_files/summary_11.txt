OpenAI Rolls Out GPT-5.6 After Government Greenlight, Announces 'ChatGPT Work'
============================================================

OpenAI has received federal approval to release its GPT-5.6 model and the new ChatGPT Work agent, which integrates directly with professional software tools to automate complex workplace tasks.

### Key Points
- OpenAI is rolling out GPT-5.6 and the ChatGPT Work agent to global users on desktop, web, and mobile platforms.
- The new agent connects to external applications like Slack, Gmail, Google Drive, and various CRM systems to generate documents and web apps.
- The model suite includes "Sol," a high-performance version designed for advanced coding, cybersecurity, and scientific research tasks.
- ChatGPT Work is positioned as a direct competitor to Anthropic’s Claude Cowork, emphasizing lower costs to address industry-wide pricing concerns.
- Desktop users gain immediate access, while mobile and web availability will expand to all user tiers over the next 24 hours.

### Why it Matters
This release marks a significant shift toward autonomous AI agents that can actively manipulate files and workflows across multiple software platforms. By offering these capabilities at a lower price point, OpenAI aims to capture a larger share of the enterprise market while intensifying competition with Anthropic.
