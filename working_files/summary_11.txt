The ELIZA Archaeology Project
============================================================

The ELIZA Archaeology Project is an interdisciplinary research initiative documenting the history, cultural impact, and technical development of the original 1960s MIT chatbot created by Joseph Weizenbaum.

### Key Points
- The project explores the origins of ELIZA, the first chatbot developed at MIT’s Project MAC during the 1960s.
- Researchers are analyzing the programming culture of the era and Joseph Weizenbaum’s subsequent warnings regarding human-machine interactions.
- The team is currently writing a book titled *Inventing ELIZA: How the First Chatbot Shaped the Future of AI*.
- The website provides an accurate technical reimplementation of the original ELIZA program for public use.
- The project investigates ELIZA’s lasting influence on literature, film, and modern artificial intelligence development.

### Why it Matters
This research provides critical historical context for the evolution of conversational agents and the ethical concerns surrounding human-computer interaction. Understanding these early developments helps frame contemporary debates regarding the societal risks and capabilities of modern generative AI systems.
