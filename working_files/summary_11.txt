HP's OmniBook 3 16" beats everything else on the budget laptop market right now — if only the pricing were easier to predict
============================================================

The HP OmniBook 3 16" is a capable, budget-friendly laptop featuring a Snapdragon X processor and exceptional battery life, though its value depends heavily on finding significant retail discounts.

### Key Points
- The laptop features a Qualcomm Snapdragon X (X1-26) chip, 16GB to 32GB of LPDDR5x RAM, and a 16-inch display.
- Pricing fluctuates significantly, with base models frequently available for $400 to $700, while higher-spec configurations can exceed $1,200.
- Key hardware includes a 1080p IR webcam, a full-sized keyboard with a number pad, and a 68Wh battery offering up to 30 hours of life.
- Build quality concerns include a plastic chassis that flexes and creaks, along with speakers that underperform compared to competing devices.
- Connectivity options consist of two USB-C ports, two USB-A ports, and an HDMI 2.1 port for multi-monitor setups.

### Why it Matters
The OmniBook 3 16" serves as a high-value productivity tool when purchased at its frequent sale price, but it struggles to compete with premium alternatives at its full retail cost. Consumers should prioritize finding deep discounts to ensure the hardware's performance and battery life justify the investment over more refined, higher-priced laptops.
