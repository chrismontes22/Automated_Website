Prioritize GPS or Risk Losing Future Wars
============================================================

The United States must prioritize the modernization of its Global Positioning System infrastructure through incremental upgrades to ensure national security and economic stability against increasing foreign electronic warfare threats.

### Key Points
- The Architecture Evolution Plan (AEP) is replacing the troubled Operational Control Segment to provide a more resilient, cyber-secure ground system with 99.9% availability.
- M-Code encryption and Regional Military Protection (RMP) are being deployed on GPS III and GPS IIIF satellites to counter enemy jamming and signal spoofing.
- Current military requirements necessitate a minimum of 24 modernized satellites in orbit to maintain full global positioning, navigation, and timing effectiveness.
- Experts urge Congress and the Department of Defense to protect GPS funding from budget reallocations to prevent critical capability gaps in national infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
GPS serves as the foundational backbone for global financial networks, commercial aviation, and precision-guided military operations. Failing to modernize this infrastructure leaves the United States vulnerable to sophisticated electronic attacks from adversaries like Russia and China that could cripple essential societal functions.
