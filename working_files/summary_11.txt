SpaceX IPO Is A $1.77 Trillion Bet On An Orbital Economy
============================================================

Space Exploration Technologies Corporation, known as SpaceX, is preparing for an initial public offering on the Nasdaq under the ticker symbol SPCX with shares priced at $135 each.

### Key Points
- SpaceX operates over 10,000 active Starlink satellites, representing approximately two-thirds of all working satellites currently in orbit.
- The Starlink division reported an operating profit of $1.19 billion in the first quarter of 2026, despite a total company operating loss of $1.94 billion.
- Starlink currently serves 10.3 million subscribers across 164 countries, providing high-speed, low-latency broadband internet.
- The company’s Starship rocket is designed to carry over 100 tonnes to orbit, potentially reducing launch costs to hundreds of dollars per kilogram.
- Future orbital infrastructure enabled by Starship could support AI data centers, large-scale communications platforms, and space-based solar power systems.

### Why it Matters
The SpaceX IPO represents a shift in investor focus from traditional rocket manufacturing to the development of critical infrastructure for a new orbital economy. By lowering launch costs and expanding global connectivity, the company is positioning itself as a foundational provider for future industries in space.
