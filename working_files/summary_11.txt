US Conservatives organize ‘Nationwide Day of Protest against the unchecked and unwanted expansion of AI data centers’ – organization pledges to give grassroots Americans “a voice in the critical debate over policies”
============================================================

The Republican-aligned group Humans First has scheduled a nationwide day of protest for July 18 to oppose the rapid expansion of AI data centers across the United States.

### Key Points
- Humans First, chaired by Tea Party activist Amy Kremer, is organizing rallies in 13 locations across Georgia, California, Texas, Florida, and Virginia.
- The protests target the construction of massive AI data centers, which have faced over $130 billion in project delays and cancellations from local opposition groups.
- Some Republican officials have requested an FBI investigation into potential foreign influence, alleging that China is fueling opposition to hinder U.S. artificial intelligence development.
- The movement highlights a growing political rift within the Republican base regarding the balance between AI infrastructure growth and local community concerns.

### Why it Matters
This movement signals a significant shift in conservative politics as grassroots opposition to AI infrastructure begins to challenge the party's traditional support for industrial expansion. The resulting tension could complicate national technology policy and create friction between local constituents and party leadership.
