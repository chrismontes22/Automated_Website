Cannes Briefing: Cannes is selling AI transformation. Off stage the talk is about the bill
============================================================

At the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity, advertising industry leaders are shifting their focus from AI’s creative potential to the practical, often unexpected financial costs of implementation.

### Key Points
- Industry executives report that AI token costs and infrastructure requirements are frequently exceeding initial budget projections.
- Companies are moving from experimental AI adoption to evaluating the long-term unit economics and sustainability of these tools.
- Agencies face increasing pressure from clients to provide transparency regarding where and how generative AI is utilized in campaigns.
- Experts note that while AI excels at routine tasks, it struggles with nonlinear creative thinking, forcing brands to weigh efficiency against quality.
- Analysts suggest that debates over AI disclosure may eventually fade as the technology becomes a standard production tool, similar to CGI or Photoshop.

### Why it Matters
The advertising industry is transitioning from a phase of speculative AI hype to a period of rigorous procurement and financial scrutiny. As businesses integrate these technologies into their core operations, the ability to manage AI costs at scale will become a primary factor in determining long-term profitability and agency-client relationships.
