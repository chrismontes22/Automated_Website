The Politics Of AI: Inside Anthropic’s Clash With The Pentagon
============================================================

EconTalk host Russ Roberts and guest Dean Ball examine the complex policy conflict between Anthropic and the Department of War regarding the deployment of AI in classified military operations.

### Key Points
- The Department of War sought to utilize Anthropic’s Claude AI model for broad, lawful military applications.
- Dean Ball and Russ Roberts analyze the tension between private technology companies and federal agencies over national security contracts.
- The discussion addresses critical risks, including the development of autonomous weapons systems and the potential for domestic mass surveillance.
- The conversation explores the erosion of constitutional norms and the decline of institutional trust in the context of rapid technological advancement.

### Why it Matters
This debate highlights the growing friction between private AI developers and government entities as they navigate the ethical boundaries of national security. It underscores the urgent challenge of governing powerful new technologies at a time when traditional institutional frameworks are increasingly strained.
