FBI accuse Steam user of stealing over $220,000 via malware-infected games like BlockBlasters, Lunara and Dashverse
============================================================

The FBI has arrested Florida student Zyaire Dontaevious Zamarion Wilkins for allegedly distributing malware through Steam games to steal over $220,000 in cryptocurrency from approximately 8,000 infected computers.

### Key Points
- Zyaire Dontaevious Zamarion Wilkins was arrested on July 14 following an FBI investigation into malware-infected games on the Steam platform.
- The malicious software was embedded in titles including PirateFi, Dashverse, Lampy, Lunara, and BlockBlasters, all of which have since been removed.
- Investigators linked the suspect to the scheme by tracking stolen cryptocurrency used to purchase Uber gift cards delivered to his residence.
- The operation, which targeted users via Discord, LinkedIn, and Telegram, successfully compromised roughly 8,000 PCs over a two-year period.
- One victim, streamer RastalandTV, reportedly lost $32,000 while playing the game BlockBlasters to raise funds for his cancer treatment.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing threat of sophisticated malware distribution through legitimate gaming platforms and social media channels. It serves as a critical reminder for users to exercise caution when downloading free software from unverified sources to protect their digital assets.
