I stopped hitting cloud storage limits for good by changing this Google Photos option
============================================================

Google Photos users can avoid hitting their 15GB free cloud storage limit by adjusting the default backup quality settings to compress files and save significant account space.

### Key Points
- Google Photos defaults to "Original quality" backups, which consume storage space rapidly by saving high-resolution files.
- Users can switch to "Storage saver" or "Express" modes to compress photos and videos, significantly reducing their impact on the 15GB free tier.
- The "Storage saver" option compresses photos to 16MP and videos to high-definition, balancing quality with space efficiency.
- The "Express" option provides more aggressive compression, reducing photos to 3MP and videos to standard definition.
- To change these settings, navigate to the Google Photos app, select the profile icon, go to Photos settings, and tap Backup.

### Why it Matters
Managing backup quality settings allows users to maximize their limited free cloud storage without needing to purchase additional Google One subscriptions. This simple adjustment helps prevent service interruptions and ensures that essential data remains backed up without exceeding storage quotas.
