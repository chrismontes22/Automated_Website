The $7 Trillion AI Boom Is Running Out of Power
============================================================

Bitzero is pivoting from Bitcoin mining to AI infrastructure, securing a $2.6 billion contract with OneQode to provide high-voltage power and data center capacity for future computing workloads.

### Key Points
- Bitzero signed a binding letter of intent for a 15-year, 110MW lease at its Norway data center, projected to generate $2.6 billion in revenue.
- The company leverages its status as a licensed grid operator to secure electricity at 4.3 cents per kWh, significantly lower than industry averages.
- Bitzero maintains a global infrastructure footprint with over 1 Gigawatt of potential capacity across sites in Norway, Finland, and North Dakota.
- Bitcoin mining operations currently provide immediate cash flow while the company transitions its facilities to support high-margin AI hosting.
- The firm’s infrastructure-first model bypasses the multi-year wait times for utility grid upgrades that currently hinder major technology companies.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of AI is creating a critical physical bottleneck as data centers struggle to secure sufficient power from aging electrical grids. By controlling existing high-voltage connections and energy-ready sites, companies like Bitzero are positioning themselves as essential landlords for the next generation of AI compute.
