Foldable 'iPhone Ultra' Rumored to Feature Vapor Chamber Cooling Despite Thin Design
============================================================

Apple is reportedly on track for a September launch of its first foldable iPhone, the "iPhone Ultra," which will feature advanced vapor chamber cooling despite ongoing manufacturing production challenges.

### Key Points
- The iPhone Ultra is expected to debut in September alongside the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max with a starting price near $2,000.
- Leaker "Fixed Focus Digital" reports the device will utilize vapor chamber cooling to manage thermal performance for its A20 chip.
- The foldable design measures 4.5mm in thickness, leading to the omission of features like Face ID, MagSafe, and a physical SIM slot.
- Production reports indicate difficulties with surface-mount technology and pre-assembly processes, though the overall launch timeline remains unchanged.
- Hardware specifications include a 7.8-inch inner display, a 5.5-inch cover display, and a C2 modem.

### Why it Matters
This device represents Apple's first major entry into the foldable smartphone market, signaling a significant shift in the company's hardware design strategy. Successfully balancing extreme thinness with advanced thermal management will be critical for Apple to compete with established foldable rivals while maintaining its performance standards.
