WhatsApp is now testing its subscription service, here's what you get and how much it costs
============================================================

WhatsApp has officially launched a testing phase for its new subscription service, WhatsApp Plus, offering premium customization and enhanced chat management features to users in select international markets.

### Key Points
- The subscription service is currently being tested in Europe, Pakistan, and Mexico with monthly pricing set at €2.49, PKR 229, and MXN 29 respectively.
- Subscribers gain access to exclusive features including 18 app themes, custom home screen icons, and 10 premium ringtones.
- The service increases the chat pinning limit from three to 20 and introduces bulk settings management for custom chat lists.
- WhatsApp Plus is currently limited to Android users, with plans to expand to iOS in the future.
- All core messaging functions, including end-to-end encryption, remain free for all users without any features being moved behind the paywall.

### Why it Matters
This move marks a significant shift in WhatsApp's monetization strategy by introducing optional paid tiers for power users without compromising the core free experience. If successful, this subscription model could provide a new, sustainable revenue stream for the platform while setting a precedent for how Meta integrates premium features into its messaging ecosystem.
