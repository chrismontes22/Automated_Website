Fighting Spyware: An Update From WhatsApp
============================================================

WhatsApp is seeking to hold spyware firm NSO Group in contempt of court for allegedly violating a permanent injunction by continuing to target users with malicious social engineering campaigns.

### Key Points
- WhatsApp identified NSO-linked attempts to trick users into clicking malicious links and creating unauthorized test accounts on the platform.
- The company released specific threat indicators, including three malicious domains, to help users identify potential targeting across various communication platforms.
- NSO Group remains on the U.S. government’s Entity List due to its history of developing surveillance tools used against journalists, officials, and humanitarian workers.
- WhatsApp is contributing to the Spyware Accountability Initiative to support global organizations focused on forensic research and advocacy against spyware.
- A coalition of 12 civil rights organizations recently filed amicus briefs to support the legal fight against NSO Group’s appeal of the 2023 injunction.

### Why it Matters
This legal escalation highlights the ongoing struggle between major technology platforms and surveillance-for-hire firms that exploit vulnerabilities in browsers and operating systems. The outcome of this case carries significant implications for global digital privacy and the enforcement of U.S. national security restrictions on private spyware developers.
