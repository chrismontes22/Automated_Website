SSD prices are sky high, but these 3 Prime Day deals make upgrading hurt less
============================================================

Amazon Prime Day offers significant discounts on high-performance SSDs from Samsung and Western Digital, providing a timely opportunity for consumers to upgrade hardware amid ongoing global NAND memory shortages.

### Key Points
- The Samsung 990 Pro 2TB features PCIe 4.0 x4 speeds up to 7,450 MB/s and includes 2GB of DRAM cache for demanding workloads.
- Western Digital’s 1TB My Passport Portable SSD offers shock-resistant storage with read speeds up to 1,050 MB/s and 256-bit AES hardware encryption.
- The Samsung 990 EVO Plus 1TB supports PCIe 4.0 x4 or PCIe 5.0 x2 configurations with read speeds reaching 7,150 MB/s.
- Global NAND shortages have caused SSD prices to more than double, with market analysts expecting these elevated costs to persist for several years.
- Prime Day promotions currently offer discounts of up to 42% on select storage hardware to help mitigate rising consumer costs.

### Why it Matters
These discounts provide a rare window of affordability for users facing a long-term trend of rising hardware prices driven by AI-related memory demand. Securing high-performance storage now helps consumers avoid the projected multi-year period of inflated costs while ensuring their systems remain capable of handling modern gaming and professional workloads.
