Who Owns Anker, The Mobile Accessory Brand?
============================================================

Anker Innovations, the global mobile accessory manufacturer known for its charging products and smart home gadgets, operates as a publicly traded company listed on the Shenzhen and Hong Kong exchanges.

### Key Points
- Founded in 2011 by former Google engineer Steven Yang, the company originally sold replacement laptop batteries before pivoting to power banks in 2012.
- Founders Steven Yang and CEO Zhao Dongping maintain majority control of the company, collectively holding approximately 51% of total shares.
- The company went public on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange in 2020 and expanded its listing to the Hong Kong Stock Exchange on July 2, 2026.
- Anker operates a diverse portfolio of sub-brands, including Eufy for smart home devices, Soundcore for audio equipment, and Anker Solix for portable power stations.
- Institutional investors, including the National Council for Social Security Fund, hold an 11.5% stake, while individual public investors own roughly 26% of the firm.

### Why it Matters
Anker’s transition from an Amazon-focused startup to a publicly traded multinational corporation highlights the significant market demand for reliable third-party mobile accessories. Its diversified brand strategy allows the company to maintain a strong competitive position across the consumer electronics, smart home, and renewable energy sectors.
