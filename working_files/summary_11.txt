The Venture-Capital Populist
============================================================

Venture capitalist David Sacks leveraged his influence as a Trump administration advisor to deregulate the artificial intelligence and cryptocurrency sectors while navigating significant conflicts of interest and political controversy.

### Key Points
- David Sacks served as a special government employee and "czar" for AI and crypto, allowing him to maintain his role at Craft Ventures while shaping federal policy.
- Sacks successfully pushed for the GENIUS Act, which established a regulatory framework for stablecoins, and influenced the lifting of export bans on advanced AI chips to China.
- The Trump administration’s tech policies, including the reversal of AI safety mandates, faced criticism for prioritizing the commercial interests of Silicon Valley over national security and public safety.
- Ethics experts and congressional critics raised concerns regarding Sacks’s continued financial stakes in hundreds of AI-related companies while overseeing industry regulations.
- Sacks’s tenure concluded in March 2026, after which he was appointed co-chair of the President’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology.

### Why it Matters
The integration of Silicon Valley venture capitalists into the highest levels of government signals a shift toward a policy environment where private industry interests and state power are increasingly indistinguishable. This fusion of influence risks eroding public trust and institutional oversight, potentially leaving the American economy and its regulatory future vulnerable to the priorities of a small, elite group of tech investors.
