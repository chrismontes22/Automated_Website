Software Developers Say AI Is Rotting Their Brains
============================================================

Major technology companies including Google, Microsoft, and Meta are increasingly relying on AI to generate code, sparking internal debates regarding software quality, developer skill degradation, and widespread workforce layoffs.

### Key Points
- Google reported that 75% of its new code is AI-generated, while Microsoft projects 95% of its codebase will be AI-produced by 2030.
- Developers report that AI-generated code often requires significant manual correction, leading to increased technical debt and frustration among engineering teams.
- Tech executives are utilizing AI implementation as a primary justification for significant workforce reductions across the industry.
- Recent layoffs include approximately 8,000 employees at Meta, 1,000 at Snapchat, and voluntary retirement offers for 7% of Microsoft’s American workforce.
- Employees express concerns that mandatory AI adoption is causing professional de-skilling and creating security vulnerabilities within complex software architectures.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI-generated code signals a fundamental change in software development that prioritizes corporate efficiency metrics over long-term product stability. This trend suggests that businesses across all sectors may soon face significant technical debt and reduced human expertise as AI tools replace traditional engineering workflows.
