Alibaba To Ban Claude Code In Workplace Over Alleged Backdoor Risks
============================================================

Alibaba has officially banned employees from using Anthropic’s Claude Code tool, directing staff to its internal Qoder platform following allegations of unauthorized model distillation and data security disputes.

### Key Points
- Anthropic accused Alibaba of illicitly extracting its AI model capabilities through a process known as distillation to accelerate Chinese development.
- Claude Code includes mechanisms that inspect user environments, such as timezones and proxy data, to identify and restrict unauthorized access.
- Anthropic stated these tracking features were launched in March to prevent account abuse and protect its proprietary technology from being copied.
- Alibaba’s internal directive mandates that employees transition to the company's proprietary Qoder platform for their development needs.
- The dispute highlights ongoing geopolitical tensions and the intense competition between U.S. and Chinese firms to lead in artificial intelligence innovation.

### Why it Matters
This conflict underscores the growing legal and compliance risks companies face as they attempt to protect advanced AI models from unauthorized international replication. It also illustrates the technical challenges firms encounter when trying to enforce regional usage restrictions in an increasingly globalized digital infrastructure.
