Broad-Based Growth Drivers Lifted nLIGHT (LASR) in Q1
============================================================

Prosper Stars & Stripes reported a 5.6% net loss in Q1 2026, though the fund highlighted significant gains from its long-term investment in defense technology firm nLIGHT, Inc.

### Key Points
- The Prosper Stars & Stripes fund underperformed the Russell 2000 Index and HFRX Equity Hedge Index during the first quarter of 2026.
- nLIGHT, Inc. (NASDAQ:LASR) served as the second-largest contributor to the fund's long book before the position was exited in April 2026.
- Defense sector revenue for nLIGHT grew from 18% of total sales in 2018 to 70% in 2026, driven by directed energy technology.
- nLIGHT shares reached a market capitalization of $3.74 billion, with a 52-week gain of 251.39% as of June 17, 2026.
- The fund cited the U.S. Department of War’s strategic shift toward cost-effective directed energy systems as a primary driver for nLIGHT's growth.

### Why it Matters
This report highlights the growing financial importance of directed energy technology within the defense sector as governments prioritize cost-effective alternatives to traditional missile interceptors. Investors are increasingly monitoring companies like nLIGHT that provide specialized hardware for mission-critical military applications amid rising geopolitical and inflationary pressures.
