My burner email blocklist blocked me
============================================================

Software developers are increasingly urged to stop blocking privacy-focused email aliases at signup, as these tools protect users without effectively deterring the sophisticated spammers they were meant to stop.

### Key Points
- Developers are encouraged to distinguish between public, shared burner inboxes and private, permanent email forwarding aliases.
- Popular privacy tools like Apple Hide My Email and Firefox Relay are now mainstream, making them common for legitimate users.
- Automated domain blocklists often fail to stop determined abusers who utilize custom domains or Gmail sub-addressing to bypass filters.
- The author has deprecated the Burnex library and proposed splitting blocklists into separate categories for public inboxes and personal aliases.
- Relying on email verification alone is insufficient for proving account authenticity or preventing platform abuse.

### Why it Matters
Blocking privacy-conscious users creates unnecessary friction and alienates customers who prioritize data security and spam reduction. By refining signup policies, companies can maintain security without penalizing legitimate users who use modern tools to manage their digital identity.
