Two-Thirds of Ransomware Victims Say AI Boosted Attack Effectiveness
============================================================

A new report from Proofpoint reveals that 65% of organizations hit by ransomware attribute the increased effectiveness of these attacks to the integration of generative AI tools.

### Key Points
- The 2026 AI-Era Ransomware Report, released July 22, identifies AI as a standard component in modern cyberattacks.
- Attackers use AI to generate highly convincing phishing emails, malicious attachments, and sophisticated credential theft campaigns.
- Human interaction remains the primary vulnerability, with 47% of attacks involving malicious links and 46% using deceptive attachments.
- Approximately 40% of victims failed to detect threats because AI-generated lures appeared indistinguishable from legitimate business communications.
- One-third of surveyed organizations reported that their existing email security controls failed to identify malicious activity entirely.

### Why it Matters
The integration of AI into the attacker playbook shifts the primary security challenge from technical endpoint defense to the protection of human identities and communications. Organizations must now prioritize advanced detection at the point of entry to prevent attackers from exploiting trust before extortion occurs.
