Stanford was their golden ticket - could AI help or hinder that?
============================================================

Stanford University graduates expressed significant apprehension toward artificial intelligence during recent commencement ceremonies, leading to protests and walkouts during an address by Google CEO Sundar Pichai.

### Key Points
- Stanford graduates staged walkouts and carried protest signs during Sundar Pichai’s commencement speech to criticize Google’s AI contracts and corporate ethics.
- Students expressed concerns regarding AI’s environmental impact, the potential for "cognitive offloading," and the technology's role in displacing entry-level job opportunities.
- Research from the Federal Reserve Bank of New York and Stanford indicates that early-career employment is declining in sectors most exposed to AI automation.
- Despite widespread skepticism, some graduates view AI as a transformative tool for scientific discovery and climate modeling if developed and managed ethically.
- Stanford remains a central hub for AI development, with deep historical ties to the industry and a high concentration of influential tech companies nearby.

### Why it Matters
The backlash at a premier institution like Stanford highlights a growing generational divide regarding the rapid integration of AI into the workforce and society. As these graduates enter a shifting job market, their concerns reflect broader anxieties about ethical development, environmental sustainability, and the long-term impact of automation on human cognition.
