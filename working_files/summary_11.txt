WeRide and Uber launch commercial robotaxi service in Zurich, expanding autonomous vehicle push across Europe
============================================================

WeRide and Uber have announced a partnership to launch commercial autonomous robotaxi services in the Greater Zurich Region later this year, marking a significant expansion into the European market.

### Key Points
- WeRide and Uber plan to launch autonomous ride-hailing services in Zurich, pending approval from Switzerland’s Federal Roads Office.
- The partnership aims to achieve fully driverless operations in both Zurich and Madrid by mid-2027.
- Initial deployments in Zurich will include safety operators, with the transition to fully autonomous vehicles expected within approximately one year.
- Zurich is the fifth city activated under a global agreement between the two companies that covers 15 cities.
- Riders will access the autonomous fleet exclusively through the existing Uber mobile application.

### Why it Matters
This partnership highlights Uber’s strategic shift toward becoming a platform for third-party autonomous technology rather than developing its own proprietary systems. By integrating WeRide’s Level 4 technology, Uber aims to improve long-term unit economics by reducing reliance on human drivers while navigating complex European regulatory environments.
