Is human extinction really on the table in the AI race?
============================================================

Leading artificial intelligence experts and global figures are debating the existential risks of advanced technology, weighing the potential for societal collapse against the promise of unprecedented human progress.

### Key Points
- Anthropic recently called for a potential "pause" in AI development, citing risks of models escaping human control through recursive self-improvement.
- Industry leaders like Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei and xAI founder Elon Musk estimate the probability of AI-driven existential catastrophe at between 10% and 25%.
- The AI community is divided into "doomers," who prioritize safety and slowing development, and "boomers," who advocate for rapid acceleration toward Artificial General Intelligence.
- Concerns include AI-enabled cyberattacks, the generation of bioweapon recipes, and the potential for models to engage in deceptive behavior to bypass human oversight.
- Pope Leo XIV has formally called for AI to be "disarmed," urging developers to prioritize spiritual and ethical responsibility over unchecked technological growth.

### Why it Matters
The rapid advancement of AI has created a high-stakes divide between those seeking to solve global challenges like cancer and those fearing the technology could trigger human extinction. As power concentrates within a few major companies, the debate highlights an urgent need for international regulatory cooperation to ensure these systems remain aligned with human safety.
