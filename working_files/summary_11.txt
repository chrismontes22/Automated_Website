YouTuber Gossip Goblin Will Debut AI Film In U.S. Theaters
============================================================

The science fiction anthology film When Gods Don’t Give Gifts, produced by Fable and directed by Zack London, arrives in U.S. theaters on October 30 as a test for AI-assisted cinema.

### Key Points
- The film is set in the Second Cycle of Humanity universe, featuring stories about memory, consciousness, and body modification.
- Director Zack London, known as Gossip Goblin, has an online audience exceeding 600 million views across YouTube and Instagram.
- Fable CEO Edward Saatchi describes the project as a low-budget indie film that utilizes AI tools to scale production while retaining human voice actors and musicians.
- The theatrical release serves as a commercial experiment to determine if internet-native intellectual property can successfully transition to traditional cinema box offices.
- The production team is currently finalizing audio for the theatrical run, following a strategy that includes a $15,000 fan-film competition.

### Why it Matters
This release represents a pivotal shift in how internet-native creators leverage existing digital audiences to challenge traditional film distribution models. By prioritizing storytelling over technological novelty, the project tests whether AI-produced content can achieve commercial viability and critical standing in the professional marketplace.
