Report: Cisco could acquire AI agent security startup Astrix Security for $250M+
============================================================

Cisco Systems is reportedly in advanced negotiations to acquire Tel Aviv-based AI security startup Astrix Security in a deal potentially valued between $250 million and $350 million.

### Key Points
- Astrix Security specializes in identifying and securing AI agents, non-human identities, and MCP servers within corporate networks.
- The platform provides automated risk mitigation, including just-in-time access controls and the ability to revoke permissions for malicious agents.
- Astrix has previously secured funding from investors including Workday Inc. and the Anthology Fund, which is backed by Anthropic PBC.
- This potential acquisition follows Cisco’s recent agreement to purchase Galileo Technologies, a startup focused on AI hallucination firewalls.
- The proposed deal valuation represents approximately triple the total funding Astrix has raised since its inception.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition highlights Cisco's aggressive strategy to integrate specialized security layers into its enterprise portfolio as businesses increasingly adopt autonomous AI agents. By securing these non-human identities, Cisco aims to address critical vulnerabilities that traditional cybersecurity tools are not yet equipped to manage.
