============================================================

Google maintains its dominance in the global search market with 90% share, though rising competition from AI tools and platform-specific search engines is forcing a shift in SEO strategies.

### Key Points
- Google’s global search market share remains at 90.01% as of March 2026, despite fluctuations and increased competition from AI-driven platforms.
- Microsoft Bing holds approximately 5% of global traffic, with significantly higher penetration in the U.S. and on desktop devices.
- AI search tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity are rapidly growing, with ChatGPT now reaching 900 million weekly active users as of February 2026.
- Amazon and TikTok have emerged as critical discovery platforms, capturing significant search intent for products and recommendations outside traditional search engines.
- Regional engines like Yandex and Baidu remain essential for businesses targeting Russia and China, respectively, due to their localized market dominance.

### Why it Matters
The era of relying exclusively on Google for search traffic is ending as user behavior fragments across AI interfaces, social platforms, and e-commerce marketplaces. Businesses must now diversify their digital presence and adopt Generative Engine Optimization to remain visible in an increasingly complex and automated search landscape.
