Xbox CEO Asha Sharma Joins Federal Reserve Task Force On ‘Jobs And Productivity’ Days After Announcing Mass Layoffs
============================================================

The Federal Reserve has appointed Xbox CEO Asha Sharma and other industry leaders to a new task force focused on analyzing the economic impacts of artificial intelligence and productivity.

### Key Points
- Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh announced the formation of new advisory task forces on July 9 to modernize economic policy approaches.
- Xbox CEO Asha Sharma will lead a task force examining how AI and emerging technologies influence national jobs and productivity metrics.
- The task force includes Stanford economist Charles I. Jones and venture capitalist Marc Andreessen as key advisors.
- The appointment follows Sharma’s recent announcement of 3,200 layoffs across Microsoft’s gaming division to reduce organizational complexity.
- The initiative aims to refine the Federal Reserve’s analytical tools to better align with current economic shifts and technological advancements.

### Why it Matters
This collaboration signals a strategic effort by the Federal Reserve to integrate private-sector expertise into its policy framework regarding the rapid adoption of artificial intelligence. By involving leaders from the tech and venture capital sectors, the central bank seeks to better understand how automation and corporate restructuring influence long-term employment and economic stability.
