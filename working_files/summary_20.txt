'WhatsApp Gold' is real scam, but 'Martinelli' video allegedly spreading hoax doesn't exist
============================================================

Social media users are circulating warnings about the "WhatsApp Gold" update, a decade-old scam designed to install malicious software and compromise personal data on mobile devices.

### Key Points
- WhatsApp confirmed that "WhatsApp Gold" and "WhatsApp Plus" are not official products and pose significant security risks to users.
- The scam typically uses fraudulent links promising exclusive features like new emojis or improved video quality to harvest sensitive personal information.
- Cybersecurity firms including Norton and Avira advise users to download the app only through official channels like the Apple App Store or Google Play Store.
- The "Martinelli" video, often linked to these warnings, is a separate, long-standing hoax with no evidence of existence or ability to hack phones.
- Experts recommend blocking suspicious numbers immediately and avoiding interaction with any messages promoting premium or unofficial versions of the messaging platform.

### Why it Matters
These recurring hoaxes highlight the persistent threat of malware distribution through social engineering and deceptive messaging campaigns. Understanding that official apps do not offer premium variants is essential for protecting personal data and preventing unauthorized device access.
