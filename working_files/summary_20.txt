John Ternus set to re-establish importance of Apple’s design team when he takes over as CEO: report
============================================================

Incoming Apple CEO John Ternus plans to restore the influence of the company's industrial design team, potentially shifting power away from operations to prioritize product aesthetics and innovation.

### Key Points
- Bloomberg reporter Mark Gurman notes that Apple’s design influence declined significantly following the 2019 departure of former design chief Jony Ive.
- The company currently lacks a dedicated senior design executive, with oversight previously falling under Chief Operating Officer Jeff Williams.
- John Ternus, who assumes the CEO role on September 1, has reportedly spent significant time collaborating with the industrial design group.
- Apple is positioning Ternus as the leader behind recent hardware successes, including the MacBook Neo and an upcoming foldable iPhone.
- Ternus has publicly emphasized that maintaining superior product design remains a core priority for the company's future roadmap.

### Why it Matters
This leadership shift signals a potential strategic pivot for Apple as it moves away from an operations-led culture toward a design-centric product philosophy. Re-empowering the design team could influence the company's long-term competitive edge and the aesthetic direction of its next generation of hardware.
