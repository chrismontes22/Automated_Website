How Did the Feds Get Into Anti-ICE Activists’ Signal Messages?
============================================================

Federal authorities in Minnesota have indicted 15 activists for allegedly using Signal to coordinate anti-ICE protests, highlighting how physical device access can bypass encrypted messaging security measures.

### Key Points
- The Department of Homeland Security indicted 15 individuals for conspiracy to impede federal officers and other charges related to ICE-tracking activities.
- Evidence in the case includes over 100 messages, call logs, and transcripts recovered from Signal, suggesting investigators gained access to physical devices.
- While Signal provides end-to-end encryption for data in transit, it does not protect communications if a device is seized and unlocked by law enforcement.
- Experts recommend hardening security by enabling disappearing messages, disabling call history integration, and regularly deleting entire chat threads to minimize forensic footprints.

### Why it Matters
This case demonstrates that encrypted messaging apps are not a complete safeguard against surveillance when physical hardware is compromised. It serves as a critical reminder for activists and privacy-conscious users that digital security requires both robust software settings and strict physical device management.
