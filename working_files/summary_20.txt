'Users no longer need to choose between powerful AI capabilities and meaningful privacy protections': Proton makes its Lumo privacy-first ChatGPT alternative a lot more powerful
============================================================

Proton has launched Lumo 2.0, a privacy-focused AI assistant featuring multimodal capabilities, persistent memory, and advanced reasoning modes designed to compete with major industry models like ChatGPT and Gemini.

### Key Points
- Lumo 2.0 introduces image generation, recognition, and cited live web search capabilities.
- The service utilizes zero-access encryption and no-logs policies to ensure user data privacy.
- Performance benchmarks show the Lite and Max models scoring 127% and 240% higher than the previous 1.4 version.
- Pricing includes a free tier, a $12.99 monthly Plus plan, and a $14.99 per-user Professional tier for teams.
- The underlying technology integrates models including Qwen 3.5 and GLM 5.2 to support complex reasoning tasks.

### Why it Matters
Proton’s update provides a viable alternative for privacy-conscious users who are wary of the data-harvesting practices common among major proprietary AI developers. By balancing competitive performance with strict encryption, the company is carving out a distinct market niche for secure, enterprise-ready artificial intelligence tools.
