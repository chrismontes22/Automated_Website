Jamie Dimon says AI could help shorten work week, cure cancer
============================================================

JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon predicts that artificial intelligence will significantly improve human longevity and productivity while potentially reducing the standard work week to three and a half days.

### Key Points
- Jamie Dimon expects AI to facilitate medical breakthroughs, including potential cures for cancer and increased life expectancy.
- The banking executive anticipates that AI-driven productivity gains could shorten the average work week to 3.5 days within 30 years.
- Dimon warned that rapid technological adoption poses risks to the labor market by potentially displacing millions of workers.
- He advocated for public-private partnerships to scale worker retraining programs and transition employees into new sectors.
- Dimon suggested that redeploying labor into critical industries like advanced manufacturing could help address current workforce shortages.

### Why it Matters
This perspective from a major financial leader highlights the tension between long-term economic prosperity and short-term labor market instability caused by automation. It underscores the growing necessity for coordinated policy and corporate strategies to manage the societal transition as AI becomes deeply embedded in the global economy.
