Microsoft accidentally made a better Surface by failing its own AI requirements
============================================================

Microsoft has released new 12-inch Surface Pro and 13-inch Surface Laptop models featuring 8GB of RAM, which excludes them from the company's Copilot+ PC branding and AI-integrated feature set.

### Key Points
- Microsoft launched new Surface Pro and Surface Laptop configurations equipped with 8GB of RAM.
- The 8GB memory limit prevents these devices from meeting the minimum hardware requirements for Copilot+ PC certification.
- These models do not support exclusive AI features like Recall, Click To Do, or advanced Windows Studio Effects.
- The shift reflects a broader industry trend of reintroducing 8GB RAM options due to ongoing component supply constraints.
- Microsoft appears to be de-emphasizing Copilot+ branding in its recent marketing materials for new hardware releases.

### Why it Matters
These new Surface configurations offer a leaner Windows 11 experience by omitting resource-heavy AI tools that many mainstream users may find unnecessary. This development highlights a potential cooling of consumer demand for AI-branded PCs and suggests that hardware manufacturers are prioritizing affordability and core performance over integrated generative AI features.
