Seth Michael Larson: United Nations Open Source Week 2026
============================================================

The Sovereign Tech Agency hosted the 2026 UN Open Source Week in New York City, focusing on the intersection of open source software, artificial intelligence, and global digital infrastructure.

### Key Points
- The event featured daily themes including Maintain-a-thon, Open Source × AI, Digital Public Infrastructure, OSPOs for Good, and Community Day.
- Participants explored the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals to align open source projects with global humanitarian and infrastructure needs.
- A technical session addressed the "vulnerability flood," examining how LLMs impact security, vulnerability disclosure, and the risks of automated code rewrites.
- Discussions highlighted the need for mental health support for maintainers and clearer job descriptions to combat burnout in open source communities.
- Experts noted that open-weights models are rapidly closing the performance gap with "frontier" AI models, potentially democratizing advanced cybersecurity capabilities within 6 to 12 months.

### Why it Matters
Open source software is increasingly recognized as a critical component for global digital infrastructure and achieving international development goals. As AI-driven security threats evolve, the collaboration between developers and policymakers is essential to ensure that open source projects remain secure, sustainable, and effectively managed.
