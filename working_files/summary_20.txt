Roblox wants kids to vibe-code their own video games
============================================================

Roblox has introduced an AI-powered tool called Build that allows users to create playable video games directly within the mobile app by entering simple descriptive text prompts.

### Key Points
- The Build tool enables users to generate game environments and mechanics by typing plain-language prompts into the Roblox mobile application.
- Testing for the new feature is scheduled to begin in New Zealand on July 28 for age-verified users aged 9 and older.
- Roblox intends to offer a free version of the tool alongside future paid tiers that provide access to more advanced development capabilities.
- This initiative aligns with the industry trend of "vibe coding," which utilizes generative AI to lower the technical barriers required for software development.

### Why it Matters
This tool significantly lowers the barrier to entry for game development by allowing younger users to create content without needing traditional programming skills or expensive hardware. By integrating these capabilities into its mobile app, Roblox is positioning itself to expand its creator base and increase user engagement across its platform.
