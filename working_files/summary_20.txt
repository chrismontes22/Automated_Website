CEO of $248 billion cybersecurity company says workers are about to face a ‘Darwinian moment’ thanks to AI: Evolve or get cut
============================================================

Palo Alto Networks CEO Nikesh Arora warns that employees must rapidly develop AI proficiency or face career displacement as companies increasingly automate roles and prioritize tech-savvy talent for survival.

### Key Points
- Palo Alto Networks CEO Nikesh Arora claims 90% of employees at large corporations currently lack the necessary AI skills to remain competitive.
- A 2025 Orgvue study indicates that 39% of business leaders have already reduced their workforce due to the implementation of AI technologies.
- Companies including Coinbase, Block, and Cloudflare have executed significant layoffs to restructure operations around AI-driven efficiency.
- Arora is actively replacing departing staff at his 21,000-employee firm by recruiting exclusively through hackathons to ensure new hires possess advanced technical capabilities.
- The company aims to transform 20% to 25% of its workforce within one year by leveraging natural attrition and targeted AI-focused hiring.

### Why it Matters
This shift signals a fundamental change in the labor market where adaptability and self-taught technical skills are becoming the primary requirements for job security. As major firms prioritize AI fluency over traditional experience, workers face increasing pressure to integrate these tools into their daily workflows to avoid obsolescence.
