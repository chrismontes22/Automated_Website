‘Declarations: Black Americans & The Revolutionary War’ Doc Filmmakers On Using GenAI To “Give Our Historical Subjects Agency”
============================================================

The PBS documentary Declarations: Black Americans and the Revolutionary War utilizes generative AI to animate historical portraits and provide visual agency to underrepresented figures from the American independence era.

### Key Points
- The documentary premieres on PBS on June 29 at 10 p.m. ET.
- Directors Stacey Holman and Maya Tepler use AI to animate portraits of James Lafayette, Harry Washington, Elizabeth Freeman, and Abraham Peyton Skipwith.
- Artist Hudson Campbell created oil paintings based on historical context, which were then animated using generative AI tools.
- The project was developed with oversight from historical advisors including Dr. Ed Ayers and filmmaker Sam Pollard.
- The film aims to address the lack of archival visual records for Black Americans during the 18th century.

### Why it Matters
This project establishes a potential ethical framework for using generative AI to reconstruct historical narratives where traditional archival materials are missing or biased. By prioritizing artistic vision and historical accuracy, the filmmakers demonstrate how emerging technology can be used to responsibly reclaim and center marginalized voices in American history.
