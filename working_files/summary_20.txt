I used my Pixel as a Windows webcam and immediately stopped using the built-in one
============================================================

**Google Pixel users can significantly improve their video conferencing quality by using their smartphone as a high-resolution webcam instead of relying on standard built-in laptop cameras.**

**Key points**
- Android users can connect their phones to Windows 11 PCs via USB-C or the wireless Phone Link feature to serve as a high-quality webcam.
- Manual configuration through Android settings is often required, as the device may not automatically trigger the "Webcam" mode upon connection.
- The Pixel 9 camera offers superior color reproduction and lighting handling compared to standard integrated laptop webcams and dedicated hardware like the Logitech C920.
- Windows 11 users can manage wireless camera integration through the "Mobile devices" section in system settings.
- Third-party applications like DroidCam remain a viable alternative for users seeking additional functionality or compatibility outside of native OS features.

**Why it matters**
Using a smartphone as a webcam provides a cost-effective way to achieve professional-grade video quality for remote meetings without purchasing additional hardware. This functionality leverages the advanced sensors already present in modern mobile devices to overcome the limitations of standard, low-resolution laptop cameras.
