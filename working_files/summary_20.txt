Who Does What? Team Topologies for the Agentic Platform
============================================================

This article explores how organizations can use the Team Topologies framework to manage the cognitive load and operational complexity inherent in scaling agentic AI application production.

### Key Points
- The agentic platform absorbs technical anticipation burdens, allowing non-technical business teams to act as stream-aligned product teams.
- Four team types—stream-aligned, platform, enabling, and complicated subsystem—are adapted to distribute AI production responsibilities and technical complexity.
- The platform team industrializes systemic guardrails, security, and deployment pipelines, moving from collaboration to X-as-a-Service as maturity increases.
- Enabling teams provide temporary support to bridge the gap between business intent and technical requirements until product teams achieve autonomy.
- A "graduation" mechanism, based on the rule of three, allows recurring local guardrails to be promoted into systemic platform capabilities.

### Why it Matters
By shifting the developer's role from building applications to building platforms, organizations can empower business experts to drive production without needing deep technical expertise. This structural approach prevents the "agentic waterfall" and shadow IT risks, ensuring that AI-driven development remains scalable, reliable, and governed.
