A USB-C dongle unlocked audio quality my phone was hiding all along
============================================================

Using a USB-C digital-to-analog converter (DAC) allows smartphone users to bypass system-level audio resampling and compression, enabling bit-perfect playback for high-fidelity lossless music streaming services.

### Key Points
- Android devices typically downsample high-resolution audio to 16-bit or 24-bit, 48kHz, limiting the fidelity of lossless files.
- External USB-C DACs, such as the Linsoul Kiwi Ears Allegro Mini, support up to 32-bit/384kHz audio playback.
- Bluetooth connections introduce compression and latency, making wired connections essential for true high-resolution audio performance.
- Dongle DACs provide necessary power to drive high-impedance headphones that standard smartphone ports cannot adequately support.
- Many modern DACs offer versatile connectivity, including both 3.5mm single-ended and 4.4mm balanced headphone outputs.

### Why it Matters
While many smartphones can technically play lossless files, external DACs ensure the audio remains uncompressed and bit-perfect by bypassing internal system limitations. This hardware is essential for audiophiles seeking to maximize the performance of high-end wired headphones and high-resolution streaming subscriptions.
