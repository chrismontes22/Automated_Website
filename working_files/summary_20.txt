Carnival confirms ShinyHunters cruised off with 6M customer records after April breach
============================================================

23andMe faces a new lawsuit from the California Attorney General regarding a 2023 data breach, alleging the company downplayed the incident while paying a ransom to the attackers.

### Key Points
- The California Attorney General filed a lawsuit against 23andMe following a massive 2023 data breach.
- The legal action claims the genetics company failed to adequately protect sensitive user information.
- Prosecutors allege 23andMe downplayed the severity of the leak while secretly paying a ransom to hackers.
- The breach exposed the genetic data of millions of customers, raising significant privacy and security concerns.

### Why it Matters
This lawsuit highlights the growing legal and regulatory scrutiny surrounding how biotechnology firms handle sensitive genetic information. It serves as a critical reminder for companies that transparency and robust cybersecurity protocols are essential to maintaining consumer trust and avoiding severe legal consequences.
