A24 Defends Google AI Partnership, Studio Doesn’t “Necessarily Love” GenAI Content But “We’d Rather Have A Seat At The Table”
============================================================

A24 has entered a $75 million research partnership with Google DeepMind to develop behind-the-scenes production tools while maintaining full creative control over its intellectual property and filmmaking processes.

### Key Points
- Google DeepMind is investing $75 million into a research partnership with the independent production company A24.
- A24 spokesperson Sophia Shin stated the collaboration focuses on improving production workflows rather than generating AI-based onscreen content.
- The agreement grants A24 access to DeepMind’s research, infrastructure, and global reach to help shape future industry tools.
- A24 confirmed it has no plans to utilize its existing intellectual property to train or create generative AI models.
- The partnership aims to give filmmakers a voice in the development of new technology instead of relying on pre-built industry solutions.

### Why it Matters
This collaboration highlights the growing tension between traditional creative studios and the rapid integration of generative AI in Hollywood production pipelines. By securing a seat at the development table, A24 aims to influence how new technologies are built to ensure they serve artist needs rather than replacing human creative labor.
