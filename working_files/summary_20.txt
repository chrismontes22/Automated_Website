Global Market Today: Asian stocks slip, Samsung slides after results
============================================================

The MSCI Asia Pacific Index declined 0.3% as technology stocks retreated, despite Samsung reporting a 19-fold surge in quarterly profit driven by high demand for artificial intelligence memory chips.

### Key Points
- The MSCI Asia Pacific Index fell 0.3%, with the South Korean Kospi Index dropping 3.5% during the session.
- Samsung shares declined over 5% despite a 19-fold increase in quarterly profit fueled by AI-related memory chip demand.
- SK Hynix shares fell 1% as the company initiated the formal marketing process for a potential US stock listing.
- West Texas Intermediate crude oil traded below $69 per barrel amid concerns regarding global supply increases.
- Hedge funds have reached their most negative position on the Japanese yen since 2007, with the currency trading at 162.08 per dollar.

### Why it Matters
Investors are increasingly questioning whether the current artificial intelligence boom can sustain its momentum and justify high capital expenditures. The market is now focused on whether AI-driven productivity gains will eventually deliver the earnings growth required to support current valuations.
