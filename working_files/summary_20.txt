Apple iPhone 18 Pro secrets leaked in Tata Electronics hack: What we know
============================================================

The ransomware group World Leaks has stolen 630 gigabytes of confidential data from Apple supplier Tata Electronics, exposing sensitive supply chain details and images of the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro.

### Key Points
- The breach involved the theft and public release of over 200,000 files containing proprietary component data and supplier contracts.
- Tata Electronics confirmed the cybersecurity incident and is currently conducting a forensic investigation into the unauthorized access.
- Stolen documents include technical specifications for iPhone 18 Pro battery parts, camera modules, and circuit board chips.
- World Leaks, a group known for targeting major firms like Dell and Nike, claimed responsibility for the hack on June 12.
- There is currently no evidence that consumer payment information or personal user data was compromised in the attack.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the significant cybersecurity risks inherent in Apple’s complex global supply chain as the company shifts more manufacturing operations to India. The exposure of sensitive vendor intelligence could provide competitors and bad actors with unprecedented insight into Apple’s operational vulnerabilities and manufacturing strategies.
