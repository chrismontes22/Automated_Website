As AI wipes out white-collar jobs, one Alabama high school and Toyota are training students for roles that pay $40 an hour and can’t be automated
============================================================

The Huntsville Center for Technology in Alabama is addressing the national skilled trades shortage by partnering with Toyota to train students for high-paying industrial maintenance and manufacturing careers.

### Key Points
- The $40 million Huntsville Center for Technology facility serves 700 students through industry-standard vocational training programs.
- Toyota Alabama invested $1 million to develop the "Inditech" program, which specifically targets the local need for industrial maintenance workers.
- The National Association of Manufacturers estimates the U.S. will require 1.9 million new manufacturing workers by 2033 to avoid significant economic losses.
- Data from Skillit indicates that construction workers in the booming data center sector can earn average annual salaries of approximately $81,800.
- The Alabama School of Cyber Technology and Engineering utilizes similar partnerships with companies like Raytheon and Airbus to prepare students for defense and aerospace roles.

### Why it Matters
The growing shortage of skilled tradespeople threatens to cost the U.S. economy $1 trillion annually while hindering critical infrastructure and defense production. By shifting focus toward vocational training, these public-private partnerships offer a viable path to financial stability for Gen Z workers while addressing the nation's urgent labor gaps.
