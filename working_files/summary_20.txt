Right now is a terrible time to buy a Microsoft Surface — here's why
============================================================

Microsoft advises consumers to delay purchasing new Surface hardware until the company announces its upcoming spring lineup, which is expected to feature new Qualcomm and Intel-powered PC models.

**Key points**
- Microsoft plans to unveil new Surface PC hardware this spring, potentially featuring Snapdragon X2 and Intel Core Ultra 3 processors.
- Current-generation Surface devices, including the Surface Pro 11 and Surface Laptop 7, have seen recent price discounts expire at major retailers.
- The $599 Apple MacBook Neo has increased market pressure, offering a lower-cost alternative that currently challenges the value proposition of existing Windows laptops.
- Waiting for the spring announcement may lead to deeper price cuts on older Surface models as retailers clear inventory for new releases.

**Why it matters: **
Delaying a purchase allows consumers to access the latest hardware specifications or secure better discounts on outgoing models once the new generation launches. This strategy helps buyers avoid overpaying for aging technology while the market adjusts to new competitive pricing from rivals like Apple.
