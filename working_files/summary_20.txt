Zoom buys Common Room to push past the video call into AI sales
============================================================

Zoom is expanding its enterprise sales capabilities by acquiring Seattle-based startup Common Room, integrating AI-driven customer intelligence tools to help businesses identify and engage potential buyers more effectively.

### Key Points
- Zoom announced the acquisition of Common Room on Thursday to bolster its enterprise sales software offerings.
- Common Room uses AI to aggregate data from CRM records and product usage to provide a unified view of potential customers.
- The startup was founded in 2020 by tech veterans and previously raised $52 million in venture capital funding.
- Common Room’s client list includes major technology companies such as Notion, Okta, Snowflake, and Anthropic.
- The acquisition aims to integrate Common Room’s intelligence platform with Zoom’s existing Revenue Accelerator tool.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition signals Zoom's strategic shift from a video conferencing provider to a comprehensive "system of action" for enterprise workflows. By embedding AI-driven sales intelligence into its platform, Zoom aims to capture more value from the pre-meeting sales process and remain competitive in the evolving enterprise software market.
