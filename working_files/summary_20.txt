ASUS ROG turns 20 at Computex, and I've never seen such a grand reception — Gold-plated peripherals, special-edition devices, and thousands chanting its name
============================================================

ASUS showcased its latest Republic of Gamers hardware and 20th-anniversary peripherals at Computex 2024, drawing massive crowds alongside a high-profile appearance by NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang.

### Key Points
- ASUS debuted the Strix OLED XG259QWPG, the world's first 540Hz esports gaming monitor featuring a 24.5-inch tandem OLED panel.
- New laptop releases include the ROG Strix Scar 18, G18, and G16, with the Scar 18 highlighting a 4K Mini LED display at 240Hz.
- The 20th-anniversary collection features the ROG Azoth Extreme keyboard and Harpe II Extreme mouse, both upgraded with gold accents and premium components.
- The ROG Ally X20 special edition bundle was introduced, pairing a transparent handheld console with ROG XREAL R1 Edition 20 smart glasses.
- All new Edition 20 peripherals, including the limited-edition hand-painted keycap mystery box, are currently available for consumer preorder.

### Why it Matters
These product launches demonstrate ASUS's continued dominance in the high-end gaming hardware market by pushing display refresh rates and premium build materials to new industry standards. The strong reception at Computex highlights the brand's significant influence on global gaming trends and its ability to maintain consumer interest through long-term product evolution.
