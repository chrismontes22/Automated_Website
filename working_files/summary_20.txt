The model wars are over. Now, Google is fighting for something bigger
============================================================

Google Cloud unveiled its Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform at Google Cloud Next 2026, signaling a strategic shift toward controlling the agentic control plane to dominate the enterprise AI market.

### Key Points
- Google introduced the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform to centralize AI-driven agentic workflows within a single, vertically integrated architectural stack.
- The company launched two new custom Tensor Processing Units (TPUs) that deliver 2.7x better training and 5x better inference price-to-performance ratios compared to previous generations.
- Vertical integration allows Google to bypass third-party silicon margins, providing a significant cost advantage over competitors reliant on external hardware providers like Nvidia.
- Industry analysts emphasize that agentic governance and data layer interoperability are the critical, yet underdeveloped, requirements for successful enterprise AI production deployments.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward a unified agentic control plane forces enterprises to weigh the benefits of Google’s cost-efficient, full-stack integration against the risks of platform lock-in. As AI moves into production, the ability to govern autonomous agents and manage data across heterogeneous environments will determine which organizations successfully scale their AI-native applications.
