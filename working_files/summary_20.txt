============================================================

UC Davis researchers have developed a machine learning-powered brain-computer interface that enables a speechless ALS patient to perform full-time work by translating neural activity into text with 92% accuracy.

### Key Points
- The research team at UC Davis utilized advanced machine learning to decode brain signals into coherent sentences.
- The system achieved a 92% accuracy rate, significantly improving communication speed and reliability for the patient.
- This technology allows individuals with severe motor impairments, such as those caused by ALS, to engage in professional employment.
- The breakthrough demonstrates the practical application of brain-computer interfaces in restoring functional independence for patients with speech loss.

### Why it Matters
This development highlights the transformative potential of machine learning in medical technology to bridge the gap between neural intent and digital output. By enabling high-accuracy communication, such tools offer a viable path for individuals with severe disabilities to maintain professional careers and personal autonomy.
