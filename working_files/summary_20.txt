Your travel habits can leak sensitive info about you. Here’s what to do instead
============================================================

Travelers are increasingly vulnerable to identity theft and data breaches due to insecure habits involving boarding passes, public Wi-Fi networks, shared computers, and public USB charging stations.

### Key Points
- Boarding pass barcodes often contain sensitive data like passport numbers, birth dates, and frequent flyer information.
- Frontier Airlines systems have previously allowed unauthorized access to stored credit card numbers and addresses via boarding pass QR codes.
- Public computers and printers can store personal information or contain malware designed to capture passwords and login credentials.
- Public Wi-Fi networks pose risks through potential spying or fake access points, making VPN usage essential for secure browsing.
- "Juice jacking" involves hackers modifying public USB charging ports to install malware or steal data from connected mobile devices.

### Why it Matters
Cybersecurity threats during travel can lead to severe consequences, including identity theft, financial loss, and the compromise of personal accounts. Adopting simple protective measures helps travelers maintain digital privacy and prevents bad actors from exploiting lax security habits while on vacation.
