I Didn’t Realize How Much AI Chatbots Were Stealing My Work—Until Now
============================================================

Journalists and labor advocates are raising concerns as large language models like ChatGPT and Gemini continue to train on human-authored work without providing compensation to the original creators.

### Key Points
- The website "In the Weights" allows users to check if their work was used to train major AI models like OpenAI’s ChatGPT, Anthropic’s Claude, and Google’s Gemini.
- Many journalists and creative professionals report that their years of labor-intensive reporting are being utilized by AI firms without consent or financial remuneration.
- Senator Bernie Sanders has introduced legislation proposing a 50 percent tax on large AI firms to fund a sovereign wealth fund for U.S. citizens.
- Labor organizations, including the AFL-CIO, are calling for policies that ensure workers have a say in the development and deployment of advanced AI technologies.
- Economic data shows a long-term decline in labor's share of GDP, fueling concerns that AI will further concentrate wealth among tech executives and investors.

### Why it Matters
The rapid development of generative AI highlights a growing conflict between the intellectual property rights of human creators and the profit-driven models of Big Tech companies. This debate underscores a broader economic trend where technological gains increasingly benefit capital owners while failing to provide fair compensation to the workers whose labor fuels these systems.
