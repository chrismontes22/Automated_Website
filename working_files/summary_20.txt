The Most Dangerous Surveillance Bill You’ve Probably Never Heard Of
============================================================

Canada’s proposed Bill C-22, the Lawful Access Act, faces intense scrutiny from privacy advocates and major technology companies over concerns regarding expanded government surveillance and mandatory metadata retention requirements.

### Key Points
- Bill C-22 aims to modernize investigative tools for law enforcement to combat crimes involving encrypted communications and cloud services.
- Critics, including the Electronic Frontier Foundation and OpenMedia, warn the bill could force companies to weaken encryption and privacy protections.
- The legislation includes provisions for mandatory metadata retention for up to one year and new powers to compel data preservation before warrants are issued.
- Major technology firms, such as Apple, Google, Meta, and Signal, have publicly expressed opposition to the proposed surveillance measures.
- Opponents have criticized the government for limiting parliamentary debate on the bill's most controversial provisions.

### Why it Matters
This legislation represents a significant test case for how democratic nations balance modern law enforcement requirements with individual digital privacy rights. The outcome in Canada may set a global precedent for how governments regulate encryption and access to personal data in the digital age.
