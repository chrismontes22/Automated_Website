Gravity, Attention & A Cinema Bounceback: Antenna Group CEO Outlines The Trends Setting The Path For European Entertainment
============================================================

Antenna Group CEO Henning Tewes argues that European media companies must achieve greater scale and create immersive brand experiences to compete effectively against dominant American tech and entertainment giants.

### Key Points
- Henning Tewes emphasized that European firms must consolidate to counter the market dominance of U.S. entities like Netflix, Disney, Amazon, and YouTube.
- The strategy focuses on building "gravity" by integrating content, live events, and physical experiences to foster deeper emotional attachments with audiences.
- Tewes highlighted the importance of cinema as a strategic asset, noting Antenna Group’s recent acquisition of Options & Town Cinemas in Greece.
- European media companies are encouraged to leverage their local cultural roots and news brands to build trust and reach that global tech platforms cannot replicate.
- The executive advocated for a "new logic" combining regional scale, free-to-air reach, paid streaming depth, and real-life entertainment experiences.

### Why it Matters
This shift in strategy reflects a broader realization that European media companies can no longer rely on outdated national-level regulations to protect them from global digital competitors. By pivoting toward integrated ecosystems and physical experiences, these firms aim to secure long-term relevance in an increasingly consolidated and tech-driven global market.
