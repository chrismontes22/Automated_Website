The biggest privacy leak in my living room was the TV itself
============================================================

Modern smart TVs from manufacturers like Samsung, LG, and Roku collect extensive user data through Automatic Content Recognition and default tracking settings that are often enabled out of the box.

### Key Points
- Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) technology tracks viewing habits across streaming apps, cable, and gaming consoles to build profiles for advertisers.
- Major manufacturers including Samsung, LG, Sony, Fire TV, and Roku include ACR features that are typically enabled by default.
- Smart TVs frequently request unnecessary access to microphones, cameras, and location services to facilitate targeted advertising and voice assistant functionality.
- Users can mitigate data collection by disabling ACR, resetting advertising IDs, and configuring private DNS providers like Cloudflare or Quad9 in the device settings.
- Physical privacy measures, such as using electrical tape over camera lenses, provide an effective barrier against unauthorized visual monitoring.

### Why it Matters
These default data collection practices turn consumer electronics into sophisticated tracking devices that monetize personal viewing habits and location data. By adjusting privacy settings, users can regain control over their digital footprint and limit the amount of sensitive information shared with third-party advertisers.
