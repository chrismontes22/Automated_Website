The laptop middle class is vanishing
============================================================

The laptop market is increasingly bifurcating into a K-shaped economy, leaving consumers with few mid-range options as prices for reliable, high-performance devices continue to rise significantly above $1,300.

### Key Points
- The laptop market is splitting into budget-constrained models around $700 and premium performance devices starting at $1,300 to $2,000.
- Many entry-level Windows laptops are limited to 8GB of RAM, which experts warn is insufficient for modern software and Copilot+ features.
- Nvidia is entering the high-end laptop space with its new RTX Spark chip, targeting premium users with high memory requirements and advanced AI capabilities.
- Manufacturers like Dell are releasing budget-tier devices using Intel’s "Wildcat Lake" processors, though these often lack the performance of higher-end alternatives.
- Consumers are increasingly looking toward smartphone desktop modes from companies like Google and Samsung as functional, lower-cost alternatives to traditional PCs.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward a K-shaped market makes it difficult for average consumers to find reliable, mid-priced laptops that offer long-term value. As hardware requirements for AI and modern operating systems drive up costs, many users may be forced to choose between underpowered budget machines or expensive premium hardware.
