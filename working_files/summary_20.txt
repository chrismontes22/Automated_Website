Web Review, Week 2026-25
============================================================

The 2026-25 web review highlights critical developments in artificial intelligence ethics, open-source economics, and technical advancements in programming languages like Rust and C++ for software developers.

### Key Points
- The Software Freedom Conservancy (SFC) released new, balanced recommendations regarding the ethical use of Large Language Models (LLMs).
- Analysts warn that current AI search mechanisms are highly susceptible to manipulation, raising concerns about misinformation and automated scams.
- Geopolitical tensions are rising as the U.S. government begins classifying LLMs as high-risk technologies, impacting international cooperation and open-source development.
- Technical discussions emphasize the security benefits of Rust over C/C++ and the importance of refining UI/UX animation transitions for better user experiences.
- Experts advocate for increased adoption of pair programming and suggest that job candidates prioritize evaluating potential employers during the interview process.

### Why it Matters
These insights reflect a growing tension between rapid technological acceleration and the need for sustainable, ethical digital infrastructure. Understanding these shifts is essential for professionals navigating the evolving landscape of AI regulation, software security, and the broader digital economy.
