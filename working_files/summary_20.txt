Apple Stock Hits Record Territory as Traders Sour on AI Spending
============================================================

Apple shares have surged 15 percent since late June, adding $600 billion in market value as investors favor the company's cautious approach to artificial intelligence infrastructure spending.

### Key Points
- Apple stock reached $315.32 on Friday, marking a 16 percent gain for 2026 and outperforming all other "Magnificent Seven" tech companies.
- The company is integrating Google’s Gemini models into Siri and Apple Intelligence rather than investing heavily in proprietary data centers.
- Apple raised prices on Macs, iPads, and Home devices on June 25 to offset rising memory chip costs, triggering a temporary stock decline.
- Suppliers are preparing for a production increase of the upcoming foldable iPhone, with output targets raised to approximately 10 million units.

### Why it Matters
Apple’s strategy of avoiding the expensive AI data-center arms race is increasingly viewed by traders as a prudent financial advantage. This shift in sentiment suggests that investors are prioritizing capital efficiency and software integration over the massive infrastructure spending currently defining the broader tech sector.
