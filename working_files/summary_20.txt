How AI stacks are rewriting the rules of business
============================================================

The integration of artificial intelligence into enterprise software stacks is shifting business models from human-coordinated silos to automated, platform-like operations that prioritize shared truth and repeatable outcomes.

### Key Points
- Enterprises are moving beyond simple AI copilots to build a "System of Intelligence" that harmonizes data, business logic, and rules across fragmented departments.
- The new AI stack requires a "deterministic digital twin" to provide a foundation of truth, allowing probabilistic AI agents to act with confidence and governance.
- Companies like Palantir Technologies and Celonis are emerging as key players in building the necessary middle layer to integrate siloed enterprise applications.
- Adopting this model allows firms to scale revenue without proportional labor growth by automating the "human glue" of meetings, spreadsheets, and manual reconciliations.
- Dell Technologies is utilizing these principles to compress cross-functional decision-making cycles from weeks into minutes by leveraging a unified data mesh.

### Why it Matters
This transition represents a fundamental shift in how companies operate, moving away from labor-intensive coordination toward software-like marginal economics. By treating intelligence as a core production input, businesses can achieve greater agility and compounding competitive advantages that are difficult for rivals to replicate.
