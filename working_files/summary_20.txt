Canaries in the coal mine? How AI could reshape work in Ireland
============================================================

Rising artificial intelligence adoption is reshaping Ireland’s labor market, causing significant uncertainty for graduates and entry-level workers as companies shift hiring strategies and automate traditional professional roles.

### Key Points
- IrishJobs reports that nearly 50% of Irish employers have reduced entry and graduate-level roles this year.
- Ireland has lost approximately 20,000 ICT jobs in the past 12 months, with companies citing both post-pandemic corrections and AI integration.
- The Department of Finance and the IMF identify Ireland as one of the economies most exposed to AI-driven labor disruption.
- Recruitment firm Morgan McKinley notes the emergence of over 80 new AI-specific job titles, such as AI Ethics and Data Curation, to offset losses.
- Ibec is calling for the utilization of the €2 billion National Training Fund surplus to support urgent worker reskilling and education initiatives.

### Why it Matters
The rapid integration of AI into the workforce creates a skills gap that threatens to leave new graduates and entry-level employees without viable career pathways. While some experts argue that new roles will emerge to replace those lost, the speed of this transition requires immediate government and educational intervention to ensure the labor market remains competitive and resilient.
