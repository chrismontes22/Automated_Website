Bitcoin’s June Bloodbath Explained: Causes, Market Impact, And Outlook
============================================================

Bitcoin experienced a sharp decline in early June, triggering over $1.28 billion in long liquidations as investors reacted to strong US labor data and shifting Federal Reserve interest rate expectations.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin prices faced significant downward pressure, testing the critical $60,000 support level following a period of heavy selling.
- Stronger-than-expected US labor data showing 172,000 new jobs in May increased market expectations for restrictive Federal Reserve monetary policy.
- A broad market sell-off on June 5 erased approximately $2.5 trillion in value across the S&P 500, Nasdaq, gold, silver, and Bitcoin.
- US Bitcoin spot ETFs recorded $1.40 billion in weekly net outflows, contributing to the increased selling pressure on the cryptocurrency.
- Elevated funding rates and high open interest indicate that excessive leverage remains a risk factor for further forced liquidations.

### Why it Matters
The recent market volatility highlights how macroeconomic data and interest rate expectations continue to drive significant capital outflows from both traditional and digital assets. Investors are closely monitoring the $60,000 support level, as a breach could trigger further cascading liquidations across the cryptocurrency market.
