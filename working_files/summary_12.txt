Former Apple Employees Stole Trade Secrets for OpenAI, Lawsuit Alleges
============================================================

Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI in the Northern District of California, alleging that former Apple employees stole trade secrets to benefit OpenAI’s emerging hardware division.

### Key Points
- Apple accuses former employees Tang Tan and Chang Liu of misappropriating confidential data regarding unreleased products and proprietary manufacturing techniques.
- The complaint alleges Tan solicited Apple employees for internal project details during job interviews and that Liu downloaded sensitive hardware files after leaving the company.
- Apple claims it contacted OpenAI regarding these concerns in February 2025, but received no response from the company.
- OpenAI, which is currently developing hardware like AI-integrated earbuds and smartphones, denied the allegations and stated it does not seek other companies' trade secrets.
- The lawsuit could impact the existing partnership between the two companies, which currently integrates ChatGPT into Apple’s Siri assistant.

### Why it Matters
This litigation marks a significant escalation in the competitive battle for AI talent and intellectual property between major technology firms. The outcome could disrupt OpenAI’s hardware ambitions and complicate its potential transition into a publicly traded company.
