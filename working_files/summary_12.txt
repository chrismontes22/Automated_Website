5 stress tests built to crash your PC on purpose, and why you want them to
============================================================

Proactive stress testing using specialized software tools allows PC users to identify hardware instabilities and thermal limitations before they cause unexpected system crashes during critical tasks or gaming.

### Key Points
- Prime95 uses Small FFTs and Blend tests to push CPU cores and memory to their thermal and operational limits.
- y-cruncher utilizes AVX and AVX512 instructions to detect complex math errors and memory instabilities that other tests often miss.
- Karhu RAMTest provides a specialized environment to verify memory stability, with a community standard of 200% coverage for reliability.
- FurMark acts as a GPU "power virus," using fur rendering algorithms to test graphics card thermal limits and clock stability.
- AIDA64 Extreme performs simultaneous stress testing on the CPU, FPU, and GPU to simulate total system load and identify power supply or shared thermal bottlenecks.

### Why it Matters
Running these diagnostic tools helps users verify the stability of their hardware components under extreme conditions, preventing data loss or system failure during intensive workloads. By identifying thermal throttling or faulty memory early, users can proactively adjust settings or cooling solutions to ensure long-term system reliability.
