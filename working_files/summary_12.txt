Bitcoin set for 'choppy summer' as capital chases high-flying AI stocks, K33 says
============================================================

K33 Research warns that Bitcoin faces a volatile summer as institutional investors rotate capital away from cryptocurrency and into high-performing artificial intelligence stocks and upcoming technology initial public offerings.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin spot exchange-traded products recorded their second-largest three-week outflow streak, shedding 62,794 BTC.
- K33 Research analyst Vetle Lunde noted that Bitcoin has failed to reclaim its 200-day moving average while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq reach record highs.
- CME Bitcoin futures open interest has dropped to its lowest level since October 2023, signaling reduced institutional market participation.
- Rising funding rates in perpetual futures suggest that leveraged long positions are increasing despite the recent downward price trend.
- Investors are shifting focus toward potential IPOs from major technology firms including SpaceX and Anthropic.

### Why it Matters
This capital rotation highlights a significant shift in investor sentiment as the opportunity cost of holding stagnant digital assets outweighs the potential gains in the booming AI sector. The trend suggests that Bitcoin may face continued downward pressure if institutional demand remains weak and leveraged positions continue to build in a softening market.
