Bavarian Court Tells Gemini It Can’t Be a Real Boy Until It Tells the Truth
============================================================

A Munich court has ruled that Google is legally liable for defamatory content generated by its AI search summaries, marking a significant potential shift in platform accountability standards.

### Key Points
- A Bavarian court determined that Google is responsible for AI-generated hallucinations that slandered two Munich-based companies.
- Google argued it should not be held accountable for AI summaries, comparing them to links to third-party websites.
- The court rejected this defense, ruling that Google acts as the creator of the AI-generated text rather than a mere intermediary.
- Google intends to appeal the decision, which could lead to the removal of AI search summaries within Germany if the ruling is upheld.

### Why it Matters
This ruling challenges the long-standing legal protections that shield search engines from liability regarding the content they display. If upheld, it could force major technology companies to fundamentally alter how they deploy generative AI features to avoid significant legal risks.
