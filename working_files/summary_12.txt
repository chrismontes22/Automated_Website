The Iran war turned Mag 7 stocks into dip-buying bait. But no one is jumping in yet even though Wall Street expects U.S. tech to outperform
============================================================

**Magnificent 7 stocks have entered correction territory as geopolitical instability in Iran and cooling investor enthusiasm for artificial intelligence spending trigger a broad selloff across Big Tech.**

**Key points**
- All seven major tech stocks are down double digits from 52-week highs, with Microsoft leading the decline at 32% below its October peak.
- Combined capital expenditures for Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta are projected to reach $650 billion in 2026, a 60% increase over 2025 levels.
- Rising oil prices following the start of Operation Epic Fury have shifted market expectations from potential interest rate cuts to possible rate hikes.
- Institutional investors are rotating capital out of technology stocks and into energy, industrials, and domestic manufacturing sectors.
- Iran maintains control over the Strait of Hormuz, threatening 20% of global oil transit and creating uncertainty regarding semiconductor supply chains in Taiwan.

**Why it matters: **
The simultaneous decline of these market-leading stocks signals a significant shift in investor sentiment away from the AI-fueled growth that dominated the market for the past two years. This transition highlights how geopolitical conflicts and inflationary pressures can rapidly undermine the valuation models that previously supported high-growth technology investments.
