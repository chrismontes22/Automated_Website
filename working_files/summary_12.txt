You've Probably Watched One of the 300 Titles on Netflix Produced With AI
============================================================

Netflix reported that generative artificial intelligence was utilized in approximately 300 of its programs this year, primarily to assist with production and postproduction tasks for various streaming titles.

### Key Points
- Netflix co-CEO Ted Sarandos confirmed the company used generative AI in roughly 300 programs during the second quarter of 2024.
- The technology is primarily applied to postproduction tasks, including creating complex visual sequences that would otherwise be too costly or time-consuming to produce.
- Featured titles utilizing these tools include the Indian sports thriller *Glory* and the docuseries *The American Experiment*.
- Netflix recently acquired InterPositive, a company founded by Ben Affleck, to further integrate AI tools into its filmmaking production pipeline.
- The company maintains that AI serves as a creative assistant to human artists rather than a replacement for traditional filmmaking roles.

### Why it Matters
The widespread adoption of AI by a major studio like Netflix highlights a significant shift in how streaming platforms manage production costs and technical workflows. This trend continues to spark industry-wide debates regarding labor rights and the role of human creativity in the future of entertainment.
