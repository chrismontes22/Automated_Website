There’s Never Been a Better Time to Study Computer Science
============================================================

Computer science enrollment is declining as AI-driven automation disrupts entry-level programming roles, forcing universities to rethink how they prepare students for a rapidly evolving software industry job market.

### Key Points
- Undergraduate computer science enrollment dropped by over 8 percent last year, while graduate-level participation fell by 14 percent.
- Anthropic co-founder Jack Clark noted that approximately 90 percent of the company's new code is now generated by artificial intelligence.
- Despite higher unemployment rates for recent graduates, computer science majors continue to earn significantly higher wages than peers in most other fields.
- Universities like MIT have introduced dedicated AI majors, which have quickly become the second-most popular program on campus.
- Educators are divided on curriculum, with some emphasizing traditional manual coding fundamentals while others integrate AI tools directly into coursework.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI-generated code is fundamentally altering the career path for entry-level software developers and challenging the traditional value proposition of a computer science degree. As the industry prioritizes senior-level expertise over manual coding, educational institutions must adapt their curricula to ensure graduates remain relevant in an increasingly automated economy.
