Your next iPhone, Pixel, Xiaomi device could wirelessly charge at 50W with the same charger
============================================================

The Wireless Power Consortium is collaborating with major tech companies like Apple, Google, and Xiaomi to develop a universal Qi 50W wireless charging standard for release by 2028.

### Key Points
- The Wireless Power Consortium aims to succeed the current Qi2 25W standard with a new, unified 50W wireless charging specification.
- Major industry players including Apple, Google, Xiaomi, Huawei, Oppo, and Vivo are aligning on technical parameters and prototype testing.
- Xiaomi is contributing its proprietary low-inductance and low-voltage architecture to improve thermal management and charging efficiency.
- The initiative seeks to eliminate brand-specific charging lock-ins, allowing third-party accessories to deliver high-speed power across different smartphone models.
- Hardware design parameters for the new standard are reportedly finalized, with an official public release targeted for 2028.

### Why it Matters
This transition toward a universal 50W standard could significantly reduce electronic waste by allowing consumers to use a single high-speed charger for various smartphone brands. By standardizing high-power wireless charging, the industry is moving away from proprietary ecosystems toward a more interoperable and efficient consumer experience.
