Stack Overflow: When We Stop Asking
============================================================

Stack Overflow has experienced a dramatic decline in user activity, dropping from over 200,000 monthly questions in 2014 to fewer than 3,000 by 2026 due to AI and moderation.

### Key Points
- Stack Overflow question volume peaked in 2014, but has steadily declined due to strict moderation policies and the rise of generative AI tools.
- Research from VeraCode indicates that 45% of AI-generated code contains security flaws, highlighting risks in relying solely on automated outputs.
- A 2024 GitHub survey found that 97% of developers now incorporate AI into their workflows, signaling a major shift in software development practices.
- MIT and Cornell studies suggest that while AI can write simple code, it lacks the human ability to make complex architectural decisions or maintain long-term code quality.
- Experts warn that over-dependence on LLMs may stifle human problem-solving skills and create a feedback loop where future AI models lack fresh, human-verified training data.

### Why it Matters
The decline of community-driven platforms like Stack Overflow reflects a broader shift in how developers acquire knowledge and solve technical problems. As reliance on AI grows, the industry faces a critical challenge in maintaining code security and preserving the human expertise necessary to oversee automated systems.
