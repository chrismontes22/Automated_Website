The Tensor G6 isn't a downgrade at all; it's Google admitting what really matters
============================================================

Leaks regarding the upcoming Google Pixel 11 reveal that the Tensor G6 processor prioritizes sustained efficiency and connectivity over the high-performance benchmark scores favored by competing flagship smartphones.

### Key Points
- The Tensor G6, codenamed Malibu, reportedly features a seven-core CPU design built on TSMC’s 2nm N2 node.
- Google is transitioning from Samsung modems to a MediaTek M90 modem to improve signal reliability and reduce battery drain.
- The chip includes a new TPU codenamed Santafe for on-device AI and a Metis image signal processor for camera tasks.
- Despite efficiency gains, reports suggest the base Pixel 11 model may be limited to 8GB of RAM, potentially hindering advanced AI features.
- The design strategy focuses on thermal management and consistent performance rather than chasing peak clock speeds or gaming-focused GPU metrics.

### Why it Matters
Google’s shift toward efficiency over raw power represents a strategic pivot that could resolve long-standing issues with Pixel battery life and thermal throttling. However, the decision to potentially limit RAM may create a bottleneck for the company's ambitious on-device Gemini AI features.
