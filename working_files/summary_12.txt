OpenAI President Discloses His Stake In the Company Is Worth $30 Billion
============================================================

OpenAI president Greg Brockman testified in Elon Musk’s lawsuit against the company, revealing his stake is worth nearly $30 billion while facing scrutiny over internal communications and donations.

### Key Points
- OpenAI president Greg Brockman disclosed that his equity stake in the company is valued at approximately $30 billion.
- Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers excluded a text message from Elon Musk warning Brockman and Sam Altman they would become the most hated men in America.
- Brockman admitted he failed to fulfill a promised $100,000 donation to an OpenAI charity, citing a lack of instruction from CEO Sam Altman.
- UC Berkeley professor Stuart Russell testified as an expert witness, revealing he was paid $5,000 per hour for his preparatory work on the case.

### Why it Matters
This trial highlights the immense financial stakes and personal tensions underlying the governance dispute between Elon Musk and OpenAI's leadership. The proceedings offer a rare look into the internal dynamics and valuation of one of the world's most influential artificial intelligence companies.
