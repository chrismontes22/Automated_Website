Lexar TouchLock Portable SSD review: One of the simplest and cheapest hardware-encrypted drive for protecting files I've ever tested
============================================================

The Lexar TouchLock Portable SSD offers robust 128-bit AES hardware encryption and NFC-based unlocking, providing a secure, MagSafe-compatible storage solution for mobile devices and computers at an affordable price.

### Key Points
- The drive features 128-bit AES hardware encryption, ensuring data remains inaccessible until unlocked via the Lexar app and an NFC-enabled phone.
- It includes a MagSafe-compatible design for easy attachment to mobile devices and is available in 512GB, 1TB, and 2TB capacities.
- Performance is limited to approximately 400MB/s read speeds, which is slower than many standard portable SSDs on the market.
- The device is built to withstand two-metre drops and includes a USB-C-to-USB-A adapter for broad compatibility with laptops and desktops.
- Pricing starts at $100, positioning it as a budget-friendly option for users prioritizing file security over high-speed data transfers.

### Why it Matters
This drive provides a highly secure, user-friendly alternative to software-based encryption for individuals and small businesses needing to protect sensitive files. While its limited transfer speeds make it unsuitable for high-end professional video production, its hardware-level protection offers a significant security upgrade for everyday data archiving and backups.
