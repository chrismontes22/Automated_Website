The Battery Mistakes Making Your Laptop Slower
============================================================

Degraded battery health, improper power settings, and the use of non-certified chargers are common, overlooked factors that can significantly reduce a laptop's processing speed and overall system performance.

### Key Points
- Adjust Windows Power Mode to "Best Performance" to prevent system delays during resource-intensive tasks.
- Cap the maximum processor state at 99% in Power Options to reduce heat and prevent thermal throttling.
- Increase the minimum processor state to 10% to improve the system's responsiveness when switching from power-saving modes.
- Use only manufacturer-certified chargers to ensure the laptop receives the correct power rating for optimal component operation.
- Run the "powercfg /batteryreport" command in Windows to assess battery health and compare current capacity against original specifications.

### Why it Matters
Understanding these battery-related performance factors allows users to troubleshoot system slowdowns without needing expensive hardware upgrades or professional repairs. Properly managing power settings and hardware health extends the functional lifespan of a laptop while ensuring consistent performance during demanding tasks.
