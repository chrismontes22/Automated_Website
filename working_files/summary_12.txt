Confidential Apple Files Leaked on Dark Web After Supplier Cyberattack
============================================================

Tata Electronics confirmed a cybersecurity breach involving the leak of confidential documents belonging to major clients Apple and Tesla on the dark web following a recent ransomware attack.

### Key Points
- Tata Electronics identified a cybersecurity incident involving unauthorized access to internal systems several weeks ago.
- The ransomware group World Leaks reportedly published over 200,000 files, including proprietary Apple component designs and specifications.
- Leaked data allegedly contains sensitive employee information, including passport copies, emails, and multi-year event logs.
- Tata Electronics stated that the breach has not disrupted its ongoing business operations or manufacturing output.
- Apple is currently conducting a formal investigation into the leaked documents and the scope of the security compromise.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the growing cybersecurity risks facing global supply chains as companies like Apple increasingly rely on manufacturing partners in India. The exposure of proprietary design data could have long-term implications for intellectual property security and the operational stability of major technology firms.
