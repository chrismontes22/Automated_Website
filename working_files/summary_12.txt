Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft in Pivotal Case
============================================================

Apple Inc. has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI in the Northern District of California, alleging the startup engaged in a coordinated campaign to steal confidential hardware trade secrets.

### Key Points
- Apple accuses OpenAI of poaching over 400 former employees to gain access to proprietary information regarding unreleased products.
- The lawsuit names Tang Tan, OpenAI’s current hardware chief and former Apple vice president, for allegedly encouraging staff to share confidential data.
- Former Apple engineer Chang Liu is accused of downloading dozens of sensitive files, including technical specifications and engineering presentations, before joining OpenAI.
- Apple claims OpenAI coached departing employees on how to bypass security protocols to retain access to internal systems during their final weeks.
- OpenAI denies the allegations, stating it has no interest in other companies' trade secrets and remains focused on developing its own innovative technology.

### Why it Matters
This litigation marks a significant breakdown in the relationship between two major tech entities that previously collaborated on integrating ChatGPT into Apple’s Siri and software platforms. The dispute underscores the intense competition to dominate the next generation of AI-integrated hardware as companies race to define the post-smartphone era.
