Cybersecurity in space: Protecting the next frontier of critical infrastructure
============================================================

Cybersecurity experts are warning that the rapid expansion of space-based infrastructure requires new autonomous defense strategies, as satellites and orbital systems face unique, unpatchable vulnerabilities to cyberattacks.

### Key Points
- Cybersecurity firm OPSWAT successfully tested its MetaDefender Kiosk in near-space conditions, demonstrating the potential for autonomous, offline file sanitization in extreme environments.
- Space-based assets, including GPS, telecommunications, and financial networks, are increasingly classified as critical infrastructure vulnerable to jamming, signal spoofing, and ransomware.
- The World Economic Forum’s Global Cybersecurity Outlook 2026 highlights that organizations are now integrating space-based asset risks into their formal cyber defense planning.
- Future orbital data centers and satellite constellations require zero-trust architectures that prioritize prevention over traditional, human-led patching and monitoring.
- Canada is prioritizing space resilience as companies like Telesat expand low-Earth orbit networks, necessitating advanced security against nation-state and criminal cyber threats.

### Why it Matters
As global reliance on space-based systems grows, the inability to physically repair or manually patch orbital hardware creates a significant security gap. Developing autonomous, prevention-first cybersecurity is essential to protecting the critical infrastructure that supports modern telecommunications, military operations, and global financial stability.
