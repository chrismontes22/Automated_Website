Blogs, traffic, and Google
============================================================

Google’s decade-long shift toward centralized search results and AI-driven summaries has significantly reduced organic traffic for independent blogs, fundamentally altering how creators reach audiences and sustain their digital platforms.

### Key Points
- Google Reader’s 2013 shutdown fractured the RSS ecosystem, pushing users away from chronological feeds toward centralized social media and search platforms.
- Recent "Helpful Content" algorithm updates caused organic search visibility for many niche, independent publishers to drop by 40% to 90%.
- The 2024 rollout of AI Overviews has contributed to a "zero-click" environment where over half of all searches conclude without users visiting external websites.
- Major independent publishers and technology sites have reported traffic losses of up to 58% since early 2024 due to Google’s evolving search architecture.

### Why it Matters
This transition transforms search engines from gateways into destinations, effectively starving independent creators of the traffic and revenue necessary to maintain their operations. As search platforms prioritize internal AI summaries over external links, content creators must shift their strategies toward building direct relationships with audiences to ensure long-term viability.
