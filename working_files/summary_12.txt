Why I'm Forced to Say Farewell: Google Management Has Lost Its Moral Compass
============================================================

**Principal Engineer and former Director of Android Platform Security resigns from Google, citing the company's shift toward military contracts and the abandonment of its previous carbon-neutral goals.**

### Key Points
- The resigning executive, a tenured computer security professor, served as Director of Android Platform Security starting in 2017.
- The resignation is driven by Google’s recent contracts with the U.S. Department of Defense and a perceived departure from the company's historical "Don't Be Evil" ethos.
- The author highlights concerns regarding the potential use of Google’s AI technology for mass surveillance and offensive military operations.
- Google has reportedly abandoned its carbon-neutrality targets due to the high energy demands of its current AI model development.
- The engineer will remain with the company through August 31, 2026, to transition projects but will refuse any work related to AI systems linked to military contracts.

### Why it Matters
This high-profile departure highlights growing internal friction at major tech firms regarding the ethical implications of integrating AI with military and defense-related applications. It underscores a broader industry challenge where companies must balance lucrative government contracts against the values and moral concerns of their specialized technical workforce.
