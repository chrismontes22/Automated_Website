Sweden set to ban mobile phones in schools, joining trend of shelving screens for students
============================================================

Sweden will implement a nationwide ban on mobile phones in schools starting this fall to combat declining literacy rates and reduce classroom distractions caused by digital devices.

### Key Points
- The Swedish government is shifting toward traditional learning tools, allocating 555 million Swedish krona ($59 million) for new textbooks and teacher guides.
- Data from the 2022 Program for International Student Assessment showed 24.3% of Swedish ninth graders failed to reach basic reading comprehension levels.
- The policy follows similar international restrictions in countries including Finland, Denmark, and South Korea, as well as the Los Angeles Unified School District.
- New curriculum standards prioritizing book-based learning are expected to be fully implemented by 2028.
- Critics, including the Swedish Edtech Industry, warn that reducing digital exposure could hinder future job readiness and innovation.

### Why it Matters
This policy shift represents a significant reversal for one of the world's most digitally advanced education systems, signaling a growing global consensus that excessive screen time may impede cognitive development. The move forces a broader debate on whether traditional physical learning materials are more effective than digital tools for improving student academic performance.
