South Korea's Kospi drops 4.5% as some AI stocks swoon, while oil keeps climbing
============================================================

Wall Street indices finished lower on Monday as investors reassessed the recent artificial intelligence boom while awaiting upcoming quarterly earnings reports from major technology companies like Alphabet.

### Key Points
- The S&P 500 fell 0.2%, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 307 points amid ongoing volatility in the technology sector.
- Advanced Micro Devices rose 1.6% following the announcement of an expanded partnership with Microsoft to utilize its new Helios AI product.
- AMC Entertainment shares surged 26.8% after the theater operator reported quarterly revenue that exceeded analyst expectations.
- Domino's Pizza climbed 2.1% as the company reported growth in both carryout and delivery orders despite broader consumer spending pressures.
- Jersey Mike's initiated its roadshow for an upcoming initial public offering on the New York Stock Exchange with a target price range of $21 to $25.
- U.S. gasoline prices have climbed back above $4 per gallon, contributing to ongoing inflationary concerns for American households.

### Why it Matters
The market is currently balancing high valuations against the necessity for companies to prove that massive capital investments in artificial intelligence will yield tangible long-term profits. Upcoming earnings reports will be critical in determining whether current stock prices are justified or if the recent rally in tech-heavy indices is unsustainable.
