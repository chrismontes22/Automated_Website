Xbox Is ‘Not Healthy’, But Losing All of This Talent Isn’t the Cure
============================================================

**Microsoft’s gaming division is undergoing a major corporate restructuring under new CEO Asha Sharma, involving 3,200 job cuts and the potential divestment of several key development studios.**

### Key Points
- New Xbox CEO Asha Sharma has initiated a "reset" of the gaming division, citing the current business model as unhealthy.
- The restructuring includes the elimination of 3,200 positions across the company's various gaming studios.
- Microsoft is reportedly considering divesting from several acquired studios, potentially impacting projects like *Marvel’s Blade*, *State of Decay 3*, and *South of Midnight*.
- The company faces criticism for potentially abandoning diverse creative projects from studios like Arkane, Ninja Theory, and Double Fine.
- Microsoft’s broader hardware strategy, including the upcoming "Project Helix" console, faces challenges from rising component costs linked to the company's heavy investment in AI.

### Why it Matters
This strategic pivot signals a significant contraction for Xbox, moving away from a broad portfolio of diverse studios toward a focus on a few established "mega franchises." The shift risks alienating a loyal player base and undermines the long-term value proposition of the Game Pass ecosystem by reducing the variety of unique, award-winning content.
