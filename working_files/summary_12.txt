Data centers start to drive residential electricity bills down
============================================================

Major electric utilities in states including Georgia, Indiana, and Arkansas are lowering residential electricity rates as investments from data centers help fund new power plants and grid infrastructure.

### Key Points
- The Georgia Public Service Commission approved a plan to save typical residential customers $50 annually, totaling $285 million in savings.
- Entergy announced $5 billion in savings for 2.3 million customers across Arkansas, Louisiana, and Mississippi over the next 20 years.
- Indiana Michigan Power plans to file for a base rate reduction this summer, citing revenue growth from large data center customers.
- A May 2026 study by Energy + Environmental Economics concluded that data centers are not responsible for rising electricity costs.
- Data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration shows that Virginia and Texas, which host 25% of U.S. data centers, maintain residential rates below the national average.

### Why it Matters
These utility agreements demonstrate that large-scale industrial energy demand can subsidize infrastructure upgrades that ultimately lower costs for residential ratepayers. By leveraging data center investments to expand capacity, regulators are effectively mitigating the impact of rising electricity prices on household budgets.
