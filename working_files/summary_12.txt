Your Smart Home Is Raising Your Electric Bill. Here’s How to Stop It
============================================================

Always-on smart home devices, including speakers, consoles, and security cameras, contribute to significant "phantom load" energy consumption that can quietly increase monthly utility bills for many modern households.

### Key Points
- Smart speakers like Google Nest and Amazon Echo draw at least 1.7 watts to maintain constant connectivity and voice command readiness.
- Gaming consoles and streaming devices consume power in standby mode for background updates, which can be mitigated by enabling energy-saving settings.
- Smart TVs and security cameras often remain partially active to monitor for activity or maintain network connections, leading to continuous electricity usage.
- Routers and mesh systems are the highest consumers, often drawing over 4.3 watts, though they are essential for maintaining home network functionality.
- Smart power strips can automatically cut electricity to peripheral devices when a primary unit, such as a television, is powered down.

### Why it Matters
Reducing phantom load helps homeowners lower their monthly utility costs by eliminating unnecessary power consumption from idle electronics. Implementing these small adjustments allows users to maintain the convenience of a smart home while improving overall energy efficiency.
