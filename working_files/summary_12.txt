Ultrahuman says recent security breach didn’t affect passwords or credit cards
============================================================

Smart ring manufacturer Ultrahuman confirmed a security breach occurred on March 27, resulting in unauthorized access to internal databases containing user contact information and transaction history records.

### Key Points
- Ultrahuman reported that the breach affected an internal analytics system rather than core user accounts.
- Compromised data includes account details, contact information, and transaction history, but excludes passwords and payment data.
- The company stated there is currently no evidence that the stolen information has been misused.
- Security teams revoked unauthorized access, took the affected system offline, and implemented stricter endpoint security policies.
- Users are advised to remain vigilant against potential phishing attempts following the exposure of their contact details.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the growing cybersecurity risks facing wearable technology companies as they collect increasing amounts of sensitive personal data. Maintaining consumer trust is critical for Ultrahuman as it competes in a crowded market against established rivals like Oura and Samsung.
