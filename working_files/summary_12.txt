New York state lawmakers have passed legislation that could impose a one-year moratorium on new data center construction, pending a final signature from Governor Kathy Hochul.

### Key Points
- The proposed bill aims to pause the development of new data centers across New York state for one year.
- The legislation has successfully passed through both chambers of the state legislature.
- Governor Kathy Hochul must now sign the bill for it to officially become law.
- The move reflects growing legislative scrutiny regarding the environmental and energy demands of large-scale computing facilities.

### Why it Matters
This potential moratorium highlights the increasing tension between the rapid expansion of digital infrastructure and state-level energy sustainability goals. If enacted, the policy could significantly disrupt regional development plans for tech companies and cloud service providers relying on New York's power grid.
