MAGA’s civil war over immigration is over. Silicon Valley lost.
============================================================

The Trump administration has mandated that most visa holders must return to their home countries to apply for green cards, significantly altering long-standing U.S. immigration adjustment procedures.

### Key Points
- USCIS issued a memo requiring applicants to apply from abroad, except in cases deemed to involve "extraordinary circumstances."
- The policy threatens the residency status of hundreds of thousands of skilled workers, including physicians and Silicon Valley technology professionals.
- Current immigration law often forces applicants from countries like India and China to wait over a decade for green card approval.
- The directive marks a victory for hardline nativist factions within the MAGA movement over tech-industry leaders who advocated for merit-based immigration.
- Implementation remains ambiguous, as the memo grants USCIS officers significant discretion in determining which applicants qualify for exceptions.

### Why it Matters
This policy shift creates substantial uncertainty for the U.S. technology sector, which relies heavily on foreign-born STEM talent to maintain global competitiveness. By deterring highly skilled workers from seeking permanent residency, the administration risks a "brain drain" that could drive international talent toward other nations and disrupt domestic labor markets.
