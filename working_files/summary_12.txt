Probe into claims Southport victims' NHS records accessed
============================================================

The North West Ambulance Service is investigating claims that staff inappropriately accessed the medical records of victims injured during the July 2024 Southport stabbing attack.

### Key Points
- The North West Ambulance Service (NWAS) identified concerns that under 10 staff members may have accessed sensitive patient records without authorization.
- This follows a previous incident where nearly 50 staff at Aintree Hospital were found to have inappropriately viewed the medical records of the same victims.
- Fletchers Solicitors, representing 22 survivors, is calling for a full-scale NHS England review into disciplinary procedures regarding patient data privacy.
- The Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) has been notified and is assessing whether criminal investigations into data protection breaches are necessary.
- Affected families have criticized the lack of transparency, noting they discovered the potential breaches while reviewing documents two years after the incident.

### Why it Matters
These recurring data breaches highlight a systemic issue regarding patient privacy and the unauthorized "snooping" of medical records within the National Health Service. The incidents undermine public trust in healthcare providers and raise urgent questions about the adequacy of current data protection enforcement and staff accountability measures.
