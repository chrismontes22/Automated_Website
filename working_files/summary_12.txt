Acer Swift 16 AI (2026) Review: I Love the Giant OLED Display, but Am Less Enamored With the Oversize Touchpad
============================================================

The Acer Swift 16 AI features a vibrant 16-inch OLED display and powerful Intel Panther Lake performance, though its high price point faces stiff competition from dedicated graphics-equipped laptops.

### Key Points
- The laptop is powered by an Intel Core Ultra X7 358H processor with 32GB of RAM and integrated Intel Arc B390 graphics.
- It features a 16-inch 2880x1800 120Hz OLED touchscreen and weighs 3.3 pounds, making it exceptionally portable for its size.
- A massive 6.9-inch haptic touchpad includes stylus support, though its borderless design can lead to accidental inputs during use.
- The device lacks user-expandable memory or storage, and the webcam has been downgraded to 1080p compared to the previous 1440p model.
- Pricing starts at $1,600 for the 16GB model and reaches $1,800 for the 32GB configuration reviewed.

### Why it Matters
The Swift 16 AI serves as a capable productivity machine for creators, but its premium pricing puts it in direct competition with laptops offering superior dedicated Nvidia RTX graphics. Consumers must weigh the benefits of its thin, lightweight design against the lack of hardware expandability and underwhelming audio performance.
