Google Cloud Pushes Hard on AI Agents and Hardcore Computing
============================================================

Google Cloud announced a major shift toward agentic AI at its Cloud Next conference, introducing new enterprise platforms and advanced hardware to automate complex business workflows autonomously.

### Key Points
- Google launched the Gemini enterprise agent platform, allowing businesses to design, schedule, and manage autonomous AI bots across various applications.
- CEO Thomas Kurian emphasized that the company is prioritizing security and system integration to help enterprises scale AI-driven business processes.
- The company unveiled two eighth-generation TPUs, the 8T and 8I, specifically engineered to accelerate AI training and inference workloads.
- The 8T chip offers three times the processing power of the previous seventh-generation Ironwood hardware, while the 8I features significant memory improvements.

### Why it Matters
This strategic pivot toward agentic AI signals a transition from simple generative tools to autonomous systems capable of executing complex, multi-step business tasks with minimal human oversight. By providing both the software infrastructure and specialized hardware, Google aims to capture a larger share of the enterprise market as companies seek to improve operational efficiency and reduce costs.
