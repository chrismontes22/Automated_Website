**Music manager and venture capitalist Guy Oseary reflects on his career journey, from his early days in the music industry to his success as a prominent tech investor.**

### Key Points
- Guy Oseary managed iconic artists including Madonna, U2, and the Red Hot Chili Peppers, earning Variety’s Music Mogul of the Year award in 2022.
- His early career was defined by high-stakes meetings with industry legends like Bernie Brillstein and Freddy DeMann, which helped him secure his first record deals.
- Oseary co-founded Sound Ventures, which has achieved significant success with 86 exits and 26 IPOs, including major investments in Airbnb, Uber, and Spotify.
- He played a pivotal role in the early-stage funding of foundational AI companies, including OpenAI and Anthropic, through a targeted three-month investment window.
- Oseary emphasizes the importance of fair compensation for artists in the age of AI, advocating for systems that allow creators to opt in or out of training models.

### Why it Matters
- Oseary’s transition from music management to venture capital highlights the power of pattern recognition and creative intuition in identifying high-growth opportunities. His career demonstrates how cross-industry experience can provide a unique competitive advantage in the rapidly evolving technology and media landscape.
