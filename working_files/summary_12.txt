Liftoff CEO on the IPO rebound, AppLovin comparisons, and why mobile apps remain an AI growth story
============================================================

Liftoff Mobile has successfully entered the public markets, raising $437 million on the Nasdaq after previously delaying its initial public offering due to volatile market conditions earlier this year.

### Key Points
- Liftoff Mobile raised $437 million in its Nasdaq IPO following a successful return to the public markets.
- The company reported its tenth consecutive quarter of growth, driven by its neural network-based recommendation engine, Cortex.
- CEO Jeremy Bondy positions the firm as a specialized performance engine for the app economy, distinct from traditional ad-tech incumbents.
- The platform currently reaches approximately 1.4 billion daily active users across more than 160,000 integrated mobile applications.
- Liftoff leverages $160 billion in annual consumer purchase data to optimize advertising campaigns across sectors like travel, finance, and e-commerce.

### Why it Matters
The successful listing signals a potential thaw in the IPO market for ad-tech firms following the industry-wide volatility experienced earlier in 2026. By focusing on the fragmented app economy rather than traditional display advertising, Liftoff aims to prove that AI-driven performance marketing remains a high-growth sector for public investors.
