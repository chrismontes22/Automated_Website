Bitcoin under $64,000 after new U.S. strike on Iran. Trump's China comment adds to uncertainty
============================================================

Bitcoin prices dropped below $63,000 as geopolitical instability from U.S. airstrikes in Iran and rising diplomatic tensions between the United States and China rattled global financial markets.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell below the $63,000 threshold, dropping below its 50-day simple moving average following a 1.4% decline.
- U.S. airstrikes targeted five bridges and a maritime control tower in Iran’s Hormozgan province.
- Japan’s Nikkei index dropped nearly 3%, while Nasdaq futures declined by 0.8% amid a broader sell-off in risk assets.
- Former President Donald Trump alleged that China obtained 220 million U.S. voter records, an accusation the Chinese embassy has denied.
- The Australian dollar weakened as investors reacted to potential diplomatic friction ahead of a scheduled September meeting between U.S. and Chinese leadership.

### Why it Matters
Heightened geopolitical uncertainty often triggers a flight from risk-sensitive assets like cryptocurrencies and regional equities toward safer holdings. These developments threaten to disrupt diplomatic stability and market confidence, potentially creating sustained volatility across global financial sectors.
