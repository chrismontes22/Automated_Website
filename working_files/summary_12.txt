SpaceX and the rest: here’s why giga-scale IPOs could be a sign of a market bust to follow
============================================================

Historical data suggests that upcoming mega-IPOs from companies like SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic may signal market instability, as massive equity offerings often precede significant declines in global stock indices.

### Key Points
- Historical analysis shows that major IPOs, such as Reliance Power, Visa, and Agricultural Bank of China, frequently preceded significant market corrections.
- SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic are expected to command a collective valuation of nearly $4 trillion, potentially raising up to $200 billion in primary capital.
- Passive investment funds, which manage over $12 trillion, are required to purchase new index-listed stocks, potentially draining liquidity from existing market holdings.
- Statistical models indicate that large IPOs, when combined with high aggregate issuance and monetary tightening, predict market peaks with up to 90% accuracy.
- India is also seeing a surge in equity supply, with major unlisted entities like the National Stock Exchange and Jio preparing for potential public offerings.

### Why it Matters
The concentration of mega-IPOs represents a structural liquidity event that risks draining capital from secondary markets and signaling late-cycle investor euphoria. This shift in supply and demand dynamics, exacerbated by mandatory passive fund inflows, could trigger broader market volatility as lock-in periods expire and valuations are tested.
