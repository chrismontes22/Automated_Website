‘Let’s Go Kill the Internet’
============================================================

Doublespeed, a venture-backed startup founded by Zuhair Lakhani, utilizes large-scale phone farms and AI agents to manufacture viral social media content and replace human influencers with automated personas.

### Key Points
- Doublespeed received investment from Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) through the firm's Speedrun accelerator program.
- The company operates a fleet of thousands of physical smartphones to bypass platform bot-detection systems and simulate authentic user behavior.
- Clients pay approximately $450 per month to have AI-generated content posted across platforms like TikTok to influence consumer trends.
- The startup aims to automate the entire content creation process, from ideation to publishing, without human intervention.
- Lakhani markets the service using a nihilistic, "dystopian" brand identity, claiming that traditional organic growth is no longer viable for new companies.

### Why it Matters
This business model highlights the accelerating "enshittification" of social media, where synthetic content increasingly crowds out human interaction to manipulate platform algorithms. As companies prioritize automated virality over authentic engagement, the line between genuine consumer trends and manufactured propaganda continues to blur, challenging the future of digital trust.
