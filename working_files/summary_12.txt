Exclusive-Beijing is looking at curbing overseas access to China's top AI models, sources say
============================================================

Chinese authorities are considering new restrictions on overseas access to advanced artificial intelligence models developed by domestic firms like Alibaba, ByteDance, and Z.ai to protect national security.

### Key Points
- Chinese officials held meetings with major tech companies to discuss limiting international access to cutting-edge AI models.
- Potential regulations may classify the theft or unauthorized leakage of proprietary AI technology as a violation of national security laws.
- The government is exploring a tiered regulatory system that could restrict the most sensitive frontier models to domestic use only.
- Companies involved in the discussions include Alibaba, ByteDance, and the startup Z.ai, which produces the high-performing GLM-5.2 model.
- Authorities are also evaluating new measures to restrict foreign investment in domestic AI startups to prevent technology outflows.

### Why it Matters
These potential restrictions signal that China is increasingly treating artificial intelligence as a critical national asset, mirroring similar export control strategies adopted by the United States. If implemented, these policies could disrupt global AI markets and significantly increase operational costs for international businesses that rely on affordable, high-performance Chinese models.
