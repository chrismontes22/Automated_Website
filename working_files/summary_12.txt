ZemiSmart’s $35 Matter Gadgets Turn Old Appliances Into Smart Home Players
============================================================

ZemiSmart has released two new $34.95 infrared control devices that integrate legacy appliances into Matter-compatible smart home ecosystems like Apple Home, Google Home, and Amazon Alexa.

### Key Points
- The ZBCIR01 is a battery-powered Zigbee IR blaster requiring a ZemiSmart hub, while the ZMCIR01 connects directly to Wi-Fi for native Matter support.
- Both devices utilize the Tuya cloud platform for initial setup, firmware updates, and remote access, raising potential data privacy considerations.
- The ZMCIR01 features a unique USB-C cable with a detachable temperature and humidity sensor to ensure accurate readings away from heat-generating AC units.
- Matter’s current device specifications limit the ZBCIR01 to basic on/off controls for most appliances, whereas the ZMCIR01 offers full thermostat functionality.
- The ZBCIR01 is currently available for purchase, while the ZMCIR01 is available for pre-order with no confirmed shipping date.

### Why it Matters
These devices offer an affordable way to modernize older appliances, but they highlight the ongoing trade-offs between hardware convenience and cloud-based security risks. Consumers must weigh the benefits of Matter integration against the legal and privacy implications of relying on infrastructure tied to Chinese-domiciled companies.
