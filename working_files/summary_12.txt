Why your iOS and Android eSIM transfers always failed — and how to do it right every time
============================================================

Apple and Google have introduced a seamless cross-platform eSIM transfer feature, allowing users to move cellular service between iOS and Android devices without contacting their mobile carrier.

### Key Points
- The feature requires devices to be updated to at least iOS 26 and Android 16.
- Both phones must be connected to a stable Wi-Fi network to complete the transfer process.
- Users can initiate transfers via QR codes or manual pairing codes found in device settings.
- The update eliminates the need for physical store visits or manual IMEI verification for SIM migration.
- Peer-to-peer transfers are subject to individual carrier support for the new cross-platform functionality.

### Why it Matters
This development removes a significant technical barrier for consumers who frequently switch between Apple and Google ecosystems. By streamlining the migration process, the companies have reduced the friction associated with eSIM-only hardware, potentially increasing consumer flexibility in the smartphone market.
