Free, no-signup World Cup streams serve scams instead of football
============================================================

Malwarebytes researchers have identified over 40 fraudulent websites masquerading as free FIFA World Cup streaming platforms to trap unsuspecting users in malicious advertising and cryptocurrency scam networks.

### Key Points
- Researchers discovered more than 40 identical websites using World Cup branding to serve malicious ads instead of live match streams.
- The sites utilize scripts to force users through advertising loops, generating revenue for operators while exposing visitors to fake virus warnings and malware.
- Promoted content includes deceptive message notifications and cryptocurrency schemes promising unrealistic financial returns.
- Intel 471 reported that approximately 19,000 domains referencing FIFA have been registered since January 2026, many linked to phishing and fraud.
- Law enforcement agencies, including the FBI, have issued warnings regarding spoofed FIFA sites, fake ticket sales, and fraudulent hospitality packages.

### Why it Matters
These scams highlight the significant cybersecurity risks fans face when seeking unauthorized access to high-profile sporting events. By exploiting the popularity of the World Cup, cybercriminals can effectively distribute malware and financial fraud schemes to a global audience.
