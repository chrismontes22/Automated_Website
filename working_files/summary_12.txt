Phone Cases Are Boring, This One Puts a Living Terrarium Inside
============================================================

Designer Daniel Idle has unveiled the Terrarium Phone Case, a concept for the iPhone 16 Pro Max that features a self-sustaining, living ecosystem sealed within a clear resin shell.

### Key Points
- The case utilizes a closed-loop moisture system where condensation cycles through the resin to keep moss and small plants alive.
- Designer Daniel Idle used 3D modeling and fabrication to create a cavity deep enough to house soil, roots, and plant matter.
- The design maintains full functionality for the iPhone 16 Pro Max, including access to the camera cutout and charging port.
- The project serves as a biophilic design experiment, integrating living biological components directly into the structure of a consumer electronic accessory.

### Why it Matters
This concept challenges traditional accessory design by treating living organisms as structural components rather than mere decorative additions. It highlights a growing interest in biophilic technology, though it raises practical questions regarding the long-term safety of housing moisture-dependent ecosystems against sensitive electronic hardware.
