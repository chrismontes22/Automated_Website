Apple Reportedly Ramps Up Vapor Chamber Orders for Foldable iPhone
============================================================

Apple has reportedly increased orders for vapor chamber cooling components to support the thermal management requirements of its upcoming foldable iPhone and 20th-anniversary smartphone models.

### Key Points
- Apple increased production targets for its book-style foldable iPhone to 10 million units for 2026.
- Vapor chamber technology will manage heat dissipation in the thinner, redesigned internal structures of future devices.
- The 20th-anniversary iPhone, expected in 2027, will likely feature an edge-to-edge display with glass curving around all four sides.
- Apple previously integrated vapor chamber cooling into the iPhone 17 Pro to regulate temperatures for the A19 Pro chip.
- Manufacturing facilities are reportedly undergoing renovations to accommodate the glass-focused design of the upcoming anniversary lineup.

### Why it Matters
Advanced thermal management is critical as Apple transitions to thinner foldable designs and more complex, borderless display enclosures. These hardware changes indicate a shift toward more sophisticated internal engineering to maintain performance levels in increasingly compact smartphone form factors.
