Stock Market Volatility Rocks Bitcoin, Threatening $60K Support
============================================================

The Nasdaq composite dropped 3.2% and Bitcoin fell to $61,200 as investors retreated from risk assets amid disappointing tech earnings, rising oil prices, and ongoing geopolitical instability.

### Key Points
- The Nasdaq composite declined 3.2%, with major technology firms including Apple, Microsoft, and Nvidia experiencing losses between 4% and 6%.
- Bitcoin dropped 4.5% to $61,200, with over $120 million in leveraged long positions liquidated across major exchanges.
- Oil prices rose 3.8% due to supply disruptions in the Middle East, contributing to broader inflationary concerns and market volatility.
- Spot Bitcoin ETF flows turned negative for the first time in two weeks as institutional investors adopted a more cautious stance.
- Analysts identified $60,000 as a critical psychological support level for Bitcoin, warning that a breach could trigger further downward pressure.

### Why it Matters
The simultaneous decline in equities and cryptocurrencies highlights a growing correlation between digital assets and traditional markets during periods of macroeconomic uncertainty. This "risk-off" environment suggests that investors are prioritizing safe-haven assets like gold and U.S. Treasuries as they reassess valuations amid persistent inflation and geopolitical tensions.
