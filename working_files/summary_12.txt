Recycling Laptops and iMacs Makes PC Building Fun and Affordable Again
============================================================

YouTube creator Matt from DIY Perks demonstrates how to build custom, high-performance computers by repurposing salvaged components from discarded laptops and vintage iMacs to reduce costs and electronic waste.

### Key Points
- Matt utilizes recycled laptop motherboards featuring soldered RAM and integrated RTX graphics to build affordable, high-performance PC systems.
- The project involves complex hardware modifications, such as creating custom ribbon cables to bypass original keyboard power buttons.
- DIY Perks successfully repurposed a high-resolution LCD panel from a discarded 10-year-old Intel iMac to serve as a premium display.
- Additional components, including speakers salvaged from an old television, were integrated into a custom-built oak and aluminum chassis.

### Why it Matters
This approach highlights a growing trend of hardware upcycling as a viable alternative to purchasing expensive new components in a high-cost market. By repurposing e-waste, enthusiasts can achieve high-end performance while significantly reducing both personal expenses and environmental impact.
