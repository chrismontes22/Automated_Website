I ran Ethernet throughout my house and realized it's the cheapest upgrade you'll ever make
============================================================

Installing Ethernet cabling throughout a home provides a more stable, reliable, and cost-effective alternative to wireless networks for high-bandwidth devices like computers, game consoles, and streaming hardware.

### Key Points
- Wired Ethernet connections eliminate common wireless issues like signal interference, unpredictable speeds, and frequent network dropouts.
- Cat6 cables, such as those from CableGeeker, offer gigabit speeds and flat designs for easier installation behind baseboards or under carpets.
- Ethernet is significantly more affordable than high-end mesh Wi-Fi systems, with 50-foot cables often costing less than $10.
- Essential tools for home network installation include a standard cordless drill, such as the 2.8-pound RYOBI One 3/8-inch driver.
- Wired setups ensure consistent performance for latency-sensitive activities like Nvidia GeForce Now game streaming and professional remote work.

### Why it Matters
Hardwiring stationary devices reduces congestion on wireless networks, allowing Wi-Fi to perform better for mobile devices like smartphones and tablets. This hybrid approach provides a professional-grade, "set it and forget it" infrastructure that ensures critical home services remain online even when wireless systems fail.
