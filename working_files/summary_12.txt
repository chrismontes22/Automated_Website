Closing the security blind spots that are a prime entry point for attacks
============================================================

A new report from Barracuda highlights that most cybersecurity breaches stem from preventable vulnerabilities like unpatched firewalls, dormant accounts, and misconfigured security tools rather than sophisticated external attacks.

### Key Points
- Attackers frequently exploit "blind spots" such as forgotten third-party vendor accounts and unmanaged devices that bypass corporate security controls.
- Threat actors are increasingly using "living-off-the-land" techniques, leveraging legitimate administrative tools like PowerShell to remain undetected within a network.
- Research indicates that the time between an initial breach and a full-scale ransomware attack can be as short as three hours.
- The adoption of agentic AI by cybercriminals is accelerating the speed at which attackers can scan environments and identify configuration weaknesses.
- Barracuda recommends prioritizing consistent multi-factor authentication, disciplined patch management, and unified security visibility to mitigate these common risks.

### Why it Matters
Organizations often focus on defending against advanced threats while neglecting basic security hygiene, which creates significant operational vulnerabilities. By failing to address these fundamental gaps, businesses remain susceptible to rapid, automated attacks that can cause catastrophic damage.
