Unionized Workers At Xbox Respond To Layoff Reports, Say They ‘Will Not Be Treated As Disposable’
============================================================

Communications Workers of America members representing various Xbox unions held a press conference to protest potential mass layoffs and demand stronger job protections ahead of expected company restructuring.

### Key Points
- CWA District 9 Vice President Frank Arce criticized potential job cuts, citing Microsoft’s recent console price hikes and significant investments in artificial intelligence.
- Union representatives highlighted CEO Satya Nadella’s $96.5 million salary while questioning the company's financial necessity for reducing staff.
- Bargaining teams from ZeniMax and Activision Publishing reported that management has delayed responding to specific contract proposals regarding layoff protections.
- Xbox unions are demanding standardized protections, including advanced layoff notices, two-year recall rights, and internal hiring freezes for all employees.
- Reports suggest significant restructuring is imminent following the end of the fiscal year, coinciding with the resignation of Xbox Game Studios head Craig Duncan.

### Why it Matters
This collective action highlights growing labor tensions within the gaming industry as unions push for standardized job security amid widespread corporate restructuring. The outcome of these negotiations could set a significant precedent for how major technology companies manage workforce stability during periods of financial transition.
