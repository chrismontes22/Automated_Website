You have just 6 days left to use your Kindle app before it stops working for millions of users
============================================================

Amazon will discontinue its legacy Kindle for PC application on June 30, 2026, requiring users to transition to a new, restricted Windows 11-only version available via the Microsoft Store.

### Key Points
- Amazon is retiring the legacy 32-bit Kindle for PC application, which has been in service since 2009.
- The replacement app will be exclusive to Windows 11 and distributed solely through the Microsoft Store.
- Legacy software allowed users to download local files, a feature often used to remove Digital Rights Management (DRM) protections.
- The new application enforces stricter DRM, preventing users from easily accessing or converting their purchased ebook files.
- This shift aligns with Amazon's broader strategy of restricting file transfers and locking digital content into its proprietary ecosystem.

### Why it Matters
This transition highlights the ongoing shift in digital media where consumers purchase access licenses rather than permanent ownership of their content. By tightening DRM, Amazon is effectively limiting user control over their libraries and pushing customers toward more restrictive, platform-dependent reading experiences.
