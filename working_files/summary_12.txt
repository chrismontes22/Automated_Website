I benchmarked Quad9, NextDNS, and Cloudflare, and the winner wasn't the big name
============================================================

A comparative performance analysis of NextDNS, Quad9, and Cloudflare reveals that DNS resolution speeds fluctuate significantly based on local network conditions, ISP peering, and specific geographic routing paths.

### Key Points
- Testing compared DNS resolution speeds for Cloudflare, Quad9, and NextDNS across two distinct network environments using a standardized PowerShell script.
- Cloudflare demonstrated the most consistent performance across both café and home networks, despite not always achieving the fastest individual resolution times.
- Quad9 showed high variability, ranging from the slowest performer on public Wi-Fi to the fastest during home network testing.
- NextDNS experienced significant reliability issues on public Wi-Fi but performed competitively with a 7.5ms average on a stable home connection.
- All three providers utilize anycast routing, which directs traffic to the network-closest server rather than the geographically nearest one, causing inconsistent results.

### Why it Matters
DNS performance is highly dependent on individual network infrastructure and ISP routing, meaning there is no single "fastest" provider for every user. Choosing a DNS service should prioritize reliability, security features like malware blocking, and custom configurability over generalized speed benchmarks.
