Wired for War: Pax Silica is AI slavery disguised as strength
============================================================

The United States has launched the Pax Silica initiative, a 19-nation pact designed to consolidate global AI supply chains and restrict Chinese access to critical semiconductor and mineral resources.

### Key Points
- The Pax Silica coalition includes 19 signatories, such as the European Union, Japan, the United Kingdom, and Australia, aiming to integrate technology stacks and raw material logistics.
- The initiative seeks to reduce dependency on nations deemed to undermine fair competition, specifically targeting China’s influence in the global AI and semiconductor sectors.
- US Under Secretary of State Jacob Helberg, a former adviser to Palantir, is the primary architect of the pact, which explicitly rejects the concept of national digital sovereignty.
- While the US controls 75% of global AI compute power, the treaty only obligates Washington to "endeavor" to provide access to partners rather than guaranteeing it.
- France has notably declined to join the pact, continuing its pursuit of independent digital infrastructure and homegrown alternatives like Mistral AI.

### Why it Matters
Pax Silica represents a significant shift toward a US-led, centralized AI supply chain that prioritizes geopolitical alignment over individual national autonomy. By binding member nations to American computing infrastructure, the pact effectively limits the ability of allies to pursue independent technological strategies while bolstering the influence of US defense-tech firms.
