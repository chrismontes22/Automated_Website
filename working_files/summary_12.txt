America's shutdown of Anthropic's AI models is not a surprise. And India has seen it coming for 30 years
============================================================

The United States government restricted international access to Anthropic’s advanced AI models, Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5, citing national security concerns regarding potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities and misuse.

### Key Points
- US Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick signed an export control order on June 12, 2026, blocking non-American users from accessing the models.
- The action followed reports that researchers discovered methods to bypass safety guardrails on the Mythos 5 model.
- Anthropic complied with the mandate, immediately disabling access for users outside the United States.
- The move signals that the US now classifies advanced artificial intelligence as a strategic national security asset rather than a global commercial product.
- The decision highlights the vulnerability of nations like India that rely heavily on foreign-owned AI infrastructure and developer toolchains.

### Why it Matters
This event demonstrates that global access to transformative technology can be revoked instantly through routine export control laws, ending the era of unrestricted digital globalization. It serves as a critical warning for nations to prioritize indigenous AI development and sovereign infrastructure to avoid total dependence on foreign geopolitical decisions.
