Microsoft cuts AI costs by leaning on its own models instead of OpenAI and Anthropic
============================================================

Microsoft is shifting some Office 365 AI workloads from OpenAI and Anthropic models to its own in-house MAI models to reduce operational costs and gain greater infrastructure control.

### Key Points
- Microsoft is routing Word and Excel user prompts to proprietary MAI models instead of relying exclusively on third-party providers.
- The company recently unveiled seven new MAI models at its annual Build conference, including tools for coding and image generation.
- This transition follows a broader industry trend of companies like Amazon, Uber, and Meta tightening AI spending due to high infrastructure costs.
- Microsoft remains a major investor in OpenAI, but the move signals a strategic effort to improve the economic sustainability of its Copilot services.

### Why it Matters
This shift marks a transition toward a more disciplined phase in the AI industry where companies must prioritize profitability over unchecked infrastructure spending. Microsoft’s ability to maintain service quality while reducing reliance on external vendors will serve as a critical benchmark for other enterprises managing AI costs.
