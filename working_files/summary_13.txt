Can Apple and Google stop children from sharing explicit images?
============================================================

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer has issued a three-month ultimatum for tech companies to implement robust safeguards preventing children from creating or sharing sexually explicit images on devices.

### Key Points
- Prime Minister Keir Starmer warned that the UK government will introduce new legislation if tech firms fail to implement effective child protection measures within three months.
- Apple and Google currently offer on-device machine-learning tools that blur nudity in messages, but these features do not cover third-party apps or images captured by cameras.
- The government aims to extend nudity-detection systems to all smartphone applications and integrate mandatory age-assurance services for users.
- Millions of older devices running outdated operating systems, specifically those below iOS 17 or Android 9, currently lack the hardware or software support for these safety features.
- Experts warn that relying on "technosolutionism" may lead to censorship, privacy concerns, and the unintentional blocking of legitimate educational or medical content for minors.

### Why it Matters
This mandate signals a significant shift toward stricter government oversight of smartphone operating systems and third-party app functionality. If enforced, these requirements could force major tech companies to fundamentally alter their privacy architectures and content moderation standards to comply with UK law.
