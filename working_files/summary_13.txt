Elon Musk becomes the world's first trillionaire with SpaceX's IPO
============================================================

Elon Musk has officially become the world’s first trillionaire after SpaceX launched its initial public offering, with shares closing at $160.95 on their first day of trading.

### Key Points
- SpaceX set its initial share price at $135, pushing Musk’s total net worth to approximately $1.14 trillion by the end of Friday's trading session.
- Musk owns 4.8 billion shares of SpaceX, representing roughly 42% of the company, alongside 350 million exercisable stock options.
- The IPO is expected to create wealth for approximately 4,400 SpaceX employees who hold company stock.
- Musk’s current fortune exceeds the GDP of all but 19 countries worldwide, according to World Bank data.
- Critics, including Oxfam America, have labeled the milestone a new pinnacle of wealth inequality and oligarchy.

### Why it Matters
This historic valuation highlights the unprecedented concentration of wealth in the private space sector and its potential to rival the economic output of entire nations. The milestone sparks significant debate regarding global wealth distribution and the societal implications of individual fortunes surpassing the GDP of most countries.
