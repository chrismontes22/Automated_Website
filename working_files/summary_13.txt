Who Owns Wyze, The Controversial Smart Home Device Manufacturer?
============================================================

Wyze is an independent smart home manufacturer based in Kirkland, Washington, founded by former Amazon employees to provide affordable security cameras and devices to budget-conscious consumers.

### Key Points
- Wyze is an independent company founded by former Amazon employees and headquartered in Kirkland, Washington.
- The company manufactures its hardware in China, utilizing the services of Tianjin Hualai Technology, a partner of Amazon Kinesis.
- A 2024 security incident exposed camera feeds between users, leading to temporary criticism from outlets like The New York Times' Wirecutter.
- Wyze has since implemented enhanced security measures, including regular penetration testing and metadata overhauls, to regain consumer trust.
- The company collects user data for interest-based advertising but maintains that it does not sell personal information directly to third parties.

### Why it Matters
The company’s history highlights the ongoing tension between consumer demand for affordable smart home technology and concerns regarding data privacy and international manufacturing. As the industry faces scrutiny over security standards, Wyze’s efforts to improve its infrastructure reflect a broader market trend toward increased transparency and rigorous cybersecurity protocols.
