These are the 5 popular apps I switched to this year
============================================================

Tech enthusiasts are increasingly migrating from mainstream platforms like Google and Notion to privacy-focused alternatives such as Proton, Brave, Claude, Inoreader, and Obsidian to enhance their digital security.

### Key Points
- Proton Unlimited provides an encrypted suite of services, including Mail, Drive, and Calendar, as a privacy-centric alternative to Google’s ecosystem.
- Brave browser offers a Chromium-based experience that prioritizes user privacy by blocking trackers and ads by default without requiring manual configuration.
- Claude serves as a more private AI chatbot alternative to Google Gemini, featuring specialized tools like Artifacts for content creation.
- Inoreader functions as an RSS aggregator that allows users to curate specific news feeds, replacing the algorithm-heavy experience of Google News.
- Obsidian provides an offline, privacy-first note-taking environment that replaces cloud-based productivity tools like Notion and Evernote.

### Why it Matters
These shifts reflect a growing consumer trend toward prioritizing data sovereignty and privacy over the convenience of integrated big-tech ecosystems. While users may sacrifice some speed or advanced AI features, these alternatives offer greater control over personal information and digital workflows.
