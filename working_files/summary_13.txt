The USB drive in your drawer is slowly losing your data right now
============================================================

USB flash drives are unreliable for long-term data storage because NAND flash memory degrades over time, making them better suited for temporary file transfers rather than permanent archival purposes.

### Key Points
- USB flash drives typically have a lifespan of about 10 years due to electron leakage within their NAND flash memory cells.
- Frequent read and write cycles accelerate data deterioration, increasing the risk of file corruption or permanent loss.
- Environmental factors like extreme temperatures, high humidity, and dust can significantly shorten a drive's functional life.
- Users should prioritize cloud storage or dedicated solid-state hard drives for sensitive, long-term backups of important personal documents and photos.
- Proper maintenance, such as using reputable brands like SanDisk and safely ejecting drives, can help prevent immediate file corruption.

### Why it Matters
Relying on USB drives for long-term archival creates a significant risk of losing irreplaceable personal data like family photos or legal documents. Users should adopt more stable storage solutions to ensure their files remain accessible for decades rather than just a few years.
