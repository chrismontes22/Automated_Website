Bitcoin under pressure as U.S.-Iran escalation lifts oil
============================================================

Bitcoin and major cryptocurrencies declined as renewed aerial strikes between the United States and Iran triggered a surge in oil prices and heightened global economic inflation concerns.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell to $62,657, while Ether, XRP, and Solana recorded losses between 1% and 2.3% during Asian trading hours.
- WTI crude oil futures rose over 2% to $72.27 per barrel following the escalation of military hostilities in the Middle East.
- The U.S. launched strikes against Iran after attacks on tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, prompting retaliatory Iranian strikes on military installations.
- Former President Donald Trump declared the existing ceasefire with Iran effectively over, citing a breakdown in diplomatic relations.
- Rising oil prices have renewed fears of global interest rate hikes, which typically reduce investor appetite for high-risk assets like digital currencies.

### Why it Matters
The escalation of geopolitical conflict threatens to destabilize global markets by reigniting inflation fears and forcing central banks to maintain higher interest rates. This environment discourages investment in volatile assets like cryptocurrencies as traders shift capital toward safer, yield-bearing alternatives.
