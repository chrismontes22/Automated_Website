Italian Prime Minister Confronts Disturbing AI Threat With Self‑Posted Lingerie Image As Warning To Country
============================================================

Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni has publicly condemned the circulation of AI-generated deepfake images of her, warning that such malicious content poses a significant threat to all citizens.

### Key Points
- Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni addressed the viral spread of AI-generated images depicting her in lingerie, labeling the content a malicious political attack.
- Meloni is currently pursuing a €100,000 libel lawsuit against a man accused of creating non-consensual deepfake adult content using her likeness.
- In September 2025, Italy enacted legislation to regulate artificial intelligence, introducing potential prison sentences for individuals who use the technology to cause harm.
- Research indicates that 98% of deepfake videos found online are non-consensual adult content, while 77% of U.S. voters encountered AI-generated political misinformation during the 2024 election cycle.

### Why it Matters
The incident highlights the growing challenge of managing AI-driven misinformation and non-consensual imagery in both political and personal spheres. As deepfake technology becomes more accessible, it creates significant risks for public figures and private citizens alike, necessitating stronger legal frameworks and increased public awareness.
