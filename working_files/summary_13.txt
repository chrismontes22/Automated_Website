How physicists proved that quantum weirdness is a feature, not a bug
============================================================

A. M. Turing Award winners Charles H. Bennett and Gilles Brassard are highlighting the transformative potential of quantum information while warning of its threat to global digital security.

### Key Points
- Charles H. Bennett and Gilles Brassard received the A. M. Turing Award for pioneering work in quantum information theory.
- The duo developed the "BB84" protocol, which uses quantum mechanics to create uncrackable encryption keys.
- Their research demonstrates that quantum phenomena like superposition and entanglement can be used as resources rather than obstacles.
- Quantum computers pose a significant risk to current financial and internet security by potentially breaking existing cryptographic standards.
- Experts recommend adopting post-quantum and quantum cryptography to protect future data from decryption by advanced quantum systems.

### Why it Matters
The work of Bennett and Brassard shifts quantum mechanics from a theoretical nuisance to a foundational element of future computing and secure communication. As quantum technology matures, businesses and financial institutions must prepare for the obsolescence of current encryption methods to prevent widespread data vulnerability.
