The EZVIZ EP4 is a great home security camera for renters — but its facial recognition won't stop it from spamming you with alerts when you come home
============================================================

The EZVIZ EP4 Wire-Free Peephole Door Viewer offers a subscription-free, easy-to-install security solution for renters, though it currently suffers from persistent, unavoidable facial recognition notification issues.

### Key Points
- The device installs in approximately 10 minutes by replacing an existing door peephole without requiring drilling or permanent modifications.
- It features 4K video recording and supports local storage via a microSD card up to 512GB, eliminating the need for mandatory cloud subscriptions.
- The camera includes AI-powered person identification, though users cannot currently disable alerts for recognized faces, including their own.
- The unit is priced at £229.99 in the UK, with battery life lasting up to 120 days depending on usage frequency.
- The field of view is optimized for identifying visitors but does not capture packages left directly on the doorstep.

### Why it Matters
This device provides a practical, non-destructive security alternative for apartment dwellers and renters who are typically restricted from installing traditional wired doorbells. While the hardware design is highly accessible, the current limitations in notification settings may frustrate users seeking a more refined, automated smart home experience.
