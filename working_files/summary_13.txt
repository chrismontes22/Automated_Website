US government wants to have a useful quantum computer by 2028
============================================================

The U.S. Department of Energy has launched the Quantum Genesis initiative, aiming to develop a functional, large-scale quantum computer capable of solving complex scientific problems by the year 2028.

### Key Points
- The Quantum Genesis initiative plans to establish a national quantum supercomputing facility to advance research in chemistry, materials science, and plasma physics.
- Officials cite recent improvements in qubit reliability and error-correction algorithms as evidence that the 2028 goal is technically achievable.
- The project will integrate artificial intelligence to help researchers optimize the control systems required for complex quantum hardware.
- The U.S. government recently supported the sector through two executive orders and a $2 billion investment from the Department of Commerce.
- Experts note that scaling current devices remains a significant challenge due to supply chain fragility and the complexity of building large-scale systems.

### Why it Matters
This initiative represents a strategic push to secure a competitive advantage in a field that could revolutionize pharmaceutical development, manufacturing, and national security encryption. By setting an aggressive 2028 timeline, the U.S. is positioning itself to lead global quantum development against similar national strategies currently being pursued by China and the United Kingdom.
