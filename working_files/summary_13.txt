American Decline, Chinese Exposure | Sinification: June 2026
============================================================

Chinese foreign-policy discourse in June 2026 reflects a growing confidence in relative American decline, while analysts remain focused on managing internal economic imbalances and complex global strategic challenges.

### Key Points
- Analysts are debating the significance of Donald Trump’s recent comments on Taiwan, weighing whether they represent a fundamental policy shift or tactical maneuvering.
- New outbound investment regulations, effective July 1, grant Beijing legal authority to blacklist foreign entities that discriminate against Chinese investors.
- Experts identify "over-competition" as a core pathology driving domestic social issues, including industrial overcapacity and declining marriage rates.
- China’s AI strategy is shifting toward making domestic firms indispensable to global value chains rather than seeking total market dominance.
- Reports highlight critical mineral vulnerabilities, noting that China’s rare-earth dominance is increasingly dependent on supply chains in Southeast Asia and Central Asia.

### Why it Matters
The Chinese establishment is increasingly preoccupied with the second-order effects of its global power, such as exposed firms, resource chokepoints, and a social model struggling with weak domestic demand. These internal and external pressures suggest that while Beijing feels emboldened by perceived American instability, it faces significant structural hurdles in sustaining its long-term economic and geopolitical influence.
