The Sunday Papers
============================================================

This weekly gaming and culture roundup explores the history of Left 4 Dead graffiti, the evolution of AI from Black & White to Google DeepMind, and music industry controversies.

### Key Points
- Writer Jay Pinkerton explains that Left 4 Dead graffiti was crowdsourced by inviting office visitors to contribute authentic, improvised messages.
- The article highlights the single-player extraction shooter genre, specifically praising the game Witchfire for its gothic atmosphere and lack of microtransactions.
- A retrospective on the 2001 game Black & White examines its ambitious AI creature design and the subsequent career of engineer Richard Evans at Google DeepMind.
- Google DeepMind co-founder Demis Hassabis recently won the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for protein structure research.
- The band Sylvan Esso removed their music from Spotify to protest CEO Daniel Ek’s investment in the AI-focused defense company Helsing.

### Why it Matters
These stories illustrate the complex intersection of creative game design and the broader societal implications of artificial intelligence. By tracing the path from early gaming experiments to modern corporate AI, the analysis highlights how technology once confined to virtual worlds now carries significant real-world ethical and political consequences.
