Bitcoin Hits 21-Month Low As Expiring Crypto Bets Threaten More Selling
============================================================

Bitcoin dropped to a 21-month low of $58,131 on Thursday, extending a broader cryptocurrency market selloff as investors prepare for the expiration of $10 billion in options contracts.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell to $58,131, marking its lowest price point since September 2024 and a 54% decline from its October record high.
- Major cryptocurrencies including Ethereum, Solana, and Dogecoin also saw significant losses, ranging between 6% and 12.6% over the past week.
- Deribit, the world’s largest crypto options venue, faces the expiration of $10 billion in contracts on Friday, which analysts expect will increase market volatility.
- The total global cryptocurrency market capitalization has plummeted by $2.2 trillion since October, falling to a current valuation of $2 trillion.
- Industry experts, including BlackRock’s Robert Mitchnick, attribute the capital flight to surging investor interest in artificial intelligence and competing investment opportunities.

### Why it Matters
The decline signals a potential bear market for digital assets as capital shifts toward high-growth sectors like artificial intelligence. This trend highlights a cooling of the pro-crypto sentiment that previously drove record valuations, leaving investors to navigate increased volatility and shifting macroeconomic priorities.
