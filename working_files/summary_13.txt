WSJ: Apple avoided semiconductor tariffs last year thanks to Intel chip deal
============================================================

Apple has agreed to manufacture select Mac and iPhone chips domestically through Intel following negotiations that secured the company a critical exemption from semiconductor import tariffs last year.

### Key Points
- Apple secured a 100% semiconductor tariff exemption after committing to significant new investments within the United States.
- President Trump and Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick facilitated the partnership between Apple and Intel during high-level trade discussions.
- Intel shares reached record highs following the official announcement that the company will produce chips for future Apple hardware.
- The agreement aims to bolster domestic semiconductor manufacturing capabilities as part of a broader strategy to reduce reliance on foreign chip imports.

### Why it Matters
This partnership marks a significant shift in Apple’s supply chain strategy by integrating domestic manufacturing into its high-volume product lines. The deal serves as a major lifeline for Intel while demonstrating how the current administration is leveraging trade policy to force collaboration between American technology giants.
