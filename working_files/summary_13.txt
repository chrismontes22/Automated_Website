Give Smart People the Tools to Do Smart Things
============================================================

Tech industry critics argue that AI companies are prioritizing aggressive marketing and hype over practical utility to secure investor funding before market expectations shift toward long-term profitability.

### Key Points
- AI firms like OpenAI and Anthropic are accused of using "cyberweapon" and "replacement" narratives to drive subscription sales and capital investment.
- Critics argue that AI models produce artifacts rather than understanding, failing to replace the complex decision-making processes of human experts.
- The article highlights that historical tools like compilers and CAD systems empowered professionals rather than rendering them obsolete.
- Anthropic’s marketing of the Mythos model is cited as an example of sensationalism that undermines the work of professional security researchers.
- The author suggests that AI development should focus on augmenting human expertise, similar to how game engines like Unity empowered independent developers.

### Why it Matters
This critique highlights a growing tension between the current AI marketing cycle and the actual operational needs of businesses seeking measurable returns on investment. As investor scrutiny shifts from hype to revenue, companies that prioritize genuine expert-level utility over replacement narratives may be better positioned for long-term sustainability.
