‘Selfish Bravado’ Behind TfL Cyber-Attack, Judge Says as Pair Jailed
============================================================

Two young men linked to the Scattered Spider hacking group have been sentenced to five years and six months in prison for the 2024 cyber-attack on Transport for London.

### Key Points
- Owen Flowers and Thalha Jubair pleaded guilty to unauthorized system access under the UK’s Computer Misuse Act.
- The 2024 attack cost Transport for London approximately £39 million in recovery expenses and lost revenue.
- The breach compromised data for up to 10 million people, forcing the suspension of Oyster photocard and Dial-a-Ride services.
- Perpetrators used social engineering and SIM swapping to bypass two-factor authentication and escalate privileges within internal systems.
- The National Crime Agency identified this as the largest cybercrime prosecution in UK history, significantly disrupting the Scattered Spider collective.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the severe operational and financial risks posed by organized cybercriminal groups targeting critical national infrastructure. It serves as a landmark legal precedent for the UK, demonstrating the government's commitment to prosecuting high-level digital threats despite the perpetrators' youth.
