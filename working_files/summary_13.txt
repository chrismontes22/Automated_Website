DOGE Goes Nuclear: How Trump Invited Silicon Valley Into America’s Nuclear Power Regulator
============================================================

The Trump administration is aggressively restructuring the Nuclear Regulatory Commission to accelerate nuclear energy production, prioritizing rapid deployment for artificial intelligence data centers over traditional safety oversight.

### Key Points
- The Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) has installed political appointees, including Seth Cohen, to overhaul nuclear policy and bypass long-standing safety regulations.
- Over 400 staff members have left the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) since the start of the administration, with hiring for replacements significantly slowed.
- The administration is pushing to relax radiation exposure limits and abandon the "As Low As Reasonably Achievable" (ALARA) safety principle to reduce costs for private reactor companies.
- Silicon Valley-backed firms, such as Valar Atomics and Oklo, have gained unprecedented access to draft executive orders and regulatory discussions.
- The Department of Energy has begun using military assets to transport experimental reactor components, citing national security interests to bypass standard bureaucratic hurdles.

### Why it Matters
This shift represents a fundamental change in the U.S. approach to nuclear safety, moving from a cautious, independent regulatory model to one driven by industry-aligned political objectives. By prioritizing speed for AI-related energy demands, the administration risks undermining public trust and the global safety standards that have historically prevented major nuclear incidents.
