Mosyle launches new service to help parents manage Mac and iPad screen time for K-12 devices at home
============================================================

Mosyle has launched Mosyle@Home, a new management platform that allows parents to control school-issued iPads and Macs during after-school hours through a dedicated, free-to-use parental dashboard.

### Key Points
- Parents can set screen time limits, pause device access, and view detailed app usage reports for school-issued hardware.
- The platform includes web filtering tools and the ability to reset forgotten iPad lock screen passcodes.
- Mosyle@Home replaces the deprecated ScreenGuide service, with a seamless migration process for existing users.
- The service is available at no additional cost to families when enabled by schools using Mosyle OneK12 or other paid plans.
- Parents register devices by scanning a QR code to gain control over app restrictions and internet filtering outside of school hours.

### Why it Matters
This platform bridges the gap between institutional IT security and parental oversight for school-issued technology. By integrating these controls directly into the device management software, schools can provide families with necessary oversight tools without compromising administrative security protocols.
