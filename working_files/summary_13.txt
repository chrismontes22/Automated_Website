Personal Encyclopedias
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
The open-source project whoami.wiki uses MediaWiki and AI agents to transform personal digital archives, photos, and location data into a structured, searchable, and interconnected personal encyclopedia.

**Key points**
* The project utilizes MediaWiki architecture to organize life events, family history, and personal memories into a browsable, Wikipedia-style format.
* Users can integrate diverse data sources, including Google Photos EXIF metadata, bank transactions, Uber trip history, and social media archives.
* AI tools like Claude Code analyze visual content, timestamps, and cross-referenced data to draft detailed narratives and identify people or locations.
* The platform emphasizes data privacy by running locally on the user's machine, ensuring personal information remains under the owner's control.
* The open-source tool is designed to help users preserve oral histories and rediscover forgotten life experiences through automated editorial workflows.

**Why it matters**
This project demonstrates a novel way to reclaim personal data from fragmented digital silos, turning scattered logs into a meaningful, permanent narrative. By leveraging established encyclopedia conventions, it provides a structured framework for long-term memory preservation and deeper reflection on personal relationships.
