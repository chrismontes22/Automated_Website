Crypto steadies as Middle East tensions counter U.S. inflation report boost
============================================================

Bitcoin and ether prices consolidated near multi-week highs as geopolitical tensions between the United States and Iran offset positive market sentiment following a softer-than-expected U.S. inflation report.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin reached a three-week high of $65,200, while ether hit its highest level since June 3 at $1,895.
- Rising U.S.-Iran hostilities in the Strait of Hormuz tempered gains triggered by Tuesday's favorable U.S. inflation data.
- Hyperliquid (HYPE) rose 4% toward a record high, while rival token LIT stalled near its all-time peak of $2.76.
- CoinMarketCap’s Altcoin Season indicator dropped to 46/100 as market strength remained concentrated in major cryptocurrencies.
- Derivatives data shows $357 million in 24-hour liquidations, with Bitcoin and ether accounting for the majority of the total.

### Why it Matters
The current market consolidation reflects a tug-of-war between macroeconomic optimism and geopolitical risk, signaling a cautious environment for digital asset investors. Increased trading volumes on centralized exchanges suggest that despite these tensions, institutional and retail interest in crypto markets remains robust.
