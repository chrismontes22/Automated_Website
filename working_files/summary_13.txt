**Author Sebastian Mallaby explores the rise of DeepMind and the pursuit of artificial general intelligence in his new book, *The Infinity Machine*.**

### Key Points
- Sebastian Mallaby’s *The Infinity Machine* chronicles the career of Demis Hassabis and the development of DeepMind.
- The book details the evolution of AI from early neural network research to modern breakthroughs like AlphaGo and AlphaFold.
- Mallaby emphasizes the importance of "preparing the mind" to navigate the rapid societal and economic shifts driven by AI.
- The narrative highlights the role of venture capital, specifically the early support from investors like Luke Nosek and Founders Fund.
- Mallaby argues that while AI offers potential for superabundance, it also presents significant risks that require international dialogue and safety frameworks.

### Why it Matters
Understanding the history and trajectory of AI is essential for navigating the profound economic and political disruptions that accompany rapid technological advancement. By examining the intersection of high-stakes venture capital and scientific ambition, Mallaby provides a framework for evaluating both the transformative promises and the existential risks of superintelligence.
