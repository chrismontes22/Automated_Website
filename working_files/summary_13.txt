Law proposed to ban AI companies from selling your health data
============================================================

Senator Elizabeth Warren and Representative Mary Gay Scanlon are introducing legislation to ban the sale of sensitive health data collected by artificial intelligence chatbots and third-party data brokers.

### Key Points
- The proposed Health and Location Data Protection Act aims to prevent AI companies from selling user medical information to data brokers.
- Major AI developers, including OpenAI, xAI, and Anthropic, have recently encouraged users to upload medical records and health data into their respective chatbot platforms.
- Current terms of service for many AI tools allow companies to utilize user conversations as training data or share information with third parties.
- The legislation serves as a response to the growing trend of individuals seeking medical advice from generative AI models despite significant privacy and accuracy risks.

### Why it Matters
This legislation highlights the urgent need for federal privacy standards as AI companies increasingly integrate sensitive personal health information into their training models. Establishing these protections could force a shift in how tech firms handle user data, potentially limiting the commercialization of private medical records in the digital age.
