Brandon Sanderson vs. AI Art
============================================================

Fantasy author Brandon Sanderson argues that generative AI misses the fundamental purpose of art, urging society to reclaim its agency by rejecting machine-generated content as a substitute for human creation.

### Key Points
- Brandon Sanderson addressed the ethical and philosophical implications of generative AI during his talk at the Dragonsteel Nexus conference.
- Sanderson dismissed common arguments against AI, such as environmental impact and copyright concerns, as secondary to his core objection.
- The author contends that the true value of art lies in the transformative process of creation rather than the final product.
- Sanderson emphasizes that society retains the power to define art and can choose to reject AI-generated works to preserve human expression.
- The commentary critiques a trend of "nihilistic passivity" regarding AI development, advocating for active human control over technological integration.

### Why it Matters
This perspective challenges the inevitability of AI adoption by reframing art as a deeply human act of communication rather than a mere commodity. It encourages creators and consumers to assert their agency in determining the role of technology in cultural production.
