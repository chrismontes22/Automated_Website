No, everyone is not using AI for everything
============================================================

Recent data indicates that widespread adoption of generative AI is a myth, with usage patterns showing that most Americans use these tools only occasionally or not at all.

### Key Points
- Microsoft telemetry shows only 30% of the U.S. working-age population actively engages with major AI services like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude.
- Gallup polling reveals that 21% of Americans never use AI, while 31% use it only on a monthly or infrequent basis.
- Primary barriers to adoption include concerns over job displacement, data privacy, and the potential for AI to spread misinformation.
- Public sentiment toward AI is increasingly negative, with Gallup reporting a 40% year-over-year increase in anger regarding the technology among Gen Z.
- Studies from Datos and the Searchlight Institute confirm that regular, daily usage of AI tools remains limited to a small minority of users.

### Why it Matters
The disconnect between the media narrative of universal AI adoption and the reality of cautious, limited usage suggests that many consumers do not yet find enough individual value to justify daily integration. Businesses and policymakers must recognize this skepticism to avoid miscalculating market demand and to better address public concerns regarding safety and utility.
