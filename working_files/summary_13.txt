Can Europe Seize the AI Moment?
============================================================

Meta hosted the ‘Build to Lead’ symposium in Brussels, where industry leaders and policymakers discussed how Europe can overcome regulatory barriers to remain competitive in the global AI race.

### Key Points
- Meta hosted the ‘Build to Lead’ symposium on March 24, featuring European Parliament President Roberta Metsola and UK AI Adviser Matt Clifford.
- Markus Reinisch, Meta’s VP of Public Policy for Europe, argued that the global AI market is currently dominated by the United States and China.
- The event highlighted the need for Europe to shift from a "sovereignty" focus to prioritizing innovation, speed, and the removal of regulatory barriers.
- Meta cited its partnership with EssilorLuxottica on AI-integrated wearables as an example of European design and engineering excellence.
- Speakers warned that Europe faces a "relevance crisis" if it continues to apply outdated regulatory frameworks to emerging technologies like AI.

### Why it Matters
The symposium highlights a growing tension between European regulatory frameworks and the need for rapid technological innovation. By advocating for fewer self-imposed barriers, Meta is signaling that the future of the European tech sector depends on its ability to balance democratic values with the agility required to compete globally.
