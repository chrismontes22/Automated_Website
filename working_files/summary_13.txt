First Cases for Apple's Foldable iPhone Surface Online
============================================================

Accessory manufacturer iFunSmart has listed protective cases for Apple’s rumored foldable iPhone, providing new design details ahead of the device's anticipated launch alongside the iPhone 18 series.

### Key Points
- iFunSmart listings reveal a dual-lens camera setup, a slim profile, and a dedicated cutout for a Camera Control button.
- The cases feature integrated N52 magnets for accessory compatibility, though it remains unclear if the phone itself includes native MagSafe support.
- Leaked specifications suggest a 5.5-inch external display, a 7.8-inch inner screen, and an A20 Pro chip with 12GB of RAM.
- The foldable device is expected to debut in fall 2026 with a starting price point of approximately $2,000.
- Design details, including a 9.5mm folded thickness, align with previous dummy unit leaks and CAD-based industry rumors.

### Why it Matters
These early accessory listings provide credible insight into Apple's hardware strategy for its first foldable device, which is expected to compete in the ultra-premium smartphone market. The move suggests that third-party manufacturers are preparing for a 2026 release, signaling that the product's design phase is nearing completion.
