There's one simple way to tell whether bitcoin has really bottomed. Right now, it hasn't.
============================================================

Bitcoin remains in a bearish trend as its weekly relative strength index stays below the critical 41.5 threshold, a key indicator historically used to confirm market cycle bottoms.

### Key Points
- The 14-week relative strength index (RSI) for Bitcoin is currently at 34.00, signaling continued bearish pressure.
- Analysts at Material Indicators identify 41.5 as the definitive level required to confirm a transition from a bear to a bull market.
- Historical data shows the 41.5 RSI level successfully marked bull market regimes during the 2015-2017, 2020-2021, and 2024-2025 cycles.
- A drop below the previous weekly reading of 31.89 would indicate potential for further price declines for the cryptocurrency.
- Bitcoin is currently trading near $63,000, with market participants waiting for technical confirmation before declaring a definitive price bottom.

### Why it Matters
This technical indicator serves as a vital tool for traders to distinguish between temporary relief rallies and the start of a sustained bullish trend. By monitoring the 41.5 RSI level, investors can better manage risk and avoid premature entries during periods of broader market volatility.
