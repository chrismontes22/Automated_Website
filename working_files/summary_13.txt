Today Marks 22 Years Of Phoronix For Linux Hardware Testing & Benchmarking
============================================================

Phoronix celebrates its 22nd anniversary as a leading source for Linux hardware testing and benchmarking, reflecting on two decades of industry evolution and the ongoing challenges of independent publishing.

### Key Points
- Phoronix has maintained a streak of daily original content since September 26, 2012, spanning over 13 years of continuous reporting.
- The platform highlights significant Linux advancements, including Valve’s Steam Deck, the dominance of Linux in AI and HPC, and improved open-source GPU driver support.
- Key industry shifts include the rise of OpenBMC, LVFS/Fwupd for firmware updates, and increased open-source contributions from vendors like AMD and NVIDIA.
- The site faces financial sustainability challenges due to high ad-blocker usage and a low conversion rate of less than 0.5% for Phoronix Premium subscriptions.
- To mark the anniversary, the site is offering a discounted Phoronix Premium subscription rate through June 8 to support ongoing hardware testing and benchmarking operations.

### Why it Matters
The longevity of Phoronix provides a unique historical perspective on the maturation of the Linux ecosystem from a niche hobbyist project to a dominant force in data centers and gaming. Its struggle to remain independent highlights the broader economic difficulties faced by specialized, ad-supported technical journalism in an era of automated content scraping and widespread ad-blocking.
