Trump administration imposes 25% tariffs on advanced semiconductors to boost domestic manufacturing
============================================================

The Trump administration has implemented a 25% tariff on specific advanced semiconductors from Nvidia and AMD to incentivize domestic manufacturing and reduce reliance on foreign technology supply chains.

### Key Points
- The Presidential Proclamation, signed January 14, 2026, imposes a 25% tariff on advanced computing chips including Nvidia’s H200 and AMD’s MI325X processors.
- Tariffs were enacted following a national security investigation launched by the Commerce Department on April 1, 2025, under Section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act.
- A 90-day negotiation window concludes April 14, 2026, with potential for expanded tariffs on additional semiconductor products.
- Exemptions are available for chips used in US data centers, research, startups, consumer goods, and government applications.
- The administration plans to introduce an offset program to subsidize companies that invest directly in domestic semiconductor production facilities.

### Why it Matters
These tariffs represent a shift toward aggressive industrial policy by combining trade barriers with domestic subsidies to force a restructuring of the global semiconductor supply chain. Companies must now navigate complex compliance requirements or face increased costs, which could influence future hardware investment strategies across the technology sector.
