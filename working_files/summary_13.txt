The CMO And CIO Friction Point: Navigating The AI Agent And AEO Ecosystem
============================================================

The rapid surge in AI agent traffic requires CMOs and CIOs to align on website accessibility strategies to ensure brands remain discoverable and competitive in AI-driven search results.

### Key Points
- AI agent activity grew 150% month-over-month between November 2025 and March 2026, with agents now accounting for 15% of total website traffic.
- OpenAI’s GPTBot and OAI-SearchBot dominate the landscape, while crawlers like ClaudeBot and ByteSpider have seen significant usage spikes.
- 81% of organizations incorrectly categorize modern AI agents as legacy bot traffic, often blocking them through outdated firewall or robots.txt configurations.
- Only 21% of brands have established specific strategies for search-side crawlers, and just 38% have policies for user-facing AI browsers.
- Failure to optimize for AI agents risks losing an estimated $40 billion in potential search-driven revenue across the broader economy.

### Why it Matters
Misalignment between marketing and IT departments creates a critical revenue risk where brands are unintentionally hidden from AI-driven discovery and consumer research. By treating external AI agents as security threats rather than potential customers, companies cede their brand narrative and market share to competitors who are properly optimized for machine-readable content.
