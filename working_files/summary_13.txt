What Google’s New AI Guide Actually Debunks. And What It Doesn’t via @sejournal, @slobodanmanic
============================================================

Google’s new optimization guide clarifies that tactics like llms.txt and AI-specific content rewriting do not improve search citations, urging businesses to focus on standard SEO practices instead.

### Key Points
- Google explicitly debunked five optimization tactics for AI Overviews: llms.txt files, content chunking, AI-specific rewriting, inauthentic mentions, and structured-data obsession.
- The guide confirms that optimizing for generative AI search remains fundamentally tied to traditional SEO principles rather than specialized AI-only formatting.
- While these tactics fail to boost search citations, the report notes that machine-readable maps may still hold future utility for autonomous agents performing specific tasks.
- Standard schema.org markup remains valuable for entity recognition and identity infrastructure, even though it does not provide a direct lift in AI citation frequency.
- Google advises that content should be written clearly for all users, as modular, well-structured information benefits both human readers and automated retrieval systems.

### Why it Matters
This guidance forces a shift in digital strategy by invalidating many expensive, niche services marketed as "AI optimization" tools. Businesses should prioritize high-quality, accessible content and foundational technical SEO rather than chasing unproven hacks that offer no measurable impact on search visibility.
