The FBI says it’s already seized 13 drones in Atlanta near World Cup stadiums — and pilots could face fines up to $100,000 if they don’t follow these new rules
============================================================

The FBI is actively seizing drones and issuing fines to pilots who violate strict temporary flight restrictions near stadiums hosting FIFA World Cup 2026 matches across the United States.

### Key Points
- The FBI has confiscated dozens of drones near venues including SoFi Stadium, the L.A. Memorial Coliseum, and Hard Rock Stadium.
- FAA regulations prohibit drone flights within a 3-nautical-mile radius and 3,000 feet of altitude around active World Cup match stadiums.
- Law enforcement maintains a zero-tolerance policy, with violators facing potential federal criminal charges and fines reaching up to $100,000.
- The FBI utilizes a dedicated Counter Drone Enforcement Team to monitor airspace and intercept unauthorized aircraft during tournament events.
- Pilots are encouraged to use the FAA website or the B4UFLY app to verify restricted zones before operating drones near host cities.

### Why it Matters
These enforcement actions highlight the severe legal and financial risks for drone operators who ignore federal airspace restrictions during high-profile international events. Maintaining these no-fly zones is critical for public safety and security, as authorities prioritize preventing potential threats to players and spectators.
