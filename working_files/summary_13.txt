Before Mass Layoffs, Meta Reassigns 7,000 Workers To Focus On AI
============================================================

Meta is restructuring its workforce by reassigning 7,000 employees to new artificial intelligence initiatives while simultaneously preparing for a separate round of layoffs affecting 8,000 staff members.

### Key Points
- Meta is moving 7,000 employees into four new organizations specifically focused on developing artificial intelligence tools and applications.
- The new AI-focused units will utilize "A.I. native design structures" and feature a flatter management hierarchy to increase overall productivity.
- The company is proceeding with a separate layoff of approximately 8,000 workers, representing 10 percent of its total global workforce.
- Impacted U.S. employees will receive 16 weeks of base severance pay plus two additional weeks for every year of service at the company.
- Affected staff were instructed to work remotely on the day of the layoffs, with official notifications delivered via email at 4 a.m. local time.

### Why it Matters
This significant internal reorganization highlights Meta's aggressive pivot toward artificial intelligence as the company seeks to compete in a rapidly evolving technology market. By flattening management structures and prioritizing AI development, Meta is attempting to streamline operations while navigating the difficult human and financial costs of large-scale workforce reductions.
