Blue Origin’s New Glenn rocket exploded on the launch pad, destroying its only pad and threatening Amazon’s satellite deadline
============================================================

A Blue Origin New Glenn rocket exploded during a static fire test at Cape Canaveral on May 28, destroying the vehicle and critical launch pad infrastructure at the site.

### Key Points
- The explosion occurred at 9 p.m. EDT during a test of the rocket’s seven BE-4 methane-fueled engines.
- The blast destroyed the rocket, the erector-gantry, and a lightning tower at Launch Complex 36.
- No personnel were injured during the incident, which Blue Origin officially classified as an "anomaly."
- The failure jeopardizes the upcoming NG-4 mission, which was intended to carry 48 Amazon Leo broadband satellites into orbit.
- Amazon faces a strict FCC deadline to deploy 1,618 satellites by July 30, 2026, to maintain its spectrum license.
- The incident occurred one day after NASA awarded Blue Origin a $188 million contract for lunar rover deliveries.

### Why it Matters
The destruction of the only launch pad capable of supporting New Glenn creates a significant bottleneck for Amazon’s satellite internet constellation and threatens the company's regulatory compliance. This failure undermines Blue Origin's commercial credibility at a pivotal time as the firm seeks to compete with SpaceX for dominance in the global launch market.
