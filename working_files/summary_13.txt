From Assistive to Agentic: The AI Shift That's Redefining Threat Management
============================================================

Enterprise security teams are adopting agentic AI to bridge gaps between siloed tools, enabling continuous threat exposure management and faster, autonomous responses to modern, machine-speed cyber threats.

### Key Points
- Enterprise security teams currently manage over 40 disparate tools, leading to fragmented data and average breach dwell times of approximately 43 days.
- Gartner’s Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM) framework aims to replace reactive, point-in-time assessments with a continuous cycle of discovery, validation, and mobilization.
- Unlike assistive AI that summarizes data, agentic AI autonomously executes multi-step workflows across security systems to close the gap between threat intelligence and remediation.
- Effective CTEM requires an orchestration layer that integrates threat intelligence, security posture validation, and automated response into a single, closed-loop operational model.
- Filigran is hosting a webinar to demonstrate how security teams can implement agentic AI to eliminate manual handoffs and improve overall threat response speed.

### Why it Matters
Transitioning to an agentic security architecture allows organizations to move beyond manual, human-coordinated responses that are increasingly ineffective against fast-moving, AI-driven adversaries. By automating the "white space" between security tools, companies can reduce analyst burnout and achieve a sustainable, proactive defense posture.
