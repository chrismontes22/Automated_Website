The FBI has arrested a 21-year-old alleged to have stolen $220,000 through Steam using malware-ridden games
============================================================

The FBI has arrested a 21-year-old Florida man for allegedly stealing $220,000 in cryptocurrency by distributing malware hidden within eight video games previously available on the Steam platform.

### Key Points
- The suspect and his co-conspirators operated for two years, using malware to scrape passwords and access victims' cryptocurrency wallets.
- Infected titles included Lunara, PirateFi, BlockBlasters, and Lampy, all of which were removed from Steam following an FBI investigation.
- Investigators tracked the suspect by linking stolen Bitcoin to gift cards used to purchase food delivery services like UberEats.
- The group utilized social media promotion and automated bots to specifically target individuals known to hold significant cryptocurrency assets.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing security risks associated with digital game distribution platforms as smaller developers and AI-generated content increase the volume of new releases. It underscores the urgent need for platform owners to implement more rigorous vetting processes to protect users from sophisticated malware distribution schemes.
