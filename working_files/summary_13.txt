AI agents are changing work — and Dell’s John Roese says it’s just beginning
============================================================

Dell Technologies Chief Technology Officer John Roese reports that the integration of autonomous AI agents is fundamentally reshaping corporate job structures and shifting organizational capital expenditure strategies for businesses.

### Key Points
- Dell has been testing fully autonomous AI agents within its internal operations for nearly two years to analyze their impact on workforce productivity.
- CTO John Roese emphasizes the need for a common language to classify job tasks, allowing companies to effectively rebalance work between humans and AI.
- The deployment of agents necessitates a comprehensive organizational redesign to account for roles where AI automates significant portions of existing workflows.
- The rise of "tokenomics" is forcing CFOs to reconsider capital expenditures as usage-based API costs for advanced AI models become increasingly complex.
- Dell is advocating for hybrid AI strategies to help organizations maintain control over token consumption costs and overall economic expansion.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward agentic workflows forces companies to move beyond simple automation and engage in a fundamental restructuring of human roles and operational budgets. By mastering the balance between human labor and AI-driven tasks, organizations can better manage the rising costs of token-based services while maintaining long-term economic efficiency.
