USB Vs. SD Card: Which Is Better For Storing Photos?
============================================================

Choosing between USB flash drives and SD cards for photo storage depends on your specific needs for device compatibility, transfer speeds, and the convenience of portable file management.

### Key Points
- USB flash drives offer universal compatibility across computers and devices, with transfer speeds ranging from 5 Gbps to 40 Gbps depending on the hardware version.
- SD cards are ideal for expanding smartphone or camera storage, with capacities ranging from 2GB in SDSC models to 128TB in SDUC formats.
- Both storage mediums rely on flash cells that typically degrade within 10 years, making them unsuitable for permanent long-term archival compared to hard disk drives.
- SD card performance varies by speed class, with write speeds ranging from 30 Mbps for U3 cards to 600 Mbps for E600 models.
- USB sticks provide a plug-and-play experience, whereas SD cards often require a dedicated card reader to transfer files to a computer.

### Why it Matters
Selecting the correct storage medium prevents data loss and ensures efficient file management for photographers and casual users alike. Understanding the limitations of flash-based storage helps users choose more reliable long-term solutions for preserving important digital memories.
