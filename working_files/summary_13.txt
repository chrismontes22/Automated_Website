Could Americans Build Wealth Through AI? Why Trump May Be Considering Equity-Sharing Scheme
============================================================

The Trump administration is reportedly negotiating with AI companies like OpenAI to acquire equity stakes, aiming to distribute wealth to Americans as a strategy against AI-driven job displacement.

### Key Points
- The proposal, known as "universal basic capital," involves seeding investment accounts for citizens using equity or dividends from major AI firms.
- Discussions between the White House and OpenAI CEO Sam Altman focus on companies voluntarily transferring shares to the federal government.
- Sen. Bernie Sanders plans to introduce legislation for a 50% tax on AI companies to fund a government-managed sovereign wealth fund.
- California Gov. Gavin Newsom has directed state officials to study similar wealth-sharing models to address economic inequality.
- Proponents argue these programs could replace traditional welfare, while critics warn of potential market interference and risks to individual investors.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward universal basic capital represents a significant intersection of emerging technology and national economic policy. By exploring ways to democratize ownership in the AI sector, policymakers hope to mitigate the social and financial risks posed by widespread automation and labor displacement.
