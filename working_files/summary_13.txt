Figure AI's CEO just raised $700 million for his next big bet
============================================================

San Jose-based startup Hark, founded by Figure AI CEO Brett Adcock, has secured $700 million in Series A funding to develop personalized AI hardware and interactive software systems.

### Key Points
- The funding round values Hark at $6 billion and was led by Parkway Venture Capital with investments from Nvidia, AMD Ventures, and Salesforce Ventures.
- Hark plans to launch its initial AI models this summer, offering users early access to a personalized platform designed for natural physical-world interaction.
- The company has recruited former Apple designer Abidur Chowdhury alongside engineering talent from Google, Meta, and Amazon to accelerate hardware development.
- CEO Brett Adcock, who previously founded Archer Aviation and Figure AI, provided $100 million in initial capital to launch the startup late last year.

### Why it Matters
This significant capital injection highlights the intensifying race among major tech firms to transition artificial intelligence from text-based chatbots into dedicated, personalized physical hardware. By attracting top-tier industry talent and major corporate backers, Hark is positioning itself to compete directly with hardware initiatives currently under development at Apple, Google, and OpenAI.
