T-Mobile Expected to Announce Changes to Device Promotion Program on April 2
============================================================

T-Mobile is reportedly planning to overhaul its device promotion program on April 2, potentially reducing the number of free devices offered and altering installment terms for wearable products.

### Key Points
- T-Mobile will reportedly limit free device promotions from four units down to two per cycle starting April 2.
- The carrier is expected to discontinue the 36-month installment plan currently available for Galaxy Watch promotions.
- Retail staff have expressed concerns that these reduced incentives could hinder their ability to attract customers from competitors like Verizon and AT&T.
- The company has not issued an official statement, though internal communications suggest staff should prioritize sales before the policy shift takes effect.
- T-Mobile shares fell 2.75 percent ahead of the anticipated announcement, though market analysts reported no unusual trading activity.

### Why it Matters
These changes represent a significant shift in T-Mobile’s competitive strategy, potentially weakening its ability to lure subscribers away from rival carriers through aggressive hardware incentives. If implemented, the reduced promotional structure could impact both retail sales performance and the company's overall market share in the telecommunications sector.
