There Are Three Types of AI Users
============================================================

Research indicates that widespread AI adoption is increasing workplace intensity and multitasking while potentially diminishing critical thinking skills and cognitive effort among users who rely on automated tools.

### Key Points
- ActivTrak data shows AI adopters increased their use of business software by 94% and doubled time spent on communication apps.
- Studies from MIT and other institutions suggest that over-reliance on AI can lead to a 40% to 55% decline in cognitive effort and brain connectivity.
- Research by Carnegie Mellon University found that users who lose access to AI tools perform worse on tasks than those who never used them.
- Experts identify a growing "cognitive polarization," where some users leverage AI to expand their capabilities while others experience a decline in mental sharpness.
- Recommended strategies to maintain cognitive health include starting tasks with a blank page, asking AI for hints rather than answers, and rotating between AI-assisted and manual work.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI-driven optimization threatens to hollow out human critical thinking and motivation by replacing the "cultivation" of skills with passive reliance on automation. This trend risks creating a societal divide between those who use AI to enhance their agency and those who suffer from cognitive atrophy, making the development of human volition and desire more essential than ever.
