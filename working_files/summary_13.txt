Ronald Reagan's Landmark Cybersecurity Policies Were Influenced By A Classic '80s Techno-Thriller
============================================================

President Ronald Reagan’s 1983 viewing of the film WarGames prompted significant concerns regarding national security, leading to the creation of the first federal cybersecurity policies in the United States.

### Key Points
- President Ronald Reagan held a private screening of the film WarGames at Camp David shortly after its 1983 release.
- The film’s depiction of a teenager hacking into a military AI system prompted Reagan to question the vulnerability of U.S. government networks.
- White House staff confirmed to the president that existing cybersecurity measures were insufficient to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive systems.
- These concerns led to the passage of National Security Decision Directive 145, the first presidential directive focused on cybersecurity.
- The administration subsequently enacted the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, which established the legal foundation for modern federal cyber-crime prosecution.

### Why it Matters
This historical event demonstrates how popular media can directly influence high-level government policy and national security priorities. The resulting legislation remains the foundational framework for modern American cyber-crime law and digital infrastructure protection.
