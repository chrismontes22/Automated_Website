Smartphone Prices Are Still Climbing. Here Are 3 Ways to Get Around That
============================================================

Smartphone manufacturers including Samsung and Motorola are raising prices on new and existing models due to inflation, component shortages, and shifting tariffs, prompting consumers to seek alternative purchasing strategies.

### Key Points
- Samsung’s new Galaxy S26 starts at $900, while older models like the Galaxy S25 have also seen midlife price increases.
- Motorola has increased prices on several Moto G models shortly after their initial market release.
- Electronics across the industry, including PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S consoles, are experiencing similar upward price trends.
- Experts recommend purchasing older flagship models or refurbished devices directly from manufacturers to avoid paying premiums for stagnant hardware upgrades.
- Consumers should prioritize devices with long-term software support, such as Google’s Pixel A-series or Samsung’s Galaxy A-series, to ensure longevity.

### Why it Matters
Rising hardware costs and minimal year-over-year innovation are forcing consumers to rethink traditional upgrade cycles to maintain value. By opting for older models or refurbished units, buyers can avoid inflated prices while still securing devices that remain functional and secure for several years.
