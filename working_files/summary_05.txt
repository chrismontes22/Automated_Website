This chunky little tablet got my kid to clean up his toys
============================================================

The Skylight Buddy is a $140 kid-centric tablet designed to help children aged four to 10 manage daily routines and chores through an interactive, app-controlled visual interface.

### Key Points
- The device costs $139.99, with an optional $39-per-year "Buddy Plus" subscription required for advanced features like rewards and visual timers.
- Parents manage tasks and routines through the Skylight app, which displays chores as emoji-labeled cards on the tablet screen.
- Built-in features include a night light and wake-up alarm, while the hardware is specifically angled for a child's eye level.
- Current software limitations include an inability to easily reorder tasks or move recurring chores between days without reconstructing routines.
- The device is designed for individual use, supporting only one child profile per unit.

### Why it Matters
The Skylight Buddy offers a digital alternative to traditional analog chore charts, potentially reducing the parental effort required to manage household routines. While the hardware is effective at fostering independence in young children, its high price point and subscription-gated features may limit its appeal for families seeking a simple, low-cost organizational tool.
