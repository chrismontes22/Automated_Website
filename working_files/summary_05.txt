Jon Prosser responds to Apple lawsuit by blaming the other guy
============================================================

YouTuber Jon Prosser has formally responded to Apple’s lawsuit regarding stolen iOS secrets, denying conspiracy allegations while admitting to recording unreleased software and sharing revenue with his source.

### Key Points
- Jon Prosser admitted to recording a FaceTime call that displayed unreleased iOS 19 features from a development iPhone.
- Prosser confirmed he shared YouTube advertising revenue with co-defendant Michael Ramacciotti to maintain exclusive access to information.
- The filing denies that Prosser conspired to access the device or knew how Ramacciotti obtained the proprietary data.
- Prosser argues that Ramacciotti is solely responsible for the disclosure of trade secrets and should indemnify him for resulting damages.
- The YouTuber maintains he reported on the leaked information as a news organization would and has requested a jury trial.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the ongoing legal tension between tech giants protecting proprietary software and independent creators who monetize leaked information. The outcome could set a significant precedent for how courts distinguish between investigative journalism and the illicit acquisition of corporate trade secrets.
