Satya Nadella says AI should benefit everyone — not just a few powerful firms "eating up the economy”
============================================================

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella warns that the artificial intelligence industry must prove its tangible value to the public to avoid backlash regarding resource consumption and workforce displacement concerns.

### Key Points
- Satya Nadella argues that AI should augment human capabilities rather than serve primarily as a tool for cutting jobs and reducing operational costs.
- The CEO introduced the concept of "token capital," a framework where AI tools and human workers coexist to create a continuous learning system.
- Microsoft is addressing public concerns regarding the high electricity and water demands required to power the massive data centers supporting AI infrastructure.
- Industry leaders, including Microsoft’s Mustafa Suleyman, are clarifying that AI is intended to automate mundane tasks rather than replace white-collar workers entirely.
- Nadella emphasized that corporations bear the responsibility of demonstrating the long-term economic opportunities that AI can unlock for the global workforce.

### Why it Matters
This shift in messaging reflects a broader corporate effort to manage public perception as AI integration faces scrutiny over environmental impact and job security. By framing AI as a collaborative partner, Microsoft aims to secure the social license necessary to continue scaling its technology investments.
