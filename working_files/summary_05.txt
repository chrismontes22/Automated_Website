I stopped relying on Google Play for apps, and here's what I switched to
============================================================

The new Android app store SafeHaven is gaining popularity by simplifying the discovery and installation of open source applications that are often unavailable or difficult to find on Google Play.

### Key Points
- SafeHaven functions as a privacy-focused storefront that aggregates open source Android apps from multiple sources, including F-Droid repositories.
- The platform provides security transparency by displaying verified developer status, malware scan results, and automated security check performance for each listing.
- Users can browse, rate, and install apps directly without creating an account or signing in, though the process requires enabling sideloading permissions.
- The interface features categories, charts, and search tools designed to make open source software discovery more approachable for general users.

### Why it Matters
SafeHaven addresses the significant discovery gap for high-quality, ad-free open source software that often gets buried in the Google Play Store. By prioritizing security verification and ease of use, it offers a viable alternative for users seeking more control over their mobile privacy and software ecosystem.
