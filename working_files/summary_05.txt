China hits back at U.S. sanctions restricting its exports to American defense firms
============================================================

China has imposed retaliatory sanctions on 10 American military-related companies and restricted government procurement from 46 U.S. firms following recent U.S. export bans on Chinese technology corporations.

### Key Points
- China’s Commerce Ministry banned the export of "dual-use" goods to 10 U.S. companies, including drone manufacturers and rare earth mining firms.
- The Finance Ministry prohibited government entities from purchasing products from 46 American companies, specifically naming units of Lockheed Martin, Raytheon, and General Dynamics.
- Sanctioned firms include AVEOX, Red Cat Holdings, Teal Drones, IMSAR, Jaia Robotics, Ball Aerospace, Oshkosh Defense, L3Harris Maritime, MP Materials, and USA Rare Earth.
- The move follows the U.S. Defense Department’s decision to add Chinese tech giants, including Alibaba and Baidu, to a list of firms with alleged military ties.
- Third-party entities are now prohibited from transferring Chinese dual-use items to the 10 targeted American companies without specific government approval.

### Why it Matters
These reciprocal sanctions escalate trade tensions between the world's two largest economies, potentially disrupting global supply chains for defense and high-tech components. The move forces multinational firms to navigate increasingly complex regulatory environments as both nations prioritize national security over established commercial partnerships.
