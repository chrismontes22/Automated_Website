The Circular Ring 2 is a decent, subscription-free smart ring, but it misses out on what made Circular rings truly unique
============================================================

The Circular Ring 2 offers health tracking features like ECG and sleep monitoring, but struggles to compete with industry leaders due to design flaws and missing promised functionality.

### Key Points
- The Circular Ring 2 features a titanium body with IP68 protection and weighs 3 grams.
- Battery life ranges from four days in Performance mode to eight days in Power mode.
- Key health sensors include ECG, PPG, temperature monitoring, and an accelerometer.
- The device lacks a vibration motor and physical buttons, limiting its interactive capabilities.
- Promised features like blood pressure and blood glucose trend estimates are currently unavailable and will be paywalled.
- The companion app suffers from slow data syncing and a dense, overwhelming user interface.

### Why it Matters
The Circular Ring 2 highlights the difficulty smaller manufacturers face when attempting to challenge established market leaders like Oura. While the device offers a subscription-free alternative, its design limitations and reliance on future paywalled features may hinder its long-term value for consumers.
