OnePlus never had a chance in the US
============================================================

OnePlus has officially exited the United States market twelve years after its debut, marking the end of the brand's struggle to compete within the carrier-dominated American smartphone landscape.

### Key Points
- OnePlus launched its first device, the OnePlus One, in 2014, building a loyal following through an online-only, direct-to-consumer sales model.
- The company began official carrier partnerships in 2018, starting with T-Mobile, but struggled to maintain shelf space as carriers prioritized premium flagship devices.
- Verizon ended its partnership with OnePlus in 2021, and T-Mobile stopped stocking flagship OnePlus models in 2022, limiting sales to low-end Nord devices.
- The US market is dominated by Apple and Samsung, which together account for approximately 90 percent of all smartphones sold through major carriers.
- The carrier-driven "bill credit" model, which subsidizes expensive phones over long-term contracts, undermined the value proposition of OnePlus’s affordable "flagship killer" strategy.

### Why it Matters
The departure of OnePlus highlights the difficulty for independent manufacturers to gain traction in a US market where carriers control the vast majority of device sales. This shift reinforces the dominance of Apple and Samsung, as the industry's reliance on long-term financing plans continues to marginalize alternative brands that prioritize upfront value.
