Elon Musk is steamrolling Wall Street to become a trillionaire
============================================================

**Elon Musk is preparing to take SpaceX public in a massive IPO that challenges traditional corporate governance standards and could make him the world’s first trillionaire.**

### Key Points
- The SpaceX IPO is projected to be one of the largest in history, with a valuation potentially reaching $2 trillion.
- SpaceX has secured rule changes allowing it to enter major index funds just 15 days after going public, significantly faster than the standard 90-day cooling period.
- Elon Musk maintains approximately 85% voting control through super-voting shares, effectively bypassing traditional board oversight and shareholder accountability.
- Financial filings reveal that while Starlink is profitable, other divisions—including AI and launch services—are currently operating at a significant deficit.
- The company’s S-1 filing includes ambitious, non-standard milestones, such as establishing a million-person colony on Mars, which are tied to Musk’s compensation and voting power.

### Why it Matters
The SpaceX IPO represents a significant shift in market dynamics, as the company’s structure forces passive index fund investors to participate in a high-risk, celebrity-led venture. This offering tests the limits of corporate accountability, as Musk’s influence and the "herd mentality" of institutional investors appear to override standard financial safeguards and governance norms.
