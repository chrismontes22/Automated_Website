Let him cook: How Weber Blackstone CEO Roger Dahle went from upstart to the biggest name in grilling
============================================================

**Blackstone founder Roger Dahle is leading the integration of his griddle company with the iconic Weber brand to create a unified, product-focused outdoor cooking powerhouse.**

### Key Points
- Roger Dahle, who founded Blackstone in 2008, acquired the legacy brand Weber in 2024 following a lengthy antitrust review by the Federal Trade Commission.
- The combined company now employs approximately 2,100 people and manages manufacturing operations across the United States, Poland, and Southeast Asia.
- Dahle is shifting Weber’s historically siloed, divisional structure toward a more functional, product-led model to increase speed to market.
- The company maintains two distinct brand identities: Weber focuses on premium, "crafting" experiences, while Blackstone targets fast, versatile, and accessible cooking.
- Despite the merger, the business faces ongoing challenges from high tariffs, fluctuating energy costs, and a competitive retail landscape dominated by private-label products.

### Why it Matters
This merger represents a significant shift in the outdoor cooking industry by combining a disruptive, viral-growth brand with a 70-year-old legacy manufacturer. By consolidating R&D and supply chains, Dahle aims to modernize Weber’s operations while leveraging Blackstone’s entrepreneurial culture to navigate a difficult retail environment and changing consumer spending habits.
