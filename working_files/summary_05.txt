It’s Not Just You, Everyone Is Mad At Roku’s Horrible Update
============================================================

Roku users are expressing widespread frustration following a June software update that introduced a cluttered, ad-heavy home screen interface and several persistent technical bugs across various TV models.

### Key Points
- The June update replaced a simple, user-customizable interface with an algorithm-driven layout featuring increased advertisements and promotional content.
- Users report difficulty organizing apps and HDMI inputs, which have been relegated to a less accessible "Quick Access" section.
- Technical issues include screen-scaling bugs when switching between gaming consoles like the PS5 and Xbox, often requiring a full power cycle to resolve.
- Community members have voiced significant dissatisfaction on platforms like Reddit, Twitter, and YouTube, with many requesting the option to revert to the previous interface.
- Forced content modules, such as a recurring "Soccer Zone," have proven difficult for users to permanently remove from their home screens.

### Why it Matters
This update highlights a growing trend of "enshittification" in smart TV software, where manufacturers prioritize ad revenue and algorithmic content over user experience and interface stability. As hardware becomes increasingly tied to intrusive software, consumers face fewer options for simple, non-intrusive television experiences in the modern market.
