Meta Tapped a Pentagon Supplier to Prototype Face Recognition for Its Glasses
============================================================

Meta is testing face-recognition software from Rank One Computing, a firm that supplies surveillance tools to the U.S. military and police, for potential integration into its smart glasses.

### Key Points
- Meta licensed facial recognition and liveness detection software from Denver-based Rank One Computing for its Meta AI app.
- Rank One’s technology is currently used by the U.S. Marshals Service, the Naval Criminal Investigative Service, and various police departments.
- Code remnants of the Rank One integration were discovered in a version of the Meta AI app shipped to millions of consumers.
- Meta removed the dormant face-recognition code from its app on June 5 following inquiries regarding the software's presence.
- NIST testing indicates Rank One’s algorithms exhibit varying error rates based on demographic factors, including sex and country of birth.

### Why it Matters
This development highlights the blurring lines between high-stakes military surveillance technology and mass-market consumer electronics. The integration of such powerful biometric tools into wearable devices raises significant privacy concerns regarding how personal data is collected and managed in public spaces.
