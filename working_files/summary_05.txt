Hasbro Tries To Force Child Actors Into Signing AI Voice Contracts
============================================================

Hasbro is facing backlash from the Agents of Young Performers Association for allegedly requiring child actors to sign over their voice rights for use in AI-generated commercial assets.

### Key Points
- The Agents of Young Performers Association issued an open letter signed by over 1,000 performers opposing mandatory AI voice clauses in children's entertainment contracts.
- Hasbro, the owner of the popular animated franchise Peppa Pig, has acknowledged the letter but did not deny being the studio targeted by the performers' concerns.
- Industry tensions regarding AI have escalated following recent disputes involving the likeness of Stan Lee, Capcom’s Mega Man series, and TikTok voice-cloning lawsuits.
- The proposed contracts would allow studios to utilize a child's voice for toys, merchandise, and digital media without requiring the actor to return for additional recording sessions.

### Why it Matters
This controversy highlights a growing conflict between major entertainment studios and performers over the ethical use of generative AI in commercial media. The outcome of this dispute could set a significant legal precedent for how voice rights are managed for minors in the global animation and toy industries.
