Dell CEO Describes Nightmarish Future Where AI Eats Up All The RAM For Years
============================================================

Dell Technologies CEO Michael Dell warns that surging demand for AI infrastructure and datacenters could increase global memory requirements by up to 625 times in the coming years.

### Key Points
- Dell Technologies CEO Michael Dell projects a massive surge in memory demand driven by the rapid expansion of AI accelerators and large-scale datacenter infrastructure.
- Industry analysts estimate that even conservative growth projections suggest memory demand will increase by 180 times current levels as AI adoption continues.
- The ongoing supply shortage has already forced manufacturers to raise prices on prebuilt PCs, gaming consoles, and handheld devices by 15 to 20 percent.
- Major tech companies, including Valve and various console manufacturers, are facing significant production challenges due to the limited availability of essential RAM and storage components.
- Industry experts have identified 2026 as a potentially critical year for hardware supply chains as AI hyperscalers continue to prioritize datacenter resource acquisition.

### Why it Matters
The aggressive consumption of memory components by AI developers is creating a long-term supply squeeze that threatens to keep consumer electronics prices elevated for the foreseeable future. This trend forces average users to face higher costs and limited availability for essential hardware like PCs, consoles, and storage upgrades.
