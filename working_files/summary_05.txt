I waited years for Googlebooks — but I wish I hadn’t
============================================================

Google’s new Googlebook laptop line is facing criticism for failing to offer a significant departure from existing Chromebooks, relying on AI features rather than professional-grade software capabilities.

### Key Points
- Googlebooks utilize Android-based software rather than a new, specialized operating system for professional workflows.
- The devices feature Snapdragon chipsets and a minimum of 12GB of RAM, positioning them as premium hardware.
- Key marketing features like the "Magic Pointer" AI tool are not exclusive to the new Googlebook platform.
- The devices continue to rely on web, Android, and Linux apps, which critics argue lack the professional software parity of macOS or Windows.
- Google has not yet confirmed pricing, though the "premium" branding suggests a high cost that may not be justified by current software limitations.

### Why it Matters
The launch of Googlebooks highlights a persistent gap in Google's ecosystem regarding professional-grade productivity and creative software support. By prioritizing AI integration over native application development, the company risks alienating power users who require reliable alternatives to established platforms like Windows and macOS.
