AI Job Grief: The Unnamed Psychological Crisis Hitting Tech Workers
============================================================

Tech workers are experiencing a form of "disenfranchised grief" as AI-driven job displacement threatens their professional identities and creates a psychological crisis without established clinical or social support.

### Key Points
- Psychiatrists Stephanie McNamara and Joseph E. Thornton have proposed "Artificial Intelligence Replacement Dysfunction" (AIRD) to describe symptoms like insomnia, anxiety, and identity confusion.
- Research indicates that cognitive professionals view their expertise as a core personality trait, making AI displacement feel like an erosion of the self rather than just a career change.
- A 2025 survey found that 29% of knowledge workers have sabotaged company AI initiatives, with the rate rising to 44% among Gen Z employees.
- Unlike previous industrial shifts, the rapid pace of AI adoption and the lack of institutional "shock absorbers" prevent workers from reaching the acceptance stage of grief.
- Public discourse on platforms like Reddit shows that workers are increasingly using anger and protest as a substitute for formal, socially sanctioned mourning rituals.

### Why it Matters
The lack of a recognized framework for this grief leaves workers in a state of "professional identity purgatory," which negatively impacts both mental health and corporate productivity. As companies continue to prioritize AI investment over human labor, the resulting unresolved trauma risks creating a volatile and disengaged workforce.
