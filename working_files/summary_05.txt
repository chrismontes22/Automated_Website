This startup wants to build an army of humanoid robot soldiers
============================================================

Robotics startup Foundation recently tested its Phantom humanoid robots in Ukraine to demonstrate their potential for military supply transport and future surgical operations in high-risk combat zones.

### Key Points
- Foundation deployed two Phantom humanoid robots to Ukraine in February for a pilot demonstration focused on supply logistics.
- CEO Sankaet Pathak aims to shift humanoid development away from domestic tasks toward military applications like reconnaissance and target extraction.
- The company has secured a $24 million contract with the Pentagon to further its defense-related robotics technology.
- Current technical limitations include battery life, durability in harsh environments, and the need for highly dexterous, reliable robotic hands.
- Eric Trump joined the company as chief strategy advisor in March to assist with its strategic direction.

### Why it Matters
The integration of humanoid robots into military operations represents a significant shift in defense technology, potentially reducing human casualties in dangerous urban combat environments. This development highlights an intensifying global race to leverage autonomous systems for national security, raising complex ethical questions regarding accountability and the role of humans in lethal decision-making.
