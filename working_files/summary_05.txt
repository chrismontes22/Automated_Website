Zhipu’s Chairman Amasses $22.4 Billion Fortune As Chinese AI Model Developer Soars Almost 1,000%
============================================================

Shares of Chinese AI developer Zhipu have surged nearly 1,000% this year, propelling Chairman Liu Debing to a $22.4 billion fortune as the company establishes itself as a tech leader.

### Key Points
- Zhipu’s market rally follows the successful launch of its GLM-5 and GLM-5.1 AI models, which demonstrate reasoning and coding capabilities comparable to global competitors.
- Chairman Liu Debing is now China’s wealthiest AI billionaire, while Chief Scientist Tang Jie has reached a net worth of $5 billion.
- The Beijing-based company is seeking to raise at least 15 billion yuan through a new share issuance on the Shanghai Sci-Tech Innovation Board.
- Despite a 132% revenue increase last year, the firm reported a 4.7 billion yuan loss due to heavy investments in research and development.
- Analysts warn of high volatility and speculative trading, noting that the stock price currently reflects growth projections extending through 2030.

### Why it Matters
Zhipu’s rapid ascent highlights the growing investor appetite for domestic AI alternatives in China amid ongoing international trade restrictions. While the company’s technological breakthroughs have secured its position as a market leader, its high valuation and lack of near-term profitability present significant risks for investors.
