If You Buy a New Router, It Might ‘Turn Into a Pumpkin’ Next Year
============================================================

**The Federal Communications Commission has issued an unprecedented ban on new foreign-made routers, effectively freezing the U.S. market while raising concerns about future security updates for consumer devices.**

**Key points**
- The FCC order restricts new models manufactured, assembled, or designed outside the United States due to national security risks.
- Existing routers currently authorized for sale remain available, but manufacturers face uncertainty regarding future supply chain compliance.
- Essential security firmware and software updates for affected devices are currently slated to end on March 1, 2027.
- Cybersecurity experts warn that the broad scope of the ban may inadvertently leave consumers with vulnerable, unsupported hardware in the coming years.
- Industry observers expect further clarification on specific company exemptions and compliance requirements within the next one to two months.

**Why it matters**
This regulatory shift creates significant uncertainty for consumers and manufacturers, as the global nature of router supply chains makes immediate compliance difficult. Because the order threatens to cut off critical security patches by 2027, experts advise delaying new router purchases until the market stabilizes and long-term support becomes clearer.
