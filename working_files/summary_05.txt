Your smart home is killing your Wi-Fi — here's the solution nobody talks about
============================================================

LoRaWAN technology offers an alternative to Wi-Fi for smart home connectivity by utilizing low-power, long-range radio signals to manage devices without congesting a standard home wireless network.

### Key Points
- LoRaWAN operates on a low-frequency, long-range protocol capable of reaching distances up to 15 kilometers, significantly outperforming traditional Wi-Fi coverage.
- The system requires specific hardware, including LoRaWAN-compatible sensors or nodes, a central gateway, and a network server like a Raspberry Pi running Home Assistant.
- Unlike mesh networks, LoRaWAN uses a single central gateway to manage device communication, effectively penetrating walls and covering large indoor or outdoor areas.
- Users must ensure devices explicitly state "LoRaWAN" compatibility on packaging, as standard LoRa-only hardware may not support the necessary network protocol.
- Practical applications include monitoring temperature, humidity, and security sensors in rural settings or large buildings where standard Wi-Fi signals are unreliable.

### Why it Matters
Adopting LoRaWAN reduces network congestion by offloading smart home traffic from standard Wi-Fi, leading to more stable and efficient device performance. This technology provides a scalable solution for users needing deep wall penetration and long-range connectivity that traditional consumer networking hardware cannot achieve.
