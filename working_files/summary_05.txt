5 Raspberry Pi projects I'm self-hosting this year instead of wasting money on monthly subscriptions
============================================================

**Raspberry Pi users can significantly reduce monthly subscription costs by hosting open-source alternatives like Nextcloud, Vaultwarden, and Jellyfin to manage files, passwords, and media on their own hardware.**

### Key Points
- Nextcloud serves as a private, self-hosted alternative to Google Drive, offering file synchronization and a suite of office applications.
- Vaultwarden provides a lightweight, secure password management solution that functions as a private alternative to LastPass.
- BentoPDF and OmniTools offer browser-based, client-side processing for PDF editing and file conversions, minimizing the resource load on Raspberry Pi hardware.
- Jellyfin acts as a free, feature-rich media streaming server that avoids the paywalls and promotional content found in platforms like Plex.
- Tailscale is recommended for securely accessing these locally hosted services from outside the home network.

### Why it Matters
Self-hosting tools on a Raspberry Pi allows users to regain control over their personal data while eliminating recurring monthly subscription fees for popular software services. This approach provides a cost-effective, privacy-focused alternative for managing digital workflows without sacrificing essential features.
