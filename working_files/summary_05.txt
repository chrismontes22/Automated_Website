New Apple TV and HomePod Mini Are 'Nearly Ready' to Launch, New Siri Remote Also Rumored
============================================================

Apple is reportedly delaying the release of updated Apple TV 4K and HomePod mini models until the launch of a more personalized version of Siri in September 2026.

### Key Points
- New Apple TV 4K and HomePod mini hardware has been finalized for months and is currently undergoing internal testing at Apple's Cupertino headquarters.
- The updated devices are expected to debut alongside iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 following the WWDC 2026 keynote on June 8.
- Hardware upgrades will primarily focus on newer chips to support advanced Siri features, with the Apple TV potentially receiving a refreshed remote control.
- The next Apple TV may feature the A17 Pro chip and an N1 chip for improved connectivity, while the HomePod mini could receive an S9 processor.
- Apple is also planning to release an updated full-sized HomePod and a new smart home hub later this year.

### Why it Matters
These hardware updates signal Apple's strategy to align its smart home ecosystem with the capabilities of its next-generation, AI-powered Siri. By synchronizing product launches with major software releases, the company aims to ensure its devices can fully support advanced intelligence features upon arrival.
