Google caps Meta’s access to Gemini amid AI demand surge
============================================================

Google has implemented new compute-based usage limits on its Gemini AI platform to manage surging API demand, affecting major enterprise clients including Meta as they integrate advanced language models.

### Key Points
- Google introduced rolling 5-hour refresh windows and weekly usage caps for Gemini API requests starting May 17, 2026.
- API demand for Gemini models more than doubled between March and August 2025, straining available infrastructure capacity.
- Meta has been in discussions to integrate Google’s Gemini models to enhance its advertising targeting capabilities.
- The new restrictions apply broadly across all customers through standardized spending tiers rather than targeting specific companies.
- Google reports continued sales growth for Gemini products, indicating that constraints are driven by high commercial demand.

### Why it Matters
These usage limits highlight the immense capital expenditure required to sustain frontier AI models, even for industry leaders like Google. The situation underscores a shifting market dynamic where tech rivals increasingly rely on each other's infrastructure to maintain competitive advantages in advertising and AI development.
