Dell 14S Review: Higher Price, Better Quality
============================================================

Dell has launched the 14S, a new mid-tier laptop featuring an aluminum chassis and optional OLED display, entering the competitive 2026 market with a starting price of $1,270.

### Key Points
- The Dell 14S serves as the premium successor to the Dell 14 Plus, positioning itself below the flagship XPS 14 lineup.
- The device measures 0.61 inches thick and offers high-end display configurations, including an OLED panel.
- Starting at $1,270, the laptop represents a significant price increase compared to previous models in the midrange series.
- Hardware compromises include a lower-quality webcam, mediocre 2-watt speakers, and plastic edge detailing.
- The keyboard design requires higher actuation force, which may cause user fatigue during extended typing sessions.

### Why it Matters
The release of the Dell 14S highlights a broader industry trend where mid-tier laptops are adopting premium materials and higher price points despite economic pressures. This shift forces consumers to weigh the value of high-end aesthetics against the performance trade-offs often found in non-flagship hardware.
