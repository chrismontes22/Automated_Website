Sony and Honda Cancel Their Big PlayStation-Integrated EV
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
Sony Honda Mobility has officially canceled the development of its Afeela electric vehicle line following a major strategic restructuring of Honda’s automotive electrification business in the United States.

**Key points**
- Sony Honda Mobility will issue full refunds to all customers who placed reservation deposits for the Afeela 1 sedan.
- The cancellation includes both the Afeela 1 sedan and the previously teased Afeela SUV concept.
- Honda cited a $15.7 billion writedown and a broader restructuring of its U.S. EV strategy as the primary reasons for the project's termination.
- The decision follows Honda’s move to cancel its Honda 0 series EVs and the Acura RDX in the American market.
- Market pressures, including the removal of federal EV tax credits and intense competition from manufacturers like BYD and Tesla, contributed to the shift.

**Why it matters**
The collapse of this high-profile partnership highlights the significant financial strain traditional automakers face while attempting to pivot toward electric vehicles amid shifting government policies and global competition. This move marks a major setback for Sony’s automotive ambitions and underscores the volatility currently impacting the U.S. electric vehicle market.
