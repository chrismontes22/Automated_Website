Pixel 11 Pro renders show off a missing sensor you might actually like
============================================================

Leaked CAD renders of the upcoming Google Pixel 11 Pro reveal a streamlined camera bar design and the potential removal of the infrared temperature sensor found in previous models.

### Key Points
- Leaked renders indicate the Pixel 11 Pro will feature a simplified, single-tone camera bar design.
- The infrared temperature sensor, introduced in the Pixel 10 Pro, appears to be absent from the new model.
- The device maintains a compact form factor with a 6.3-inch LTPO AMOLED display and dimensions of 152.7 x 71.8 x 8.4mm.
- Internal hardware is expected to include the new Tensor G6 chip, with potential adjustments to RAM capacity and base storage options.
- The Pixel 11 series is currently projected to follow Google's standard release schedule with an official launch in August.

### Why it Matters
The removal of the temperature sensor suggests Google is prioritizing a cleaner aesthetic over niche hardware features that differentiate its flagship lineup. These design and potential memory adjustments reflect broader industry trends as manufacturers balance component costs with the demand for premium, compact smartphone hardware.
