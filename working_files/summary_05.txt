Jamie Dimon says if you want to win competitive battles, you need small 'Navy SEAL' teams to move faster
============================================================

JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon advocates for organizing the bank’s workforce into small, agile teams to combat bureaucracy and maintain a competitive edge in his latest annual shareholder letter.

### Key Points
- Jamie Dimon emphasizes that small, highly focused teams are essential for winning specific competitive battles within the 320,000-employee organization.
- The CEO compares effective team structures to elite military units like Navy SEALs, prioritizing speed and autonomous decision-making.
- Dimon argues that while small teams drive innovation, companies must maintain efficient, enterprise-wide platforms for data, AI, and financial systems.
- The letter notes that AI integration will shift job roles, with the bank planning to redeploy employees affected by technological automation.
- Dimon continues to support in-office work mandates, asserting that remote environments hinder professional development for younger staff members.

### Why it Matters
This management philosophy highlights a growing trend among large corporations to adopt startup-like agility to remain competitive against smaller, faster rivals. By balancing decentralized team structures with centralized technological platforms, JPMorgan aims to scale innovation while mitigating the inefficiencies typically associated with massive global institutions.
