Nvidia salaries revealed: How much the AI giant pays software engineers and researchers as it ramps up H-1B hiring
============================================================

Nvidia is bucking the broader tech industry trend by increasing its H-1B visa sponsorships to aggressively recruit top global talent for its expanding artificial intelligence hardware and software operations.

### Key Points
- Nvidia secured certification for approximately 1,200 H-1B roles in the first two quarters of fiscal 2026, an increase from 1,000 during the same period last year.
- Major tech competitors including Google and Amazon have significantly reduced their foreign worker sponsorships amid a shifting regulatory environment and immigration crackdown.
- Federal filings reveal Nvidia offers high base salaries, with specialized roles like directors and distinguished AI algorithm engineers earning base pay exceeding $400,000.
- CEO Jensen Huang maintains that immigrant talent is essential to the company's mission as it scales its AI infrastructure and customer-facing support teams.

### Why it Matters
Nvidia’s commitment to international hiring highlights its aggressive strategy to maintain dominance in the competitive AI chip market. By securing top-tier global talent, the company ensures it has the specialized workforce necessary to sustain its rapid innovation and deployment of complex AI systems.
