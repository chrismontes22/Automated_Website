Join Our Livestream: Musk v. Altman and the Future of OpenAI
============================================================

Elon Musk and OpenAI CEO Sam Altman are set to face off in a high-stakes trial beginning April 27 to determine if the company abandoned its founding mission.

### Key Points
- The legal battle, Musk v. Altman, centers on whether OpenAI has prioritized profit over its original commitment to ensure artificial general intelligence benefits humanity.
- A judge and jury will preside over the proceedings, which could fundamentally alter how OpenAI develops and distributes its future AI technologies.
- WIRED will host a subscriber-only livestream on May 8 at noon ET to analyze the trial's developments and implications.
- The panel features WIRED experts Zoë Schiffer, Maxwell Zeff, and Paresh Dave, who cover Silicon Valley business and the inner workings of Big Tech.

### Why it Matters
This trial represents a critical legal challenge to the governance structure and ethical direction of one of the world's most influential artificial intelligence developers. The court's decision could set a significant precedent for how AI companies balance commercial interests with their stated public-interest mandates.
