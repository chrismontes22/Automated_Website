3 of the biggest mistakes a top Robinhood exec sees investors make, and how to avoid them
============================================================

**Robinhood Chief Brokerage Officer Steve Quirk identifies three common investor mistakes and provides actionable strategies to improve long-term portfolio performance through discipline, early participation, and effective asset diversification.**

**Key points**
- Steve Quirk advises investors to maintain a durable plan to avoid panic-selling during market volatility or failing to exit positions after reaching predetermined gain targets.
- Starting early allows investors to leverage compound interest, with Quirk noting that even small amounts invested via fractional shares can build significant wealth over decades.
- To mitigate concentration risk, Quirk recommends building a core portfolio using four specific indexes: the S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, Russell 2000, and MSCI Emerging Markets.
- Investors should limit individual stock holdings to a small percentage of their total portfolio to ensure they remain comfortable with their overall market exposure.

**Why it matters**
These insights provide retail investors with a framework to manage emotional decision-making and portfolio risk in an unpredictable market environment. By emphasizing long-term consistency and diversification, these strategies help individuals build sustainable wealth while minimizing the potential for significant financial losses.
