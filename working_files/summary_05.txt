The $6 Billion Chinese Startup Trying to Build Hands for Every Robot
============================================================

Chinese robotics startup LinkerBot has emerged as a dominant market leader by manufacturing affordable, dexterous robotic hands that are currently being adopted by global manufacturers and research laboratories.

### Key Points
- LinkerBot produces robotic hands with at least 11 joints, priced as low as $600, with plans to reduce costs to $200 within five years.
- The company shipped 10,000 units last year, capturing approximately 80 percent of the global market demand for dexterous robotic hands.
- Founded in 2023, the startup is currently seeking a new funding round at a $6 billion valuation and is reportedly exploring a Hong Kong IPO.
- Investors in the company include Ant Group, HongShan Capital, and the Chinese government.
- LinkerBot’s hands are capable of complex tasks including playing piano, threading needles, and assembling electronics.

### Why it Matters
By focusing exclusively on the most difficult component of humanoid robotics, LinkerBot is positioning itself as a critical supplier for the broader automation industry. This strategy allows the company to scale rapidly while helping other manufacturers lower costs and accelerate the global adoption of humanoid robots.
