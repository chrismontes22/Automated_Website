My AI startup pays people to film themselves taking out the trash. It's now valued at $150 million.
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
San Francisco startup Kled AI has raised $6.5 million at a $150 million valuation to pay everyday users for personal data used to train artificial intelligence and robotics.

**Key points**
- Kled AI, founded by 22-year-old Avi Patel, compensates users for uploading specific photos and videos from newer iPhones.
- The platform has reached 200,000 users and processes approximately 5 million data uploads daily.
- Investors include Waymo founder Sebastian Thrun and the venture fund of billionaire Bernard Arnault.
- Data is sold to AI, robotics, and self-driving vehicle companies to improve model accuracy and performance.
- Top contributors can earn significant monthly income, with some users reporting earnings of up to $7,400 per month.

**Why it matters**
Kled AI represents a shift toward a "consented data" model, challenging the industry standard of scraping information without user compensation. This approach provides a new income stream for individuals while offering tech companies a verified, high-quality alternative to unvetted training data.
