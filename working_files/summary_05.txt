Polestar Faces a Ban on Selling Its EVs in the US
============================================================

Polestar will cease selling new vehicle models in the United States starting in 2027 following a federal ban on Chinese-linked connected technology within the automotive industry.

### Key Points
- The U.S. Department of Commerce banned future Polestar models due to security concerns regarding the company's ownership by China-based Geely.
- Polestar confirmed it will stop selling new vehicles in the U.S. from the 2027 model year onward, including the upcoming Polestar 7 SUV.
- The company will continue selling existing inventory of the Polestar 3 and Polestar 4 while maintaining its current U.S. customer service network.
- Polestar is shifting its manufacturing focus toward Europe in response to the regulatory environment and ongoing trade restrictions.
- The Commerce Department is actively restricting Chinese-made connected technology across the automotive sector, recently penalizing other firms for similar security violations.

### Why it Matters
This decision highlights the growing impact of national security regulations on the global automotive supply chain and the integration of connected software in modern vehicles. It creates significant uncertainty for international manufacturers operating in the U.S. market as federal agencies continue to scrutinize foreign-owned technology.
