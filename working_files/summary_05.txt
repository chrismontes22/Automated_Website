Meta is tracking its employees' every mouse click — and they're mad
============================================================

Meta employees are protesting the mandatory installation of the Model Capability Initiative software, which tracks keystrokes, mouse movements, and screen activity to gather data for artificial intelligence training.

### Key Points
- Meta began installing the Model Capability Initiative software on US employee laptops last month to record screen activity and user navigation.
- The tracking tool captures specific data points, including mouse movements, button clicks, and dropdown menu interactions, to improve AI model training.
- Nearly 20,000 employees signed an internal petition demanding the company discontinue the monitoring program.
- Staff members expressed concerns regarding personal privacy and the ethical implications of using employee labor to generate training data.

### Why it Matters
This internal conflict highlights growing tensions between tech companies and their workforces regarding the ethical boundaries of AI development and workplace surveillance. The situation underscores the broader industry challenge of balancing the aggressive pursuit of proprietary training data with employee expectations for privacy and professional autonomy.
