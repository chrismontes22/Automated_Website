No more lightbulbs, much more sports: Five predictions for Roku’s future
============================================================

Fox’s acquisition of Roku is expected to reshape the streaming landscape by consolidating ad-supported services, phasing out IoT hardware, and prioritizing international growth and sports content integration.

### Key Points
- Fox plans to make The Roku Channel a platform exclusive while positioning Tubi as the primary ad-supported app for third-party devices.
- Roku is expected to discontinue its low-margin IoT product line, including smart cameras and lightbulbs, to focus on core streaming operations.
- The budget-friendly streaming service Howdy may receive increased investment to leverage Fox’s extensive content library and compete with premium rivals.
- Both companies will likely accelerate international expansion, building on Roku’s existing market presence in Canada and Mexico to scale their global reach.
- Fox intends to integrate its Fox One sports streaming service deeply into the Roku interface, including prominent placement within the platform's Sports Zones.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition signals a strategic shift toward consolidating media assets to better compete with major streaming incumbents like Netflix and Amazon. By streamlining hardware and software offerings, Fox aims to maximize viewer engagement and advertising revenue across its combined digital ecosystem.
