We talked to the comedians behind the viral fake AI ads on the subway
============================================================

New York comedians Dave Ross and Harris Alterman gained viral attention after placing satirical, nonsensical AI startup posters throughout Manhattan subway stations to mock current tech advertising trends.

### Key Points
- Comedians Dave Ross and Harris Alterman created and installed ten fake AI startup posters, including one titled "Cutlery.ai," across New York City subway stations.
- A video documenting the prank has accumulated over 3 million views across Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook.
- The duo spent approximately $200 on the project, which they removed shortly after filming to avoid potential issues with MTA officials.
- One of the fictional company names used, Wireflow, was discovered to be a legitimate AI business, prompting a response from its founder, Andrew Adams.
- Both creators have professional backgrounds in the tech industry, having previously worked as a social media director and a web developer.

### Why it Matters
The stunt highlights growing public fatigue and skepticism toward the influx of vague, buzzword-heavy advertising from emerging artificial intelligence companies. By parodying these marketing tactics, the creators have tapped into a broader cultural conversation about the perceived lack of substance in the current AI boom.
