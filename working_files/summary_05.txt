DOGE's Ethan Shaotran Is Now Running a Defense Tech Startup
============================================================

Ethan Shaotran, a former engineer for the Department of Government Efficiency, has launched a defense startup called Blitz Industries after departing his federal role in January 2026.

### Key Points
- Ethan Shaotran founded Blitz Industries, a defense-focused startup currently registered in the federal System for Award Management (SAM) database.
- The company is based in Hawthorne, California, and was incorporated in Delaware on February 12, 2026, with 25,000,000 authorized shares.
- During his tenure at the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE), Shaotran worked across multiple federal agencies, including the GSA and the Social Security Administration.
- Shaotran is among several former DOGE members who have transitioned into private sector startups or venture firms following their government service.
- The Pentagon is currently expanding its contractor pool, with venture capital firms investing over $49.1 billion into defense technology startups in 2025.

### Why it Matters
This trend highlights a growing intersection between government service and the private defense technology sector, raising questions about potential conflicts of interest. Critics argue that using government experience to quickly pivot into lucrative contracting roles may prioritize private profit over the public interest and the original mission of government efficiency.
