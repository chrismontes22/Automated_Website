Spotify’s Daniel Ek is bringing his body-scanning clinics to the US
============================================================

Neko Health, the preventive care startup co-founded by Spotify’s Daniel Ek, is expanding into the United States after securing $700 million to launch its AI-driven body-scanning clinics in New York.

### Key Points
- Neko Health plans to open its first U.S. clinic in New York this year, with a waitlist currently active on the company's website.
- The startup utilizes proprietary medical equipment and artificial intelligence to screen patients for conditions like skin cancer, heart disease, and diabetes.
- The $700 million funding round includes high-profile investors such as Mark Zuckerberg, Priscilla Chan, will.i.am, Maria Sharapova, and Thierry Henry.
- Founded in 2018, the company currently operates eight clinics across London and Stockholm, having performed over 100,000 scans to date.
- Reports indicate the company’s valuation has reached approximately $7 billion following this latest capital injection.

### Why it Matters
This expansion signals a significant shift toward private, proactive healthcare models that prioritize early disease detection over traditional reactive medical systems. As the longevity and biohacking markets grow, Neko Health’s ability to scale its technology will test whether high-end preventive screening can become a viable, mainstream alternative to conventional clinical care.
