ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 14 Review: Lenovo's Flagship Ultraportable Is Now More Repairable
============================================================

The Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 14 Aura Edition prioritizes modular repairability and a redesigned internal chassis over performance gains, maintaining a familiar design while offering a free OLED upgrade.

### Key Points
- Features a new "Space Frame" chassis that allows for easier replacement of the battery, cooling fans, keyboard, and specific ports.
- Powered by Intel Core Ultra 300 series (Panther Lake) processors, though performance remains largely unchanged from the previous generation.
- Includes a high-quality 2.8K OLED display as a standard upgrade option at no additional cost compared to previous models.
- Battery life has decreased by approximately three hours compared to the Gen 13 model due to higher power consumption.
- Offers expanded memory configurations up to 64GB, though high-capacity RAM upgrades carry a significant price premium.
- Weighs 2.16 pounds and retains the classic ThinkPad aesthetic, including the signature keyboard and a choice between mechanical or haptic touchpads.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward a more repairable internal design represents a significant improvement for long-term sustainability and return on investment for business users. While the lack of performance growth and reduced battery life may disappoint power users, the consistent pricing and easier component maintenance make this a practical choice for enterprise longevity.
