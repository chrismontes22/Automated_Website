CNBC's Jim Cramer Says He Needs 'Cold Hard' Proof AI Is Paying Off
============================================================

CNBC host Jim Cramer is urging corporations to provide concrete financial evidence that their significant investments in artificial intelligence are generating measurable revenue growth or operational cost savings.

### Key Points
- Jim Cramer warned that companies must prove AI investments are yielding tangible financial returns during the current earnings season.
- While AI infrastructure providers continue to see growth, many businesses adopting the technology have yet to report material gains.
- Block and Cloudflare are among the few firms that have explicitly linked recent workforce reductions to AI-driven operational changes.
- Critics are increasingly using the term "AI washing" to describe companies that may be using the technology as a convenient excuse for layoffs.
- Sustained lack of clear financial performance data could lead to increased market skepticism regarding the long-term value of AI spending.

### Why it Matters
This shift in sentiment highlights a growing pressure on tech-heavy portfolios to move beyond speculative spending toward proven profitability. If companies fail to demonstrate clear ROI, the current market enthusiasm for AI could face a significant correction, impacting future capital allocation across the technology sector.
