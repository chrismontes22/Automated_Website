Touchscreen Macbook '100% Confirmed,' Says Reputable Leaker
============================================================

Apple is reportedly developing a touchscreen MacBook Pro, marking a significant strategic shift for the company as it prepares to integrate touch-friendly features into its next-generation laptop lineup.

### Key Points
- Reliable leaker Instant Digital claims a touchscreen MacBook is confirmed for future high-end models.
- Upcoming hardware upgrades may include M6 Pro and M6 Max chips, OLED displays, and a thinner design.
- The devices could be rebranded as MacBook Ultra to reflect the significant hardware and interface changes.
- Apple intends to position the laptops as "touch-friendly" rather than "touch-first," allowing interchangeable use with mouse gestures.
- macOS 27, codenamed Golden Gate, is expected to introduce interface updates to support these new touch capabilities.

### Why it Matters
This development represents a major reversal of Apple’s long-standing design philosophy, which previously prioritized indirect input for the Mac. By adopting touch technology, Apple aims to bridge the gap between its tablet and laptop ecosystems, potentially influencing future industry standards for professional computing hardware.
