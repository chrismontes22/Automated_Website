Apple's legal team is striking social media sharing stolen iPhone 18 Pro data
============================================================

Apple is aggressively issuing DMCA takedowns on social media to remove leaked footage and technical documents of the unreleased iPhone 18 Pro stolen from a Tata assembly plant.

### Key Points
- Hackers stole over 630 gigabytes of data from a Tata manufacturing facility in India, including proprietary iPhone 18 Pro designs.
- Leaked content circulating on X includes videos of drop testing, A20 Pro data sheets, and logic board schematics.
- Apple is utilizing DMCA claims to force the removal of posts and suspend accounts that share the stolen material.
- Prominent leaker accounts, including a revived version of @EVLeaks, have been suspended by X following Apple's intervention.
- The breach provides direct access to sensitive trade secrets rather than speculative rumors, prompting an unusually aggressive legal response from Apple.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the severe risks associated with supply chain security and the potential for stolen intellectual property to disrupt product secrecy. By aggressively targeting these leaks, Apple is attempting to protect its competitive advantage and prevent the widespread dissemination of verified, commercially sensitive manufacturing data.
