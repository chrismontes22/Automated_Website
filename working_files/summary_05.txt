US Army soldiers say you can't just pour AI on new warfighting tech like ranch dressing
============================================================

The U.S. Army is testing its new Next Generation Command and Control software, integrating artificial intelligence to accelerate battlefield decision-making while maintaining strict human oversight for all targeting operations.

### Key Points
- The Army is testing the Next Generation Command and Control (NGC2) platform to streamline data processing and target identification.
- Soldiers from the 4th Infantry Division and 25th Infantry Division are evaluating AI tools to reduce cognitive load during complex combat scenarios.
- AI within NGC2 is designed to assist with logistics, such as predicting ammunition and supply needs, alongside tactical target analysis.
- The software development follows a "fail fast, fix fast" model, incorporating various commercial applications to modernize battlefield communications.
- Field exercises near Colorado Springs recently stress-tested the platform's ability to function on mobile devices and within vehicle-based command posts.

### Why it Matters
This methodical integration of AI reflects a strategic shift toward using technology as a specialized tool rather than a universal solution for battlefield challenges. By keeping humans in the loop, the Army aims to increase operational speed while mitigating the risks of automated errors in high-stakes combat environments.
