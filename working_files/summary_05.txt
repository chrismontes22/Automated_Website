Your Mac and iPad prices just went up, here’s why AI data centers are to blame
============================================================

Apple has announced widespread price increases across most of its product lineup, citing an unsustainable global memory supply shortage driven by the rapid expansion of AI data centers.

### Key Points
- Apple raised prices on most devices, excluding the iPhone and Apple Watch, due to surging costs for high-end memory components.
- The memory crisis is fueled by massive demand from AI data centers, which has caused the market price of DDR5 memory to quadruple.
- Micron is constructing new memory fabrication plants in the U.S., though these facilities are not expected to be fully operational until 2030.
- Apple is lobbying the U.S. government to allow procurement from blacklisted Chinese memory suppliers to alleviate supply chain constraints.
- Analysts project further price volatility, with potential quarter-over-quarter cost increases of 30% to 50% expected throughout 2026.

### Why it Matters
These price hikes signal a broader industry shift where the massive infrastructure requirements of artificial intelligence are directly inflating consumer electronics costs. As memory supply remains constrained, consumers may face a multi-year period of higher hardware prices and reduced value across the entire technology sector.
