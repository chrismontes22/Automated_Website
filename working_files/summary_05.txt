The Korean Telecom Giant at the Center of Anthropic’s Mythos Controversy
============================================================

The Trump administration has ordered Anthropic to revoke access to its advanced Claude Mythos and Fable 5 AI models for all foreign nationals following national security and vulnerability concerns.

### Key Points
- The White House mandated that Anthropic restrict access to its most powerful AI models to US citizens only, citing potential security risks.
- Anthropic chose to disable access to Claude Mythos and Fable 5 entirely rather than implement complex nationality-based screening processes.
- US officials expressed concern over South Korean telecom giant SK Telecom’s historical business ties to China and its access to the Project Glasswing program.
- Amazon researchers identified vulnerabilities in the Fable 5 model that could potentially allow users to bypass safety guardrails and access cyber-offensive capabilities.
- SK Telecom, which invested $100 million in Anthropic in 2023, denies having current ties to China that would compromise national security.

### Why it Matters
This dispute highlights the growing tension between AI developers and the US government regarding the export and accessibility of powerful, dual-use artificial intelligence technologies. The decision sets a significant precedent for how AI labs must manage international partnerships and security protocols to comply with evolving federal oversight.
