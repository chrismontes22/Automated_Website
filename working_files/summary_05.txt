So much for the AI jobs apocalypse. Demand for tech talent is rising.
============================================================

Tech job openings have increased by nearly 14% this year, according to TrueUp data, signaling a resilient labor market despite widespread concerns regarding the impact of artificial intelligence.

### Key Points
- Open tech job postings at 9,000 tracked companies rose 14% year-to-date, indicating a steady recovery from previous industry corrections.
- Hardware engineering roles saw a significant 52% surge, driven by heavy corporate investment in AI infrastructure like chips and data centers.
- Software engineering job openings experienced more modest growth, increasing by just over 2% since January.
- Large public tech companies reported an 18% increase in open roles compared to the start of the year.
- Increased competition for entry-level positions persists as a high volume of computer science graduates and laid-off workers enter the applicant pool.

### Why it Matters
This data suggests that the tech sector is successfully integrating AI tools without triggering the widespread job displacement previously feared by industry analysts. The trend highlights a shift in demand toward physical infrastructure and hardware, providing a more nuanced outlook for the future of the professional tech workforce.
