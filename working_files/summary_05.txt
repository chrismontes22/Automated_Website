Your Android phone has a hidden dashboard that shows which apps are spying on you
============================================================

Android’s built-in privacy dashboard provides users with a centralized, user-friendly interface to monitor and manage app permissions, helping identify which applications are accessing sensitive data like location or contacts.

### Key Points
- The privacy dashboard displays a 24-hour timeline of app permissions, including specific timestamps for camera, microphone, and location access.
- Users can navigate to the tool via Settings, Security & privacy, or by searching for "Privacy dashboard" directly in the device menu.
- Samsung Galaxy devices feature a customized version of the dashboard located within the Security and privacy settings under One UI.
- The interface allows users to instantly revoke permissions for specific apps that may be accessing data unnecessarily.
- Additional security can be maintained by running Google Play Protect scans to identify potential malware or unauthorized app behavior.

### Why it Matters
Regularly auditing app permissions is essential for maintaining digital security and preventing unauthorized data collection by third-party applications. This tool empowers users to make informed decisions about their privacy settings, reducing the risk of background tracking and unnecessary battery consumption.
