Analog hobbies are booming as AI burnout hits Gen Z hardest — and the Windows PC could be the unexpected winner
============================================================

Younger generations are driving a significant analog revival, prompting Microsoft to reconsider its aggressive AI-focused strategy for Windows 11 in favor of a more intentional, tool-based computing experience.

### Key Points
- Michaels reported a 919% increase in searches for analog hobbies like knitting and journaling in March 2026.
- Yarn accessory sales grew by 40% in 2026 compared to the previous year, signaling a shift toward hands-on activities.
- Microsoft is scaling back Copilot AI features in Windows 11 apps like Notepad and Photos following user backlash regarding performance and reliability.
- Windows president Pavan Davuluri confirmed in early 2026 that the company is prioritizing system stability and addressing user pain points over AI integration.
- The PC is increasingly viewed as a "workbench" for intentional tasks, contrasting with the constant notifications and distractions of smartphone-based digital environments.

### Why it Matters
This shift suggests that consumers are seeking an escape from the attention economy and generative AI fatigue by prioritizing manual, offline hobbies. By pivoting Windows toward a focused, tool-oriented platform, Microsoft has a strategic opportunity to align with this cultural movement and regain user trust.
