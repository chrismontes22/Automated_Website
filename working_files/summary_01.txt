Why AI hasn't replaced software engineers, and won't
============================================================

Evidence suggests that artificial intelligence will not replace software engineers because the profession relies on human decision-making and accountability that cannot be fully automated by current coding agents.

### Key Points
- Software development functions as a "decide-execute-deliver" sandwich, where AI only compresses the execution layer while leaving critical decision and delivery phases to humans.
- Recent corporate layoffs attributed to AI, such as those at Block, Snap, and Intuit, often reflect financial restructuring or "AI washing" rather than actual technological displacement.
- Data from New York’s WARN Act filings shows that AI-related layoffs account for less than 0.2% of total job losses in the state.
- Research indicates that AI increases the volume of code written but does not significantly reduce the human labor required for verification, debugging, and system integration.
- Software demand remains highly price-elastic, meaning cheaper production costs likely lead to increased software creation rather than a reduction in the need for engineering talent.

### Why it Matters
The misconception that AI will trigger mass unemployment in software engineering stems from a misunderstanding of the actual bottlenecks in the development process. By recognizing that human judgment and accountability remain essential, businesses can better focus on integrating AI as a tool for productivity rather than a replacement for skilled labor.
