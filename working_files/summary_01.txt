Here is what 3 AI startup CEOs say they're looking for when deciding to hire a candidate
============================================================

AI startup CEOs are increasingly prioritizing candidate adaptability, curiosity, and proficiency with artificial intelligence tools over traditional résumés when hiring for evolving roles in a competitive job market.

**Key points**
- Glean CEO Arvind Jain tests candidates by assigning complex tasks to see if they instinctively utilize AI to improve efficiency and overcome time constraints.
- Checkr CEO Daniel Yanisse seeks self-driven employees comfortable with uncertainty who are willing to reimagine traditional job functions using new AI technologies.
- Together AI cofounder Vipul Ved Prakash prioritizes candidates who demonstrate genuine passion for AI and startup intensity before evaluating specific technical role requirements.
- Companies are shifting hiring strategies to focus on long-term flexibility as AI automation continues to redefine the scope of engineering, product, and HR positions.

**Why it matters: **
These hiring shifts signal a broader transition in the tech industry where technical pedigree is becoming secondary to an employee's ability to integrate AI into their daily workflow. For job seekers, this means demonstrating a proactive, tool-agnostic approach to problem-solving is now as critical as traditional professional experience.
