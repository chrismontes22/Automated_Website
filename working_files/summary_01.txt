There's a 30-second change that makes public Wi-Fi noticeably safer — and it costs nothing
============================================================

Using encrypted DNS settings on public Wi-Fi networks protects your browsing activity from being intercepted or redirected by malicious actors who monitor unencrypted traffic on shared internet connections.

### Key Points
- Public Wi-Fi networks often use unencrypted DNS, allowing others on the network to track your browsing history or perform man-in-the-middle attacks.
- Implementing DNS over HTTPS (DoH) or DNS over TLS (DoT) encrypts your lookup requests, preventing unauthorized parties from harvesting your data.
- Users can manually configure secure DNS providers like Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) or Google in their device network settings to ensure private lookups.
- Android devices support encrypted DNS via the "Private DNS" setting, while iOS and desktop systems allow manual configuration or profile-based setups.
- Encrypted DNS is not a complete security solution and does not replace the privacy benefits of a VPN or the need to avoid sensitive logins on public networks.

### Why it Matters
Securing your DNS settings is a simple, free, and effective way to prevent network administrators or hackers from monitoring your online behavior on public Wi-Fi. While this does not provide total anonymity, it significantly reduces the risk of being redirected to fraudulent websites designed to steal credentials or distribute malware.
