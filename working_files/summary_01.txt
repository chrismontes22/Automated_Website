Who decides who gets to use a piece of software?
============================================================

**The U.S. Commerce Department recently ordered Anthropic to disable its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models globally, citing national security concerns regarding potential cybersecurity vulnerabilities and unauthorized access.**

### Key Points
- The directive required Anthropic to block all foreign nationals from accessing the Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models, regardless of their physical location.
- Anthropic complied by temporarily shutting down both models for all users worldwide, including its own international staff and domestic customers.
- Officials reportedly issued the order due to concerns that a China-linked group discovered a method to bypass safeguards and access raw cyberattack capabilities.
- This marks the first time the U.S. government has utilized national-security export controls to force a commercial AI provider to revoke access to a live service.
- While the models were restored within days, the incident highlights the vulnerability of global institutions that rely on U.S.-based AI services for critical operations.

### Why it Matters
This event establishes a significant precedent by demonstrating that the U.S. government can unilaterally disable commercial AI services globally with little notice or transparent process. It forces international businesses and governments to reconsider their reliance on American-controlled software, potentially accelerating the development of sovereign AI initiatives in Europe, India, and beyond.
