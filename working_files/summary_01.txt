SoftBank is entering the competitive GPU rental market to support AI training, leveraging a massive 10-gigawatt server farm currently under construction in the United States to meet demand.

### Key Points
- SoftBank aims to capitalize on the surging demand for high-performance computing power required for large-scale AI model training.
- The Japanese conglomerate is developing a 10-gigawatt server farm in the United States to anchor its new infrastructure-as-a-service offering.
- This move positions SoftBank as a direct competitor to established cloud providers and specialized GPU rental firms in the AI hardware sector.
- The initiative reflects a broader industry trend where major investors are securing physical infrastructure to control the supply chain for artificial intelligence development.

### Why it Matters
- This expansion highlights the critical shortage of compute resources that currently acts as a primary bottleneck for global AI development. By building massive, dedicated server capacity, SoftBank is attempting to capture significant market share in the high-growth AI infrastructure economy.
