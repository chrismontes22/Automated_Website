Google and Epic give up fighting — third-party Android app stores are coming next week
============================================================

Google and Epic Games have withdrawn their motion to modify a court injunction, clearing the way for third-party app stores to launch within the Google Play Store on July 22nd.

### Key Points
- Google will begin allowing rival Android app stores inside the Google Play Store starting Wednesday, July 22nd.
- The move follows a permanent injunction from Judge James Donato aimed at dismantling Google's illegal monopoly over Android applications.
- Google will charge third-party stores a $5,000 annual fee to cover security and policy reviews for the Play Catalog Access Program.
- Participating stores must maintain strict safety standards, including a requirement that no more than 1 percent of install attempts result in malware.
- US app developers will have their listings automatically shared with these third-party stores unless they choose to opt out.

### Why it Matters
This shift marks a significant reduction in Google's control over the Android ecosystem, potentially lowering fees and increasing competition for developers and consumers. By allowing rival storefronts directly within its platform, Google is fundamentally changing the distribution model for mobile software in the United States.
