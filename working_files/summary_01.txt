FBI arrests 21-year-old accused of infecting 8,000 PCs with malware through fake Steam games
============================================================

Florida resident Zyaire Dontaevious Zamarion Wilkins faces federal charges for allegedly orchestrating a malware scheme that stole over $220,000 in cryptocurrency from 80 victims via infected video games.

### Key Points
- Zyaire Dontaevious Zamarion Wilkins allegedly infected 8,000 PCs using malware embedded in games like BlockBlasters, Dashverse, Lunara, and PirateFi.
- The operation targeted cryptocurrency wallets between May 2024 and February 2026, with BlockBlasters alone accounting for $150,000 in losses.
- Investigators tracked the stolen funds by linking bitcoin transactions to over 150 Bitrefill gift cards used for Uber Eats orders.
- The suspects promoted the malicious software on platforms including Discord, Telegram, X, and LinkedIn to reach potential victims.
- FBI agents identified the operation through recovered Signal chats and evidence linking Wilkins to the purchase of a $10,000 remote access trojan.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing risk of cybercriminals using legitimate gaming storefronts to distribute malware and target high-value cryptocurrency holders. It serves as a stark reminder for users to exercise caution when downloading software from unverified sources, as even popular platforms can be exploited to facilitate sophisticated financial theft.
