Motorola gives its earbuds a luxury twist, but the Moto Tag 2 might be the bigger upgrade
============================================================

Motorola is launching the Moto Tag 2 in North America featuring Bluetooth 6.0 and UWB support, alongside new Swarovski-studded and Pantone-colored editions of the Moto Buds 2 Plus.

### Key Points
- The Moto Tag 2 includes Bluetooth 6.0, Ultra Wideband (UWB) technology, and integration with Google’s Find My Device network.
- The tracker offers over 500 days of battery life using a replaceable CR2032 battery and carries an IP68 dust and water resistance rating.
- Additional Moto Tag 2 features include remote camera controls, location sharing, and end-to-end encryption for enhanced user privacy.
- The Moto Buds 2 Plus Brilliant Collection features 12 Swarovski crystals per earbud and 41 crystals on the charging case, priced at $200.
- New Pantone color options for the Moto Buds 2 Plus, including Cool White and Silhouette, are priced at $150 starting June 30.

### Why it Matters
These updates expand Motorola's connected ecosystem by providing Android users with more precise item-tracking capabilities and competitive hardware specifications. By integrating advanced wireless standards like Bluetooth 6.0, the company aims to improve device interoperability and security within the broader Google Find My Device network.
