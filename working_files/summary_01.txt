Hope, Hype or Horror? 'The AI Doc' Director Charlie Tyrell Questions What Comes Next
============================================================

The new documentary *The AI Doc: Or How I Became an Apocaloptimist* explores the complex tension between existential dread and technological optimism surrounding the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence.

**Key points**
- The 104-minute film, directed by Daniel Roher and Charlie Tyrell, premiered in theaters on March 27.
- The documentary features interviews with industry leaders including OpenAI CEO Sam Altman and AI safety expert Dan Hendrycks.
- Production was led by the teams behind *Navalny* and *Everything Everywhere All at Once*.
- The narrative uses the directors' shared experience of new fatherhood to frame the debate over AI's future impact on humanity.
- The film highlights critical concerns regarding algorithmic bias, energy consumption, and the lack of regulatory oversight in AI deployment.

**Why it matters: **
This documentary provides a accessible, human-centric entry point for general audiences to understand the polarized debate surrounding AI. By balancing technical risks with potential benefits, it encourages viewers to engage with the ethical and environmental implications of a technology that is rapidly reshaping society.
