‘Our success with becoming… unicorns… isn't an accident – it’s the result of a 15-year bet on digital infrastructure and talent’: The story behind how Lithuania is perfectly aligned to capitalize on an emerging space economy
============================================================

Lithuania is rapidly emerging as a key player in the global space economy by leveraging its robust tech sector to specialize in modular, low Earth orbit satellite manufacturing.

### Key Points
- Lithuania aims for the space sector to contribute 1% of its national GDP by 2027, following 170% growth in high-tech industries over three years.
- Kongsberg NanoAvionics secured a €122.5 million contract to build 280 satellites for the Meridian Space broadband constellation.
- The country hosts over 40 space-related companies and maintains partnerships with the European Space Agency, the UK Space Agency, and NATO’s DIANA accelerator.
- Small satellite constellations offer increased resilience and lower costs compared to traditional, large-scale geostationary satellites.
- NanoAvionics utilizes standardized "satellite buses" to mass-produce spacecraft, similar to how smartphone platforms support various applications.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward mass-produced, low Earth orbit satellites is lowering the barrier to entry, allowing smaller nations to establish sovereign space infrastructure. This transition enables faster technological upgrades and more resilient communication networks, fundamentally changing the economics of the global aerospace industry.
