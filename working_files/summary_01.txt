There’s a devious new free phone scam, to add to the lengthy list
============================================================

Scammers are increasingly sending unsolicited smartphones to victims' homes to harvest personal data, urging recipients to avoid activating or interacting with any unexpected packages delivered to their doorsteps.

### Key Points
- Scammers send unrequested phones to victims' homes, hoping they will activate the devices and input sensitive personal information.
- Once a victim sets up the phone, the device locks or freezes, allowing attackers to steal data or compromise the user's identity.
- Experts warn that powering on these devices, scanning QR codes, or inserting SIM cards can grant scammers access to accounts and personal credentials.
- Previous iterations of phone scams include porch piracy, where criminals intercept packages ordered in a victim's name, or fraudulent return requests for legitimate orders.
- Security professionals recommend leaving unexpected packages untouched and avoiding any engagement with the hardware or preloaded software.

### Why it Matters
These scams represent a sophisticated evolution of identity theft that exploits the curiosity and trust of unsuspecting consumers. By compromising personal data through malicious hardware, attackers can gain unauthorized access to financial accounts and sensitive digital identities.
