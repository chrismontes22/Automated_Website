The Smart TV in Your LivingRoom Is a Node in the AIScraping Economy
============================================================

Bright Data utilizes a residential proxy network of over 400 million IP addresses, often sourced through software development kits embedded in consumer mobile apps and smart TVs.

### Key Points
- Bright Data’s SDK turns consumer devices into exit nodes, allowing third-party customers to route web-scraping traffic through residential IP addresses.
- Smart TVs are considered ideal proxies due to their constant power connection, high-speed Wi-Fi, and lack of corporate or family oversight.
- The SDK includes features that bypass VPNs and standard network inspection tools by binding traffic directly to physical network interfaces.
- Partner apps identified in public manifests include various gaming and utility applications, with some configurations allowing up to 200 GB of monthly bandwidth usage.
- Security researchers identified specific DNS hostnames and TLS fingerprints that can be used to block the SDK’s peer-tunnel traffic at the network level.

### Why it Matters
Residential proxy networks are increasingly used by AI companies to bypass anti-scraping protections that block traffic from known datacenter IP addresses. This practice effectively turns personal home internet connections into infrastructure for large-scale data harvesting without most users fully understanding the scope of the resource consumption.
