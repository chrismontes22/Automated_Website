Google now lets you sign in to your account using a selfie video
============================================================

Google has introduced a new account recovery feature that allows users to verify their identity by recording and comparing short selfie videos to regain access to locked accounts.

### Key Points
- Users can set up the feature by following onscreen prompts to capture multiple angles of their face through head movements.
- The reference video is encrypted and stored in the cloud, allowing for account access across different devices.
- Google employs security measures to detect and prevent impersonation attempts using static photos or deepfake technology.
- Users can manage or delete their stored reference video at any time through their Google account settings.
- The feature is currently undergoing a phased global rollout, with eligibility status available at g.co/signin-selfie.

### Why it Matters
This update provides a critical alternative for users who lose access to traditional two-factor authentication methods or forget their passwords. By leveraging biometric verification, Google aims to reduce account lockouts while maintaining security standards against sophisticated digital impersonation threats.
