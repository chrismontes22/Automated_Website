'Your login credentials may already be slipping into the hands of a cybercriminal': Hackers target LinkedIn accounts with devious new phishing attacks — here's how to stay safe
============================================================

Cybersecurity researchers at Cofense have identified a sophisticated phishing campaign where hackers use fraudulent LinkedIn notifications and spoofed domains to steal sensitive user login credentials from unsuspecting professionals.

### Key Points
- Attackers use deceptive domains like "inedin[.]digital" to mimic legitimate LinkedIn notifications and gain user trust.
- Phishing emails frequently pose as urgent job opportunities from reputable firms to manipulate recipients into clicking malicious links.
- Campaigns incorporate stolen personal data, such as home addresses and Google Maps screenshots, to increase the credibility of extortion attempts.
- Threat actors are rapidly deploying new infrastructure, with many fraudulent websites and email accounts created only days before an attack.
- Cofense has released technical indicators of compromise, including IP addresses and payload URLs, to help security teams mitigate these threats.

### Why it Matters
These highly personalized phishing attacks demonstrate how threat actors are leveraging automation and public data to bypass traditional security awareness. Organizations must prioritize rapid threat detection and employee training to prevent credential theft and potential unauthorized access to corporate networks.
