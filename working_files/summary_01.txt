Cybercrime Crew Claims It Hacked Mike Lindell’s MyPillow
============================================================

The Russian-speaking ransomware group Play claims to have stolen sensitive financial and personal data from MyPillow, though CEO Mike Lindell denies the breach and alleges political motivation.

### Key Points
- The ransomware group Play claims to possess payroll, tax, and client documents stolen from the Minnesota-based company MyPillow.
- MyPillow CEO Mike Lindell dismissed the claims as a "hit job" related to his gubernatorial campaign and denied any data security breaches.
- The FBI recently warned that the Silent Ransom Group is now physically sending individuals to company offices to steal data directly via USB drives.
- BusPatrol is expanding its school bus camera technology to function as automatic license plate readers, providing vehicle location data to law enforcement without warrants.
- A University of Chicago study found that Chicago police response times improved after the city discontinued the use of ShotSpotter gunshot detection technology.

### Why it Matters
These incidents highlight the evolving risks of data extortion and the increasing use of surveillance technology by both private companies and law enforcement. As ransomware groups adopt physical intrusion tactics and corporate data becomes a target for political or financial leverage, organizations face heightened pressure to secure their digital and physical infrastructure.
