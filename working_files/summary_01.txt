Hacker wipes Romania's land registry database
============================================================

A hacker breached Romania’s National Agency for Cadastre and Real Estate Advertising, wiping the country’s land registry database and halting real-estate transactions after a failed extortion attempt.

### Key Points
- The breach at the National Agency for Cadastre and Real Estate Advertising (ANCPI) occurred on July 14, forcing the agency to take its systems offline.
- The attacker, identified as Zakaria Mahdjoub (alias ByteToBreach), gained access using valid credentials and deleted both live data and backups.
- Stolen internal documents and employee credentials were subsequently posted for sale on a known hacking forum.
- ANCPI is currently rebuilding its entire network from scratch, though officials reportedly recovered an offline backup to prevent long-term data loss.
- This incident marks the latest in a series of land registry hacks targeting government agencies across Europe and beyond over the last three years.

### Why it Matters
The total loss of digital land records has paralyzed Romania's real-estate market, preventing notaries from processing transactions and citizens from verifying property ownership. This event highlights the critical vulnerability of national infrastructure to extortion-driven cyberattacks and underscores the necessity of maintaining secure, offline backups for essential government services.
