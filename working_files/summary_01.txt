The Threat Landscape Has Evolved to Target Your Data, Not Just Your Devices
============================================================

Modern cybercriminals are increasingly shifting their focus from crashing computers to stealing personal and financial data, resulting in significant identity theft risks and billions of dollars in annual losses.

### Key Points
- Cyber-related crimes cost Americans approximately $21 billion in 2025, according to the FBI’s Internet Crime Report.
- Common attack methods include social engineering, ransomware, and adversary-in-the-middle attacks to intercept sensitive information.
- The Identity Theft Resource Center recorded 3,322 data breaches in 2025, highlighting the scale of institutional security vulnerabilities.
- Personal data is also harvested through non-malicious tools like tracking pixels, cookies, and web scrapers used by third-party advertisers.
- Security experts recommend using password managers, VPNs, passkeys, and antivirus software to minimize digital footprints and protect against identity theft.

### Why it Matters
The evolution of cybercrime toward data theft creates long-term financial and personal consequences that can take years for victims to resolve. By understanding these threats and adopting proactive security measures, individuals can significantly reduce their vulnerability to identity fraud and financial loss.
