The World Food Programme has confirmed a data breach exposing the personal information of 600,000 vulnerable families in Gaza, though aid operations will continue despite the security incident.

### Key Points
- The breach compromised sensitive data belonging to 600,000 individuals receiving humanitarian assistance in Gaza.
- The World Food Programme confirmed the incident occurred within a territory currently facing severe famine and ongoing conflict.
- Affected families have been notified that their aid distributions will remain unaffected by the security compromise.
- The organization is currently managing the fallout of the data exposure while maintaining its critical support services for the region.

### Why it Matters
This breach highlights the extreme risks associated with digitizing humanitarian aid data in active conflict zones where privacy is a matter of physical safety. The incident underscores the urgent need for robust cybersecurity protocols to protect vulnerable populations from potential exploitation or targeted harm.
