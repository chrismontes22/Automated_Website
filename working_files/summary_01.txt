Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand Wants to Save Crypto — But Trump Windfall Is a Political Obstacle
============================================================

The proposed Clarity Act faces legislative uncertainty in the Senate as concerns over Donald Trump’s $1.4 billion crypto windfall complicate efforts to secure necessary Democratic support for the bill.

### Key Points
- The Clarity Act aims to establish a comprehensive regulatory framework for the cryptocurrency industry and unlock significant institutional capital.
- Senate Democrats hold the deciding votes, as the bill requires support from at least seven members to overcome a potential filibuster.
- Critics and some lawmakers, including Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand, are demanding ethics provisions to prevent presidential profiteering before backing the legislation.
- The crypto industry has invested $189 million in the current election cycle, including $82 million through the industry-backed super PAC Fairshake.
- Bitcoin prices have experienced significant volatility, currently trading near $63,000 after reaching highs of approximately $120,000 last year.

### Why it Matters
The passage of the Clarity Act would signal a major shift toward integrating digital assets into the mainstream financial system, potentially inviting massive institutional investment. However, the intersection of industry lobbying and presidential ethics has turned the bill into a political flashpoint that tests the influence of corporate campaign spending on legislative priorities.
