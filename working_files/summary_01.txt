A VPN Can Only Protect Your Privacy So Much. Here’s What to Consider
============================================================

A virtual private network (VPN) encrypts internet traffic and masks IP addresses, but it is not a comprehensive security solution and cannot guarantee total online anonymity or malware protection.

### Key Points
- VPNs hide browsing activity from internet service providers and bypass region-based content restrictions.
- They do not prevent tracking via browser fingerprinting or identify users who are logged into personal accounts like Google or Facebook.
- VPNs lack built-in protection against malware, phishing scams, and social engineering attacks.
- Users remain vulnerable to WebRTC and DNS leaks, which can expose real IP addresses despite an active VPN connection.
- Reliable providers should offer transparent no-logs policies, undergo regular independent third-party audits, and operate in privacy-friendly jurisdictions.
- Cybersecurity experts recommend supplementing VPNs with antivirus software, password managers, and browser-based security extensions like Bitdefender TrafficLight.

### Why it Matters
Relying solely on a VPN creates a false sense of security that leaves users exposed to common digital threats like identity theft and malware. A robust defense requires a layered approach that combines privacy tools with dedicated security software to protect personal data effectively.
