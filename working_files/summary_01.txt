Worries over water as a giant data center moves into the New Mexico desert
============================================================

The massive Project Jupiter data center in New Mexico’s Chihuahuan Desert faces intense local scrutiny over its potential impact on regional water supplies and long-term agricultural sustainability.

### Key Points
- Project Jupiter is a massive data center development in Doña Ana County backed by tech giants Oracle and OpenAI.
- The project involves a $165 billion investment and aims to power AI infrastructure using a 2.5-gigawatt energy capacity.
- Developers plan to utilize existing water rights purchased from a local sod farm to cool the facility’s server systems.
- Local residents and farmers express concern that the facility will deplete groundwater levels during an ongoing, severe regional drought.
- The Doña Ana Board of County Commissioners approved the project’s industrial revenue bonds despite significant public opposition and calls for more transparency.

### Why it Matters
The project highlights the growing tension between the massive resource demands of the AI industry and the environmental realities of drought-stricken regions. As tech companies seek remote locations for hyperscale data centers, local governments must balance the promise of significant economic investment against the long-term security of essential natural resources.
