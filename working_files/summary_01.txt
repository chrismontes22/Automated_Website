How to sideload books on your Kindle after Amazon cuts off the store
============================================================

Amazon has officially ended support for Kindle models released before 2012, preventing these devices from connecting to the Kindle Store to purchase or download new digital book content.

### Key Points
- Affected devices include the original Kindle, Kindle Keyboard, Kindle Touch, Kindle 4, Kindle 5, first-generation Kindle Paperwhite, and early Kindle Fire tablets.
- Users can still read books already downloaded to their devices, provided they do not perform a factory reset or deregister the account.
- Owners can continue adding content by sideloading DRM-free EPUB or PDF files via a USB cable or by using management software like Calibre.
- Jailbreaking is a technical alternative for advanced users to install open-source viewers, though it carries a high risk of permanently damaging the hardware.

### Why it Matters
This policy change highlights the limitations of hardware longevity in an ecosystem reliant on cloud-based digital storefronts. While users can extend the life of their devices through manual file management, the loss of direct store access serves as a practical prompt for consumers to consider upgrading to modern, supported eReaders.
