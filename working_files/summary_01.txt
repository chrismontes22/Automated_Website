Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang says ‘I think we’ve achieved AGI’
============================================================

**Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang claims we have reached Artificial General Intelligence, though he later qualified the statement.**

**Key points**
*   During a podcast interview, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang stated that AI has already reached the level of "Artificial General Intelligence" (AGI).
*   AGI is generally defined as AI that can match or exceed human intelligence across a wide range of tasks.
*   Huang’s definition was linked to the ability of AI agents to perform complex, autonomous tasks, such as running a successful business.
*   Huang later walked back the claim, noting that while AI agents are capable of viral, short-term tasks, they are currently nowhere near capable of complex, long-term operations like running a company like Nvidia.
*   The term "AGI" remains highly controversial and lacks a universal definition, leading many tech leaders to use it inconsistently.

**Why it matters**
The debate over AGI is critical because it influences massive financial investments and shapes public expectations about the future of human labor. Whether we have truly reached this milestone or are simply using the term as marketing hype has significant implications for the safety, regulation, and economic impact of AI.
