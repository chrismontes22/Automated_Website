Security News This Week: LastPass Users Had Their Data Stolen—Again
============================================================

Former national security adviser John Bolton pleaded guilty to mishandling classified defense information, while international law enforcement agencies disrupted major malware infrastructure used by global cybercriminal networks this week.

### Key Points
- John Bolton, 77, faces a potential five-year prison sentence and a $2.25 million fine after pleading guilty to the illegal retention of classified defense information.
- Microsoft and Europol’s Operation Endgame seized 326 servers and 142 domains linked to the Amadey and StealC infostealer malware.
- The operation recovered 27 million stolen access credentials and identified $47 million in illicit cryptocurrency.
- Australia’s ASIO discovered nation-state hackers inside a critical infrastructure provider, noting the actors were mapping networks to prepare for potential future sabotage.
- LastPass confirmed a data breach involving customer contact information, resulting from a third-party security compromise at the business intelligence firm Klue.

### Why it Matters
These events highlight the escalating risks posed by both internal security lapses and sophisticated state-sponsored cyber threats to global infrastructure. The successful disruption of major malware networks demonstrates the growing role of AI-assisted analysis in modern law enforcement, even as organizations struggle to secure sensitive data against persistent digital attacks.
