PSA: Incognito Mode Doesn't Totally Hide Your Online Activity
============================================================

Private browsing modes in web browsers like Chrome, Safari, and Firefox primarily clear local history and cookies, but they do not provide complete anonymity from websites or network administrators.

### Key Points
- Private browsing modes, such as Chrome's Incognito or Edge's InPrivate, prevent browsers from saving search history, cookies, and site data locally.
- These features do not hide activity from internet service providers, employers, schools, or the websites themselves.
- Downloaded files and saved bookmarks remain on the device even after a private session is closed.
- IP addresses are not masked by private browsing, meaning your general location and connection remain visible to third parties.
- A 2024 legal settlement required Google to destroy billions of data records collected from users who believed they were browsing privately.

### Why it Matters
Understanding the limitations of private browsing is essential for maintaining digital privacy, as many users mistakenly believe these modes offer total invisibility. Relying on these tools for sensitive tasks can lead to unintended data exposure, highlighting the need for additional security measures like VPNs for those seeking to mask their network activity.
