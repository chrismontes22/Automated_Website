US Sanctions First VPN in Crackdown on Ransomware Criminals
============================================================

The U.S. Department of the Treasury has sanctioned the First VPN service and two administrators for providing infrastructure and obfuscation tools that facilitated global ransomware attacks against American institutions.

### Key Points
- The Treasury sanctioned First VPN, administrator Dmytro Rashevskyi, and Yegeniy Vladimirovich Silayev for enabling cybercriminal activity.
- First VPN, also known as 1VPNS, provided anonymous infrastructure and "cryptor" tools designed to bypass security systems and hide malware.
- European authorities dismantled the service and arrested Rashevskyi in May following a multi-year investigation that began in 2021.
- The service was widely advertised on Russian-language cybercrime forums since 2014, promising no data logging and refusal to cooperate with law enforcement.
- Sanctions prohibit U.S. persons and financial institutions from engaging in transactions involving the property or interests of the designated individuals.

### Why it Matters
These sanctions represent a significant effort to disrupt the infrastructure that supports the global ransomware economy, which is estimated to cost $74 billion annually. By targeting the specific tools used to mask malicious activity, the Treasury aims to increase the operational costs and risks for cybercriminals targeting critical U.S. infrastructure.
