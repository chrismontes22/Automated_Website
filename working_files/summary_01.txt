Week in review: High severity WordPress vulnerabilities, fake OAuth IDs bypass sign-in logs
============================================================

Recent cybersecurity developments include critical WordPress vulnerabilities, sophisticated OAuth-based sign-in bypasses, and a surge in AI-driven threats targeting both enterprise infrastructure and individual user accounts.

### Key Points
- WordPress released a security update addressing one critical and one high-severity vulnerability.
- Attackers are spoofing OAuth client IDs to evade detection within Microsoft Entra ID sign-in logs.
- A ransomware attack on Coca-Cola’s Fairlife brand halted milk production, as reported in an SEC filing.
- Microsoft’s July 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed over 570 vulnerabilities, including two actively exploited zero-days.
- Researchers identified a new macOS infostealer, CrashStealer, which masquerades as Apple’s crash-reporting tool to harvest credentials.
- SonicWall issued urgent firmware updates for its SMA 1000 Series appliances to mitigate two actively exploited zero-day vulnerabilities.

### Why it Matters
These incidents highlight a growing trend where attackers leverage both legacy software flaws and modern AI-driven automation to bypass traditional security perimeters. For organizations, this necessitates a shift toward more rigorous patch management and the adoption of advanced, context-aware security tools to defend against increasingly sophisticated social engineering and automated exploitation.
