Here is what 3 AI startup CEOs say they're looking for when deciding to hire a candidate
============================================================

AI startup CEOs are increasingly prioritizing candidate adaptability, curiosity, and proficiency with generative tools over traditional résumés when hiring for evolving roles in a rapidly changing technology landscape.

Key points
- Glean CEO Arvind Jain tests candidates by assigning complex tasks to see if they instinctively utilize AI to improve efficiency and overcome time constraints.
- Checkr CEO Daniel Yanisse seeks employees who thrive in uncertain environments and are willing to reimagine traditional job functions using new AI assistance tools.
- Together AI cofounder Vipul Ved Prakash prioritizes genuine passion for AI and startup intensity over specific technical requirements during the initial hiring phase.
- Companies are shifting away from hiring for static, narrow job descriptions in favor of versatile individuals who can evolve alongside shifting business needs.
- Hiring remains active for these firms across major hubs including San Francisco, Denver, and San Diego despite broader economic uncertainty in the white-collar job market.

Why it matters: 
These hiring shifts signal a broader transition in the labor market where technical agility and the ability to integrate AI into workflows are becoming more valuable than traditional pedigree. As companies automate routine tasks, the ability to adapt to changing job functions will likely become a primary requirement for long-term career stability in the tech sector.
