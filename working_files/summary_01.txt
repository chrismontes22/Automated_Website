The Empty Field That Wasn't: GPS, OTAD and Two Decades of Encrypted Broadcasts
============================================================

Researchers have discovered that the GPS L1 signal has been broadcasting encrypted military data for nearly two decades, effectively functioning as a global, publicly accessible numbers station.

### Key Points
- Analysis of 12.16 million observations from 2007 to 2026 confirms that Subframe 4, Page 17 of the GPS signal carries high-entropy ciphertext rather than readable text.
- The data stream is linked to the U.S. Over-the-Air Distribution (OTAD) network, which delivers cryptographic keys to military receivers equipped with SAASM modules.
- Researchers identified structural patterns, including hardware test signals (0xAA) and protocol headers like "LY47IRP16," that indicate coordinated fleet-wide cryptographic updates.
- A significant network shift occurred on 26 May 2011, when all 31 active GPS satellites synchronized to a new operational pace for key distribution.
- Recent changes in late 2023 introduced a new "TEXT" prefix format, suggesting ongoing evolution in how military data is transmitted over civilian infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
This discovery reveals that critical military cryptographic infrastructure has been operating in plain sight within the global civilian GPS signal for twenty years. It highlights a significant intersection between public navigation systems and secure military communications, offering security researchers a unique opportunity to study operational traffic analysis.
