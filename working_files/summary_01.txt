**Tech investor Elad Gil discusses the current AI landscape, the "personal IPO" phenomenon among top researchers, and the critical compute constraints shaping the industry's near-term future.**

### Key Points
- Meta’s aggressive hiring and competitive compensation packages have effectively triggered a "personal IPO" for hundreds of top AI researchers.
- The AI industry is currently facing a significant supply chain bottleneck specifically related to high-bandwidth memory, which is expected to persist for approximately two years.
- Compute constraints are creating an artificial ceiling on model scaling, preventing any single lab from pulling significantly ahead of competitors in the short term.
- AI companies are reaching $30 billion revenue run rates in record time, with the sector now contributing a measurable percentage of U.S. GDP.
- Gil advises founders of AI startups to evaluate their "value-maximizing moment" for an exit within the next 12 to 18 months, given the high historical failure rate of tech companies.

### Why it Matters
The current AI boom is characterized by unprecedented capital concentration and rapid market shifts that mirror previous technology cycles. Understanding these bottlenecks and the "oligopoly" structure of the AI lab market is essential for investors and founders navigating the next two years of development.
