7 Habits That Are More Important Than Using Antivirus on Your Phone
============================================================

Modern smartphones feature robust built-in security, making traditional antivirus software largely unnecessary for most users who should instead prioritize account protection against phishing and social engineering scams.

### Key Points
- Apple and Google integrate advanced protections like app sandboxing and regular security updates to prevent malware from compromising mobile operating systems.
- Mobile antivirus apps are often limited by operating system restrictions, preventing them from performing the deep system scans common on desktop computers.
- Cybercriminals increasingly favor social engineering tactics, such as phishing texts and fake login pages, over technical exploits to steal user credentials.
- Security experts recommend using password managers, enabling two-factor authentication, and downloading apps exclusively from official stores to maintain device safety.
- Sideloading apps on Android devices or downloading files from unverified websites increases risk, potentially making additional security software useful for those specific users.

### Why it Matters
Focusing on account security rather than antivirus software addresses the most common modern cyber threats, which target human behavior instead of device vulnerabilities. By adopting better digital hygiene, users can significantly reduce the risk of identity theft and unauthorized account access.
