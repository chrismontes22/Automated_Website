'Baffling' or 'based'? Tech world reacts to export controls on Anthropic's new AI models
============================================================

The Trump Administration has ordered Anthropic to block all foreign access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models, effectively restricting use by foreign nationals globally.

### Key Points
- The U.S. government mandate applies to both international users and foreign-national employees working within the United States.
- Critics argue the move stems from Anthropic’s own marketing, which previously characterized the models as dangerous "munitions" or "bombs."
- Industry experts warn that "deemed export" rules prevent Anthropic’s own foreign staff from accessing the technology they helped develop.
- Observers suggest the policy could stifle U.S. innovation, drive foreign talent toward international competitors, and trigger significant First Amendment legal challenges.
- The directive has sparked debate over whether the Department of Commerce’s export control strategy is incoherent or a necessary step in national security.

### Why it Matters
This unprecedented intervention signals a major shift toward aggressive federal oversight of frontier AI development, moving beyond voluntary safety commitments to strict, government-enforced export controls. The decision creates a restrictive precedent that could fundamentally alter how AI labs operate, recruit global talent, and deploy their most advanced models.
