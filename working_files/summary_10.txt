10 Friday AM Reads
============================================================

U.S. GDP per capita now exceeds the European Union average by approximately 38%, highlighting a growing economic divide driven by differing approaches to growth, insurance, and market efficiency.

### Key Points
- U.S. GDP per capita is currently 38% higher than the EU average when adjusted for purchasing power parity.
- San Francisco’s artificial intelligence sector attracted nearly $35 billion in venture capital funding last year, fueling a local economic resurgence.
- Baby boomers are projected to transfer $36 trillion in wealth to heirs over the next two decades, further concentrating intergenerational assets.
- Lyft CEO David Risher has led the company to profitability, reporting $1.7 billion in revenue and 28.3 million active riders in Q1 2026.
- A White House report has sparked controversy by alleging radical bias within the Smithsonian’s National Museum of American History.

### Why it Matters
The widening economic gap between the U.S. and Europe reflects fundamental differences in how these regions prioritize growth versus social safety nets. These trends, combined with shifting wealth concentration and the rapid expansion of the AI industry, signal significant changes for global markets and future economic policy.
