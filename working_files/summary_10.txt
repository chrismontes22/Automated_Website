'World's 1st mass-produced humanoid robot' motors to market in China
============================================================

Chinese robotics firm Ubtech has launched the Uworld U1 series, a line of mass-produced humanoid companion robots designed to provide emotional support and interaction for home users.

### Key Points
- The Uworld U1 series includes three models ranging from a semi-torso version to the full-body U1 Ultra.
- Robots feature 88 servo joints, silicone skin with realistic textures, and the ability to replicate approximately 90% of human movements.
- Pricing for the units ranges from roughly $17,655 to $145,717 depending on the specific model and capabilities.
- Despite claims of advanced AI, demonstrations showed significant latency in speech responses and unnatural movement patterns.
- Ubtech reported over 13,000 orders on launch day, primarily driven by government procurement and demonstration hall acquisitions rather than private households.

### Why it Matters
These robots represent a significant attempt to commercialize emotional companionship, yet they highlight the current technological gap between sci-fi expectations and functional reality. The widespread adoption of such technology could fundamentally alter human social dynamics and raise ethical concerns regarding the healthiness of replacing real-world relationships with artificial ones.
