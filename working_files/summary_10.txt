The Intersection of Encryption and AI
============================================================

Renowned security expert Bruce Schneier reflects on the limitations of cryptography in modern network defense while highlighting how artificial intelligence is accelerating the ongoing cybersecurity arms race.

### Key Points
- Bruce Schneier argues that while cryptography is mathematically robust, it remains insufficient for securing complex, buggy software systems and human-centric networks.
- The author notes that cryptography is a niche mathematical tool that cannot address vulnerabilities in operating systems, hardware, or user configurations.
- Cybersecurity is described as a fragile, fast-paced arms race between attackers and defenders that shifts balance frequently.
- Artificial intelligence is now capable of identifying software vulnerabilities and writing exploits at superhuman speeds.
- Schneier suggests that AI will likely soon be able to write security patches, further complicating the landscape of instant software.

### Why it Matters
This perspective challenges the industry to look beyond mathematical encryption as a total security solution in an era of automated threats. As AI accelerates the discovery of vulnerabilities, organizations must prepare for a more volatile environment where the speed of defense must match the speed of AI-driven attacks.
