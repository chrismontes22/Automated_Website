Meta’s glasses will turn off the camera if you tamper with the privacy light
============================================================

Meta is updating its smart glasses to automatically disable the camera if the device’s privacy LED light is tampered with, aiming to address growing concerns regarding unauthorized recording.

### Key Points
- The software update prevents camera functionality if users physically damage or obstruct the integrated privacy LED indicator.
- Meta previously implemented prompts to discourage users from covering the light with tape, though many modders successfully bypassed these measures.
- The move follows reports of individuals using the devices for harassment and public scrutiny regarding potential facial recognition features.
- New York State courtrooms and other public venues are implementing bans on camera-equipped eyewear due to ongoing privacy and security concerns.

### Why it Matters
This update reflects Meta's attempt to mitigate reputational risks and legal challenges as smart glasses face increasing scrutiny from public institutions and privacy advocates. By enforcing hardware-level safeguards, the company hopes to maintain market access in venues that are currently considering or enacting bans on wearable recording technology.
