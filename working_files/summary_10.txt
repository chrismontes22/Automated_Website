Your Google Pixel Is Getting Smarter At Detecting Scam Calls
============================================================

Google is rolling out a new fake call detection feature for Pixel devices to protect users from AI-driven impersonation scams and fraudulent voice cloning attempts starting this month.

### Key Points
- The feature uses a silent verification signal between devices to confirm if a caller is legitimately initiating a call.
- It will be available on Android devices running version 12 or newer that utilize the Phone by Google application.
- Users will receive an automatic popup warning to hang up if the system detects a potential impersonator.
- The tool is enabled by default but can be manually disabled by users within the phone's settings menu.
- This update follows the "verified financial calls" feature launched in May to combat scams posing as banking institutions.

### Why it Matters
This technology addresses the rising threat of AI-powered fraud, which caused $2.95 billion in losses from impersonation scams in 2024 alone. By integrating real-time verification, Google aims to mitigate the risks posed by sophisticated Deepfake-as-a-Service tools that can convincingly mimic a victim's acquaintances.
