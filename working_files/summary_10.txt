Job scams are getting more sophisticated, and they’re costing Americans millions
============================================================

Sophisticated AI-driven employment scams are surging, targeting job seekers with professional-looking fake recruitment offers that aim to steal personal information or extract money through fraudulent upfront fees.

### Key Points
- Employment scam losses grew from $90 million in 2020 to $501 million by 2024, according to Better Business Bureau data.
- Over 80% of phishing attempts now utilize AI, which helps scammers overcome language barriers and create highly convincing, personalized communications.
- Common tactics include requesting payments for fake background checks, sending malicious software links, and creating false urgency regarding job offers.
- Security experts advise verifying recruiters through official company websites and avoiding any requests for upfront payments or software downloads.
- Nearly one-third of Gen Z job seekers report having been targeted by employment scams, making them a primary demographic for these attacks.

### Why it Matters
The rise of AI-enabled fraud has transformed job hunting into a high-risk activity, as scammers now produce professional, error-free communications that are increasingly difficult to distinguish from legitimate offers. This trend forces both job seekers and organizations to adopt more rigorous verification processes to protect personal data and financial security in an evolving digital landscape.
