Fareed Zakaria told Bard grads we're asking the wrong question about AI
============================================================

CNN host Fareed Zakaria argued during his Bard College commencement address that human intelligence remains distinct and irreplaceable despite the rapid technological advancements currently being driven by artificial intelligence.

### Key Points
- Fareed Zakaria delivered a commencement speech at Bard College in New York addressing the rapid evolution of artificial intelligence.
- Zakaria contrasted the high energy demands of AI data centers with the human brain, which operates on approximately 20 watts of power.
- The speaker emphasized that human intelligence excels at understanding context, forming relationships, and experiencing emotions that machines cannot replicate.
- The address occurred amid a trend of student protests against AI-related topics during graduation ceremonies at universities like Arizona and Central Florida.

### Why it Matters
This perspective highlights a growing cultural tension regarding the role of automation in professional and academic environments. By shifting the focus toward human-centric skills, the speech suggests that future workforce value may increasingly rely on emotional intelligence and lived experience rather than technical output alone.
