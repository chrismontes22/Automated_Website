What Is The 30-30-30 Hard Reset Rule For Routers?
============================================================

The 30-30-30 hard reset rule is an outdated troubleshooting method for older routers that can potentially damage modern Wi-Fi 5, Wi-Fi 6, and Wi-Fi 7 networking hardware.

### Key Points
- The 30-30-30 process involves holding the reset button for 30 seconds while powered on, 30 seconds while unplugged, and 30 seconds after plugging it back in.
- Performing this reset on modern routers risks corrupting firmware or bricking the device, as seen with models like the Netgear Nighthawk R7000.
- A hard reset restores a router to its original factory settings, which differs from a standard reboot that preserves user configurations.
- Most modern manufacturers recommend shorter reset durations, such as seven seconds for Netgear devices, to safely restore factory defaults.
- Users should consult their specific device manual rather than relying on legacy troubleshooting techniques for current networking equipment.

### Why it Matters
Using obsolete reset procedures on modern hardware can lead to permanent device failure and unnecessary technical support costs. Understanding the correct reset protocol ensures users can troubleshoot connectivity issues safely without compromising the integrity of their home network firmware.
