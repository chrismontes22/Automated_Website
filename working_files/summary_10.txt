Here's What 'Military-Grade' Encryption Really Means
============================================================

The term "military-grade encryption" is primarily a marketing buzzword used by tech companies to describe the AES-256 standard, which is widely available and not exclusive to the military.

### Key Points
- "Military-grade encryption" refers to the Advanced Encryption Standard with a 256-bit key, known as AES-256.
- The U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology introduced AES-256 in 2001 to protect top-secret government data.
- Products marketed with this term are not officially tested, certified, or endorsed by the U.S. military or Department of Defense.
- AES-256 remains highly secure against current decryption methods and is expected to withstand future threats from quantum computing.
- Encryption strength depends on the entire system architecture, not just the use of the AES-256 algorithm.

### Why it Matters
Consumers should understand that this marketing language does not guarantee a product has undergone rigorous military-level security vetting. Recognizing that encryption is only one component of a system helps users make more informed decisions about their personal data privacy and digital security.
