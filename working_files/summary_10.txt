Apple: ‘Due to DMA, Siri AI Delayed in EU for iOS 27 and iPadOS 27’
============================================================

Apple has delayed the launch of its new Siri AI features for iOS 27 and iPadOS 27 in the European Union due to ongoing regulatory conflicts regarding the DMA.

### Key Points
- Apple will withhold Siri AI from iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and watchOS 27 in all 27 EU member states.
- EU users will still be able to access Siri AI features on macOS 27 and visionOS 27.
- Apple cites the Digital Markets Act's requirements for third-party AI access to private user data as the primary security concern.
- The European Commission rejected Apple’s proposed "Trusted System Agent" solution, which aimed to provide secure, gradual access for third-party virtual assistants.
- No timeline currently exists for the release of these AI capabilities on iPhone or iPad within the European Union.

### Why it Matters
This standoff highlights the growing friction between major technology companies and EU regulators over the balance between platform interoperability and user data security. The delay limits the functionality of new Apple devices for European consumers and creates uncertainty for developers looking to integrate advanced AI features into their applications.
