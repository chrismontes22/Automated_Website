Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade secret theft
============================================================

Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI in the U.S. District Court, alleging that the company orchestrated a systematic theft of trade secrets and confidential hardware development data.

### Key Points
- Apple accuses OpenAI leadership of directing former employees to steal proprietary technical specifications, engineering presentations, and unannounced product data.
- The complaint names OpenAI Chief Hardware Officer Tang Tan, a former Apple VP, for allegedly using confidential project code names during recruitment.
- Former Apple engineer Chang Liu is accused of retaining an Apple-issued laptop to download and share sensitive technical documents after joining OpenAI.
- Apple claims OpenAI requested that job candidates bring Apple hardware prototypes and component designs to interviews to gain competitive intelligence.
- The lawsuit follows OpenAI’s $6.5 billion acquisition of Jony Ive’s startup, io, as the company reportedly develops a hardware device to compete with the iPhone.

### Why it Matters
This legal battle highlights the intensifying competition between tech giants and AI firms as they vie for dominance in the emerging personal hardware market. If proven, these allegations could significantly disrupt OpenAI’s product roadmap and force stricter industry standards regarding the handling of intellectual property during executive recruitment.
