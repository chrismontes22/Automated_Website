Transcript: Jean Eric Salata, Chair of EQT group
============================================================

**EQT Group Chair Jean Eric Salata discusses the evolution of private equity, the firm's global expansion, and the strategic importance of cross-border investment in a shifting macroeconomic landscape.**

### Key Points
- Jean Eric Salata serves as chair of EQT Group, a global alternative asset manager overseeing more than $316 billion in assets.
- The firm recently completed a $7 billion merger with Baring Private Equity Asia (BPEA), combining regional expertise with EQT’s global platform.
- EQT is currently managing a $5 billion "Scale Up Europe" fund focused on advancing AI, quantum computing, and life sciences technology.
- Salata highlights a "CapEx supercycle" driven by AI infrastructure, energy transition, and reindustrialization as a primary global investment theme.
- The firm achieved a record $40 billion in distributions last year, largely through public market exits and strategic sales of assets like Galderma.

### Why it Matters
The integration of BPEA into EQT reflects a broader industry trend toward consolidation and scale, allowing firms to navigate increasingly complex geopolitical and regulatory environments. By diversifying across regions and asset classes, EQT provides institutional and private investors with a hedge against the concentration risks often found in US-centric portfolios.
