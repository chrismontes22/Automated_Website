I freed up 143 GB by letting Claude scan my C: drive — Windows never found any of it
============================================================

Anthropic’s AI coding agent, Claude Code, successfully recovered 143 GB of storage space on a Windows laptop by identifying and safely removing hidden junk files that native tools ignored.

### Key Points
- Claude Code operates directly within the terminal, allowing it to execute commands and analyze file structures on a local machine.
- The AI identified 72.6 GB of unused VirtualBox images and approximately 30 GB of leftover AI model data from uninstalled applications.
- It cleared 19 GB of Nvidia driver caches and DirectX shader files that Windows Storage Sense failed to detect or categorize as removable.
- The tool provided a structured breakdown of files, explicitly labeling which items were safe to delete to prevent system instability.
- Total free space on the 470 GB partition increased from 13.63 GB to 157.06 GB after the cleanup process.

### Why it Matters
This demonstration highlights the shift from rigid, rule-based system utilities to intelligent agents capable of nuanced, context-aware file management. By reasoning through complex directory structures, AI agents can perform deep maintenance tasks that traditional software avoids to prevent potential system errors.
