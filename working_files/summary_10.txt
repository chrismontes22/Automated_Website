AI-Powered War Is Coming. This Fight Over a Data Center Just Made That Case
============================================================

The US government is intervening in an NAACP lawsuit against xAI, arguing that the company's Memphis data center is essential for national security and military AI operations.

### Key Points
- The NAACP filed a class-action lawsuit alleging xAI’s Colossus 2 data center operates without required Clean Air Act permits.
- The Department of Defense uses a specialized version of xAI’s Grok model, integrated into the Maven Smart System for military intelligence and targeting.
- Defense officials claim Grok was instrumental in Operation Epic Fury, enabling the deployment of 2,000 munitions against 2,000 targets within 96 hours.
- The US government filed a motion to dismiss the lawsuit, marking a rare instance of federal intervention in a citizen-led environmental case.
- The Pentagon has significantly increased AI integration, with a $13.4 billion budget allocation for autonomous systems in 2026.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights a growing tension between environmental regulations and the rapid integration of AI into national defense infrastructure. It forces a legal debate over whether private data centers should be classified as critical infrastructure, potentially granting them immunity from local environmental oversight in the name of military readiness.
