Bitcoin's dearth of fresh investors matters more than Strategy's sale, Citi says
============================================================

Citigroup analysts report that MicroStrategy’s recent bitcoin sale is less significant to market performance than the ongoing decline in investor demand and negative flows from spot bitcoin ETFs.

### Key Points
- MicroStrategy’s recent bitcoin sale was a pre-planned tax-optimization move rather than a shift in the firm's long-term holding strategy.
- Spot bitcoin ETFs have recorded 11 consecutive days of net outflows, which Citi identifies as a primary driver of recent price volatility.
- Citi estimates that spot bitcoin ETF flows account for approximately 45% of weekly price fluctuations for the cryptocurrency.
- The probability of passing a U.S. crypto market structure bill this year is declining, removing a potential catalyst for new investor interest.
- Bitcoin has declined 9% since Sunday, reaching its lowest price point since March amid broader market sentiment concerns.

### Why it Matters
The lack of new investor participation and negative ETF flows suggest that bitcoin may face continued price pressure without significant regulatory progress or a shift in market sentiment. This trend highlights the growing reliance of the cryptocurrency market on institutional ETF adoption as a primary indicator of health and price stability.
