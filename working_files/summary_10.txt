Kickbacks Takes An Outsider’s View While Bringing Ads To AI Agents
============================================================

Varda Space Industries engineer Andrew McCalip has launched Kickbacks, a platform that allows LLM users to earn revenue by displaying advertisements during the processing time of AI agent prompts.

### Key Points
- Kickbacks currently integrates with Anthropic’s Claude and OpenAI’s Codex, with plans to add support for Cursor and other AI agents.
- Users receive a 50% revenue share from ads, with average monthly payouts of $25 and top users earning up to $75.
- The platform has reached 25,000 total downloads, with daily active users ranging between 5,000 and 10,000.
- McCalip is developing an opt-in feature for data collection and prompt-based targeting, which aims to increase user payouts by up to 5x.
- The system uses behavioral analysis to detect and mitigate bot fraud by identifying non-human engagement patterns.

### Why it Matters
This model introduces a novel monetization strategy for AI users by turning idle processing time into a revenue-generating asset. However, the platform faces potential regulatory and legal challenges if major AI labs decide that third-party ad injection violates their terms of service.
