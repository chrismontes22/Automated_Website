"They have the biggest bullsh*t detectors on the planet": How the unlikely EVE Online x Google DeepMind AI partnership landed with players
============================================================

Fenris Creations has partnered with Google DeepMind to integrate AI research into EVE Online, aiming to modernize the space MMO’s aging codebase and enhance non-player character interactions.

### Key Points
- Fenris Creations, formerly CCP Games, granted Google DeepMind a minority stake to train AI agents on an offline version of EVE Online.
- Developers are utilizing AI "coaching agents" to identify memory and security issues within the game's 30-year-old "spaghetti code."
- The partnership aims to revitalize mission-giving NPCs by using generative AI to make character interactions feel more dynamic and alive.
- Despite widespread industry backlash against AI in gaming, EVE Online’s playerbase has remained largely receptive or cautiously curious toward the initiative.
- Google DeepMind previously gained recognition for developing Grandmaster-ranked AI agents for StarCraft 2 and mastering complex board games like Go.

### Why it Matters
This collaboration represents a rare instance where a dedicated gaming community has embraced AI integration, largely due to the potential for fixing long-standing technical debt. If successful, the project could provide a blueprint for using machine learning to maintain and evolve massive, legacy software environments without alienating core users.
