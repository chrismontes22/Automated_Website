How Elon Musk Became More Powerful Than Ever
============================================================

Historians Quinn Slobodian and Ben Tarnoff analyze the rise of Elon Musk and the ideology of "Muskism" in their new book, examining how the billionaire reshapes global politics and industry.

### Key Points
- The authors define "Muskism" as a strategy where Musk sells the promise of self-reliance through his infrastructure, ultimately deepening dependency on his companies.
- Musk’s business model relies heavily on government interaction, utilizing public funding and policy shifts to backstop his ventures like SpaceX and Tesla.
- The book explores how Musk’s childhood in apartheid-era South Africa and his early years in Silicon Valley influenced his "fortress futurism" and man-machine fusion ideology.
- SpaceX recently achieved an astronomical valuation, though the authors argue that Musk’s political alignment with Donald Trump creates significant regulatory and reputational risks.
- Musk’s acquisition of X has transformed the platform into a political megaphone and a testing ground for his AI model, Grok.

### Why it Matters
Understanding Musk’s ideology is essential for grasping how a single industrialist can influence global infrastructure, government policy, and public discourse. By analyzing his methods, critics hope to develop the political and economic strategies necessary to challenge his unprecedented concentration of power.
