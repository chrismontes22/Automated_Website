How the gaming and gambling industry can strengthen their cyber defenses
============================================================

The global online gambling industry faces a surge in cyberattacks and data breaches, prompting operators to adopt rigorous security frameworks to protect sensitive customer information and maintain compliance.

### Key Points
- The global online gambling market is projected to reach $154 billion by 2030, growing at an annual rate of nearly 12%.
- iGaming fraud incidents surged by an average of 64% year-over-year between 2022 and 2024.
- Major industry players, including Flutter Entertainment and Wynn Resorts, have recently suffered significant data breaches affecting hundreds of thousands of users.
- Supply chain vulnerabilities are a growing concern, with the global cost of software supply chain attacks expected to hit $138 billion by 2031.
- Industry experts recommend adopting ISO 27001 and ISO 27701 standards to manage data risks and meet regulatory requirements like those from the Gambling Commission.

### Why it Matters
Cybersecurity threats pose an existential risk to gambling operators because downtime and data loss directly erode customer trust and revenue in a highly competitive, 24/7 market. By implementing standardized security frameworks, firms can better navigate complex international regulations while defending against increasingly sophisticated attacks on their interconnected digital supply chains.
