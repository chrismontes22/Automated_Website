Google’s Updated Guidance Urges FTC Complaints Against Shady SEOs via @sejournal, @martinibuster
============================================================

Google has updated its "Do you need an SEO?" guidance to include AI optimization services while warning businesses against third-party tools and encouraging reports of deceptive practices to the FTC.

### Key Points
- Google officially added Generative AI Optimization (AEO/GEO) to its list of recognized SEO services.
- The updated guidance explicitly warns businesses that Google does not endorse or provide internal ranking data to third-party SEO tools.
- Businesses are now encouraged to report unethical or fraudulent SEO service providers directly to the U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC).
- The documentation clarifies that no SEO provider can guarantee a top-ranking position or claim a "special relationship" with Google.
- Content was rewritten to be more concise, emphasizing that site owners should verify all third-party audit recommendations against official Google documentation.

### Why it Matters
These updates signal a shift in how Google manages its relationship with the SEO industry by moving beyond internal policy enforcement into the realm of legal accountability. By directing businesses to the FTC, Google is raising the stakes for providers who use deceptive marketing or spam-heavy tactics to manipulate search results.
