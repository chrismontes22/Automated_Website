Weekly poll results: iOS 27 is okay, just okay
============================================================

Apple’s iOS 27 update has received a lukewarm reception from iPhone users, as the release focuses on refining the Liquid Glass design rather than introducing major new consumer features.

### Key Points
- User sentiment for iOS 27 leans positive, with "love it" votes outnumbering "hate it" votes by a 2-to-1 margin.
- The update prioritizes stability and design refinements over new functionality, functioning primarily as a maintenance release.
- Apple Intelligence features, including the upgraded Siri AI, are delayed and expected to launch later this year alongside iPhone 18 Pro models.
- Access to the new Siri AI remains restricted to a waiting list, even for users currently running the iOS 27 beta.
- New utility features include independent volume controls for alarms and ringtones, plus improved Control Center functionality for external displays.

### Why it Matters
The delayed rollout of Apple Intelligence highlights the company's ongoing struggle to deliver on high-profile AI promises made during previous hardware launches. This cautious approach to software updates suggests a shift toward prioritizing system stability and long-term hardware integration over rapid feature deployment.
