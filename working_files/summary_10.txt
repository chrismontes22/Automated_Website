How to Think About AI Before It’s Too Late
============================================================

Author and activist Cory Doctorow argues that the current AI boom is driven by corporate power dynamics and unsustainable growth, rather than genuine technological necessity or human flourishing.

### Key Points
- Doctorow defines "reverse centaurs" as workers forced to act as extensions of machines, often leading to exhaustion and dehumanization.
- The "enshittification" thesis suggests platforms degrade services to extract value once they have captured a market.
- AI development is characterized as a massive, potentially unsustainable capital expenditure bubble exceeding $1 trillion.
- Corporate reliance on AI often creates "accountability sinks," where companies blame automated systems for foreseeable errors to avoid responsibility.
- Doctorow advocates for structural regulation and antitrust enforcement to curb the influence of oligarchy on technological development.

### Why it Matters
The unchecked pursuit of AI-driven automation risks creating unstable, inefficient systems that prioritize corporate cost-cutting over human needs and public safety. Addressing these power imbalances is essential to preventing long-term economic volatility and the further erosion of labor rights.
