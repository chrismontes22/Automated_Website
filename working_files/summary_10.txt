Worm rubs out competitor's malware, then takes control
============================================================

Akamai shares surged following a significant large language model deal, while competitor Cloudflare experienced a decline as the broader technology sector navigates shifting market demands and rising AI costs.

### Key Points
- Akamai reported a stock surge driven by a new partnership involving large language model infrastructure.
- Cloudflare shares faced downward pressure as investors reacted to changing competitive dynamics in the cloud and AI space.
- Frontier-model pricing continues to climb, with future iterations like GPT-5.5 expected to increase operational costs despite improved token efficiency.
- Hardware supply chain turbulence and rising AI demand are forcing IT departments to adopt new infrastructure playbooks.
- The UK Home Office is seeking new suppliers for a £300 million biometrics platform used for police and immigration systems.

### Why it Matters
These developments highlight the growing financial strain and strategic volatility inherent in the current AI-driven infrastructure market. As companies balance the high costs of frontier models against shifting cloud service valuations, businesses must carefully evaluate their long-term technology investments and vendor dependencies.
