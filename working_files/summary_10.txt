Notepad++ isn't the best app for huge files anymore — I tested the alternatives
============================================================

**Geany outperforms popular text editors like Notepad++ and Sublime Text when handling large files, offering the best balance of fast startup times, smooth scrolling, and efficient search performance.**

### Key Points
- Geany proved the most responsive for a 500MB log file, featuring a 2.10-second open time and the fastest search speed at 8.20 seconds.
- Notepad++ offers fast startup speeds but suffers from significant stuttering and lag when navigating or searching through large documents.
- Sublime Text provides a polished, smooth experience once loaded, but requires a lengthy 11.14-second wait to open large files.
- Notepad3 and Kate are suitable for small text and configuration edits but struggle significantly with performance when processing files in the hundreds of megabytes.
- Geany requires higher RAM usage, consuming approximately 1,101MB, which is double the memory footprint of Notepad++ during large file operations.

### Why it Matters
Choosing the right text editor is critical for developers and system administrators who frequently analyze massive server logs or large datasets. While many editors excel at lightweight daily tasks, performance bottlenecks in popular tools can significantly hinder productivity during intensive debugging sessions.
