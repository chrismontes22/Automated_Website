TrueConf Zero-Day Exploited in Attacks on Southeast Asian Government Networks
============================================================

A high-severity zero-day vulnerability in TrueConf video conferencing software is being exploited by a Chinese-nexus threat actor to deploy malware across government networks in Southeast Asia.

### Key Points
- The vulnerability, tracked as CVE-2026-3502 with a CVSS score of 7.8, allows attackers to execute arbitrary code by bypassing update integrity checks.
- Attackers gain control of on-premises TrueConf servers to distribute malicious updates to all connected client endpoints.
- The "TrueChaos" campaign utilizes the Havoc command-and-control framework and DLL side-loading to maintain persistence and conduct reconnaissance.
- Check Point researchers identified the campaign, which has targeted government and law enforcement agencies since early 2026.
- TrueConf has released a security patch for the Windows client in version 8.5.3 to address the update validation flaw.

### Why it Matters
This exploit highlights the significant risks posed by supply chain attacks that weaponize trusted software update mechanisms to bypass individual endpoint security. By compromising a central server, attackers can efficiently infiltrate entire government networks, underscoring the critical need for rigorous code validation and prompt patching in enterprise communication tools.
