SpaceX scrubs launch of Starship V3—the tallest and most powerful rocket ever built
============================================================

SpaceX has delayed the inaugural test flight of its upgraded Starship V3 megarocket, the most powerful launch vehicle ever built, with a second attempt potentially scheduled for Friday.

### Key Points
- The Starship V3 vehicle stands 408 feet tall and generates over 18 million pounds of thrust using new Raptor 3 engines.
- SpaceX has invested approximately $15 billion into the Starship program, including $3 billion spent during 2025 alone.
- The upcoming test flight aims to demonstrate successful stage separation and a controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean.
- The mission will deploy 20 dummy Starlink satellites and two operational units designed to monitor the spacecraft's heat shield performance.
- The Super Heavy booster is slated to land in the Gulf of Mexico, while the spacecraft will perform reentry maneuvers before splashdown.

### Why it Matters
A successful demonstration of the V3 design is critical for SpaceX as the company prepares for a highly anticipated initial public offering. Furthermore, the rocket's performance is essential for NASA’s Artemis program, which relies on Starship to return astronauts to the moon by 2028.
