Why Everyone Is Suddenly Talking About ‘Universal Basic Capital’
============================================================

Universal basic capital is gaining bipartisan support as a potential solution to AI-driven wealth inequality, though experts warn that government-managed sovereign wealth funds could trigger significant regulatory capture.

### Key Points
- Universal basic capital proposes giving citizens ownership stakes in AI companies to ensure the public shares in the economic gains of automation.
- Proponents include figures across the political spectrum, such as Bernie Sanders, Donald Trump, Sam Altman, and Vivek Ramaswamy.
- The "American A.I. Sovereign Wealth Fund Act" proposed by Sanders would grant the federal government equity and board representation in firms like OpenAI and Anthropic.
- Critics argue that government ownership could lead to regulatory capture, where the state prioritizes the financial success of AI firms over safety or labor standards.
- Alternative models, such as individual brokerage accounts for citizens, aim to provide wealth distribution without granting the government direct control over corporate operations.

### Why it Matters
This policy debate represents a rare moment of bipartisan consensus on addressing the potential economic displacement caused by artificial intelligence. However, the shift toward government-controlled sovereign wealth funds risks centralizing power and creating conflicts of interest that could undermine the very democratic oversight intended to protect the public.
