I’ve Sent Students to Every Ivy League School. Here’s What the Debate Over Standardized Testing Is Missing.
============================================================

Brown University and other elite institutions are grappling with a crisis of academic integrity as widespread artificial intelligence usage and the decline of standardized testing undermine traditional learning.

### Key Points
- A Brown University economics professor discovered students likely used AI to achieve a 96 average on a midterm, while scores plummeted to 48.6 on a proctored final.
- The University of Chicago Law School and UC Berkeley have implemented strict bans on electronic devices and AI tools to combat academic dishonesty.
- Educators report that the removal of standardized testing in admissions has made it difficult to assess student readiness, leading to increased needs for remedial instruction.
- Pandemic-era policy shifts, such as flexible deadlines and open-book assessments, have created long-term expectations that conflict with traditional academic rigor.
- Teachers are increasingly using "Trojan horse" methods, such as hidden text in assignments, to detect AI-generated content in student submissions.

### Why it Matters
The convergence of AI integration and the abandonment of standardized testing threatens the credibility of American higher education degrees. Without objective benchmarks for student ability, universities risk devaluing their credentials and failing to prepare students for the cognitive demands of professional life.
