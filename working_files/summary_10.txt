'It almost defied belief': here are the 5 best OnePlus phones ever, as the 'flagship killer' leaves the US and Europe
============================================================

Parent company Oppo has officially announced it will cease sales of all new OnePlus smartphones, tablets, and smartwatches across United States and European markets due to economic pressures.

### Key Points
- OnePlus will continue providing software updates and after-sales support for existing customers in the affected regions.
- The decision follows long-standing difficulties in securing essential carrier partnerships within the competitive U.S. smartphone market.
- OnePlus faced intense sales pressure from major industry rivals, including Apple, Samsung, Google, and various Chinese manufacturers.
- The company, founded in 2013, gained early fame with the "flagship killer" OnePlus One and recently produced high-rated devices like the OnePlus 15.
- Strategic shifts were driven by the inability to maintain sufficient brand visibility and retail presence compared to dominant market leaders.

### Why it Matters
This exit marks the end of a significant era for a brand that once disrupted the mobile industry with high-performance, value-driven hardware. The move highlights the extreme difficulty smaller manufacturers face when attempting to compete against established tech giants without strong support from major telecommunications carriers.
