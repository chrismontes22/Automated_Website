Trump and Xi to meet in Beijing: The key issues shaping the China summit
============================================================

United States President Donald Trump has arrived in Beijing for a high-stakes summit with President Xi Jinping to address trade, Taiwan, artificial intelligence, and the ongoing Iran war.

### Key Points
- The summit marks the first visit by a U.S. leader to China since 2017, focusing on economic policy and geopolitical stability.
- Trade discussions will prioritize Chinese purchases of American goods, including Boeing aircraft, beef, and soya beans, alongside tariff negotiations.
- Beijing is seeking the removal of U.S. restrictions on advanced semiconductor exports and chip-making technology.
- The leaders will address the conflict in Iran and the security of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical route for global energy supplies.
- Taiwan remains a primary point of contention, with Beijing demanding limits on U.S. arms sales to the self-ruled island.
- Both nations are navigating a complex relationship defined by mutual economic dependence and strategic rivalry in technology and rare earth minerals.

### Why it Matters
This summit highlights the delicate balance between the world's two largest economies as they attempt to manage deep-seated structural rivalries while maintaining essential trade ties. The outcome of these talks will likely influence global market stability, supply chain security, and the future of international diplomatic relations.
