Florida GOP lawmaker and governor candidate says AI data center legislation keeps utility costs down
============================================================

Florida Republican Congressman Byron Donalds has introduced federal legislation requiring artificial intelligence data centers to source all electricity and water privately to prevent rising utility costs for residential ratepayers.

### Key Points
- The proposed legislation mandates that data centers secure independent power and water supplies rather than relying on public utility grids.
- The bill aims to mitigate public backlash against the rapid nationwide expansion of energy-intensive AI infrastructure.
- Florida has already seen over 20 counties and municipalities implement bans or rejections of new data center construction projects.
- Governor Ron DeSantis previously signed state-level legislation in May requiring large-scale data centers to cover their own energy and water expenses.
- Tech companies are facing increasing political pressure from both conservative populists and climate activists to address concerns regarding resource consumption.

### Why it Matters
This proposal highlights the growing tension between the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure and the protection of public utility resources for residential consumers. If enacted, such mandates could significantly alter the operational costs and development strategies for major technology firms currently scaling their data center capacity.
