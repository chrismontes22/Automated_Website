Seasonal trends favor bulls even as bitcoin ends April in a defensive mood
============================================================

Bitcoin faces a complex outlook as historical May seasonality and strong ETF inflows compete against rising U.S. bond yields and geopolitical instability impacting global risk asset markets.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin is consolidating below recent highs, with a potential bullish crossover of the 50-day and 100-day moving averages signaling strengthening momentum.
- U.S.-listed spot bitcoin ETFs recorded over $1.8 billion in net inflows during April, reflecting sustained institutional demand for the cryptocurrency.
- The U.S. 30-year Treasury yield reached 5%, creating significant macro headwinds that threaten to cap near-term upside for risk-sensitive assets.
- Geopolitical tensions involving Iran and rising energy prices have pushed Brent crude to $126 per barrel, fueling concerns about broader economic stability.
- Historical data since 2013 shows bitcoin has posted gains in seven of the past 13 months of May, averaging an 8% return.

### Why it Matters
The tug-of-war between bullish seasonal trends and macroeconomic pressures highlights the current fragility of the cryptocurrency market. Investors must weigh institutional adoption against the risk that high interest rates and energy-driven inflation could trigger a broader economic downturn.
