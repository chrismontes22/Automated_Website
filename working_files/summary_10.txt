4 security features I always set up on every new Android phone, and you should too
============================================================

Android users can significantly enhance device security by manually configuring essential features like Find Hub, Theft Protection, Advanced Protection, and SIM locks to prevent unauthorized access and data loss.

### Key Points
- Find Hub uses a crowdsourced network of Android devices to locate lost phones even when they are offline.
- Theft Detection Lock features, including Identity Check and Offline Device Lock, use AI and sensors to secure devices during potential theft.
- Advanced Protection, introduced in Android 16, blocks insecure 2G networks and restricts sideloading to prevent malicious software installation.
- Enabling a SIM card PIN prevents thieves from moving your SIM to another device to intercept calls or two-factor authentication codes.
- Users should enable two-factor authentication on their Google accounts to prevent unauthorized remote tracking or device erasure.

### Why it Matters
Properly configuring these built-in security settings provides a critical layer of defense against physical theft and remote account hijacking. By proactively managing these features, users can better protect their personal data and maintain control over their devices even if they are lost or stolen.
