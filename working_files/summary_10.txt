Xi Vows to Make AI for All in Debut at China's Top Tech Summit
============================================================

President Xi Jinping advocated for international AI cooperation at China's World AI Conference while Beijing simultaneously evaluates potential security restrictions on domestic model access for foreign users and companies.

### Key Points
- President Xi Jinping emphasized global collaboration over technological rivalry during his first appearance at China's World AI Conference.
- Chinese AI models currently account for nearly 60% of usage among U.S. firms on the OpenRouter marketplace.
- Beijing officials are consulting with companies like Alibaba to address security risks associated with the rapid advancement of powerful AI models.
- Government discussions have included the possibility of restricting foreign access to top-tier Chinese AI technology.
- The Beijing-based startup Moonshot released a new model that outperformed competitors like Anthropic’s Fable 5 and OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 in coding benchmarks.

### Why it Matters
China’s push to balance global market dominance with strict national security oversight highlights the growing geopolitical tension surrounding artificial intelligence development. These regulatory deliberations could significantly impact international access to high-performance Chinese models and reshape the competitive landscape for global tech firms.
