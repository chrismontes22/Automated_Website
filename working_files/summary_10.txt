Edelman Financial Engines To Appeal Decision in Mariner Lawsuit
============================================================

Edelman Financial Engines has filed an appeal with the Tenth Circuit Court of Appeals to challenge a federal judge's decision to dismiss its trade-secrets lawsuit against Mariner Wealth Advisors.

### Key Points
- Judge Holly Teeter dismissed the case in June, ruling that Edelman failed to prove that client lists taken by departing advisors constituted protected trade secrets.
- Edelman originally sued Mariner in 2023, alleging the firm encouraged advisors to breach non-solicitation agreements and misappropriate confidential client information.
- The lawsuit specifically highlighted the recruitment of former Edelman advisor Michael Horne, involving direct communications from Mariner CEO Marty Bicknell.
- Judge Teeter criticized Edelman’s legal team for improper briefing practices, including the misrepresentation of evidence and a consistent failure to provide necessary citations.

### Why it Matters
This appeal highlights the ongoing legal tensions regarding advisor mobility and the protection of client data within the competitive wealth management industry. The outcome could set a significant precedent for how firms enforce non-solicitation agreements and define trade secrets when recruiting talent from rival companies.
