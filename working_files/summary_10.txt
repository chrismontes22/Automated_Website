Congress Must Stop Enabling Trump’s Crypto Corruption
============================================================

The cryptocurrency industry is facing intense scrutiny as critics argue its massive political spending and influence over federal legislation are fueling corruption and undermining democratic processes in Washington.

### Key Points
- The cryptocurrency industry has spent nearly $200 million to influence the 2026 election cycle through super PACs and lobbying efforts.
- President Donald Trump reported earning over $1.4 billion from cryptocurrency ventures last year, raising significant concerns regarding potential conflicts of interest.
- Congress recently passed the industry-friendly GENIUS Act, which lacked ethics provisions despite warnings regarding potential self-enrichment by the president.
- Lawmakers are currently considering the CLARITY Act, a proposal that would shift oversight to a weaker regulatory body and potentially reduce investor protections.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of crypto-funded political influence highlights a growing tension between concentrated corporate wealth and the integrity of federal regulatory processes. This debate forces a broader examination of whether special interest spending is effectively allowing industries to write the laws that govern their own operations.
