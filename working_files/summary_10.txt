Ryuk Ransomware Member Pleads Guilty Over Attacks on U.S. Organizations
============================================================

Armenian national Karen Serobovich Vardanyan pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court to conspiracy and computer fraud charges related to his role in the global Ryuk ransomware extortion campaign.

### Key Points
- Karen Serobovich Vardanyan admitted to providing initial network access for Ryuk ransomware attacks between November 2019 and April 2020.
- The criminal operation targeted various U.S. entities, including a Michigan-based company, an Oregon technology firm, and a Texas school.
- Victims were coerced into paying ransoms in Bitcoin, with the group collecting approximately 1,610 Bitcoin valued at over $15 million.
- Vardanyan faces a maximum sentence of 15 years in prison, with a formal sentencing hearing scheduled for September 22, 2026.
- As part of his plea agreement, Vardanyan is required to pay more than $1.1 million in restitution to affected victims.

### Why it Matters
This conviction highlights the ongoing international efforts to dismantle sophisticated ransomware syndicates that utilize cryptocurrency to launder millions in illicit proceeds. The case underscores the significant financial and operational risks that ransomware groups pose to critical infrastructure and private businesses across the United States.
