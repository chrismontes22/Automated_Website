I’ve waited years for Apple to fix iPhone-to-Android texting, and it’s finally happening
============================================================

Apple and Google have introduced end-to-end encrypted RCS messaging for cross-platform communication between iPhone and Android devices through the latest iOS 26.5 beta software update.

### Key Points
- The new encryption standard is integrated directly into the GSMA’s RCS Universal Profile rather than relying on proprietary solutions.
- Users can verify secure connections via a lock icon displayed within active chat threads.
- Major North American carriers including AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, Rogers, Bell, and Telus currently support the feature.
- The update enables secure messaging automatically for users running the latest software versions on both platforms.
- Apple and Google intend to make end-to-end encryption the default standard for all RCS chats following the beta testing period.

### Why it Matters
This update eliminates a long-standing security gap that previously left cross-platform messages vulnerable to interception when communicating between iOS and Android. By adopting a universal standard, the companies are ensuring that private data remains protected regardless of the hardware or operating system used by participants.
