India froze Starlink approvals over Iran fears, days before SpaceX’s record IPO
============================================================

India has paused regulatory approvals for SpaceX’s Starlink satellite service due to security concerns regarding the company's ability to unilaterally control network access in unauthorized regions like Iran.

### Key Points
- Indian security agencies withheld final clearances for Starlink following reports of unauthorized terminal usage within Iran.
- SpaceX, led by Elon Musk, is currently preparing for a highly anticipated IPO valued at approximately $1.75 trillion.
- India previously required SpaceX to meet strict local data storage, network security, and potential joint venture mandates for market entry.
- Similar concerns regarding Starlink’s centralized control have previously impacted operations in Ukraine and stalled negotiations in Taiwan.
- Starlink’s growth strategy relies heavily on the Indian market, which contains 1.4 billion people and significant underserved rural populations.

### Why it Matters
The delay in India highlights a growing geopolitical tension between national sovereignty and the centralized control of global satellite networks. As SpaceX approaches its public market debut, these regulatory hurdles threaten to undermine the company's projected revenue growth and long-term expansion strategy.
