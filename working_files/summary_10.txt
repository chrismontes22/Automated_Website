I replaced 6 official Android apps with open-source alternatives — they're all much better
============================================================

Users can enhance their digital privacy on Android by replacing standard Google applications with open-source alternatives that prioritize data ownership and minimize tracking without sacrificing essential device functionality.

### Key Points
- **HeliBoard:** A privacy-focused keyboard that lacks internet permissions, preventing data collection while supporting gestures and multilingual typing.
- **Firefox:** A web browser that supports essential extensions like uBlock Origin and offers superior control over tracking and DNS settings compared to Chrome.
- **Ente:** An end-to-end encrypted photo storage service that provides a secure, cross-platform alternative to Google Photos for managing personal media.
- **Joplin:** A robust note-taking application that supports Markdown, end-to-end encryption, and flexible synchronization options via services like Nextcloud or Dropbox.
- **Fossify Calendar:** A lightweight, open-source scheduling tool that manages local events and reminders without integrating into a broader corporate productivity ecosystem.
- **Material Files:** A file management app featuring a clean interface, archive support, and network storage connectivity for users who prefer direct file system access.

### Why it Matters
Transitioning to open-source software allows users to reclaim control over their personal data by decoupling their daily digital habits from large-scale corporate ecosystems. This shift reduces the amount of behavioral tracking and metadata collection occurring on mobile devices while maintaining the practical utility required for modern productivity.
