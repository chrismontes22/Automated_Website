7 Ways to Boost the Privacy of Your Home and Gadgets
============================================================

**Homeowners can enhance their digital privacy by adjusting smart device settings, utilizing local storage for security footage, and removing sensitive property information from public real estate and mapping platforms.**

### Key Points
- Use physical shutters or power-off switches on smart cameras and microphones to prevent unauthorized remote access.
- Disable unnecessary data-sharing, voice history, and cloud storage features within device settings to minimize personal information collection.
- Opt for local storage solutions, such as microSD cards or hubs from brands like Eufy or Wyze, to keep video data off company servers.
- Remove outdated property photos and floor plans from real estate websites like Zillow and Trulia to prevent unauthorized home exposure.
- Request that Google Maps and Apple Maps blur your home address in their street-view imagery to increase physical and digital security.
- Consider privacy-focused, open-source platforms like Home Assistant or Homey to reduce reliance on data-collecting smart home ecosystems.

### Why it Matters
Treating smart home devices as connected computers rather than simple appliances is essential for maintaining personal security in an increasingly digitized living space. Proactively managing these settings helps users mitigate risks associated with data breaches, unauthorized surveillance, and the exposure of private home details.
