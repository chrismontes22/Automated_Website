2026.17: He Came, He Saw, He Cooked
============================================================

Apple CEO Tim Cook will step down this September after a historic tenure, while industry analysts evaluate the potential leadership transition to John Ternus and broader tech market shifts.

### Key Points
- Tim Cook is set to retire as Apple CEO this September, concluding a leadership era that surpassed the duration of Steve Jobs' tenure.
- John Ternus is widely considered the likely successor, signaling a continued strategic focus on Apple’s hardware-defined product roadmap.
- SpaceX is reportedly exploring a partnership or potential $60 billion acquisition of the AI coding platform Cursor to bolster its technological capabilities.
- SpaceX is currently preparing for an initial public offering with a targeted valuation of $1.75 trillion.
- New Chinese legislation targeting economic decoupling and U.S. efforts to restrict semiconductor equipment sales are intensifying global trade tensions.

### Why it Matters
The transition at Apple marks a significant turning point for the world's most valuable company as it navigates a maturing tech landscape. Simultaneously, the potential integration of AI tools like Cursor into SpaceX highlights how major firms are aggressively pursuing vertical integration to maintain competitive advantages in the evolving artificial intelligence market.
