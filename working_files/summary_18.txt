US lists China’s BYD, Alibaba, Baidu as ‘Chinese military companies’
============================================================

The Pentagon has added major Chinese corporations Alibaba, BYD, and Baidu to its military blacklist, citing alleged support for China’s military-civil fusion strategy and national security concerns.

### Key Points
- The Pentagon updated its list of "Chinese military companies" to include 188 firms, up from 134 in 2025.
- Alibaba, BYD, and Baidu were designated for alleged affiliations with state-owned commissions and the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology.
- Entities on the blacklist are barred from consideration for future United States defense contracts.
- Other new additions to the list include Shenzhen-based RoboSense Technology and Hangzhou-based Unitree Robotics.
- Alibaba and Baidu have publicly denied the allegations, labeling the designations as baseless and discriminatory.

### Why it Matters
This expansion of the blacklist signals a hardening stance on Chinese tech giants, potentially complicating recent diplomatic efforts to stabilize US-China relations. The move forces American companies to re-evaluate their supply chains and business partnerships with some of China's most prominent commercial brands.
