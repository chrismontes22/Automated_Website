EU AI: the fables we told ourselves - Pleias
============================================================

Europe’s reliance on foreign artificial intelligence models has left the bloc vulnerable after recent suspensions of access to advanced systems, exposing a lack of domestic infrastructure and expertise.

### Key Points
- The suspension of Anthropic’s Fable series models highlights Europe’s over-dependence on foreign AI rather than building its own foundational substrate.
- Europe lacks a dense market of AI labs, with only one or zero competitive labs compared to roughly ten each in the United States and China.
- European AI strategy has historically favored "application layers" and academic consortia, which fail to provide the continuous, long-term model training required for frontier innovation.
- Public initiatives like the Data Governance Act and various "European Data Spaces" have struggled to become operational, failing to create a viable commercial ecosystem.
- Expertise in large-scale distributed training remains scarce in Europe, as private R&D often fails to move beyond limited, short-term experiments.

### Why it Matters
Europe’s strategy of "prospering below the technology frontier" has proven fragile, as it leaves the continent’s industrial and economic sectors at the mercy of foreign providers who can withhold or reprice critical tools. This dependency risks long-term technological obsolescence as leading labs shift toward autonomous R&D and synthetic outputs that Europe is currently unequipped to replicate or control.
