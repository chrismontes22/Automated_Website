How Drone Boats Are Working As Patrols On The Southern Border
============================================================

The Pentagon’s Joint Task Force-Southern Border is deploying autonomous Lightfish vessels from Seasats to patrol the Rio Grande and deter drug smuggling through enhanced, continuous surveillance capabilities.

### Key Points
- The Pentagon is utilizing solar-powered Unmanned Surface Vessels (USVs) to monitor difficult-to-access areas along the Rio Grande.
- Manufacturer Seasats provides the Lightfish vessels, which are equipped with onboard AI, cameras, and depth sensors for autonomous navigation.
- These drone boats operate alongside U.S. Coast Guard and military personnel to fill security gaps and provide early warning detection.
- The vessels are designed to withstand challenging river conditions, including strong currents and underwater obstacles, for weeks or months without maintenance.
- The deployment aims to replace fixed security installations with mobile, unpredictable patrols that cover wider areas more cost-effectively.

### Why it Matters
This deployment marks a significant shift in border security by integrating autonomous, AI-driven technology into complex, high-risk environments. The success of these vessels could influence future security strategies, potentially expanding the use of unmanned systems for surveillance in both public and private sectors.
