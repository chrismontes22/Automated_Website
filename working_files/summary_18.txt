Roborock's First Robot Lawn Mower Is Here
============================================================

Roborock has officially entered the United States robot lawn mower market with the launch of the wire-free RockNeo Q110H, featuring advanced satellite navigation and AI-powered obstacle avoidance technology.

### Key Points
- The RockNeo Q110H utilizes full-band real-time kinematic positioning and 3D visual SLAM to navigate narrow passages and dense tree cover.
- The mower features a floating cutting deck capable of handling slopes up to 45% and obstacles measuring up to 1.57 inches in height.
- It is rated IPX6 waterproof and includes a rain-detection sensor that automatically returns the unit to its docking station during inclement weather.
- Users can manage mowing zones and schedules via the Roborock app, which also includes a wildlife-friendly mode to protect nocturnal animals.
- An optional 4G Anti-Theft Module provides real-time tracking, geofencing, and a high-decibel alarm to prevent unauthorized removal of the device.
- The mower is available on Amazon for $1,299, with an early bird promotional price of $1,169 offered through June 16.

### Why it Matters
This launch marks a significant expansion for Roborock as it leverages its expertise in indoor robotics to compete in the growing outdoor autonomous maintenance market. By offering wire-free installation and advanced mapping, the company aims to challenge established players in the residential lawn care sector.
