Tailscale Aperture targets shadow AI with new controls for IT teams
============================================================

Tailscale has introduced new capabilities for its Aperture platform, providing IT departments with a centralized, vendor-neutral layer to manage AI tools, data access, and agent security across organizations.

### Key Points
- Aperture now features a browser-based chat interface that allows users to switch between various LLM providers, including OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, and Amazon Bedrock.
- Universal data connectors enable AI tools to access internal documents and operational data without requiring individual teams to build custom integrations.
- The platform integrates with Tailscale to maintain consistent user identity and permissions throughout the entire lifecycle of an AI agent.
- A new sandbox environment, currently in private alpha, allows AI agents to operate in controlled spaces rather than on local devices.
- Research indicates that 64% of activity on personal AI accounts is work-related, contributing to significant security blind spots for corporate IT teams.

### Why it Matters
This platform addresses the growing challenge of shadow AI by allowing companies to maintain control over data and security without being locked into a single vendor's ecosystem. By decoupling the AI stack, organizations can remain agile and adopt new models as they emerge while ensuring consistent governance across their infrastructure.
