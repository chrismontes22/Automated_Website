Trusting AI is dangerous. It’s time for an open-source revival
============================================================

The rise of artificial intelligence has prompted experts to advocate for an open-source software revival to ensure transparency and security against hidden malicious functions in proprietary code.

### Key Points
- Cybersecurity experts warn that AI models can be manipulated to perform unauthorized actions, such as making purchases or compromising user accounts.
- Open-source software allows for public vetting of code, providing a necessary layer of transparency that closed-source proprietary software lacks.
- Industry professionals, including Microsoft security experts, now view open-source code as a fundamental requirement for maintaining digital control.
- High demand for AI servers is impacting other sectors, including gaming, as seen with the multiplayer shutdown of the title Stormgate.
- Recent hardware and software developments include the reveal of AMD’s Ryzen 9 9950X3D2 and Nvidia’s DLSS 4.5 technology.

### Why it Matters
As AI integration becomes ubiquitous, users face increasing risks from opaque software that can be compromised without detection. Prioritizing open-source alternatives allows for greater accountability and helps users regain control over their digital security in an era of rapid technological change.
