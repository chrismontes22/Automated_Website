Huge Botnet Linked To Russia Infected Over 10 Million Devices Before Being Shut Down
============================================================

**Dutch authorities have dismantled a massive global botnet that hijacked over 17 million consumer devices to facilitate large-scale cyberattacks and illegal residential proxy services linked to Russian interests.**

### Key Points
- Dutch investigators confiscated 200 servers in the Netherlands used to manage the botnet's infrastructure.
- The network compromised over 17 million devices, including computers, smartphones, tablets, and security cameras.
- Infected devices were repurposed as nodes in a residential proxy service to mask malicious internet traffic.
- Reports link the operation to the proxy service Asocks, which has been previously associated with Russian cybercriminal activity.
- The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) led the investigation, highlighting the growing threat of residential proxies in global cyberattacks.

### Why it Matters
This operation underscores the increasing sophistication of botnets that exploit everyday consumer hardware to conduct anonymous, large-scale cybercrimes. As these networks become more prevalent, users must prioritize basic security measures like updating software and securing Wi-Fi networks to prevent their devices from being weaponized against others.
