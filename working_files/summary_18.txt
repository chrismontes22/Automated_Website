AI browsers haven't gone anywhere, and this one claims you can have personalization without giving up your privacy
============================================================

The new Jatter web browser distinguishes itself from other AI-powered competitors by prioritizing user privacy and local data processing over agentic, task-oriented automation features for daily browsing.

### Key Points
- Jatter is a Chromium-based browser available on macOS, Windows, iOS, and Android that focuses on "personal answers" derived from user browsing habits.
- Unlike agentic browsers that perform tasks like booking flights, Jatter functions as a passive assistant that synthesizes information from sites the user explicitly authorizes.
- The browser features five integrated apps—Chats, Personal Answers, Notes, Maps, and Settings—to organize and query data learned from the user's digital activity.
- Developers claim all personal data is encrypted end-to-end on the device, with no information used for model training and a one-click option to wipe learned history.
- The company behind Jatter, formerly known as Beacon, has a history of developing privacy-focused tools, including encrypted location-sharing and anonymized chat applications.

### Why it Matters
Jatter represents a shift in the AI browser market toward privacy-first personalization, challenging the industry trend of building autonomous agents that require broad account access. While its security claims remain difficult for external users to audit, the browser offers a transparent, opt-in model that may appeal to privacy-conscious users wary of data-hungry AI tools.
