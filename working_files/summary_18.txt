MemoMind One AI glasses open preorders at $399
============================================================

MemoMind has launched the MemoMind One, a pair of camera-free AI display glasses designed for productivity, featuring real-time translation, navigation, and note-taking capabilities starting at $399 on Kickstarter.

### Key Points
- The glasses feature dual Micro-LED displays with 640x350 resolution, 2000 nits brightness, and a 25-degree field of view.
- Hardware includes Harman AudioEFX open-ear audio, triple microphones, and a lightweight magnesium-aluminum alloy frame weighing 46.6 grams.
- Core features include Memo AI for live translation in over 26 languages, meeting summaries, and an adaptive teleprompter.
- The device offers up to 16 hours of battery life and supports iOS, Android, and HarmonyOS connectivity.
- An optional $19.99 monthly subscription, Memo+, provides an "AI Long Memory" system for organizing daily tasks, journals, and searchable conversation history.
- The product is available in three frame styles—Nomad, Archive, and Gotham—with options for prescription lenses.

### Why it Matters
By omitting a camera, MemoMind One avoids the social friction and privacy concerns associated with recording devices, positioning itself as a practical, unobtrusive productivity tool. This approach shifts the focus of smart eyewear from content creation to glanceable information, testing whether consumers prioritize utility and comfort over advanced visual capture.
