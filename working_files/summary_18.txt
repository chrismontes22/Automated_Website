SpaceX developing AI hardware product that’s ‘slimmer than an iPhone,’ reports WSJ
============================================================

SpaceX is reportedly developing a slim artificial intelligence hardware prototype featuring a proprietary operating system and Qualcomm Snapdragon chips, though CEO Elon Musk has denied the report's accuracy.

### Key Points
- The Wall Street Journal reported that SpaceX showcased an AI hardware prototype thinner than an iPhone to investors.
- The device reportedly utilizes proprietary software and hardware components from Qualcomm.
- SpaceX recently integrated the AI firm xAI and acquired the agentic coding platform Cursor into its corporate structure.
- Elon Musk publicly refuted the report on social media, labeling the claims regarding the hardware development as "utterly false."
- The development efforts coincide with broader industry trends, including AI hardware projects at Apple and OpenAI.

### Why it Matters
This report highlights the intensifying competition among major technology firms to integrate artificial intelligence into dedicated consumer hardware devices. If accurate, such a move would signal a significant strategic expansion for SpaceX beyond its core aerospace and satellite internet operations.
