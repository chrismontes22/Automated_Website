Oura says it gets government demands for user data. Will it share how many?
============================================================

Health wearable manufacturer Oura faces scrutiny over its data security practices as the company declines to release transparency reports regarding government requests for sensitive user health information.

### Key Points
- Oura has sold over 5.5 million health-monitoring rings that track heart rate, sleep, location, and menstrual cycles.
- The company confirmed that user data is not end-to-end encrypted, allowing Oura staff to access information stored on its servers.
- Oura acknowledged receiving infrequent government requests for user data but refuses to disclose the frequency or nature of these demands.
- Despite previous indications that it was evaluating the release of a transparency report, Oura has remained unresponsive to recent inquiries on the matter.
- The company is currently valued at over $11 billion as it prepares for a potential public offering.

### Why it Matters
The lack of transparency regarding government data access creates significant privacy concerns for millions of users who entrust the company with intimate health metrics. As a market leader, Oura’s security architecture and refusal to disclose legal requests may impact consumer trust and set a precedent for data handling in the broader wearable technology industry.
