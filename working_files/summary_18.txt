I made my Wi-Fi channel narrower, and my whole house got faster
============================================================

Adjusting your router's 2.4GHz channel width to 20MHz can significantly improve home network stability and reduce connection drops caused by signal congestion in densely populated residential areas.

### Key Points
- Narrowing the 2.4GHz channel width from 40MHz to 20MHz minimizes interference from neighboring networks and Bluetooth devices.
- Only three non-overlapping channels exist on the 2.4GHz band, making wider 40MHz settings prone to performance degradation.
- Apple and TP-Link both recommend 20MHz for the 2.4GHz band to ensure consistent connectivity for smart home devices.
- Intel notes that while wider channels like 80MHz or 160MHz may increase speed on the 5GHz band, they also heighten the risk of interference.
- Users can modify these settings by accessing their router's administrative interface, typically found under wireless or advanced network configuration menus.

### Why it Matters
Optimizing channel width provides a cost-free method to resolve persistent connectivity issues without requiring new hardware or complex mesh system installations. This simple configuration change is particularly effective for users in crowded environments where signal interference frequently disrupts smart home devices and video calls.
