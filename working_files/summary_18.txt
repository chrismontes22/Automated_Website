Cloudflare’s CEO told analysts that 40% of internet traffic is now AI bots and it’s breaking the web’s business model
============================================================

Cloudflare CEO Matthew Prince projects that AI bot traffic will surpass human internet activity by 2027, threatening the advertising-based economic model that currently sustains the open web.

### Key Points
- AI bots are expected to exceed human web traffic by 2027, driven by agents that visit hundreds of sites per query.
- The $700 billion digital advertising market faces potential disruption as bot traffic renders traditional metrics like click-through rates and time-on-page unreliable.
- Publishers are experiencing increased infrastructure costs from automated scrapers while advertising revenue remains stagnant or declines.
- Cloudflare is developing bot management tools and metered-access infrastructure to help websites identify, throttle, or monetize automated traffic.
- Legal and technical efforts to block scrapers are struggling to keep pace with sophisticated bots that mimic human browsing patterns.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI-dominated traffic threatens to collapse the long-standing economic bargain where free content is supported by human-focused advertising. If publishers cannot monetize AI interactions, the internet may transition into a two-tier system where content access is restricted to those who can afford direct licensing fees.
