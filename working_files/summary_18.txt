Google Pics is the first AI image tool that actually listens to what you want
============================================================

Google unveiled Google Pics at I/O 2026, a new image-generation tool powered by the Nano Banana model that allows users to perform precise, localized edits to AI-generated visuals.

### Key Points
- Google Pics enables users to drag, resize, or replace specific objects and text within an image without regenerating the entire prompt.
- The underlying Nano Banana model maintains character and object consistency, which is essential for professional brand campaigns and marketing materials.
- Google is phasing out the Pixel Studio app to prioritize professional-grade AI workflows over novelty features.
- The tool currently supports text correction and translation, allowing users to update fonts and languages while preserving the original design style.
- Access is currently limited to a small group of Trusted Testers, with a broader rollout planned for Workspace and Google AI Pro subscribers.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward granular control marks a transition from novelty AI image generation to practical, professional-grade creative tools. By prioritizing predictable editing over simple text-to-image prompts, Google aims to integrate generative AI into serious business workflows and deadline-driven marketing projects.
