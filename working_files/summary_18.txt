### Key Points
- Palantir CEO Alex Karp claims enterprises are increasingly frustrated with frontier AI labs that focus on token-based metrics rather than solving real-world business problems.
- Anthropic has launched "Claude Corps," a dedicated initiative aimed at expanding the adoption of its Claude AI models within the nonprofit sector.
- Google introduced a new open-weights model that utilizes diffusion technology to improve text generation performance by up to four times.
- AWS released its Graviton 5 processor, though industry analysts have cautioned against labeling the hardware strictly as an "AI chip."
- Private equity firm EQT acquired a majority stake in the Swiss cybersecurity company Acronis at a valuation exceeding $3.5 billion.

### Why it Matters
The tension between AI developers and enterprise users reflects a maturing market that is shifting its focus from experimental hype to measurable return on investment. As businesses demand more practical utility, the industry must reconcile the gap between high-level model performance and the specific operational requirements of large-scale organizations.
