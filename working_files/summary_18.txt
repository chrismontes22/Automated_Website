Bitcoin Falls Below $70,000 For The First Time Since April
============================================================

Bitcoin prices dropped below $70,000 for the first time since April after Michael Saylor’s firm, Strategy, sold a portion of its holdings to fund dividend payments to stockholders.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell to a low of $69,690 on Tuesday, marking a 3.8% daily decline and an 8.4% drop over the past week.
- Strategy sold 32 bitcoins for approximately $2.5 million at an average price of $77,135 per token.
- The company remains the largest institutional holder of the cryptocurrency, currently retaining 843,706 bitcoins.
- Strategy’s stock price declined 5.85% following the SEC filing, reducing founder Michael Saylor’s net worth by $183 million.
- Other major cryptocurrencies, including Ether, BNB, and XRP, remained largely unaffected by the bitcoin selloff.

### Why it Matters
This rare divestment by a major institutional holder highlights the tension between maintaining long-term crypto reserves and meeting immediate corporate financial obligations like dividend payouts. The market reaction underscores investor sensitivity to any reduction in holdings by the world's largest corporate bitcoin owner.
