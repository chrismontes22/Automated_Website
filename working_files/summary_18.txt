Cloudflare – China Digital Times and the Fight Against Censorship
============================================================

China Digital Times has partnered with Cloudflare’s Project Galileo to secure its extensive archive of censored Chinese internet content against persistent, large-scale cyberattacks and potential data breaches.

### Key Points
- China Digital Times (CDT) has spent over 20 years archiving social media posts and articles deleted by Chinese authorities.
- The organization faces frequent cyberattacks that threaten to expose the identities of its staff and destroy two decades of historical data.
- Project Galileo provides CDT with enterprise-grade cybersecurity tools at no cost, allowing the small team to focus resources on editorial work.
- CDT is currently integrating AI technology to track the systematic disappearance of media accounts across the Chinese internet.
- Cloudflare’s infrastructure ensures the website remains accessible to a global audience despite ongoing attempts to knock the platform offline.

### Why it Matters
This partnership highlights the critical role of private-sector cybersecurity initiatives in protecting independent journalism and human rights documentation from state-sponsored digital censorship. By offloading complex security requirements to Cloudflare, small media organizations can maintain a persistent online presence while safeguarding sensitive contributor data.
