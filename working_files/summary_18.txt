The Information’s Ken Brown To Join CNBC As Managing Editor of Digital And Editorial Strategy
============================================================

CNBC has appointed former The Information editor Ken Brown as its new managing editor of digital and editorial strategy to oversee digital operations and enhance cross-platform journalism.

### Key Points
- Ken Brown will lead CNBC Digital starting June 8, reporting to Editor in Chief David Cho.
- Brown previously held leadership roles at The Wall Street Journal and most recently covered AI financing at The Information.
- The appointment aims to sharpen CNBC’s coverage of business, markets, and the economic impact of emerging technologies.
- Brown is tasked with integrating digital reporting more closely with the network's television editorial leadership.
- His background includes extensive experience in investigative reporting, financial crisis coverage, and subscription-based business models.

### Why it Matters
This strategic hire signals CNBC's intent to strengthen its digital presence and compete more aggressively for audiences interested in complex financial and technological reporting. By bridging the gap between television and digital platforms, the network aims to leverage its existing brand authority to drive growth in an increasingly digital-first media landscape.
