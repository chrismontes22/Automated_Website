I lost 4TB of files in a single SSD failure, and now I understand why a NAS should come first
============================================================

A sudden, silent failure of a 4TB high-capacity SSD highlights the critical risks of storing irreplaceable data on single internal drives and the necessity of robust backup strategies.

### Key Points
- High-capacity SSDs often fail without warning due to silent NAND flash degradation or controller firmware panics.
- Dense TLC and QLC V-NAND storage technology is prone to wear from intense thermal cycles and continuous write operations.
- Modern SSDs may enter a read-only "safe mode" upon detecting errors, which prevents operating systems from booting and accessing stored files.
- Network-attached storage (NAS) systems provide superior data security by using multi-drive arrays with distributed parity to prevent total data loss.
- Moving data to a NAS allows users to treat PCs as compute nodes while ensuring files remain accessible even if individual hardware components fail.

### Why it Matters
Relying on a single internal drive for long-term storage creates a single point of failure that can lead to permanent data loss. Transitioning to a NAS or redundant storage architecture ensures that hardware malfunctions remain minor maintenance tasks rather than catastrophic events.
