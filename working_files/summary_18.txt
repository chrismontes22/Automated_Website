Mozilla’s Inaugural ‘State of Open Source AI’ Report Is Here
============================================================

Mozilla’s inaugural State of Open Source AI report reveals that open models have nearly closed the performance gap with proprietary systems while significantly reducing operational costs for global developers.

### Key Points
- Open source AI models now perform within 3% of top proprietary systems like ChatGPT and Claude.
- Costs for running open models have decreased by up to 50 times over the past three years.
- While 79% of developers use open models, only 51% have successfully deployed them into production environments.
- China and East Asia lead global adoption at 89%, treating open source AI as a core national strategy.
- Open models currently power one-third of real-world AI applications but generate only 4% of total industry revenue.
- The report identifies "agentic harnesses"—the software layer controlling AI actions—as the most critical area for future development and governance.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward open source AI offers organizations the ability to own their infrastructure rather than renting proprietary services from major tech providers. However, a lack of mature deployment tooling and sustainable revenue models threatens to limit the long-term viability of open ecosystems compared to closed, scalable alternatives.
