"My jaw dropped": Dell just launched its new $599 XPS 13, and I don't think the Windows 11 laptop market will ever be the same
============================================================

Dell has officially launched its 2026 XPS 13 laptop, featuring a starting price of $699 and a discounted $599 rate for students to compete with the Apple MacBook Neo.

### Key Points
- The base model includes an Intel Core 5 320 processor, 8GB of RAM, a 512GB SSD, and a 13.4-inch 2.5K touch display.
- The device features an aluminum chassis weighing 2.2 pounds, Wi-Fi 7, Bluetooth 6, and a 1080p webcam with Windows Hello support.
- Initial units utilize Intel Core Series 3 chips, with more powerful Intel Core Ultra Series 3 "Panther Lake" models arriving later this summer.
- Unlike the MacBook Neo, the XPS 13 allows users to configure memory up to 32GB of RAM for increased performance.
- The laptop is currently available for purchase exclusively through the official Dell website.

### Why it Matters
This launch positions Dell as a direct competitor in the entry-level premium laptop market by offering flexible hardware configurations that exceed the limitations of rival devices. By providing a lower price point for students, Dell aims to capture the education sector while maintaining a path for users to upgrade their specifications as needed.
