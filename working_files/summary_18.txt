Asia’s stock markets dive amid Iran-Israel conflict, Wall Street jitters
============================================================

Asian stock markets experienced a sharp decline on Monday as renewed conflict between Israel and Iran and expectations of US interest rate hikes triggered a regional sell-off.

### Key Points
- South Korea’s KOSPI index plunged 8.29%, triggering a circuit breaker after falling nearly 9% during early morning trading.
- Tech giants Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix saw shares drop 10.2% and 7.6%, respectively, amid a broader retreat from AI-linked equities.
- Japan’s Nikkei 225 fell 3.9%, while Taiwan’s TAIEX, led by TSMC, declined 3.5% following negative momentum from Wall Street.
- Brent crude oil prices rose 3.7% to exceed $88.50 per barrel as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East intensified.
- US economic data showing strong job growth fueled investor concerns that the Federal Reserve may implement further interest rate hikes.

### Why it Matters
The market volatility highlights how sensitive global equities remain to both geopolitical instability and shifts in US monetary policy. This downturn signals a potential cooling of the recent AI-driven investment boom as investors reassess risk in a high-interest-rate environment.
