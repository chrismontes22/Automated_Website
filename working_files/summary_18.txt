Netflix Co-CEO Explains How Gen-AI Was Used in 300 Different Titles: ‘We Believe It Is Going to Enhance Their Abilities’
============================================================

Netflix revealed during its Q2 earnings call that generative AI tools have been utilized in 300 titles to enhance production quality, reduce costs, and accelerate creative timelines.

### Key Points
- Netflix used generative AI in 300 titles for pre-visualization, visual effects, and post-production enhancements.
- The docuseries "The American Experiment" featured 17 minutes of AI-enhanced footage produced at half the cost and twice the speed of traditional methods.
- Co-CEO Ted Sarandos confirmed the company acquired Ben Affleck’s InterPositive AI earlier this year to further drive production innovation.
- Other international titles utilizing these tools include the Indian film "Glory" and the Brazilian production "Brasil 70: A Saga do Tri."
- Netflix plans to reinvest cost savings from AI-driven efficiencies into producing more high-quality content for its global subscriber base.

### Why it Matters
The integration of generative AI into Netflix's production pipeline signals a significant shift in how major studios manage content budgets and creative workflows. By scaling these tools, Netflix aims to improve production value while reinvesting savings to fuel its long-term revenue and profit growth.
