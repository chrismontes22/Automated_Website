Complete Google Chrome Privacy Audit: Securing Passwords, Payments, and History
============================================================

Conducting a comprehensive privacy audit of your Google Chrome browser helps you identify, clean, and secure sensitive personal data like saved passwords, payment methods, and browsing history.

### Key Points
- Use the built-in "Safety Check" tool to identify compromised passwords, harmful extensions, and outdated software.
- Review and delete unused credentials within Google Password Manager to reduce your digital attack surface.
- Audit "Payments and autofill" settings to remove expired credit cards and unnecessary saved addresses.
- Clear browsing history and site data using the "Delete browsing data" menu to remove accumulated digital footprints.
- Manage site permissions for camera, microphone, and notifications to revoke access for sites you no longer visit.
- Evaluate browser extensions and remove any that are unused or request excessive permissions to access your data.

### Why it Matters
Regularly auditing your browser settings prevents the quiet accumulation of sensitive data that could be exploited if your account or device is compromised. By taking control of these permissions and stored credentials, you ensure that your browsing experience remains both convenient and secure.
