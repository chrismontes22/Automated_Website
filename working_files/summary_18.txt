Anthropic Urges Global Pause in AI Development, Flags 'Self-Improvement' Risk
============================================================

Anthropic is calling for a global, verifiable pause in frontier AI development, warning that rapid advancements in recursive self-improvement could soon outpace human oversight and safety measures.

### Key Points
- Anthropic reports that its engineers now ship eight times more code per quarter than they did between 2021 and 2025.
- The company warns that AI systems capable of building their own successors could pose significant risks to human control.
- The Anthropic Institute is researching mechanisms to verify global compliance with potential development slowdowns or pauses.
- A coordinated pause aims to allow alignment research and societal safety structures to catch up with rapid technological progress.
- Anthropic emphasizes that a unilateral slowdown could be dangerous if it allows less cautious actors to gain a competitive advantage.

### Why it Matters
The potential for AI to achieve recursive self-improvement represents a critical turning point that could redefine global security and technological governance. Establishing verifiable international standards is essential to ensure that safety protocols keep pace with the rapid evolution of frontier models.
