Strong economies, strongman leaders: how China (and America’s) AI boom could create a new class of kowtowing elites
============================================================

The 2026 V-Dem report reveals that 41% of the global population lives in autocratizing nations, as Arizona State University’s José Kaire links economic liberalization to increased political repression.

### Key Points
- The V-Dem Project reports that global democracy has regressed to levels not seen since 1978, with 92 countries currently classified as autocracies.
- José Kaire’s book, "The Road to Repression," argues that economic liberalization often forces dictators to increase repression to appease powerful regime elites.
- Research indicates that approximately half of all autocracies experienced increased human rights abuses after implementing market-oriented economic reforms.
- In countries like Cuba and Venezuela, strong regime elites may use economic liberalization as a catalyst to consolidate power and suppress political opposition.
- China is centralizing control over its artificial intelligence sector to ensure new tech elites remain aligned with the leadership of Xi Jinping.

### Why it Matters
The findings challenge the long-held assumption that economic liberalization naturally fosters democratic transition by empowering citizens. Instead, this research suggests that international pressure for market reform can inadvertently incentivize authoritarian regimes to increase repression to maintain internal stability.
