What Apple’s Gemini-Powered Siri Means For Search Visibility via @sejournal, @MattGSouthern
============================================================

Apple has unveiled a new AI-powered version of Siri that integrates web-based generative answers directly into Spotlight and system-wide interfaces across iPhone, iPad, and Mac devices.

### Key Points
- Siri AI uses Apple Foundation Models, built in collaboration with Google’s Gemini, to provide conversational, up-to-date answers from the web.
- The assistant features "Visual Intelligence," allowing users to query information about objects captured through the device camera.
- Apple’s updated support documentation indicates that AI-generated answers may include links to sources, though the frequency and format remain unspecified.
- Site owners can manage their content’s inclusion in AI training and answer generation using `Applebot-Extended` and `nosnippet` tags in robots.txt.
- The rollout begins with a user beta in English later this year, with initial exclusions for the European Union and China.

### Why it Matters
This update introduces a significant new "answer surface" that may resolve user queries without requiring a click-through to a website, potentially impacting organic traffic for informational content. Search professionals must now navigate a landscape where brand visibility depends on an assistant's ability to interpret and cite structured data rather than traditional search engine rankings.
