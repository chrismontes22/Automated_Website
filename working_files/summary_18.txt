Why Palantir is fast becoming one of the world’s most notorious companies
============================================================

Palantir Technologies, a data analytics firm led by CEO Alex Karp, provides critical software for global military, intelligence, and healthcare operations while facing scrutiny over its expanding influence.

### Key Points
- Founded in 2003 by Peter Thiel, the company currently holds a market capitalization of approximately $350 billion.
- Core software platforms include "Gotham" for military and law enforcement use and "Foundry" for civilian data integration.
- The firm is a primary contractor for the US military's Project Maven, which utilizes AI to identify and target military objectives.
- Palantir holds significant government contracts, including a £330 million deal to develop the Federated Data Platform for NHS England.
- CEO Alex Karp has recently advocated for increased military-tech integration and universal conscription, drawing both support and intense public criticism.

### Why it Matters
Palantir’s technology is fundamentally reshaping modern warfare by accelerating the "kill chain" through AI-driven target identification. As the company integrates into sensitive public sectors like the NHS, policymakers are increasingly debating the risks of relying on a private firm with deep ties to foreign military and intelligence agencies.
