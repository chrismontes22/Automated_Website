YouTube is making it easier to spot AI-generated videos
============================================================

YouTube is enhancing transparency by introducing more prominent AI labels and automated detection systems to identify realistic, AI-generated or significantly altered content across its long-form and Shorts videos.

### Key Points
- YouTube is moving AI disclosure labels from video descriptions to more visible locations, including below the player for long-form content and as an overlay for Shorts.
- The platform is implementing automated detection to label AI-generated videos if creators fail to disclose the use of synthetic media during the upload process.
- Creators retain control over their content and can manually remove or update labels in YouTube Studio if the automated system incorrectly flags a video.
- These updates specifically target realistic-looking content, while videos that are clearly unrealistic or only slightly modified will continue to use standard description-based disclosures.
- The new labeling system is currently rolling out to all users and will eventually integrate with C2PA metadata and Google’s proprietary AI tools like Veo.

### Why it Matters
These changes address growing concerns regarding misinformation by helping viewers distinguish between authentic footage and realistic synthetic media. By automating the disclosure process, YouTube aims to standardize transparency across its massive library while reducing the burden on creators to manually label every AI-assisted upload.
