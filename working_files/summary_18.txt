Google CEO Largely Avoids Discussing AI In Stanford Commencement Speech
============================================================

Google CEO Sundar Pichai delivered the 2026 commencement address at Stanford University, focusing on themes of optimism and personal perseverance rather than the ongoing artificial intelligence industry boom.

### Key Points
- Sundar Pichai addressed the Stanford University class of 2026 during their official commencement ceremony.
- The speech emphasized the importance of choosing optimism and reframing perspectives when facing new challenges.
- Pichai notably avoided detailed discussions regarding AI, automation, or the shifting job market for graduates.
- The CEO shared a personal anecdote about his arrival in California to illustrate the power of positive reframing.

### Why it Matters
The decision to omit AI from the address highlights a shift in how tech leaders communicate with graduates entering a workforce increasingly defined by automation. By focusing on timeless values, Pichai prioritized long-term career adaptability over the immediate, often controversial, technological trends currently dominating the industry.
