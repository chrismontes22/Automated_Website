Your browser is too nosy. Change these 5 settings now
============================================================

**Users can significantly enhance their online privacy and system performance by adjusting five critical default settings in popular web browsers like Chrome, Edge, and Firefox.**

### Key Points
- Disable intrusive browser notifications and autoplay videos to prevent spam and reduce unnecessary background activity.
- Block third-party cookies and set tracking protection to "Strict" to limit cross-site user profiling and personalized advertising.
- Restrict telemetry and cloud synchronization to prevent browsers from sharing browsing history and usage data with tech companies.
- Enable device authentication, such as Windows Hello or biometric prompts, to secure stored passwords within the browser's manager.
- Turn off "Startup Boost" and background processes in Chrome and Edge to conserve RAM and improve battery life.
- Utilize the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal, either natively in Firefox or via extensions like Privacy Badger, to legally restrict tracking.

### Why it Matters
Default browser configurations often prioritize data collection and convenience over user privacy, turning software into a source of constant surveillance. By manually tightening these settings, users can reclaim control over their personal data and prevent corporations from building detailed profiles of their online behavior.
