Trump's DOT proposes new rules for driverless vehicles
============================================================

The U.S. Department of Transportation has proposed new federal safety rules that would eliminate the requirement for brake pedals in vehicles designed exclusively for autonomous driving systems.

### Key Points
- The proposal aims to remove regulatory hurdles for companies like Tesla and Amazon-owned Zoox that are developing robotaxis without traditional driver controls.
- Current federal standards require physical brake pedals, forcing companies to seek limited government exemptions to deploy autonomous vehicles on public roads.
- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration is accepting public comments on the proposed rule change for a 30-day period.
- Industry leaders including Waymo and Zoox support the move, noting that updated regulations would reduce compliance costs and allow for faster fleet scaling.
- Safety advocacy groups, including the American Automobile Association, have raised concerns regarding the lack of research on emergency responder access and passenger safety.

### Why it Matters
This regulatory shift signals a significant move toward the mass deployment of driverless vehicles by removing legacy design requirements that no longer apply to automated systems. While the change could accelerate the commercialization of robotaxis, it also highlights an ongoing debate regarding the balance between fostering technological innovation and ensuring public safety.
