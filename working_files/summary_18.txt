Nasdaq expands distribution of its market data into blockchain infrastructure
============================================================

Nasdaq is expanding the distribution of its TotalView equity market data by integrating the feed into the Pyth Network to support blockchain-based financial applications and institutional developers.

### Key Points
- Nasdaq will provide its full depth-of-book equity data, including buy and sell orders, through the decentralized Pyth Data Marketplace.
- The integration allows developers to access institutional-grade market information via programmable interfaces rather than traditional, dedicated data feeds.
- The move supports the growing trend of financial institutions building trading and settlement infrastructure compatible with tokenized assets and on-chain services.
- Nasdaq joins other major data contributors on the Pyth Network, including Tradeweb, SGX, and the U.S. Department of Commerce.
- Users can leverage the data to enhance quantitative trading models, improve trade execution, and analyze real-time market depth for various listed securities.

### Why it Matters
This partnership signals a significant shift in how traditional exchanges deliver market data as financial infrastructure migrates toward cloud-based and blockchain-powered systems. By making core data accessible to decentralized applications, Nasdaq is positioning itself to remain relevant in an evolving market that increasingly demands programmable, on-chain financial tools.
