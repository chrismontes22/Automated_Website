Nobody Wants These Goddamn Data Centers. Trump's Blowing $17.5 Billion On Nukes To Power Them
============================================================

Growing public opposition to AI data centers is intensifying across the United States, prompting the Trump administration to propose $17.5 billion in loans for new nuclear power projects.

### Key Points
- A Gallup poll indicates 71 percent of Americans oppose the construction of AI data centers in their local communities.
- Citizen activism has successfully blocked or delayed approximately $64 billion in data center projects over the past two years.
- The Trump administration plans to offer $17.5 billion in loans to support the construction of 10 large-scale nuclear reactors by the mid-2030s.
- Rep. Frank Pallone (D-NJ) and other lawmakers are calling for a national moratorium on new data center construction due to concerns over energy costs and grid strain.
- Recent elections in states like Utah saw incumbents defeated after supporting large-scale "hyperscale" data center developments.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of AI infrastructure is creating a significant political and environmental conflict between tech industry demands and local community interests. This tension forces policymakers to balance the need for massive new energy capacity against public concerns regarding utility rates, water usage, and the long-term viability of large-scale nuclear investments.
