Emergent Ventures India, 17th cohort
============================================================

Emergent Ventures has announced its latest cohort of Indian grant recipients, supporting diverse projects ranging from AI-driven healthcare and space-based solar power to sustainable manufacturing and educational innovation.

### Key Points
- The cohort includes 24 innovators working on projects like synthetic fuel production, gene editing, and autonomous aerial defense systems.
- Notable ventures include HackerFab IITB for microfabrication, Neurosole for diabetic neuropathy detection, and Avinya Vegan Leather for compostable materials.
- Several recipients are developing AI-focused solutions, including tools for radiology workflows, drug discovery, and personalized storytelling for children.
- The 1825 Fund, led by Shreyansh Diwakar, provides equity-free capital to support young builders and hackers across India.
- Projects span various sectors, including clean energy, biotechnology, public policy, and decentralized travel infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
These grants provide critical early-stage funding to young Indian entrepreneurs and researchers tackling complex challenges in deep tech, sustainability, and public health. By supporting these diverse initiatives, Emergent Ventures fosters a bottom-up innovation ecosystem that aims to accelerate scientific progress and economic development across the region.
