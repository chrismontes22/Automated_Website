Crime blotter: School board vice president accused of having illicit images on iPhone
============================================================

Recent reports highlight a series of criminal incidents involving Apple products, ranging from the use of Find My tracking to recover stolen goods to serious legal indictments.

### Key Points
- A former New York school board official was indicted for child sex abuse material discovered on his iPhone 17 Pro Max.
- Police in Kentucky and Wyoming used Find My signals from stolen AirPods to locate suspects and recover property from vehicle burglaries.
- A Texas woman was arrested for criminal trespass after refusing to leave an Illinois Apple Store following a service dispute.
- South Korean broadcaster Kim Hwan recovered his stolen MacBook and iPad in Mexico after tracking the devices to a vacant lot.
- Authorities are investigating the theft of an iPhone 17 Pro Max from Bangladeshi presenter Neel Hurerzahan and an iPad from a New York charity.

### Why it Matters
These incidents demonstrate how Apple’s integrated tracking technology frequently serves as a critical tool for law enforcement and victims in recovering stolen property. However, the recurring involvement of high-end devices in criminal investigations also underscores the ongoing security and privacy challenges associated with personal technology.
