iOS 26.5 gave Messages app encrypted RCS, here’s how to check it’s working
============================================================

Apple has introduced end-to-end encryption for RCS messaging in the iOS 26.5 update, allowing iPhone users to secure cross-platform conversations with non-Apple devices that support the communication standard.

### Key Points
- iOS 26.5 enables end-to-end encryption for RCS messages by default, provided both participants use carriers that support the security feature.
- Users can verify encryption status by navigating to Settings, selecting Apps, choosing Messages, and checking the RCS Messaging toggle.
- A specific "Encrypted" lock icon appears at the top of supported conversation threads when the security protocol is active.
- Encryption status can be confirmed manually by tapping a contact's profile image and viewing the security message at the bottom of the details screen.
- Encryption availability depends on the technical capabilities of both the sender's and the recipient's mobile carriers.

### Why it Matters
This update significantly improves privacy for iPhone users communicating with Android devices by extending security standards previously reserved for iMessage. It marks a major shift in cross-platform messaging, though widespread adoption remains dependent on individual carrier support for the RCS encryption protocol.
