The Pulse of Experience
============================================================

Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG) technology enables non-invasive, real-time measurement of human physiological states, allowing organizations to replace subjective sentiment analysis with objective biological data for improved experience design.

### Key Points
- rPPG uses high-resolution cameras to detect invisible skin color fluctuations caused by blood flow, providing metrics like heart rate variability and respiration.
- The technology shifts focus from lagging Service Level Agreements (SLAs) to real-time Experience Level Measures (XLMs) that quantify human emotional responses.
- Key industry players include Philips Biosensing, Blue Spark Technologies, Circadify, Darwin Edge, and IntelliProve, which are commercializing contactless biometric solutions.
- Applications range from UX testing and retail journey mapping to high-stress workforce training in fields like aviation and healthcare.
- Ethical deployment requires local edge processing, transparent user consent, and a commitment to avoiding surveillance or manipulative behavioral exploitation.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward biological data allows companies to move beyond unreliable surveys and superficial facial tracking to understand the true emotional impact of digital and physical interactions. By integrating these metrics, organizations can create more empathetic, adaptive systems that reduce user friction and prioritize human well-being.
