Report: Apple considers Intel and Samsung to diversify chip manufacturing away from TSMC
============================================================

Apple is exploring potential chip manufacturing partnerships with Intel and Samsung Electronics to reduce its long-standing reliance on TSMC for producing processors used in iPhones and Mac computers.

### Key Points
- Apple is currently in early-stage discussions with Intel and evaluating Samsung Electronics' manufacturing facilities in Texas.
- The company aims to diversify its supply chain to mitigate risks associated with its heavy dependence on Taiwan-based TSMC.
- Recent supply-chain constraints, driven by high demand for AI-capable hardware, have increased the urgency for Apple to secure additional production capacity.
- Apple recently reorganized its hardware engineering teams under Chief Hardware Officer Johny Srouji to streamline its silicon development efforts.
- Challenges remain regarding manufacturing scale and consistency, as Intel and Samsung currently struggle to match TSMC’s advanced production capabilities.

### Why it Matters
Diversifying chip production is a strategic move to mitigate geopolitical risks and supply-chain vulnerabilities that could disrupt the availability of Apple’s core devices. Securing alternative manufacturing partners would also support broader industry efforts to expand advanced semiconductor production capacity within the United States.
