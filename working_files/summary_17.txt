The 2000s called. They want their digital camera back
============================================================

Digital cameras are experiencing a significant resurgence as Gen Z and millennial consumers embrace retro point-and-shoot devices to capture nostalgic, authentic photos while reducing their daily smartphone reliance.

### Key Points
- Canon reported that sales of its PowerShot point-and-shoot cameras increased nearly sevenfold between 2022 and 2025.
- Camp Snap, a company originally targeting summer camps, saw its annual sales more than double as adults adopted its screen-free digital cameras.
- The trend is driven by a desire for the "vintage" aesthetic of grainy, overexposed, and date-stamped images common in early 2000s photography.
- Consumers are increasingly using dedicated cameras to avoid the distractions of smartphone notifications and constant internet connectivity.
- Marketplaces like eBay report a broader surge in consumer interest for legacy technology, including iPods, CDs, and portable cassette players.

### Why it Matters
This shift reflects a growing "analog reboot" movement where users seek to disconnect from hyper-connected digital environments to preserve more genuine, intentional memories. The trend highlights a significant market opportunity for manufacturers to capitalize on nostalgia and the consumer desire for simplified, single-purpose hardware.
