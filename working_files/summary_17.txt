Claude's popularity is forcing it to hit the brakes on users
============================================================

**Anthropic has implemented stricter usage caps for Claude subscribers during peak hours to manage high demand and compute strain following a surge in mainstream interest for its models.**

**Key points**
- Anthropic adjusted five-hour session limits for free, Pro, and Max subscribers during peak hours of 5 a.m. to 11 a.m. PT.
- Approximately 7% of users, particularly those on Pro tiers, are expected to hit their session limits more frequently under the new policy.
- Weekly usage limits remain unchanged, though the company recommends shifting token-intensive background tasks to off-peak hours.
- The adjustments follow increased public interest in Anthropic after CEO Dario Amodei restricted the Pentagon’s access to the company’s AI models.
- The company is currently balancing compute resources as the rise of automated AI agents leads to higher token consumption across the industry.

**Why it matters: **
These usage adjustments highlight the ongoing struggle AI companies face in balancing massive consumer demand with limited compute infrastructure. The move underscores the broader industry challenge of scaling high-performance models while maintaining service reliability for enterprise and individual users.
