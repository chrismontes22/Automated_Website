DOGE whistleblower's brake line was cut after Musk posted about him on X: Timeline of what we know
============================================================

Federal IT staffer Dan Berulis is suing Elon Musk for defamation after discovering his car's brake line was cut shortly after reporting a cybersecurity breach involving DOGE officials.

### Key Points
- In April 2025, NLRB staffer Dan Berulis reported that Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) access to agency systems led to suspicious data traffic and Russian login attempts.
- Elon Musk publicly accused Berulis of filing a false whistleblower claim on April 19, 2025, after reposting content alleging the breach never occurred.
- On April 20, 2025, police documented a cut brake line on Berulis' vehicle, though investigators closed the case without identifying a suspect.
- Berulis filed a defamation lawsuit against Musk in April 2026, alleging the billionaire acted with reckless disregard for the truth regarding his whistleblower status.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the intense legal and personal risks faced by government whistleblowers when their claims intersect with high-profile public figures and political initiatives. The ongoing litigation serves as a significant test for defamation standards regarding social media commentary and the protection of individuals reporting potential federal security vulnerabilities.
