QoS is the one setting that fixes the lag you blame your ISP for, and it takes two minutes to enable
============================================================

Enabling Quality of Service (QoS) settings on your home router can effectively eliminate gaming lag and network jitter by prioritizing latency-sensitive traffic over large background downloads and streaming.

### Key Points
- Gaming lag is often caused by routers treating all network traffic equally rather than prioritizing time-sensitive data packets.
- Users can configure QoS, Adaptive QoS, or Game Boost settings via their router’s admin page or companion apps like TP-Link Tether.
- For optimal performance, set bandwidth limits to approximately 90% of your actual speed test results rather than the ISP's advertised speeds.
- Older routers may require flashing custom firmware like OpenWrt to utilize Smart Queue Management (SQM) for reducing network bufferbloat.
- Enabling traffic prioritization may slightly reduce total available bandwidth, but it ensures a more stable connection for competitive online gaming.

### Why it Matters
Upgrading to a faster internet plan often fails to resolve gaming lag if the router lacks proper traffic management capabilities. By configuring QoS, users can stabilize their connection without purchasing expensive new hardware, ensuring a smoother experience during high-traffic periods.
