The third way: Navigating AI’s knife edge
============================================================

AI researcher Samuel Hammond joins BlazeTV hosts Christopher Rufo and Jonathan Keeperman to advocate for a pragmatic middle path between utopian and apocalyptic views regarding artificial intelligence development.

### Key Points
- Samuel Hammond, a researcher at the Foundation for American Innovation, argues that AI presents both significant societal benefits and severe security risks.
- The discussion highlights the dual nature of AI, noting its potential to discover new drugs while simultaneously enabling the creation of novel bioweapons.
- Hammond emphasizes that achieving advanced AI capabilities first provides the United States with a strategic advantage to harden critical infrastructure against cyber threats.
- The conversation addresses the difficulty of regulating AI, comparing its broad scope to the foundational impact of electricity.
- Hammond warns that the current landscape exists on a precarious edge between state-controlled surveillance and chaotic anarchy.

### Why it Matters
This dialogue highlights the growing tension between rapid technological advancement and the need for effective governance in an era of powerful, autonomous systems. By rejecting extreme narratives, the discussion underscores the urgent requirement for institutions that can harness AI's potential while preventing its use as a tool for tyranny or systemic disorder.
