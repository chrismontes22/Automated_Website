Coffee Break: Is Mark Zuckerberg Pulling Meta’s Business Into a Death Spiral?
============================================================

Meta is facing significant internal and external turmoil as CEO Mark Zuckerberg aggressively pivots the company toward artificial intelligence through massive layoffs and controversial engineering reassignments.

### Key Points
- Meta laid off approximately 8,000 employees in May while reassigning 7,000 others to AI-focused roles, including data labeling and model training.
- The company implemented the "Model Capability Initiative," a tool that tracks employee mouse movements and keystrokes to train autonomous AI agents.
- Meta’s Reality Labs division reported a $19.1 billion loss in 2025, continuing a trend of heavy spending on virtual reality and wearable technology.
- Reports indicate Meta secretly integrated facial recognition code, internally called "NameTag," into its AI app despite public claims that the feature was still under review.
- Meta remains heavily reliant on advertising, which accounted for 98% of its $56.3 billion revenue in the first quarter of 2025.

### Why it Matters
Meta’s shift from a high-performance engineering culture to a centralized AI-focused organization has sparked widespread employee dissatisfaction and concerns regarding long-term stability. As the company struggles to diversify beyond its core advertising business, these aggressive internal changes and privacy controversies pose significant risks to its market position and future growth.
