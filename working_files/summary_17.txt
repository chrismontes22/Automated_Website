Dell Takes Aim at the MacBook Neo With Its $599 XPS 13 Laptop
============================================================

Dell is challenging Apple’s MacBook Neo in the student laptop market by launching a new XPS 13 model featuring premium hardware specifications and a competitive $599 education price point.

### Key Points
- The Dell XPS 13 features an aluminum chassis weighing 2.2 pounds, making it half a pound lighter than the MacBook Neo.
- The base model includes a 13.4-inch 2.5K touchscreen display with a 120Hz refresh rate, surpassing the Neo’s 60Hz non-touch screen.
- Internal hardware includes an Intel Core 5 Series 3 processor, 8GB of RAM, and a 512GB SSD, doubling the Neo's base storage capacity.
- Additional features include Wi-Fi 7 connectivity, quad speakers, keyboard backlighting, and Windows Hello facial recognition for secure logins.
- Dell is offering a $100 student discount for users aged 16 and older, bringing the starting price down to $599 beginning in late June.

### Why it Matters
This release intensifies the competition for the student laptop market by offering high-end features like touchscreens and biometric security at a price point previously dominated by Apple. By matching the MacBook Neo's entry-level cost, Dell provides students with a powerful Windows-based alternative that prioritizes hardware specifications and connectivity.
