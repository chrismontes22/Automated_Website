Kevin O'Leary claims Chinese propaganda is to blame for anti-datacenter backlash, 'hundreds of millions of dollars' being spent to kill US dominance in AI — industry proponents and Trump administration reinforce claims of foreign interference
============================================================

Investor Kevin O'Leary and U.S. officials allege that foreign propaganda is fueling local opposition to massive data center projects, though residents cite legitimate environmental and resource concerns.

### Key Points
- Billionaire investor Kevin O'Leary is proposing a $100-billion, 40,000-acre data center project in Utah.
- O'Leary and Interior Secretary Doug Burgum claim opposition to such projects is driven by Chinese-funded "dark money" and foreign-directed propaganda.
- Neither O'Leary nor government officials have provided verifiable evidence to support the allegations of foreign interference in local protests.
- Local residents and critics argue that data centers strain limited water supplies, increase electricity costs, and contribute to environmental degradation.
- Industry analysts note that while foreign influence campaigns may exist, they are not the primary driver of widespread public skepticism toward AI infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
The debate highlights the growing tension between the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure and the resource constraints of local communities. As the U.S. and China engage in a global AI arms race, these projects face increasing scrutiny over their long-term impact on power grids, water security, and local economies.
