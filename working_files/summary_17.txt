Data breach exposes up to 14.2 million email logins at six ISPs
============================================================

Japanese telecommunications giant KDDI Corporation confirmed a data breach involving a third-party software vulnerability that potentially exposed email addresses and passwords for over 14 million customer accounts.

### Key Points
- KDDI discovered the unauthorized access on June 17 and immediately implemented defensive measures to block the attackers.
- The breach impacts five partner ISPs: STNet, JCOM, Chubu Telecommunications, NIFTY Corporation, and BIGLOBE.
- Up to 14.22 million current, former, and inactive customer accounts may have had their credentials compromised.
- Some exposed passwords were stored using encryption or hashing, though the company has not disclosed the specific security protocols used.
- KDDI has notified Japan’s Personal Information Protection Commission and the Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications regarding the incident.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the significant security risks posed by vulnerabilities in third-party software integrated into large-scale telecommunications infrastructure. The potential exposure of millions of user credentials necessitates immediate password resets and the adoption of two-factor authentication to prevent widespread account hijacking.
