Spain Blacklists Palantir Over Domestic Security Concerns
============================================================

Spain has blacklisted U.S. data analytics firm Palantir from government contracts following security evaluations by the National Intelligence Center and the Ministry of Defense regarding sensitive data handling.

### Key Points
- Spain’s National Intelligence Center and Ministry of Defense cited concerns over potential foreign interference and unauthorized access to sensitive data.
- Palantir has provided surveillance and predictive policing software to Spanish law enforcement and border control agencies since 2017.
- The blacklisted contracts were valued at approximately €10 million, according to procurement documents reviewed by Reuters.
- Officials expressed specific concerns regarding compliance with Spanish data protection laws and the U.S. Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act.
- A Palantir spokesperson disputed the allegations, claiming the company’s technology is secure and that they have not received formal notification of the ban.

### Why it Matters
This decision reflects growing European skepticism toward U.S. tech dominance and signals a stricter approach to data sovereignty within the European Union. The move could set a precedent for other member states to increase scrutiny of foreign technology providers, potentially impacting Palantir's future expansion across the continent.
