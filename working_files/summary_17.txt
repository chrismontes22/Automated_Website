3 easy-to-miss cybersecurity risks for small businesses
============================================================

Small business owners can significantly improve their security by separating personal and professional data, using dedicated tax identifiers, and strictly managing device access within their home environments.

### Key Points
- Obtain a free Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS to avoid sharing your personal Social Security Number on business W-9 forms.
- Separate business documents from personal cloud storage accounts like Google Drive or iCloud to prevent unauthorized access by family members.
- Enable multi-factor authentication and unique passwords on all business-related accounts to mitigate the risk of identity theft and data breaches.
- Treat work devices as professional assets by requiring passcodes and installing always-on antimalware software to protect against accidental malware downloads.
- Limit device access within the home to prevent non-malicious "insider threats" from accidentally compromising sensitive business records or financial data.

### Why it Matters
These non-technical security measures help small business owners reduce their vulnerability to identity theft and data loss without requiring advanced engineering skills. By establishing clear boundaries between personal and professional digital footprints, entrepreneurs can better protect their assets and maintain client trust.
