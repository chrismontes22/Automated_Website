Android Auto is crashing (again), and nobody knows why (again)
============================================================

Android Auto users are reporting frequent crashes and connectivity failures following the release of version 17.2, marking the latest in a series of persistent technical issues for the platform.

### Key Points
- Version 17.2.662404, released on July 3, 2026, is causing widespread app crashes and random disconnections.
- Users report that the software frequently fails specifically when audio playback is initiated.
- The glitches affect a diverse range of Android devices rather than being limited to a single manufacturer.
- Some users have found temporary relief by rolling back to previous software versions, such as 17.0.
- Google has acknowledged similar reports on its Issue Tracker, though the company has not yet provided a definitive fix.

### Why it Matters
These recurring stability issues undermine user trust in a critical automotive interface used by millions of drivers daily. Persistent software failures may complicate Google's efforts to roll out its upcoming CarPlay-inspired redesign while maintaining a reliable experience for the broader Android ecosystem.
