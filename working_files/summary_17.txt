'Like my lover'...
============================================================

China’s new national framework for emotionally interactive AI took effect July 15, 2026, forcing major platforms like ByteDance’s Doubao and Alibaba’s Qwen to shut down personalized AI companion features.

### Key Points
- The "Interim Measures for the Administration of AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services" mandates anti-addiction systems, real-time dependence monitoring, and mandatory machine-disclosure notifications.
- ByteDance and Alibaba opted for service shutdowns because persistent-memory architecture, which enables emotional bonding, is fundamentally incompatible with the law's required interruption mechanisms.
- Doubao users have until October 15, 2026, to export data, while Qwen users face immediate, permanent deletion of all conversation histories and agent configurations.
- The regulation specifically targets consumer-facing emotional companions, while exempting workplace productivity, educational, and customer service AI tools.
- Platforms must now implement tiered security assessments and algorithm filings if they reach 100,000 monthly active users or one million registered users.

### Why it Matters
This regulatory shift forces a global reckoning for AI developers, as the core features that make virtual companions feel personal are now legally classified as high-risk for addiction. Companies worldwide must now determine if they can engineer compliant, "friction-filled" AI architectures without destroying the emotional engagement that drives their business models.
