What to know about the AI chip stock selloff
============================================================

Shares of major artificial intelligence chipmakers declined on Friday as investors reacted to TSMC’s capital expenditure forecast and broader market concerns regarding the sustainability of high-cost AI investments.

### Key Points
- TSMC shares fell over 3% following an earnings report that signaled higher-than-expected capital expenditure requirements.
- Nvidia shares dropped 2.2%, contributing to a 1.4% decline for the Nasdaq index on Friday.
- Semiconductor stocks including Applied Materials, Intel, and AMD also saw share price decreases during the trading session.
- The SMH semiconductor index has declined 9.5% over the past month, reflecting a period of market volatility.
- Analysts attribute the downturn to profit-taking after significant annual gains and growing skepticism regarding the immediate profitability of AI infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
The recent selloff highlights growing investor anxiety over whether the massive capital expenditures required for AI development will yield sufficient returns in the near term. This market correction reflects a shift in sentiment as traders weigh the potential for future interest rate hikes against the long-term viability of the current AI investment cycle.
