Your smart TV is tracking everything you watch — here's why and how to stop it
============================================================

Smart TVs utilize Automatic Content Recognition technology to track user viewing habits across streaming, cable, and external devices, often sharing this data with third-party advertisers for targeted marketing.

### Key Points
- Automatic Content Recognition (ACR) technology continuously scans audio and video signals to monitor and log user viewing habits multiple times per minute.
- Data collection occurs across all inputs, including live cable, streaming platforms, HDMI-connected gaming consoles, and external media players.
- Manufacturers typically enable ACR by default, requiring users to manually navigate privacy settings to disable data tracking and targeted advertising features.
- Privacy settings for ACR are often located under menus labeled "Viewing Information Services," "Smart TV Experience," or "User Agreements" depending on the brand.
- Additional privacy measures include disabling voice assistant microphones, covering integrated cameras, or disconnecting the television from the internet entirely.

### Why it Matters
This data collection practice allows companies to monetize user behavior by selling granular viewing profiles to advertisers for highly targeted marketing campaigns. Understanding how to manage these privacy settings is essential for consumers who wish to limit corporate surveillance and reduce the volume of personalized ads they receive.
