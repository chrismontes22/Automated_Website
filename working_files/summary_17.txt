Meta is Increasing the Snack Budget After Staff Morale Plummets
============================================================

Meta Chief Technical Officer Andrew Bosworth acknowledged that employee morale has reached near-record lows following significant layoffs, increased workplace surveillance, and the mandatory reassignment of staff to AI training.

### Key Points
- Meta laid off 8,000 employees in April 2026 and implemented new computer activity monitoring to facilitate AI model training.
- Approximately 6,500 staff members, or 10% of the workforce, have been reassigned to full-time data-labeling tasks for the Applied AI unit.
- CTO Andrew Bosworth admitted during a June 2 internal meeting that current company morale is among the worst in Meta's history.
- Leadership plans to address declining sentiment by increasing budgets for snacks, travel, and corporate events.
- Bosworth issued a memo pledging to improve company culture and better support employees in their long-term professional roles.

### Why it Matters
The internal unrest highlights the growing tension between aggressive corporate AI integration and workforce stability at major technology firms. If Meta fails to stabilize its culture, the company risks losing top engineering talent to competitors during a critical period of industry-wide artificial intelligence development.
