Kash Patel Late Stock Disclosure Raises STOCK Act Questions...
============================================================

FBI Director Kash Patel failed to timely disclose a $100,000 to $250,000 investment in MicroStrategy, a government contractor that maintains significant business ties with the Department of Justice.

### Key Points
- FBI Director Kash Patel purchased between $100,001 and $250,000 in MicroStrategy stock on November 21.
- The trade was not disclosed to federal regulators until May 26, missing the 45-day deadline required by the STOCK Act.
- Patel attributed the delay to an inadvertent omission and a miscommunication, according to letters sent to the Office of Government Ethics.
- MicroStrategy is a government contractor that has conducted millions of dollars in business with the Department of Justice.
- The Department of Justice has not issued a fine for the late disclosure, and officials maintain there is no conflict of interest.

### Why it Matters
The incident highlights ongoing concerns regarding the ethics of federal officials trading individual stocks while overseeing agencies that interact with those companies. It fuels the broader debate among government watchdogs and lawmakers about whether a total ban on stock trading for high-ranking officials is necessary to prevent potential conflicts of interest.
