Ad Tech Briefing: Containerization moves from concept to competition
============================================================

PubMatic and Index Exchange are leading a shift toward containerization in programmatic advertising, allowing firms to run decisioning models directly within supply-side infrastructure to improve operational efficiency.

### Key Points
- PubMatic launched its Decision Fabric layer on June 1, enabling partners like MiQ and inPowered to execute bidding logic directly within the supply path.
- Index Exchange previously introduced its Index Cloud initiative, which allows demand-side platforms to deploy applications within its exchange infrastructure.
- Containerization moves compute power closer to the data source, reducing latency and the high costs associated with processing bidstream data in public clouds.
- By eliminating the need to move data across multiple hops, companies can maintain higher signal fidelity and avoid the throttling of bid requests.
- This architectural shift supports the growing trend of media agencies and advertisers seeking more direct control over their supply chains and data flows.

### Why it Matters
This transition represents a fundamental change in programmatic infrastructure as firms move away from fragmented, high-cost processing models toward more centralized, efficient compute environments. By bringing decisioning closer to the auction, the industry aims to reduce operational overhead while providing advertisers with greater transparency and control over their media investments.
