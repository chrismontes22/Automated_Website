I make these 3 tweaks to every pair of earbuds I buy — they all sound better than stock
============================================================

Users can significantly improve the audio quality of their wireless earbuds by manually adjusting Bluetooth codecs, applying custom equalizer settings, and carefully managing spatial audio features on Android.

### Key Points
- Enable high-resolution Bluetooth codecs like LDAC or LHDC via Android's Developer options to bypass the bitrate limitations of standard SBC or AAC.
- Use the Wavelet app to apply AutoEQ presets, which adjust frequency response curves to match the scientifically backed Harman Curve.
- Access Developer options by tapping the build number seven times in the Android Settings menu to unlock advanced Bluetooth audio codec selection.
- Evaluate spatial audio and Dolby Atmos settings carefully, as these features can sometimes reduce audio detail compared to standard stereo playback.
- Ensure streaming services are configured for lossless audio to maintain high-fidelity quality throughout the entire playback chain.

### Why it Matters
Optimizing these hidden software settings allows users to extract superior performance from their existing hardware without purchasing new equipment. By moving beyond default manufacturer profiles, listeners can achieve a personalized audio experience that better aligns with their specific hearing preferences and high-resolution source material.
