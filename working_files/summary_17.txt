Indian stocks and rupee fall on global selloff, further escalation in West Asia
============================================================

Indian stock markets and the rupee declined on Monday as rising geopolitical tensions in West Asia and strong US employment data dampened global investor sentiment and increased crude prices.

### Key Points
- The Nifty 50 and BSE Sensex both fell by 0.8% during early Monday trading sessions.
- Brent crude prices climbed above $96 per barrel following the escalation of conflict between Iran and Israel.
- US non-farm payrolls added 172,000 jobs, leading markets to price in a 98% probability of the Federal Reserve holding interest rates steady.
- Major IT stocks including Wipro, TCS, and LTM dropped between 2% and 5% amid a broader global sell-off in the technology sector.
- Asian indices, including the Nikkei 225 and Kospi, recorded losses ranging from 1% to 5% in early trade.

### Why it Matters
The combination of geopolitical instability and robust US labor data suggests that interest rates will remain elevated for longer than previously anticipated. This shift in expectations creates significant headwinds for emerging markets like India, which rely on foreign capital inflows to sustain growth.
