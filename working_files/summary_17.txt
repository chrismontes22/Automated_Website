Browser Fingerprinting – How websites track you across internet –without cookies
============================================================

Browser fingerprinting is a sophisticated tracking technique that identifies users by collecting unique technical device data, operating silently in the background without relying on traditional cookies or user consent.

### Key Points
- Fingerprinting scripts aggregate data like GPU details, installed fonts, screen resolution, and browser versions to create a mathematically unique identifier for each device.
- Techniques such as canvas, WebGL, and audio probing extract hardware-specific characteristics that remain consistent even in incognito mode or after clearing browser history.
- While ad tech companies use these profiles for behavioral tracking, banks and security platforms utilize the same data for fraud detection and bot prevention.
- A VPN does not prevent fingerprinting because it only masks an IP address, leaving the underlying hardware and software signals fully exposed to tracking scripts.
- Privacy-focused browsers like Brave, Firefox, and Tor offer varying levels of protection by either randomizing signals or standardizing device data to make users appear identical.

### Why it Matters
Browser fingerprinting represents a significant shift in digital privacy because it targets inherent device characteristics that users cannot easily delete or reset. This creates a persistent tracking method that challenges current regulatory frameworks and necessitates specialized browser configurations to maintain anonymity online.
