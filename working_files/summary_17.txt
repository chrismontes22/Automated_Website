Texas State Agency Breach Exposes Driver’s Licenses and Passports of 3 Million Residents
============================================================

The Texas Parks and Wildlife Department is notifying over three million residents that a third-party vendor data breach exposed sensitive personal information, including driver’s license and passport details.

### Key Points
- An unauthorized actor accessed a third-party vendor's systems, compromising personal data for more than three million individuals.
- Exposed information includes driver’s license numbers, passport details, email addresses, phone numbers, and residential addresses.
- The Texas Parks and Wildlife Department is offering one year of free credit monitoring to all affected residents.
- Texas Cyber Command is currently conducting an investigation to determine the full scope of the security incident.
- The agency has implemented additional security measures and is working with the vendor to prevent future data breaches.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the significant security risks associated with third-party data management for large government agencies. It underscores the growing necessity for organizations to enforce rigorous cybersecurity standards across their entire supply chain to protect sensitive citizen information.
