Apple and Google are launching new AI agents this June that interact with websites differently, forcing developers to prepare for automated visitors that can now perform tasks by default.

### Key Points
- Apple’s updated Siri, launching this fall, focuses on reading web content to answer user queries and performing actions within specific mobile applications.
- Google’s Chrome Auto-Browse, arriving on Android in late June, acts as an agent that navigates websites to complete tasks like filling forms and booking appointments.
- Both platforms utilize models from the Gemini family, though they deploy these technologies through distinct user interfaces and operational strategies.
- Google’s Auto-Browse is enabled by default at the operating-system level, significantly increasing the scale of automated traffic interacting with web forms and checkout flows.
- Website owners are advised to test their high-value task flows against non-human navigation to ensure compatibility with automated agents.

### Why it Matters
The shift from opt-in AI tools to default, system-level agents marks a fundamental change in how websites must be optimized for non-human traffic. Businesses that fail to test their site's functionality against these automated visitors risk losing conversions as AI agents begin performing tasks on behalf of users at a massive scale.
