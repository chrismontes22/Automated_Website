Pros and cons of using a VPN
============================================================

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) enhances online privacy by encrypting internet traffic and masking IP addresses, though users must weigh these security benefits against potential connection speed trade-offs.

### Key Points
- VPNs protect sensitive data on public Wi-Fi and allow users to bypass geo-restrictions for streaming or accessing censored content.
- Encryption and routing through remote servers can increase latency and reduce overall internet connection speeds.
- Reliable providers typically require paid subscriptions, as free services may log user data, serve intrusive ads, or offer weak encryption.
- VPNs do not provide total anonymity and cannot protect users from phishing, malware, or voluntary data sharing on websites.
- Legal restrictions on VPN usage exist in countries including China, Russia, Iran, Belarus, and North Korea.
- Recommended features for selecting a provider include WireGuard or OpenVPN protocols, verified no-logs policies, and a kill switch.

### Why it Matters
Understanding the limitations of VPNs is essential because they are not a universal solution for online security or total anonymity. Users must balance the need for privacy against costs and performance impacts to determine if a VPN is necessary for their specific digital habits.
