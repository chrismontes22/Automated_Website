Earning taste and judgment
============================================================

The rise of AI agents is automating entry-level software development tasks, forcing junior engineers to prioritize developing professional judgment and technical taste over routine coding and boilerplate work.

### Key Points
- Entry-level tech roles are declining, with recent computer science graduates facing unemployment rates as high as 7.5% as of March 2026.
- Data from the Indeed Hiring Lab shows a 34% drop in junior tech job postings since 2020, while demand for senior roles remains stable.
- Research indicates that humans often accept incorrect AI-generated code 80% of the time, highlighting the danger of over-reliance on automated tools.
- Experts recommend that developers focus on "the last mile" of projects, which involves complex architecture, edge cases, and critical decision-making that AI cannot replicate.
- Building a public track record and solving difficult, non-routine problems are identified as essential strategies for career longevity in an AI-driven market.

### Why it Matters
The automation of entry-level tasks threatens the traditional training pipeline that allows junior developers to mature into senior roles. While AI increases overall productivity, it creates a "paradox of supervision" where workers must possess the very skills they are no longer practicing to effectively verify machine output.
