10 Monday AM Reads
============================================================

Economists are raising long-term inflation forecasts as geopolitical conflicts persist, while major publishers consider blocking Google search indexing to protect their content and revenue models from shifting digital trends.

### Key Points
- Economists have lowered recession probabilities but warn that ongoing conflicts in Iran are embedding higher, more stubborn inflation into the global economy.
- Major media publishers are evaluating plans to opt out of Google search indexing to reclaim control over their content and address broken search economics.
- Forterra has deployed over 100 autonomous ground vehicles in Ukraine over the past nine months, marking a significant milestone in combat-ready AI technology.
- Foreign state actors from China, Russia, and Iran are reportedly exploiting local environmental concerns to slow the development of U.S. artificial intelligence data centers.
- Only two companies from the 1976 Dow Jones Industrial Average remain in the index today, highlighting the rapid evolution of the modern corporate landscape.

### Why it Matters
These developments signal a period of significant structural change across global finance, defense, and digital media. As businesses and governments navigate these shifts, the intersection of geopolitical instability and technological disruption is forcing a reevaluation of long-term economic and security strategies.
