What managing partners should ask AI vendors before signing any contract
============================================================

TMF Group’s Kumar Ravi advises managing partners to prioritize rigorous identity management and vendor accountability when integrating AI tools to mitigate silent, long-term security risks in professional services.

### Key Points
- Over-privileged access and weak workflow controls are identified as more dangerous than ransomware due to their cumulative, unnoticed impact on data confidentiality.
- Firms must treat vendors as integrated supply chain partners, requiring full disclosure of sub-processors and periodic due diligence beyond initial questionnaires.
- Before adopting AI-native tools, firms should verify if data is used for model training and confirm specific jurisdictional laws and deletion processes.
- Security should be elevated to a board-level business control, supported by consistent measurement and independent assurance to ensure organizational resilience.
- Contracts must explicitly define security obligations, incident-notification timelines, and liability terms to prevent accountability gaps during third-party engagements.

### Why it Matters
Professional services firms often struggle to balance legal privilege with the need for rapid threat intelligence sharing, which can leave them vulnerable to evolving cyber threats. By treating security as a core business function rather than an IT issue, leadership can better protect client data and maintain regulatory compliance in an increasingly complex digital supply chain.
