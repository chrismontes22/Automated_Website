The 400-year-old lesson for SpaceX investors: Chart of the Day
============================================================

Market analysts are comparing a potential SpaceX initial public offering to the historic Dutch East India Company, highlighting the long-term challenges of valuing high-growth, frontier-focused business models.

### Key Points
- Market technician JC Parets suggests SpaceX mirrors the Dutch East India Company by offering investors exposure to a massive, emerging commercial frontier.
- Data from University of Florida professor Jay Ritter shows 60% of IPOs between 1975 and 2021 finished flat or lower three years after their debut.
- Historical analysis indicates that major IPOs, such as US Steel, Ford, and Rivian, often launch near significant market peaks, increasing short-term volatility risks.
- Expert Kathy Donnelly notes that most IPOs experience price declines within the first ten weeks, suggesting that immediate investment may not be necessary.

### Why it Matters
A potential SpaceX public offering represents a significant test for investors attempting to balance long-term growth potential against the historical tendency for major IPOs to underperform shortly after launch. Understanding these patterns helps market participants manage expectations regarding entry timing and the inherent risks of investing in companies tied to emerging infrastructure sectors.
