Canada Is Forging Ahead with Its Dangerous Surveillance Bill
============================================================

Canada is fast-tracking Bill C-22, a controversial legislative proposal that would mandate metadata retention, expand foreign information sharing, and require tech companies to create encryption backdoors for government surveillance.

### Key Points
- Bill C-22, also known as the Lawful Access Bill, is currently moving toward a final vote before the government's June 19 deadline.
- The legislation grants the Ministry of Public Safety authority to demand that companies bypass encryption protocols to facilitate law enforcement access.
- Major technology firms, including Apple, Google, and Signal, have expressed strong opposition, warning that the bill could force them to restrict or terminate services in Canada.
- Organizations such as the Canadian Civil Liberties Association and Citizen Lab have criticized the bill for its significant privacy risks and lack of independent debate.
- Advocacy group OpenMedia has launched a campaign allowing citizens to contact their elected representatives to voice concerns regarding the proposed surveillance measures.

### Why it Matters
The passage of Bill C-22 could fundamentally alter the digital privacy landscape for millions of Canadians by weakening standard encryption protections. If enacted, the legislation may force global technology providers to choose between complying with invasive government mandates or withdrawing their services from the Canadian market entirely.
