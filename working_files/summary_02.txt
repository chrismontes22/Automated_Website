Ken Griffin's talent machine is getting bigger with its most competitive intern class ever
============================================================

Citadel and Citadel Securities have launched their largest internship class to date, selecting over 350 students from a record-breaking pool of more than 115,900 global applicants this year.

### Key Points
- Citadel and Citadel Securities accepted only 0.36% of applicants for their 2024 summer internship program.
- Interns receive weekly base salaries ranging from $4,300 to $5,800, plus signing bonuses and a $15,000 housing stipend.
- Recruitment focused on AI fluency and judgment, with candidates evaluated on their ability to leverage new technology for complex problem-solving.
- The firms are expanding their talent pipeline to compete with major banks and AI labs for top quantitative finance professionals.
- Interns work on projects with real business implications and are evaluated by leadership, including Ken Griffin and CEO Peng Zhao.

### Why it Matters
Hedge funds are aggressively scaling their entry-level hiring to secure top-tier quantitative talent amidst increasing competition from the artificial intelligence sector. This shift highlights how major financial institutions are integrating AI tools into their workflows while prioritizing human judgment and adaptability as essential competitive advantages.
