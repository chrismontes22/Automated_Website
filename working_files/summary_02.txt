iPhone-Android RCS messaging finally gets the security upgrade we’ve been waiting for
============================================================

Apple has introduced end-to-end encryption for cross-platform RCS messaging between iPhone and Android devices in the latest iOS 26.5 release candidate, significantly improving communication security for all users.

### Key Points
- Apple’s iOS 26.5 release candidate officially adds end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for RCS conversations between iPhone and Android platforms.
- A new encryption toggle will be enabled by default within the Messages settings menu to prevent third-party interception of text messages.
- Senior Engineering Manager Emad Omara confirmed the rollout of interoperable E2EE RCS via the latest software update.
- The feature is currently in the release candidate phase and is expected to reach the general public in the stable branch within days.

### Why it Matters
This update resolves a long-standing security gap by enabling private, encrypted communication between the two most popular mobile operating systems. By standardizing E2EE across platforms, Apple and Google are significantly reducing the risk of data interception for millions of daily users.
