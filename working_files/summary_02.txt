There is a shadow hanging over this Fable thing
============================================================

The United States government has ordered Anthropic to disable access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 AI models for all foreign nationals, citing national security concerns.

### Key Points
- The directive requires Anthropic to block access for any foreign national, including those residing within the United States and the company's own employees.
- Anthropic complied with the order on Friday evening, effectively removing the models from public availability for all users.
- The government claims the models possess vulnerabilities that could be exploited, though Anthropic argues these capabilities are common among other publicly available AI systems.
- This action follows a history of strained relations between the current administration and Anthropic, raising questions about the political motivations behind the enforcement.
- The sudden restriction has sparked industry concerns regarding the future of open-access, high-performance AI models and potential government overreach in the technology sector.

### Why it Matters
This move sets a significant precedent for government intervention in the AI industry, signaling that powerful models may increasingly be treated as restricted national security assets rather than commercial products. By unilaterally cutting off access, the government has introduced new regulatory risks that could threaten the multi-trillion-dollar infrastructure investments currently fueling the global AI boom.
