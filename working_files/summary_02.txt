Media Briefing: The new World Cup SEO playbook: What’s in and what’s out
============================================================

Publishers are evolving their World Cup coverage strategies to prioritize direct audience relationships and multiplatform video content as AI-driven search changes traditional traffic patterns for major sporting events.

### Key Points
- Publishers are shifting focus from search-based traffic to direct channels like email newsletters, mobile apps, and podcasts to ensure long-term audience retention.
- Video-first strategies on platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram are being used to capture engagement that is increasingly moving away from traditional news websites.
- Data from the Reuters Institute shows that 54% of global audiences now prefer social and video platforms over news sites for information consumption.
- Media companies are integrating AI tools to automate breaking news workflows and create personalized experiences, such as chatbots, to keep readers engaged.
- Despite the rise of AI summaries, publishers continue to produce "commodity content" like scores and schedules to maintain SEO authority and utility for their audiences.

### Why it Matters
The decline of guaranteed search traffic forces publishers to move beyond simple pageview metrics and invest in sustainable, direct-to-consumer digital ecosystems. By diversifying their distribution and fostering loyal communities, media organizations aim to protect their revenue models against the ongoing disruption caused by AI-powered search and social media platforms.
