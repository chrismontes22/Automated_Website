Tokenmaxxing is so over. It's all about modelmaxxing now.
============================================================

Companies are shifting away from "tokenmaxxing" by adopting model switching, a cost-saving strategy that routes complex tasks to premium AI models while offloading simpler work to cheaper alternatives.

### Key Points
- Tech companies are moving from unlimited AI usage to strategic "token hygiene" to manage rising API costs and budget constraints.
- Professionals are using design-first workflows and model routing tools to optimize performance and reduce unnecessary token consumption.
- Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong predicts that 80% of AI workloads will transition to significantly cheaper models within the next 12 to 18 months.
- Adoption of model routing platforms, such as OpenRouter and Rayline, has increased from 1% to 5% of firms over the past year.
- Behavioral economists note that strict token budgets create a scarcity mindset, encouraging users to be more selective about which AI models they employ.

### Why it Matters
This transition signals a maturing AI market where efficiency and cost-optimization are replacing the initial "hype-driven" phase of unrestricted usage. By strategically matching model intelligence to task complexity, businesses can maintain high productivity while significantly reducing their operational overhead.
