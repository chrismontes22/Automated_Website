I switched to an open-source notes app for 2 weeks, and I wish I had done it sooner
============================================================

Notesnook has emerged as a compelling alternative to Simplenote, offering users enhanced privacy through end-to-end encryption, rich-media support, and hierarchical organization tools across all major desktop and mobile platforms.

### Key Points
- Notesnook provides end-to-end encryption for all notes, ensuring data remains private on devices, in transit, and on company servers.
- The platform features a robust free plan that includes cross-device syncing and a fully functional offline mode for editing and searching.
- Users can organize information using a hierarchical system of notebooks and sub-notebooks, which supports rich-media attachments like PDFs and images.
- The app includes a web clipper for Firefox and Chromium browsers that allows for precise text selection and saving of online content.
- Notable limitations include a lack of raw Markdown support on the free tier and the absence of cross-device synchronization for note revision history.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward privacy-focused productivity tools reflects a growing user demand for secure, feature-rich alternatives to traditional note-taking applications. By balancing encryption with versatile organizational features, Notesnook challenges established market players while highlighting the trade-offs between lightweight simplicity and comprehensive data management.
