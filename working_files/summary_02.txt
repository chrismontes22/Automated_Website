Markets move to price in rate hikes as inflation fears and geopolitics reshape Fed expectations
============================================================

Financial markets are shifting from anticipating Federal Reserve interest rate cuts to pricing in potential hikes as energy-led inflation concerns and Middle East geopolitical tensions reshape economic expectations.

**Key points**
- CME FedWatch Tool data indicates a 30% probability that the federal funds rate will exceed the current 3.50%-3.75% range by year-end.
- Brent Crude oil prices have surged from $70 to $111 per barrel following the escalation of Middle East tensions in late February.
- The 10-year U.S. Treasury yield has climbed to 4.40%, up from below 4% just weeks ago.
- Core inflation remains at 2.5% year-over-year, consistently exceeding the Federal Reserve’s 2% target since April 2021.
- The Nasdaq has entered correction territory, falling more than 10% from its 2026 highs, while gold prices have declined by approximately 20%.

**Why it matters: **
The rapid pivot in interest rate expectations signals a significant cooling of investor sentiment as persistent inflation threatens to keep borrowing costs elevated. This shift forces a reassessment of asset valuations across equities and commodities, highlighting the sensitivity of global markets to energy price volatility and geopolitical instability.
