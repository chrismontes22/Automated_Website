ZeroTier Quantum RC2 brings post-quantum security closer to general availability
============================================================

ZeroTier has released the second candidate for its Quantum networking platform, advancing post-quantum security standards to protect enterprise and government data against future decryption threats from quantum computing.

### Key Points
- ZeroTier Quantum RC2 meets the U.S. government’s CNSA 2.0 standards for post-quantum cryptography.
- The platform utilizes ML-KEM-1024 and hybrid AES-384 encryption to defend against "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks.
- Built in Rust, the architecture features a peer-to-peer mesh design that reduces CPU utilization by 30% compared to similar networking solutions.
- The software supports diverse environments, including public clouds, air-gapped systems, and edge or IoT devices.
- Final commercial availability will follow a third-party code audit and penetration testing phase.

### Why it Matters
- This release provides organizations with a proactive defense against the long-term threat of quantum-enabled decryption of sensitive data. By aligning with federal security mandates, ZeroTier enables critical infrastructure and defense sectors to secure their networks against future vulnerabilities today.
