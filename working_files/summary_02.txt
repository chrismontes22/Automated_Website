Top Stories: iOS 26.5 Coming Soon, Goodbye $599 Mac Mini, and More
============================================================

Apple is preparing for WWDC by releasing iOS 26.5, while simultaneously navigating supply chain constraints for the Mac mini and managing record-breaking consumer demand for the new MacBook Neo.

### Key Points
- iOS 26.5 introduces end-to-end encryption for RCS messaging, location-based "Suggested Places" in Apple Maps, and a new Pride Luminance wallpaper.
- The Mac mini starting price increased to $799 after Apple discontinued the 256GB storage model due to ongoing industry-wide memory shortages.
- CEO Tim Cook reported "off the charts" demand for the MacBook Neo, leading to potential production adjustments for the A18 Pro chips.
- Reports suggest the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro will retain the controversial anodized aluminum finish despite user complaints regarding surface chipping and durability.
- Leaks indicate the standard iPhone 18 and the lower-cost iPhone 18e will share interchangeable components, signaling a convergence in their manufacturing specifications.

### Why it Matters
These updates highlight Apple's ongoing struggle to balance high consumer demand for entry-level hardware with persistent global component shortages. Investors and users should monitor these supply chain shifts, as they are directly influencing product pricing and the availability of Apple's most popular devices.
