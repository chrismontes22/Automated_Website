Faster and further reaching — NordVPN quietly brings its obfuscated servers to its in-house censorship-resistant protocol
============================================================

NordVPN has transitioned its obfuscated server infrastructure from OpenVPN to the proprietary NordWhisper protocol, aiming to provide users with faster connection speeds and access to more global server locations.

### Key Points
- NordVPN replaced OpenVPN with its in-house NordWhisper protocol for all obfuscated server connections.
- The update is currently live for iOS users, with Android, Windows, and macOS support arriving in the coming weeks.
- NordWhisper uses web tunnel technology to disguise VPN traffic as standard internet activity to bypass restrictive firewalls.
- The shift is designed to reduce performance overhead, resulting in faster speeds compared to the previous OpenVPN-based obfuscation.
- The transition expands the number of available server locations for users attempting to bypass network censorship.

### Why it Matters
This transition represents a significant shift in how VPN providers manage traffic to bypass sophisticated network filtering and deep packet inspection. By moving to a proprietary protocol, NordVPN aims to improve user experience by balancing high-level stealth with the faster performance speeds typically associated with modern, non-obfuscated connections.
