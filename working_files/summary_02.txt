Meet the crypto billionaires building a world where money buys you a vote
============================================================

The Free Republic of Liberland, a self-proclaimed micronation on the Danube, serves as a testing ground for tech billionaires aiming to replace traditional democratic governance with blockchain-based systems.

### Key Points
- Liberland is a disputed territory between Serbia and Croatia, founded by Vít Jedlička as a libertarian, tax-free state governed by cryptocurrency tokens.
- Chinese crypto entrepreneur Justin Sun, who serves as Liberland’s prime minister, has invested heavily in the project alongside other tech billionaires.
- Governance in Liberland is tied to "Liberland Merits," a digital token system that grants voting power proportional to an individual's financial investment.
- The project draws inspiration from "Dark Enlightenment" philosophy, which advocates for replacing democratic nation-states with corporate-style, blockchain-run entities.
- Similar initiatives, such as Prospera in Honduras and Tim Draper’s digital nation projects, reflect a broader movement among tech elites to decentralize state authority.

### Why it Matters
These projects represent a growing effort by influential tech figures to shift political power from public institutions to private, code-based systems controlled by wealthy shareholders. By lobbying heavily in the U.S. and funding alternative governance models, these investors are testing whether blockchain technology can effectively bypass traditional democratic oversight and taxation.
