Indonesia’s jailing of Gojek founder raises fears for investor confidence
============================================================

**Former Indonesian education minister and Gojek cofounder Nadiem Makarim was sentenced to 10 years in prison for corruption involving a government contract for Google Chromebook laptops.**

### Key Points
- Nadiem Makarim was convicted on June 30 of abusing his authority to favor Google in a $120 million procurement deal for over 1 million school laptops.
- Prosecutors alleged the laptops were unsuitable for remote areas, causing significant state losses during the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Critics and some legal experts suggest the trial may be politically motivated, while others argue the verdict demonstrates judicial independence.
- Google, which was not indicted, denied providing inducements to secure the government contract.
- The conviction has sparked debate among analysts regarding Indonesia's legal certainty and its long-term appeal to foreign investors.

### Why it Matters
The imprisonment of a prominent tech entrepreneur and former minister highlights growing concerns over the stability of Indonesia's business environment and the transparency of its judicial system. This case serves as a critical test for investor confidence, forcing global stakeholders to weigh the risks of political volatility against the country's potential as a major emerging market.
