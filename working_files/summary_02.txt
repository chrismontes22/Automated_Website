OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft, and Amazon are behind a new organization that aims to help prepare workers for AI
============================================================

Major tech companies including OpenAI, Anthropic, Amazon, and Microsoft have launched the nonprofit Raise US, securing $500 million to help state governments prepare workforces for AI-driven labor market disruptions.

### Key Points
- Raise US aims to raise $1 billion to provide governors with policy guidance and AI-powered career navigation tools for their residents.
- Former Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo and former Indiana Governor Eric Holcomb are leading the organization’s efforts.
- Initial state partnerships include Arkansas, Connecticut, Maryland, and Utah, focusing on personalized learning and workforce development models.
- The organization’s advisory board features prominent figures from labor, finance, and politics, including AFL-CIO President Liz Shuler and Blackstone CEO Steve Schwarzman.
- The initiative seeks to implement real-world policy pilots, such as wage insurance and short-time compensation, to mitigate potential job displacement.

### Why it Matters
This initiative represents a significant effort by the private sector to proactively address concerns regarding AI-driven job displacement and public skepticism toward automation. By partnering directly with state governments, the organization aims to create scalable workforce models that could stabilize the labor market as AI adoption accelerates.
