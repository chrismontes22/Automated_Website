iOS 27 Beta 2 Adds Inline Replies to iPhone-to-Android RCS Chats
============================================================

Apple’s second beta of iOS 27 introduces enhanced RCS messaging features, including support for message threading and improved emoji reactions when communicating with Android users across different mobile platforms.

### Key Points
- Users can now long-press messages to reply directly to specific texts within RCS conversations, mirroring existing iMessage functionality.
- Tapback reactions now display as actual emojis on images and videos instead of text descriptors.
- RCS functionality requires both the sender and recipient to use compatible smartphones and carriers.
- The update builds upon previous RCS improvements, such as the end-to-end encryption introduced in iOS 26.5.
- iOS 27 is currently available to developers, with a public beta scheduled for July and a full release expected in September.

### Why it Matters
These updates significantly bridge the feature gap between iMessage and cross-platform RCS messaging, creating a more seamless communication experience for iPhone and Android users. By standardizing reactions and threading, Apple is reducing the friction often associated with mixed-ecosystem text conversations.
