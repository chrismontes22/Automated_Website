Your VPN is probably doing way more than you need it to — here's what actually matters
============================================================

Selecting a reliable Virtual Private Network requires looking past marketing buzzwords to evaluate technical architecture, independent security audits, and the legal jurisdiction of the service provider.

### Key Points
- Prioritize providers that use RAM-only, diskless servers to ensure data is wiped automatically upon reboot.
- Verify "no-logs" claims by checking for independent third-party audits of server configurations and code.
- Choose companies headquartered outside of the 5, 9, or 14 Eyes intelligence-sharing alliances to minimize legal data exposure.
- Ensure the VPN supports full IPv6 tunneling or blocks IPv6 traffic to prevent location leaks that trigger streaming service proxy errors.
- Recognize that VPNs are not tools for total anonymity, as logging into personal accounts or using cookies still links activity to your identity.

### Why it Matters
Understanding the technical realities of VPNs helps users avoid ineffective services that rely on misleading marketing rather than robust security protocols. By focusing on verifiable architecture and jurisdiction, consumers can better protect their data and maintain consistent access to restricted digital content.
