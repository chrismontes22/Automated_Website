Inside China's top computer science university training the next generation of AI engineers
============================================================

Students at Tsinghua University, China’s premier computer science institution, endure an intense, high-pressure academic culture to secure elite roles and lead the nation’s rapidly expanding artificial intelligence sector.

### Key Points
- Tsinghua University is China’s most selective school, with an admission rate of approximately 0.05% for Gaokao test-takers.
- Students frequently adopt a "996" work schedule, often working seven days a week to manage research, coding, and multiple thesis projects.
- Alumni have founded over 1,000 companies, including more than a dozen unicorns, and frequently secure positions at major firms like Huawei, ByteDance, and Tencent.
- Coding is treated as a baseline skill across disciplines, with students increasingly integrating AI tools to accelerate research and development.
- The university serves as a primary pipeline for China’s AI ambitions, channeling top talent into strategically important technological fields.

### Why it Matters
Tsinghua’s rigorous environment acts as a critical engine for China’s global competitiveness in artificial intelligence and high-tech innovation. By producing a steady stream of elite researchers and entrepreneurs, the university directly influences the trajectory of the nation's digital economy and its ability to challenge international tech leaders.
