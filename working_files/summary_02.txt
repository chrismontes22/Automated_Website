From Polestar to OnePlus to DJI, the US is becoming a harder place for globally influential brands
============================================================

Polestar, OnePlus, and DJI are retreating from the United States market due to a combination of regulatory pressures, political scrutiny, and the high costs of competing against domestic incumbents.

### Key Points
- Polestar exited the US market after the Commerce Department denied authorization under the Connected Vehicle Rule due to its Chinese ownership.
- OnePlus ceased US smartphone sales, citing the high costs of navigating a market dominated by powerful carrier gatekeepers and established incumbents.
- DJI faces ongoing challenges from its placement on the government’s Entity List, which restricts access to US technology and complicates its long-term operations.
- These departures reflect a broader trend where global hardware brands increasingly view the US as a high-risk, low-reward environment for product launches.

### Why it Matters
The departure of these major brands limits consumer choice and reduces the competitive pressure on domestic companies to innovate. By isolating the US market from global alternatives, the country risks a technological stagnation where incumbents face fewer challenges to their market dominance.
