Orbia CISO Miranda Ritchie on building security into sustainable infrastructure
============================================================

Orbia CISO Miranda Ritchie explains how the company integrates cybersecurity into industrial infrastructure to protect physical processes, human safety, and environmental sustainability across its global manufacturing operations.

### Key Points
- Orbia operates over 100 production sites in more than 50 countries, managing diverse industrial systems including chemical production and precision agriculture.
- The company prioritizes a "safety-first" culture, treating cyber incidents as potential threats to physical equipment, human health, and the environment.
- Ritchie emphasizes moving away from the "trusted network" assumption by implementing zero-trust, network segmentation, and secure remote access for legacy hardware.
- Cyber teams are embedded early in project design phases to ensure secure-by-design principles are applied before new technologies are deployed.
- Orbia recently conducted its first operational technology (OT) cyber incident tabletop exercise to align leadership on the intersection of security and business resilience.

### Why it Matters
Integrating cybersecurity into industrial operations is critical for companies managing physical infrastructure where digital failures can cause real-world environmental or safety disasters. By treating security as a core component of sustainability and operational resilience, organizations can scale new technologies faster while mitigating the risks associated with aging hardware and distributed networks.
