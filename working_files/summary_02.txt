iPhone Fold is ‘no doubt’ coming later than iPhone 18 Pro, says Gurman
============================================================

Apple is expected to delay the shipping of its first foldable iPhone until late 2025, positioning the device to arrive after the launch of the standard iPhone 18 Pro.

**Key points**
- Bloomberg’s Mark Gurman confirmed the foldable iPhone will likely ship later than the iPhone 18 Pro due to complex display manufacturing requirements.
- While Apple is expected to unveil the device in September, shipping could slip into October, November, or December.
- Analysts from Barclays previously suggested a potential December release date for the new foldable form factor.
- The delay mirrors previous Apple product rollouts, such as the iPhone X, which faced staggered availability following its initial announcement.

**Why it matters: **
This staggered release highlights the significant engineering challenges Apple faces when introducing complex new hardware designs to the mass market. The delay may influence consumer purchasing cycles and Apple's ability to compete immediately with existing foldable devices from rivals.
