Some bitcoin indicators are still going the wrong way, challenging the bullish $70,000 holdout story
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
Bitcoin maintains a price near $70,000 despite geopolitical instability, though cooling institutional ETF inflows and a negative Coinbase Premium suggest potential weakness in underlying market demand.

**Key points**
- Bitcoin continues to trade around $70,000 despite rising oil prices and reduced expectations for Federal Reserve interest rate cuts.
- The Coinbase Premium has reached its most negative level in over a month, signaling softer demand from U.S.-based institutional investors.
- Net inflows into 11 U.S.-listed spot bitcoin ETFs have slowed significantly, totaling only $195 million since the middle of the month.
- Data from Coinglass shows bitcoin currently trades at a discount on Coinbase compared to the offshore exchange Binance.
- Analysts note that while institutional interest remains, the current accumulation phase is less linear and more selective than previous market cycles.

**Why it matters**
The divergence between Bitcoin’s price resilience and weakening institutional indicators suggests that current market momentum may be fragile. Investors are closely watching these metrics to determine if the asset can sustain its current valuation without consistent, high-volume inflows.
