Why did Anthropic block Claude OpenClaw use? #tech
============================================================

Anthropic has restricted Claude Pro and Max subscription coverage for third-party agent tools like OpenClaw, requiring users to pay additional fees for these specific external integrations starting this April.

### Key Points
- Anthropic updated its subscription policy to exclude third-party agent frameworks from flat-rate pricing models.
- OpenClaw is the first major integration impacted by the company's new capacity and cost-management strategy.
- Users must now pay extra beyond their standard monthly subscription to maintain access to these external agent tools.
- The company cited the need to manage operational loads and compute costs as the primary driver for this policy shift.

### Why it Matters
This change signals that AI companies are increasingly unwilling to absorb the high compute costs associated with autonomous agent workflows under standard subscription tiers. For businesses, this shift increases the total cost of ownership and creates uncertainty for developers relying on flexible, third-party tool integrations.
