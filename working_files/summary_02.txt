I didn't understand the RCS hype until I tried these features
============================================================

The widespread adoption of Rich Communication Services (RCS) across iOS and Android devices is finally replacing outdated SMS and MMS standards with modern, secure, and feature-rich messaging capabilities.

### Key Points
- RCS supports high-quality media sharing with file limits exceeding 100MB, eliminating the pixelation common with legacy MMS standards.
- Users can now edit messages within 15 minutes of sending or unsend them before the recipient opens the chat.
- The standard introduces reliable delivery and read receipts, providing confirmation that messages have reached the recipient.
- Cross-platform reactions now display correctly between iPhone and Android users instead of appearing as separate, disruptive text notifications.
- RCS enables end-to-end encryption and allows messaging over Wi-Fi or cellular data, improving both security and connectivity.

### Why it Matters
The transition to RCS marks a significant improvement in cross-platform communication by bridging the long-standing functional gap between iPhone and Android users. By standardizing these features, the industry is effectively phasing out insecure legacy protocols in favor of a more reliable, modern messaging experience for all mobile consumers.
