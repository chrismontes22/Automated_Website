Android's big June Drop keeps kids safe this summer, adds 'Fake Call' protections for phones
============================================================

Google is rolling out its June Android Drop, introducing new scam-detection tools, enhanced safety features for children, and expanded file-sharing compatibility between Android devices and iPhones.

### Key Points
- The Phone by Google app is adding "Fake Call Detection" to warn users when an incoming call may be a scammer spoofing a known contact.
- Children aged 13 and under will gain access to the Personal Safety app, allowing them to display medical information and emergency contacts on their lock screens.
- New safety features for minors include Car Crash detection, real-time location sharing, and Safety Check functionality.
- Google is expanding Quick Share support to more Android devices, enabling offline file transfers to iOS devices.
- Fake Call Detection requires Android 12 or higher and will trigger a red alert on the call screen if a caller's identity cannot be verified.

### Why it Matters
These updates strengthen the Android ecosystem by addressing critical security concerns regarding identity theft and child safety. By bridging the file-sharing gap between Android and iOS, Google continues to improve cross-platform interoperability for its users.
