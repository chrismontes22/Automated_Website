I thought I owned my Kindle books — I was wrong
============================================================

Digital storefronts like Amazon Kindle provide users with revocable licenses rather than permanent ownership, meaning purchased e-books can be removed from personal libraries without warning or financial recourse.

### Key Points
- Digital purchases are governed by conditional licensing agreements rather than traditional property rights, preventing users from selling or gifting e-books.
- Digital Rights Management (DRM) software allows retailers to remotely delete content from user devices if licensing agreements with publishers expire or change.
- Amazon restricts access to files through proprietary encryption, such as KFX-ZIP, and limits options for transferring content via USB.
- Consumers can secure permanent ownership by purchasing DRM-free files from independent platforms like Smashwords, StoryBundle, or Baen Books.
- Third-party tools like Epubor Ultimate or AnyEBook Converter can be used to decrypt and back up existing digital libraries to personal storage drives.

### Why it Matters
This shift from ownership to licensing creates significant risks for consumers who may lose their entire digital collection due to corporate policy changes. Understanding these limitations encourages users to prioritize DRM-free media to ensure long-term access to their purchased content.
