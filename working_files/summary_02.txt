What happens when your phone is confiscated at the airport
============================================================

Minnesota labor organizer Janette Zahia Corcelius is suing the Department of Homeland Security after Customs and Border Protection agents confiscated her phone upon her return from Europe.

### Key Points
- Janette Zahia Corcelius filed a federal lawsuit alleging that the seizure of her phone violates Fourth Amendment protections against unreasonable searches and seizures.
- The Council on American-Islamic Relations claims the government is systematically targeting political activists by using national security tools to monitor domestic dissent.
- Customs and Border Protection conducted 55,318 electronic device searches during the 2025 fiscal year, representing a 32 percent increase from 2023.
- Border agents utilize both basic inspections and advanced forensic tools, such as those produced by Cellebrite, to extract and analyze data from travelers' devices.
- Legal standards for border searches remain inconsistent, with conflicting court rulings creating a patchwork of regulations regarding the necessity of warrants across different U.S. jurisdictions.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing tension between national security powers at the border and the constitutional privacy rights of American citizens. The outcome could influence how federal agencies use terrorism designations to justify the surveillance and investigation of domestic political activists.
