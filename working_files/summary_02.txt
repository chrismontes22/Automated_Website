After a nearly 800% explosion, this AI supplier is about to make its U.S. debut and could signal if the market can still boom—or is headed for a bust
============================================================

South Korean chipmaker SK Hynix is preparing for a major Nasdaq listing following a 770% stock rally driven by its role as a primary high-bandwidth memory supplier for Nvidia.

### Key Points
- SK Hynix is expected to raise approximately $29 billion in its upcoming U.S. market debut this Friday.
- The company currently serves as the leading supplier of high-bandwidth memory chips essential for artificial intelligence development.
- Shares of the South Korean firm rose 770% over the past year, significantly outpacing the 700% growth seen by competitor Micron Technology.
- The company plans to invest hundreds of billions of dollars into two new production facilities in South Korea to meet rising global demand.
- Analysts at Bank of America have warned that current market speculation and high valuations may precede a significant downward correction.

### Why it Matters
The listing of SK Hynix serves as a critical barometer for the sustainability of the broader AI-driven market rally. Growing concerns over excessive debt and potential oversupply in the chip sector suggest that investor confidence may be shifting as the industry faces increasing volatility.
