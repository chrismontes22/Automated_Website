Walmart's people chief says these 10 jobs are still hot in the age of AI
============================================================

Walmart, the world's largest private employer, is prioritizing human labor alongside artificial intelligence by highlighting diverse career pathways and extensive internal training programs for its 2.1 million workers.

### Key Points
- Walmart currently lists nearly 41,000 active job openings with plans to fill an additional 273,000 future roles across its global operations.
- The company is promoting 10 high-demand career tracks, including data engineering, advertising sales, and supply chain management, with some roles offering six-figure salaries.
- Over 126,000 employees have utilized Walmart’s online education portal to earn credentials ranging from AI certifications to four-year college degrees.
- Internal re-skilling initiatives have successfully trained over 1,000 truck drivers and 600 skilled trades technicians, such as HVAC specialists and electricians.
- Despite integrating new technology, the retailer maintains that its service-driven business model requires a massive, evolving human workforce to sustain long-term growth.

### Why it Matters
Walmart’s commitment to human-centric growth provides a counter-narrative to industry trends where companies often use automation as a justification for reducing headcount. By investing in internal mobility and re-skilling, the retailer aims to stabilize its massive workforce while filling specialized roles that are increasingly critical to its digital and physical operations.
