Kevin O'Leary has entered a new villain era
============================================================

"Shark Tank" investor Kevin O'Leary has become a polarizing public face for AI infrastructure development as he faces significant community backlash over his proposed data center projects.

### Key Points
- Kevin O'Leary is developing massive AI data center campuses, including the $70 billion Wonder Valley project in Alberta and a planned facility in Utah.
- A March Gallup poll revealed that over 70% of Americans oppose having data centers built near their homes due to noise, water usage, and energy grid strain.
- Local opposition in Utah led to protests, lawsuits, and the defeat of political officials who initially supported the project, forcing O'Leary to halve the site's size.
- O'Leary argues that expanding domestic compute capacity is essential for national security and economic competition, often citing potential foreign influence as a driver of opposition.
- More than 1,400 data centers have been built or approved in the U.S. as of late last year, with major tech firms like Amazon and Meta leading the industry.

### Why it Matters
Data centers have become a flashpoint in the national debate over the environmental and social costs of the artificial intelligence boom. O'Leary's high-profile involvement highlights the growing tension between the urgent demand for AI infrastructure and the increasing resistance from local communities concerned about their quality of life.
