Samsung Messages is going away, so here are 5 the best messaging alternatives
============================================================

Samsung has officially announced it will discontinue its native Samsung Messages app by July 2026, prompting users to transition to alternative platforms like Google Messages for continued RCS support.

### Key Points
- Samsung will fully discontinue the Samsung Messages app in July 2026 and has already stopped pre-installing it on newer devices like the Galaxy S26 series.
- Google Messages is the recommended successor, serving as the primary Android application that supports Rich Communication Services (RCS) features.
- Users seeking alternatives can choose from platforms including WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal, or Instagram DMs, depending on their specific needs for privacy or features.
- While third-party SMS apps like Textra remain available, they lack the RCS functionality found in Google Messages.
- Samsung Messages will remain functional for standard texting until the official shutdown date, though it will be removed from the Galaxy Store afterward.

### Why it Matters
This transition marks a significant shift toward a unified messaging standard across the Android ecosystem by prioritizing Google Messages and RCS technology. Users must now evaluate their communication habits to choose a replacement that balances their requirements for cross-platform compatibility, encryption, and feature sets.
