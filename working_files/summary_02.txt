I'm Getting into Mesh Networks (Meshtastic, MeshCore, and Reticulum)
============================================================

Reticulum offers a decentralized, highly scalable alternative to traditional internet service providers by enabling seamless, encrypted communication across diverse physical networks like LoRa, Wi-Fi, and fiber optics.

### Key Points
- Reticulum functions as a versatile networking stack that intelligently routes data across heterogeneous connections without requiring central servers or proprietary gateways.
- Unlike Meshtastic and MeshCore, which are primarily limited to LoRa-based messaging, Reticulum supports global, interoperable mesh networks that can bridge different radio frequencies and internet protocols.
- The platform utilizes unique, encryption-based addressing, eliminating the need for central authorities like ARIN or IANA to manage network resources.
- Current applications for the Reticulum ecosystem include NomadNet and Sideband, which provide messaging, file sharing, and terminal-based browsing capabilities.
- A primary technical hurdle remains the lack of standalone, low-power firmware for remote LoRa nodes, though development of "microReticulum" aims to address this hardware limitation.

### Why it Matters
Reticulum provides a blueprint for building resilient, sovereign communication infrastructure that remains functional during internet outages or in regions with limited connectivity. By prioritizing open-source, decentralized routing, it offers a viable path toward a truly independent, user-owned network that is not subject to the censorship or fees of centralized service providers.
