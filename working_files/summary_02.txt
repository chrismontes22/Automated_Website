21 successful tech moguls who never graduated college
============================================================

Many prominent tech industry leaders, including Mark Zuckerberg and Sam Altman, have achieved massive success after leaving higher education to pursue entrepreneurial ventures and build global technology companies.

### Key Points
- Mark Zuckerberg left Harvard to launch Facebook, while Sam Altman dropped out of Stanford to cofound Loopt and later lead OpenAI.
- Microsoft cofounders Bill Gates and Paul Allen both exited university early to establish their software company in the mid-1970s.
- Stripe cofounders John and Patrick Collison and Canva CEO Melanie Perkins left their respective degree programs to focus on scaling their startups.
- Other notable dropouts include Steve Jobs (Apple), Daniel Ek (Spotify), Travis Kalanick (Uber), and Michael Dell (Dell).
- Recent tech figures like Polymarket CEO Shayne Coplan and Scale AI founder Alexandr Wang also bypassed traditional graduation to build AI and prediction market platforms.

### Why it Matters
The success of these executives highlights a growing debate regarding the necessity of a college degree for career advancement in the rapidly evolving technology sector. As AI and digital innovation reshape the workforce, these examples challenge traditional educational paths and emphasize the increasing value of practical experience and early-stage entrepreneurship.
