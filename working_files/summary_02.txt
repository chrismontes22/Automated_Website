Apple Seeds Revised iOS 26.5 and iPadOS 26.5 Betas to Developers
============================================================

Apple has released revised first beta versions of iOS 26.5 and iPadOS 26.5 to developers, introducing new features for Maps, RCS messaging, and third-party wearable integration.

### Key Points
- Apple released the updated beta software just four days after the initial version to address potential bugs.
- The Maps app now includes a Suggested Places feature that recommends nearby locations based on user search history and trends.
- Apple is retesting end-to-end encryption for RCS messaging between iPhone and Android devices after previously removing the feature.
- Developers in the European Union can test new support for third-party wearables, including notification forwarding, proximity pairing, and Live Activities.
- The current beta releases do not include new Siri capabilities, which are reportedly reserved for the upcoming iOS 27 update.

### Why it Matters
These updates signal Apple's ongoing efforts to refine cross-platform communication and comply with European Union regulations regarding third-party hardware interoperability. By testing these features early, the company aims to improve ecosystem functionality and user experience before the final public release.
