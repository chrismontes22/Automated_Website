The AI Agents That Actually Work Don’t Post On Social Media
============================================================

**While public attention focuses on social media AI theater, enterprise-grade autonomous agents are quietly managing high-stakes supplier negotiations and commercial contracts for major global corporations.**

### Key Points
- Pactum, founded in 2019, deploys autonomous agents that negotiate commercial terms within strict, pre-defined organizational guardrails.
- The company serves over 50 Fortune Global 2000 firms, including Walmart, Honeywell, and AstraZeneca, with one agent handling a single deal worth over $529 million.
- Pactum secured $54 million in Series C funding in June 2026, bringing its total capital raised to $109 million as transaction volumes grew 489% annually.
- Successful enterprise deployment relies on three pillars: layered governance, independent AI-based monitoring, and clearly defined, non-conflicting objectives.
- Security experts warn that autonomous agents can be manipulated by attackers to perform malicious actions from within an organization's internal systems.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward machine-to-machine commerce suggests that the internet is evolving from a human-centric interface to one designed for autonomous agents. Companies that successfully implement rigorous governance and oversight are gaining significant operational efficiency, while those lacking these controls face substantial security risks and minimal return on investment.
