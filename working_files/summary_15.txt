Security boss thought MFA would be too much security
============================================================

Security researchers and industry analysts are highlighting a surge in AI-driven vulnerabilities, regulatory shifts for autonomous vehicles, and significant corporate cybersecurity investments across the global technology landscape.

### Key Points
- Amazon Q developers patched a critical flaw that allowed malicious Git repositories to execute unauthorized code and steal cloud credentials.
- The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration is proposing the removal of mandatory manual brake pedals in fully autonomous robotaxi fleets.
- Private equity firm EQT acquired a majority stake in Swiss cybersecurity company Acronis at a valuation exceeding $3.5 billion.
- Microsoft failed to fully resolve a zero-day vulnerability affecting on-premises SharePoint servers, leaving systems exposed to active exploitation.
- DEF CON founder Jeff Moss announced the expansion of the Franklin project to harden critical infrastructure through collaborative hacker initiatives.

### Why it Matters
These developments underscore the growing tension between rapid AI integration and the persistent need for robust security infrastructure. As companies navigate these technical risks and regulatory changes, the ability to secure automated systems will become a primary factor in long-term market stability.
