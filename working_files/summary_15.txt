Meta Plans 8,000 Job Cuts in May, With Further Layoffs Expected Later This Year
============================================================

Meta Platforms Inc. plans to eliminate approximately 8,000 jobs, representing 10% of its global workforce, as the company shifts its strategic focus toward artificial intelligence development in 2026.

### Key Points
- Meta will begin notifying employees of the 8,000 job cuts starting May 20, 2026.
- The company has committed $135 billion in capital spending for 2026, primarily targeting AI infrastructure.
- Additional workforce reductions are expected later in the year, though the final scale remains subject to AI development progress.
- Meta’s stock rose nearly 2% following the announcement, reflecting positive investor sentiment toward the restructuring.
- This move follows a previous "year of efficiency" in 2022-2023, during which Meta eliminated more than 20,000 positions.

### Why it Matters
These layoffs signal a broader industry trend where major technology firms are aggressively reallocating capital from human labor toward AI infrastructure and automation. This shift suggests a fundamental restructuring of the knowledge economy that prioritizes machine-driven productivity over traditional corporate headcount.
