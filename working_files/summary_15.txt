Linus Torvalds says Linux is not "anti-AI", tells haters to 'fork it' and 'just walk away'
============================================================

Linux creator Linus Torvalds confirmed that developers are free to use artificial intelligence tools for coding, noting that those who oppose the technology are welcome to fork the project.

### Key Points
- Linus Torvalds stated that AI usage within the Linux kernel project remains optional rather than mandatory for contributors.
- Torvalds addressed concerns regarding AI-generated code, noting that it can increase workloads and create noise for human maintainers.
- The Linux project will prioritize technological utility over ideological debates regarding the implementation of artificial intelligence tools.
- Torvalds emphasized that human developers are just as prone to errors as AI, rejecting perfection as a standard for contributions.

### Why it Matters
This stance clarifies the role of emerging automation tools within one of the world's most significant open-source software projects. By maintaining a neutral position, the Linux kernel leadership avoids internal fragmentation while acknowledging the practical challenges AI poses to code maintenance and quality control.
