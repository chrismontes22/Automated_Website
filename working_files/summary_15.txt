Samsung will kill your health data if you don’t consent to AI training
============================================================

Samsung Health users must now consent to having their personal medical and activity data used for AI training or face the deletion of their synced account information.

### Key Points
- Samsung introduced a mandatory toggle requiring users to permit AI training and modeling to maintain health data synchronization.
- Refusing consent results in the loss of cloud-synced health records, including sleep, nutrition, medication, and clinical data.
- The company’s policy explicitly states that collected data may be subject to human review to improve its health algorithms and AI features.
- Affected data categories include sensitive information such as menstrual cycle tracking, body measurements, and past medical diagnoses.

### Why it Matters
This policy change forces a trade-off between data privacy and the basic functionality of syncing health records across devices. It raises significant concerns regarding user autonomy and the ethical standards of utilizing highly sensitive personal health information for corporate AI development.
