Tame Impala’s Kevin Parker Co-Designed a Synth So Good It’s Now a Collector’s Item
============================================================

Telepathic Instruments has announced the Clear Orchid: Arctic, a limited-edition transparent synthesizer featuring a chord-first design, launching globally on May 11 with a production run of 3,000 units.

### Key Points
- The Arctic edition features a transparent polycarbonate shell revealing custom internal hardware, green PCBs, and intentional cable routing.
- The instrument utilizes a patent-pending chord-first synthesis system with three engines, including virtual analogue, FM, and vintage reed piano emulation.
- Telepathic Instruments is releasing the Pistil VST plugin alongside the hardware, offering full DAW integration for Mac and Windows users.
- The Orchid hardware has gained significant industry traction, with 12,000 units sold across 60 countries and high-profile users including Janelle Monáe and Kid Cudi.
- Priority access for the 3,000-unit drop begins May 11 at 9 AM PDT for waitlist members, followed by a general public release one hour later.

### Why it Matters
The release of the Arctic edition signals the maturation of the Orchid ecosystem from a niche hardware experiment into a professional-grade musical instrument platform. By combining transparent industrial design with the launch of the Pistil plugin, Telepathic Instruments is successfully bridging the gap between tactile hardware and digital production workflows.
