It’s not just Apple: Android users are more loyal than ever
============================================================

A new SellCell survey reveals that Android user loyalty has reached 86.4 percent, with Samsung and Google showing significant retention gains compared to data collected in 2021.

### Key Points
- Apple maintains the highest industry loyalty at 96.4 percent among surveyed US smartphone users.
- Samsung leads the Android market with a 90.1 percent loyalty rate, followed by Google at 86.8 percent.
- Android loyalty has increased significantly since 2021, when Samsung and Google recorded rates of 74.0 percent and 65.2 percent, respectively.
- Price and perceived value remain the primary drivers for users considering a switch between smartphone brands.
- Approximately 26.8 percent of Android users who consider switching report that they would move to an iPhone.

### Why it Matters
Rising retention rates across major smartphone manufacturers suggest that the hardware market is becoming increasingly stable as the feature gap between competing devices narrows. This trend indicates that consumers are finding less incentive to switch brands, which may force companies to focus more on ecosystem integration and value propositions to capture new market share.
