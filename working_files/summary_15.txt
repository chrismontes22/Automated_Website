Elon Musk: the making of a trillionaire
============================================================

SpaceX has officially listed on the Nasdaq at a record-breaking $1.77 trillion valuation, marking the largest flotation in history and establishing Elon Musk as the world’s first trillionaire.

### Key Points
- The initial public offering raised $86 billion in capital to fund the development of fully reusable Starship rockets.
- SpaceX plans to deploy orbital data centers powered by solar energy and expand the Starlink satellite network to provide global internet access.
- Despite the record valuation, the company has yet to generate a profit, leading to a high price-to-sales ratio of 92 to one.
- Current operations include 10,000 active Starlink satellites serving 12 million users and various United States government agencies.
- Long-term objectives for the company include establishing a human colony on Mars using next-generation aerospace technology.

### Why it Matters
This historic flotation integrates SpaceX into the global financial system, making it a core holding for most retirement and savings funds. The company's massive valuation reflects significant investor confidence in Musk’s ability to scale space infrastructure despite the substantial technical and financial risks involved.
