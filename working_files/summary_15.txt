5 Easy Ways To Make Your Router More Secure
============================================================

Securing your home router is essential to prevent cyberattacks, as 75% of IoT-targeted malware specifically exploits vulnerabilities in networking hardware like those manufactured by Netgear.

### Key Points
- Enable automatic firmware updates to ensure your router receives the latest security patches against known exploits.
- Change default SSIDs and admin passwords to prevent hackers from identifying your device model and using factory-set credentials.
- Implement MAC address filtering to restrict network access to only authorized devices via a whitelist or blacklist.
- Create a separate guest Wi-Fi network to isolate visitors and prevent potential malware from spreading to your primary devices.
- Disable Universal Plug and Play (UPnP) to close unnecessary ports that could allow unauthorized devices to communicate with your network.
- Use the WPA3 security protocol for stronger encryption and better protection against brute-force password attacks.

### Why it Matters
Routers serve as the primary gateway for your digital life, making them a high-value target for hackers seeking to steal data or recruit devices into botnets. Proactively hardening these settings significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access and protects your personal information from sophisticated cyber threats.
