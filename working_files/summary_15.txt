South Korean chip giant SK Hynix raises $26.5bn in US share sale
============================================================

South Korean chip manufacturer SK Hynix raised $26.5 billion in a record-breaking Nasdaq debut, reflecting intense global investor demand for companies central to the ongoing artificial intelligence infrastructure boom.

### Key Points
- SK Hynix sold 177.9 million American depositary shares at $149 each, marking the largest US listing by a foreign company.
- Shares surged 17% on their first day of trading, with demand reportedly exceeding available supply by seven times.
- The company is a critical supplier to Nvidia and currently holds a market valuation exceeding $1 trillion.
- Proceeds from the offering are earmarked for expanding manufacturing capacity and developing advanced high-end memory chips.
- The listing provides US investors direct access to the firm, bypassing the complexities of trading on the South Korean stock exchange.

### Why it Matters
This successful listing serves as a key indicator of sustained investor appetite for the semiconductor sector amid the global AI spending surge. It provides SK Hynix with significant capital to fund domestic expansion while highlighting the growing influence of AI-focused firms on international financial markets.
