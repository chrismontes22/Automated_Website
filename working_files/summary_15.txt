Bitcoin slips from near $80,000 as oil price increase weighs on risk assets
============================================================

Bitcoin retreated from the $80,000 resistance level on Thursday as rising oil prices and broader market volatility pressured risk assets, despite signs of a potential short-squeeze rally.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell 0.7% after failing to break the $80,000 resistance, while Ether dropped 2.5% to trade at $2,320.
- Oil prices climbed 1.5% to $103 per barrel following the U.S. seizure of three Iranian tankers, negatively impacting risk asset sentiment.
- Derivatives data shows a rare combination of high open interest and negative funding rates, which could trigger a short-squeeze if bearish positions are forced to unwind.
- The CoinDesk 20 index declined 1.1%, and the "Altcoin Season" index hit a 10-day low as capital flows shifted toward Bitcoin.
- Spark (SPK) surged over 70% following its listing on the South Korean exchange Upbit, bucking the broader market's downward trend.

### Why it Matters
The current market behavior highlights a growing divergence between Bitcoin's potential breakout and the broader crypto sector's weakening participation. Investors are closely monitoring these price levels and derivatives data to determine if the rally can sustain momentum or if macroeconomic pressures will force a deeper correction.
