Planned obsolescence is now a Windows PC security nightmare
============================================================

Expired Secure Boot certificates on June 24 created significant security vulnerabilities for many Windows PCs, highlighting concerns over Microsoft’s delayed communication and the broader implications of planned hardware obsolescence.

### Key Points
- Security certificates for the Windows Secure Boot feature began expiring on June 24, leaving many systems vulnerable to hard-to-detect malware.
- Microsoft issued limited warnings before the expiration and later expanded automatic upgrades, causing widespread confusion among users regarding update status and device safety.
- The incident underscores a growing trend where tech companies prioritize newer hardware, often leaving users with older, unsupported systems at risk.
- Rising consumer hardware costs, partly driven by AI development, make frequent device upgrades financially difficult for many users.

### Why it Matters
This situation illustrates the tension between rapid technological advancement and the practical need for long-term security support on aging consumer hardware. When major companies fail to provide clear, accessible update paths, they inadvertently force users to choose between using vulnerable devices or facing the financial burden of premature hardware replacement.
