Infinix HOT 70 Series Review: Designed Like a Flagship, Not Priced Like One
============================================================

The new Infinix HOT 70 and HOT 70 Pro 5G smartphones prioritize expressive, tactile design and AI integration to challenge the standard aesthetic limitations of the affordable mobile market.

### Key Points
- The HOT 70 series features unique finishes, including color-shifting Thermo Orange and luminous Mirage Green, alongside a tactile "Clous de Paris" inspired AI button.
- The HOT 70 Pro 5G includes a 144Hz display, a 50MP Sony IMX882 camera sensor, and IP68 water resistance, while the base model is limited to 4G connectivity.
- Both devices house large 6,000 mAh batteries with 45W fast charging and offer three years of major XOS software upgrades for models with 6GB of RAM or more.
- The Pro model utilizes a MediaTek Dimensity 7100 5G chip, whereas the standard HOT 70 is powered by the 4G-only MediaTek Helio G100 Ultimate processor.
- Pricing starts at approximately $120 for the HOT 70 and $200 for the HOT 70 Pro 5G, positioning them as design-focused alternatives in the budget segment.

### Why it Matters
By focusing on premium-feeling materials and experimental aesthetics, Infinix is attempting to shift the budget smartphone category away from generic, utilitarian designs. This approach provides younger, cost-conscious consumers with high-end visual features and AI-driven functionality that are typically reserved for significantly more expensive flagship devices.
