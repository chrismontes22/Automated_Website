EA are making another round of layoffs as they seek "to better meet fans’ changing needs", according to reports
============================================================

Electronic Arts is conducting a new round of layoffs across multiple departments, including recruitment and IT, as the publisher seeks to restructure operations to meet evolving player needs.

### Key Points
- Affected departments include recruitment, IT, customer support, and the trust and safety division.
- The layoffs impact remote roles in the United States and staff members based in Hyderabad, India.
- This follows previous workforce reductions at Skate developer Full Circle in February and the Battlefield 6 team in March.
- Internal communications suggest the company is shifting work to different teams, locations, and service partners.
- Electronic Arts has declined to provide an official comment regarding the specific number of employees impacted by these cuts.

### Why it Matters
These recurring layoffs reflect a broader trend of cost-cutting measures within the gaming industry as major publishers attempt to streamline operations. The restructuring highlights ongoing uncertainty for employees as companies balance shifting market demands with the financial pressures of large-scale acquisitions and debt management.
