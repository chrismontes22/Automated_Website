Why human-centric displays matter in the screen-first workplace
============================================================

Lenovo executive George Toh explores how the integration of AI and human-centric design in display technology is transforming monitors to prioritize user comfort, digital wellness, and workplace productivity.

### Key Points
- Adults now spend approximately 7 to 10 hours daily looking at screens, driving a need for more ergonomic display solutions.
- Manufacturers are incorporating adaptive brightness, low blue light settings, and intelligent color optimization to reduce digital fatigue.
- Future display innovations may include AI-driven features that monitor gaze duration, blinking frequency, and posture to provide real-time wellness feedback.
- Research from Deloitte indicates that organizations adopting a human-centered approach to AI are significantly more likely to realize returns on their technology investments.
- Data from McKinsey shows that 84% of U.S. consumers prioritize wellness, influencing how employees evaluate the technology tools provided by their employers.

### Why it Matters
As screen time becomes central to modern work, businesses must shift from purely performance-based hardware to tools that support long-term employee health and sustainability. Prioritizing digital wellness in hardware design helps reduce burnout and creates a more intuitive, responsive environment that sustains productivity.
