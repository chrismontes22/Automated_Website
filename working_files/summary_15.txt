FBI Warns of Deepfake Videos Impersonating IC3 Leadership
============================================================

The FBI has issued a warning regarding scammers using deepfake videos and spoofed websites to impersonate the Internet Crime Complaint Center and defraud previous victims of financial crimes.

### Key Points
- Scammers are using AI-generated videos of senior FBI officials to direct victims to fraudulent websites that mimic the official ic3.gov portal.
- The FBI confirmed that criminals are contacting victims via social media platforms like Facebook Messenger to solicit sensitive financial information or credentials.
- Fraudulent sites collect personal data under the guise of updating existing reports, often providing fake reference numbers to build false credibility.
- The Bureau explicitly stated it does not maintain a social media presence and never requests payment to assist in the recovery of lost funds.
- Security experts warn that the use of generative AI has made these phishing campaigns significantly more convincing and difficult for the average user to identify.

### Why it Matters
This escalation represents a sophisticated evolution in "recovery scams" that specifically target individuals who have already suffered financial losses. By leveraging the perceived authority of federal law enforcement, these campaigns threaten to compromise sensitive data and cause further economic harm to vulnerable populations.
