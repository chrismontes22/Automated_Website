Comstock Holding Companies Conference: CHCI details fee-based growth, ParkX surge and new data centers
============================================================

**One-sentence headline summary**
Comstock Holding Companies reported strong 2025 financial growth, highlighting its debt-free, fee-based business model and expansion into transit-oriented developments and new data center infrastructure projects.

**Key points**
* Comstock reported 2025 revenue of $63 million, a 23% increase, and net income of $17 million, up 17% year-over-year.
* The company operates an asset-light model, earning recurring revenue through asset management, property management, and parking services via its ParkX subsidiary.
* Assets under management grew 28% to 92 total assets, with stabilized commercial and residential portfolios maintaining 93% occupancy.
* Management launched a data center platform, including a joint venture with Jericho Energy Ventures and development services for power-ready sites in the Mid-Atlantic.
* The company maintains a debt-free balance sheet by separating operating activities from the private, family-office-owned "anchor portfolio" assets.

**Why it matters**
Comstock’s unique structure allows it to capture development and management fees while avoiding the debt risks typically associated with traditional real estate developers. This approach provides a distinct model for investors looking for exposure to large-scale, transit-oriented projects without the balance-sheet liabilities of standard REITs.
