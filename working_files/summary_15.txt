8 Iconic Apple Products We Will Never Forget
============================================================

As Apple celebrates its 50th anniversary on April 1, 2026, the company reflects on five decades of innovation that transformed personal computing, mobile technology, and global consumer habits.

**Key points**
- Apple was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in a Los Altos garage.
- Iconic products like the Macintosh, iMac G3, iPod, and iPhone redefined their respective industries through intuitive design and hardware integration.
- The company transitioned from a hobbyist computer manufacturer to a global technology leader headquartered at a $5 billion campus in Cupertino.
- Anniversary celebrations include global events featuring artists like Alicia Keys and a private employee concert at Apple Park.
- CEO Tim Cook marked the milestone with an open letter emphasizing the company's core value of making technology personal and accessible.

**Why it matters**
Apple’s evolution from a small partnership to a trillion-dollar entity has fundamentally altered how the world interacts with digital tools and media. Its history of prioritizing design and user experience continues to set the standard for the consumer electronics industry and influences market expectations for hardware and software integration.
