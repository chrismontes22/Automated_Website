Motorola Razr Ultra (2026) Review
============================================================

The Motorola Razr Ultra (2026) offers a highly polished flip phone experience with premium materials and class-leading battery life, though its $1,499 price tag makes it a difficult recommendation.

### Key Points
- The device features a 4-inch external cover display that supports full Android app functionality and a 7-inch 165Hz internal AMOLED screen.
- Hardware includes a Snapdragon 8 Elite processor, 16GB of RAM, 512GB of storage, and a 5,000mAh silicon-carbon battery.
- The design incorporates a titanium-reinforced hinge, IP48 water and dust resistance, and unique finishes like Alcantara and real wood.
- Motorola promises three years of Android OS upgrades and five years of security updates, which trails behind competitors offering seven-year support.
- The primary 50MP camera utilizes a new LOFIC sensor to improve dynamic range, though software image processing still requires further refinement.

### Why it Matters
The Razr Ultra represents a mature, high-quality foldable that excels in daily usability but struggles to justify its premium price point against competitors with longer software support. While the hardware is top-tier, the device faces a challenging market position that makes it a much more attractive option once retail discounts are applied.
