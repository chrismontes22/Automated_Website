Here are all the ways Apple is raising prices and why
============================================================

Apple has implemented widespread price increases across its hardware lineup, AppleCare+ plans, and subscription services, citing rising component costs, licensing fees, and global economic pressures as primary drivers.

### Key Points
- Hardware prices rose on June 25, including a $200 increase for the 13-inch MacBook Air and a $100 hike for the base iPad.
- CEO Tim Cook attributed hardware price hikes to high-bandwidth memory shortages caused by increased demand from AI data centers.
- Apple Music individual plans increased to $11.99 per month, while Apple One Family and Premier bundles rose by $2 monthly.
- AppleCare+ costs for Mac and iPad devices increased by $0.50 per month or $5 annually.
- iPhone prices in Japan rose between 8% and 11% due to currency fluctuations, with potential U.S. price hikes expected for the upcoming fall lineup.

### Why it Matters
These broad price adjustments signal a shift in Apple's pricing strategy as the company navigates supply chain constraints and rising operational expenses. Consumers may face higher costs across the entire ecosystem, potentially impacting brand loyalty and the perceived value of Apple's integrated service bundles.
