The Fourth Inning in the Future of Work
============================================================

Technology consultant Geoffrey A. Moore outlines the evolution of the modern workplace, identifying the current rise of artificial intelligence as the fourth major era of enterprise computing innovation.

### Key Points
- The first era (1970s–80s) focused on mainframe computing and management information systems led by IBM and Oracle.
- The second era (1990s) shifted to client-server computing and real-time business processes driven by Microsoft and Cisco.
- The third era (2000s) prioritized cloud computing and mobile applications, with Google and Apple emerging as dominant industry players.
- The current fourth era centers on artificial intelligence, subscription-based business models, and a primary focus on customer success.
- Future enterprise workflows will transition from transaction-based models to those organized around specific customer outcomes.

### Why it Matters
This shift marks the transition of information technology from a back-office utility to the central driver of an enterprise's core mission and values. Businesses must now adapt their strategies to prioritize customer outcomes to remain competitive in an era defined by collaboration and AI-driven services.
