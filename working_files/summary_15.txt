Victims of 23andMe data breach to get $47m payout, judge rules
============================================================

A California bankruptcy court has ordered 23andMe to pay $46.75 million in compensation to victims following a massive 2023 data breach that exposed the genetic information of millions.

### Key Points
- A California judge mandated the $46.75 million settlement to be paid by Chrome Holding, which currently controls 23andMe assets.
- The 2023 security incident compromised the sensitive genetic and health data of approximately 6.9 million 23andMe users.
- Funds will be transferred to Kroll Restructuring within five business days for distribution to the affected individuals.
- The UK Information Commissioner's Office previously fined the company £2.31 million for failing to implement adequate data security measures.
- California Attorney General Rob Bonta filed a lawsuit alleging the company misled consumers regarding the severity of the breach.

### Why it Matters
This settlement highlights the significant legal and financial liabilities companies face when failing to protect highly sensitive biometric and genetic data. It serves as a critical reminder of the ongoing risks to consumer privacy in the biotechnology sector and the potential for substantial regulatory and judicial consequences following large-scale data breaches.
