Windows updated Secure Boot just in time. Here’s what happened
============================================================

Microsoft has released the Secure Boot 2023 certificate update for Windows 10 and 11 devices to replace expiring 2011 security certificates and maintain critical firmware-level protection against malicious software.

### Key Points
- Microsoft is replacing 2011-era Secure Boot certificates that expire between June and October 2026.
- The update protects Windows 10 and 11 systems from rootkits and bootkits by verifying digital signatures during startup.
- Users can verify their status by navigating to Windows Settings under Privacy & security and selecting Device Security.
- A green status indicates the update is successful, while yellow or red icons may require manual BIOS updates from the manufacturer.
- The rollout is being conducted in phases based on device compatibility and successful update signals.

### Why it Matters
This update is essential for maintaining the integrity of the boot chain and ensuring continued security patch support for Windows devices. Failure to update these certificates leaves computers vulnerable to sophisticated firmware-level threats that can bypass standard operating system defenses.
