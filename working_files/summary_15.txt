So Reddit has decided that plain HTML is unsafe
============================================================

Reddit has restricted access to the "Old Reddit" interface for logged-out users, citing security concerns and the need to prevent automated scraping of human-generated content from its forums.

### Key Points
- Reddit now requires users to be logged in to view content on the legacy "old.reddit.com" interface.
- The company claims the change is necessary to mitigate abusive scraping and automated traffic that threatens platform safety.
- Critics argue the move is a strategic effort to protect valuable user-generated data, which is increasingly sought after for training large language models.
- Unlike the legacy site, the modern "New Reddit" interface remains accessible to logged-out users, though it utilizes more complex JavaScript that may deter some scrapers.
- The policy shift has drawn criticism from long-term users who prefer the lightweight, text-heavy design of the original platform.

### Why it Matters
This change highlights the ongoing tension between platform owners and users as companies prioritize protecting data assets for AI development. By restricting access to legacy interfaces, Reddit is effectively tightening control over its content ecosystem to prevent unauthorized data harvesting.
