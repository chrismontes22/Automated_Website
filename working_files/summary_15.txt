When Honoring A Brand’s Past Means Changing Its Future
============================================================

The Blake Project has officially repositioned its brand identity to focus on mid-market value creation, shifting its strategic messaging from heritage-based branding to measurable financial and boardroom performance.

### Key Points
- The Blake Project is pivoting its consulting services to target mid-market companies, private equity-backed firms, and growth-stage businesses.
- The firm introduced the Value Acceleration Studio to integrate AI-enabled execution with brand strategy for faster, growth-oriented results.
- The repositioning emphasizes brand as a financial asset that directly impacts EBITDA, pricing power, and enterprise value.
- The firm maintains its core philosophy that brand is a source of meaning and trust, while adopting a more assertive, performance-oriented identity.
- The decision follows over 20 years of experience serving global category leaders across six continents.

### Why it Matters
This transition reflects a broader trend where professional service firms must align their visual identity with specific, high-stakes business outcomes to remain relevant to modern executives. By prioritizing financial metrics over traditional branding aesthetics, the firm aims to reduce market friction and better serve leaders who view brand performance as a critical boardroom priority.
