China can build humanoids at scale. The hard part is finding enough buyers
============================================================

Chinese robotics startups are scaling production of humanoid robots to address labor shortages and aging demographics, positioning the country as a global leader in mass-manufacturing advanced automated hardware.

### Key Points
- Chinese firms like Matrix Robotics and EngineAI are developing humanoids capable of tasks ranging from parcel sorting and security to coffee preparation.
- The MATRIX-3 robot stands 5.6 feet tall and is priced at approximately $99,000, while EngineAI offers basic models starting at roughly $26,600.
- Morgan Stanley estimates the global humanoid robot market could reach $5 trillion, with China leveraging its manufacturing capacity to compete against U.S. artificial intelligence dominance.
- Despite thousands of reported orders, some industry experts warn that current demand remains limited and many robots are still better suited for performance than complex, real-world labor.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of China’s robotics sector highlights a strategic effort to mitigate rising labor costs and an aging workforce through automation. While mass production capabilities provide a competitive edge, the industry must overcome significant functional limitations to prove these robots can operate effectively in unpredictable, real-world environments.
