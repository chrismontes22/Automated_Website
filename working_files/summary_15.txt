Apple confirms iOS 26.5 Messages app adds RCS end-to-end encryption
============================================================

Apple has officially confirmed that the upcoming iOS 26.5 update will introduce end-to-end encryption for RCS messaging, significantly enhancing security for cross-platform communication between iPhone and Android users.

### Key Points
- The iOS 26.5 Release Candidate is now available, confirming the inclusion of end-to-end encrypted RCS messaging.
- This feature will roll out gradually across supported carriers, with a full list of compatible providers expected on Apple's website soon.
- Although labeled as a beta feature, the encryption will be included in the final public release of iOS 26.5.
- RCS support improves the messaging experience between iPhones and Android devices by enabling features previously exclusive to iMessage.

### Why it Matters
This update marks a significant step in bridging the security gap between Apple’s iMessage and cross-platform messaging standards. By implementing end-to-end encryption for RCS, Apple is addressing long-standing privacy concerns for users who frequently communicate with Android devices.
