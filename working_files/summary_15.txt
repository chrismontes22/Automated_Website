The KIDS Act Would Require Age Checks To Get Online
============================================================

**Congress is preparing to vote on the KIDS Act, a legislative package that could force online platforms to implement widespread age verification and stricter content moderation policies for users.**

### Key Points
- The KIDS Act combines the Kids Online Safety Act (KOSA) with the SAFE BOTS Act and the SCREEN Act into one expedited legislative package.
- Provisions require platforms to identify users under 17, creating significant legal pressure for companies to mandate age verification via government IDs or facial scans.
- The bill mandates that platforms establish policies to restrict content related to drugs, alcohol, gambling, and financial fraud, potentially suppressing lawful speech.
- New regulations target private and ephemeral messaging, raising concerns about the future of end-to-end encryption and user privacy.
- The "knows or should have known" liability standard creates high legal risks for startups and smaller services that cannot afford to defend against regulatory challenges.

### Why it Matters
These regulations could fundamentally alter the internet by effectively ending anonymous browsing and forcing platforms to monitor private communications to avoid legal liability. The resulting shift toward mandatory age verification threatens the privacy of all users while potentially restricting access to sensitive, lawful discussions for minors.
