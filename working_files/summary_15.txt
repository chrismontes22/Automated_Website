PSA: RCS E2EE is missing in iOS 27 beta 1, but it will be back
============================================================

Apple’s iOS 27 developer beta 1 is currently missing the RCS end-to-end encryption toggle, though the company confirms this is a temporary software branching issue rather than a removal.

### Key Points
- The RCS end-to-end encryption feature, introduced in iOS 26.5, is absent from the initial iOS 27 developer beta release.
- Apple attributes the missing toggle to standard software branching, where features from previous builds are temporarily excluded during early development cycles.
- RCS encryption is a collaborative effort between Apple, Google, and the GSMA to secure cross-platform messaging between iPhone and Android devices.
- Users currently relying on encrypted RCS messaging are advised to remain on iOS 26.5 until the feature is restored in a future beta update.

### Why it Matters
This temporary omission highlights the inherent instability of early-stage developer betas and the complexities of maintaining cross-platform security standards. Ensuring consistent encryption across messaging protocols remains a critical priority for Apple as it bridges the communication gap between iOS and Android ecosystems.
