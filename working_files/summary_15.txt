The Banality of Peter Thiel’s Evil
============================================================

Leaked documents from Wired reveal that tech billionaire Peter Thiel’s exclusive, invitation-only "Dialog" gatherings facilitate private networking between high-ranking government officials and influential Silicon Valley surveillance industry leaders.

### Key Points
- Wired magazine published leaked records from Dialog, a private, $16,000-per-ticket retreat hosted by Peter Thiel since 2006.
- Attendees include sitting U.S. senators, Trump administration officials, NATO commanders, and executives from data-brokerage and surveillance firms like Palantir and LiveRamp.
- Session topics at the August retreat include "Navigating WWIII," "Build-a-Cult," and discussions on the societal impacts of artificial intelligence.
- The guest list features individuals who hold direct regulatory oversight over the companies represented by other attendees, raising concerns about conflicts of interest.
- Thiel has previously used his wealth to influence political outcomes and suppress media outlets, including his role in the closure of Gawker.

### Why it Matters
These gatherings highlight the growing convergence between private tech interests and the public officials responsible for regulating them. By creating an off-the-record environment for power brokers to align their agendas, these meetings potentially undermine democratic oversight and transparency in government policy.
