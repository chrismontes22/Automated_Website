The Science Of What AI Actually Rewards via @sejournal, @Kevin_Indig
============================================================

New research analyzing 98,000 ChatGPT citations reveals that AI content preferences vary significantly by industry, debunking universal SEO formulas and highlighting the importance of vertical-specific strategies.

### Key Points
- Declarative opening statements provide a 14% aggregate lift in citation rates across all seven analyzed industry verticals.
- Including specific dates and numerical data consistently improves citation likelihood, while pricing-focused introductions often suppress visibility.
- Corporate and editorial content accounts for 94.7% of all AI citations, significantly outperforming user-generated content like Reddit or forums.
- Heading structures are binary; pages with three to four headings perform worse than those with zero, requiring a full commitment to either minimal or deep hierarchy.
- Knowledge Graph-verified entities are not a primary driver, as AI models prioritize niche, specific data points over generic, well-known brand mentions.
- Optimal heading depth varies by sector, ranging from zero for healthcare to 20-plus sections for CRM and SaaS documentation.

### Why it Matters
These findings suggest that businesses should abandon generic "best practices" in favor of content strategies tailored to their specific industry's unique information needs. By prioritizing authoritative, structured corporate content over community-driven threads, organizations can better align their digital presence with the specific patterns used by LLMs to source information.
