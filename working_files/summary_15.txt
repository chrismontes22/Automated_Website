Your Home Is Full of Energy Vampires. This $12 Meter Helped Me Find the Biggest Ones
============================================================

Many household electronics continue to consume electricity while powered off, with a recent test identifying cable boxes as the most significant source of passive energy waste in homes.

### Key Points
- A cable box with DVR functionality was the highest energy consumer, drawing 19.9 watts even when turned off.
- Desktop computers and gaming consoles in sleep mode accounted for secondary power drains ranging from 1.3 to 3.1 watts.
- Many modern appliances, including large televisions and dishwashers, demonstrated zero-watt power draw when not in use.
- Smart home devices like the Amazon Echo Dot maintain a constant phantom load of 1.7 watts to remain ready for voice commands.
- Using a power meter to identify specific high-draw devices can help homeowners decide which electronics are worth unplugging or scheduling via smart plugs.

### Why it Matters
Phantom energy loads contribute to unnecessary household expenses and increased overall electricity demand. By identifying and managing these "energy vampires," consumers can reduce their annual utility bills and improve the energy efficiency of their homes without sacrificing essential functionality.
