Meta Partners With Broadcom to Co-Develop Custom AI Silicon
============================================================

Meta has expanded its strategic partnership with Broadcom to co-develop multiple generations of custom MTIA silicon chips designed to power AI workloads across its global social media platforms.

### Key Points
- Meta will utilize Broadcom’s XPU platform and advanced Ethernet technology to design and scale its next-generation MTIA inference and recommendation accelerators.
- The collaboration includes a commitment to deploy over 1 gigawatt of custom silicon, with plans for a multi-gigawatt rollout in the coming years.
- Meta intends to develop and deploy four new generations of MTIA chips within the next two years to support generative AI and ranking services.
- Broadcom CEO Hock Tan will step down from Meta’s Board of Directors to serve as an advisor focused on the company’s custom silicon roadmap.

### Why it Matters
This partnership provides Meta with the specialized hardware infrastructure necessary to scale generative AI and real-time recommendation engines for billions of users. By securing a long-term supply of custom silicon, Meta reduces its reliance on third-party chips while optimizing performance and energy efficiency for its massive computing clusters.
