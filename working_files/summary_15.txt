Changing this one setting solved all of my Windows 11 Bluetooth issues
============================================================

Windows users experiencing persistent Bluetooth connectivity issues after waking their PCs from sleep can resolve the problem by disabling a specific power management setting within the Device Manager.

### Key Points
- Windows often defaults to turning off Bluetooth adapters to save power, causing peripherals like controllers and headphones to disconnect during idle periods.
- Users can fix this by navigating to Device Manager, selecting the Bluetooth adapter properties, and unchecking "Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power."
- This power-saving feature is intended for laptops to extend battery life but frequently disrupts connectivity for desktop users who keep their systems running constantly.
- The setting is buried deep within system menus, making it difficult for average users to identify as the root cause of intermittent hardware connection failures.

### Why it Matters
This issue highlights a common friction point where aggressive power management settings prioritize negligible energy savings over consistent user experience. For desktop users, disabling this hidden setting ensures seamless peripheral connectivity without the need for expensive hardware replacements or constant manual troubleshooting.
