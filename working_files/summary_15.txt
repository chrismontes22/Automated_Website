Ford’s latest anti-theft measure stops thieves from starting your car
============================================================

Ford is introducing new security features, including remote ignition disabling and real-time theft alerts, to combat the rising rates of vehicle theft across Canada and North America.

### Key Points
- Ford’s new security package includes Start Inhibit, which allows owners to remotely disable their vehicle's ignition via a smartphone app.
- Theft Alerts notify owners of suspicious activity, such as cabin intrusions, forceful impacts, or unauthorized door openings.
- Stolen Vehicle Services provides 24/7 support from live agents who assist owners and law enforcement with vehicle recovery.
- The Start Inhibit feature is available on 2024 and 2025 Ford F-150 models and has expanded to the 2026 Bronco, Expedition, Mustang Mach-E, and Lincoln Navigator.
- Ford includes these security features for free for the first year, followed by a subscription fee of $9.99 per month.

### Why it Matters
These technological advancements represent a shift toward software-based vehicle security as automakers attempt to counter sophisticated methods like key fob cloning. By providing tools that directly assist law enforcement in tracking and immobilizing stolen property, these features may help reduce the financial and logistical burden of rising auto theft rates.
