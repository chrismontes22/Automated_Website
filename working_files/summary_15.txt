After Mythos: New Playbooks For a Zero-Window Era
============================================================

Anthropic’s Claude Mythos AI model has reduced software vulnerability exploit windows to near-zero, forcing organizations to adopt assume-breach security models to detect and contain threats in real time.

### Key Points
- Anthropic’s Claude Mythos and Project Glasswing can identify exploitable software vulnerabilities in minutes, a task that previously required weeks of human expert analysis.
- Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell recently met with major financial CEOs to address the systemic risks posed by advanced AI capabilities.
- The assume-breach security model prioritizes reducing mean-time-to-contain (MTTC) by utilizing automated network detection and response (NDR) to limit the blast radius of cyberattacks.
- Modern NDR platforms, such as Corelight’s Open NDR, monitor for living-off-the-land techniques and lateral movement to identify malicious activity hidden within legitimate network traffic.
- Organizations are encouraged to automate asset inventories and attack chain reconstruction to maintain visibility against rapidly evolving, AI-assisted threats.

### Why it Matters
The emergence of AI-driven vulnerability discovery has rendered traditional patch-based security strategies insufficient, as attackers can now exploit software flaws almost immediately after they are identified. This shift necessitates a fundamental change in corporate cybersecurity, moving from reactive patching to proactive, automated network containment to ensure institutional stability.
