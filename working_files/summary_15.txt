Framework Laptop 13 Pro: Major Upgrades and Linux Front and Center
============================================================

Framework announced the Laptop 13 Pro on April 21, 2026, featuring a redesigned CNC aluminum chassis, significantly improved battery life, and continued support for modular, user-upgradeable hardware components.

### Key Points
- The Laptop 13 Pro features a 74Whr battery, Intel Core Ultra Series 3 processors, and a custom 13-inch IPS display with 700 nits brightness.
- Framework introduced a haptic touchpad from LiteOn and transitioned to user-replaceable LPCAMM2 memory modules to maintain upgradeability.
- The device offers backward compatibility, allowing users to install new motherboards into older chassis or purchase upgrade kits for existing units.
- This model marks the first time Framework is selling hardware with Ubuntu Linux pre-installed and officially certified.
- Pricing starts at $1,199 for the DIY Edition, with shipping scheduled to begin in June 2026.

### Why it Matters
This release demonstrates that modular hardware can compete with premium, non-repairable laptops by addressing previous performance and build-quality limitations. By maintaining backward compatibility while integrating modern standards like LPCAMM2 memory, Framework continues to challenge industry trends toward soldered components and planned obsolescence.
