Bitcoin holds above $70,000, but future direction hinges on Iran-U.S. 'talks'
============================================================

**Bitcoin holds steady above $70,000 as markets react to a temporary pause in U.S.-Iran tensions.**

**Key points**
* Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies rose after President Trump announced a five-day pause on strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure.
* Crypto-related mining stocks and broader stock markets (S&P 500 and Nasdaq) also saw gains of roughly 1.2%.
* Despite Iranian officials denying that formal talks are taking place, investors remain optimistic, keeping risk assets stable for now.
* Analysts warn that Bitcoin’s next move depends on whether energy prices and shipping through the Strait of Hormuz stabilize over the next few days.
* If tensions ease, Bitcoin could test record highs of $74,000–$76,000; if they escalate, prices could drop back toward the mid-$60,000s.

**Why it matters**
Geopolitical stability in the Middle East is currently a major driver for global markets, as it directly influences oil prices and inflation expectations. Because Bitcoin is viewed as a "risk asset," its value is highly sensitive to these broader economic conditions.
