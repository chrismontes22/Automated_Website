Apple's £3 Billion UK iCloud Case Cleared for Trial
============================================================

The U.K. Competition Appeal Tribunal has certified a £3 billion class action lawsuit against Apple, alleging the company overcharged millions of users for iCloud storage services since 2015.

### Key Points
- The lawsuit represents approximately 40 million U.K. iPhone and iPad users who could receive up to £77 each if the claim succeeds.
- Consumer group Which? alleges Apple engaged in anti-competitive behavior by restricting rival cloud services and forcing users into paid iCloud subscriptions.
- The trial is currently scheduled for October 2028, with Apple planning to appeal the tribunal's decision to proceed.
- Eligibility covers U.K. iCloud users between November 8, 2018, and June 8, 2026, with an opt-out deadline set for October 8.
- Apple maintains the claims are unfounded, arguing that customers are not required to use iCloud and that viable alternatives remain available.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights increasing regulatory scrutiny regarding the closed ecosystems maintained by major technology firms and their impact on consumer pricing. A successful ruling could set a significant legal precedent for how tech giants manage cloud storage integration and competition on their mobile devices.
