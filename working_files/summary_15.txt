Texas Can Keep Enforcing Its Age Verification Law for Mobile Apps, Supreme Court Says
============================================================

The U.S. Supreme Court has declined to block a Texas law requiring app stores to verify user ages and obtain parental consent before allowing app downloads or purchases.

### Key Points
- Justice Samuel Alito denied a request to pause the Texas App Store Accountability Act, allowing the law to remain in effect during ongoing litigation.
- The law, signed by Governor Greg Abbott in 2025, mandates age verification and parental consent for minors accessing digital applications.
- Plaintiffs including the Computer and Communications Industry Association and Students Engaged in Advancing Texas are challenging the law on constitutional grounds.
- The Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals is scheduled to hold an expedited hearing regarding the legal challenges in early August.
- Critics argue the mandate threatens user privacy by requiring government-issued identification to access online services and digital content.

### Why it Matters
This ruling sets a significant precedent for similar age-verification proposals currently circulating in state legislatures across the United States. It highlights the ongoing tension between legislative efforts to protect minors online and concerns regarding digital privacy, data security, and free speech rights.
