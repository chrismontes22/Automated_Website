Virginia employee refuses to use software that records customers without approval, seeks legal action after employer threatens termination
============================================================

A Virginia-based supervisor is considering resigning over a new company policy that mandates recording customer phone calls to train generative artificial intelligence programs despite potential legal and financial risks.

### Key Points
- The supervisor faces a conflict between company policy and personal moral standards regarding data privacy.
- Recording customer calls for AI training purposes may lead to significant backlash in the court of public opinion.
- Employees who resign over policy disagreements may forfeit their legal entitlement to unemployment benefits.
- The company remains in compliance with current laws, though the ethical implications of their AI training methods remain under scrutiny.

### Why it Matters
This situation highlights the growing tension between corporate AI development and employee ethical concerns regarding data collection practices. Companies risk significant reputational damage and loss of consumer trust if their internal AI training policies are perceived as invasive or non-transparent.
