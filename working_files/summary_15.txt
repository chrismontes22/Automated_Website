DGA Board Approves 4-Year AMPTP Deal That Addresses Unemployment Issues With Health Plan Increases, New AI Protections, Multi-Hyphenate Guardrails & More
============================================================

The Directors Guild of America National Board has unanimously recommended ratification of a new four-year contract with major studios, aiming to secure health benefits and address artificial intelligence concerns.

### Key Points
- The DGA National Board voted to recommend the tentative agreement with the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers to its full membership.
- The contract includes a 24.4% increase in employer contributions to the union’s health plan to address rising costs and industry unemployment.
- New provisions establish "directorial authority" over AI-generated footage, ensuring it remains under the control of the director.
- The agreement introduces a "skills enhancement program" to help members integrate new technologies into their professional workflows.
- Studios committed to lobbying for federal production tax incentives and agreed to prohibit the exclusion of DGA members from directing opportunities outside the U.S. and Canada.

### Why it Matters
This agreement provides a measure of stability for the film and television industry during a period of significant contraction and technological disruption. By securing protections against AI and establishing new job security measures, the DGA sets a precedent for how labor unions can navigate the evolving landscape of modern media production.
