Google employee charged with fraud after winning $1.2m on Polymarket
============================================================

Google information security engineer Michele Spagnuolo was arrested and charged with fraud for allegedly using nonpublic internal search data to win $1.2 million betting on the platform Polymarket.

### Key Points
- Michele Spagnuolo faces federal charges including commodities fraud, wire fraud, and money laundering after allegedly exploiting confidential Google search trends.
- Prosecutors claim Spagnuolo used the pseudonym "AlphaRaccoon" to place bets on outcomes like the most-searched person of 2025, which he knew via internal data.
- The defendant, a 36-year-old security engineer, was identified after a payment processor linked his identity to cryptocurrency transfers used to fund the bets.
- Spagnuolo has been released on a $2.25 million bond following his arrest in New York.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing intersection of insider trading risks and decentralized prediction markets like Polymarket. It underscores the vulnerability of proprietary corporate data when employees with high-level security access misuse internal information for personal financial gain.
