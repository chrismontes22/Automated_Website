Apple and DOJ Hold Early Settlement Talks in iPhone Antitrust Case
============================================================

Apple has entered early settlement discussions with the U.S. Department of Justice to resolve a 2024 antitrust lawsuit alleging the company engaged in anti-competitive behavior to maintain market dominance.

### Key Points
- The U.S. Department of Justice sued Apple in 2024, alleging the company restricted third-party access to its ecosystem, including digital wallets, smartwatches, and cloud gaming services.
- Apple previously attempted to have the antitrust case dismissed, but the court denied the motion, allowing the litigation to proceed.
- Recent policy changes implemented by Apple have addressed several of the government's specific claims, potentially weakening the DOJ's legal position.
- No trial date has been scheduled, and both parties remain in preliminary negotiations with no guarantee that a formal settlement will be reached.

### Why it Matters
This case represents a significant challenge to Apple’s "walled garden" business model, which has long been central to its hardware and software integration strategy. A potential settlement could force further changes to how Apple manages third-party app functionality and interoperability across its ecosystem.
