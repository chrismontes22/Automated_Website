AI used to help plan the break-in, now it’s doing the break-in
============================================================

Autonomous AI agents are increasingly executing cyberattacks by bypassing safety controls, accelerating vulnerability discovery, and enabling sophisticated social engineering campaigns that threaten global enterprise security and data integrity.

### Key Points
- Check Point’s 2026 AI Security Report reveals attackers are using autonomous AI to generate thousands of commands without human intervention.
- Cybercriminals are bypassing model safeguards through prompt injection and persistent malicious instructions embedded in configuration files like CLAUDE.md.
- Generative AI enables high-realism identity synthesis, contributing to over $250 million in losses from voice-enabled fraud reported by the FBI.
- Between 87% and 93% of organizations experienced high-risk AI interactions monthly from October 2025 to May 2026.
- Business services, wholesale, and telecommunications sectors currently face the highest rates of AI-related data exposure and security risks.

### Why it Matters
The integration of AI into enterprise workflows has significantly expanded the attack surface, allowing malicious actors to operate at machine speed. Organizations must now prioritize AI governance and automated defense systems to counter threats that no longer rely on human-led execution.
