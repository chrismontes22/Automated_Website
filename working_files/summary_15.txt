Can't afford a high-powered graphics card? This DIY engineer made his own GPU out of 8,192 RISC-V chips
============================================================

German DIY creator bitluni has successfully constructed a custom graphics processing unit using over 8,000 affordable RISC-V microcontrollers to explore the potential of massive parallel computing hardware architectures.

### Key Points
- The project utilizes 8,192 CH570 RISC-V microcontrollers, each operating at 100 MHz with 12KB of SRAM.
- The custom-built hardware achieves a display resolution of 320x200 pixels.
- PCB design firm Altium partnered with the creator to help manage the complex circuit board requirements.
- Testing revealed the cluster outperforms an 8-core CPU in hashing tasks while consuming only 4 watts of power.
- The creator is currently planning a future iteration of the device that incorporates 32,000 individual microcontrollers.

### Why it Matters
This project demonstrates the potential for using low-cost, parallelized microcontrollers to perform specialized computing tasks like hashing more efficiently than traditional consumer CPUs. It highlights an alternative approach to hardware design that could influence future discussions regarding energy-efficient computing and the scalability of DIY electronics.
