Bitcoin, ether start June in the red while futures show taste for risk. XLM, HYPE gain
============================================================

Bitcoin and ether prices declined at the start of June amid geopolitical tensions, while Stellar’s XLM token surged following a major integration announcement with the financial firm DTCC.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin fell to $72,700 as spot bitcoin ETFs recorded 10 consecutive days of net outflows totaling $2.97 billion.
- Stellar’s XLM token jumped 40% after the DTCC selected the Stellar network for its upcoming tokenized securities platform.
- Derivatives data shows institutional risk appetite remains stable, with bitcoin open interest holding steady at $19.5 billion.
- Hyperliquid’s HYPE token reached a record high of $73.94, driven by capital inflows into newly launched ETFs.
- Global market sentiment was pressured by ongoing geopolitical tensions between the United States and Iran.

### Why it Matters
The integration of the Stellar blockchain into the DTCC’s infrastructure marks a significant milestone for the adoption of public blockchains within traditional Wall Street clearing systems. This development highlights a growing institutional shift toward tokenizing real-world assets, potentially bridging the gap between decentralized finance and global securities markets.
