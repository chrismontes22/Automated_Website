TikTok side hustlers beware: AI is coming for your job
============================================================

The rise of AI-generated influencers and product videos on TikTok Shop is disrupting the affiliate marketing landscape, challenging human creators while raising concerns regarding marketplace quality and consumer trust.

### Key Points
- TikTok’s GMV Max automated ad system is increasingly surfacing AI-generated videos that mimic human creators to drive affiliate sales.
- Brands are utilizing generative AI to produce hundreds of product videos monthly, significantly reducing costs associated with human influencers and sample distribution.
- Major companies like SharkNinja have implemented bans on AI-generated content in their affiliate programs due to concerns over brand reputation and consumer backlash.
- Legal experts warn that synthetic influencers may violate FTC guidelines requiring "honest opinions" in testimonials and could facilitate deceptive advertising at scale.
- TikTok’s official policy permits generative AI content provided it is clearly labeled, does not misrepresent products, and respects intellectual property rights.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward synthetic content threatens the business model of human affiliate creators who rely on TikTok Shop for income. As brands weigh the cost-efficiency of AI against the risk of consumer distrust, the platform faces a critical challenge in maintaining marketplace quality and authenticity.
