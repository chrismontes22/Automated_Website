Stocks Rebound as Crude Oil Falls on Hopes for a US-Iran Deal
============================================================

US stock indexes closed higher on Thursday as investors reacted to positive economic data and renewed diplomatic optimism regarding the ongoing conflict between the United States and Iran.

### Key Points
- The Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a 3.25-month high, supported by a 12% surge in IBM shares following news of a $1 billion government quantum-computing grant.
- Crude oil prices retreated from early highs after reports suggested progress in diplomatic negotiations to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.
- US manufacturing activity unexpectedly expanded to a four-year high, while weekly initial unemployment claims remained stable at 209,000.
- AI-infrastructure stocks, including ARM Holdings and Rigetti Computing, saw significant gains, while Intuit and Walmart shares declined following disappointing earnings forecasts.
- Approximately 83% of S&P 500 companies that have reported first-quarter earnings have exceeded analyst expectations.

### Why it Matters
Market sentiment remains heavily influenced by the volatility of crude oil prices and the potential for energy supply disruptions stemming from the US-Iran conflict. Investors are balancing these geopolitical risks against strong corporate earnings and resilient domestic economic indicators, which continue to provide a floor for equity valuations.
