The 10th Generation Kindle can’t be jailbreaked anymore
============================================================

Amazon has released a software update for 10th-generation Kindle e-readers that restricts device modding and prevents the use of third-party tools to remove digital rights management from e-books.

### Key Points
- The 5.18.1.1.1 software update targets the Kindle Paperwhite 4 and base Kindle models to block jailbreaking tools.
- Amazon transitioned older e-readers to the KFX-ZIP format, which is incompatible with common DeDRM plugins and Calibre software.
- The company has discontinued USB file transfers and replaced the Kindle for PC application with a native Windows 10 and 11 app.
- These changes effectively prevent users from backing up their purchased e-books or modifying their device software.

### Why it Matters
These updates signal a shift toward stricter digital rights management, limiting how users manage and store their personal e-book libraries. By restricting third-party software compatibility, Amazon is centralizing control over content access and reducing the long-term flexibility of older hardware.
