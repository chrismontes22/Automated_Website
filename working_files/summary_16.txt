Meta Discontinues End-to-End Encryption For Instagram Direct Messages
============================================================

Meta has officially discontinued manual end-to-end encryption for Instagram Direct Messages, requiring users to export their encrypted chat histories before the data is permanently deleted from the platform.

### Key Points
- Meta ended manual end-to-end encryption for Instagram DMs on May 8 due to low user adoption rates.
- Affected users must download their encrypted chat histories via in-app tools to prevent permanent data loss.
- The company recommends that users seeking high-level privacy migrate their secure conversations to the WhatsApp platform.
- Instagram users are advised to update their applications to the latest version to ensure data retrieval tools function correctly.
- Standard, unencrypted messaging features on Instagram remain unaffected by this change in service.

### Why it Matters
This shift consolidates Meta’s security strategy by positioning WhatsApp as the company's primary destination for encrypted communication. By removing underutilized features from Instagram, the firm is streamlining its product ecosystem while forcing privacy-conscious users to adopt its dedicated messaging platform.
