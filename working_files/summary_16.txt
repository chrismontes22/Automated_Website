GCHQ Chief Urges Action as AI Reshapes Cyber Threats
============================================================

GCHQ director Anne Keast-Butler has urged UK businesses to prioritize cybersecurity as a national defense issue to counter rapidly evolving AI-powered threats and emerging risks from quantum computing.

### Key Points
- GCHQ plans to launch a national cyber defense system using agentic AI to protect critical infrastructure within five years.
- Anne Keast-Butler identified Russia and China as primary state actors scaling up hybrid cyber activity against the UK and its allies.
- Industry experts warn that human-paced defenses are insufficient against machine-speed attacks, necessitating immediate improvements to basic security controls like MFA and EDR.
- The agency advised organizations to begin migrating to quantum-resistant cryptography to protect sensitive data from future decryption capabilities.
- Individuals are encouraged to transition from traditional passwords to passkeys to improve personal security against automated threats.

### Why it Matters
This shift in rhetoric frames cybersecurity as a critical boardroom responsibility rather than a routine IT maintenance task. By emphasizing the speed of AI-driven threats, the government is pressuring private firms to accelerate their defensive investments to prevent systemic failures in national infrastructure.
