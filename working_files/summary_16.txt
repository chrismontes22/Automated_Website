SpaceX Wants to Launch 1 Million AI Data Center Satellites. Experts Share the High Cost of Turning Space Into a Junkyard
============================================================

SpaceX has proposed a massive plan to deploy one million orbital data centers, sparking significant concerns among scientists regarding space debris, orbital overcrowding, and potential environmental impacts on Earth.

### Key Points
- SpaceX filed plans with the FCC for "AI1" satellites, each measuring 1,400 square meters—roughly the size of an NHL hockey rink.
- The proposed constellation would operate between 946 and 1,002 kilometers, an altitude experts warn is prone to collisions and lacks natural atmospheric clearing.
- Scientists fear the project could trigger the Kessler syndrome, a catastrophic chain reaction of orbital collisions that would render space unusable.
- Disposal plans involve moving defunct satellites into "graveyard" orbits, which critics argue would create a hazardous ring of debris around the planet.
- Experts suggest the ambitious proposal may serve as a promotional tool for the company's upcoming IPO rather than a technically viable infrastructure project.

### Why it Matters
This proposal highlights the growing tension between rapid technological expansion and the long-term sustainability of the Earth's orbital environment. If realized, the project could fundamentally alter space traffic management and increase the risk of debris-related disasters that threaten global satellite communications and navigation systems.
