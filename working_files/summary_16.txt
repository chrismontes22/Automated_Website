60Hz Thermal and 4K Night Vision in One Device. SpectraEyes Basically Gives You Superman’s Vision
============================================================

SpectraEyes has launched a new binocular-style device that integrates a 60Hz thermal sensor and a 4K digital night vision camera into a synchronized, dual-screen display for field use.

### Key Points
- The device features two independent 1280×720 LCD screens, allowing users to view thermal and night vision feeds simultaneously or separately.
- The thermal sensor offers sub-25mk sensitivity and a 60Hz refresh rate, while the 4K CMOS sensor operates in conditions as low as 0.0001 lux.
- Built for rugged environments, the unit carries an IP67 waterproof rating and functions in temperatures ranging from -20 to 50 degrees Celsius.
- Integrated IR illuminators support 850nm and 940nm wavelengths, providing visibility up to 800 meters in total darkness.
- The system is powered by hot-swappable lithium batteries and supports up to 512GB of storage for 4K video recording.
- Currently available via a crowdfunding campaign, the device is priced at $514 with global shipping scheduled to begin in June 2026.

### Why it Matters
This technology democratizes advanced sensor fusion capabilities that were previously restricted to high-cost, military-grade equipment. By providing professional-grade situational awareness in a consumer-accessible form factor, it offers significant utility for search and rescue teams, wildlife researchers, and outdoor professionals.
