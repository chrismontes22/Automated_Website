Foreign Company at Center of Trade Dispute Paid Trump Millions
============================================================

The Trump Organization received a $2 million payment from South Korean firm Base Group, a major investor in an aluminum company currently under investigation by the U.S. government.

### Key Points
- President Trump disclosed a $2 million "nonrefundable development fee" from Base Group in his June financial filing.
- The Trump Organization stated the payment relates to an unannounced golf course project.
- Base Group holds a significant stake in Korea Aluminum, which was found by the Commerce Department to have evaded trade duties on Chinese-made products in 2023.
- Base Group maintains a long-term business relationship with the Trump family, including the exclusive distribution of Trump-branded wine in South Korea.
- Eric Trump visited the Base Group headquarters in Seoul during February to discuss expanding U.S.-South Korea trade.

### Why it Matters
This transaction highlights ongoing concerns regarding potential conflicts of interest between the president's private business ventures and his official government duties. The lack of transparency surrounding these international payments complicates public oversight of how foreign entities may interact with the executive branch.
