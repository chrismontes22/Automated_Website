Apple testing ‘Live Notes’ AI system to record Genius Bar sessions: report
============================================================

Apple is currently piloting an artificial intelligence tool called Live Notes at select retail locations to transcribe and summarize customer conversations during Genius Bar technical support sessions.

### Key Points
- The Live Notes system automatically records and transcribes interactions to help employees focus on customer service.
- Participation in the program is currently optional, requiring consent from both the Apple Store employee and the customer.
- Employees retain the ability to edit transcripts and summaries before they are saved to Apple’s internal database.
- Apple has stated that managers will not receive the transcripts and the raw audio recordings will not be stored.
- Some staff members have expressed concerns that the technology could eventually be used for performance monitoring or employee coaching.

### Why it Matters
This pilot program highlights Apple's ongoing efforts to integrate generative AI into its internal operations to improve efficiency and service quality. If expanded, the technology could set a new standard for how retail companies document customer interactions while raising significant questions regarding workplace privacy and data management.
