5 Android features I miss every time I switch to an iPhone
============================================================

Users transitioning from Android to iPhone often report missing key productivity and customization features, including advanced multitasking, desktop modes, flexible file management, and broader app installation options.

### Key Points
- Android devices offer superior multitasking through split-screen views, pop-up windows, and app bubbles, which are not natively supported on iOS.
- Samsung DeX allows Galaxy smartphones to function as desktop computers, providing a PC-like interface for productivity tasks when connected to external displays.
- Android allows sideloading apps from third-party sources and repositories like GitHub, bypassing the restrictions and commissions of the official Apple App Store.
- The Android file system provides deeper access for bulk operations and background syncing, whereas iOS remains a strictly sandboxed environment.
- Reverse wireless charging on Android phones enables users to power up accessories like earbuds or secondary devices on the go.

### Why it Matters
These feature disparities highlight the fundamental philosophical differences between the open, utility-focused nature of Android and the controlled, streamlined ecosystem of Apple’s iOS. For power users, these missing capabilities can significantly impact daily workflows and device versatility, often making the switch to an iPhone feel like a functional limitation.
