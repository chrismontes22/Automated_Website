European Court: Apple Can Not Shirk Off its Interoperability Requirements
============================================================

The General Court of the European Union ruled against Apple, affirming that the company must comply with Digital Markets Act interoperability requirements to ensure fair competition in European markets.

### Key Points
- The General Court rejected Apple’s legal challenges regarding its designation as a "gatekeeper" under the European Union's Digital Markets Act.
- Apple unsuccessfully argued that its various App Stores across iOS, iPadOS, and watchOS should not be classified as a single core platform service.
- The court ruled that Apple’s interoperability obligations are legally binding and do not violate the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union.
- Apple is one of seven major technology companies, including Meta, Microsoft, and Alphabet, currently designated as gatekeepers under the new regulatory framework.
- The court dismissed Apple's arguments regarding iMessage, noting the service was not designated as an "important gateway" subject to current DMA obligations.

### Why it Matters
This ruling reinforces the European Union's authority to regulate dominant technology platforms and mandates greater openness within previously closed digital ecosystems. By prioritizing interoperability, the decision aims to increase consumer choice and reduce the market power held by major gatekeeper companies.
