The Privacy Loophole Meta Just Closed on Its AI Glasses
============================================================

Meta has updated its second-generation AI glasses to disable the camera if users attempt to tamper with or cover the mandatory LED recording indicator, effectively closing privacy loopholes.

### Key Points
- Meta’s second-generation smart glasses automatically shut down the camera if the front-facing capture LED is physically blocked or tampered with.
- The capture LED blinks white to signal when a photo is taken or video is recording, a feature Meta refined through brightness and frequency testing.
- Meta is actively removing online listings for LED-tampering services and may pursue legal action or account bans against those selling such modifications.
- Captured media remains stored locally on the glasses until the user chooses to import and share the content via Meta’s apps or social media.
- The company maintains that the blinking light serves as the primary privacy signal, as audible shutter sounds are impractical for bystanders in public spaces.

### Why it Matters
This shift marks a transition from voluntary privacy compliance to enforced hardware-level security for wearable camera technology. By disabling functionality when safety features are compromised, Meta is attempting to standardize public expectations for privacy in an era of increasingly common smart eyewear.
