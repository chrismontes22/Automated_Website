Is VeriSign Stock Underperforming the Nasdaq?
============================================================

VeriSign, a major internet infrastructure provider managing .com and .net domains, faces stock performance challenges despite reporting steady revenue growth and maintaining a $24.9 billion market capitalization.

### Key Points
- VeriSign maintains critical internet infrastructure, including two of the thirteen global root servers and registry services for .com and .net domains.
- The company reported Q1 2026 revenue of $429 million, a 6.6% year-over-year increase, with net income reaching $215 million.
- Total domain registrations for .com and .net grew 3.7% to 176.1 million, supported by an improved renewal rate of 75%.
- Shares have underperformed the Nasdaq Composite over the past year, currently trading below the 200-day moving average.
- Analysts maintain a "Moderate Buy" consensus rating with a mean price target of $320, representing a potential 20.3% upside.

### Why it Matters
As a foundational entity for global e-commerce and internet navigation, VeriSign’s operational stability remains vital for digital infrastructure. While the stock has lagged behind broader market indices, its consistent revenue growth and essential role in domain management continue to attract analyst interest.
