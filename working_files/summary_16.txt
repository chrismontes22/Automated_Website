Nvidia ramps up production of Vera Rubin, the foundation of the next generation of AI factories
============================================================

Nvidia has officially launched volume production of its Vera Rubin platform, a next-generation architectural foundation designed to power autonomous agentic AI workloads at massive enterprise scale.

### Key Points
- The Vera Rubin platform features new Rubin GPUs, Vera CPUs, NVLink 6 switches, and BlueField-4 data processing units.
- The system delivers 10 times the throughput of the previous Grace Blackwell platform while significantly reducing the cost per token.
- Manufacturing involves over 350 supply chain partners, including Dell, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, SuperMicro, and Lenovo.
- The platform introduces full-stack confidential computing and silicon-level security to protect sensitive data during multi-turn AI agent interactions.
- Vera Rubin systems are expected to begin shipping to cloud and enterprise customers starting in the fall.

### Why it Matters
This platform represents a strategic shift in enterprise infrastructure, moving from traditional computing to specialized engines optimized for autonomous, reasoning-based AI agents. By drastically improving efficiency and security, Nvidia aims to redefine the economics of AI factories and accelerate the adoption of complex, large-scale industrial automation.
