Adobe’s Project Indigo camera app gains experimental AI photo editing tools
============================================================

Adobe has launched an experimental AI Playground within its Project Indigo iPhone camera app, offering select users free access to generative photo-editing tools for a limited testing period.

### Key Points
- The AI Playground features four tools: Object Editing, Styles, Photo Guidance, and Custom Edit for text-based prompts.
- Adobe is utilizing Google’s Nano Banana model, supplemented by a custom machine learning model to restore HDR quality to processed images.
- Access is currently limited to a small percentage of Project Indigo users and requires no sign-in or account registration.
- Adobe states that user prompts and images are not uploaded to servers or used for model training, with data collection limited to anonymous button presses.
- The new generative features are restricted to photos captured directly within the Project Indigo app and cannot be applied to imported images.

### Why it Matters
This experiment allows Adobe to gather real-world data on how users interact with generative AI tools in a mobile photography environment. The findings will likely influence the development of future paid features and determine how the company integrates advanced AI editing into its broader creative software ecosystem.
