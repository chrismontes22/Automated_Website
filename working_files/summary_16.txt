Apple Ramps Foldable iPhone 'Ultra' Production to 10 Million Units
============================================================

Apple has increased its production forecast for the upcoming foldable iPhone to 10 million units, signaling a major expansion into the foldable smartphone market by late 2026.

### Key Points
- Apple plans to produce approximately 10 million foldable iPhones, potentially branded as the "iPhone Ultra," for its 2026 launch.
- The device is expected to feature a 7.8-inch inner display, an A20 chip, and a 5.5-inch cover display.
- Total production for the second half of 2026 is projected to reach 80 million units, including the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max.
- Engineering challenges regarding the device's hinge have been resolved, though initial shipments may be limited before full-scale production begins.
- Industry analysts estimate the foldable iPhone could carry a starting price of $2,500, with high-capacity storage models reaching $3,000.

### Why it Matters
This move marks Apple's formal entry into the foldable device category, challenging competitors like Xiaomi and Oppo who have recently scaled back production targets. By leveraging its massive supply chain influence, Apple aims to set a new premium price benchmark for the smartphone industry.
