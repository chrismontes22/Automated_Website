Google’s New Guidance Claims Authority Over SEO, Tools, And AEO/GEO via @sejournal, @martinibuster
============================================================

Google has released new official guidance asserting itself as the primary authority on SEO, AI optimization, and the evaluation of third-party tools and services for website owners.

### Key Points
- Google now explicitly positions its own documentation as the canonical source of truth for SEO, AEO, and GEO practices.
- The guidance warns businesses to think critically about third-party SEO services and tools that claim Google approval or endorsement.
- Google clarifies that third-party tools lack access to internal ranking data and cannot guarantee performance improvements or search success.
- The company recommends using Google Search Console as the primary, reliable source for direct search data and performance insights.
- The documentation advises users to weigh all external SEO advice against official Google guidelines before implementation.

### Why it Matters
This shift signals a more aggressive stance by Google to minimize the influence of external SEO consultants and data providers on site owners' decision-making processes. By framing third-party insights as potentially misleading, Google is effectively centralizing control over how businesses optimize their web presence for search and generative AI.
