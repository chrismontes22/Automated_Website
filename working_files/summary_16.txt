With the economy in the doldrums, Albanese looks to AI for the answer
============================================================

Australia faces sluggish economic growth and declining real wages, prompting the federal government to explore artificial intelligence as a critical driver for future productivity and long-term national prosperity.

### Key Points
- The OECD reports that Australia’s real minimum wage declined between April 2025 and April 2026.
- Deloitte Access Economics and the IMF project Australia’s annual GDP growth will remain below 2.0 percent through 2027.
- Per capita GDP growth has stagnated, with the economy contracting in two of the past five quarters.
- A government report indicates that occupations highly exposed to generative AI experienced 3.9 percent slower employment growth than less-exposed sectors.
- The federal government is balancing AI adoption with regulatory oversight, including the establishment of an AI Safety Institute and new data center requirements.

### Why it Matters
Stagnant productivity and low economic growth are fueling voter dissatisfaction and challenging the government's fiscal outlook. Successfully integrating artificial intelligence could provide the necessary boost to the economy, but policymakers must navigate significant social, labor, and regulatory risks to maintain public trust.
