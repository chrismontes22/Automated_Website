Amazon is expanding its cloud and artificial intelligence infrastructure in India with a $13 billion investment, marking a significant portion of its $48 billion five-year commitment to the region.

### Key Points
- Amazon plans to expand its datacenter footprint in Mumbai and Hyderabad to support growing cloud and AI demand.
- The $13 billion investment is part of a broader $48 billion pledge allocated for infrastructure development over five years.
- This initiative aims to bolster Amazon Web Services' capacity to provide scalable computing and machine learning resources to Indian enterprises.
- The expansion reflects Amazon's strategy to capture market share in rapidly digitizing economies through localized infrastructure investments.

### Why it Matters
This massive capital injection underscores the intensifying global competition among major cloud providers to secure dominance in emerging AI markets. By strengthening its physical infrastructure in India, Amazon positions itself to better serve regional businesses while scaling its global AI capabilities.
