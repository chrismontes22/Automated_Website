I've been paying for Microsoft 365 wrong — here's what I actually get for the money
============================================================

**Microsoft 365 subscribers can access several underutilized productivity and creative tools, including AI-powered design, video editing, and document generation software, potentially reducing the need for additional third-party subscriptions.**

**Key points**
- Microsoft Designer provides AI-driven graphic design tools for creating images, banners, and social media content as a potential alternative to Canva.
- The premium version of Clipchamp allows web-based video editing with 4K exports, AI-generated voiceovers, and access to a library of stock media.
- Microsoft Forms offers advanced customization and template options for data collection, serving as a robust alternative to Google Forms.
- Office Agent utilizes AI to generate Word documents and PowerPoint presentations based on user prompts, templates, and specific audience requirements.
- Subscription plans include a monthly allotment of AI credits, which are required to utilize advanced features across these integrated applications.

**Why it matters**
Many users pay for Microsoft 365 primarily for core Office apps, often overlooking the suite of creative and automation tools included in their existing subscription. By leveraging these built-in features, individuals and businesses can consolidate their software stack and potentially eliminate the costs of redundant third-party services.
