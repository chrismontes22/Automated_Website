Education must go beyond the mere production of words
============================================================

Generative artificial intelligence tools like ChatGPT challenge modern education by prioritizing verbal fluency over deep understanding, forcing institutions to reconsider how they foster genuine intellectual growth and personal judgment.

### Key Points
- Large language models can automate tasks like drafting essays and summarizing research, but they lack the capacity for critical inquiry and personal responsibility.
- The author argues that education is the formation of a person capable of truth and judgment, rather than the mere production of acceptable academic outputs.
- Pedagogical strategies such as in-class writing, oral defenses, and seminar-based discussions are recommended to ensure students engage directly with complex material.
- Transparency in AI usage is encouraged to promote intellectual ownership and ensure students remain the primary agents of their own learning process.
- The rise of AI serves as a catalyst for educational renewal by highlighting the necessity of human-led inquiry and the limitations of automated information processing.

### Why it Matters
The integration of AI into classrooms risks reducing learning to the efficient production of content, which undermines the development of critical thinking and intellectual honesty. By shifting the focus back to human-led inquiry and the formation of judgment, educators can ensure that technology serves as a tool for growth rather than a substitute for the student's own cognitive development.
