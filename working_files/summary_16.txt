Google Says LLMs.txt Is Purely Speculative… For Now via @sejournal, @martinibuster
============================================================

Google Search Advocate John Mueller clarified that LLMs.txt files remain speculative and unnecessary, suggesting site owners focus on ensuring AI agents are not blocked from accessing their web content.

### Key Points
- Google Search Central confirms that publishers do not require special files like LLMs.txt to appear in generative AI search results.
- John Mueller described LLMs.txt as a speculative convention that is not currently utilized by major AI systems.
- Chrome Lighthouse documentation refers to LLMs.txt as an emerging proposal rather than a functional industry standard.
- Mueller advocates for the WebMCP protocol, which allows AI agents to interact with website functionality beyond simple content crawling.
- The most critical step for site owners is ensuring that robots.txt or other security measures do not inadvertently block AI agents.

### Why it Matters
The distinction between speculative file formats and functional standards helps site owners avoid unnecessary technical work while focusing on actual AI accessibility. By prioritizing open access for agents, publishers ensure their content remains discoverable and functional for emerging AI-driven search and task-oriented tools.
