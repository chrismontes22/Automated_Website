No Major Apple Watch Redesign Expected This Year
============================================================

Apple analyst Mark Gurman reports that upcoming Apple Watch models will likely feature no major design changes, while also noting updates on the foldable iPhone and HomePod development.

**Key points**
- Bloomberg’s Mark Gurman stated that the next generation of Apple Watches will maintain the current design aesthetic.
- Rumors regarding a radical "Apple Watch X" redesign or new magnetic band attachment systems remain unconfirmed for this year.
- The Apple Watch Ultra will continue to follow an iterative design path rather than receiving a significant structural overhaul.
- Reports suggest a major redesign for the Apple Watch lineup is unlikely to arrive for at least two years.
- Gurman also indicated that a new full-sized HomePod is currently in development at Apple.
- The anticipated foldable iPhone is expected to launch after the release of the iPhone 18 Pro models.

**Why it matters: **
These insights suggest that Apple is prioritizing internal hardware improvements over external form-factor changes for its wearable devices in the near term. This strategy signals a period of design stability for consumers while shifting focus toward long-term product development for foldable technology and smart home hardware.
