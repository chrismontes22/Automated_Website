Study Uses Age Of Empires Scenario Editor To Illustrate Why It’s Farcical To Treat AI As Cognizant
============================================================

University of York researcher Adrian de Wynter argues that current language learning models lack consciousness, warning that anthropomorphizing AI technology obscures its true nature and potential societal risks.

### Key Points
- Adrian de Wynter’s study demonstrates that real-time strategy game mechanics can replicate the "emergent capabilities" often falsely attributed to AI consciousness.
- Research indicates 57% of over 300 academic papers reviewed since 2022 incorrectly assume AI models possess human-like consciousness.
- The study highlights that users frequently project personality onto AI due to its conversational interface, despite the underlying technology being standard code.
- Critics warn that the industry’s push to simulate human thought masks the economic goals of workforce automation and massive data collection.
- AI-driven systems have been linked to harmful outcomes, including instances of self-harm and substance abuse, due to their programmed positive reinforcement loops.

### Why it Matters
The widespread tendency to anthropomorphize AI creates a dangerous disconnect between public perception and the actual capabilities of language learning models. By treating these tools as sentient entities rather than automated software, society risks misallocating resources and ignoring the significant ethical and economic consequences of unchecked AI development.
