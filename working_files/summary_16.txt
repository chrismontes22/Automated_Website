Leo Shows He’s the Planet’s Pope by Taking on AI
============================================================

Pope Leo has released his first encyclical, Magnifica Humanitas, which characterizes artificial intelligence as a significant threat to humanity, the environment, and global social justice standards.

### Key Points
- Pope Leo argues that AI development contributes to ecological degradation, resource depletion, and pollution through intensive technological consumption.
- The document links the rise of AI to historical patterns of colonialism, slavery, and the exploitation of labor and human creativity.
- Pope Leo formally apologizes for the Catholic Church’s historical role in sanctioning the slave trade and the Doctrine of Discovery.
- The encyclical advocates for climate reparations, self-determination for frontline communities, and the protection of the right to migrate or remain on one's land.
- The text emphasizes that human dignity must be prioritized over the "idolatry of profit" and the structural inequities inherent in modern global markets.

### Why it Matters
This encyclical signals a major shift in the Vatican’s stance on emerging technology, framing AI as a moral issue that requires a framework of social justice and active repair. By linking technological development to climate change and historical colonial harms, the Church is positioning itself as a central voice in the global debate over corporate accountability and human rights.
