Canada’s Bill C-22: Why Signal, Apple, and top VPNs are fighting the 'surveillance' law
============================================================

Major technology companies and privacy advocates are opposing Canada’s Bill C-22, arguing that proposed metadata retention and encryption requirements threaten user security and could lead to mass surveillance.

### Key Points
- Tech firms including Signal, Windscribe, Google, and Apple warn that the bill could force the creation of security backdoors.
- The Canadian government claims the legislation modernizes lawful data access and denies that it mandates the weakening of encryption.
- Privacy experts and companies argue that mandatory metadata retention creates a "national surveillance map" of citizen movements and communications.
- A joint report from Citizen Lab and the Canadian Civil Liberties Association labeled the proposed legislation as constitutionally fatal.
- Minister of Public Safety Gary Anandasangaree aims to pass the bill before the parliamentary summer break on June 19.

### Why it Matters
The outcome of this legislation could set a significant global precedent for how G7 nations balance law enforcement access with digital privacy rights. If passed, the bill may force major international service providers to choose between complying with Canadian law or exiting the market entirely.
