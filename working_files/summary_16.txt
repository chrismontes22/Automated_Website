Six principles for AI democracy
============================================================

Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham has proposed a six-point framework for AI governance in the UK, focusing on democratic oversight, public service augmentation, and corporate accountability for infrastructure.

### Key Points
- Burnham proposes binding national guardrails to prevent tech corporations from bypassing state regulation and to mandate disclosure of high-risk AI incidents.
- The plan prioritizes using AI to improve public services, including NHS triage systems, welfare claims assistance, and reducing the 80,000-case criminal justice backlog.
- Datacenter operators would face statutory requirements to fund local electrical grid upgrades and provide direct financial contributions to host communities.
- The framework suggests wealth redistribution measures, such as giving voters a stake in future AI profits to mitigate fears of human economic displacement.
- New transparency rules would be established to protect UK artists from unauthorized data scraping and the commercialization of AI-generated content.
- The government would utilize the UK AI Security Institute to provide independent, public data on AI’s impact on employment, energy consumption, and the environment.

### Why it Matters
This proposal signals a shift toward a more interventionist approach to AI regulation that prioritizes state capacity and public benefit over corporate autonomy. By framing AI policy as a matter of democratic sovereignty, it highlights the growing political pressure to ensure that technological advancements do not undermine local economies or public infrastructure.
