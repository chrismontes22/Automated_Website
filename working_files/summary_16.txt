Ray-Ban Meta privacy problems go from bad to worse with nightmarish 'super sensing' feature
============================================================

Meta is reportedly testing a "super sensing" feature for Ray-Ban Meta smartglasses that could enable continuous AI-powered recording and potentially disable the mandatory LED privacy indicator light.

### Key Points
- The "super sensing" prototype aims to capture constant audio, video, and photographic data to create a searchable AI-indexed catalog of the user's daily activities.
- Meta is currently testing this feature internally, with the possibility of deploying it to existing Ray-Ban Meta smartglasses via a future software update.
- The company recently implemented a software patch that disables the camera if the recording LED indicator is tampered with or obstructed.
- Meta is actively moderating third-party tools designed to bypass the LED recording light to prevent unauthorized or covert surveillance.
- This development follows the emergence of other life-logging devices, such as the camera-free MemoMind One, which uses audio to summarize daily events.

### Why it Matters
This potential feature raises significant privacy concerns regarding the ability to record individuals without their knowledge or consent. If implemented, it could force a major regulatory and public debate over the balance between AI-driven convenience and the fundamental right to privacy in public spaces.
