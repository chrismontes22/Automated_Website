A24’s Bad Excuse For Its Controversial Google AI Partnership Isn’t Impressing Fans: ‘We’d Rather Have A Seat At The Table’
============================================================

Independent film studio A24 faces significant backlash from its fanbase and creative partners after announcing a $75 million artificial intelligence research partnership with technology giant Google DeepMind.

### Key Points
- A24 entered a $75 million research agreement with Google DeepMind to develop new production tools and workflows.
- Company representative Sophia Shin stated the partnership aims to give artists a voice in shaping future creative technology.
- Fans and online communities, including the r/A24 subreddit, have expressed widespread disapproval of the studio's involvement with AI.
- Director Kane Parsons publicly distanced his "Backrooms" project from the partnership, citing concerns shared by many industry creators.

### Why it Matters
This partnership highlights the growing tension between independent creative studios and the integration of generative AI in film production. The negative reception suggests that audience loyalty and director autonomy remain critical risks for production companies attempting to adopt emerging technologies.
