FOCUS specification eyes AI token economics as AI billing complexity hits a new frontier
============================================================

The FinOps Foundation is expanding its FOCUS open standard to address AI token economics and complex billing challenges as enterprises struggle to track rising artificial intelligence infrastructure costs.

### Key Points
- The FinOps Foundation launched FOCUS 1.4 at the FinOps X 2026 conference to standardize billing data across cloud providers and AI technology stacks.
- Approximately 68% of organizations spending over $100 million annually are currently utilizing or testing FOCUS-formatted data for financial reporting.
- Measuring AI inference costs requires granular tracking at the per-user, per-session, and per-request levels, creating significant data engineering and cardinality challenges.
- The working group is evaluating whether to develop a new observability dataset or integrate with existing frameworks like the OpenTelemetry project.
- FOCUS is broadening its scope beyond data engineers to support finance, leadership, and procurement teams with a unified financial language.

### Why it Matters
Standardizing AI cost data is critical for enterprises attempting to manage the high financial overhead of modern AI factories and GPU clusters. By providing a common billing language, FOCUS helps organizations reduce the time and expense required to normalize complex data for accounting and analytical decision-making.
