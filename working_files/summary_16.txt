Microsoft deleted a hacked user's 25-year-old Xbox account, along with his OneDrive baby photos
============================================================

Microsoft faces growing scrutiny over digital account security after a streamer lost access to thousands of euros in games and personal photos following a permanent account suspension.

### Key Points
- Streamer Joshua Khane lost access to his Microsoft account and OneDrive data after an unauthorized user compromised his security credentials.
- Microsoft informed Khane that his account was unrecoverable, requiring him to repurchase his entire digital library dating back to 2001.
- The incident follows Sony’s announcement to cease physical PlayStation disc production by 2028, fueling concerns over digital-only ownership models.
- Microsoft is reportedly planning to abandon physical media for its upcoming "Helix" console, expected to launch between 2027 and 2028.
- A recent legal precedent in Brazil forced Microsoft to restore a hijacked account and pay damages, highlighting potential regulatory challenges for digital storefronts.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the significant consumer risks associated with the industry-wide transition from physical media to cloud-based digital storefronts. As companies move toward exclusive digital ecosystems, users face the potential loss of entire libraries and personal data if account security protocols fail or access is revoked.
