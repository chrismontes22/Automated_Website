As Google sits back and watches, terrible ads are ruining Android’s free app ecosystem
============================================================

Google faces growing criticism for allowing predatory, intrusive advertising practices to dominate the Android Play Store, effectively degrading the user experience and stifling independent app development on the platform.

### Key Points
- Modern Android apps frequently utilize "dark patterns," including unclosable interstitial video ads, deceptive playable mini-games, and unauthorized notification spam.
- Aggressive ad SDKs from major brokers have become the primary revenue source for developers, as traditional banner ad payouts have significantly declined.
- Independent developers are increasingly leaving the ecosystem because they cannot compete with low-effort, ad-heavy "copycat" apps that prioritize data harvesting over utility.
- Critics argue that Google, as the owner of both the Android platform and the AdMob network, has a financial conflict of interest that discourages strict enforcement.
- While Google claims to use AI and developer verification to block policy-violating apps, users report that fraudulent and hostile advertising remains widespread.

### Why it Matters
The proliferation of toxic advertising is eroding user trust in the Android ecosystem, potentially rendering the platform's library of free utilities unusable. If Google fails to regulate these predatory practices, it risks permanently damaging the reputation of its mobile software marketplace and alienating its massive global user base.
