We Asked the ‘Future of Truth’ Author to Explain How He Used AI. It Didn’t Go Well
============================================================

**Author Steve Rosenbaum faces scrutiny after his book about AI-driven misinformation, "The Future of Truth," was found to contain synthetic quotes and significant AI-generated content.**

### Key Points
- Steve Rosenbaum admitted to using AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude for research, brainstorming, and language refinement in his book.
- *The New York Times* identified over six made-up or misattributed quotes within the text of the book.
- AI-detection software Pangram estimated the book was 53 percent AI-generated, with an additional 9 percent likely AI-assisted.
- WIRED retracted its excerpt of the book after questions arose regarding the extent of Rosenbaum's reliance on generative AI tools.
- Rosenbaum maintains that his core ideas and reporting are original, despite his refusal to clarify which specific passages were AI-assisted.

### Why it Matters
This controversy highlights the growing tension between traditional editorial standards and the increasing integration of generative AI in the publishing industry. It underscores the difficulty of verifying authorship and accuracy in an era where AI tools can easily blur the lines between human-led research and synthetic content.
