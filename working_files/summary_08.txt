8 phishing red flags hidden in everyday emails
============================================================

PCWorld has identified eight critical red flags in routine emails, warning that human vigilance remains the most essential defense against evolving phishing scams targeting personal and financial data.

### Key Points
- Watch for urgent demands for money, cryptocurrency, or sensitive information, as scammers use pressure to bypass critical thinking.
- Verify suspicious account activity alerts by navigating directly to the service provider’s website rather than clicking links within an email.
- Inspect sender addresses for generic domains or random character strings that do not match official company communications.
- Avoid opening unexpected attachments, which are common vehicles for malware, and verify their legitimacy through independent communication channels.
- Check URLs by hovering over buttons or links to ensure the destination matches the expected service before clicking.
- Use pre-determined family passwords to verify the identity of callers, protecting against sophisticated AI voice-spoofing attacks.

### Why it Matters
Despite advancements in antivirus software, human error remains the primary vulnerability that allows cybercriminals to access private accounts and financial assets. Adopting a skeptical approach to unsolicited digital communication is a necessary practice for maintaining personal security in an era of increasingly convincing, AI-assisted scams.
