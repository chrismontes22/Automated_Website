Google's latest Android 17 beta fixes Bluetooth headaches and Gemini crashes
============================================================

Google has officially launched the Android 17 QPR2 Beta 1 for compatible Pixel devices, initiating testing for the upcoming December 2026 Feature Drop with several critical system stability improvements.

### Key Points
- The update, build number CP41.260701.005, is available for devices ranging from the Pixel 6a through the Pixel 10 series, including the Pixel Fold and Pixel Tablet.
- Google addressed a system crash that caused devices to reboot unexpectedly when launching the Gemini AI assistant.
- New patches resolve Bluetooth reconnection failures and eliminate lock screen media control glitches that occurred when waking the device.
- Developers and users can access the beta via the Android Beta Program or the Android Emulator.
- Support for the original Pixel 6 and 6 Pro has officially ended, marking the final beta track for those specific models.

### Why it Matters
This update prioritizes system stability and bug fixes over new features to improve the daily reliability of the Pixel user experience. By addressing critical crashes and connectivity issues, Google aims to refine the software performance of its hardware lineup ahead of the official December release.
