Windows Central Podcast: The path ahead for Xbox with Jez Corden
============================================================

The Windows Central Podcast examines the current state of Xbox under CEO Asha Sharma, addressing studio closure rumors, rising hardware costs, and the future of next-generation console strategy.

### Key Points
- New Xbox CEO Asha Sharma is navigating corporate belt-tightening driven by massive Microsoft-wide investments in artificial intelligence infrastructure.
- Rising component costs, specifically RAM, have rendered the traditional console subsidy model unsustainable for both Microsoft and Sony.
- Microsoft is evaluating the future of first-party studios, including potential sales or closures of teams like Ninja Theory and Undead Labs.
- The upcoming "Helix" console project faces internal debates regarding whether to function as an open Windows-based device or a closed ecosystem.
- New technology codenamed "Positron" may allow users to convert physical disc ownership into permanent digital licenses on their Microsoft accounts.

### Why it Matters
These shifts signal a fundamental change in the gaming industry as hardware manufacturers struggle to balance premium pricing with the declining profitability of traditional consoles. The potential move toward an open Windows ecosystem or all-digital models reflects a broader effort to adapt to the dominance of free-to-play titles and rising development costs.
