Apple Begins Testing Controversial Chinese Memory Chips
============================================================

Apple is currently testing DRAM memory chips from Chinese manufacturer ChangXin Memory Technologies as the company seeks to diversify its supply chain and mitigate rising global component costs.

### Key Points
- Apple is conducting technical validation of CXMT memory chips, a process typically required before approving a new production supplier.
- CXMT is currently the world's fourth-largest DRAM producer, holding an 11% global market share that is projected to reach 15% by 2028.
- Apple is lobbying U.S. officials to ensure CXMT is not added to the Commerce Department’s Entity List, which would restrict commercial trade.
- The company aims to use Chinese-sourced memory specifically for devices sold within the Chinese market to preserve supply from Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron.
- DRAM contract prices surged by up to 60% in early 2026, prompting Apple to seek additional leverage against major memory manufacturers.

### Why it Matters
Qualifying a new supplier provides Apple with critical leverage to negotiate lower component costs and creates a hedge against future supply shortages driven by AI server demand. Successfully navigating these geopolitical tensions could allow the company to stabilize pricing for its consumer electronics lineup while managing complex regulatory risks in the United States and China.
