Dragon Age Lead Writer Condemns AI As A ‘Virulent Plague’
============================================================

Dragon Age co-creator David Gaider has publicly condemned the use of generative AI in video game development, labeling the technology a "virulent plague" that threatens industry standards and ethics.

### Key Points
- David Gaider, lead writer for the Dragon Age series, criticized generative AI for being "soulless" and ineffective at creative iteration.
- Gaider argued that AI models rely on the unauthorized use of creator data, raising significant moral and potential legal concerns.
- The veteran developer warned that AI tools force human workers to perform tedious cleanup tasks rather than automating drudgery as originally promised.
- He expressed concern that automating entry-level tasks will prevent junior developers from gaining the necessary experience to advance in the industry.
- Gaider stated that AI-generated prototypes hinder team learning, as they fail to provide developers with a deep understanding of the final product.

### Why it Matters
This critique highlights a growing divide between industry leadership pushing for AI integration and veteran developers concerned about long-term creative quality and workforce development. The debate underscores critical questions regarding intellectual property rights and the future of professional training within the gaming sector.
