Ozzy Osbourne as an AI Hologram? 'This Isn't ChatGPT With Dad's Face,' Son Says
============================================================

The family of the late rock icon Ozzy Osbourne is partnering with technology firm Hyperreal to develop an interactive AI-powered digital avatar to preserve his musical legacy.

### Key Points
- Sharon and Jack Osbourne are developing a "digital imprint" of the late singer to allow fans to interact with a virtual version of the rock star.
- The project utilizes Hyperreal, a company previously responsible for creating an interactive AI avatar of Marvel Comics legend Stan Lee.
- Jack Osbourne clarified the system is a "closed AI" that relies exclusively on verified historical data rather than generative internet-based models like ChatGPT.
- The family intends for the digital figure to answer questions in Ozzy’s own voice using information derived from his life, interviews, and writings.
- This initiative follows similar digital celebrity projects, including the ABBA Voyage concert experience and holographic performances featuring the late Whitney Houston.

### Why it Matters
This project highlights the growing trend of using AI to manage and monetize the legacies of deceased celebrities through interactive digital experiences. It raises significant questions regarding the ethics of posthumous representation and how families balance fan expectations with the preservation of an artist's public persona.
