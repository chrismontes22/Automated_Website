Internal Microsoft employee surveys reveal how sentiment is changing inside the software giant
============================================================

Microsoft’s latest internal employee survey reveals that staff feel increasingly energized and empowered, though they report declining satisfaction with managerial coaching, feedback, and overall team motivation.

### Key Points
- Microsoft’s "Thriving" score, measuring feelings of empowerment and meaningful work, rose 3 points to 79.
- Employees rated security prioritization and team inclusion as top company strengths, both scoring 88 and 86 respectively.
- Managerial effectiveness remains steady at 85%, but scores for coaching and clear feedback dropped by 5 and 4 points.
- The survey included feedback from 71% of the workforce, totaling nearly 265,000 comments in the "Employee Signals" report.
- Chief People Officer Amy Coleman acknowledged employee uncertainty resulting from recent organizational changes and increased performance pressure.

### Why it Matters
These survey results highlight a growing disconnect between high-level corporate culture and the day-to-day support provided by middle management during a period of intense organizational transition. Addressing these gaps in leadership communication and coaching is critical for Microsoft to maintain employee retention and productivity amid ongoing shifts in company strategy.
