5 abandoned Android launchers I wish were still alive today
============================================================

**Five iconic but discontinued Android launchers, including Nokia’s Z Launcher and Yahoo’s Aviate, remain influential for their innovative interface designs and unique features that shaped modern mobile home screens.**

### Key Points
- Nokia’s Z Launcher introduced a unique "scribble search" feature that allowed users to trace letters on the screen to quickly locate apps and contacts.
- Yahoo’s Aviate Launcher utilized contextual awareness to dynamically adjust home screen layouts based on a user's specific location and time of day.
- Evie Launcher gained popularity for balancing a clean, out-of-the-box user experience with deep customization options before its removal from the Play Store.
- Apex Launcher, once a top-tier customization tool, declined in quality following version 4.0 due to intrusive advertisements and performance issues.
- ADW Launcher 2 remains technically available on the Play Store but has not received a software update since September 2018, rendering it incompatible with modern devices.

### Why it Matters
The rise and fall of these launchers highlight the rapid evolution of Android software and the ongoing challenge of maintaining niche, feature-rich applications. While many of these projects were abandoned, their innovative concepts continue to influence current developers and the broader mobile user experience.
