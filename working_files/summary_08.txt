Carl Pei tells Apple he's stealing its customers, as if iMessage isn't holding them hostage
============================================================

Nothing founder Carl Pei has publicly challenged Apple’s market dominance, aiming to attract dissatisfied iPhone users by expanding the brand's unique, transparent hardware presence into US Best Buy stores.

### Key Points
- Carl Pei, founder of the London-based smartphone company Nothing, launched a public campaign to convert iPhone users to his brand.
- Nothing differentiates its products through transparent hardware designs and highly customized software interfaces.
- The company plans to increase its US market share by expanding its retail availability through a partnership with Best Buy.
- Industry analysts note that Nothing faces significant competition from established giants like Apple, Samsung, Xiaomi, and OPPO.
- Critics argue that Nothing lacks a comprehensive services ecosystem, such as iMessage or Apple Watch, to effectively challenge Apple's user retention.

### Why it Matters
This expansion represents a strategic effort to move beyond niche marketing and compete directly with industry leaders in physical retail spaces. While the brand offers a distinct aesthetic alternative, its long-term success depends on whether it can overcome the strong ecosystem lock-in that keeps consumers within Apple’s walled garden.
