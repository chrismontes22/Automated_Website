Escaping the loop? Google speaks up about that huge Pixel booting problem
============================================================

Google is investigating persistent bootloop issues affecting various Pixel smartphone models following recent software updates, advising affected users to contact official support channels for device-specific troubleshooting and repair assistance.

### Key Points
- Reports indicate Pixel devices are freezing on the "G" logo or rebooting after the March, April, and May security updates.
- Affected models include a wide range of devices, spanning from the Pixel 4a to the newer Pixel 9 and Pixel 10 series.
- Some users report receiving "Cannot load Android system and data corrupt" error messages during the failed startup process.
- Google has officially acknowledged the issue on its Issue Tracker and is currently investigating the root cause.
- The company recommends that users submit a support ticket explicitly mentioning the "reboot loop issue after recent software update" to receive tailored assistance.

### Why it Matters
This ongoing technical failure impacts a broad range of Pixel users, potentially rendering their primary communication devices unusable for extended periods. The lack of a universal software patch forces customers to rely on individual support interventions, which may lead to significant service delays and hardware repair requirements.
