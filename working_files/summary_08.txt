Cardano—Once The Third-Largest Cryptocurrency—Hits 6-Year Low: Here’s Why
============================================================

Cardano’s ADA token dropped to a six-year low of $0.21 on Wednesday, driven by a broader cryptocurrency market selloff and the cancellation of the project's flagship annual summit.

### Key Points
- Cardano’s market value fell to $7.7 billion, a significant decline from its 2021 all-time high of $91.6 billion.
- The Cardano Foundation canceled its flagship summit after a community vote narrowly rejected a $2 million funding proposal.
- Bitcoin prices dipped below $67,000 following a $2.5 million sale of holdings by Michael Saylor’s Strategy.
- Major cryptocurrencies including Ethereum, XRP, and Tron also experienced notable price declines throughout the week.
- Approximately 67% of the total Cardano supply is currently held by large-scale investors owning at least 1 million tokens.

### Why it Matters
The decline highlights the volatility of the cryptocurrency market and the challenges of decentralized governance models in managing project funding. Investors are closely monitoring these trends as broader market pessimism impacts both established tokens and smaller blockchain ecosystems.
