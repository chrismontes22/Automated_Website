AI is reducing hours of work to minutes. Some employees say they're just as busy.
============================================================

Tech workers at major companies like Amazon, Google, and Apple are utilizing artificial intelligence tools to significantly reduce time spent on documentation, coding, and complex data analysis tasks.

### Key Points
- Employees use AI tools like Gemini, Claude Code, and internal Amazon systems to automate reporting, meeting summaries, and code reviews.
- Tasks that previously required hours or days, such as drafting product documents or analyzing six months of meeting notes, are now completed in minutes.
- Some engineers report that initial time savings are being reinvested into building complex automation pipelines, leading to longer work hours in the short term.
- AI allows professionals to reach the "starting line" of projects faster, shifting their focus from administrative scaffolding to high-level strategic decision-making.
- Software engineers at startups like Double Nickel report that AI-assisted coding and automated reviews have accelerated development cycles from weeks to days.

### Why it Matters
While AI significantly boosts individual productivity by automating repetitive tasks, the immediate result is often a shift in workload rather than a reduction in total hours. This transition highlights a broader industry trend where efficiency gains are being redirected toward more complex problem-solving and infrastructure development.
