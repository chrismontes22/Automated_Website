I used the MemoMind One: these camera-free smart glasses made me feel like 007
============================================================

The new MemoMind One smart glasses from XGIMI feature camera-free Micro-LED waveguide technology, offering users integrated AI utilities, real-time translation, and navigation in a lightweight, stylish wearable design.

### Key Points
- The MemoMind One utilizes dual Micro-LED waveguides capable of 2000 nits brightness for clear outdoor visibility.
- Available in three styles—Nomad, Gotham, and Archive—the glasses weigh 46g and feature audio tuned by Harman.
- Core features include turn-by-turn navigation, audio transcription, and notification mirroring without requiring a camera.
- Crowdfunding pricing starts at $399 for the base model and $499 for prescription versions, with deliveries expected in August 2026.
- The device offers a free tier for essential AI tools, with an optional $19 monthly subscription for advanced features.

### Why it Matters
The MemoMind One represents a competitive alternative to existing waveguide smart glasses by prioritizing high-quality audio and outdoor display performance at a lower price point. Its entry into the market signals a growing consumer interest in camera-free, notification-focused wearables that emphasize all-day comfort and utility.
