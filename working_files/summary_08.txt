Supreme Court restricts use of geofence warrants
============================================================

The Supreme Court has restricted the use of geofence warrants, requiring lower courts to determine if police requests for mass location data from tech companies violate Fourth Amendment protections.

### Key Points
- The 6-3 ruling mandates a review of whether geofence warrants, which track cellphone users near crime scenes, constitute unreasonable government intrusion.
- The case originated from a Richmond, Virginia, bank robbery where police used Google location data to identify suspect Okello Chatrie.
- Geofence warrants allow law enforcement to search millions of users' location histories to identify potential suspects without prior individualized suspicion.
- Justice Elena Kagan authored the majority opinion, while Justice Samuel Alito dissented, criticizing the court's intervention in digital privacy standards.

### Why it Matters
This decision forces a legal reevaluation of how law enforcement accesses private digital data held by major technology firms. It establishes a critical precedent for balancing modern investigative techniques against individual privacy rights under the Constitution.
