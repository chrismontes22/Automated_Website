Ukrainian Police Arrest Three Hackers Who Compromised 610,000 Roblox Accounts and Sold Them for $225,000
============================================================

Ukrainian authorities in Lviv arrested three individuals for orchestrating a massive cybercrime operation that compromised over 610,000 Roblox accounts and generated approximately $225,000 in illicit profits since 2025.

### Key Points
- The suspects, aged 19 to 22, used infostealer malware disguised as game-enhancement tools to harvest user login credentials.
- Law enforcement seized $35,000 in cash and 64 electronic devices, including computers and mobile phones, during ten targeted searches.
- Stolen accounts were categorized by the rarity of in-game items and Robux balances before being sold on Russian-language platforms.
- The group identified at least 357 high-value accounts among the 610,000 compromised profiles.
- The suspects face charges of theft and unauthorized interference with IT systems, carrying potential prison sentences of up to 15 years.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing financial risks associated with virtual economies where in-game assets and rare digital items hold significant real-world monetary value. As platforms like Roblox become increasingly lucrative targets for cybercriminals, users must remain vigilant against malware disguised as legitimate third-party gaming tools.
