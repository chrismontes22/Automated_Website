Meta’s Pursuit of the 'Careless People' Author Is Relentless and Self-Defeating
============================================================

Former Meta director Sarah Wynn-Williams is suing the company to vacate an arbitration ruling that prevents her from promoting her memoir, Careless People, citing free speech violations.

### Key Points
- Sarah Wynn-Williams received a $780,000 severance package in 2017 contingent on a non-disparagement agreement with Meta.
- An arbitrator issued an interim ruling in March 2025 barring Wynn-Williams from promoting her book, which Meta claims violates her contract.
- Wynn-Williams filed a lawsuit on June 25 seeking to move the dispute to public court, arguing the restrictions unfairly limit her professional speech.
- Meta faces potential $50,000 fines for each instance of alleged book promotion, which the company monitors through surveillance of her public appearances.
- A more comprehensive arbitration hearing regarding the dispute is scheduled for October.

### Why it Matters
This case highlights the growing tension between corporate non-disparagement agreements and the public interest in transparency regarding Big Tech's internal operations. The aggressive legal pursuit of a former employee risks damaging Meta's public reputation and reinforces narratives about the company's efforts to suppress criticism.
