This startup wants to turn the world into a searchable video feed, starting in San Francisco
============================================================

San Francisco startup Orchestra is deploying a network of AI-powered street cameras to index physical world activity, aiming to sell structured data analytics to law enforcement and businesses.

### Key Points
- Orchestra has installed over 100 street-facing cameras in San Francisco and plans to add 900 more across commercial corridors within six months.
- The company uses AI to convert live video into structured data, identifying objects and incidents while blurring faces to maintain user anonymity.
- Potential customers include police departments for evidence, insurance firms for accident analysis, and autonomous-vehicle companies for navigation data.
- The startup is developing an AI agent called "Robocop" that monitors 911 dispatch data to automatically package relevant footage into evidence files.
- Founders include CEO Drake Burciaga, COO Stephania Stavropoulos, CTO Bruno Beccaria, and AI lead Michael Coen.

### Why it Matters
This initiative represents a significant expansion of private surveillance infrastructure, raising complex questions about the balance between public safety and individual privacy. As the company seeks to scale its "Omniscience" model globally, it faces the challenge of navigating growing public and regulatory backlash against AI-enabled monitoring technologies.
