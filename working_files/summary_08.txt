The Reason Why European iPads Don't Come With A Charger In The Box
============================================================

Apple has removed power adapters from iPad and MacBook packaging in Europe, citing compliance with European Commission directives aimed at reducing electronic waste through standardized USB-C charging requirements.

### Key Points
- Apple stopped including power bricks with new iPad Pro, iPad Air, and MacBook models sold within the European Union.
- The policy change follows the European Commission’s Common Charger Directive, which mandates USB-C ports for mobile devices and laptops.
- Customers must now purchase power adapters separately, effectively increasing the total cost of new Apple hardware for European consumers.
- New EU regulations effective mid-2025 require improved device durability, energy labeling, and seven-year availability for spare parts like batteries and screens.
- Apple maintains its current pricing structure despite the removal of the previously included charging accessories.

### Why it Matters
This shift highlights the ongoing friction between Apple and European regulators regarding environmental mandates and consumer pricing. By unbundling essential accessories, Apple has effectively passed the cost of regulatory compliance onto the consumer while navigating strict new sustainability standards.
