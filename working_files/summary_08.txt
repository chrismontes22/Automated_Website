Mira Murati’s deposition pulled back the curtain on Sam Altman’s ouster
============================================================

New testimony and documents from the Musk v. Altman trial reveal former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati’s complex, pivotal role in the November 2023 ouster of CEO Sam Altman.

### Key Points
- Former board member Helen Toner testified that Murati and cofounder Ilya Sutskever provided the documentation that fueled the board's decision to fire Altman.
- Murati initially served as interim CEO following the November 16, 2023, board vote before being replaced by Emmett Shear.
- Evidence shows Murati simultaneously communicated with Altman about his potential return while privately supporting the board's initial decision to remove him.
- Over 750 OpenAI employees signed a letter threatening to resign and join Microsoft, with Murati listed as the first signatory.
- A 2022 document authored by Murati detailed long-standing internal frustrations regarding Altman’s management style, lack of transparency, and chaotic project prioritization.

### Why it Matters
The revelations highlight the internal power struggles and leadership instability that nearly dismantled one of the world's most influential artificial intelligence companies. This insight into the board's decision-making process underscores the significant governance challenges inherent in balancing rapid AI development with corporate oversight and transparency.
