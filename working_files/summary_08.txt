SpaceX No. 2 exec donates $320 million of stock to Trump Accounts
============================================================

SpaceX President Gwynne Shotwell plans to donate $320 million in company stock to government-backed Trump Accounts, providing financial assets to over 2 million children, primarily in Texas.

### Key Points
- Gwynne Shotwell is donating one share of SpaceX Class A stock, valued at $165, to each of more than 2 million children.
- The total donation represents approximately 20% of the Class A SpaceX shares currently owned or optioned by Shotwell.
- Trump Accounts were launched on July 4 to provide investment vehicles for children, with eligibility for $1,000 in government seed money for those born between 2025 and 2028.
- The Treasury Department recently updated guidelines to allow individual stock donations into these accounts, which were originally designed to hold index funds.
- SpaceX is scheduled to join the Nasdaq 100 index this week, increasing the company's presence in broader investment portfolios.

### Why it Matters
This donation marks a significant shift in the utility of Trump Accounts by introducing individual corporate stock into a program previously restricted to index funds. The move highlights the growing intersection between private sector leadership and federal financial initiatives aimed at long-term wealth accumulation for children.
