The Unihertz Titan 2 Elite offers a modern take on the physical keyboard smartphone, combining a 120Hz OLED display with reliable performance for users seeking a tactile typing experience.

### Key Points
- The device features a 4-inch 120Hz OLED display and is powered by a MediaTek Dimensity 7400 chipset.
- It includes a capacitive physical keyboard that supports touch-based scrolling and customizable app shortcuts.
- The phone is equipped with a 4,000mAh silicon-carbon battery capable of lasting two days on a single charge.
- Users can opt for an upgraded Dimensity 8400 processor variant for increased performance needs.
- The handset is priced at approximately $500 and runs on the Android operating system.

### Why it Matters
The Titan 2 Elite demonstrates that there is still a viable market for niche hardware that prioritizes tactile input and intentional usage over standard touchscreen designs. By successfully integrating modern Android capabilities with a classic form factor, Unihertz provides a functional alternative for users looking to reduce digital distraction.
