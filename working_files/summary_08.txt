Bosses Want You to Use AI but They're Not Setting a Good Example, Study Says
============================================================

A new Microsoft Work Trend Index report reveals that while employees are eager to adopt AI, a lack of clear leadership support and cultural alignment hinders workplace integration.

### Key Points
- Microsoft’s report shows 65% of AI users fear falling behind, yet only 13% feel rewarded for their innovation efforts.
- Data from 100,000 Copilot chats indicates 49% of users leverage AI for cognitive tasks like problem-solving and creative thinking.
- The use of AI agents, which are customizable bots capable of independent tasks, has increased 15 times year over year.
- Only 26% of surveyed employees report that their leadership provides clear, consistent guidance on AI implementation.
- Managers who actively model AI usage see a 30-point increase in employee trust regarding the adoption of agentic AI tools.

### Why it Matters
- The findings suggest that successful AI adoption depends more on organizational culture and leadership behavior than on technical capability alone. Companies that fail to provide clear support and resources risk stalling productivity gains despite significant investments in generative AI tools.
