Iran reinstates some internet access but restrictions remain for most
============================================================

Iranian authorities have partially restored internet access following a record-breaking 2,000-hour nationwide blackout, though severe state-imposed restrictions and filtering systems continue to limit connectivity for the general population.

### Key Points
- The three-month internet shutdown, the longest in global history, affected 90 million people during the ongoing conflict with the United States and Israel.
- Major global platforms including YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Facebook remain blocked, with no current plans for reinstatement.
- The government has implemented a tiered "Internet Pro" system, granting varying levels of access based on professional status and state-approved classifications.
- Technical infrastructure remains unstable, with reports of blocked protocols, throttled data packets, and widespread malfunctions across mobile and landline networks.
- A lucrative black market for VPNs has emerged, leaving citizens vulnerable to malware and scams while attempting to bypass state-controlled filters.

### Why it Matters
The continued restriction of digital infrastructure severely hampers Iran's economy and limits the ability of citizens to access global information during wartime. This tiered access model signals a shift toward a state-controlled "intranet," creating significant political friction between the reformist administration and hardline factions within the government.
