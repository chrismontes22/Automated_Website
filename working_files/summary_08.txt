Alleged Scattered Spider Member Extradited to US
============================================================

The U.S. Department of Justice has extradited 19-year-old Peter Stokes from Finland to face charges for his alleged role in the Scattered Spider hacking group’s international cybercrime operations.

### Key Points
- Peter Stokes, a dual U.S. and Estonian citizen, faces charges of conspiracy, computer intrusion, and fraud.
- The Scattered Spider group is linked to over 100 network intrusions resulting in more than $100 million in ransom payments.
- Prosecutors allege Stokes attempted to extort $8 million from a luxury jewelry retailer, causing over $2 million in damages.
- The FBI and Department of Justice coordinated the arrest following a multi-year investigation into the loose association of hackers.
- Other alleged members, including Thalha Jubair and Noah Michael Urban, have recently faced legal action for similar large-scale cyber extortion schemes.

### Why it Matters
This arrest highlights the increasing success of international law enforcement in tracking and extraditing young cybercriminals associated with decentralized hacking collectives. It signals a significant escalation in the government's efforts to dismantle groups like Scattered Spider that cause massive financial disruption to global businesses.
