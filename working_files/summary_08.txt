Google's new remote attestation scheme is every bit as terrible as its old remote attestation scheme
============================================================

Google is testing a new "reCAPTCHA Mobile Verification" tool that uses hardware-level device attestation to potentially block users running independent, privacy-focused versions of the Android operating system.

### Key Points
- The experimental reCAPTCHA system utilizes a device's Trusted Platform Module (TPM) or secure enclave to verify software configurations.
- This technology allows servers to identify and restrict users who employ "de-Googled" Android alternatives like CalyxOS, PureOS, or GrapheneOS.
- Google’s initiative mirrors the controversial "Web Environment Integrity" (WEI) proposal, which faced significant public backlash for threatening user privacy and ad-blocking capabilities.
- Critics argue these measures undermine the open web by forcing devices to disclose private configuration data to third-party servers.
- The move follows multiple federal antitrust rulings against Google regarding its market dominance and restrictive business practices.

### Why it Matters
These technical lockdowns threaten the fundamental principle of user agency by allowing companies to dictate how individuals interact with the internet. By restricting interoperability, Google risks turning the open web into a closed ecosystem where privacy tools and accessibility modifications can be systematically blocked.
