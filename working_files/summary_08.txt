Gmail, Drive, and other Google apps are getting a major icon redesign
============================================================

Google is preparing a major visual redesign for its Workspace suite, updating icons for Gmail, Drive, Calendar, and other core applications with a new gradient-based design language.

### Key Points
- Google is transitioning Workspace app icons to a gradient-heavy aesthetic, moving away from the traditional four-color brand palette.
- Specific apps undergoing design changes include Gmail, Google Drive, Google Calendar, Google Sheets, Google Slides, and Google Meet.
- The updated Drive icon will prioritize green, yellow, and blue, while the Calendar icon will shift toward a more dominant blue theme.
- The Gmail icon will retain its signature envelope shape while adopting a gradient finish instead of solid colors.
- These design updates have not yet been released to the public, according to reports from 9to5Google.

### Why it Matters
This visual overhaul signals a broader shift in Google’s design language as the company integrates deeper artificial intelligence features across its productivity ecosystem. Standardizing these icons helps align the Workspace brand with Google's modern aesthetic while potentially signaling functional updates to users.
