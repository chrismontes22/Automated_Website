Gboard's next big feature could translate your sign language into text
============================================================

Google is developing a new Sign-to-text feature for its Gboard keyboard on Android, which uses artificial intelligence to convert sign language gestures into written text for users.

### Key Points
- Android Authority discovered the experimental Sign-to-text feature hidden within the code of the latest Gboard beta version.
- The tool utilizes AI to process sign language data points, though Google specifies that video footage remains on the device.
- Interface images suggest the feature will prompt users to sign directly to their phone camera to generate text input.
- Potential regional limitations remain a concern due to the wide variety of distinct sign languages used globally.
- The feature is currently in development and has not yet been officially announced or released for public use.

### Why it Matters
This development represents a significant step forward in mobile accessibility, potentially removing communication barriers for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. If successfully implemented, it could establish a new standard for inclusive design in mobile software and solidify Gboard's competitive advantage in the keyboard market.
