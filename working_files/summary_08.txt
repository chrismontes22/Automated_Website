The Korean Telecom Giant At the Center of Anthropic's Mythos Controversy
============================================================

The Trump administration has ordered Anthropic to restrict access to its advanced Claude Mythos and Fable 5 AI models following national security concerns regarding foreign access and potential vulnerabilities.

### Key Points
- The White House mandated that Anthropic revoke access to its most powerful AI models for all foreign nationals, including immigrants currently residing in the United States.
- Anthropic opted to disable the models entirely rather than implement complex nationality-based screening processes that could compromise user privacy.
- Government concerns were reportedly triggered by Amazon researchers identifying security vulnerabilities in Fable 5 and alleged ties between South Korean partner SK Telecom and China.
- SK Telecom, which participated in Anthropic’s Project Glasswing, has formally denied having any business or operational ties to China.
- Negotiations between the White House and Anthropic are ongoing as the company seeks to bring the restricted AI models back online.

### Why it Matters
This dispute highlights the growing tension between federal national security mandates and the operational realities of global AI development. The decision sets a significant precedent for how AI companies may be forced to restrict access to powerful technologies to comply with government oversight.
