How marketers are reacting to Google's big search and AI changes
============================================================

Google is introducing a suite of AI-powered agents designed to automate consumer tasks, potentially shifting the traditional purchase journey and altering how brands interact with users through search results.

### Key Points
- Google’s new AI agents can autonomously manage tasks like travel bookings, restaurant reservations, and shopping through a cross-platform "Universal Cart."
- The "Ask Advisor" tool allows marketers to build campaigns using natural language, potentially disrupting traditional agency workflows and technical ad-buying roles.
- AI models are increasingly prioritizing video, image, and audio content as critical signals for making brand recommendations to users.
- Industry experts warn that these automated agents may bypass brand websites entirely, forcing companies to prioritize brand distinctiveness to remain relevant in AI-driven search.

### Why it Matters
These developments signal a structural shift in digital marketing where AI agents act as intermediaries between consumers and brands. Businesses must now adapt their strategies to ensure their products remain visible and recommended within these automated ecosystems as traditional web traffic patterns evolve.
