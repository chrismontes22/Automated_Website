Samsung's new ad blocker finally stopped my phone from pestering me all day
============================================================

Samsung’s One UI 8.5 update introduces an intelligent notification filtering feature that automatically restricts apps sending excessive ad spam to help improve device performance and user privacy.

### Key Points
- The One UI 8.5 update, based on Android 16, began rolling out to eligible Samsung devices in early May 2026.
- Users can enable the "Block apps with excessive ads" feature within the Device Care settings menu under Intelligent blocking.
- The tool automatically places apps that frequently send notification-based advertisements into a deep sleep state.
- Combining this feature with a custom Private DNS provider, such as Adguard, can further reduce intrusive in-app advertisements.
- Private DNS settings are accessible via Connections and More connection settings on Samsung Galaxy devices.

### Why it Matters
These new tools provide users with greater control over intrusive notification spam and battery-draining background activity. By automating the management of problematic apps, Samsung helps users maintain a cleaner, more secure mobile experience without requiring constant manual intervention.
