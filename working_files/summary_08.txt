Half of workers worry AI will still take their job — as new report claims use of agents soars 90% in just a year
============================================================

Nearly half of UK workers fear job displacement due to increasing artificial intelligence adoption, prompting the GMB Union to call for government intervention and mandatory employee retraining programs.

### Key Points
- GMB Union data shows 48% of UK workers worry about AI-related job losses, while 26% report AI is already performing their daily tasks.
- Approximately 48% of employees express concern that companies are utilizing AI tools for workplace surveillance and performance monitoring.
- Stack Overflow research indicates a 90% year-over-year increase in AI adoption, though 63% of users still require human oversight for agent-led tasks.
- The World Economic Forum projects 92 million jobs could be displaced by 2030, while simultaneously creating 170 million new roles.
- High-demand future skills include technological literacy, cybersecurity, data management, and AI proficiency.

### Why it Matters
The tension between rising AI integration and worker anxiety highlights a critical need for clear corporate policies and government-backed retraining initiatives. As businesses prioritize efficiency, proactive workforce development is essential to manage the transition and mitigate the risks of widespread job displacement.
