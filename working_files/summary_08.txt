This underrated Android feature gave me the work-life boundary I couldn't get any other way
============================================================

Android users can improve digital well-being and productivity by utilizing built-in user profiles and Work Profiles to separate personal, professional, and distraction-free environments on a single device.

### Key Points
- Android user profiles allow owners to create distinct, isolated environments with separate home screens, app sets, and notification settings.
- The Work Profile feature, managed via mobile device management (MDM) systems, enables users to pause work-related apps and notifications outside of business hours.
- Guest mode provides a temporary, secure profile for sharing a device without exposing personal data, photos, or private accounts.
- Users can manage these settings by searching for "Multiple users" in the Android system settings or via the Quick Settings panel.
- While widely available on stock Android, some manufacturers, such as Samsung, may limit standard user profile features in favor of enterprise-managed Work Profiles.

### Why it Matters
Leveraging these built-in tools helps users establish clear digital boundaries, reducing the stress of constant work notifications and minimizing distractions during personal time. This functionality effectively eliminates the need for carrying a second handset to maintain a healthy work-life balance.
