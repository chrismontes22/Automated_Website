'X by Xreal' wants everyone in on AR glasses, launches entry-level pair for gamers, movie-lovers
============================================================

Xreal has launched its new entry-level sub-brand, XBX, debuting the a01 AR glasses which feature a 1,600-nit MicroOLED display and will arrive in the U.S. this July for $299.

### Key Points
- The a01 glasses feature dual-layer MicroOLED screens with 1,600-nit peak brightness and SDR-to-HDR conversion technology.
- Designed for portability, the device weighs approximately 62 grams and utilizes a lightweight nylon body without an integrated camera.
- The hardware includes an anti-shake algorithm to maintain image clarity while users are in motion, such as on public transportation.
- Users can customize the glasses with interchangeable front frames, including the option to 3D print personal accessories.
- The product is positioned as an entry-level "pocket cinema" for gamers and movie enthusiasts to connect with smartphones and handheld consoles.

### Why it Matters
This launch signals a strategic shift toward making augmented reality hardware more accessible by lowering the price point for entry-level consumers. By stripping away non-essential features like cameras, Xreal is positioning its new sub-brand to compete directly for the mass-market mobile entertainment and gaming audience.
