Founder of Asian super-app Gojek sentenced to 10 years in jail for corruption
============================================================

Former Indonesian education minister and Gojek co-founder Nadiem Makarim has been sentenced to 10 years in prison following a corruption conviction related to a controversial government laptop procurement deal.

### Key Points
- Nadiem Makarim was found guilty of abusing his authority to favor Google in a 2021-2022 government contract for Chromebook laptops.
- The court ordered 809 billion rupiah ($45 million) in restitution, which Makarim must pay or face an additional five years in prison.
- Prosecutors alleged the procurement caused $125 million in state losses and created a conflict of interest due to Makarim's ties to Gojek.
- Makarim, who served as education minister from 2019 to 2024, maintains his innocence and plans to appeal the verdict.
- Supporters and activists claim the case is politically motivated, citing a broader pattern of targeting former allies of ex-President Joko Widodo.

### Why it Matters
The conviction of a prominent tech entrepreneur highlights growing concerns regarding the politicization of anti-corruption efforts within the Indonesian government. This case may discourage private sector leaders from entering public service, potentially stifling future innovation and reform efforts in the country's administration.
