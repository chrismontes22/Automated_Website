The CLARITY Act Delay Is Now A Compliance Problem, Not Just A Political One
============================================================

The CLARITY Act remains stalled in the U.S. Senate, leaving the digital asset industry without a definitive federal framework to determine SEC or CFTC regulatory jurisdiction.

### Key Points
- The CLARITY Act passed the House on July 17, 2025, but faces significant legislative hurdles in the Senate before the upcoming August recess.
- Four primary disputes, including ethics requirements, law enforcement concerns, and regulator staffing shortfalls, currently prevent the bill from reaching a floor vote.
- The GENIUS Act, which established a federal framework for payment stablecoins, was signed into law last year and faces its first rulemaking deadline on July 18.
- Senate Majority Leader John Thune has prioritized the National Defense Authorization Act, further compressing the timeline for a potential CLARITY Act vote.
- Without congressional action, the industry remains subject to "regulation by enforcement," where jurisdictional authority shifts based on the current administration.

### Why it Matters
The ongoing legislative delay forces companies to manage significant regulatory risk without a clear legal standard for digital asset classification. This uncertainty complicates long-term capital allocation and governance, as firms must prepare for two distinct regulatory frameworks while awaiting a final decision from Congress.
