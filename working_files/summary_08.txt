This USB-C Dongle Just Let You Control an iPhone From Windows
============================================================

GL.iNet has launched the Comet Q, a pocket-sized, hardware-based remote control device that allows users to manage laptops, tablets, and smartphones via a browser without installing software.

### Key Points
- The GL-RMQ1 connects via a single USB-C cable to provide video, data, and power, supporting resolutions up to 2K at 60 fps.
- It operates at the hardware level, maintaining access even when the target device is locked, sleeping, or disconnected from local Wi-Fi.
- Compatibility includes iPhones from the 15 series onward, iPads, and Android devices that support DisplayPort Alt Mode.
- Security features include physical disconnection, two-factor authentication, and built-in support for WireGuard, Tailscale, and ZeroTier VPNs.
- Users can manage devices through any web browser at glkvm.com or via the dedicated GLKVM app for mobile touch gestures.

### Why it Matters
This hardware-based approach eliminates the limitations of traditional remote desktop software, which often fails when target devices enter sleep modes or lack active network connections. By enabling cross-platform control for mobile devices, the Comet Q offers a significant efficiency upgrade for IT professionals, developers, and remote support users.
