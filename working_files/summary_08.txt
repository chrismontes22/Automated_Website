Google’s Updated Guidance Now Says It’s “Fine” To Use LLMs.txt For AI SEO via @sejournal, @martinibuster
============================================================

Google has updated its generative AI search guidance to clarify that while LLMs.txt files do not impact Google Search rankings, site owners are free to use them for other platforms.

### Key Points
- Google revised its Search Central documentation to explicitly state that LLMs.txt files and similar markups are ignored by its own generative AI search features.
- The update clarifies that using these files for third-party AI services or systems will neither help nor harm a website's visibility within Google Search.
- The previous guidance was misinterpreted by some as a broad discouragement against using AI-specific markup files across the entire web ecosystem.
- Google implemented these changes to address community confusion and distinguish between its own search requirements and those of other generative AI platforms.

### Why it Matters
This update provides necessary clarity for webmasters and SEO professionals who manage content for multiple AI-driven search surfaces. By explicitly permitting the use of these files for other systems, Google acknowledges the growing complexity of optimizing for a diverse landscape of generative AI tools.
