The US Military Quietly Turned GPS Into a Global 'Numbers Station,' Evidence Suggests
============================================================

Security researcher Steven Murdoch discovered that the U.S. military has used a hidden GPS satellite data field to broadcast encrypted cryptographic keys for nearly two decades.

### Key Points
- Researcher Steven Murdoch identified encrypted 176-bit messages hidden within Subframe 4, Page 17 of GPS satellite broadcasts.
- Analysis of 12 million observations confirmed the data aligns with the Pentagon’s Over-the-Air Distribution (OTAD) and Over-the-Air Rekeying (OTAR) systems.
- The system allows the military to remotely update cryptographic keys on global receivers, replacing manual, onsite security procedures.
- A major synchronization event occurred on May 26, 2011, when all 31 operational GPS satellites transmitted a specific key-repeating pattern.
- Recent data shifts observed in 2022 and 2023 suggest the military may be modernizing the infrastructure or introducing new communication protocols.

### Why it Matters
This discovery highlights how sensitive military operations have been hiding in plain sight within publicly accessible satellite signals for years. It serves as a reminder that vast amounts of data are constantly being transmitted overhead, offering significant opportunities for security researchers to uncover hidden infrastructure through open-source analysis.
