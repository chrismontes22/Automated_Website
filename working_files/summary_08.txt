The UK Home Office is actively recruiting three new Chief Technology Officers to oversee critical national infrastructure, including border control systems and passport services, with salaries reaching £105,000.

### Key Points
- The Home Office is seeking three CTOs to manage core IT operations for borders, visas, and asylum applications.
- Compensation for these high-level public sector roles is set at up to £105,000 per position.
- Responsibilities include maintaining eGates, passport issuance systems, and broader enterprise service delivery.
- These appointments aim to modernize and secure essential government digital infrastructure against evolving operational demands.

### Why it Matters
These strategic hires are essential for maintaining the stability and security of the United Kingdom's critical border and immigration technology platforms. By filling these leadership roles, the government aims to ensure the long-term reliability of systems that process millions of sensitive citizen and traveler records annually.
