Anthropic struggling with Chinese competition, its own safety obsession
============================================================

Anthropic is facing significant financial and competitive pressure as it prepares for a 2026 IPO, struggling against low-cost Chinese AI models and internal debates over model safety.

**Key points**
- Anthropic has spent $10 billion to generate $5 billion in revenue, raising concerns about the company's long-term financial sustainability.
- Market share for Claude models dropped from 29.1% in March 2025 to 13.3% in March 2026, according to recent industry data.
- Chinese AI models, including those from MiniMax and DeepSeek, currently dominate top performance rankings while offering significantly lower costs than Western alternatives.
- Security researchers are increasingly abandoning Claude due to aggressive safety guardrails that frequently trigger false positives during legitimate vulnerability testing.
- Anthropic is reportedly planning an initial public offering as early as the fourth quarter of 2026.

**Why it matters: **
Anthropic’s struggle highlights the growing tension between maintaining strict safety standards and remaining price-competitive in a rapidly evolving global AI market. If the company cannot balance its high operational costs with the need for developer-friendly utility, it risks losing further ground to international rivals that offer similar performance at a fraction of the price.
