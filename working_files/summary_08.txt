Lose your laptop, not your data: 7 Windows settings to enable now
============================================================

PCWorld recommends seven essential security practices for Windows users to protect sensitive data and improve the chances of recovering a laptop in the event of theft or loss.

### Key Points
- Enable BitLocker or Device Encryption to prevent unauthorized access to internal storage if the device is stolen.
- Use Windows Hello biometric authentication to eliminate the risk of password theft through shoulder surfing in public spaces.
- Activate the Find My Device feature in Windows settings to assist in locating a misplaced or stolen laptop.
- Maintain regular data backups using cloud services like Microsoft OneDrive or Google Drive to ensure file availability.
- Utilize Bluetooth trackers such as Apple AirTags or Tile to monitor the physical location of your laptop bag.
- Secure devices physically by using Kensington locks or the Windows key + L shortcut to lock the screen when unattended.

### Why it Matters
Implementing these proactive security measures significantly reduces the risk of data breaches and permanent loss of personal or professional information. By combining software encryption with physical tracking and locking habits, users can better safeguard their hardware against both digital and physical threats.
