Cannes Briefing: The Cannes confessional
============================================================

Attendees at the Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity are increasingly questioning the event's high costs as industry leaders scrutinize the return on investment for expensive marketing activations.

### Key Points
- Marketing executives reported that event costs have surged, with some setups on the Croisette reaching $25 million.
- Industry professionals are openly questioning the value of traditional networking meetings, noting a lack of hard metrics to justify the high price of attendance.
- Concerns were raised regarding the sustainability of AI tool pricing, with some vendors reportedly subsidizing costs by thousands of dollars per user.
- Agencies and brands are struggling to bridge the gap between creative ideation and long-term campaign execution.
- Despite financial skepticism, the festival continues to serve as a significant venue for peer recognition and industry celebration.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward fiscal scrutiny suggests that the marketing industry is moving away from prioritizing presence over measurable business outcomes. As budgets tighten, companies are demanding more transparency and concrete data to justify the massive expenditures associated with global industry events.
