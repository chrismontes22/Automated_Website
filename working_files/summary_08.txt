YouTube now works with Android Auto, but not in the way you'd expect
============================================================

**Google has updated the YouTube app for Android Auto to include native playback controls, allowing users to pause, play, and skip audio content directly from their vehicle's dashboard.**

**Key points**
- YouTube on Android Auto now features integrated playback controls, eliminating the need to manage audio from a smartphone.
- The update supports audio-only playback, as video streaming remains disabled on car displays for safety reasons.
- Users still require a YouTube Premium Lite subscription or higher to enable background playback functionality.
- The rollout was first identified by Reddit users and reported by 9to5Google as a server-side update.

**Why it matters**
This update improves driver safety and convenience by integrating standard media controls into the vehicle's native interface. It addresses a long-standing user frustration by aligning the YouTube experience with other supported media apps like Spotify and YouTube Music.
