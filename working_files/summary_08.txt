Why I abandoned LastPass after moving my passwords to this Bitwarden setup
============================================================

Many users are migrating from LastPass to the open-source password manager Bitwarden after LastPass restricted its free tier by limiting cross-device syncing capabilities to paid Premium subscribers.

### Key Points
- LastPass restricted free users to a single device type in 2021, ending unlimited cross-platform syncing.
- Bitwarden offers free, unlimited syncing across all devices, including PCs, Macs, Android phones, and iPhones.
- Bitwarden utilizes AES 256-bit end-to-end encryption to secure user vaults across all platforms.
- The open-source nature of Bitwarden allows for public code review, increasing transparency compared to closed-source alternatives.
- Bitwarden supports biometric unlocking and automatic credential autofill on both desktop and mobile applications.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward open-source security tools highlights a growing consumer preference for transparent, cost-effective alternatives to subscription-based software. As password management becomes essential for digital security, users are increasingly prioritizing cross-device functionality and data portability over proprietary user interfaces.
