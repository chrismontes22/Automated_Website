Researchers find all big-name bots bomb EU compliance tests
============================================================

A disgruntled security researcher is threatening to release six zero-day vulnerabilities, including three currently under active exploitation, following a public dispute and legal involvement with Microsoft.

### Key Points
- A security researcher claims to possess six zero-day vulnerabilities and has scheduled a public disclosure for July 14.
- Three of the identified vulnerabilities are reportedly being exploited in the wild.
- Microsoft has involved law enforcement in response to the researcher's public threats and grievances.
- The conflict escalated after the researcher expressed public humiliation regarding their interactions with the software giant.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the volatile relationship between independent security researchers and major technology vendors regarding vulnerability disclosure processes. The potential release of active exploits poses a significant security risk to enterprise systems that rely on Microsoft software.
