Jeff Bezos' AI Startup Aims To Build an 'Artificial General Engineer'
============================================================

Jeff Bezos has unveiled his new AI startup, Prometheus, which aims to develop an artificial general engineer capable of designing complex physical products like robots, drugs, and rocket engines.

### Key Points
- Prometheus secured $12 billion in funding, resulting in a company valuation of $41 billion.
- Jeff Bezos serves as co-CEO of the startup alongside Vik Bajaj, a co-founder of Alphabet’s Verily.
- The company currently employs approximately 150 people focused on advanced engineering automation.
- The technology is designed to assist industries including manufacturing, robotics, and pharmaceutical drug development.
- Bezos identified his own aerospace company, Blue Origin, as a primary beneficiary of these new engineering tools.

### Why it Matters
This startup represents a significant shift in AI development by focusing on physical engineering rather than just digital or language-based tasks. If successful, these tools could drastically accelerate innovation cycles for companies building complex hardware and critical infrastructure.
