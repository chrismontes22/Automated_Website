The UK’s Most Unpopular Government on Record Just Went All-In On Digital Surveillance, Censorship and Control
============================================================

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer is facing significant political instability while pushing controversial legislation that mandates digital age verification and device-level content scanning for all internet users.

### Key Points
- The UK government has issued a three-month ultimatum for tech companies to implement client-side scanning on all devices to detect explicit images.
- New "Australia-plus" legislation aims to ban social media access for those under 16 by Spring 2027, requiring identity verification for all users.
- Critics, including leading cryptographers, warn that device-level scanning undermines end-to-end encryption and facilitates mass government surveillance.
- Experts argue that mandatory age verification acts as a "Trojan horse" for the widespread adoption of digital IDs and potential central bank digital currencies.
- Despite facing a potential leadership challenge and record-low popularity, Starmer maintains a large parliamentary majority to advance these digital control policies.

### Why it Matters
These measures represent a fundamental shift in internet architecture by effectively ending online anonymity and requiring biometric or financial data for basic digital access. The move signals a broader global trend toward state-mandated digital identity systems that could centralize control over personal information and political speech.
