Five reasons why I use an email alias, and you should too
============================================================

Using unique email aliases for different online services helps users prevent spam, identify data leaks, and protect their primary accounts from credential stuffing and invasive advertising tracking.

### Key Points
- Email aliases allow users to deactivate specific addresses instantly if they become sources of spam or security threats.
- Unique addresses for every service make it easy to pinpoint which company leaked or sold personal contact information.
- Using distinct aliases prevents hackers from using a single email address to perform credential stuffing across multiple platforms.
- Aliases disrupt data brokers by preventing them from linking disparate online activities to a single, persistent consumer profile.
- Services like SimpleLogin, AnonAddy, Proton Mail, and Apple offer tools to manage these masked addresses without requiring technical expertise.

### Why it Matters
Adopting an alias-based email strategy shifts digital security from a reactive process to a proactive defense mechanism. By decoupling your primary identity from casual online interactions, you significantly reduce your vulnerability to data breaches and unwanted corporate surveillance.
