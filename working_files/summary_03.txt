The theory taking the rich by storm: China funds data center haters
============================================================

Silicon Valley investors are increasingly alleging that Chinese-funded influence campaigns are fueling local opposition to U.S. data center projects, despite a lack of concrete evidence supporting these claims.

### Key Points
- Interior Secretary Doug Burgum and tech investors like Kevin O'Leary have publicly suggested that foreign "dark money" is driving anti-data center sentiment.
- OpenAI recently banned a cluster of Chinese accounts that used ChatGPT to generate anti-data center content, though the company noted the campaign had minimal impact.
- Kevin O'Leary accused Utah-based organizations, including Alliance for a Better Utah, of acting as Chinese "cells," a claim the groups have categorically denied.
- A Gallup poll indicates that 71% of Americans oppose local data center construction, suggesting that public resistance is largely driven by domestic concerns.
- Congressman Brett Guthrie has requested a briefing from federal officials to investigate potential Chinese influence in the ongoing national debate over AI infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
These allegations highlight the growing tension between the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure and local community concerns regarding energy consumption and land use. The debate underscores how national security narratives are increasingly being used to discredit grassroots opposition to large-scale industrial development projects.
