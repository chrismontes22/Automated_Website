The Three-Second Theft: Why AI Voice Fraud Outruns Every Defence
============================================================

AI-powered voice cloning has enabled a surge in sophisticated financial fraud, with criminals using just three seconds of audio to impersonate loved ones and steal billions from victims.

### Key Points
- The FBI reported over 22,000 AI-nexus fraud complaints in 2025, with adjusted losses exceeding $893 million.
- Older adults are the primary target, accounting for $352 million of those AI-specific losses and $7.7 billion in total cybercrime.
- Modern voice-cloning tools require only three seconds of audio, which is easily harvested from social media or public recordings.
- INTERPOL estimates global financial fraud losses reached $442 billion in 2025, noting that AI-enhanced scams are 4.5 times more profitable than traditional methods.
- Major cloning providers often lack robust verification, frequently relying on simple self-attestation checkboxes rather than mandatory consent protocols.

### Why it Matters
The rapid industrialization of AI fraud has rendered traditional detection methods and individual vigilance ineffective, as even experts can no longer reliably distinguish synthetic audio from reality. Meaningful protection requires shifting the burden of security from vulnerable individuals to the financial and telecommunications institutions that control the infrastructure where these crimes occur.
