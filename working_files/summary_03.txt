Officials powerless to stop 8 new data centers that could transform small Texas county
============================================================

Hood County residents are fighting a surge of massive data center proposals as developers exploit the region's lack of zoning regulations and limited county oversight authority.

### Key Points
- Developers have proposed eight data centers in Hood County, covering over 7,600 acres of rural land.
- The Comanche Circle project alone could require 3 gigawatts of electricity, enough to power 3 million homes.
- Proposed facilities face intense local opposition due to concerns over massive water consumption, noise, and the industrialization of rural landscapes.
- Texas law currently restricts counties from blocking development, leading to multiple lawsuits against Hood County officials who attempted to implement moratoriums.
- State grid operator ERCOT reports that data centers account for 89% of new power capacity requests, totaling hundreds of gigawatts.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of "hyperscale" data centers in unincorporated Texas areas highlights a growing conflict between industrial growth and local governance. As these projects outpace existing regulations, rural communities are struggling to balance the promise of tax revenue against the long-term strain on local water supplies and the electrical grid.
