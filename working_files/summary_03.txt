Mayor to homeowner losing sleep over data center plant: "Consider selling"
============================================================

Southaven, Mississippi residents are suing xAI and its subsidiary, MZX Tech, alleging that persistent noise and vibrations from local data center power plants are causing significant health issues.

### Key Points
- Residents Jason Haley and Taylor Logsdon filed a lawsuit claiming constant noise from xAI facilities causes sleep deprivation, migraines, and anxiety.
- Noise levels recorded by residents have exceeded 60 decibels, surpassing World Health Organization recommendations for nighttime noise.
- The NAACP has filed a separate legal challenge regarding air pollution concerns from the plant's 59 methane gas turbines.
- Southaven Mayor Darren Musselwhite has acknowledged the noise as a problem but faced criticism for suggesting residents move rather than resolving the issue.
- xAI has invested millions in sound mitigation efforts, including sound walls and berms, which residents report have been ineffective.
- The project is part of a $20 billion investment in the region, described by Mississippi Governor Tate Reeves as the state's largest economic development project.

### Why it Matters
The conflict highlights the growing tension between rapid AI infrastructure expansion and the quality of life for local communities living near high-capacity data centers. As companies like xAI build massive power-intensive facilities, local governments face increasing pressure to balance significant economic investment with the health and environmental concerns of their constituents.
