WTF is SPUR’s publisher-run Content Telemetry Framework?
============================================================

The Associated Press has joined the Standards for Publisher Usage Rights (SPUR) coalition, a publisher-led initiative aiming to establish transparent, usage-based licensing standards for AI-generated content consumption.

### Key Points
- SPUR is a coalition of over 30 publishers, including the BBC, Financial Times, The Guardian, and the Associated Press, focused on tracking AI content usage.
- The initiative proposes a telemetry standard to track five specific AI interactions: content retrieval, grounding, citation, display, and user engagement.
- A technical working group released a draft data schema for public comment, which remains open until July 24.
- The coalition aims to shift the industry from opaque, unlicensed AI scraping toward a transparent, commercial model where publishers control their intellectual property.
- Tech companies and infrastructure providers, including Microsoft, Fastly, TollBit, and Redpine, have engaged with the coalition to discuss implementation.

### Why it Matters
This initiative represents a strategic shift for media organizations attempting to reclaim control over their intellectual property in an era of automated content scraping. By establishing a unified technical standard, publishers hope to force AI developers toward licensed, paid models rather than relying on the current, largely unregulated ecosystem.
