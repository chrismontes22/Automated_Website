Microsoft’s AI chief says superintelligence is near, but won’t take your job
============================================================

Microsoft AI CEO Mustafa Suleyman is prioritizing the development of superintelligence while restructuring the company’s internal teams to pursue frontier model research independently of its partnership with OpenAI.

### Key Points
- Microsoft has shifted its strategy to build its own frontier models, aiming for long-term self-sufficiency rather than relying solely on third-party intellectual property.
- The company recently launched seven new models, including the reasoning-focused MAI-Thinking-1, which was trained without distilling data from other AI labs.
- Suleyman emphasizes a "humanist" approach to AI, focusing on practical applications in healthcare, such as a new partnership with the Mayo Clinic.
- Microsoft continues to utilize a six-to-eight-week development cycle, organizing teams into interdisciplinary "squads" to maintain agility in a rapidly evolving market.
- Suleyman distinguishes between "tasks" and "jobs," arguing that AI will automate specific administrative functions rather than eliminating entire professional roles.

### Why it Matters
This strategic pivot signals a major shift in the AI landscape as Microsoft moves from being a primary distributor of OpenAI’s technology to a direct competitor in the race for superintelligence. By focusing on enterprise-grade tools and healthcare integration, Microsoft is attempting to build the "social permission" and tangible value necessary to overcome growing consumer and political skepticism toward AI.
