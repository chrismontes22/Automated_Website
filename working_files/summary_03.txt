Steve Jobs and the greatest run of products in tech history
============================================================

Following his 1997 return, Steve Jobs led Apple through a transformative decade of innovation, revitalizing the company with iconic products like the iMac, iBook, and the original iPod.

**Key points**
- Steve Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, inheriting a struggling company with a bloated, unfocused product lineup.
- The 1998 launch of the iMac, which sold 800,000 units in five months, marked the beginning of Apple's financial and creative turnaround.
- Design executive Jony Ive played a central role in establishing Apple’s signature aesthetic, prioritizing form and user experience over traditional PC standards.
- The 2001 release of the iPod and the transition to the Mac OS X operating system provided the foundation for Apple’s long-term market dominance.
- By 2007, the company’s rapid innovation cycle culminated in the launch of the iPhone, shifting Apple from a computer manufacturer to a global technology leader.

**Why it matters**
This period of rapid product development, often called the "iDecade," demonstrates how a focused design-led strategy can rescue a failing corporation from the brink of insolvency. The success of this era established the blueprint for Apple’s modern business model, proving that hardware-software integration and aggressive product iteration can create lasting market dominance.
