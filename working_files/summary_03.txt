Why Everyone Hates AI Data Centers
============================================================

The rapid expansion of AI-powered data centers has triggered a bipartisan grassroots backlash across the United States, pitting local community concerns against the massive infrastructure needs of Big Tech.

### Key Points
- A May Gallup poll indicates that 70 percent of Americans oppose the construction of AI data centers within their local communities.
- Tech giants including Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, and Google have invested more in data centers since the launch of ChatGPT than the federal government spent on the interstate-highway system.
- Local opposition led to the cancellation of at least 20 proposed data center projects during the first quarter of 2026.
- Residents frequently cite noise pollution, excessive water consumption, and the secrecy of real-estate shell companies as primary drivers for their opposition.
- Political figures ranging from Bernie Sanders and Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez to populist conservatives have expressed skepticism or support for moratoriums on new facility construction.

### Why it Matters
Data centers have become a potent symbol of the perceived disconnect between wealthy tech elites and the daily realities of average citizens. This emerging conflict threatens to disrupt the AI industry's growth trajectory and will likely become a significant, polarizing issue in upcoming midterm and 2028 presidential elections.
