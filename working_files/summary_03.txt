Major Publishers Sue ‘WeLib’, a Pirate Site Built on Anna’s Archive Code
============================================================

Major publishers including Penguin Random House and Elsevier have filed a lawsuit against the pirate site WeLib, seeking $19.5 million in damages for widespread copyright infringement of literary works.

### Key Points
- Thirteen major publishers filed the complaint in a New York federal court, targeting WeLib for unauthorized distribution of copyrighted books and articles.
- WeLib reportedly hosts 43 million books and 98 million articles, serving over 80,000 active monthly users.
- The site generates revenue through subscription tiers ranging from $7 to $90 per month, processed via cryptocurrency and third-party payment services.
- Publishers allege WeLib copied the codebase and collection of Anna’s Archive, which recently faced a similar $19.5 million default judgment.
- The lawsuit seeks a permanent injunction to force domain registrars and hosting providers to take the website offline.

### Why it Matters
This legal action highlights the ongoing struggle between major publishing houses and shadow libraries that monetize stolen intellectual property. By targeting the infrastructure and revenue streams of these sites, publishers aim to curb the unauthorized distribution of content and its potential use in training artificial intelligence models.
