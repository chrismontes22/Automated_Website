Amazon Employees Show Up to City Council Meeting to Demand Limits on Data Centers
============================================================

Three Amazon software engineers publicly urged Seattle officials to implement strict regulations on data center development, citing concerns over environmental impacts and the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence.

### Key Points
- Amazon employees Liesl Wigand, Patrick Schloesser, and Darius Irani testified at Seattle city hearings regarding the risks of unchecked data center construction.
- The workers proposed mandates for renewable energy usage, power storage, and increased transparency regarding water and electricity consumption.
- Seattle’s Land Use and Sustainability Committee voted to advance a potential one-year moratorium on issuing new permits for data center projects.
- Amazon stated it has no current plans to build data centers within Seattle city limits and emphasized its commitment to environmental efficiency.
- The employees are members of the Amazon Employees for Climate Justice group, which advocates for greater corporate accountability in AI development.

### Why it Matters
This public dissent marks a rare escalation in internal activism, as tech workers increasingly challenge the infrastructure demands of the generative AI boom. The outcome of Seattle's proposed moratorium could set a significant regulatory precedent for how municipalities balance local resource management with the massive energy requirements of global tech companies.
