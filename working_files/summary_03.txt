The subtext of Robinhood's layoff memo: It's not us, it's you
============================================================

Robinhood CEO Vlad Tenev announced a 10% workforce reduction, citing a need for higher talent density and operational efficiency despite the company reporting record-high trading volumes this month.

### Key Points
- Robinhood is cutting 10% of its workforce, which totaled 2,900 full-time employees as of February 2025.
- CEO Vlad Tenev stated the layoffs are intended to "maximize talent density" and remove organizational layers rather than address financial instability.
- The company reported record average daily trading volumes across equities, options, and prediction markets during June.
- First-quarter cryptocurrency revenue fell 47% year over year to $134 million, reflecting a broader slump in retail crypto trading.
- The move follows a trend of major firms, including Microsoft and Block, conducting layoffs while maintaining that their core business operations remain robust.

### Why it Matters
This shift in corporate messaging signals a move toward performance-based restructuring where layoffs are framed as strategic optimization rather than financial necessity. Such policies create a culture of job insecurity for remaining staff while pressuring employees to constantly prove their value to avoid future cuts.
