Apple's latest Android joke would have made sense 10 years ago — not in 2026
============================================================

Apple’s latest "Privacy on iPhone" advertisement faces criticism for depicting Android smartphones as outdated devices with thick bezels and single-lens cameras that do not reflect current market reality.

### Key Points
- The advertisement promotes Safari privacy features by contrasting iPhone security against the tracking risks faced by users of generic, outdated Android-style devices.
- Critics note that the Android phones shown in the ad feature thick bezels and notches, despite modern flagships like the Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra utilizing slim designs.
- The ad portrays Android devices with single rear cameras, ignoring the advanced multi-lens hardware found on current premium models like the Oppo Find X9 Ultra.
- Apple continues to sell the iPhone 16e with a notch, even as the company uses dated hardware tropes to mock the aesthetic of competing Android smartphones.

### Why it Matters
These marketing tactics highlight the ongoing competitive tension between Apple and Google, reflecting a persistent strategy to frame the iPhone as the superior choice for user privacy. However, the use of inaccurate hardware depictions risks alienating tech-savvy consumers who recognize that modern Android flagships have largely surpassed the dated designs presented in the ad.
