The AI industry’s race for profits is now existential
============================================================

Leading artificial intelligence firms OpenAI and Anthropic face mounting pressure to achieve profitability as they navigate a critical period of high infrastructure costs and impending public offerings.

### Key Points
- OpenAI and Anthropic are under intense pressure to transition from massive capital investment to sustainable revenue models before potential market instability.
- The shift toward AI agents has significantly increased compute consumption, forcing companies to prioritize resource-heavy projects over others.
- OpenAI recently discontinued its Sora video-generation app, reportedly abandoning a $1 billion Disney licensing deal to reallocate compute resources toward its Codex project.
- Anthropic has restricted the use of the OpenClaw agent framework on standard subscription plans, requiring users to switch to more expensive pay-as-you-go models.
- Both companies are preparing for major IPOs while aiming to meet ambitious revenue projections that anticipate profitability by the end of the decade.

### Why it Matters
The ability of these industry leaders to monetize their technology will determine the long-term viability of the current AI investment boom. If these firms fail to balance high operational costs with sustainable revenue, the resulting market correction could significantly impact the broader technology sector.
