Would you host part of an AI data center in your home?
============================================================

Sunrun is launching a pilot program to build a nationwide distributed AI compute network by installing specialized hardware nodes within the homes of its existing solar and battery customers.

### Key Points
- Sunrun plans to place compute nodes in residential homes equipped with its solar and battery storage systems.
- Participating customers will receive financial compensation for hosting the hardware units on their property.
- The company intends to sell the aggregated computing power from these nodes to enterprise AI developers.
- This initiative aims to bypass local opposition to large-scale data center construction, which currently faces resistance from over 70 percent of Americans.
- The pilot program follows a successful proof of concept, with Sunrun currently accepting waitlist sign-ups from its 1.1 million existing customers.

### Why it Matters
This decentralized approach offers a novel solution to the growing infrastructure constraints and public backlash currently hindering the expansion of traditional, centralized AI data centers. By leveraging existing residential energy networks, Sunrun could create a scalable, alternative model for meeting the massive electricity and space demands of the artificial intelligence industry.
