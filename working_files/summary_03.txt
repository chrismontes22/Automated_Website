Sam Bankman-Fried loses appeal of fraud conviction in FTX case
============================================================

A federal appeals court has upheld the fraud conviction of FTX founder Sam Bankman-Fried, who is currently serving a 25-year prison sentence for orchestrating a massive financial scheme.

### Key Points
- The Second Circuit Court of Appeals rejected Bankman-Fried’s challenge to his 2023 conviction on seven counts of fraud, conspiracy, and money laundering.
- Prosecutors proved that Bankman-Fried misappropriated customer deposits from the FTX exchange to fund his hedge fund, personal real estate, and various loans.
- Bankman-Fried was sentenced to 25 years in prison in 2024 and ordered to forfeit $11 billion to provide restitution for victims of the collapse.
- The ruling follows a federal judge's April decision to deny a request for a new trial based on claims of newly discovered exculpatory witnesses.
- Bankman-Fried recently submitted a formal application for a presidential pardon as he continues to contest the legal outcomes of the FTX collapse.

### Why it Matters
This ruling reinforces the legal accountability of high-profile executives involved in major cryptocurrency exchange failures. The decision maintains the integrity of the $11 billion forfeiture order intended to compensate victims of one of the largest financial frauds in American history.
