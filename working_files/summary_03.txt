Trump’s plan to run a bank
============================================================

Donald Trump’s cryptocurrency venture, World Liberty Financial, is seeking a national trust bank charter while facing intense scrutiny over potential conflicts of interest and significant foreign investment ties.

### Key Points
- World Liberty Financial, co-founded by Donald Trump and Steve Witkoff, is currently applying for a national trust bank charter from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency.
- A member of the United Arab Emirates royal family reportedly acquired a 49 percent stake in the company, netting the Trump family approximately $187 million.
- The proposed charter would allow the company to issue its USD1 stablecoin independently, bypassing the need for third-party cryptocurrency exchanges and increasing transaction fee revenue.
- Critics and advocacy groups like Public Citizen argue the application represents extreme regulatory capture, as the firm is seeking approval from a federal agency under the president's control.
- The venture has contributed to substantial increases in the reported net worth of Trump’s family members since his return to the presidency.

### Why it Matters
The potential approval of this banking charter raises significant questions regarding presidential ethics and the separation of personal business interests from federal regulatory oversight. By moving under federal jurisdiction, the company could consolidate its financial operations while insulating itself from stricter state-level scrutiny.
