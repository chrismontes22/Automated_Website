China Defies US Restrictions and Builds the World’s Fastest Supercomputer
============================================================

China’s new LineShine supercomputer has overtaken the United States' El Capitan to claim the top spot on the TOP500 list, marking a significant shift in global high-performance computing.

### Key Points
- The Shenzhen-based LineShine system delivers 2,198 exaflops, exceeding the performance of the US-based El Capitan by more than 20 percent.
- LineShine utilizes a unique architecture built entirely on domestic Chinese hardware, featuring 45,000 LingKun LX2 processors instead of traditional graphics processing units.
- The system consumes 42.2 megawatts of power and operates on the Linux-based Kylin OS to manage its high-speed LingQi network infrastructure.
- This achievement marks China's return to the number one position on the biannual TOP500 ranking for the first time in nearly a decade.

### Why it Matters
- This development demonstrates that China can achieve world-leading computational performance despite ongoing US export controls on advanced semiconductors and artificial intelligence hardware. The success of the domestically produced LineShine architecture signals a potential shift in the global technology supply chain as nations prioritize self-reliance in critical computing infrastructure.
