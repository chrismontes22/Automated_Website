Pope Leo Is Challenging Much More Than Big Tech
============================================================

Pope Leo XIV’s new encyclical, Magnifica Humanitas, calls for the regulation of artificial intelligence while challenging society to address the human exploitation and moral crises underlying modern technological advancement.

### Key Points
- Pope Leo XIV released the encyclical Magnifica Humanitas to address the ethical implications of artificial intelligence and its impact on human dignity.
- The document calls for the disarmament of AI and the implementation of regulatory tools to curb the distorting effects of technological power on global society.
- The Pope highlights the environmental and human costs of AI, specifically citing the exploitation of laborers and children in rare earth element extraction.
- Leo argues that AI has accelerated existing societal problems, including economic inequality, individualism, and a loss of historical memory.
- The encyclical advocates for a "civilization of love" and asserts that human conscience and self-giving remain superior to any computational system.

### Why it Matters
This encyclical shifts the focus of the AI debate from mere technical regulation to a broader critique of global consumption and moral responsibility. By linking technological convenience to human exploitation, the Pope challenges individuals to reconsider their own complicity in the systems that prioritize efficiency over human value.
