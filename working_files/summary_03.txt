The AI Super PACs Trying to Influence the Midterms
============================================================

Tech-affiliated super PACs are spending tens of millions of dollars to influence AI regulation, mirroring cryptocurrency industry strategies as they back competing candidates in upcoming midterm election cycles.

### Key Points
- The super PAC Leading the Future, backed by Andreessen Horowitz and Greg Brockman, has raised over $140 million to support regulation-light, pro-AI political candidates.
- Anthropic has donated $20 million to Public First Action, a nonprofit group advocating for AI safety and comprehensive regulatory oversight.
- In New York’s Twelfth Congressional District, tech-affiliated groups have spent a combined $44 million to influence the race between Alex Bores and his opponents.
- Public polling indicates a majority of American voters believe the risks of artificial intelligence outweigh the benefits, fueling widespread public skepticism.
- Recent public opposition to AI has manifested in protests at university commencements and violent incidents targeting local politicians over data center construction.

### Why it Matters
This political spending marks a pivotal shift as the AI industry attempts to shape federal policy through aggressive campaign financing. The outcome of these electoral battles will determine whether the U.S. adopts a permissive regulatory environment or implements strict safety guardrails for future technology development.
