South Korea's oldest crypto exchange is now part of the $1 trillion Mirae Group family
============================================================

Mirae Asset Consulting has acquired a 97.15% controlling stake in Korbit, marking the first time a traditional South Korean financial group has taken ownership of a domestic cryptocurrency exchange.

### Key Points
- Mirae Asset Consulting, an affiliate of the $1 trillion Mirae Asset Group, is now the largest shareholder of the 2013-founded exchange.
- The acquisition does not change the corporate structure of Korbit Co., Ltd. or disrupt existing user services, including deposits and withdrawals.
- Korbit will continue to operate under South Korea's Act on the Protection of Virtual Asset Users, ensuring client assets remain segregated from company funds.
- This move reflects a growing trend of traditional financial conglomerates acquiring established crypto platforms to expand their footprint in the digital asset market.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition signals a significant shift as traditional financial institutions increasingly integrate digital asset infrastructure into their broader portfolios. By securing a licensed exchange in a high-volume market like South Korea, Mirae Asset Group gains a strategic foothold in the evolving cryptocurrency sector.
