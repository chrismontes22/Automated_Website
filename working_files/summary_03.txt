It's officially the end of an era for Adobe
============================================================

Adobe is facing a significant shift in its market dominance as competition from agile rivals, regulatory scrutiny, and changing creative workflows challenge its long-standing software monopoly.

### Key Points
- Adobe transitioned from a research-driven engineering firm to a subscription-based enterprise model under CEO Shantanu Narayen, who oversaw the 2013 shift to Creative Cloud.
- The company’s failed $20 billion acquisition of Figma in 2023 resulted in a $1 billion breakup fee and highlighted the rise of browser-based design competitors.
- In 2026, Adobe settled an FTC lawsuit for $150 million over deceptive subscription cancellation practices and hidden termination fees.
- Emerging generative AI tools and lower-cost alternatives like Canva and Affinity are reducing the industry's historical reliance on Adobe’s core creative suite.
- Despite record revenues, Adobe’s stock currently trades at a low valuation of nine times earnings, signaling investor concern regarding the company's long-term growth prospects.

### Why it Matters
Adobe’s struggle reflects a broader transition in the software industry where proprietary, desktop-bound monopolies are being disrupted by accessible, cloud-native, and AI-integrated tools. The company's current market valuation suggests that investors are skeptical of its ability to maintain its historical dominance as creative professionals increasingly find viable alternatives to the Creative Cloud ecosystem.
