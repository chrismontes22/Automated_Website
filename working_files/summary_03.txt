ChatGPT is no longer OpenAI's most important product. Here's why.
============================================================

OpenAI and Anthropic are shifting their business strategies from model performance to customer retention by developing integrated coding platforms designed to increase long-term user dependency and profit margins.

### Key Points
- OpenAI and Anthropic are prioritizing high-margin, sticky products like Claude Code and Codex to secure durable revenue streams ahead of potential IPOs.
- AI coding tools generate higher token usage and revenue compared to standard chatbots by managing complex, large-scale software projects.
- Companies like Walmart are developing internal systems, such as Code Puppy, to maintain model flexibility and avoid vendor lock-in.
- The industry is experiencing a strategic conflict between AI labs seeking ecosystem control and enterprise clients demanding portability and cost management.

### Why it Matters
This shift signals a transition in the AI industry from a race for technical superiority to a battle for long-term enterprise integration. Companies that successfully balance proprietary platform lock-in with customer demand for flexibility will likely define the next phase of the artificial intelligence market.
