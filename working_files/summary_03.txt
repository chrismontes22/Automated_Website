Simon Willison says the 'dark factory' is the next big thing in AI
============================================================

Django co-creator Simon Willison warns that rapid advancements in artificial intelligence could lead to a "dark factory" era where software development occurs entirely without human intervention or oversight.

### Key Points
- Simon Willison, co-creator of the Django web framework, suggests AI may soon automate the entire software development lifecycle.
- The "dark factory" concept describes fully automated systems that operate without human presence, mirroring trends in industrial manufacturing.
- Willison reports that AI currently generates approximately 95% of his own code, shifting the human role toward oversight rather than manual typing.
- Major corporations including IBM, Klarna, Block, and Oracle have already cited AI integration as a contributing factor in recent workforce layoffs.
- While AI lowers the barrier to building software, Willison emphasizes that original, creative ideas remain essential for successful product development.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward autonomous coding environments signals a fundamental change in the software engineering labor market and corporate operational strategies. As companies increasingly rely on AI to replace manual programming tasks, the industry faces significant questions regarding long-term job security and the future necessity of human developers.
