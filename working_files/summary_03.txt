Xbox outlines new mission statement, says it will ‘reevaluate’ approach to exclusives and AI
============================================================

Xbox leadership has announced a strategic shift to unify its brand identity and address consumer concerns regarding hardware performance, service pricing, and inconsistent multiplatform release strategies for games.

### Key Points
- Xbox is rebranding its internal structure back to the "Xbox" name to better reflect its core mission and long-term brand ambition.
- The company identified four strategic priorities: stabilizing current hardware, expanding content, improving user experience, and ensuring sustainable economics for Game Pass.
- Future hardware plans include the development of "Project Helix," a console designed to lead in performance across both console and PC gaming environments.
- Xbox is actively reevaluating its approach to game exclusivity and release windowing to resolve consumer confusion regarding multiplatform availability.
- To improve accessibility, the company recently reduced Game Pass pricing and is exploring more flexible subscription options, including a potential partnership with Discord.
- An annual Xbox Games Showcase is scheduled for June 7, followed by a dedicated presentation for the upcoming title Gears of War: E-Day.

### Why it Matters
This strategic pivot signals a major attempt by Microsoft to repair its brand image and clarify its fragmented gaming ecosystem for players and partners. By addressing pricing and exclusivity, the company aims to stabilize its market position while navigating the transition toward a more flexible, multiplatform-focused future.
