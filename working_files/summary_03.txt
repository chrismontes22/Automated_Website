Electronic Arts is betting that in-game ads can out-earn CTV
============================================================

Electronic Arts has launched a dedicated advertising platform to integrate brand partnerships and dynamic ad units into its major gaming titles, aiming to capture significant digital marketing spend.

### Key Points
- Electronic Arts (EA) launched an internal ad server and SDK to manage brand partnerships and gameplay integrations across titles like FC, Madden, and The Sims.
- The company is shifting toward advertising as a primary revenue stream to offset rising game development costs and move beyond traditional $70 unit sales.
- Current ad capabilities include geographic targeting, frequency capping, and budget pacing, with future plans to leverage player engagement data around live sports events.
- EA is prioritizing a custom-built ad stack over third-party providers to ensure ad formats remain non-intrusive and maintain the integrity of the gaming experience.
- U.S. game advertising spend is projected to reach $10.2 billion by 2028, according to eMarketer data.

### Why it Matters
EA’s move signals a strategic shift to position console gaming as a premium, interactive alternative to Connected TV (CTV) for major advertisers. By building its own infrastructure, the company aims to standardize in-game ad formats, potentially unlocking a massive new revenue stream while navigating the delicate balance between monetization and player satisfaction.
