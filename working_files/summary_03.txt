From Truman's pension to Trump's billions - a White House windfall unmatched by any president
============================================================

Donald Trump reported $2.2 billion in earnings during his first year back in office, a figure that historians describe as unprecedented and a departure from presidential financial norms.

### Key Points
- Donald Trump’s 2025 financial disclosure shows earnings of $2.2 billion, nearly four times his reported income from the previous year.
- Cryptocurrency ventures accounted for $1.4 billion of the total, including $635 million from Celebration Coins and $500 million from World Liberty Financial.
- The president’s sons, Donald Trump Jr. and Eric Trump, are involved in the management of World Liberty Financial.
- Ethics experts and historians note that Trump did not place his business interests into a traditional blind trust, unlike many of his predecessors.
- The White House maintains that the president and his family have not engaged in conflicts of interest and are acting in the public's best interest.

### Why it Matters
These record-breaking earnings highlight a significant shift in presidential conduct regarding personal wealth and potential conflicts of interest while in office. The situation raises ongoing questions about the intersection of government policymaking and the private business ventures of a sitting president and his family.
