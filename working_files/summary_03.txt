Google must let publishers opt out of AI Search features, rules UK
============================================================

The UK Competition and Markets Authority has mandated that Google provide publishers with tools to opt out of AI search features and the training of its generative models.

### Key Points
- Google must allow website owners to block their content from appearing in AI Overviews and from being used to fine-tune AI models.
- The ruling requires Google to provide clear attribution and links for any publisher content utilized within AI-generated search results.
- Publishers can manage these settings via a new toggle in the Google Search Console, which also provides metrics on AI-driven traffic.
- Google has begun rolling out these controls to a subset of UK website owners with plans for a global release following testing.
- The CMA stated that opting out of AI features will not negatively impact a website's standard search ranking.

### Why it Matters
This regulatory decision shifts the power dynamic between major search engines and content creators by establishing a framework for digital property rights in the age of generative AI. It provides publishers with the leverage to negotiate content deals while ensuring that AI-driven search results remain transparent and properly attributed.
