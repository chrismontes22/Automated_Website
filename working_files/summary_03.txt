10 Monday AM Reads
============================================================

New research indicates that global artificial intelligence sales reached $25 billion in the first quarter of 2026, suggesting that massive data-center investments are finally becoming economically sustainable for tech companies.

### Key Points
- Global AI revenue, excluding China, hit $25 billion in Q1 2026, surpassing initial analyst projections of $21 billion.
- A report from Exponential View suggests that the current multi-billion dollar capital expenditure binge on AI infrastructure is beginning to yield measurable financial returns.
- Recent studies indicate that while large-language models show initial promise, they struggle to consistently outperform the market over long periods or during changing economic conditions.
- Traditional media companies are pursuing strategic megadeals to compete with tech giants like Meta, Amazon, and Apple as AI continues to disrupt the entertainment industry's economic model.

### Why it Matters
The transition of AI from a speculative investment to a revenue-generating sector marks a critical turning point for the broader technology market. This shift helps validate the massive infrastructure spending by major firms and provides a clearer outlook for the long-term sustainability of the artificial intelligence boom.
