Texas Democrat’s congressional win is a generational shift backed by cryptocurrency
============================================================

Christian Menefee defeated longtime U.S. Representative Al Green in the 18th Congressional District primary runoff, signaling a generational shift in Texas politics fueled by cryptocurrency industry campaign spending.

### Key Points
- Christian Menefee, 38, defeated 78-year-old incumbent Al Green in the Democratic primary runoff for Texas' 18th Congressional District.
- The cryptocurrency-aligned super PAC Protect Progress spent over $5 million to support Menefee, making it the most expensive House runoff in Texas.
- Menefee advocates for blockchain technology and clear regulatory frameworks, while Green expressed skepticism regarding the industry's impact on the U.S. dollar.
- The industry group Stand with Crypto awarded Menefee an "A" rating and Green an "F" based on their respective legislative stances.
- Political analysts attribute the outcome to a broader trend of younger, millennial and Gen-Z candidates gaining traction within the Democratic Party.

### Why it Matters
The race highlights the growing influence of single-issue super PACs in shaping congressional outcomes through significant financial intervention. It also underscores a broader generational transition within the Democratic Party as younger candidates increasingly challenge established incumbents.
