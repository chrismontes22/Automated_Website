Steve Jobs and the greatest run of products in tech history
============================================================

Following his 1997 return to Apple, Steve Jobs led a decade of unprecedented innovation, transforming the struggling company through iconic product launches like the iMac, iBook, and iPod.

**Key points**
- Steve Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, inheriting a company near insolvency with a disorganized product lineup.
- The 1998 launch of the iMac, which sold 800,000 units in five months, marked the beginning of Apple’s turnaround.
- Design executive Jony Ive played a critical role in establishing Apple’s signature aesthetic and product focus.
- The 2001 release of the iPod and the introduction of Mac OS X provided the foundation for Apple’s future market dominance.
- Apple’s "iDecade" concluded with the 2007 launch of the iPhone, which shifted the company from a computer manufacturer to a global technology leader.

**Why it matters: **
This period of rapid innovation saved Apple from collapse and established the design-led strategy that defines the company today. By prioritizing user experience and streamlined product lines, Apple created a blueprint for success that fundamentally altered the consumer electronics industry.
