Trump’s Anti-Growth Agenda
============================================================

The Trump administration’s current economic and scientific policies risk eroding American global hegemony by undermining the institutional foundations and immigrant-driven innovation that have historically sustained the nation's long-term growth.

### Key Points
- The Trump administration canceled nearly 8,000 scientific grants in 2025, with federal agencies like the NSF and NIH issuing roughly 25 percent fewer grants than in previous years.
- Over 65 percent of the world’s top AI researchers working in the U.S. are foreign-born, yet new policies include a $100,000 H-1B visa fee and four-year limits on student visas.
- The federal government has increasingly intervened in private enterprise, seizing control of U.S. Steel and becoming the largest shareholder of Intel to influence corporate operations.
- The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" provides permanent tax reductions for corporations but adds trillions to the national debt and cuts funding for Medicaid and health insurance subsidies.
- Trump has repeatedly challenged the independence of the Federal Reserve and imposed sweeping tariffs, actions that critics argue threaten the dollar's status as the global reserve currency.

### Why it Matters
These policies represent a fundamental shift toward a more dirigiste government that prioritizes political loyalty and short-term protectionism over the pluralistic institutions that drive economic productivity. By weakening the pillars of scientific research, immigration, and institutional trust, the administration risks a slow, structural decline similar to the historical trajectory of the British Empire.
