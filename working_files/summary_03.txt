White-collar baby boomers are facing a dilemma: Embrace AI or retire early
============================================================

Older workers are increasingly navigating the integration of artificial intelligence into the workplace, balancing the need for continued employment with the challenges of adapting to rapidly evolving corporate technologies.

### Key Points
- A Pew Research Center survey indicates that only 25% of adults aged 50 to 64 reported using ChatGPT as of early 2025.
- U.S. Census data shows the proportion of workers older than 55 in the labor force rose from 10% in 1994 to 25% in 2022.
- Experts suggest that older employees' deep professional experience and critical thinking skills may provide a competitive advantage in an AI-driven job market.
- Many older professionals are actively upskilling through AI subscriptions or career coaching to remain relevant and avoid forced early retirement.
- Unlike younger generations, many Gen X and Baby Boomer workers report less pessimism regarding AI, drawing on their experience navigating previous economic and technological shifts.

### Why it Matters
The rise of AI is forcing older workers to choose between upskilling to maintain job security or accelerating their retirement plans amid corporate automation. This shift highlights a critical intersection of ageism, economic necessity, and the evolving value of human expertise in a technology-dominated workforce.
