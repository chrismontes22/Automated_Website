SpaceX is poised for blastoff with an IPO likely to break records
============================================================

SpaceX has launched a record-breaking $75 billion initial public offering, with shares priced at $135 each, marking the largest IPO in history and signaling a major shift for AI markets.

### Key Points
- SpaceX raised $75 billion by offering 555,555,555 shares, surpassing the previous record held by Saudi Aramco.
- The company will trade under the ticker symbol SPCX starting Friday, with a total valuation reaching approximately $1.75 trillion.
- CEO Elon Musk retains control over the board and holds more than 80% of the company's voting power.
- Proceeds from the IPO are earmarked for expanding AI compute infrastructure, launch facilities, and satellite constellations.
- OpenAI and Anthropic are expected to follow with their own major IPOs later this year, potentially reshaping the broader equity market.
- Major stock indexes have agreed to fast-track SpaceX inclusion, requiring passive investment funds to acquire shares.

### Why it Matters
The entry of SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic into the public market forces these previously private firms to undergo rigorous financial scrutiny and demonstrate long-term profitability. This shift could trigger significant market volatility as investors reallocate capital toward AI-focused companies while navigating the risks associated with concentrated ownership and speculative tech valuations.
