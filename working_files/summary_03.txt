Artificial intelligence could eliminate thousands of jobs in New York City: Report
============================================================

New York City Comptroller Brad Lander warns that artificial intelligence could trigger significant job losses or economic shifts, urging local policymakers to prepare for the technology's uncertain financial impact.

### Key Points
- Comptroller Brad Lander released a report analyzing five potential economic scenarios for New York City driven by artificial intelligence adoption.
- The "AI Falls Flat" scenario predicts a potential loss of 52,500 jobs as early as this year if investment drops and markets slide.
- The most likely outcome, termed the "AI-Empowered Economy," suggests moderate growth with an average of 52,000 new jobs added annually through 2030.
- The report highlights that New York City’s massive concentration of office workers makes it uniquely exposed to both the benefits and risks of AI.
- Lander advocates for proactive local policy, including the creation of a multi-billion dollar financial cushion to mitigate potential economic instability.

### Why it Matters
As the global financial capital, New York City serves as a bellwether for how artificial intelligence will reshape urban labor markets and white-collar employment. The report underscores the urgent need for local governments to balance technological innovation with economic safeguards to protect workers from potential displacement.
