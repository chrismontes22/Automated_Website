You might want to pull the trigger on that Apple purchase — the company plans to raise prices
============================================================

Apple CEO Tim Cook warned that rising global memory chip costs may force the company to increase prices on future iPhone, MacBook, and iPad models for consumers.

### Key Points
- Apple faces unsustainable supply chain costs due to a global shortage of DRAM and NAND memory chips.
- Surging demand for memory from AI data center developers is significantly reducing supply for consumer electronics.
- Tim Cook described the current market volatility as a "hundred-year flood" with no immediate end in sight.
- Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan projects that the semiconductor industry may not see relief from these supply constraints until 2028.
- Apple previously increased prices on 14-inch and 16-inch MacBook Pro models earlier this year by $200 each.

### Why it Matters
These price hikes reflect a broader industry trend where the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence infrastructure is directly inflating the cost of everyday personal computing devices. Consumers should anticipate higher retail prices as tech manufacturers struggle to absorb the sustained increase in essential component costs.
