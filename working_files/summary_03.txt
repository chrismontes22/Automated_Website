Bitcoin Miner Bitzero Signs $2.6 Billion AI Hosting Deal, Highlighting Power Infrastructure Shift
============================================================

Bitcoin miner Bitzero has signed a lease agreement with OneQode to repurpose its energy infrastructure for AI computing, a deal expected to generate $2.6 billion in total contracted revenue.

### Key Points
- Bitzero leased 110 megawatts of its 325 MW Norway facility to OneQode, utilizing low-cost hydroelectric power at 4.3 cents per kilowatt-hour.
- The company holds over 1 gigawatt of potential capacity across sites in Norway, Finland, and North Dakota.
- Bitcoin miners are gaining a competitive advantage by bypassing utility interconnection delays through the ownership of private substations and high-voltage grid connections.
- Global data center power demand is projected to rise 165% by 2030, while lead times for critical power transformers have reached up to 144 weeks.
- The OneQode agreement is estimated to yield $151 million in annual net operating income with an 85% operating margin for Bitzero.

### Why it Matters
This deal highlights a strategic shift where bitcoin miners with pre-existing electrical infrastructure become essential partners for AI firms struggling with grid capacity and equipment shortages. As traditional data center construction faces delays, companies that control direct access to low-cost, reliable power are gaining significant leverage in the artificial intelligence market.
