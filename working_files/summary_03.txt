Better Than Free: How To Differentiate In The Age Of AI
============================================================

WIRED co-founder Kevin Kelly explains how creators can maintain profitability in the age of AI by selling eight "generative" values that cannot be replicated or copied by machines.

### Key Points
- Digital copies of content are now effectively free due to the internet and AI, rendering traditional copyright-based business models increasingly obsolete.
- Kelly identifies eight "generatives"—immediacy, personalization, interpretation, authenticity, accessibility, embodiment, patronage, and findability—as inherently uncopyable assets.
- These generative values must be cultivated through human interaction, trust, and unique experiences that machines cannot replicate.
- Successful creators like Brandon Sanderson and Ben Thompson demonstrate these principles through premium physical goods, expert analysis, and direct fan relationships.
- The shift requires creators to move away from hoarding scarcity and toward a business model centered on generosity and human-centric engagement.

### Why it Matters
As AI lowers the cost of producing content to near zero, creators must pivot from selling copies to selling intangible, human-driven experiences. This shift highlights a fundamental change in the digital economy where value is derived from attention and relationship-building rather than the distribution of digital files.
