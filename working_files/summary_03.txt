Apple spotlights Detroit app makers as local Developer Academy marks fifth graduating class
============================================================

Apple celebrated the fifth graduating class of its Detroit Developer Academy, highlighting the achievements of local students who developed new apps and businesses through the free training program.

### Key Points
- The Detroit Apple Developer Academy, launched in 2021, has trained over 1,800 students in collaboration with Michigan State University and the Gilbert Family Foundation.
- The program offers a nine-month curriculum and a four-week Apple Foundation Program focused on app development, design, and business skills.
- Recent graduates developed projects including the American Sign Language app Sign & Says and the accessibility tool BeAware Deaf Assistant.
- A recent report by Wired questioned the program's effectiveness, noting a 71 percent full-time job placement rate for graduates over the last two years.
- Apple maintains that the academy provides valuable skills in project management and marketing that apply to a wide range of technology-focused careers.

### Why it Matters
The academy serves as a central component of Apple’s Racial Equity and Justice Initiative, aiming to foster economic empowerment within the Detroit technology sector. While the program provides significant educational resources, the debate over its job placement outcomes highlights the ongoing challenge of measuring the long-term professional impact of corporate-sponsored coding initiatives.
