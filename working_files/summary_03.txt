Comcast is breaking up with NBCU. Why did it ever buy it in the first place?
============================================================

Comcast is spinning off its NBCUniversal entertainment assets into a separate company, effectively ending its 15-year experiment of combining content production with broadband distribution infrastructure.

### Key Points
- Comcast is separating its broadband and cable business from NBCUniversal, which includes the NBC network, Peacock, Universal Studios, and theme parks.
- The move follows an earlier spin-off of cable networks like CNBC and MSNBC into a new entity called Versant.
- The "content plus pipes" strategy, which aimed to leverage distribution networks to boost media assets, has historically failed at companies like AT&T and Verizon.
- Comcast’s broadband division faces increasing competition from fixed wireless providers, leading to stagnant growth and subscriber losses.
- The split is widely viewed as a capitulation to Wall Street demands to separate declining cable assets from core infrastructure businesses.

### Why it Matters
This restructuring signals the end of a long-standing media industry trend where conglomerates attempted to control both the delivery of internet and the content consumed over it. By unbundling these assets, Comcast is acknowledging that the convergence of telecom and entertainment failed to provide the expected market value or competitive advantage.
