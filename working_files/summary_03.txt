Studio Behind NFT Battle Royale Game Accused Of Not Paying Staff For Months
============================================================

Current and former employees of video game developer Gunzilla have publicly accused the studio of failing to pay staff salaries for several months while ignoring requests for compensation.

### Key Points
- Former head of talent acquisition Anna Savina and senior VFX animator Paul Creamer allege they are owed months of unpaid wages.
- Multiple staff members, including QA developer Vladyslav Makarevych and artist Andrew Snitsar, report receiving no salary since late 2024.
- Employees claim CEO Vlad Korolev previously promised that payment delays were temporary and that the company remained profitable.
- Staff report that the studio has recently changed company registration details and revoked access to internal communication tools like Jira and email.
- Gunzilla is the developer of the NFT-integrated battle royale game Off the Grid and the current owner of the publication Game Informer.

### Why it Matters
These allegations raise serious concerns regarding the financial stability and management practices of a studio that recently expanded its influence by acquiring Game Informer. The situation highlights potential labor exploitation within the gaming industry and could damage the reputation of the developer behind the blockchain-based title Off the Grid.
