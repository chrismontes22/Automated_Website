Bitcoin ETFs see new money again, but inflows remain ‘peanuts’ relative to the recent exodus
============================================================

U.S.-listed spot bitcoin ETFs recorded $273 million in inflows over the past two weeks, marking a potential stabilization after an eight-week period of significant capital outflows totaling $8 billion.

### Key Points
- Spot bitcoin ETFs attracted $273 million in new capital over the last two weeks, ending an eight-week streak of net outflows.
- The recent inflows are modest, totaling only slightly more than the $226.84 million recorded during the smallest single-week outflow of the previous slump.
- Bitcoin prices have recently stabilized within the $64,000 to $65,000 range, suggesting a potential market bottom to some analysts.
- Data from SoSoValue indicates that $75.67 million entered the funds in the week ending June 17, following $197.40 million the previous week.
- Analysts from firms like BRN caution that consistent, larger inflows are required to confirm a definitive return of institutional demand.

### Why it Matters
These inflows signal that the recent period of heavy selling has subsided, though the current volume is insufficient to confirm a major institutional recovery. Investors are monitoring these trends closely as a primary indicator of whether the broader cryptocurrency market is entering a sustained period of growth or merely experiencing a temporary bounce.
