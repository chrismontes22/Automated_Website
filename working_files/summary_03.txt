South Korea unveils $1tn chip and AI investment plan
============================================================

South Korea has announced an $880 billion investment strategy to expand its semiconductor manufacturing and artificial intelligence infrastructure, aiming to boost regional economic growth and maintain global technological competitiveness.

### Key Points
- The initiative focuses on the "Three Mega Projects," which include developing new chip production hubs, data centers, and robotics technology.
- President Lee Jae-myung introduced the plan alongside executives from major domestic chipmakers Samsung and SK Hynix.
- The strategy seeks to decentralize the nation's industrial base by moving advanced infrastructure projects away from the capital city of Seoul.
- This investment responds to rising global demand for semiconductors driven by the rapid expansion of artificial intelligence technologies.

### Why it Matters
This massive capital injection aims to secure South Korea's position as a critical supplier in the global AI supply chain amid intense competition from Taiwan, China, and Japan. By decentralizing its industrial hubs, the government hopes to address rural economic decline while ensuring the long-term sustainability of its dominant semiconductor sector.
