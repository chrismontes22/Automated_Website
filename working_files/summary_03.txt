There's a new type of VC in Silicon Valley: the creator-investor
============================================================

Lightspeed Venture Partners has hired investor and social media creator Zau to lead seed investments and spearhead a new media strategy, including hosting the firm's upcoming AI podcast.

### Key Points
- Lightspeed Venture Partners hired Zau to bridge the gap between traditional venture capital investing and digital content creation.
- Zau brings a following of over 350,000 social media users to the firm, where she will cohost the "Lightwork" podcast.
- The role involves a dual focus on seed-stage investing and managing the Lightspeed Launch program for new founders.
- The firm aims to leverage Zau’s audience to build trust with early-stage founders and increase visibility for portfolio companies.
- This move follows a broader industry trend of venture firms, including Andreessen Horowitz and OpenAI, acquiring or developing in-house media channels.

### Why it Matters
Venture capital firms are increasingly adopting creator-led marketing strategies to secure early access to promising startups and control their own industry narratives. By integrating influencers into their investment teams, these firms hope to bypass traditional media gatekeepers and build direct, trust-based relationships with the next generation of founders.
