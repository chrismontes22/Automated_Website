I replaced Portainer, Grafana, and Prometheus with this stack
============================================================

A home lab enthusiast has transitioned from Portainer, Prometheus, and Grafana to a more efficient, lightweight stack consisting of Dockhand, Beszel, and Glance for managing Docker containers.

### Key Points
- Dockhand provides a streamlined GUI for container management, offering easier stack creation and simplified image updates compared to Portainer.
- Beszel replaces Prometheus and Grafana, offering a low-resource monitoring solution that consumes less than 50 MB of memory.
- Beszel includes built-in alert systems and supports monitoring multiple systems, including Raspberry Pi devices and laptops, from a single interface.
- Glance serves as a centralized dashboard, allowing users to monitor container status and integrate external data like RSS feeds and weather.
- The new software trio reduces system overhead, making it suitable for low-power hardware like single-board computers and mini PCs.

### Why it Matters
This shift highlights a growing trend among home lab users toward lightweight, specialized tools that prioritize resource efficiency over enterprise-grade complexity. By adopting these streamlined alternatives, users can maintain robust container environments on low-power hardware without the performance penalties associated with heavier monitoring and management platforms.
