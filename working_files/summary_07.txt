A Pixel Watch 5 may have leaked from the most unlikely place yet
============================================================

An unreleased Google Pixel Watch 5 prototype was reportedly discovered by a scuba diver on the ocean floor near St. Martin, revealing potential new health and connectivity features.

### Key Points
- Gearbox Software co-founder Randy Pitchford shared photos of the device, which was allegedly found while scuba diving.
- The watch casing features branding for the "Pixel Watch 5" and lists specifications including SpO2, EDA, heart-rate tracking, and UWB support.
- The device is labeled with IP68 water resistance and appears to be a near-final retail unit rather than a camouflaged engineering prototype.
- Google has not officially commented on the authenticity of the device or how an unreleased product could have been lost at sea.
- Pitchford stated that the owner of the watch has been identified and efforts are underway to return the hardware.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the ongoing challenge of securing unreleased hardware and provides an unexpected, real-world test of the device's durability. If authentic, the leak confirms key technical specifications for Google's upcoming wearable before the company has made any official product announcements.
