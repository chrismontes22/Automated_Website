I ran 4 simple tests to diagnose my TV’s buffering problem, and my internet speed wasn’t to blame
============================================================

Smart TV users experiencing persistent video buffering despite high-speed internet can resolve performance issues by testing hardware connections, clearing app cache, and switching from Wi-Fi to Ethernet.

### Key Points
- Smart TVs often feature weaker Wi-Fi antennas and less powerful processors compared to smartphones or laptops, leading to potential signal degradation.
- Comparing internet speeds across multiple devices helps determine if buffering is caused by the network, the streaming application, or the television hardware.
- Connecting a TV directly to a router via an Ethernet cable can significantly increase bandwidth and eliminate wireless interference.
- Accumulated temporary data in streaming applications can cause sluggish playback, which is often resolved by clearing the app cache in the settings menu.

### Why it Matters
Many consumers incorrectly blame their internet service providers for streaming issues, leading to unnecessary plan upgrades or hardware replacements. Identifying that the bottleneck is often internal to the TV allows users to optimize their existing equipment for a smoother viewing experience.
