Three questions to ask yourself before you ask AI
============================================================

PCWorld provides a strategic framework for users to effectively delegate repetitive, data-heavy tasks to AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini while retaining final human decision-making authority.

### Key Points
- Identify tasks suitable for AI by determining if they are boring, repetitive, or require tedious data compilation.
- Utilize AI tools for administrative chores such as email triaging, voice memo transcription, and organizing complex information into charts.
- Avoid delegating end-to-end processes that require critical judgment, such as final report generation or high-stakes personal decisions.
- Use AI to perform the necessary legwork and data gathering to better inform your own final conclusions.

### Why it Matters
Establishing a clear boundary between AI-assisted preparation and human decision-making helps users maximize productivity without sacrificing accountability. This approach ensures that individuals leverage the efficiency of large language models while maintaining control over the quality and ethics of their final output.
