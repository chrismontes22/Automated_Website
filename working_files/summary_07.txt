The TikTok Ban Was Never About TikTok
============================================================

The new documentary *TikTok Never Dies*, directed by Hao Wu, chronicles the complex legal battle over the potential US ban of TikTok through the experiences of three American influencers.

### Key Points
- The film premieres at the Tribeca Film Festival and covers the six-year saga from 2020 to 2026.
- Director Hao Wu follows three diverse TikTok creators—Steven King, Chloe Sexton, and Topher Townsend—who were involved in the legal challenge against the US government.
- The documentary captures the 2025 period when TikTok briefly went dark in the United States as a protest against the proposed ban.
- The narrative explores the shift in political stances, including Donald Trump’s transition from threatening a ban to brokering a sale of the app's US operations.
- The film focuses on American domestic perspectives and the tension between national security concerns and the protection of free speech.

### Why it Matters
The documentary highlights how TikTok became a central battleground for American political anxieties regarding social media regulation and free speech. It serves as a case study on how legislative and judicial processes can be influenced by shifting political agendas rather than concrete evidence of harm.
