Rumored open-ear Galaxy Buds 'Able' may use your bones to make sound
============================================================

Samsung is reportedly developing new "Galaxy Buds Able" featuring an open-ear clip design and bone conduction technology, marking a potential shift in the company's wearable audio product strategy.

### Key Points
- Samsung's alleged "Galaxy Buds Able" (model SM-U600) were discovered in recent One UI software files.
- The earbuds reportedly utilize bone conduction to transmit sound via cheekbone vibrations rather than traditional air-pushing drivers.
- The design features an open-ear clip mechanism, which may offer a more secure fit for high-activity users compared to standard in-ear models.
- Early reports suggest the device may lack the deep bass and audio richness found in the popular Galaxy Buds 4 Pro.
- The "U" in the model number deviates from Samsung's traditional naming conventions for U.S.-market mobile devices.

### Why it Matters
This potential product launch signals Samsung's intent to compete in the growing open-ear audio market, which prioritizes situational awareness and stability during physical activity. By diversifying its lineup beyond the high-fidelity Galaxy Buds 4 series, Samsung aims to capture a broader consumer base that values comfort and secure fit over traditional sound isolation.
