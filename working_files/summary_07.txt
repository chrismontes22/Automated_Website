Quote of the day by inventor of the World Wide Web, Tim Berners-Lee: "Data is a precious thing and will last longer than the systems themselves" — highlighting the longevity of data as a resource
============================================================

World Wide Web inventor Tim Berners-Lee emphasizes that data remains the fundamental driver of technological innovation, currently powering the rapid development of generative AI and autonomous agent systems.

### Key Points
- Tim Berners-Lee highlights the critical role of data in building future-proof systems and maintaining professional standards within the IT industry.
- Data scientist Clive Humby famously compared raw data to oil, noting its immense value only when properly refined and harnessed by modern businesses.
- Despite the obsolescence of various hardware and software platforms, data has remained the consistent core of the internet for nearly 40 years.
- Generative AI and AI agents are currently utilizing vast amounts of scraped internet data to create new features for global businesses and employees.

### Why it Matters
- Data serves as the essential fuel for the next generation of technological advancements, dictating how companies develop and deploy new digital tools. Understanding this reliance is crucial for professionals aiming to build sustainable systems in an era dominated by artificial intelligence.
