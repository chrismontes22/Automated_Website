The main course: Google Health app focuses on Nutrition Logging in another Android update
============================================================

Google is rolling out version 5.04 of its Health app for Android and iOS, introducing custom food logging, quick calorie tracking, and refined cycle trend monitoring for users.

### Key Points
- Users can now create, edit, and delete custom food entries with an integrated search function.
- Quick Logging allows users to input calorie and macronutrient estimates without searching for specific food names.
- The update includes refined chronological viewing for Cycle Health trends and adds nap tracking to the Today tab.
- iOS users receive bug fixes for incomplete heart rate charts, exercise maps, and friend invitation functionality.
- Naps lasting 20 minutes or longer are now integrated into main sleep session statistics.

### Why it Matters
This update enhances the utility of the Google Health app by streamlining dietary tracking and improving data visualization for long-term wellness monitoring. These features provide users with more flexible tools to manage personal health metrics, reflecting Google's ongoing commitment to expanding its fitness ecosystem.
