'27 million stolen login credentials have been recovered': Global coordinated takedown hits SocGholish, Amadey, and StealC malware networks where it hurt
============================================================

EUROPOL and international law enforcement agencies dismantled major malware infrastructure, freezing $47 million in cryptocurrency and taking down hundreds of servers to disrupt global cybercriminal operations.

### Key Points
- Operation Endgame targeted the distribution networks of SocGholish, Amadey, and StealC malware variants.
- Authorities seized 326 servers and 142 domains while remediating 14,971 compromised websites.
- The operation successfully recovered 27 million stolen login credentials from criminal control.
- Law enforcement agencies from the U.S., U.K., Germany, Netherlands, Denmark, and Canada collaborated with Microsoft.
- No arrests were reported, and experts caution that criminal groups may rebuild their infrastructure over time.

### Why it Matters
This operation significantly increases the operational costs and friction for cybercriminals by removing key distribution tools and financial assets. While the disruption is temporary, it highlights the effectiveness of public-private partnerships in mitigating large-scale malware threats to businesses and individual users.
