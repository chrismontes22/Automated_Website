Why the Next Era of AI Is About Infrastructure, Not Just Models
============================================================

As enterprise AI adoption shifts from experimentation to production, Mozilla has launched Otari, an open-source control plane designed to manage multi-model infrastructure, costs, and governance at scale.

### Key Points
- Enterprise AI has evolved from 2023 "pet projects" to 2026 production-scale deployments, creating urgent needs for reliability and cost management.
- Organizations now face operational chaos due to model fragmentation, using dozens of different providers and versions simultaneously.
- Unpredictable inference costs and non-linear scaling have made real-time budget tracking and cost-per-outcome analysis critical for financial sustainability.
- Regulated industries like healthcare and finance require strict governance, auditability, and data sovereignty that current black-box vendor solutions lack.
- Otari provides a centralized control layer for routing, observability, and policy enforcement, allowing teams to swap models without rewriting application code.

### Why it Matters
- As AI models become commoditized, the primary competitive advantage for enterprises is shifting from model selection to the operational ability to deploy AI reliably and cost-effectively. By providing an open-source control plane, Mozilla aims to prevent vendor lock-in and give organizations the necessary infrastructure to maintain ownership over their data and AI stacks.
