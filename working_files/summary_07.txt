You can now spot AI-generated Google ads, if you know where to look
============================================================

Google is implementing global AI disclosure labels for advertisements across Search, YouTube, and Discover to increase transparency regarding content created or edited using generative artificial intelligence tools.

### Key Points
- Google is introducing a "Created or edited with AI" disclosure within the "How this ad was made" section of My Ad Center.
- Advertisers using Google’s internal generative AI tools will have labels applied automatically to their campaigns.
- Users must manually disclose the use of third-party AI tools, though some markets may display these labels directly on the ad.
- This initiative expands upon existing mandatory disclosure requirements previously established for digitally altered political advertisements.
- The company is leveraging technologies like SynthID and C2PA to improve the identification and labeling of AI-generated content.

### Why it Matters
This update establishes a standardized framework for AI transparency in digital advertising, helping consumers distinguish between human-made and machine-generated marketing content. While it provides greater clarity, the reliance on self-reporting for third-party tools highlights ongoing challenges in enforcing industry-wide accountability for AI usage.
