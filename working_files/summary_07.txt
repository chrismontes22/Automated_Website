ZTE releases Sustainability Report 2025: driving a new chapter in sustainable development through AI
============================================================

The UK government faces significant digital transformation challenges as a new report warns that reliance on aging legacy IT systems threatens the success of future public sector modernization plans.

### Key Points
- A think tank report warns that outdated infrastructure could undermine Whitehall’s ambitious digital strategy and long-term technological goals.
- The UK government recently increased its health AI tender framework value by 400%, raising the maximum budget from £150 million to £600 million.
- This funding hike followed extensive intelligence gathering and consultations with various technology suppliers regarding health sector requirements.
- Security vulnerabilities remain a critical concern, highlighted by a recent incident where a dormant "zombie" user account allowed hackers to compromise city water systems.

### Why it Matters
These developments underscore the tension between the urgent need for AI-driven innovation and the persistent risks posed by neglected legacy systems. Organizations must balance aggressive digital investment with rigorous security hygiene to prevent operational failures and ensure that new technologies do not inherit the vulnerabilities of the past.
