I drove the Slate Truck — there’s more to it than EV minimalism
============================================================

Slate Auto has unveiled an American-made electric pickup truck starting at $24,950, aiming to disrupt the automotive market by prioritizing affordability over the luxury features found in modern vehicles.

### Key Points
- The base model features manual windows and no built-in infotainment system, relying instead on a smartphone dash mount.
- The vehicle offers a 205-mile range, a 1,550-pound payload capacity, and a 2,000-pound towing limit.
- Customers can customize the truck using over 200 accessories or convert the two-seat pickup into a five-seat SUV for approximately $5,000.
- Slate has secured roughly 180,000 reservations, with production scheduled to begin in Warsaw, Indiana, ahead of fourth-quarter deliveries.
- The company provides a 10-year or 110,000-mile battery and powertrain warranty to ensure long-term reliability.

### Why it Matters
- Slate is challenging the industry trend of "trimflation," where automakers bundle expensive technology into vehicles to increase profit margins. By offering a stripped-down, lower-cost alternative, the company is testing whether consumers prioritize basic utility and affordability over the advanced digital features currently dominating the new car market.
