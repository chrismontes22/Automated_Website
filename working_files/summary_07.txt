Google Chrome Installed a 4GB AI Model on Your PC
============================================================

Google Chrome has been found silently installing a 4GB Gemini Nano AI model on user devices, raising significant concerns regarding storage consumption, transparency, and the lack of explicit consent.

### Key Points
- Google Chrome automatically downloads the 4GB "weights.bin" file to user devices if hardware requirements, such as 16GB of RAM and 22GB of free storage, are met.
- Privacy researcher Alexander Hanff discovered that the model reinstalls itself even after manual deletion, as Chrome treats the removal as a temporary system error.
- While Google claims the model enables local features like scam detection, many users report the absence of an "On-Device AI" toggle to disable the background installation.
- The deployment has sparked legal scrutiny, with experts suggesting the practice may violate the EU’s ePrivacy Directive and GDPR transparency requirements.
- Chrome’s "AI Mode" in the address bar continues to process queries via the cloud, meaning the locally installed model does not necessarily power all visible AI features.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights a growing trend where tech companies utilize consumer hardware as distributed infrastructure to reduce their own cloud computing costs. It challenges the current framework of digital consent, as users are forced to sacrifice storage and bandwidth for background AI processes without being given the option to opt-in.
