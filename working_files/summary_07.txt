5 Android Auto settings I always change on any new Android phone
============================================================

Android Auto users can significantly improve their driving experience and safety by adjusting specific settings to manage media playback, notification alerts, and custom shortcuts on their mobile devices.

### Key Points
- Disable "Start music automatically" in the Android Auto settings menu to prevent audio from playing immediately upon vehicle connection.
- Manage distractions by toggling off "Play message chime" or "Show message notifications" within the Messaging sub-menu.
- Customize the app drawer by unchecking unwanted applications in the "Customize Launcher" menu to reduce screen clutter.
- Create one-tap shortcuts for frequent tasks like calling specific contacts or triggering complex Google Assistant commands.
- Enable "Taskbar widgets" to maintain playback controls for music while simultaneously using navigation apps in full-screen mode.
- Prevent automatic startup by disabling "Start Android Auto while locked" to save battery life during short trips.

### Why it Matters
Optimizing these settings allows drivers to minimize cognitive distractions and maintain better focus on the road while using their vehicle's infotainment system. These adjustments provide a more personalized, efficient interface that reduces the need for manual interaction with a phone while driving.
