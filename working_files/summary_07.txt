'Move Fast and Break Things' Broke Everything
============================================================

The "move fast and break things" ideology popularized by Silicon Valley leaders has faced growing criticism for causing widespread societal harm, eroding public trust, and compromising digital security.

### Key Points
- The "move fast and break things" mantra, famously championed by Mark Zuckerberg, is increasingly blamed for prioritizing corporate profit over user safety and social stability.
- Research indicates that algorithmic social media platforms, including Instagram, contribute to body image issues and mental health crises among teenage girls.
- Academic and medical experts have linked the rise of addictive social media and AI chatbots to increased rates of teen suicide and academic dishonesty.
- Major cybersecurity failures, such as the 2017 Equifax data breach and the 2024 CrowdStrike IT outage, are attributed to the industry's history of shipping rushed, vulnerable code.
- Meta has faced multiple legal challenges and fines regarding user safety, data privacy, and the platform's role in real-world conflicts like the Rohingya genocide.

### Why it Matters
This shift in public sentiment reflects a broader disillusionment with the tech industry's tendency to externalize the costs of its rapid innovation onto society. As trust in major technology firms continues to decline, the industry faces increasing pressure from regulators and the public to prioritize ethical development over unchecked growth.
