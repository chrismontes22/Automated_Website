Will Apple Launch New Hardware at WWDC Next Week?
============================================================

Apple is unlikely to unveil new hardware at the 2026 Worldwide Developers Conference, as the company prioritizes software updates and faces ongoing supply chain constraints for its desktop computers.

### Key Points
- Apple will focus the June 8, 2026, event on software updates for iOS, macOS, and visionOS rather than new hardware releases.
- New Apple TV 4K and HomePod mini models are reportedly ready but delayed until the public release of advanced Apple Intelligence features in September.
- Global memory chip shortages have forced Apple to limit configurations for the Mac mini and Mac Studio, impacting current product availability.
- Rumored devices like smart glasses and a dedicated smart home hub remain on hold until Apple’s AI-driven Siri capabilities mature.
- Apple has already released several products in 2026, including the iPhone 17e, M5 MacBook models, and the $599 MacBook Neo.

### Why it Matters
The shift away from hardware announcements at WWDC reflects Apple's strategic focus on stabilizing its AI-integrated software ecosystem before launching compatible smart home devices. This approach highlights the company's current supply chain challenges and its commitment to ensuring that new hardware launches align with the full public release of its next-generation Siri features.
