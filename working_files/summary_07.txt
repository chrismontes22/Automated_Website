If the FCC Bans Burner Phones, It Could Be a Privacy Nightmare
============================================================

The Federal Communications Commission is considering new "Know-Your-Customer" rules that would mandate government identification for all cellphone users, effectively banning anonymous burner phones to combat rising robocall scams.

### Key Points
- The proposed FCC mandate requires telecom companies to collect names, addresses, and government ID numbers from all new and renewing mobile customers.
- Officials frame the policy as a necessary measure to curb robocalls, which have increased at a 16% annual rate since 2023.
- Privacy advocates warn the rule could disenfranchise the 21 million voting-age Americans without driver's licenses and millions of undocumented immigrants.
- Critics highlight that major providers like AT&T, Verizon, and Lumen have recently suffered significant data breaches, raising concerns about the security of centralized personal information.
- Public comments on the proposal, filed under proceeding 17-59, are being accepted by the FCC until June 25.

### Why it Matters
This policy shift represents a significant departure from current privacy standards by effectively eliminating the ability for individuals to maintain anonymity through mobile devices. If enacted, the rule could create a centralized database of sensitive user information that may be vulnerable to future cyberattacks or government surveillance.
