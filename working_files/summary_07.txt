Touchscreen MacBook '100% Confirmed,' Says Reputable Leaker
============================================================

Apple is reportedly developing its first touchscreen MacBook Pro, with industry analysts and supply chain leaks pointing toward a potential product launch between late 2026 and early 2027.

### Key Points
- Supply chain leaker Instant Digital confirmed Apple is actively developing a touchscreen MacBook model.
- Analyst Ming-Chi Kuo projects mass production for the touchscreen OLED MacBook Pro will begin in 2026.
- Bloomberg’s Mark Gurman suggests a release window between late 2026 and 2027, potentially branded as MacBook Ultra.
- Expected hardware upgrades include M6 Pro and M6 Max chips, OLED display technology, and a thinner chassis.
- The interface will be designed as "touch-friendly" rather than "touch-first," allowing users to alternate between touch and mouse inputs.

### Why it Matters
This development represents a significant strategic reversal for Apple, which previously maintained that vertical touchscreens were ergonomically impractical for laptop users. Integrating touch capabilities could reshape the high-end laptop market by bridging the functional gap between macOS and iPadOS hardware.
