Pokémon GO scan controversy resurfaces after Niantic Spatial defense partnership
============================================================

Niantic Spatial is facing renewed scrutiny after partnering with defense contractor Vantor to integrate its Visual Positioning System, which was trained using billions of images from Pokémon GO players.

### Key Points
- Niantic Spatial’s Visual Positioning System (VPS) uses 30 billion images collected from optional AR scans in games like Pokémon GO.
- The company recently partnered with Vantor, a defense contractor formerly known as Maxar Intelligence, to develop GPS-independent navigation technology.
- Vantor holds a $70 million contract with the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency to provide intelligence and navigation services for U.S. government users.
- Niantic stated that Pokémon GO data is no longer shared with Niantic Spatial following the game's transition to Scopely.
- There is no public evidence that player-contributed scan data is currently being deployed in military weapons or drone systems.

### Why it Matters
The controversy highlights growing ethical concerns regarding how consumer-generated data from entertainment apps is repurposed for industrial and defense applications. It raises significant questions about transparency and whether users can provide informed consent when their data contributes to technologies that evolve far beyond their original context.
