Leaked Anthropic Model Presents ‘Unprecedented Cybersecurity Risks,’ Much to Pentagon’s Pleasure
============================================================

**Anthropic has confirmed the existence of Claude Mythos, a highly advanced, unreleased AI model that the company is currently withholding due to significant cybersecurity and safety concerns.**

**Key points**
- Anthropic accidentally leaked details about Claude Mythos, a model described as significantly more powerful than the current Claude Opus 4.6.
- The company is delaying the public release of Mythos, citing unprecedented cyber capabilities that pose potential security risks.
- A federal judge recently issued a temporary ruling blocking the Department of Defense from labeling Anthropic a security risk.
- Under Secretary of War Emil Michael publicly criticized Anthropic following the leak, despite the company holding government clearance for classified materials.
- Reports suggest Anthropic is considering an initial public offering (IPO) later this year, potentially leveraging the model's capabilities to attract investors.

**Why it matters: **
The leak highlights the ongoing tension between AI developers and government agencies regarding the balance of national security, corporate autonomy, and public safety. Furthermore, the revelation of a high-performance model serves as a significant signal to the market as Anthropic prepares for a potential public offering.
