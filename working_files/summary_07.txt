Is AI making us dumber?
============================================================

Emerging research suggests that widespread reliance on generative AI may negatively impact human cognitive functions, including critical thinking, creativity, and mental persistence, by automating complex intellectual tasks.

### Key Points
- MIT researcher Nataliya Kosmyna found that students using generative AI for writing performed worse over time than those using traditional search tools or no assistance.
- A study from Carnegie Mellon University indicated that even brief AI use can reduce a person's willingness to persist through difficult math problems.
- Georgetown University researchers observed that while AI-assisted essays feature unique language, they contain fewer original ideas compared to human-written work.
- Studies on GPS and pilot training demonstrate that cognitive skills often atrophy when individuals consistently outsource procedural or spatial tasks to technology.
- Experts emphasize that unlike previous innovations, AI is the first tool capable of performing the actual process of ideation and thinking for the user.

### Why it Matters
The potential for cognitive decline poses significant risks to workforce development and the long-term preservation of essential human skills. As AI becomes deeply integrated into daily life, society must determine which intellectual capabilities to protect through practice and which to safely outsource to automated systems.
