Stop Paying for a VPN: Firefox Just Built One Right Into Your Browser
============================================================

Mozilla has integrated a new, limited-use VPN directly into the Firefox browser, providing users with free, privacy-focused protection for web traffic without requiring a separate subscription or app.

### Key Points
- The new Firefox VPN feature is built into the browser and includes a 50GB monthly data limit for users.
- Mozilla’s VPN technology utilizes the WireGuard protocol and has undergone independent security audits by Cure53.
- Unlike standalone VPN services, this tool only secures traffic within the Firefox browser rather than protecting entire system processes or external applications.
- Cybersecurity experts warn that the feature is intended for casual browsing and should not be used for sensitive data or large-scale operations.

### Why it Matters
This update represents a significant shift in how privacy tools are delivered by making essential protection accessible to casual users without additional costs. While it offers a convenient layer of security, users must understand that its limited scope does not provide the comprehensive protection of a full-system VPN.
