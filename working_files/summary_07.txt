We Need Old Filmmakers, but Why Are So Many of Them Obsessed with AI? — Opinion
============================================================

A generational divide is emerging in Hollywood as legendary filmmakers like Paul Schrader embrace generative AI, while younger creators and audiences increasingly prioritize human-led, authentic cinematic storytelling.

### Key Points
- Director Paul Schrader recently advocated for using ChatGPT to generate script ideas during the "AI on the Lot" industry symposium.
- Other prominent filmmakers, including James Cameron and Gareth Edwards, have expressed varying levels of support for integrating AI into film production.
- Conversely, directors like Guillermo del Toro have publicly rejected the use of generative AI in their creative processes.
- Younger audiences are increasingly turning to repertory screenings and physical media, signaling a desire for human connection over digital automation.
- The debate highlights tensions between using technology for efficiency and preserving the unique, human perspective essential to artistic legacy.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI in filmmaking reflects broader anxieties about how automation and digital speed threaten to erode human creativity and professional stability. As industry veterans and newcomers navigate this transition, the debate underscores a critical need to balance technological innovation with the preservation of authentic, experience-driven storytelling.
