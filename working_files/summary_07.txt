The EU becomes the latest authority to signal that VPNs are next, after launching its age verification app — here's how VPNs went from a necessity security tool to circumvention software that needs to be restricted
============================================================

Democratic governments worldwide are increasingly considering restrictions on VPN technology to prevent users from bypassing mandatory age verification laws, sparking significant concerns among cybersecurity experts and privacy advocates.

### Key Points
- EU Executive Vice-President Henna Virkkunen recently suggested that VPNs should not be used to circumvent new age verification requirements for online services.
- Utah has become the first U.S. state to enforce legal restrictions on VPN usage as part of its broader age verification regulatory framework.
- The United Kingdom’s Children's Wellbeing and Schools Bill mandates that adult service providers implement "reasonable anti-circumvention measures" to block unauthorized access.
- Cybersecurity organizations, including the VPN Trust Initiative and Mozilla, warn that restricting privacy tools undermines essential online security for all users.
- Digital rights experts argue that blocking VPNs is technically impractical and may lead to ineffective, costly, and invasive surveillance measures.

### Why it Matters
These legislative trends signal a shift where privacy-enhancing technologies are increasingly viewed by regulators as tools for circumvention rather than essential security infrastructure. This policy direction risks compromising the digital safety of the general public while creating significant technical and ethical challenges for global internet governance.
