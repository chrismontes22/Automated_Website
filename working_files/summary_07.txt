Lawsuit Claims Meta's Layoff Decisions Were Made By AI, Not Humans
============================================================

Twenty-six former Meta employees have filed a lawsuit alleging the company used AI-driven monitoring and ranking systems to unfairly target workers with disabilities or those on protected leave.

### Key Points
- The lawsuit claims Meta utilized internal AI tools like "Metamate" and activity-monitoring dashboards to score and select employees for termination.
- Plaintiffs allege the automated systems penalized workers for taking medical, family, or pregnancy leave by failing to adjust productivity metrics for time away.
- Employees were reportedly categorized by their adoption of AI tools, with labels including "AI Native," "AI First," and "AI Enabled."
- The 26 plaintiffs all requested disability accommodations or protected leave within the 24 months preceding their selection for layoffs.
- Meta has denied the allegations, stating that workforce management decisions are made by human managers rather than artificial intelligence.
- The scheduled job cuts for the affected employees are set to begin on July 22.

### Why it Matters
This case represents the first legal challenge against a major U.S. corporation regarding the use of algorithmic systems to conduct mass layoffs. It highlights growing concerns over how automated performance tracking may inadvertently violate labor laws by penalizing employees for exercising their rights to medical or family leave.
