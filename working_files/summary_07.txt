Onchain commodity trading is here to stay, but liquidity remains an issue
============================================================

Hyperliquid recorded a record $5.4 billion in perpetual futures volume on March 23, highlighting growing demand for onchain commodity trading during traditional market closures on weekends.

**Key points**
- Hyperliquid’s HIP-3 market reached a $5.4 billion daily volume record, led by silver at $1.3 billion and WTI crude oil at $1.2 billion.
- Onchain platforms provide 24/7 trading access, filling the 49-hour gap when traditional exchanges like the CME are offline.
- Traditional finance venues still maintain significantly higher liquidity, with daily oil futures volumes often ranging between $100 billion and $300 billion.
- Industry experts identify limited liquidity, price aggregation gaps, and regulatory uncertainty as the primary barriers to institutional-scale adoption.
- Onchain markets currently serve as a price discovery layer during off-hours, while traditional exchanges remain the primary venues for large-scale execution.

**Why it matters: **
The rise of onchain commodity trading demonstrates a shift in how market participants react to geopolitical events that occur outside of standard business hours. While traditional exchanges remain the dominant source of liquidity, decentralized platforms are increasingly serving as critical price discovery venues for global macro assets.
