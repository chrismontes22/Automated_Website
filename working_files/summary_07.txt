The class of 2026 might have to pivot in their job hunt
============================================================

Recent college graduates are facing a challenging labor market characterized by slower hiring rates, increased competition, and a growing reliance on artificial intelligence to handle entry-level job tasks.

### Key Points
- The underemployment rate for recent college graduates remains high at 41.5% as of March 2025.
- Hiring rates in finance, business services, and healthcare have declined to levels not seen since the pandemic.
- Economists suggest that companies are increasingly using AI to replace entry-level roles, leading to a broader pullback in hiring for younger workers.
- Experts recommend that job seekers consider smaller employers, internships, or contract work to gain experience and build professional networks.
- Data from the National Federation of Independent Business indicates that one-third of small businesses currently have unfilled job openings.

### Why it Matters
The current job market shift forces new graduates to abandon traditional career ladders in favor of more flexible, non-linear paths to employment. This trend highlights a significant transition in how entry-level talent enters the workforce, emphasizing the need for adaptability in an era of technological disruption.
