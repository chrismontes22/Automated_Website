You Can Finally Buy Snap’s New AR Specs—for $2,150
============================================================

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel unveiled the new consumer-ready Specs, a pair of augmented-reality smart glasses priced at $2,195 that feature standalone processing and immersive spatial computing capabilities.

### Key Points
- The new Specs are available for preorder now with a $2,195 price tag and are expected to ship this fall in the US, UK, and France.
- The glasses feature dual Qualcomm Snapdragon processors, allowing for standalone operation without the need for a companion app, external compute puck, or tethered battery.
- Hardware specifications include a 51-degree field of view, four hours of battery life, and two frame sizes weighing between 132 and 136 grams.
- The device supports various AR experiences, including web browsing, AI-powered visual assistance, and spatial interaction with 3D models and room-aware objects.
- Snap is positioning the product as a developer-focused platform, emphasizing that the device's long-term utility depends on third-party software creation.

### Why it Matters
This launch represents a significant strategic pivot for Snap as it attempts to transition from a social media software company into a hardware-focused augmented reality provider. By targeting developers with a standalone device, the company is positioning itself to compete directly with tech giants like Meta and Apple in the emerging wearable computing market.
