And then there were none: Musk's last xAI cofounder is out
============================================================

Ross Nordeen, the final remaining cofounder of xAI, has departed the company as Elon Musk continues a significant organizational restructuring of the artificial intelligence startup.

**Key points**
- Ross Nordeen, a former Tesla technical program manager, was the last of the 11 original xAI cofounders to leave the firm.
- Eight cofounders have exited xAI since January, including key leaders like Manuel Kroiss and Greg Yang.
- Elon Musk recently stated that xAI was not built correctly initially and is currently undergoing a foundational reorganization.
- The company has recently hired new talent, including senior leaders from the AI coding firm Cursor.
- xAI has faced internal flux and staff reductions following its February merger with SpaceX.

**Why it matters: **
The departure of the entire original founding team signals a major shift in strategy as Elon Musk attempts to pivot xAI to better compete with industry leaders like OpenAI and Anthropic. This restructuring is critical for the company's long-term viability as it seeks to scale its technology and improve its market position.
