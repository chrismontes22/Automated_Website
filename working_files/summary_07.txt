Artemis II astronauts are capturing high-quality lunar imagery using iPhones during their historic NASA mission, utilizing unique vantage points while traveling further from Earth than any previous humans.

### Key Points
- NASA astronauts on the Artemis II lunar flyby mission are using standard iPhones to photograph the moon's surface.
- The crew captured detailed images of lunar canyons and craters while reaching the midpoint of their ten-day journey.
- Astronauts improved image clarity by turning off cabin lights to reduce glare and reflections inside the spacecraft.
- The mission marks a record-breaking distance from Earth, providing the crew with an unprecedented perspective for mobile photography.

### Why it Matters
This development demonstrates the increasing capability of consumer-grade technology to perform in extreme space environments. It highlights a shift in space exploration where accessible hardware can effectively document complex scientific missions for the public.
