Commodore is back with a de-Googled feature phone for the anti-doomscrolling crowd
============================================================

**The Commodore Callback 8020 is a Y2K-inspired flip phone designed to combat smartphone fatigue by blocking social media and web browsers while maintaining essential app functionality for users.**

### Key Points
- The device runs a custom version of Sailfish OS, developed in collaboration with the Finnish company Jolla.
- It features system-level blocks on social media and web browsers to reduce digital distractions and screen addiction.
- Despite lacking a browser, the phone supports over 99% of Android applications, including WhatsApp, Spotify, and Signal.
- Hardware includes a 3.5mm headphone jack, an audiophile-grade DAC, a removable battery, and interchangeable covers.
- Pricing ranges from $500 for standard models to $640 for the gold-plated Founders Edition, with shipping targeted for Q4 2026.

### Why it Matters
This device represents a growing market trend toward "digital minimalism," where consumers seek to reclaim their attention from hyper-connected smartphone ecosystems. By offering essential utility without the addictive nature of social media, Commodore is positioning itself to capture users looking for a healthier balance between technology and daily life.
