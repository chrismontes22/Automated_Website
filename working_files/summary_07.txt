Platform Stability reached: Android 17 QPR1 Beta 6 is here for Pixels, quickly after Beta 5
============================================================

Google has released Android 17 QPR1 Beta 6 for enrolled Pixel testers, marking a significant milestone as the quarterly update officially reaches Platform Stability ahead of a wider launch.

### Key Points
- The update, version vCP31.260618.005, is available for Pixel 6 and newer smartphone models.
- Beta 6 addresses specific bugs, including visual glitches in the media carousel and unintended volume button behavior in the Clock app.
- Additional fixes resolve issues with spell checker language selection, app crashes in WindowManagerGlobal, and incorrect Wi-Fi hotspot SSID displays.
- This release follows Beta 5 by only one week, demonstrating an accelerated development cycle for the Android 17 quarterly update.

### Why it Matters
Reaching Platform Stability indicates that the software is nearing its final, public-ready state for all compatible Pixel devices. This milestone suggests that Google is prioritizing a faster release cadence to resolve persistent bugs and improve overall system performance for users.
