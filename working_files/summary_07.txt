Lenovo’s $799 Puck-Shaped PC Weighs 600g and Drives 4 Displays
============================================================

Lenovo has unveiled the Yoga Mini i Gen 11, a compact, circular desktop PC featuring Intel’s Panther Lake processors and advanced AI-accelerated performance for modern professional workspaces.

### Key Points
- The device features a 0.65-liter aluminum chassis weighing 600 grams, designed for desk-saving or monitor-mounted setups.
- It is powered by Intel’s Core Ultra X7 358H processor, utilizing the 18A manufacturing process and an NPU capable of 50 TOPS.
- Connectivity options include dual Thunderbolt 4 ports, HDMI 2.1, Wi-Fi 7, and Bluetooth 6.0, supporting up to four simultaneous displays.
- Security features include a fingerprint reader, Microsoft Pluton integration, and Secured-core PC certification.
- The unit launched in China at approximately $799, with a global rollout expected before mid-2026.

### Why it Matters
This release signals a shift in the desktop market toward aesthetic, high-performance hardware that prioritizes space efficiency without sacrificing professional-grade computing power. By integrating advanced AI capabilities and sustainable design, Lenovo is positioning the mini PC as a viable, premium alternative to traditional bulky desktop towers for creative and office environments.
