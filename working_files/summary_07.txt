Smartphone sales are down, but Fairphone is having a breakout year
============================================================

Sustainable smartphone manufacturer Fairphone reported a 116% year-over-year increase in device shipments, marking three consecutive quarters of growth despite a broader 6% decline in the global electronics market.

### Key Points
- Fairphone achieved a 116% year-over-year shipment increase in the first quarter of 2026, following a 42% sales jump throughout 2025.
- European market expansion is led by the UK with 241% growth, followed by the Netherlands at 200% and France at 121%.
- The Amsterdam-based company is approaching the milestones of one million total devices sold and $500 million in cumulative revenue.
- Annual revenue for 2025 reached €73.3 million, representing a 35% increase compared to the previous year.
- Growth spans multiple product categories, including modular smartphones, accessories, and audio equipment.

### Why it Matters
Fairphone’s performance demonstrates that consumer demand for repairable, sustainable hardware can remain resilient even when the broader smartphone industry faces economic headwinds and supply shortages. This success suggests a shifting market preference toward longevity and transparency over the industry-standard cycle of planned obsolescence.
