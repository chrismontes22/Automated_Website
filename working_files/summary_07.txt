The Right Way to Get Rid of Old Laptops, PCs and Printers (and It's Free)
============================================================

Major retailers like Best Buy and Staples offer convenient, often free, electronics recycling programs to help consumers responsibly dispose of old computers, printers, and other household tech devices.

### Key Points
- Best Buy accepts up to three household items daily for free, with a higher limit of five laptops per household.
- Staples provides free in-store recycling for computers and printers regardless of where the original items were purchased.
- Office Depot offers a trade-in program for store credit, with free recycling available for devices that hold no resale value.
- Apple provides free recycling for its own products, though the service often requires the purchase of a new qualifying device.
- Consumers should perform a factory reset or use data-scrubbing tools to protect personal information before recycling any hardware.
- Earth911 and the Consumer Technology Association’s Greener Gadgets locator help users find local, specialized electronics recycling centers.

### Why it Matters
Properly recycling electronics prevents hazardous materials from entering landfills while allowing valuable components to be reclaimed and reused. By utilizing these retail programs, consumers can securely clear out unused clutter while ensuring their sensitive data is handled safely.
