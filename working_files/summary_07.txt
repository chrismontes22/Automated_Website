Nvidia announces RTX Spark as ‘the most efficient PC chip ever built’
============================================================

Nvidia is entering the consumer PC market with its new Arm-based RTX Spark chip family, aiming to power high-performance Windows laptops and desktops starting this fall.

### Key Points
- The RTX Spark features up to 20 CPU cores, 6,144 GPU cores, and 128GB of LPDDR5X unified memory.
- Major manufacturers including Microsoft, Acer, Asus, Dell, HP, and Lenovo are developing over 40 devices using the new silicon.
- Microsoft’s Surface Laptop Ultra is confirmed as one of the first premium devices to feature the RTX Spark processor.
- The chip supports native Arm applications for creative software like Adobe Premiere and Blender, alongside major gaming titles with anti-cheat support.
- Nvidia claims the architecture enables advanced local AI agent capabilities, allowing users to automate complex tasks without relying on cloud-based processing.

### Why it Matters
Nvidia’s transition into consumer PC silicon challenges the long-standing dominance of x86 architecture providers like Intel and AMD. By integrating high-end graphics and AI capabilities into a single Arm-based chip, the company aims to redefine laptop performance and power efficiency for the Windows ecosystem.
