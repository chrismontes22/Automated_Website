1Password Will Let You Allow Claude to Use Your Passwords, but Not See Them
============================================================

Anthropic and 1Password have launched a new integration allowing the Claude AI assistant to securely log users into websites without ever exposing their actual password credentials to the model.

### Key Points
- The integration uses a "zero-exposure" method that requires manual biometric approval for every individual login attempt.
- When active, Agentic Mode locks down the 1Password vault, restricting the AI to only the specific credentials required for a requested task.
- The feature is currently available on Mac for 1Password business, family, and individual plans.
- Users must have the 1Password and Claude desktop apps and browser extensions installed to enable the functionality.
- Future updates are expected to expand the tool's capabilities to include payment cards and identity details.

### Why it Matters
This partnership addresses significant privacy concerns regarding the use of AI agents by ensuring sensitive data remains encrypted and inaccessible to the model itself. By implementing strict biometric guardrails, the integration provides a secure framework for the growing trend of agentic AI performing automated tasks on behalf of users.
