Bitcoin drops below $73,000 as US strikes on Iran spark $1 billion liquidations
============================================================

Bitcoin prices fell below $73,000 as U.S. airstrikes on Iranian military sites triggered a massive market sell-off, resulting in nearly $1 billion in liquidated leveraged cryptocurrency positions.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin dropped below $73,000 following U.S. military strikes near the Strait of Hormuz and new sanctions against Iran.
- Approximately $958.8 million in leveraged crypto positions were liquidated within 24 hours, affecting over 167,000 traders.
- Long positions accounted for 93% of the total liquidations, with Bitcoin and Ether leading the market decline.
- Global stock indices retreated and oil prices rose as regional military tensions reversed recent optimism regarding a Middle East ceasefire.
- U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs have recorded nine consecutive days of net outflows, totaling roughly $1.3 billion in withdrawals this week.

### Why it Matters
This event highlights the extreme sensitivity of cryptocurrency markets to geopolitical instability and the risks associated with high-leverage trading strategies. The rapid liquidation cascade demonstrates how quickly market sentiment can shift when unexpected military conflicts disrupt established price floors for risk assets.
