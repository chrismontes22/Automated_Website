Tidal to Label AI-Generated Music and Remove Fraudulent Material
============================================================

Tidal is implementing new automated detection tools in mid-July to label fully AI-generated music and remove fraudulent tracks from its platform to protect authentic artist royalties and content.

### Key Points
- Tidal will display an "AI" icon on tracks identified as 100% generated by artificial intelligence starting in mid-July.
- The platform will restrict royalty payments to music that is directly produced, written, and performed by human creators.
- Automated systems will target fraudulent activity, including deceptive content, high-volume uploads, and unusual streaming patterns.
- Artists can dispute incorrect AI designations by contacting the Tidal support team for a manual review of their work.
- This policy aligns Tidal with competitors like Spotify and Deezer, which have also introduced detection tools to manage synthetic content.

### Why it Matters
These measures address the growing challenge of synthetic media by protecting the economic interests of human musicians against automated content proliferation. As the AI music market is projected to reach $4 billion by 2028, establishing clear standards for transparency and monetization is essential for maintaining platform integrity.
