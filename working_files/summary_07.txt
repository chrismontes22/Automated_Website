xAI Adds 16 New Gas Turbines at Mississippi Facility Despite Ongoing Lawsuit
============================================================

xAI has expanded its Southaven, Mississippi data center by installing 19 additional natural gas turbines, bringing the total to 46 units amid ongoing legal challenges regarding air quality permits.

### Key Points
- xAI installed 19 portable gas turbines between late March and early May, adding over 500 megawatts of power capacity to the Colossus 2 site.
- The NAACP and environmental groups filed a lawsuit alleging the company is operating these turbines without required Clean Air Act permits.
- Eight of the new turbines were installed after the initial lawsuit was filed in April, according to internal Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality emails.
- The 46 turbines currently at the Southaven site are not covered by the air permit granted by the MDEQ in March.
- xAI argues that the onsite power generation is essential for maintaining the data centers required for its artificial intelligence operations.

### Why it Matters
The rapid expansion of xAI’s onsite power generation highlights the growing tension between the massive energy demands of artificial intelligence and local environmental regulations. This conflict underscores the potential for significant legal and public relations risks as tech companies bypass traditional grid infrastructure to accelerate data center deployment.
