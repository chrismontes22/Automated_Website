Meta Employees Absolutely Hate Zuckerberg’s Plan for a Companywide AI Hackathon
============================================================

Meta employees have expressed frustration and skepticism toward CEO Mark Zuckerberg’s announcement of a companywide AI hackathon scheduled for July 14 to 16 following recent mass layoffs.

### Key Points
- Meta plans to host a companywide AI innovation hackathon from July 14 to July 16.
- Employees criticized the event, citing increased workloads and reduced staffing following the layoff of 8,000 workers.
- Staff members noted that hackathon participation does not count toward performance evaluations, discouraging involvement.
- Internal feedback highlighted concerns regarding low morale, declining trust in management, and a perceived shift in company culture.
- Zuckerberg also announced increased budgets for team offsites and the end of hot-desking policies to address workforce unrest.

### Why it Matters
The backlash highlights a growing disconnect between Meta’s leadership and its workforce regarding corporate culture and productivity expectations. This tension suggests that morale issues may hinder the company's ability to foster innovation during its transition toward AI-focused development.
