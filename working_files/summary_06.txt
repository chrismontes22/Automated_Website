Apple Crackdown Suspected After iPhone 18 Pro Leak Videos Disappear
============================================================

Apple is reportedly working to remove leaked footage of the upcoming iPhone 18 Pro from social media following a significant data breach at its manufacturing partner, Tata Electronics.

### Key Points
- Leaked video clips appearing to show iPhone 18 Pro drop testing surfaced on X before being removed for platform policy violations.
- The breach originated from Tata Electronics, where confidential files, component details, and supplier information were stolen and circulated on the dark web.
- Industry analyst Evan Blass denied involvement with the account that posted the leaks, noting the unusual speed of Apple's intervention.
- Apple is currently investigating the security incident and collaborating with Tata Electronics to implement improved data protection measures.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the increasing vulnerability of Apple’s global supply chain to cyberattacks and the subsequent exposure of proprietary product designs. The company's aggressive response suggests a shift toward stricter enforcement to protect intellectual property and maintain secrecy ahead of official product launches.
