My first 24 hours with Siri AI on the Mac
============================================================

**Early testing of the new Siri AI in the macOS 27 developer beta reveals improved data analysis capabilities alongside significant limitations when interacting with third-party applications and complex workflows.**

### Key Points
- The macOS 27 developer beta introduces Siri AI, which can now analyze local files, calculate data averages, and provide creative suggestions for photo editing.
- Testing on M5 MacBook Air and M5 Max MacBook Pro hardware shows the assistant struggles to access data stored in non-Apple apps like Signal, Google Photos, and Lightroom.
- Siri AI currently lacks the ability to execute complex automated tasks within third-party software, such as running specific hardware benchmarks.
- The assistant occasionally provides inaccurate data extraction or sycophantic feedback, indicating the software is still in an early, unrefined state.
- Integration remains heavily optimized for Apple’s native ecosystem, creating a disparity between its performance on mobile devices versus professional desktop workflows.

### Why it Matters
The performance of Siri AI on macOS highlights the ongoing challenge of integrating generative AI into professional desktop environments where users rely on diverse, non-Apple software ecosystems. While the tool shows promise for basic data management, its current inability to bridge the gap between different platforms may limit its immediate utility for power users and creative professionals.
