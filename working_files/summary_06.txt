As Suspected, The ‘Trump Phone’ Is Just A Cheap Overseas Knockoff With Some Garish Yellow Paint
============================================================

Technical analysis by iFixit reveals the Trump Mobile T1 smartphone is a rebranded HTC U24 Pro, contradicting previous marketing claims that the device was manufactured in the United States.

### Key Points
- The Trump Mobile T1 is a rebadged version of the two-year-old Taiwanese HTC U24 Pro, which utilizes Chinese-made internal components.
- Despite initial marketing promises of "Made in the USA" production, the Trump Organization later revised its messaging to claim the phone was made with "American values."
- The device retails for $500, representing a markup over the original $469.99 launch price of the underlying HTC hardware.
- A data breach revealed the company had sold approximately 30,000 units, significantly lower than the 600,000 devices previously claimed by the organization.
- The phone comes pre-loaded with proprietary software, including the Truth Social application, and operates as a reseller of T-Mobile network services.

### Why it Matters
This situation highlights the risks associated with consumer electronics marketed through political branding rather than technical innovation or transparent supply chains. The discrepancy between marketing claims and the actual origin of the hardware raises significant questions regarding consumer protection and the integrity of business ventures tied to political figures.
