Apple Briefly Became the World’s Most Valuable Company, Toppling Nvidia
============================================================

Apple briefly reclaimed its position as the world’s most valuable company on Friday, surpassing Nvidia as investors reassess the long-term profitability of massive artificial intelligence infrastructure spending.

### Key Points
- Apple reached a valuation of $4.9 trillion, narrowly overtaking Nvidia’s $4.86 trillion market capitalization during Friday's trading session.
- Investors are shifting focus toward Apple’s capital-efficient AI strategy, which allocates only 2.5% of its budget to physical data center infrastructure.
- Major tech competitors like Google, Meta, and Amazon currently dedicate up to 39% of their capital expenditure to energy-intensive AI data centers.
- Apple’s stock has risen 23% this year, bolstered by the positive reception of its revamped Siri virtual assistant and its 2.5 billion active devices.
- Global markets, including the NASDAQ and S&P 500, experienced declines as skepticism grows regarding the immediate revenue returns on heavy AI investments.

### Why it Matters
This shift highlights a growing investor preference for companies that can monetize artificial intelligence through existing consumer ecosystems rather than those relying on massive, unproven infrastructure spending. Apple’s ability to leverage its massive installed base may provide a more sustainable path to AI profitability compared to the high-cost models currently favored by other Big Tech firms.
