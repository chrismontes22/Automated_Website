Apple's AI Smart Glasses Likely to Support Hand Gesture Controls
============================================================

Apple is developing AI-powered smart glasses featuring dual cameras and gesture-based controls to compete directly with Meta’s Ray-Ban smart glasses, with a potential market launch targeted for 2027.

### Key Points
- The glasses will feature a high-resolution camera for media capture and a low-resolution wide-angle lens for hand gesture recognition and Siri input.
- To maintain a lightweight design and manage battery constraints, the initial model will exclude displays, LiDAR, and 3D camera technology.
- Apple is testing frames constructed from acetate, a flexible, plant-based material designed for comfort and durability.
- The device will integrate with the upgraded version of Siri expected in iOS 27 to provide contextual information about the user's surroundings.
- Development is currently focused on balancing a slim form factor with the energy demands of onboard AI processing and camera hardware.

### Why it Matters
This development signals Apple's strategic entry into the wearable AI market, directly challenging Meta's established position in the smart eyewear sector. By prioritizing gesture-based interaction and lightweight design, Apple aims to create a practical, screen-free interface that could redefine how users interact with digital assistants in daily life.
