Apple registers new ‘gen AI’ subdomain ahead of next month’s WWDC keynote
============================================================

Apple has registered a new subdomain, genai.apple.com, signaling a major expansion of generative artificial intelligence features expected to debut at the upcoming Worldwide Developers Conference.

### Key Points
- The new subdomain was identified by MacRumors contributor Aaron Perris ahead of the WWDC 2026 event.
- Apple plans to integrate Google’s Gemini-diffused models via Private Cloud Compute to enhance Siri’s contextual awareness and app-control capabilities.
- A dedicated Siri app will launch, enabling users to review past interactions and engage in text-based conversations with the assistant.
- New Apple Intelligence features include generating digital wallet passes from physical tickets and advanced editing tools for the Photos app.
- The company intends to prioritize system stability and performance improvements alongside these new AI-driven software updates.

### Why it Matters
This development marks a critical shift in Apple’s strategy to modernize its ecosystem through deep integration of generative AI and cloud-based partnerships. By leveraging external models to power core services like Siri, Apple aims to regain its competitive edge in the rapidly evolving artificial intelligence market.
