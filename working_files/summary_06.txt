This robotic self-driving toilet comes to you
============================================================

Chinese company Yueban has unveiled the Xiaoban, an autonomous, self-driving smart toilet designed to assist individuals with mobility issues by navigating to users and handling waste disposal independently.

### Key Points
- The Xiaoban utilizes lidar and ultrasonic sensors to navigate homes and care facilities while avoiding obstacles.
- The device features a built-in bidet, warm air dryer, and ultraviolet light sterilization to maintain hygiene and minimize odors.
- It is priced at approximately ¥28,999, or $4,300, though international availability remains unconfirmed.
- The unit automatically returns to a docking station to recharge, refill water reservoirs, and empty waste into plumbing systems.
- It is intended to reduce the physical burden on caregivers by eliminating the need to transport patients to traditional bathrooms.

### Why it Matters
This innovation represents a significant advancement in assistive technology by integrating autonomous robotics into personal healthcare and daily living. By automating the toileting process, the device could improve independence for elderly or disabled users while reducing the labor-intensive tasks currently required of professional and family caregivers.
