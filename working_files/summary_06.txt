The Speed of Prototyping in the Age of AI
============================================================

Software engineer and developer reports a fourfold increase in productivity by integrating AI agents into their workflow, enabling rapid prototyping and the successful completion of multiple complex projects.

### Key Points
- The author developed several new projects, including the Sakoa systems language, the Kato notation language, and the Plim block editor.
- AI integration has shifted the engineering focus toward high-level system architecture, contract definition, and clear specification writing.
- The developer reports an average 4x increase in speed for typical engineering tasks, measured by time-to-pull-request.
- Professional impact includes a 50% reduction in internal codespace bootstrap times and the implementation of new automation support tools.
- To maintain technical proficiency, the author intentionally reserves time for manual coding, debugging, and reading source code without AI assistance.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward AI-assisted development fundamentally changes the scope of what individual engineers can achieve by lowering the barrier to entry for complex projects. This evolution forces a transition from manual implementation to high-level systems thinking, effectively turning developers into architects who manage and delegate tasks to automated agents.
