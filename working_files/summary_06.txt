'There is no reason for any individual to have a computer in his home': Quote of the day by Digital Equipment Corporation co-founder Ken Olsen on the future of smart homes
============================================================

Digital Equipment Corporation co-founder Ken Olsen famously predicted in 1977 that individuals would have no reason to own a home computer, a statement later clarified as a misunderstanding.

### Key Points
- Ken Olsen, CEO of Digital Equipment Corporation, made his controversial remarks during a 1977 World Future Society convention.
- Olsen later argued his comments were taken out of context and referred specifically to the impracticality of a centralized home automation system.
- The evolution of smart home technology has been hindered for decades by high costs, hardware limitations, and a lack of device interoperability.
- The introduction of the Matter smart home standard is currently addressing long-standing integration issues between different manufacturers and devices.

### Why it Matters
This historical miscalculation highlights the difficulty of predicting consumer technology adoption before the rise of personal computing and the modern smart home. The shift toward standardized protocols like Matter demonstrates how industry-wide collaboration is essential for moving complex, automated home technologies into the mainstream market.
