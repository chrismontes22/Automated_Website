A mining billionaire made a major bet on SpaceX — and is eyeing potential ties to Musk's company
============================================================

Australian mining billionaire Gina Rinehart has invested over $1 billion in SpaceX, signaling a strategic move to align her critical minerals portfolio with the aerospace company's future resource needs.

### Key Points
- Hancock Prospecting, led by Gina Rinehart, secured a significant stake in SpaceX during its record-breaking $85 billion initial public offering.
- SpaceX shares surged nearly 20% following their market debut, pushing the company's total market capitalization above $2 trillion.
- Hancock Prospecting holds major investments in rare-earth producers, including Lynas Rare Earths and MP Materials.
- The partnership explores potential synergies between SpaceX’s space exploration ambitions and the supply of critical minerals required for advanced technology.

### Why it Matters
This investment bridges the gap between traditional resource extraction and the emerging space economy, positioning Rinehart to supply materials for future lunar and asteroid mining projects. It highlights a growing trend of industrial giants securing stakes in aerospace firms to capitalize on the long-term demand for specialized minerals in space infrastructure.
