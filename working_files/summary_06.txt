Stock market today: Dow, S&P 500, Nasdaq futures steady after record-setting session
============================================================

The Dow Jones Industrial Average reached a record high and the S&P 500 achieved its best quarterly performance since 2020, driven by a significant rally in semiconductor stocks.

### Key Points
- The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed above 52,000, while the Nasdaq Composite gained 1.5% on Tuesday.
- The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index recorded its strongest quarter in history, fueling broader market gains.
- Brent crude futures fell below $74 per barrel as markets shifted from supply shortage concerns to potential oil gluts.
- The U.S. dollar reached a 40-year high against the yen, raising concerns regarding potential currency intervention by Japan.
- May JOLTS data showed better-than-expected job openings, influencing investor expectations for future Federal Reserve interest rate policy.

### Why it Matters
These market gains reflect strong investor confidence despite ongoing concerns regarding currency volatility and potential Federal Reserve policy tightening. The shift in energy markets and labor data provides critical signals for the economic outlook as investors prepare for upcoming employment reports.
