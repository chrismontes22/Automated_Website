AI cryptomining network's 320,000 RTX 3090-class GPUs allegedly burn 112 megawatts of power on ‘zero useful AI computation’ — GPU rental costs jump 38%, but Pearl’s cards are doing random matrix math, study claims
============================================================

A new research preprint suggests the Pearl blockchain’s "Proof-of-Useful-Work" system generates no actual artificial intelligence computation, despite marketing itself as a sustainable alternative to traditional cryptocurrency mining.

### Key Points
- Researchers estimate the Pearl network consumes 112 megawatts of power while performing random matrix multiplications that lack real-world AI training or inference utility.
- The study found that Pearl mining software drove a 38% increase in budget GPU rental prices on the marketplace vast.ai, costing independent researchers an estimated $600,000 annually.
- Analysis of 8,012 mining workers revealed that the dominant mining binary contains no identifiable machine-learning framework code.
- The protocol’s verification process confirms that matrix math was performed correctly but fails to verify if the input data originated from a legitimate AI workload.
- The research successfully demonstrated that random, non-AI data can earn mining rewards just as effectively as genuine computational tasks.

### Why it Matters
The findings highlight a significant "usefulness gap" in blockchain projects that claim to repurpose mining hardware for productive AI tasks. This discrepancy suggests that such networks may function as traditional, energy-intensive cryptomining operations rather than viable distributed computing platforms, potentially inflating hardware costs for legitimate AI researchers.
