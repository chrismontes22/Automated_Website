Google's Nest Hub has no clue what time it is, and it's messing with our heads
============================================================

Google Nest Hub Gen 2 users are reporting a software bug where the device displays the correct alarm time but verbally confirms the incorrect AM or PM setting.

### Key Points
- Users report the Nest Hub Gen 2 incorrectly announces alarm times, such as stating 3 am when 3 pm is selected.
- The device display correctly registers the intended alarm time, indicating the issue is limited to the AI speech output.
- Google has acknowledged the bug and confirmed that a fix is currently in development.
- The issue appears isolated to the Nest Hub Gen 2, as other devices like the Nest Hub Mini remain unaffected.
- This report follows Google's recent integration of Gemini AI features into its smart home ecosystem.

### Why it Matters
Reliable alarm functionality is a core expectation for smart home displays, and this discrepancy could lead to significant user inconvenience or missed appointments. As Google continues to integrate Gemini into its hardware, maintaining consistent performance across voice and visual interfaces is critical for user trust.
