Trump wants a deadlocked Congress to move on AI. Frustrated states say they already have
============================================================

The Trump administration is pushing for a unified federal artificial intelligence framework while actively discouraging state-level regulations that officials argue create a burdensome patchwork for technology companies.

**Key points**
- The White House is urging Congress to adopt a national AI regulatory framework to ensure consistency and foster innovation across the technology sector.
- Several Republican state lawmakers, including those in Utah and Pennsylvania, are bypassing federal delays to pass local laws addressing child safety and AI transparency.
- The administration has reportedly intervened in state legislative processes, including sending a memo to block a proposed transparency bill in Utah.
- Critics, including the Alliance for Secure AI, argue the White House framework lacks sufficient detail on critical issues like job displacement and corporate accountability.
- Public polling from Vanderbilt University indicates that a majority of voters, including many Republicans, support stricter regulation of artificial intelligence.

**Why it matters: **
The conflict highlights a growing tension between federal efforts to streamline tech policy and state-level attempts to address immediate consumer safety concerns. This power struggle will determine whether the U.S. adopts a centralized regulatory approach or continues to rely on a fragmented landscape of state-specific rules.
