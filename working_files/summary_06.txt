Top 10 richest people in the world: Elon Musk loses $22 billion, Jeff Bezos overtakes Sergey Brin
============================================================

The world's ten wealthiest individuals saw their combined fortunes drop by over $100 billion in March 2026 as global market volatility impacted major stock indices and billionaire net worths.

### Key Points
- The combined net worth of the world's top 10 billionaires fell to $2.5 trillion as of April 1, 2026.
- Elon Musk remains the world's richest person with $817 billion, despite a monthly decline of $22 billion.
- Bernard Arnault experienced the largest loss, with his fortune dropping $28 billion to $142.5 billion.
- Michael Dell and Rob Walton entered the top 10 rankings, while Warren Buffett fell out of the list.
- Market instability, driven by the conflict in West Asia, caused the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to decline by nearly 5 percent.

### Why it Matters
The significant decline in billionaire wealth highlights how geopolitical instability and market downturns directly impact the world's largest private fortunes. This volatility underscores the sensitivity of high-net-worth portfolios to broader economic shifts and global security concerns.
