HomePod Mini Is Now 2,000 Days Old
============================================================

Apple’s HomePod mini has reached a 2,000-day milestone without a hardware refresh, marking an unusually long product lifespan for the company’s popular $99 compact smart speaker.

### Key Points
- The HomePod mini launched on October 13, 2020, featuring the Apple S5 chip and a spherical design for 360-degree audio.
- Apple expanded the device's color options in 2021, but the internal hardware specifications have remained unchanged since the initial release.
- The speaker serves as a smart home hub supporting Thread networking, Ultra Wideband technology, and Siri voice integration.
- Reports suggest a second-generation model is currently on hold pending the release of a revamped, more personalized version of Siri.
- The updated Siri assistant is expected to debut with iOS 18, with a public release anticipated for September.

### Why it Matters
The extended lifecycle of the HomePod mini highlights Apple's strategic shift toward prioritizing software-driven AI improvements over frequent hardware iterations. This delay suggests that future smart home devices will rely heavily on the upcoming, more capable version of Siri to remain competitive in the evolving home automation market.
