Battery maker ProLogium to list on Nasdaq in $3.8 billion SPAC deal
============================================================

Taiwanese battery manufacturer ProLogium Technology plans to go public on the Nasdaq through a $3.8 billion merger with the special purpose acquisition company Translational Development Acquisition Corp.

### Key Points
- The merger will provide capital to scale production of fourth-generation solid-state batteries and fund a new gigafactory in Dunkirk, France.
- Construction of the French facility is scheduled to begin in 2026, with mass production and initial deliveries targeted for the second quarter of 2029.
- ProLogium, founded in 2006, has shipped over 2.4 million lithium ceramic battery cells to electric vehicle customers since 2013.
- The combined entity will trade under the ticker symbol "PRLG" following the expected deal closure in the second half of 2026.
- Proceeds from the transaction will support expansion into new sectors, including aerospace, robotics, defense, and data center infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
This deal provides ProLogium with the necessary capital to accelerate the commercialization of solid-state battery technology, which is widely viewed as a safer and more efficient alternative to current lithium-ion standards. By securing a public listing, the company gains a strategic foothold to compete in the global electric vehicle supply chain and emerging industrial energy markets.
