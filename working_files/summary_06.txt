iPhone 18 Pro's Camera Upgrade Will Cost Apple 50% More
============================================================

Apple will integrate a new variable aperture lens into the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max, increasing camera component costs by 50% compared to current iPhone 17 Pro models.

### Key Points
- The variable aperture lens allows physical adjustments to light intake, improving exposure control and depth of field flexibility.
- Supply chain analyst Ming-Chi Kuo reports that Sunny Optical will supply 40% to 50% of these new camera module orders.
- Apple doubled its 2026 shipment forecast for the MacBook Neo to 10 million units following stronger-than-expected market performance.
- Future 2028 iPhone models are expected to adopt chip-on-board packaging for ultra-wide cameras to improve space efficiency and image quality.
- Sunny Optical has secured component contracts for two upcoming OpenAI hardware products, including a new smartphone and a mobile device.

### Why it Matters
The shift to variable aperture technology represents a significant hardware investment that could differentiate future iPhone Pro models in a competitive smartphone market. This supply chain expansion also highlights Sunny Optical's growing influence as a key manufacturing partner for both Apple’s hardware ecosystem and emerging AI-focused devices.
