Your Electronics Are About to Get Way More Expensive
============================================================

Apple, Microsoft, and other major tech companies are raising consumer prices as a global memory chip shortage, driven by AI demand and supply chain disruptions, persists through 2027.

### Key Points
- Apple and Microsoft have recently increased prices on laptops, tablets, and gaming consoles due to rising component costs.
- Global memory chip shortages are being exacerbated by high demand from AI data centers operated by companies like Google and OpenAI.
- Wartime disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz are limiting essential raw materials for semiconductor manufacturing in South Korea, Taiwan, and Japan.
- Micron executives and TSMC leadership project that the current supply crunch for high-value memory processors will continue until at least 2027.
- While consumer tech companies face inventory and pricing pressures, chipmakers like Micron and SanDisk are reporting significant revenue gains.

### Why it Matters
The ongoing semiconductor scarcity forces major electronics manufacturers to pass increased production costs directly to consumers, signaling a long-term trend of higher prices for personal devices. This shift highlights the growing tension between the massive resource requirements of AI development and the availability of hardware for the broader consumer market.
