Glass Jaw? The New Economic Fragility Recasting American Power
============================================================

Broadly applied U.S. tariffs have eroded the economic system's ability to absorb global shocks, leaving American businesses and national security strategies increasingly vulnerable to compounding crises like rising oil prices.

### Key Points
- Broad tariffs have forced companies to exhaust margins and reduce product quality, eliminating the financial buffers needed to navigate sudden supply chain disruptions.
- The 2026 Iran conflict triggered an oil price surge that, when layered onto existing tariff-induced fragility, has caused rapid, widespread inflation for consumers.
- Footwear industry data shows that 70 percent of a sneaker's cost is exposed to oil price fluctuations, illustrating how energy costs now permeate the entire supply chain.
- A 2025 KPMG survey found 43 percent of consumer goods executives reported significant declines in gross margins as they struggled to absorb rising input costs.
- The Trump administration’s reliance on Section 122 and potential Section 301 tariffs continues to create an unpredictable trade environment that discourages long-term business investment.

### Why it Matters
The loss of economic resilience acts as a hard ceiling on the United States' ability to sustain military and diplomatic coercion, as domestic financial stress limits political options. Adversaries like China are observing these vulnerabilities to calibrate their own strategies, potentially viewing a financially constrained America as less capable of responding to global geopolitical challenges.
