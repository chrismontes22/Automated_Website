Crypto edges off of worst levels after Trump extends Iran strike pause
============================================================

Bitcoin prices stabilized after President Donald Trump announced a 10-day pause on potential U.S. strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure, easing broader market volatility caused by rising geopolitical tensions.

**Key points**
- Bitcoin recovered approximately 1% to trade above $69,000 following the announcement of the 10-day diplomatic pause.
- Markets experienced significant volatility on Thursday, with the Nasdaq falling 2.4% and Bitcoin dropping more than 3%.
- The U.S. 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.41%, reflecting investor concerns over potential Federal Reserve interest rate hikes.
- Rising hostilities in the Middle East have triggered a surge in global oil prices and a widespread selloff in Western bond markets.

**Why it matters: **
The stabilization of crypto and equity markets highlights how sensitive global financial assets remain to geopolitical developments and shifting interest rate expectations. Investors are closely monitoring these fluctuations as rising bond yields and energy costs continue to challenge broader economic stability.
