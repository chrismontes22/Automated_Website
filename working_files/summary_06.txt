Mozilla Accuses Microsoft of Sabotaging Firefox With Windows and Copilot Tactics
============================================================

Mozilla has accused Microsoft of unfairly prioritizing the Edge browser and Copilot AI within Windows, claiming these design choices undermine user choice and hinder competition for independent browsers.

### Key Points
- Mozilla alleges that Windows forces links from the taskbar, Outlook, and Teams to open in Edge, ignoring user-selected default browser settings.
- The company argues that Microsoft leverages its dominant operating system to steer users toward its own ecosystem rather than allowing fair competition.
- Microsoft’s integration of Copilot, including taskbar pinning and a dedicated keyboard key, is cited as an example of aggressive platform-level promotion.
- Mozilla contends that these system-level design decisions make it increasingly difficult for Firefox to maintain its market share against Microsoft’s integrated services.

### Why it Matters
These allegations highlight ongoing regulatory concerns regarding how dominant platform owners leverage their operating systems to favor their own software products. If proven, these practices could face increased scrutiny from antitrust regulators focused on maintaining fair competition in the browser and artificial intelligence markets.
