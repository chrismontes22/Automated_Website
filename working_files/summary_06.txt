Microsoft could be the next Big Tech antitrust target
============================================================

The Federal Trade Commission is investigating Microsoft over potential antitrust violations, focusing on whether the company uses exclusionary practices within its Azure cloud services and artificial intelligence sectors.

### Key Points
- The FTC issued civil investigative demands to multiple competitors to examine Microsoft’s licensing, bundling, and interoperability practices.
- Investigators are specifically probing whether Microsoft leverages its market dominance to gain an unfair advantage in cloud computing and AI.
- Microsoft maintains that its business practices promote competition and is cooperating fully with the ongoing federal inquiry.
- Similar antitrust investigations into Microsoft’s cloud services are currently underway in the European Union, the United Kingdom, and Japan.
- The probe follows years of complaints from rivals regarding 2019 licensing changes that increased costs for running Windows on non-Azure infrastructure.

### Why it Matters
This investigation signals a potential shift in regulatory scrutiny toward Microsoft, which has largely avoided the aggressive antitrust litigation faced by other major technology firms. A formal legal challenge could significantly alter the competitive landscape for cloud computing and AI, potentially forcing changes to how the company bundles its software and services.
