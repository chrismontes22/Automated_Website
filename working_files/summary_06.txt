Apple overtakes Nvidia to reclaim the title of world’s most valuable company at $4.88 trillion
============================================================

Apple has reclaimed its position as the world’s most valuable company with a $4.88 trillion market capitalization, surpassing Nvidia following a 3.5% decline in the chipmaker's share price.

### Key Points
- Apple reached a $4.88 trillion valuation, overtaking Nvidia’s $4.86 trillion market cap on Friday.
- Nvidia had previously held the top spot for nearly a year after becoming the first company to exceed $5 trillion in October.
- The semiconductor index has declined approximately 19% from its peak as investor interest shifts toward memory chipmakers like Micron and SK Hynix.
- Apple’s recent commercial success is attributed to its strategy of integrating third-party AI models rather than developing proprietary ones.
- CEO Tim Cook is scheduled to transition his leadership role to hardware veteran John Ternus this September.

### Why it Matters
This shift indicates that investors are diversifying their portfolios beyond traditional AI infrastructure providers toward a broader range of technology and memory hardware companies. While Nvidia remains central to AI development, the market is increasingly rewarding companies that successfully integrate AI tools into consumer-facing products.
