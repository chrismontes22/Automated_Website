AI Will Be Met with Violence, and Nothing Good Will Come of It
============================================================

Rising public frustration over the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence is fueling a concerning trend of targeted violence against industry leaders, infrastructure, and local government officials.

### Key Points
- Recent incidents include a Molotov cocktail attack on Sam Altman’s home and gunfire directed at an Indianapolis councilman supporting a datacenter project.
- Anti-AI activists have increasingly targeted physical infrastructure, such as datacenters, and threatened employees at major firms like OpenAI.
- Experts suggest that public anxiety stems from fears of mass job displacement and the perceived lack of a safe transition for the workforce.
- The AI industry’s frequent public discourse regarding the disruptive and potentially dangerous nature of its own technology may be exacerbating public hostility.
- Historical parallels are being drawn to the 19th-century Luddite movement, where workers destroyed machinery to protest economic displacement.

### Why it Matters
The escalation of physical violence against individuals and infrastructure signals a dangerous shift in the public’s reaction to technological disruption. If the AI industry fails to address the economic anxieties of those who feel excluded from the future, these isolated incidents could evolve into broader social instability.
