This forgotten Sony laptop brand is still making PCs in 2026
============================================================

Although Sony divested its PC division in 2014, the Vaio brand continues to operate globally under new ownership, offering a streamlined lineup of professional-grade Windows 11 laptops today.

### Key Points
- Sony sold the Vaio PC business to Japan Industrial Partners in 2014, retaining a minority stake in the brand.
- In 2025, consumer electronics conglomerate Nojima acquired a 91.4 percent stake in Vaio from the private equity firm.
- Current Vaio offerings include the FS series, featuring Intel Core Ultra processors, aluminum chassis, and Windows 11 Home.
- The brand maintains a presence in several international markets, including the U.S., China, India, and various countries across Latin America.
- Historically, Vaio pioneered mainstream features like built-in webcams, chiclet keyboards, and Blu-ray disc burners in portable computers.

### Why it Matters
The survival of the Vaio brand highlights the enduring legacy of Japanese hardware engineering in a market dominated by larger, trend-focused competitors. By shifting to a niche, functional business model, the company demonstrates how legacy brands can maintain relevance through specialized manufacturing and targeted distribution.
