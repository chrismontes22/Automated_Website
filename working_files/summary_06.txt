From page views to propensity: How the Daily Mail is retooling for a zero-click world
============================================================

The Daily Mail is shifting its digital strategy from high-volume page views to a loyalty-based model, prioritizing repeat engagement and subscription growth to counter declining referral traffic.

### Key Points
- The publisher is replacing page-view metrics with a "golden metric" that tracks time spent, repeat visits, and active user engagement.
- A new AI-powered dynamic paywall is being tested on 50% of the audience to optimize subscription conversions based on user behavior and content topics.
- The Mail aims to grow its Mail+ subscription base from 400,000 to one million subscribers by 2028.
- To foster daily habits, the company is integrating games and niche content hubs, such as a new Crime Desk, directly into its app navigation.
- The publisher is migrating from a .co.uk to a .com domain to better support its global operations and advertising strategy.
- Internal AI tools, including a platform called Mail IQ, are being used to automate administrative tasks and accelerate product prototyping.

### Why it Matters
This strategic pivot reflects a broader industry trend where publishers are moving away from reliance on search-driven traffic toward owned, direct-audience relationships. By prioritizing habit-forming products and subscription revenue, the Daily Mail is attempting to insulate its business model from the volatility of AI-driven search and declining referral traffic.
