IBM Home Director: Home Automation in 1996
============================================================

IBM’s 1990s Home Director system utilized the X10 powerline communication protocol to provide early smart home automation capabilities controlled directly through the company’s Aptiva personal computer series.

### Key Points
- IBM Home Director allowed users to automate home devices by plugging modules into standard electrical outlets.
- The system relied on the X10 protocol, which transmitted control signals over existing household power lines without requiring additional wiring.
- Users managed the automation configuration by connecting a control module to an IBM Aptiva PC via a serial port.
- Professional installation services were available for more complex setups beyond the standard consumer-grade X10 hardware.
- The system remains a notable example of early consumer-facing smart home technology that prioritized ease of use for Windows owners.

### Why it Matters
This retrospective highlights the foundational role IBM played in early home automation before the widespread adoption of modern wireless smart home standards. Understanding these legacy powerline systems provides context for the evolution of consumer connectivity and the persistent challenges associated with signal reliability in home networking.
