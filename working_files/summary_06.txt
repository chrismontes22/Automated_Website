Bipartisan lawmakers reintroduce bill to limit Big Tech gatekeeping, Apple shoots back
============================================================

A bipartisan group of U.S. senators has reintroduced the American Innovation and Choice Online Act, a legislative proposal aimed at curbing the market power of major digital technology platforms.

### Key Points
- Senators Chuck Grassley and Amy Klobuchar reintroduced the bill to prevent large platforms from favoring their own products or misusing business-user data.
- The legislation applies to companies with at least $175 billion in annual revenue and a reach of 34% of U.S. households or monthly active users.
- Supporters include Mozilla, Yelp, DuckDuckGo, and Proton, who argue the bill will restore online competition and lower prices for consumers.
- Apple issued a formal rebuttal, claiming the bill mirrors European regulations that could compromise user privacy, security, and child safety protections.
- Apple cited internal studies suggesting that similar regulations in Europe failed to lower prices for consumers while creating new security vulnerabilities.

### Why it Matters
This legislation represents a significant ongoing effort by federal lawmakers to impose stricter antitrust oversight on the world's largest technology companies. If passed, the bill would fundamentally alter how Big Tech firms manage their platforms and interact with third-party developers and competitors.
