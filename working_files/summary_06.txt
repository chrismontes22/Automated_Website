This open-source app lets you use your Whoop without a monthly fee
============================================================

An independent developer has released an unofficial, open-source application that allows Whoop fitness tracker users to access their health data locally without maintaining a paid monthly subscription.

### Key Points
- The open-source app enables users to view metrics like heart rate, sleep stages, recovery scores, and strain without an active Whoop subscription.
- The software utilizes a local-first approach, syncing data directly from the wearable device to Android or macOS devices via Bluetooth without cloud processing.
- Users can import existing health information from Apple Health and previously collected Whoop data into the unofficial interface.
- The application is currently available via GitHub, though it requires sideloading on Android devices and is not officially supported by Whoop.
- Whoop’s terms of service reportedly prohibit the use of third-party applications to access device data.

### Why it Matters
This project challenges the subscription-based business model that has long defined the Whoop user experience by turning otherwise inactive hardware into functional devices. It highlights growing consumer pushback against mandatory recurring fees for accessing personal health data collected by wearable technology.
