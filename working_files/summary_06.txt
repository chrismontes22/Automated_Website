AI is killing the cheap smartphone
============================================================

The global surge in demand for high-bandwidth memory used in artificial intelligence is causing a severe supply shortage, leading to significantly higher prices for smartphones and consumer electronics.

### Key Points
- AI-driven demand for high-bandwidth memory (HBM) has forced manufacturers like Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron to reallocate production capacity away from commodity DRAM.
- Smartphone memory costs have spiked, with LPDDR4 and LPDDR5 prices increasing by over 200% between early 2025 and 2026.
- Budget smartphone manufacturers, including Transsion and Xiaomi, have reported sharp declines in shipments as devices become unaffordable for price-sensitive consumers.
- Major tech companies, including Apple and Dell, are facing increased component costs, leading to product delays and potential price hikes for premium devices.
- Micron has exited the consumer DRAM market entirely to prioritize enterprise and AI-focused hardware production.

### Why it Matters
The era of increasingly affordable consumer electronics is ending as AI infrastructure competes directly with personal devices for limited memory production capacity. This shift threatens to reverse decades of digital inclusion by pricing out low-income users and forcing a broad, inflationary repricing of technology across global markets.
