Elon Musk loses trillionaire status as global tech rout hits SpaceX
============================================================

Elon Musk has lost his trillionaire status after a sharp decline in SpaceX and Tesla share prices triggered by a broader market sell-off in the technology sector.

### Key Points
- Elon Musk’s net worth fell to $957 billion on Tuesday, down from a peak of $1.32 trillion reached on June 16.
- SpaceX shares dropped more than 30% from their mid-June peak, while Tesla shares declined by nearly 6% this week.
- The market correction was driven by investor concerns regarding artificial intelligence infrastructure costs, capital spending, and persistent interest rates.
- Musk’s wealth remains highly concentrated, with approximately 80% of his net worth tied directly to his equity in SpaceX.
- Insider share-selling restrictions for SpaceX are scheduled to begin lifting in late July, potentially creating further market pressure.

### Why it Matters
This volatility highlights the risks associated with wealth concentrated in high-growth technology stocks that are sensitive to market sentiment and AI-related speculation. While Musk remains the world's wealthiest individual, his fluctuating status underscores the intense market scrutiny facing companies with massive valuations following recent public debuts.
