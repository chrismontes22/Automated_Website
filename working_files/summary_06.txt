AI Selloff a ‘Wake-Up Call’ for Investors, Wells Fargo Says
============================================================

Wells Fargo analysts warn that the recent technology-driven market selloff signals an end to the rapid artificial intelligence stock rally, suggesting investors should prepare for a slower growth period.

### Key Points
- Wells Fargo analyst Ohsung Kwon described the recent market decline as a positioning-led correction rather than a fundamental shift in the economy.
- The Nasdaq 100 and S&P 500 indices experienced significant drops as investors reassessed the risks associated with heavy capital expenditure in artificial intelligence.
- JPMorgan Chase has adopted a tactically cautious stance, while Bank of America identified emerging bear market indicators that suggest a potential peak in stock prices.
- Future market stability depends on whether hyperscalers can generate adequate returns on their massive debt-fueled investments in AI infrastructure.
- Analysts expect the supply-demand imbalance in the semiconductor sector to normalize as production capacity doubles annually over the next five years.

### Why it Matters
This shift in sentiment highlights growing investor skepticism regarding the immediate profitability of expensive artificial intelligence infrastructure projects. If tech companies fail to demonstrate clear returns on their capital spending, the broader market rally could face sustained pressure and increased volatility.
