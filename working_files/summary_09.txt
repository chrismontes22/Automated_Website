What Sky buying ITV could mean for your favourite shows
============================================================

Sky, owned by Comcast, has announced a £1.6bn deal to acquire ITV’s television and streaming business, aiming to create a powerful commercial competitor to global streaming giants in Britain.

### Key Points
- Sky will acquire ITV’s broadcast channels and streaming platform, ITVX, while ITV Studios will remain a separate entity owned by current shareholders.
- ITV is legally required to maintain free-to-air public service broadcasting until at least 2034, ensuring current shows remain accessible without a paywall.
- The deal includes a supply agreement ensuring ITV Studios continues producing content for the network, including popular shows like Coronation Street and Love Island.
- Sky intends to leverage ITV’s massive audience reach and public service prominence to bolster its own streaming and sports broadcasting capabilities.
- ITN’s contract to produce ITV news remains in effect until 2031, though the long-term future of the partnership remains subject to future corporate strategy.

### Why it Matters
This acquisition represents a significant consolidation of the British media landscape, positioning a US-owned entity as a dominant force in both free-to-air and subscription television. By integrating ITV’s reach with Sky’s technical infrastructure, the deal aims to create a formidable rival to international streamers like Netflix and Disney Plus.
