Amazon Web Services CEO says half of white-collar jobs may 'change' due to AI — but it won't be a 'wipe-out'
============================================================

Amazon Web Services CEO Matt Garman argues that artificial intelligence will transform white-collar roles rather than eliminate them, emphasizing that adaptability and continuous learning will drive future workforce growth.

### Key Points
- Amazon Web Services CEO Matt Garman stated that while AI will change half of white-collar jobs, it will not result in mass unemployment.
- Garman compared the impact of AI to the introduction of Microsoft Excel, which reshaped professional tasks rather than removing the need for human workers.
- Amazon is currently hiring over 11,000 software development interns and early-career engineers globally to foster new ideas and company culture.
- The company is increasingly prioritizing a candidate's ability to learn new skills over their existing technical expertise.
- Employees are encouraged to prepare for roles that will look significantly different in two years compared to their current responsibilities.

### Why it Matters
This perspective challenges the prevailing narrative that AI will trigger widespread job displacement across the technology sector. By prioritizing adaptability, companies like Amazon are signaling a shift in hiring strategies that emphasizes long-term professional evolution over static skill sets.
