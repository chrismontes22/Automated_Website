The $42 billion auto giant you've never heard of is building AI into its factories
============================================================

Automotive supplier Magna is integrating artificial intelligence across its 330 global manufacturing plants to optimize production efficiency, monitor product quality, and predict potential supply chain disruptions for major automakers.

### Key Points
- Magna supplies automotive components to 59 global manufacturers, including Ford, Toyota, Hyundai, BYD, and Volkswagen.
- The company is deploying AI-powered vision systems to detect manufacturing defects and irregularities in real time.
- Machine learning algorithms monitor factory equipment metrics like vibration and temperature to predict and prevent costly mechanical failures.
- Autonomous mobile robots are being utilized within facilities to transport heavy materials between various workstations.
- AI tools are tracking energy consumption, water usage, and industrial waste to identify cost-saving opportunities across global operations.

### Why it Matters
Magna’s integration of AI demonstrates how legacy industrial firms are leveraging software to modernize complex, large-scale manufacturing operations. By embedding intelligence into physical processes, the company aims to improve operational resilience and supply chain visibility in an increasingly volatile global automotive market.
