13 legal startups to watch in 2026, according to investors
============================================================

Artificial intelligence is transforming the legal industry as venture capitalists pour billions into startups designed to automate complex attorney tasks, streamline litigation, and improve access to legal services.

### Key Points
- Global investment in legal technology startups reached $2.1 billion during the first half of the year as AI models accelerated software adoption.
- Companies like Atticus and Manifest OS are scaling by automating legal aid for individuals and streamlining back-office operations for law firms.
- Specialized platforms including Patlytics and Solve Intelligence are targeting high-stakes patent law, while tools like Wexler and Courtroom assist with litigation and evidence review.
- In-house legal teams are increasingly adopting efficiency-focused software from providers like GC AI and Sandstone to manage rising workloads without increasing headcount.
- Startups such as Crosby and Soxton are blending AI software with human legal expertise to provide affordable, scalable services for early-stage companies.

### Why it Matters
The rapid integration of AI into legal workflows is collapsing traditional sales cycles and forcing a shift in how law firms and corporate departments bill for their time. By automating routine tasks and document analysis, these startups are creating new competitive advantages that prioritize efficiency and data-driven decision-making over traditional hourly billing models.
