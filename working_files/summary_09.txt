South Korea’s chip worker bonuses are so large the central bank is treating them as an inflation risk
============================================================

South Korean semiconductor giants Samsung and SK Hynix face scrutiny as massive AI-driven profit bonuses for employees spark concerns over national inflation and rising real estate prices.

### Key Points
- Samsung and SK Hynix employees may receive individual performance bonuses exceeding $400,000 due to record-breaking demand for AI-focused high-bandwidth memory chips.
- The combined bonus pool for both companies is projected to reach 30 trillion won by 2028, up from 4 trillion won this year.
- Unions at other major firms, including Hyundai Motor and HD Hyundai Heavy Industries, are now demanding similar profit-sharing agreements, creating a "wage domino" effect.
- The Bank of Korea warns that these payouts could drive inflation above its 2 percent target and significantly inflate property values in the semiconductor belt.
- Central bank officials are considering interest rate hikes to mitigate the economic impact of this sudden influx of wealth into the domestic market.

### Why it Matters
The rapid accumulation of wealth within the semiconductor sector threatens to destabilize South Korea's broader economy by fueling inflation and housing speculation. This situation forces the Bank of Korea to weigh the success of its most vital industry against the necessity of maintaining national price stability.
