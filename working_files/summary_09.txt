The quantum clock is ticking: it's Bitcoin's problem, not Ethereum's
============================================================

Citi analysts and Google Quantum AI researchers warn that Bitcoin faces significant quantum computing risks due to its slow governance and reliance on vulnerable elliptic curve digital signature algorithms.

### Key Points
- Google Quantum AI and Stanford researchers estimate that a quantum computer with fewer than 500,000 physical qubits could compromise Bitcoin’s private keys in nine minutes.
- Citi analysts highlighted that Bitcoin’s conservative, consensus-driven governance model makes implementing necessary post-quantum security upgrades significantly slower than Ethereum’s.
- Ethereum has already begun integrating post-quantum standards, utilizing its account abstraction architecture to allow for voluntary, account-level security transitions.
- Industry experts, including Nic Carter, estimate that approximately 6.9 million BTC could be vulnerable to quantum-scale attacks as early as 2028.
- Global regulatory bodies, including U.S. federal agencies and the G7, have established strict deadlines for transitioning critical infrastructure to post-quantum cryptography.

### Why it Matters
The disparity in quantum readiness between Bitcoin and Ethereum creates a significant long-term risk profile for institutional investors and corporate treasurers holding digital assets. As regulatory compliance requirements evolve, the ability of a blockchain to adapt its foundational security architecture will likely become a primary factor in determining its institutional viability.
