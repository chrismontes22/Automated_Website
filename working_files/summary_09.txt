It took a while, but Google's finally fixing a SiriusXM radio issue on its smart speakers
============================================================

Google is rolling out a fix in late June to resolve a month-long bug that prevented users from streaming SiriusXM radio content on various Google Home smart speakers.

### Key Points
- Google confirmed a resolution for the streaming error that previously triggered playback failure messages on smart speakers.
- The technical issue persisted for approximately one month before Google officially acknowledged and addressed the service disruption.
- Google recently launched the $99 Home Speaker, the company's first device designed specifically to integrate with the Gemini AI assistant.
- Gemini for Home has expanded to more international markets with a 40% improvement in response times and better device-command accuracy.
- Google is opening the Gemini for Home platform to third-party hardware manufacturers and carriers to increase native AI support across devices.

### Why it Matters
This fix restores core functionality for existing smart home users who rely on Google devices for satellite radio streaming. Furthermore, the expansion of Gemini to third-party hardware signals a strategic shift toward making Google's AI the standard interface for future smart home ecosystems.
