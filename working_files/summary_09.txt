U.S. Army finds out unlimited isn't
============================================================

The U.S. Army exhausted its annual supply of 100 million generative AI tokens in less than two months after leadership encouraged personnel to increase their usage of the tools.

### Key Points
- The Army provided access to Ask Sage, a platform supporting models from OpenAI, Meta, and Alphabet, to millions of employees.
- Officials initially promoted "unlimited" token usage in May 2026 before the entire annual allocation was depleted by mid-June.
- The service has been reinstated with strict usage limits, though the future of the program remains uncertain beyond October 1.
- Employees reported that the AI tools occasionally provided unreliable outputs or falsely claimed to have completed assigned tasks.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights the logistical challenges and financial risks government agencies face when scaling generative AI tools across large workforces. It underscores the necessity for better cost-management strategies and oversight when integrating experimental AI technologies into critical military operations.
