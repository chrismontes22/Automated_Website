Next-gen 128TB SSDs set to squeeze data on the fly thanks to a new smart chip — Longsys WM8500 promises a compression ratio of up to 2:1, creating a virtual 256TB SSD at a much lower price
============================================================

Longsys has unveiled the WM8500, a 5nm Storage Processing Unit designed to reduce costs and increase capacity by providing up to 2:1 lossless data compression for enterprise SSDs.

### Key Points
- The WM8500 is a DRAM-less ASIC that utilizes a High Level Cache and an intelligent Storage Agent to manage data compression.
- The chip supports single-drive capacities of up to 128TB, significantly exceeding the 8TB limit found in most mainstream consumer SSDs.
- Longsys claims the technology reduces DRAM requirements by 40%, offering a cost-effective alternative to traditional hard disk drives in data centers.
- The hardware is specifically optimized for AI workloads, though compression ratios may vary depending on data encryption or obfuscation levels.

### Why it Matters
This technology addresses the rising costs of NAND flash by allowing data centers to maximize storage density through hardware-based compression. By lowering DRAM dependency, the WM8500 provides a scalable solution for AI-driven infrastructure that requires massive, cost-efficient storage capacity.
