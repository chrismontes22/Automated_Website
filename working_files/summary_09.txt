Tribit QuietPlus 81 hands-on: Is $50 a fair price for these over-ear headphones?
============================================================

The Tribit QuietPlus 81 over-ear headphones offer impressive battery life and fast charging, but suffer from inconsistent sound quality and unintuitive controls that hinder the overall user experience.

### Key Points
- The QuietPlus 81 headphones launched in December 2025 with a retail price of $49.99.
- Key features include 40mm dynamic drivers, hybrid active noise cancellation, and Bluetooth 6.0 connectivity.
- Battery performance is a standout, offering up to 110 hours of playback with ANC disabled.
- The device includes a touch-sensitive area for transparency mode and supports multipoint connections for two devices.
- Physical controls are criticized for being confusing, lacking dedicated playback buttons, and requiring non-standard volume adjustments.
- Sound quality is described as murky and overly bass-heavy, failing to compete with similarly priced models from Sony, JBL, and Edifier.

### Why it Matters
The budget wireless headphone market is highly competitive, with established brands offering superior audio fidelity and more refined hardware at the same $50 price point. These headphones demonstrate that strong technical specifications on paper do not always translate into a high-quality, user-friendly product in real-world scenarios.
