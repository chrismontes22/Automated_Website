Security Bite: A note on camera covers in 2026
============================================================

Apple advises against using sliding webcam covers on MacBooks because they can cause permanent display damage while providing no meaningful security benefits for modern hardware with integrated privacy indicators.

### Key Points
- Apple warns that closing a MacBook with a webcam cover thicker than 0.1 mm can crack the display due to tight hinge tolerances.
- Modern Macs feature a hardware-level circuit that forces the green privacy LED to illuminate whenever the camera sensor receives power.
- The hardware-linked indicator design, implemented in all Macs since late 2008, prevents malware from activating the camera without alerting the user.
- Security researcher Patrick Wardle developed the OverSight tool to provide additional alerts whenever the webcam is actively in use.
- Using webcam covers can interfere with ambient light sensors, potentially causing features like True Tone to malfunction.

### Why it Matters
These findings highlight that physical privacy accessories often pose a greater risk to hardware integrity than they do to digital security. Users should rely on built-in macOS security features and software monitoring tools rather than potentially damaging physical modifications.
