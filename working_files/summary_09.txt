Does Your AI Agent Need a VPN? The Company Behind Norton and Avast Thinks So
============================================================

Gen Digital has launched a new VPN service for autonomous AI agents, allowing users to secure internet traffic for LLMs like ChatGPT and OpenClaw through the Agent Trust Hub.

### Key Points
- Gen Digital’s new VPN for Agents is powered by Norton VPN and requires no client downloads or software installation.
- The service supports multiple concurrent AI agents using advanced tunnel technology to route traffic securely.
- Users can mask their IP addresses, bypass regional content restrictions, and prevent internet service providers from tracking AI-driven activity.
- The integration protects users from potential security challenges or blocklists triggered by autonomous agent behavior on the web.
- Windscribe has also recently introduced support for the OpenClaw agent to provide similar privacy and security features.

### Why it Matters
This development addresses growing privacy concerns as autonomous AI agents increasingly access the internet without traditional security layers. By routing agent traffic through a VPN, users can protect their digital reputation and prevent their home networks from being flagged by security filters.
