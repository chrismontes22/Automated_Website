Google Publishes Tennessee Search “Blacklist” Guidance via @sejournal, @martinibuster
============================================================

Google has introduced new guidelines for Tennessee small businesses to comply with SB 2262, a state law granting companies legal recourse against search engine blacklisting and review removal.

### Key Points
- Tennessee law SB 2262 takes effect July 1, protecting businesses with 50 or fewer employees from search engine visibility reductions or review deletions of 25% or more.
- The legislation allows small businesses to demand a formal explanation and justification from search engines regarding adverse actions within five business days.
- Google’s new guidelines require Tennessee business owners to verify their websites via Search Console and claim Google Business Profiles to receive mandatory legal notifications.
- Businesses may initiate court actions against search engines if they believe they have been unfairly blacklisted or had their digital presence restricted.

### Why it Matters
This legislation establishes a new legal framework for transparency between major search platforms and local businesses, potentially setting a precedent for state-level regulation of big tech. By providing a clear path for appeal and accountability, the law aims to protect small enterprises from sudden, unexplained losses in digital visibility and reputation.
