GoldenEyeDog Subgroup Linked to DigiCert Breach and Code-Signing Certificate Theft
============================================================

Cybersecurity researchers have attributed the April 2026 DigiCert security breach to the Chinese threat group CylindricalCanine, which exploited support portals to steal and weaponize legitimate code-signing certificates.

### Key Points
- The threat actor CylindricalCanine, a sub-group of GoldenEyeDog, compromised two DigiCert support analyst workstations via malicious files sent through a customer chat channel.
- Attackers leveraged internal support portal functions to access initialization codes, allowing them to fraudulently obtain and issue 60 unauthorized code-signing certificates.
- DigiCert revoked the compromised certificates, 27 of which were used to digitally sign the "Zhong Stealer" malware to evade security detection.
- The group utilizes a modular remote access trojan known as Golden Gh0st RAT, which is capable of keystroke logging, data theft, and establishing SOCKS proxy tunnels.
- Attack chains typically involve DLL side-loading and decoy documents to mask the deployment of malicious payloads on target systems.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights a critical vulnerability in how certificate authorities manage internal support tools and sensitive initialization data. By weaponizing trusted certificates, attackers can bypass security software, posing a significant risk to the integrity of software supply chains globally.
