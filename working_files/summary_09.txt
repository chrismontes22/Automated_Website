This $70 Photoshop rival is now free, and I like it better than Photoshop
============================================================

Canva has launched a free, unified version of its Affinity creative suite, offering professional-grade photo editing, vector design, and page layout tools as a direct alternative to Adobe Photoshop.

### Key Points
- Canva acquired developer Serif in 2024 and released the unified "Affinity" app in late 2025.
- The software combines previous standalone tools—Affinity Photo, Designer, and Publisher—into one integrated workspace.
- The core professional toolkit is free for all users, requiring only a free Canva account for access.
- Generative AI features, such as background removal and Generative Fill, remain locked behind a $120-per-year Canva Premium subscription.
- Affinity is optimized for Apple Silicon, offering faster performance and lower memory usage compared to Adobe Photoshop on macOS.
- Over one million users registered for the new platform within the first four days of its launch.

### Why it Matters
This move disrupts the long-standing subscription model for professional creative software by providing a high-performance, no-cost alternative to Adobe’s industry-standard tools. It forces a shift in the market that challenges the necessity of monthly fees for independent creators while positioning Canva to capture a larger share of the professional design ecosystem.
