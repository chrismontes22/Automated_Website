Black Market Tinkerers on Facebook Marketplace Offer to Hide 'Recording Lights' on Meta Smartglasses
============================================================

Thousands of users are hiring third-party technicians to disable the recording indicator light on Meta’s Ray-Ban smartglasses, enabling surreptitious filming despite Meta’s efforts to remove these tampering services.

### Key Points
- Tech journalist Joanna Stern reported that individuals across at least 30 U.S. states are offering services to drill out the privacy-indicator light on Meta smartglasses.
- Meta sold 7 million units in 2025 and maintains that the majority of users do not tamper with their devices.
- The company actively removes Marketplace listings for tampering services and warns that such modifications violate terms of service and void product warranties.
- Users seeking to disable the light cite a desire to record in public without drawing attention or facing confrontation.
- Legal experts note that while the physical modification is not illegal, recording individuals without consent may violate state-specific privacy laws.
- New mobile applications have emerged that use Bluetooth scanning to detect nearby camera-equipped smartglasses as a countermeasure to surreptitious recording.

### Why it Matters
The rise of modified smartglasses complicates public privacy expectations and creates a growing tension between wearable technology adoption and individual consent. This trend forces a broader debate on the adequacy of current privacy safeguards and the potential for increased surveillance in everyday social environments.
