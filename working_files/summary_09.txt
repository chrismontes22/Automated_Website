In His “Radical Centralism” Essay, Tony Blair Is, As Always, Talking His Book
============================================================

Tony Blair’s recent essay advocating for radical economic deregulation and AI integration has sparked debate over his influence on the Keir Starmer government and ties to Oracle.

### Key Points
- Tony Blair’s essay calls for pension cuts, NHS privatization, and the abandonment of Net Zero climate commitments to prioritize cheap energy for AI data centers.
- Oracle founder Larry Ellison has invested over $348 million into the Tony Blair Institute for Global Change since 2021, raising concerns regarding corporate lobbying.
- The Starmer government has implemented "AI Growth Zones" that bypass standard environmental assessments and community planning regulations to accelerate data center construction.
- Blair’s policy recommendations include strengthening ties with the Trump administration and expanding the UK’s role in US-led military operations in the Middle East.
- Public opposition is mounting against government proposals for mandatory digital identity systems, which critics argue are designed for mass surveillance and control.

### Why it Matters
The alignment between Blair’s policy agenda and the commercial interests of his primary benefactor, Larry Ellison, highlights growing concerns over corporate influence on British public policy. This relationship suggests a shift toward a surveillance-heavy, deregulated economy that prioritizes AI infrastructure over traditional environmental and social safeguards.
