Pixel 11 Pro Fold FCC filing hints at Google's biggest modem upgrade yet
============================================================

A new FCC filing for the Pixel 11 Pro Fold indicates Google will replace Samsung’s Exynos modem with MediaTek’s M90, signaling a major shift in its hardware strategy.

### Key Points
- Google is transitioning away from Samsung modem technology in favor of the MediaTek M90 5G modem for the upcoming Pixel 11 series.
- The M90 modem supports download speeds up to 12Gbps, satellite connectivity, dual active 5G SIMs, and AI-enhanced power management.
- This hardware change follows reports that the Tensor G6 chip will move to TSMC’s 2nm manufacturing process.
- The shift aims to address long-standing user complaints regarding inconsistent cellular reception and battery drain associated with previous Samsung-based modems.

### Why it Matters
This transition represents a significant departure from Google's long-term reliance on Samsung for core mobile components. By integrating more efficient modem and chip technology, Google hopes to resolve persistent connectivity issues and improve battery performance across its future flagship smartphone lineup.
