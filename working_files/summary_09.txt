Xi in North Korea; Li Qiang on future industries; Cai Qi now Party School President; MSS on AI "transfer stations"; Updates to US "Chinese Military Companies" list
============================================================

Chinese President Xi Jinping is visiting North Korea to strengthen bilateral ties, while Beijing simultaneously updates domestic industrial policies, leadership roles, and security warnings regarding artificial intelligence technology.

### Key Points
- President Xi Jinping is in North Korea for his first visit in seven years, emphasizing strategic communication and the 65th anniversary of the bilateral Treaty of Friendship.
- The State Council is prioritizing "future industries" while warning against "blindly following trends" and urging increased investment in original, disruptive technologies.
- Cai Qi has replaced Chen Xi as President of the Central Party School, a move signaling his continued influence as a top advisor to Xi.
- The Ministry of State Security issued a warning against "AI transfer stations," citing risks of privacy leaks, unauthorized data exports, and malicious code.
- The U.S. Department of Defense updated its 1260H list of "Chinese military companies," adding major tech firms including Alibaba, Baidu, and BYD.

### Why it Matters
These developments highlight China's dual focus on reinforcing regional geopolitical alliances while aggressively managing domestic technological and political stability. The intersection of these policies suggests a tightening of state control over emerging digital sectors and a strategic consolidation of power ahead of future party congresses.
