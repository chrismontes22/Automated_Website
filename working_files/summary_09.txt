Threat actors are recruiting the people who hold cloud logins
============================================================

Cybercriminals are increasingly targeting employees to gain unauthorized access to corporate cloud environments through credential theft, social engineering, and the direct recruitment of malicious insiders for data exploitation.

### Key Points
- Intel 471 reports that cloud-reliant organizations face threats from negligent, manipulated, and malicious insiders who compromise security through poor practices or intentional cooperation.
- Information-stealer malware, including Vidar, Stealc_v2, and ACR, is used to harvest credentials and session cookies for platforms like Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and Slack.
- Adversary-in-the-middle toolkits and phishing kits are actively sold in underground markets to bypass multifactor authentication by capturing tokens in real time.
- Malicious insider recruitment is rising, with documented cases involving the sale of access to Salesforce accounts and point-of-sale systems containing millions of customer records.
- Security experts recommend implementing least-privilege access, regular permissions audits, and phishing-resistant multifactor authentication to mitigate risks posed by the human element.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward cloud-based infrastructure has transformed individual employee credentials into the primary gateway for large-scale corporate data breaches. Organizations must prioritize identity-centric security controls to defend against sophisticated social engineering and the growing underground market for insider access.
