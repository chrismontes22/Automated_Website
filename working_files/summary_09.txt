Repurposing used business-class laptops like the Dell Latitude into home servers offers a cost-effective way to build powerful hardware by leveraging Thunderbolt 4 connectivity for future expansion.

### Key Points
- Used laptops with Thunderbolt 4 ports provide 40Gbps bandwidth, enabling high-speed external storage and multi-gigabit networking.
- Business-grade machines often feature capable CPUs and RAM, making them ideal for self-hosting services like Jellyfin, SearXNG, or Immich.
- Cosmetic damage or broken displays can significantly lower the resale price of laptops without impacting their performance as dedicated home servers.
- Thunderbolt connectivity allows users to bypass hardware limitations by adding external components like 10Gb Ethernet adapters or eGPUs later.
- Prioritizing Thunderbolt-equipped models ensures long-term versatility for DIY projects, even when initial internal specifications are modest.

### Why it Matters
Repurposing older hardware reduces electronic waste while providing a budget-friendly alternative to expensive, purpose-built mini PCs. By identifying specific features like Thunderbolt, consumers can transform depreciated office equipment into highly capable, modular servers that scale with their technical needs.
