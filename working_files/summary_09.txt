Kevin O'Leary has entered a new villain era
============================================================

**Shark Tank investor Kevin O'Leary has become a polarizing figure as he aggressively promotes large-scale AI data center developments in Alberta, Canada, and Utah despite significant local community opposition.**

### Key Points
- Kevin O'Leary is developing the $70 billion "Wonder Valley" project in Alberta and a massive data center campus in Utah.
- A March Gallup poll revealed that over 70% of Americans oppose having a data center built near their homes.
- Local backlash in Utah forced O'Leary to reduce his proposed campus size by more than 20,000 acres following protests and lawsuits.
- O'Leary has faced criticism for baselessly suggesting that opposition to his data centers is funded by the Chinese government.
- More than 1,400 data centers have been built or approved in the U.S. as of late 2023 to support growing AI infrastructure needs.

### Why it Matters
The conflict highlights the growing tension between the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure and the environmental and social concerns of local communities. As a prominent public figure, O'Leary’s involvement underscores how data centers have become a central battleground in the broader national debate over technological progress and energy policy.
