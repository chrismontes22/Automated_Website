'iPhone Ultra' Likely to 'Repeat the iPhone X Story'
============================================================

Apple is expected to unveil its first foldable iPhone, potentially branded as the iPhone Ultra, alongside the iPhone 18 series in 2026 with a delayed release schedule.

### Key Points
- Analyst Ming-Chi Kuo predicts the foldable iPhone will launch after the iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max models due to manufacturing constraints.
- The device may follow a release strategy similar to the 2017 iPhone X, with pre-orders potentially pushed to the fourth quarter of 2026.
- Estimated pricing for the foldable handset is projected to range between $2,299 and $2,499 in the United States.
- Apple suppliers are expected to ship approximately 7 to 8 million units of the foldable device during its initial year of availability.

### Why it Matters
This release strategy highlights Apple's cautious approach to entering the premium foldable market while managing supply chain limitations for high-end hardware. The high price point and limited initial volume suggest the device will serve as a niche, luxury offering rather than a mass-market replacement for standard iPhones.
