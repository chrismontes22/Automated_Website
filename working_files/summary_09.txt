I bought a Lumix G1 for $50 — here’s how the world’s first mirrorless camera fares 18 years after launch
============================================================

To celebrate 25 years of Panasonic Lumix cameras, a tech journalist revisits the Lumix G1, the world’s first mirrorless camera, to evaluate its performance and relevance in 2026.

### Key Points
- The Panasonic Lumix G1, released 18 years ago, was the industry's first mirrorless camera to replace traditional DSLRs.
- Secondhand models are widely available, with complete kits often selling for as little as £80.
- The camera features a 12MP Micro Four Thirds sensor, a 3fps burst rate, and an ISO range limited to 100–3200.
- Despite its age, the G1 includes functional human subject-detection autofocus and a familiar, ergonomic DSLR-style design.
- The Micro Four Thirds system allows for cross-brand lens compatibility, enabling the use of modern lenses like the Olympus 45mm f/1.8.

### Why it Matters
The Lumix G1 serves as a historical benchmark for the mirrorless technology that currently dominates the professional and consumer photography markets. Its continued usability highlights the longevity of camera hardware and offers a budget-friendly entry point for enthusiasts interested in the evolution of digital imaging.
