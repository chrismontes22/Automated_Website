DuckDuckGo's 'No AI' Search Traffic Climbs as Users Reject Google's AI Overhaul
============================================================

DuckDuckGo reports a significant surge in user traffic to its "No AI" search option following Google's recent integration of generative artificial intelligence features into its primary search engine.

### Key Points
- DuckDuckGo visits to its "No AI" search page tripled by May 28 and remain 84 percent above baseline levels since Google's May 19 I/O event.
- The "No AI" search experience excludes chat interfaces, AI-generated answers, and minimizes AI-produced imagery for users seeking traditional search results.
- DuckDuckGo is promoting browser extensions for Chrome and Firefox that set the "No AI" search option as the default experience.
- The company plans to integrate "No AI" settings into existing extensions for Edge and Opera browsers in the near future.
- Alternative privacy-focused search engines like Kagi offer ad-free, non-AI search experiences through subscription models starting at $5 per month.

### Why it Matters
This trend highlights a growing segment of users who prefer traditional search results over AI-generated content and integrated chatbot interfaces. The shift suggests that privacy-focused companies may gain market share by offering alternatives to the increasingly automated search experiences provided by major tech platforms.
