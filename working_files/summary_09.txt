With iOS 27, Shortcuts is about to become what it was always meant to be
============================================================

Apple is reportedly developing an AI-powered upgrade for the Shortcuts app that allows users to create complex automations simply by describing their desired tasks in plain language.

### Key Points
- Apple plans to introduce a text-based prompt feature in Shortcuts that automatically builds and installs custom automations for users.
- The update aims to remove the technical barrier of manually constructing workflows, making the tool accessible to non-technical iPhone, iPad, and Mac users.
- This functionality is expected to be showcased at the upcoming Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC) next month.
- The new system will allow users to describe tasks via text or voice, shifting the app from a manual builder to an intuitive solution hub.

### Why it Matters
This shift toward natural language automation could significantly increase the utility of Apple’s ecosystem by democratizing access to advanced device customization. By lowering the technical barrier to entry, Apple is positioning its AI integration to make powerful, personalized workflows a standard feature for all users rather than a niche tool for experts.
