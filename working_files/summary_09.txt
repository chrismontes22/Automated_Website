The Apple FaceID Co-Inventor Building a Frontier AI Model for the Human Brain
============================================================

Hemispheric, a startup co-founded by former Apple engineer Gidi Littwin, has raised $52 million to develop AI models that diagnose cognitive disorders by analyzing non-invasive brain electrical activity.

### Key Points
- Hemispheric secured $52 million in funding from venture capital firms and investors including Howard Morgan.
- The company trained its AI model using 250,000 hours of brain data collected from 100,000 volunteers in Asia, Tel Aviv, and Boston.
- Patients use a lightweight EEG headset and tablet app to record brain activity for 15-minute diagnostic sessions.
- The startup plans to submit its first product for PTSD diagnosis to the FDA in early 2025, targeting a 2027 public rollout.
- Future development includes creating proprietary brain scanners specifically designed to optimize data collection for deep learning models.

### Why it Matters
This technology aims to transform mental health diagnostics from subjective behavioral assessments into objective, data-driven processes similar to standard blood tests. By providing a scalable and affordable diagnostic tool, Hemispheric could significantly improve early detection and treatment monitoring for conditions like Alzheimer’s, depression, and PTSD.
