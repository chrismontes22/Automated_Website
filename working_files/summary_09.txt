Grid War: How Geopolitics And Anxiety Drive Home Solar
============================================================

Russian state-sponsored hackers targeted Poland’s power grid in December 2025, accelerating a global shift toward residential solar and battery storage as homeowners prioritize energy security over environmental concerns.

### Key Points
- Russian hackers deployed wiper malware against Polish wind and solar farms, impacting heat-and-power plants serving 500,000 homes.
- U.S. federal agencies, including the FBI and CISA, have issued warnings regarding Iranian-affiliated groups targeting domestic critical infrastructure.
- Approximately 78% of U.S. homeowners report concerns about grid reliability, with 64% citing potential blackouts as a primary motivator for solar adoption.
- Despite the 2025 expiration of federal residential solar tax credits, consumer demand remains high due to the desire for energy independence.
- States like California and Maryland are implementing automated permitting processes to accelerate the installation of distributed energy systems.

### Why it Matters
The transition to renewable energy is shifting from a moral or environmental movement to a practical strategy for personal self-defense against cyber warfare and grid instability. As geopolitical tensions and infrastructure vulnerabilities increase, homeowners are increasingly viewing distributed energy as a necessary fail-safe to ensure domestic continuity.
