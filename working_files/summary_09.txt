“You’ll need journalism so distinctive it has its own gravity”: New York Times publisher A.G. Sulzberger on how news organizations can stand up to AI companies
============================================================

New York Times publisher A.G. Sulzberger warned that AI companies are threatening the future of journalism by using copyrighted news content without permission, compensation, or proper attribution.

### Key Points
- A.G. Sulzberger criticized tech giants for "strip-mining" news websites to train AI models and repackage original reporting as their own.
- The New York Times Company has invested over $20 million in legal actions against OpenAI, Microsoft, and Perplexity to protect its intellectual property.
- Sulzberger urged news organizations to prioritize direct audience relationships and focus on distinctive, original reporting to remain viable in an AI-driven landscape.
- Newsrooms were advised to establish clear ethical standards for internal AI use while aggressively adopting the technology to improve operational efficiency.

### Why it Matters
This confrontation highlights a critical tension between the news industry and major technology firms regarding the sustainability of professional journalism in the digital age. If news organizations cannot secure compensation for their content, the resulting decline in original reporting could significantly weaken public accountability and democratic discourse.
