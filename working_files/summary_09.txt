Newsom, Anthropic ink deal to expand government use
============================================================

California Governor Gavin Newsom has secured a partnership with Anthropic to provide state and local government agencies with discounted access to Claude AI tools and technical workforce training.

### Key Points
- The agreement offers California state agencies, counties, and cities a 50% discount on Anthropic’s Claude AI products.
- Anthropic will provide free technical support and workforce training to assist government employees in adopting the technology.
- Claude is currently utilized for state tasks including cybersecurity vulnerability scanning, DMV customer service, and managing Medicaid recipient workflows.
- The deal follows a March executive order from Newsom that allows California to independently evaluate and contract with AI companies despite federal supply chain risk designations.
- State officials intend to pursue similar discount agreements with other artificial intelligence providers to improve government efficiency.

### Why it Matters
This partnership signals California's intent to independently integrate generative AI into public services while navigating potential friction with federal regulatory stances. By securing enterprise-level discounts, the state is setting a precedent for how local governments can scale AI adoption to modernize administrative workflows and public engagement.
