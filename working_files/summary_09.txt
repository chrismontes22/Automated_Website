$4 billion gone. Spot bitcoin ETFs are on track for their worst month on record
============================================================

U.S.-listed spot bitcoin ETFs have recorded a record $4.06 billion in net outflows during June, signaling a significant decline in institutional investor demand for regulated cryptocurrency investment products.

### Key Points
- Investors withdrew $4.06 billion from spot bitcoin ETFs in June, marking the largest monthly redemption since the products launched in January 2024.
- Weekly outflows reached $1.79 billion last week, defying market expectations of increased demand following the June 12 SpaceX IPO.
- Total net outflows for May and June combined reached approximately $6.5 billion, a figure comparable to the market capitalization of the cryptocurrency Zcash.
- Bitcoin’s price has declined by roughly 30% during the first half of 2026, significantly underperforming most major asset classes.
- Shares of MicroStrategy, a major corporate holder of bitcoin, have fallen by 45% amid the broader downturn in institutional crypto interest.

### Why it Matters
The record-breaking exodus from spot bitcoin ETFs highlights a cooling of institutional appetite for regulated digital asset exposure. This trend suggests that market participants are reassessing their cryptocurrency allocations as bitcoin continues to underperform compared to traditional financial assets.
