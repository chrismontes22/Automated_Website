World Cup Propels Surveillance To New Heights
============================================================

The 2026 FIFA World Cup has triggered an unprecedented expansion of government and private surveillance across U.S. host cities, raising significant concerns regarding civil liberties and data privacy.

### Key Points
- The U.S. government allocated over US$1 billion in security funding to upgrade tactical operations, drone capabilities, and surveillance infrastructure for the tournament.
- Host cities are deploying AI-driven facial recognition cameras, robot dogs, and advanced CCTV systems capable of tracking individual behavior and biometric data.
- Over 120 civil society groups, including the ACLU and Amnesty International, issued travel advisories warning of potential racial profiling, device searches, and invasive social media screening.
- Law enforcement agencies are increasingly utilizing autonomous drones equipped with sensors that can intercept communications and monitor urban areas from high altitudes.
- Recent U.S. policy changes regarding immigration enforcement and gender markers on identification have prompted European nations to issue specific travel warnings for their citizens.

### Why it Matters
The rapid deployment of these surveillance technologies creates a permanent infrastructure that often remains in place long after the sporting event concludes. This trend risks normalizing invasive biometric monitoring and data collection without comprehensive federal privacy laws to protect citizens from long-term government and corporate overreach.
