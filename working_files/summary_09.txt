The BenQ MA270S breaks Apple’s monitor monopoly
============================================================

The BenQ MA270S is a 27-inch, 5K-resolution monitor designed specifically for Mac users, offering a high-density display and seamless integration at a lower price point than Apple’s own hardware.

### Key Points
- The monitor features a 5120 x 2880 resolution with 218 PPI, matching the visual quality of Apple’s Retina displays.
- Connectivity includes two Thunderbolt 4 ports with 96W power delivery, two HDMI 2.1 ports, and multiple USB-A and USB-C ports.
- It utilizes the DisplayPilot 2 app for advanced settings, including FocuSync, which allows the monitor to mirror Mac-specific adjustments.
- The device is priced at $900, positioning it as a more affordable alternative to Apple’s official Studio displays.
- Performance is limited by a 70Hz refresh rate and 5ms response time, making it unsuitable for high-speed gaming.
- The design lacks an integrated webcam, requiring users to rely on external cameras or their MacBook’s built-in hardware.

### Why it Matters
The BenQ MA270S provides a rare, high-quality third-party alternative for Apple users who previously faced limited, expensive options for external displays. By offering native-like compatibility and superior port variety, it challenges Apple’s market dominance in the premium monitor segment.
