Inside Microsoft’s high-stakes push to win back its AI lead
============================================================

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella is pivoting the company’s strategy toward agentic AI tools like Copilot Tasks to regain market momentum following a 34% decline in share price since October.

### Key Points
- Microsoft shares dropped 34% between October 28, 2025, and March 27, 2026, amid a broader market repricing of subscription-software companies.
- CEO Satya Nadella has shifted his focus from commercial duties to direct involvement in AI research, product innovation, and data center infrastructure.
- The company is prioritizing Copilot Tasks, an autonomous agent designed to perform computer-based actions, to differentiate its AI stack from competitors.
- Wall Street analysts have labeled the current downturn in software valuations the "SaaSpocalypse," driven by the rise of AI coding agents that threaten traditional subscription models.

### Why it Matters
Microsoft’s struggle to maintain its early lead in the generative AI race highlights the intense pressure on legacy tech giants to prove the long-term profitability of their massive infrastructure investments. The company's pivot toward agentic software represents a critical attempt to defend its margins and per-seat economics against a rapidly evolving field of AI-native competitors.
