AI Is Absorbing Your Brand’s Original Ideas. Here’s How to Keep Your Name Attached.
============================================================

Author and brand strategist Karl Sakas highlights how AI-generated search results often absorb original intellectual property and present it as common knowledge without providing proper attribution to the creator.

### Key Points
- Karl Sakas developed a three-category framework for "trust signals" in 2020, which included website, inbound, and SEO trust signals.
- Google’s AI currently presents this specific framework as industry-standard knowledge without citing Sakas or his original work.
- The author notes that AI models often generalize concepts, stripping away the original source and bylines during the training and synthesis process.
- To maintain intellectual authority, creators must continuously publish new research and frameworks rather than relying on past contributions.
- The author argues that in the AI era, attribution is increasingly tied to ongoing activity rather than the initial establishment of an idea.

### Why it Matters
The rise of AI-driven search results threatens the traditional link between intellectual property and brand authority by decoupling ideas from their original creators. Businesses must now treat thought leadership as a continuous, evolving process to ensure they remain the primary recognized source for their own innovations.
