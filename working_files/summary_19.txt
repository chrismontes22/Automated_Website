Madison Square Garden Sued WIRED for Saying It Tracked Gay Celebrities. WIRED Says Msg’s Own Complaint Confirms It
============================================================

A leaked database from Madison Square Garden reveals the company tracked celebrities and VIPs using risk scores and labels, prompting a defamation lawsuit against WIRED over the reporting.

### Key Points
- A data breach by the group ShinyHunters exposed 45 GB of MSG Entertainment files, including a talent database with nearly 40,000 entries.
- The leaked records categorized celebrities by risk levels and included specific tags for individuals identified as LGBTQIA.
- MSG Entertainment filed a defamation lawsuit against WIRED, claiming the publication inaccurately characterized the company's internal tracking practices.
- WIRED maintains its reporting is accurate, noting that MSG’s own legal complaint confirms the company tracked the sexual orientation of certain performers.
- The breach also compromised personal information, including emails and birthdates, for over 10.5 million individuals associated with the venue operator.

### Why it Matters
This incident highlights growing concerns regarding corporate surveillance practices and the security of sensitive personal data held by major entertainment venues. The legal conflict underscores the tension between investigative journalism and corporate efforts to control narratives surrounding internal data management and privacy policies.
