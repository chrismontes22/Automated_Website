How To Create a Multi-Layered Security Setup for All Your Devices
============================================================

Cybersecurity experts recommend a multi-layered defense strategy combining antivirus software, encryption, and regular system updates to protect personal and professional devices from increasingly complex and evolving digital threats.

### Key Points
- Implement multi-factor authentication and passkeys to defend against credential-based attacks and phishing attempts.
- Enable automatic updates for operating systems and applications to patch known vulnerabilities before they are exploited.
- Use VPNs and HTTPS connections to encrypt data traffic, especially when accessing public Wi-Fi networks.
- Secure home routers by utilizing WPA3 encryption and changing default administrative credentials.
- Protect stored data by enabling device encryption, such as BitLocker on Windows or native iPhone security features.
- Maintain regular, secure backups to prevent permanent data loss resulting from ransomware or hardware failure.

### Why it Matters
Adopting a layered security approach significantly reduces the risk of data breaches by ensuring that a single point of failure does not compromise an entire system. This strategy provides essential resilience for individuals and organizations managing sensitive information in an era of sophisticated cyberattacks.
