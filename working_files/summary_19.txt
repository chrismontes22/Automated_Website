New Report Suggests Age Verification Service Used By PlayStation And Meta Is A Privacy Mess
============================================================

A new report from researchers at Georgia Tech and UC Berkeley alleges that Yoti’s age verification software collects excessive user data and shares sensitive information with third-party services.

### Key Points
- Researchers presented the study, "Papers, Please: A First Look at Age Verification on the Web," at the IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy on May 18.
- Yoti software reportedly gathers unnecessary device metadata, including OS versions, RAM, connection types, and CPU architecture, which could enable unauthorized user tracking.
- The report identifies that Yoti shared sensitive telemetry with the payment processor Stripe, potentially allowing for the identification of users across different websites.
- Yoti claims to have resolved the issue regarding Stripe’s access to first-party website data, though researchers have not independently verified the effectiveness of this fix.
- Yoti’s age verification tools are currently utilized by major platforms including Meta, TikTok, and PlayStation to confirm user age.

### Why it Matters
This report raises significant privacy concerns regarding the data collection practices of identity verification services used by millions of internet users. It highlights the potential for third-party tracking and the risks associated with sharing granular device telemetry during standard security checks.
