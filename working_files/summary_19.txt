"Features that fill in the MacBook Neo's blind spots": Dell's new XPS 13 makes Apple's Neo look dated as I compare the sleek laptops
============================================================

The 2026 Dell XPS 13 debuts as a competitive Windows alternative to the Apple MacBook Neo, offering students premium hardware features at a starting price of $599.

### Key Points
- The Dell XPS 13 features a 13.4-inch 2.5K display with a 120Hz variable refresh rate and touch capability.
- Hardware advantages over the MacBook Neo include a backlit keyboard, quad speakers, an IR camera for facial recognition, and Wi-Fi 7 support.
- The XPS 13 weighs 2.2 pounds, making it lighter than the 2.7-pound MacBook Neo despite housing a larger 52Wh battery.
- Connectivity options include dual Thunderbolt 4 ports on higher-end models, whereas the MacBook Neo is limited to slower USB-C standards.
- Dell’s laptop supports up to 32GB of RAM and 1TB of storage, providing more configuration flexibility than the 8GB RAM limit on the MacBook Neo.

### Why it Matters
The new XPS 13 challenges Apple’s dominance in the student laptop market by addressing specific hardware omissions found in the MacBook Neo. This competition provides Windows users with a high-performance, feature-rich alternative that maintains a comparable entry-level price point.
