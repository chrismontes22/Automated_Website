India plans VPN crackdown
============================================================

The Indian government is developing a new legal framework to regulate virtual private networks, potentially requiring providers to establish local offices and appoint compliance officers to assist law enforcement.

### Key Points
- Proposed regulations may mandate that VPN providers appoint local compliance officers and face potential prison terms for failing to address government directives.
- The initiative follows a 2022 Cert-In mandate requiring data retention, which led many major VPN providers to remove their physical servers from India.
- Authorities aim to curb the use of VPNs to bypass government-ordered content blocks, such as the recent temporary restriction of the messaging app Telegram.
- India currently hosts an estimated 400 million VPN users, representing half of the country's total internet population.
- The government reports that over 24,000 content-blocking orders were issued last year, a figure that officials claim is undermined by widespread VPN usage.

### Why it Matters
These regulations represent a significant escalation in India's efforts to exert control over digital anonymity and enforce national content-blocking policies. If implemented, the framework could force global VPN providers to choose between complying with strict data-access requirements or exiting one of the world's largest internet markets.
