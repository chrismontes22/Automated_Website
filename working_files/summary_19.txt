PS5 Disc Controversey Gets Worse With Major PSN Issue
============================================================

Sony has announced plans to cease production of physical PlayStation game discs by 2028, sparking concerns regarding digital account restrictions and the long-term ownership of user game libraries.

### Key Points
- Sony will officially stop manufacturing physical game discs for PlayStation consoles beginning in 2028.
- PlayStation Network accounts currently lack the ability to change regions, forcing users to create new accounts if they move internationally.
- Creating a new PSN account results in the loss of all legacy data, including Trophies, friends lists, and downloaded PlayStation Plus titles.
- Sony’s terms of service allow for the permanent deletion of accounts due to inactivity, raising concerns about the security of digital-only libraries.
- Unlike competitors such as Nintendo, Steam, and Xbox, Sony maintains strict regional account policies to manage licensing costs and prevent regional price exploitation.

### Why it Matters
The transition to a digital-only ecosystem highlights significant risks for consumers regarding the permanent ownership and portability of their purchased game libraries. As Sony moves away from physical media, the company faces increasing pressure to modernize its restrictive account policies to align with global user mobility and industry standards.
