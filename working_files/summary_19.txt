Car crisis takes toll on Germany's young engineers
============================================================

Germany’s automotive sector is facing a significant downturn as stagnant economic growth and intense competition from Chinese manufacturers lead to widespread job cuts and reduced hiring for young engineers.

### Key Points
- German vehicle production has declined from six million units annually a decade ago to approximately 4.2 million today.
- Volkswagen is reportedly considering up to 100,000 job cuts, sparking protests at sites across Germany.
- Total employment in the German automotive sector dropped eight percent between 2020 and 2025.
- Unemployment among qualified engineers rose to 3.8 percent last year, a nearly 50 percent increase since 2022.
- German exports to China fell by nearly 25 percent to 81.3 billion euros as domestic firms lose market share to competitors like BYD and Xpeng.

### Why it Matters
The decline of Germany's automotive industry signals a broader structural shift as the nation struggles to maintain its competitive edge against lower-cost, high-tech Chinese imports. This transition threatens the long-term job security of highly skilled workers and forces a reevaluation of the country's traditional reliance on industrial manufacturing for economic stability.
