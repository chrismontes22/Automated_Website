UGH! Here Comes Another New ‘Free iPhone’ Scam
============================================================

Scammers are increasingly mailing unsolicited smartphones to unsuspecting victims, hoping users will activate the devices and inadvertently grant criminals access to their personal data, accounts, and digital identities.

### Key Points
- Scammers send unsolicited iPhones or Android devices to victims' homes to trick them into activating the hardware.
- Activating a suspicious device by powering it on, scanning QR codes, or inserting a SIM card can install malware or compromise personal credentials.
- A separate Medicare-related scam involves criminals posing as representatives to steal health insurance information under the guise of providing a free phone.
- Security experts advise against interacting with any unexpected electronic packages to prevent identity theft and unauthorized account access.

### Why it Matters
These sophisticated social engineering tactics exploit human curiosity to bypass traditional digital security measures. Falling for these schemes can lead to severe identity theft, financial loss, and the compromise of sensitive personal information.
