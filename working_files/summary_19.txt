Elon Musk is remaking the world, like Henry Ford before him – but more dangerously
============================================================

The book *Muskism: A Guide for the Perplexed* by Quinn Slobodian and Ben Tarnoff analyzes how Elon Musk’s business empire is fundamentally reshaping global society, democracy, and state power.

### Key Points
- Authors Quinn Slobodian and Ben Tarnoff define "Muskism" as a system of "techno-sovereignty" that merges private corporate infrastructure with national government interests.
- The book traces Musk’s worldview to his upbringing in apartheid-era South Africa, emphasizing a philosophy of "fortress futurism" and self-reliance.
- Musk’s business model relies on state symbiosis, notably through SpaceX’s role as a primary U.S. government contractor and Starlink’s integration into military operations.
- The authors argue that Musk’s companies—including Tesla, Neuralink, and X—aim to create an insular, surveilled socioeconomic order that weakens democratic oversight.
- The text contrasts Musk’s approach with the "Fordism" of the 20th century, noting a shift away from mass employment toward automated, vertically integrated conglomerates.

### Why it Matters
The rise of "Muskism" suggests a significant shift in the balance of power between private tech oligarchs and sovereign states. By controlling critical infrastructure like satellite internet and AI, Musk is effectively shaping the future of global policy and social hierarchy without public mandate or democratic accountability.
