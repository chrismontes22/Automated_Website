UK Parliament begins inquiry into banking chokepoint for crypto businesses
============================================================

The U.K. Crypto and Digital Assets All-Party Parliamentary Group has launched a formal inquiry into banking restrictions and account denials currently affecting cryptocurrency businesses across the country.

### Key Points
- The APPG is chaired by Lord Ed Vaizey and Labour MP Gurinder Singh Josan to investigate systemic "debanking" of crypto firms.
- The inquiry will examine why banks are blocking payments, imposing transfer limits, and denying essential services like insurance to digital asset companies.
- Stakeholders from the banking, fintech, and crypto sectors have been invited to submit written evidence over a six-week period.
- The group intends to publish a formal report with policy recommendations for the U.K. government following the evidence collection phase.

### Why it Matters
This inquiry addresses the growing tension between traditional financial institutions and the digital asset sector, which has long struggled to maintain stable banking relationships. The findings could influence future U.K. regulatory frameworks, potentially easing operational barriers for crypto firms and fostering greater competition within the domestic fintech market.
