Agency bosses say the AI gap with clients is only getting wider
============================================================

Advertising agencies are currently outpacing their clients in artificial intelligence adoption, creating a significant industry gap as brands struggle with internal governance, budget constraints, and unclear strategic objectives.

### Key Points
- Agency executives at Cannes Lions report that while they have developed sophisticated AI platforms, many client brands remain hesitant or unprepared to implement the technology.
- CMOs are reportedly divided into four groups, ranging from those actively building with AI to those who dismiss the technology as a temporary fad.
- Clients face significant hurdles regarding data governance, cybersecurity, and legal sign-offs that often delay the deployment of agency-provided AI tools.
- Marketing budgets remain flat or shrinking, making it difficult for brands to fund AI experimentation that may not show ROI for several months.
- Some industry analysts suggest the gap is exacerbated by agencies using their "early adopter" status as a marketing pitch to justify higher service fees.

### Why it Matters
The widening divide between agency capabilities and client readiness creates a friction point that complicates digital transformation efforts across the advertising industry. As CMOs take on broader responsibility for AI strategy, they must reconcile the need for rapid innovation with the practical realities of corporate procurement and risk management.
