'It’s time to punch more holes in the closed Internet' — This free VPN now promises to be even better at evading censorship
============================================================

The non-profit organization Unredacted has launched FreeSocks v2, an open-source VPN featuring Xray-based proxy technology and post-quantum encryption to help users bypass sophisticated state-sponsored internet censorship and digital firewalls.

### Key Points
- FreeSocks v2 utilizes Xray-based pluggable proxies to evade deep packet inspection and dynamic blocking by state censors.
- The service incorporates X-Wing hybrid encryption, combining classical X25519 and post-quantum ML-KEM-768 algorithms for long-term data security.
- Unredacted now hosts the service on its own hardware in Kansas City, Missouri, to prevent third-party interference or data logging.
- Users can access the VPN without providing personal information, using a proof-of-work challenge to generate anonymous 32-digit account numbers.
- While the service is free, an optional $5 monthly membership removes traffic limits, with donations used to fund bandwidth for all users.

### Why it Matters
This release highlights the escalating technological arms race between state censors and privacy advocates as traditional VPN protocols become increasingly ineffective. By prioritizing hardware ownership and post-quantum security, Unredacted provides a model for how digital rights groups can maintain access to information in high-risk environments.
