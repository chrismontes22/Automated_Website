LGBT Q&A: What Data Are Companies in the UK Collecting When Verifying My Age?
============================================================

The Electronic Frontier Foundation outlines the privacy risks associated with mandatory age verification systems now required for online platforms and services operating within the United Kingdom as of 2025.

### Key Points
- UK law now mandates that platforms hosting content deemed harmful must verify that all users are at least 18 years old.
- Common verification methods include facial age estimation, photo-ID matching, open banking, email analysis, and mobile operator checks.
- Third-party providers like Yoti, Persona, and Incode often process sensitive user data, raising concerns regarding data retention and potential security breaches.
- Privacy risks include the exposure of personal identity, location data, or sensitive information that could lead to harassment or discrimination.
- The EFF advises users to investigate specific provider policies, such as whether data is processed on-device or stored on external servers.

### Why it Matters
Mandatory age verification creates significant privacy vulnerabilities by requiring users to share sensitive personal information with third-party services. These requirements pose particular risks to marginalized groups, as data leaks or improper retention could expose private identities to bad actors or government surveillance.
