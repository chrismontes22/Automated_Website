Esports prize pools keep growing, but crypto’s role in competitive gaming remains a question mark
============================================================

The XSE Pro League Guangzhou 2026 features a $1 million prize pool for Counter-Strike 2, highlighting the esports industry's shift away from volatile cryptocurrency sponsorships toward traditional revenue models.

### Key Points
- The XSE Pro League Guangzhou 2026 is a $1 million Counter-Strike 2 tournament held in China from July 1 to July 12.
- South American team 9z advanced to the playoffs after defeating MIBR in a decisive elimination match.
- The $1 million prize pool is split equally, with $500,000 allocated to players and $500,000 to participating clubs.
- The event has faced operational challenges, including reported malware on player computers and local power blackouts.
- Unlike previous years, the tournament features no cryptocurrency sponsors following the collapse of major industry partners like FTX.

### Why it Matters
The transition away from crypto-backed funding reflects a broader stabilization effort within the esports sector as organizations prioritize reliable revenue streams over speculative partnerships. This shift signals a maturing market that is increasingly cautious about financial sustainability following the widespread collapse of digital asset sponsors.
