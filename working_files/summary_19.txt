Kokuyo Made a Power Strip You Actually Want to Look At
============================================================

Japanese office furniture company Kokuyo has launched the Energy Line, a minimalist, clamp-on power strip designed to eliminate desk clutter with a sleek, low-profile, and continuous-slot aesthetic.

### Key Points
- The Energy Line features a flat, linear design that clamps to desk edges, replacing traditional bulky sockets with a continuous slot.
- It accommodates up to five two-pronged plugs, making it suitable for laptops, monitors, and desk lamps.
- The device is incompatible with grounded, three-prong plugs, requiring users to audit their equipment before purchase.
- Kokuyo, a company with a 120-year history in office furniture and stationery, developed the product to integrate seamlessly into workspace architecture.

### Why it Matters
The Energy Line addresses a long-standing aesthetic gap in modern workspace design by transforming a utilitarian necessity into a discreet, integrated furniture component. This product reflects a growing consumer demand for curated, intentional office environments where even functional accessories contribute to a clean and professional visual experience.
