What Can Hackers Do With Your Phone Number? What To Know
============================================================

While phone numbers are essential for daily digital authentication, they are increasingly targeted by scammers through data breaches and social engineering to facilitate identity theft and financial fraud.

### Key Points
- Verizon research indicates phone numbers were involved in 39% of all data breaches in 2024.
- Attackers use SIM swapping and port-out scams to intercept two-factor authentication codes and hijack financial accounts.
- Scammers frequently employ social engineering, such as fake fraud alerts, to trick victims into revealing sensitive passwords or verification codes.
- The FCC recommends using eSIM technology and setting up account PINs to provide stronger protection against unauthorized carrier transfers.
- Cloning and number spoofing allow criminals to impersonate trusted entities or victims to bypass security measures and deceive contacts.

### Why it Matters
Phone numbers serve as a critical gateway for account recovery and authentication, making them high-value targets for cybercriminals seeking to bypass security protocols. Protecting your number is essential to preventing unauthorized access to your financial assets and sensitive personal information.
