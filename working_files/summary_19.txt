Tesla, SpaceX fall from record highs, Elon Musk lose trillionaire status: Profit-booking or re-rating of AI pricing?
============================================================

Tesla and SpaceX shares have experienced significant declines, prompting investors to re-evaluate valuations for AI and space-sector companies amid broader market volatility and concerns over long-term profitability timelines.

### Key Points
- Tesla shares dropped approximately 8% despite reporting strong second-quarter vehicle delivery numbers.
- SpaceX stock faced a pullback following a strong market debut due to profit-taking and limited share availability.
- Market analysts suggest the decline reflects a broader re-rating of AI-linked valuations rather than fundamental business failures.
- Vested Finance CEO Viram Shah advises investors to focus on portfolio sizing rather than attempting to time market movements.
- Indian retail investors accessing US markets are cautioned that newly listed companies often experience high volatility during initial quarters.

### Why it Matters
This market correction highlights the risks associated with high-growth tech stocks that rely heavily on speculative future technologies like autonomy and AI. Investors should prioritize long-term thesis alignment and prudent position sizing to mitigate the impact of short-term price fluctuations in volatile sectors.
