iOS 27 helps apps detect when a user may be getting scammed in real time
============================================================

Apple is introducing the Trust Insights framework in iOS 27 to help applications detect and mitigate social engineering scams by analyzing user behavioral patterns and interaction context on-device.

### Key Points
- The framework monitors interaction patterns, timing, and sensor data to identify potential fraud without accessing private content in Photos, Messages, or Mail.
- Trust Insights assigns risk levels to transactions, allowing apps to trigger warnings, delays, or additional verification steps for users.
- The system covers five primary operation categories: payment, account management, resource use, communication, and a fallback category for other activities.
- Processing occurs primarily on-device, with only a single output value sent to Apple servers to maintain user privacy.
- Users can disable the feature in settings, though a cooldown period is enforced to prevent scammers from coercing victims into turning off protections.

### Why it Matters
This framework represents a significant shift in mobile security by addressing the human element of fraud that traditional automated filters often miss. By providing developers with real-time risk assessments, Apple aims to curb the rising prevalence of AI-driven scams and authority impersonation attacks.
