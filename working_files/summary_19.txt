4 devices in your home that really should be on Wi-Fi and not Ethernet
============================================================

While Ethernet is traditionally considered the gold standard for connectivity, modern Wi-Fi standards often provide faster, more convenient, and equally reliable performance for most common home electronic devices.

### Key Points
- Most smart TVs feature Ethernet ports capped at 100 Mbps, whereas modern Wi-Fi 5 and 6 standards can significantly exceed these speeds for 4K streaming.
- Popular streaming devices like the Amazon Fire TV Stick and Google Chromecast often lack built-in Ethernet, relying on wireless connectivity for stable performance.
- Printers function effectively over Wi-Fi, allowing for flexible placement without the need for cumbersome cabling in residential environments.
- Mobile devices, including laptops and handheld consoles like the Nintendo Switch, are designed for portability, making wired connections impractical for daily use.
- Ethernet remains beneficial for specific high-bandwidth tasks, such as large file downloads or competitive gaming where minimizing latency is a critical requirement.

### Why it Matters
Relying on outdated assumptions about wired connections can lead to unnecessary spending on adapters and complex cable management for home networks. Understanding when wireless performance is sufficient helps users optimize their home setups for both convenience and efficiency.
