Ask.com shuts down—what replaces Jeeves? #tech
============================================================

Ask.com has officially shut down after nearly 30 years, marking the end of the iconic Ask Jeeves era as search habits shift toward modern, AI-driven conversational interfaces.

### Key Points
- Ask.com, originally launched as Ask Jeeves, has ceased operations after three decades of service.
- The platform pioneered natural-language search queries, allowing users to ask questions in a conversational format.
- Declining user traffic and the rise of large language models contributed to the decision to close the legacy search portal.
- Modern search engines have replaced human-curated Q&A pipelines with dynamic, AI-generated responses.

### Why it Matters
The closure of Ask.com highlights the rapid evolution of search technology from simple query-based portals to sophisticated, model-driven conversational assistants. This transition signals that legacy web brands must integrate advanced AI capabilities to remain relevant in a market now dominated by dynamic, real-time information retrieval.
