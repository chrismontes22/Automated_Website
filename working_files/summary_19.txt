Ford hired AI and sacked humans. It backfired badly
============================================================

Ford has rehired over 350 veteran engineers to oversee quality control after an over-reliance on automated AI systems led to significant production errors and billions of dollars in losses.

### Key Points
- Ford recruited 350 experienced engineers, known as "gray beards," to identify failure points before parts reach the assembly line.
- The company shifted strategy after automated inspection systems failed to provide the nuanced judgment required for complex manufacturing problems.
- Ford recently topped the J.D. Power Initial Quality Survey for mainstream brands, marking its first such achievement in 16 years.
- Despite the rehiring initiative, Ford remains the most recalled automaker in the United States due to lingering issues with older vehicle models.
- Ford plans to integrate AI tools with human oversight rather than abandoning automation, citing the need for expert training data.

### Why it Matters
This shift highlights the limitations of relying solely on AI for complex industrial quality control and the enduring value of human expertise in manufacturing. By balancing automation with veteran oversight, Ford aims to reduce costly recalls and improve long-term vehicle reliability.
