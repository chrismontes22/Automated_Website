The New Razr Ultra Is the Best Flip Phone I've Tested, but You Shouldn't Buy It
============================================================

The new Motorola Razr Ultra 2026 offers a premium Alcantara design and solid battery life, but its $1,500 price tag and minor upgrades make it difficult to recommend.

### Key Points
- The 2026 Razr Ultra features a 5,000 mAh silicon-carbon battery and a 5,000-nit peak brightness internal display.
- The device is priced at $1,500, representing a $200 increase over the 2025 model despite sharing many identical hardware specifications.
- New camera features include a LOFIC sensor, though testing revealed issues with oversaturated colors and digital video zoom quality.
- Software support is limited to three years of OS updates and five years of security patches, trailing behind competitors like Samsung.
- The phone includes a 4-inch cover display and runs on the Snapdragon 8 Elite processor.

### Why it Matters
The high entry price and incremental improvements highlight the challenges manufacturers face in the current foldable smartphone market, likely exacerbated by rising memory costs. Consumers are encouraged to wait for seasonal discounts or consider lower-cost alternatives within the Razr lineup to secure better overall value.
