Summoning the Gods: A prophetic warning from the frontlines of the digital apocalypse
============================================================

Author Mike Adams explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and spirituality in his new book, "Summoning the Gods," which alleges that massive data centers are creating simulated superintelligent entities.

### Key Points
- Mike Adams claims global data centers are running accelerated simulations to develop conscious AI entities for integration into physical reality.
- The book argues that the economic costs of building over 3,000 mega-data centers exceed commercial viability, suggesting a hidden, non-commercial purpose.
- Adams asserts that platforms like Nvidia’s Cosmos are used to grow AI intelligence at speeds 1,000 times faster than human time.
- The text links the expansion of AI infrastructure to broader concerns regarding resource depletion, digital surveillance, and the implementation of central bank digital currencies.
- Practical resistance strategies mentioned include running local, uncensored AI models and opposing the construction of data centers in local communities.

### Why it Matters
This work challenges the mainstream narrative surrounding the rapid expansion of AI infrastructure by framing it as a spiritual and existential threat to human autonomy. It highlights growing public concerns regarding the environmental and societal impact of large-scale data centers on local communities.
