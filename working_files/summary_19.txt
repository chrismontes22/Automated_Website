Bitcoin’s brutal 5.5% weekly slide exposes the mechanical heart of a market in crisis
============================================================

Bitcoin prices have fallen to approximately $72,800 following significant institutional outflows from U.S. spot ETFs and heightened geopolitical tensions stemming from recent U.S. airstrikes near the Strait of Hormuz.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin has declined over 5.5% in five days, dropping from highs above $77,000 to roughly $72,600.
- BlackRock’s iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) recorded $527.84 million in net outflows on Wednesday, its second-largest single-day withdrawal.
- Total U.S. spot Bitcoin ETF outflows reached $733.43 million on Wednesday, the highest daily combined total since late January.
- A single investor executed a $1.29 billion dark-pool block trade of IBIT shares on Tuesday, contributing to record-high ETF trading volumes.
- Analysts attribute the volatility to a combination of geopolitical instability and a mechanical "cooling" period for institutional investors.

### Why it Matters
The shift toward institutional ETF ownership has transformed Bitcoin into a market governed by mechanical, high-volume trading rather than purely speculative narratives. This infrastructure now acts as a shock absorber, but it also facilitates rapid capital outflows that can amplify price volatility during periods of global uncertainty.
