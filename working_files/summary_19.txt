How Do You Scan A QR Code That's Already On Your Phone's Screen? It's Surprisingly Easy
============================================================

Scanning a QR code displayed on your own smartphone screen is a simple process that involves taking a screenshot and using built-in image recognition tools on iOS or Android.

### Key Points
- Capture a screenshot by pressing the power and volume buttons simultaneously on most Android and iPhone devices.
- iPhone users can tap and hold the QR code image in their gallery to reveal the embedded link or message.
- Android users can utilize the Google Lens icon or the Circle to Search feature to identify and open QR code data.
- Google Lens is available as a cross-platform tool for both Android and iOS devices to scan images.
- The FBI advises caution when interacting with QR codes from unknown origins to avoid potential security risks.

### Why it Matters
Mastering these scanning techniques allows users to interact with digital content and marketing materials without needing a second device. Understanding these tools also helps users remain vigilant against security threats associated with scanning unverified or suspicious QR codes.
