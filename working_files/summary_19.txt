These iPhones, iPads, Macs may not support Apple’s new software
============================================================

Apple is expected to drop support for several older iPhone, iPad, and Mac models when it unveils iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 at next week's WWDC event.

### Key Points
- Apple will officially end support for all remaining Intel-based Mac models with the release of macOS 27.
- Rumored iPhone exclusions include the iPhone 11 series and the second-generation iPhone SE.
- iPad models likely to lose support include the eighth-generation iPad, iPad Air 3, and iPad mini 5.
- The Mac compatibility list includes the 2019 Mac Pro, 2020 iMac, and specific 2019 and 2020 MacBook Pro configurations.
- Device compatibility expectations are based on industry leaks and Apple's previously announced transition away from Intel hardware.

### Why it Matters
These software changes will force users of older hardware to consider upgrading their devices to maintain access to the latest security patches and features. This transition marks a significant milestone in Apple's long-term strategy to unify its ecosystem under proprietary silicon while phasing out legacy Intel-based architecture.
