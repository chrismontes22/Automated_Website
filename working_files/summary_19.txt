‘Tomb Raider’ Remake Developed Using Some AI, Everyone Freaks, Crystal Dynamics Responds, And I’m Confused
============================================================

Crystal Dynamics faces public backlash after disclosing the use of AI-assisted tools in the development of its upcoming Tomb Raider remake, despite claims that final assets remain human-crafted.

### Key Points
- Crystal Dynamics included a mandatory Steam AI disclosure notice stating AI tools supported early exploration and temporary development content.
- The developer claims all final assets in the game are human-crafted, contradicting the necessity of the Steam disclosure under Valve's current guidelines.
- Valve’s policy requires disclosure only if generative AI assets are present in the final product or used in public marketing materials.
- Public criticism has intensified due to recent layoffs at Crystal Dynamics and comments from parent company Embracer Group regarding AI efficiency.
- The studio maintains that AI tools are used solely to iterate on ideas faster and empower developer creativity.

### Why it Matters
This situation highlights the growing tension between game developers adopting efficiency-boosting technologies and a skeptical gaming community wary of AI-generated content. It also underscores the confusion surrounding industry disclosure standards as companies struggle to balance internal development workflows with public transparency requirements.
