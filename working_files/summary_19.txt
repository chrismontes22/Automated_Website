Arctic Wolf kicks 250 employees out of the pack to save money for AI
============================================================

Cloudflare is laying off 1,100 employees, representing approximately 20 percent of its workforce, as the company shifts its internal focus and resources toward artificial intelligence-driven operations and development.

### Key Points
- Cloudflare initiated the workforce reduction by notifying approximately 20 percent of its total staff.
- Affected employees received immediate termination notices, with the company citing a strategic pivot toward AI-centric roles.
- The layoffs reflect a broader industry trend where tech firms are aggressively reallocating capital to prioritize AI infrastructure and automation.
- This move follows a period of rapid expansion for the company, now being corrected to align with current market demands for AI integration.

### Why it Matters
These layoffs highlight the significant disruption AI is causing within the tech labor market as companies prioritize automation over traditional roles. This shift signals a broader corporate trend where businesses are rapidly restructuring their workforces to remain competitive in an AI-dominated landscape.
