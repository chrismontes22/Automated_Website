Bitcoin loses $60,000, falls to weakest price since October 2024
============================================================

Bitcoin has fallen below $60,000 for the first time since October 2024, marking a nearly 20% weekly decline amid significant institutional sell-offs and shifting Federal Reserve interest rate expectations.

### Key Points
- Bitcoin dropped below the $60,000 threshold, representing a total decline of over 52% from its October peak of $126,000.
- Strategy, previously the largest buyer of the cryptocurrency, has shifted to a selling position, contributing to the market downturn.
- Spot bitcoin ETFs are experiencing persistent capital outflows as investors pivot toward artificial intelligence stocks and related technology sectors.
- The Federal Reserve is now expected to implement interest rate hikes rather than cuts due to stubborn inflation and strong labor market data.
- The broader crypto market lost approximately $390 billion in value this week, with nearly $7 billion in leveraged positions liquidated.

### Why it Matters
This sharp decline signals a major shift in investor risk appetite as capital moves away from digital assets toward the artificial intelligence trade. The transition from expected rate cuts to potential hikes creates a challenging environment for speculative assets and broader equity markets.
