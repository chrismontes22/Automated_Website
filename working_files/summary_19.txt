10 Sunday Reads
============================================================

Recent reports highlight systemic challenges across U.S. sectors, ranging from the resurgence of livestock parasites due to federal budget cuts to concerns over corporate influence and regulatory oversight.

### Key Points
- A screwworm parasite outbreak in Texas cattle threatens beef prices following federal funding cuts to prevention programs led by the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE).
- Acting Consumer Financial Protection Bureau director Russell Vought has faced criticism for rolling back enforcement actions that previously impacted major corporations like Apple and Walmart.
- A federal IT staffer is suing for defamation after reporting data handling issues at DOGE and subsequently experiencing a suspicious incident involving his vehicle's brake lines.
- China has overtaken the United States in electric vehicle adoption and production, despite the U.S. having pioneered the modern EV market with the Tesla Model S.
- Prediction markets like Polymarket and Kalshi are increasingly adopting lobbying and retention strategies similar to those used by the online gambling industry.

### Why it Matters
These developments illustrate the significant real-world consequences of shifting federal priorities and reduced regulatory oversight on public health, consumer protection, and market competitiveness. As government agencies undergo restructuring, the resulting gaps in policy enforcement and infrastructure maintenance create vulnerabilities that directly impact both the national economy and individual safety.
