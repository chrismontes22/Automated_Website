Yikes, Encryption’s Y2K Moment is Coming Years Early
============================================================

Google has accelerated its quantum preparedness deadline to 2029, urging engineers and organizations to upgrade cryptographic systems to defend against future quantum-enabled decryption and authentication attacks.

### Key Points
- Google moved its post-quantum migration deadline to 2029, citing rapid advancements in quantum technology research.
- Quantum computers threaten to decrypt historical traffic captured today and could allow attackers to forge digital signatures or impersonate users.
- NIST finalized standards for post-quantum cryptographic algorithms in 2024, and approximately 40% of websites currently support post-quantum key exchange.
- Symmetric encryption remains quantum-resistant, but key agreement and authentication protocols require urgent updates to prevent data exposure.
- Certain technologies, such as trusted execution environments, may be phased out because they cannot be effectively secured against quantum threats.

### Why it Matters
This shift highlights the urgent need for a global transition to quantum-resistant security to prevent the mass exposure of sensitive historical communications and private data. Businesses and users must prioritize software updates and hardware upgrades to ensure their digital infrastructure remains secure against the evolving capabilities of quantum computing.
