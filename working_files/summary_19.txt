Federal Reserve taps Xbox CEO Asha Sharma to lead jobs task force days after Microsoft laid off 3,200 people
============================================================

Xbox CEO Asha Sharma has been appointed to co-lead a new Federal Reserve task force investigating the impact of artificial intelligence on national productivity and employment trends.

### Key Points
- Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh established five new task forces to evaluate economic policy, including the Productivity and Jobs panel.
- Sharma will lead the panel alongside Andreessen Horowitz co-founder Marc Andreessen and Stanford economist Charles I. Jones.
- The appointment follows Microsoft’s announcement of a major restructuring plan that will eliminate 3,200 positions within its Xbox division by 2027.
- The task force will specifically analyze how general-purpose technologies and automation influence labor markets and economic growth.
- Findings from the panel will be presented to the Federal Open Market Committee to help inform future monetary policy decisions.

### Why it Matters
This appointment highlights the growing tension between corporate reliance on automation and the Federal Reserve's mandate to maintain maximum employment. The findings could significantly influence how regulators approach the economic shifts caused by rapid advancements in artificial intelligence.
