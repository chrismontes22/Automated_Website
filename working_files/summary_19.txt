Valve is reportedly importing large shipments of hardware into the country, signaling a potential release for the long-delayed Steam Machine, Steam Frame, or restocked Steam Deck handheld devices.

- Valve has faced significant product delays and stock shortages for the Steam Deck due to rising global memory and storage costs.
- Shipping manifests indicate that Valve has recently imported large quantities of hardware, suggesting a new product launch is imminent.
- The company has not yet confirmed pricing or specific release dates for the Steam Machine console or the Steam Frame VR headset.
- Other industry players, including Apple, are also adjusting product configurations and RAM options to navigate the ongoing memory supply shortage.

### Why it Matters
These shipping activities suggest that Valve is finally overcoming supply chain constraints that have hindered its hardware roadmap for months. A successful launch of new gaming hardware could significantly impact the competitive landscape for handheld and living-room gaming devices.
