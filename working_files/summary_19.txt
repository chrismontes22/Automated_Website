Microsoft’s own data suggests AI is more expensive than hiring humans, as a mystery firm burns USD 500 million on Claude in one month
============================================================

Microsoft is shifting its internal development workflows from Anthropic’s Claude Code to its own GitHub Copilot CLI as the company prepares to debut a new coding model at Build 2026.

### Key Points
- Microsoft is mandating that employees transition from Claude Code to the in-house GitHub Copilot CLI by the end of June.
- The company maintains its multibillion-dollar Foundry partnership with Anthropic despite the internal shift in coding tools.
- Microsoft plans to unveil a new proprietary coding model during its annual developer conference, Build 2026.
- Reports indicate an unnamed corporation recently incurred $500 million in costs after failing to set usage limits on employee Claude AI licenses.
- Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella noted that AI currently generates approximately 30% of the company's internal code.

### Why it Matters
This transition highlights the growing tension between the high operational costs of generative AI and the need for companies to demonstrate clear financial returns. As investors become increasingly skeptical of AI spending, Microsoft’s move reflects a broader industry effort to consolidate internal tools and manage the massive expenses associated with large-scale AI deployment.
