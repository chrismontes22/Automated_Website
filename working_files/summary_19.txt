Why Google Antigravity 2.0 Split Its Most Popular AI Tools
============================================================

Google has launched Antigravity 2.0, a modular AI management platform featuring a standalone desktop application, a command-line interface, a software development kit, and a separate integrated development environment.

### Key Points
- The new standalone desktop application serves as a centralized hub for task scheduling and multi-agent orchestration.
- The Antigravity IDE remains available as a separate download for developers despite being removed from the default installation bundle.
- The Antigravity CLI replaces the Gemini CLI, which is scheduled for full deprecation on June 18.
- The SDK allows developers to build custom AI behaviors and integrate agent workflows into existing CI/CD pipelines.
- Users can maintain existing project data by reinstalling the IDE separately after the platform's auto-update process.

### Why it Matters
This modular shift allows developers and engineers to choose specialized tools tailored to their specific workflow requirements rather than relying on a single bundled environment. By separating these components, Google provides greater flexibility for scaling complex AI operations while ensuring that power users retain access to familiar coding interfaces.
