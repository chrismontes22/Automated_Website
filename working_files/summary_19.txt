CTOs Agree: Cognitive Debt Is the New Technical Debt
============================================================

Senior engineering leaders are shifting their focus from rapid AI adoption to managing "cognitive debt," as organizations struggle to measure ROI and maintain code quality in an AI-driven environment.

### Key Points
- Engineering leaders report that the era of unrestricted AI spending is over, with CFOs now demanding clear ROI within 12 months.
- "Cognitive debt" has emerged as a primary concern, as AI-generated code increases maintenance overhead and complicates long-term system architecture.
- Human judgment remains the primary bottleneck, shifting the focus of engineering interviews from coding proficiency to code review and systematic problem-solving.
- Experts recommend standardizing AI tools and investing in robust evaluation suites to mitigate risks associated with model dependency and vendor lock-in.
- Organizations are exploring structural changes, such as dedicated "technical health teams," to balance feature velocity with necessary refactoring and maintenance.

### Why it Matters
The transition from experimental AI adoption to operational maturity highlights a critical shift in how engineering departments manage resources and technical debt. By prioritizing verification and standardized workflows over raw output, companies can avoid the long-term costs of unmanaged AI-generated code and ensure sustainable software development.
