New MacBook Ultra report covers release timing, display, more
============================================================

A new report from research firm Omdia suggests Apple’s upcoming MacBook Ultra will feature tandem OLED display technology and is currently on track for a third-quarter 2026 release.

### Key Points
- Omdia projects a third-quarter 2026 launch for the new MacBook, potentially aligning the release with Apple’s annual September iPhone event.
- The devices will feature 14.3-inch and 16.3-inch displays, representing a slight size increase over current MacBook Pro models.
- Samsung is slated to begin supplying the hybrid OLED panels, which utilize oxide TFT and RGB tandem technology, starting in July.
- The new display architecture is specifically engineered to improve power efficiency compared to existing LTPO and single-layer OLED configurations.

### Why it Matters
The transition to tandem OLED technology signals a significant shift in Apple’s hardware strategy, aiming to balance high-performance visuals with improved battery efficiency. This development could allow for thinner laptop designs without compromising the longevity or display quality that professional users expect from the MacBook line.
